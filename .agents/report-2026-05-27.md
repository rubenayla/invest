## SpaceX IPO

### Key Facts
- **Date**: S-1 filed; roadshow June 4, pricing June 11, Nasdaq listing **June 12, 2026** under ticker **SPCX**
- **Valuation**: $1.75–$2 trillion (target). Would surpass Saudi Aramco's 2019 IPO as the largest in history.
- **Raise**: ~$75–$80 billion
- **Structure**: Traditional IPO. 23 underwriters (Goldman, Morgan Stanley, JPM, Citi, BofA). Notably, **30% of shares earmarked for retail investors** — an unusually high allocation designed to activate Musk's loyal retail base.
- **Financials**: Starlink generated $3.26B revenue in the latest quarter (69% of total), but SpaceX posted a **net loss of $4.28 billion** in that quarter, and lost $4.94B in all of 2025. This is a pre-profit growth story.

### Market Effects

**Index rebalancing — the underappreciated systemic risk**: If SpaceX achieves a 50% free float and a $2T post-listing valuation and gets added to core indices (S&P 500), passive funds would be forced to sell approximately **$950 billion of existing holdings** — NVDA, AAPL, MSFT, AMZN, GOOG, AVGO, META, TSLA. This doesn't happen at IPO (S&P 500 requires 12 months of public trading), but it's a 12–18 month tail risk that should influence how you hold current Mag7 exposure.

**Space ecosystem stocks** have already run: Rocket Lab +78% YTD, Intuitive Machines +110% YTD, Satellogic +440% YTD. The "buy the ecosystem" trade is entirely priced in.

**TSLA risk**: SpaceX IPO creates a second Musk vehicle for retail capital. Fortune and multiple analysts flagged: Tesla has already been losing Musk's bandwidth to SpaceX. Post-IPO, retail money that might have rotated into TSLA will flow to SPCX instead. Tesla's $2B equity stake in SpaceX gives it some exposure, but this is a net attention-and-capital drain. TSLA at $433 is not cheap (vs fundamentals of a slowing EV maker). Avoid adding TSLA as a SpaceX proxy.

**Broad market liquidity**: $75–$80B is being pulled out of existing portfolios and parked in SPCX allocations. This creates short-term selling pressure on existing holdings in the 2–3 weeks around June 12. Be cautious about expecting rallies in non-space holdings around IPO day.

### My Take: Do Not Buy SPCX at IPO

**The valuation is indefensible on current earnings.** $1.75T on a company losing $4.28B/quarter is a P/S of ~135x annualized Starlink revenue and undefined multiples on a loss. The closest comparable is a 2000-era internet infrastructure play. SpaceX is a genuine technological achievement — Starship, Starlink, reusability — but paying 135x revenue at IPO captures that and then some.

The 30% retail allocation is not a feature; it's a warning. It's designed to create a meme-stock ecosystem around Musk. Retail FOMO will be intense. That same FOMO created first-day pops that subsequently corrected 40–60% in comparable mega-IPOs (Rivian, Palantir). The retail tilt means IPO day excitement, followed by index inclusion pressure (which takes 12 months and requires profitability), followed by forced selling from early retail holders who bought at peak.

**Position**: Hold zero SPCX at IPO. Revisit 6–9 months post-listing when the retail frenzy has passed and the 12-month profitability track record becomes legible. If Starlink's quarterly revenue hits $5B+ with approaching breakeven, and the stock is below $1.2T valuation, it becomes interesting. Until then, the only trade is patience.

**Indirect play**: You already own FSLR and NOW — both benefit from the same capital-intensive infrastructure buildout narrative without the SpaceX-specific FOMO premium. Keep those.
