# Marubeni Corporation (8002.T) — FY2026 Full-Year Earnings Review

**Date:** 2026-05-02 | **Verdict: IN-LINE (quality miss; strong capital returns)**

Marubeni's FY2026 headline net income of ¥543.9B (+8.1% YoY) nominally beat its own ¥540B guidance by less than 1%, but the beat was entirely attributable to a ¥104.8B Q3 non-recurring gain; core commodity segments (Energy & Chemicals, Metals) both declined YoY, and Q4 quarterly EPS missed analyst consensus by 5.5%. The expanded buyback (¥75B total) and FY2027 dividend guide of ¥115 are genuine positives but insufficient to reclassify this as a full BEAT.

---

## 5-Bullet Summary

- **Net Income (FY2026):** Consolidated NI ¥543.9B (+8.1% YoY from ¥503.0B), slightly above the revised ¥540B guidance. However, ¥104.8B of that came from a Q3 non-recurring gain; stripping the one-off, core NI was approximately ¥439B — below FY2025 actual. Net margin compressed from 6.9% → 6.2% YoY even with the boost. Q4 quarterly EPS of $4.33 (MARUY ADR) missed the $4.58 analyst consensus by 5.5%.

- **FY2027 Guidance:** Management guides consolidated NI at **¥580B** (+6.6% growth). CEO Masayuki Omoto noted 30–40B additional upside if current commodity conditions persist ("our forecast is based on assumptions prior to the current Middle East situation; if conditions continue, there is potential upside"). Revenue also guided higher on continued non-resource diversification. FY2027 guidance is solid but in-line with analyst expectations, not a blowout.

- **Buyback Expansion (KEY POSITIVE):** On May 1, 2026 Marubeni announced a major expansion of its share repurchase program: **¥60B / 20M shares** (1.2% of outstanding, through January 29, 2027), on top of the existing ¥15B / 5M share tranche (through June 30, 2026). Total authorized buyback: **¥75B / 25M shares** — a ~5x increase vs. the prior ¥15B program. As of April 30, ¥3.4B had been executed. Also acquired 85% of Spain's Factorenergia energy retailer (€204M), expanding the power segment.

- **Dividend:** FY2026 dividend confirmed at **¥107.5/share** (interim ¥50 + year-end ¥57.5, +7.5% vs ¥100 prior year). FY2027 dividend guided at **¥115/share** (+7% growth), continuing the progressive dividend track record. Total FY2027 shareholder yield (dividends + ¥75B buyback on ~¥9.5T market cap) rises to approximately 3.3–3.5%.

- **Segment Commentary:** Revenue increased in **Metals & Mineral Resources** and **Food & Agri Business**, but profit declined in both **Energy & Chemicals** (−¥12.8B, weaker petrochemical trading) and **Metals & Mineral Resources** (−¥11.2B, Australian steelmaking coal decline). Gavilon/agri and Roy Hill JV specifics not separately disclosed but food/agri revenue growth is a positive signal. Segment reorganization (16→10 segments, consolidating Food I, II, Agri into Food & Agri; Chemicals into Energy & Chemicals) completed. New U.S. domestic demand-focused strategy cited by management as tariff hedge. No Berkshire/Buffett stake change commentary in public releases; Berkshire remains at ~9.3%.
