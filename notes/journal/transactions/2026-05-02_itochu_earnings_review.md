# Itochu Corporation (8001.T) — FY2025 Full-Year Earnings Review

**Date:** 2026-05-02 | **Verdict: BEAT**

Itochu's FY2025 (year ending March 31, 2026) print qualifies as a **BEAT** on the thesis: net income landed at the top of the base case range while the buyback program was nearly doubled — the defining bull-case trigger — and FY2026 forward guidance sets a new record bar.

---

## 5-Bullet Summary

- **Net Income:** FY2025 consolidated NI ≈ **¥900B** (+2.3% YoY from ¥880.3B), matching full-year guidance and landing at the upper boundary of the thesis base case (¥880–900B). Note: Eulerpool database cites ¥940.6B (+6.86%), possibly reflecting a different income definition; the Quartr/Itochu official report narrative states +2.3% YoY.
- **FY2027 Guidance (Itochu "FY2026 Plan"):** Management guided FY2026 (April 2026–March 2027) consolidated NI at **¥950B** (+5.5% growth), demonstrating confidence in continued non-resource earnings momentum despite yen and tariff headwinds.
- **Buyback:** FY2026 buyback authorization raised to **¥300B or more** — up from ¥170B executed in FY2025 (+76%), the largest single-year shareholder return escalation in company history. Total payout ratio target raised from 52% → **64%**.
- **Dividend:** FY2025 actual: ¥42.0/share (post-split; ≡ ¥210 pre-split, 11th consecutive annual increase). FY2026 guidance: **¥44.0/share or higher** (post-split), continuing the progressive dividend track record.
- **Segments:** Food, ICT & Financial Business, and Textile segments drove H1 record profit (¥500.3B, +14% YoY). FamilyMart repositioned within the Consumer segment with digital/loyalty initiatives intact. Resources exposure remains minimal — management explicitly noted negligible Middle East energy investment, insulating Itochu from energy-price volatility. North American power operations cited as a growth contributor.
