# MOH — 2026 Investor Day Review
**Date:** 2026-05-09 | **Position:** ~$5,500 (IBKR + Revolut)

## Verdict: BULLISH — ADD MORE (30–50%)

Management's 2029 EPS target of $28–30 far exceeds the thesis bull case of $18–20 by 2028, validating the recovery thesis with room to spare and confirming this position was entered at an extremely early stage of the re-rating.

---

## Five Key Bullets

1. **2027 EPS trajectory — in-line/slightly below thesis base:** Morgan Stanley models $9.00 for 2027 (raised from $8.50 post-Q1); management's own "embedded earnings" framework implies higher. The $2.50/share one-time drag (MAPD + Florida implementation) rolls off in 2027, lifting from the $5 trough baseline. 2027 is still recovery-in-progress, not normalized, but the direction is unambiguous.

2. **2029 EPS target — $28–30 adjusted (thesis-beating):** Management formally guided 2029 adjusted EPS of $28–30 (confirmed by multiple coverage sources) vs. thesis bull case of $18–20 by 2028. Revenue target $50–52B, cumulative operating cash flow $5.5–6B. At 15x: $420–450 vs. ~$192 today = ~130% upside to normalized. Even at 10x (trough multiple): $280–300. This alone justifies a position add.

3. **Florida CMS contract — on-track, 2-year harvest period:** Described as a "$6B revenue program at full run-rate" with an "attractive" financial profile once implementation costs clear (~2028). The $1.50/share Florida drag in 2026 is explicitly a 2026-only drag; management expects profitability ramp through 2027–2028. Bulls (including our thesis) were right on this — the contract is large and the implementation penalty is finite.

4. **Medicare Advantage (MAPD) exit — confirmed for 2027:** MAPD exit from traditional products confirmed for plan year 2027, removing the ~$1.25/share 2026 drag. New integrated dual products (D-SNP/HIDE/FIDE) performing "better than anticipated" in Q1. Post-exit 2027 Medicare revenue projected ~$5.5B at 94% MCR — still loss-making on Medicare, but the catastrophic MAPD losses are gone. This was a known 2026 headwind and is executing on plan.

5. **Capital allocation — optionality building, no major new authorization announced:** Q1 parent cash $213M, projected year-end >$600M. Management described as "aggressively buying back shares" (Morgan Stanley). Debt-to-capital at 48% vs. target low-40s range — some deleveraging likely before large buyback authorizations. No material new buyback authorization was announced at Investor Day. M&A: RFP wins and new contracts highlighted as primary growth vehicle (organic-first). Slight negative vs. thesis expectation, but manageable.

## Next Catalyst — July 2026 Medicaid Rate Renewals (FL / CA / TX)

**Date:** July 1, 2026 (effective); preliminary notices may leak May–June 2026.

**What to watch:**
- FL, CA, TX rate increases of ≥4% close the rate-trend gap and confirm the $9+ 2027 EPS path.
- ≥3% but <4%: HOLD, wait for Q2 earnings print.
- <3% increases in key states: TRIM — the trough extends and 2027 EPS is at risk of being <$8.

Management commentary on the Q1 call was explicitly optimistic: *"States continue to update their actuarial data to reflect higher observed trend. We remain optimistic. States may provide off-cycle and retro rate increases throughout the year as they did last year."* Off-cycle increases are already happening, which is a leading indicator.

**This is the single most important remaining data point for the thesis.** The Investor Day confirmed the normalized earnings power; July rates confirm the timing.
