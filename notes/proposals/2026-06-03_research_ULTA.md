# Proposal: ULTA — overreaction to a beat-and-raise, quality franchise near 52-week lows

**Date:** 2026-06-03 | **Price:** ~$467 | **Call:** WATCH → BUY (starter, accumulate <$450)

## Why look now

Ulta Beauty reported Q1 FY2026 yesterday (2026-06-02) — a clean **beat-and-raise** — and the stock **fell ~6% today** (June 3, $495 → $467), landing near its 52-week low ($452). The drop is a "sell the cautious guidance" reaction, not a reaction to the numbers:

- Net sales **$3.16B (+11.1%)**, comparable sales **+5.3%** — *well above* the unchanged full-year guide of +2.5–3.5%.
- Operating income +11.6% to $448M; diluted **EPS $7.74 (+15.5%)**.
- Full-year EPS guidance **raised** to $28.36–$28.80.
- **$555M of buybacks in the quarter alone** (~$2B annualized pace), retiring stock at depressed prices.

The market focused on management keeping its comp/sales ranges flat (implying H2 deceleration from tariffs, the Target shop-in-shop wind-down by Aug 2026, and tougher compares) and "only modest leverage" on margins. That conservatism, not weakness, drove the sell-off.

## The setup

A 47%-ROE, ~$1B-FCF specialty-retail leader with a ~45M-member loyalty moat, trading at **~16.3x this year's *raised* EPS guide and ~14.7x next year's consensus** — below its 18–22x history. The buyback is the swing factor: at the Q1 pace it shrinks the share count ~10%/yr, growing EPS double-digits even on flat net income.

## Asymmetry

| Scenario | Target | Return |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Bear (30%) — comps to 1–2%, op margin <13% | ~$375 | −20% |
| Base (45%) — comps 3–4%, margin ~14%, modest re-rate | ~$535 | +15% |
| Bull (25%) — comps 4–5%, margin stable, full re-rate to 20x | ~$660 | +41% |

Probability-weighted EV ≈ **+11% at $467**, ~**+18% below $450**. Positive, buyback-backstopped, but not a deep bargain — hence starter sizing with adds into weakness.

## Action

- **Entry:** starter ~$467; preferred accumulation **<$450** (~15x guide, near/under 52-week low).
- **Thesis-break:** comps turn negative, op margin <~13%, or an EPS guidance cut → structural erosion, downgrade/exit.
- **Upgrade trigger:** two quarters of comps ≥3% with op margin ≥14%.

Risks to respect: Amazon (now #1 beauty retailer), Sephora/Kohl's, TikTok Shop, Target's own beauty build-out (74% store overlap), and 2026-H2 tariff pressure on beauty.
