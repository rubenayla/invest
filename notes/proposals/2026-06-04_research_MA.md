# Research proposal — Mastercard (MA), 2026-06-04

**Call:** BUY (scale-in) · **Grade:** B (solid, starter→scale-in) · **EV:** ≈+21% over ~2yr
**Price:** $471.55 (2026-06-03) · **Mkt cap:** ~$417B · Full note: [notes/companies/MA.md](../companies/MA.md)

## Why now
Mastercard — a wide-moat, capital-light, mid-teens EPS compounder and half of the global card-network duopoly — is **down ~21% off its 52-week high** ($596→$472) and ~14% YTD, even though it **beat Q1 2026 and reaffirmed guidance**. The de-rating is pure sentiment: fear that stablecoins + AI-agent commerce disintermediate the card rails. That has pushed MA to **~24x FY2026 EPS / ~20.7x FY2027 EPS**, versus a ~10-year forward-PE average near **32x** — among the cheapest the franchise has traded on forward earnings in a decade. Notably, sibling Visa (V) sits near its highs; MA de-rated harder for no fundamental reason.

## The mispricing
The *multiple* prices a structural growth/margin impairment the *fundamentals* have not shown:
- FY2025 revenue +16%, net income +16%, EPS $16.52 (+19%); net margin ~46%.
- Consensus EPS FY2026 $19.65 (+15.5%), FY2027 $22.75 (+15.8%); 35/38 analysts Buy/Outperform, **zero sells**, mean target ~$655 (+39%), low target $550 (+17%).
- Stablecoins threaten the *settlement* layer, not the consumer network (rewards, fraud, disputes, universal acceptance, credit). POS adoption faces no-rewards / taxable-event / volatility frictions and is years away at scale. Meanwhile MA is monetizing it directly — agreed to acquire **BVNK** ($1.8B) for stablecoin pay-in/out across 130+ countries, plus the Multi-Token Network. Threat → product line.

## Asymmetry (2-yr, on FY2027 EPS ~$22.75)
| Scenario | Exit PE | Price | Return |
|----------|---------|-------|--------|
| Bear (erosion bites, 10% growth, multiple stuck) | 18x | ~$387 | −18% |
| Base (moat intact, ~15% growth, partial re-rate) | 26x | ~$592 | +25% |
| Bull (fear unwinds toward ~31x historical) | 31x | ~$705 | +50% |

Prob-weighted (25/50/25): **≈+21%** plus ~0.7% dividend + ~2% buyback. Downside is multiple risk, not terminal-decline risk — even the bear case is a profitable, growing franchise.

## Entry / break
- **Entry:** attractive at ~$472; add toward **$440–450** (near 2y low $425 ≈ 21–22x FY2026).
- **Thesis-break:** switched-transaction/cross-border **volume growth below ~8% for 2+ quarters**, net-revenue growth to mid-single-digits, visible POS stablecoin displacement in volumes, or U.S. Credit Card Competition Act passing. A break below ~$410 on deteriorating *volumes* (not sentiment) = the toll is actually eroding.

## Grade rationale
**B, not A:** high conviction on quality and a genuine multi-year-low multiple, but the asymmetry is a compounder's (+21% base-case EV, ~−18% downside) rather than a deep-value double — act with a starter and scale in on weakness, don't max-size on day one. Next catalyst: Q2 2026 earnings, ~late July.
