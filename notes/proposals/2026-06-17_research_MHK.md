# Proposal: Mohawk Industries (MHK) — first look, WATCH (lean BUY LOW)

**Date:** 2026-06-17 | **Price:** $111.52 | **Scenario EV:** ~+21% (to end-2028) | **Standalone rating:** WATCH (lean BUY LOW)
**Note:** `notes/companies/MHK.md`

## One-liner
World's largest flooring manufacturer trading at **0.81x book, ~11x forward EPS, ~5.9x trailing EV/EBITDA** at a housing-cycle trough, with earnings already inflecting (**Q1 2026 net income +61% YoY**) and a clean balance sheet (net debt ~1.4x EBITDA). Cheap, deleveraging, buying back ~3%/yr of shares below book — but mediocre quality (5% ROE, no dividend, 3 years of declining revenue), so the upside is rate-/housing-dependent.

## Why it might be worth the owner's attention now
- **Asset-value downside cushion:** paying $111.52 for ~$137 of book equity; balance sheet is lightly levered (~$18M interest on $2.46B debt), so the bankruptcy/dilution risk that usually accompanies "below book" is absent.
- **Earnings turning up off the bottom:** Q1 2026 net income $117.1M vs $72.6M a year earlier. High operating leverage means a housing-turnover recovery flows hard to EPS.
- **Self-funding wait:** ~$440M capex vs >$1.1B operating cash flow funds buybacks at below book (accretive), shrinking the count from ~71M to ~61M shares.
- **Clear, single catalyst:** falling mortgage rates → existing-home sales → remodeling demand. It's a "when," and any easing is a direct tailwind.

## Why it's only a WATCH (kill-the-idea pass)
- **Structurally low returns:** ~5% ROE even into the recovery — a capital-intensive commodity manufacturer, not a compounder. Mid-cycle economics are unexciting.
- **No top-line inflection confirmed:** revenue has fallen three straight years ($11.74B → $10.79B); the recovery could be pushed out for years if rates stay high.
- **No dividend** — no carry while waiting out a long cycle.
- **"Below book" is not a floor** — traded to ~0.55x book ($76) in the 2023 stress; a recession re-tests that.
- **Secular pressure:** carpet ceding share to hard surfaces; LVT (the growth product) is the most import-exposed/lowest-moat line.

## Suggested action
Add to the deep-cyclical-value watch bucket. **Entry attractive sub-$110 (≤0.8x book), aggressive sub-$95 (~0.7x book).** Re-rate to active buy on two consecutive quarters of YoY revenue growth with expanding incremental margins. Thesis-break: below ~$85 on a *deteriorating* (not panic-driven) trend, or a fourth straight year of revenue decline with margins still compressing.
