## One-paragraph thesis

Hershey's earnings were *halved* by a once-in-a-generation cocoa-cost spike (net income $2.22B→$883M FY25, gross margin 47%→34%), not by any demand problem — **revenue actually grew** through the shock ($11.2B→$11.7B). Cocoa has since collapsed to ~$3,100–4,100/t (from a ~$12,000 2024 peak), *below* even what the market projected for 2026. Management reaffirmed (Q1, April 30 2026) a FY2026 recovery of **~400bps gross-margin improvement and +30–35% adjusted EPS (+79–89% reported), double-digit-weighted to H2**. At $183 (−24% off high) you pay ~18.5x recovered GAAP earnings — the low end of HSY's historical 22–26x — for the #1 US confection franchise, with a 15-yr-streak 3.15% dividend paying you to wait and beta ~0.07 for ballast.

## Why it clears the bar now

- **Confirmed catalyst, not a hope:** the margin inflection starts Q2 2026 as cheap cocoa clears high-cost hedged inventory; guidance reaffirmed on a strong Q1.
- **Mispriced on the right number:** market anchors on the 2026 recovery and under-weights **2027 — the first full clean low-cocoa year**, where EPS can top the 2024 ~$11 peak.
- **Asymmetry ~2:1:** base ~$200–210 (+9–15%) + 3.1% yield; bull ~$250 (+37%); bear ~$155 (−15%, near the 52w low where you'd add).
