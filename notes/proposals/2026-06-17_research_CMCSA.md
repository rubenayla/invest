# Proposal: Comcast (CMCSA) — first look, lean BUY LOW at $23.66

**Date:** 2026-06-17 | **Price:** $23.66 (52-week low $23.13) | **EV: ~+34%** | Full note: `notes/companies/CMCSA.md`

## Why look now
Comcast trades at a **2-year low, ~6.3x forward EPS, P/B 0.96 (below book), EV/EBITDA 4.8x**, with a **5.6% dividend** (26% payout) plus ~$7B/yr buybacks = **~14% total shareholder yield**. It has bought back ~19% of its shares in 4 years. This is one of the cheapest large-caps in the market, and it's pinned at the low — a deep-value income + buyback setup.

## The one debate that matters
**Is broadband a slow-declining annuity or a falling knife?** Q4 2025 lost a *record* −181k domestic broadband subs (FWA + fiber overbuild), and the losses are *widening*. But broadband **revenue** still grows on ARPU + 9.3M-and-rising Xfinity Mobile lines + business services, so connectivity revenue is flat-to-up. Peacock losses are narrowing toward breakeven (full-year EBITDA losses improved >$700M), Epic Universe is ramping, and the Versant cable-network spinoff (late 2025) leaves a cleaner story.

## Asymmetry
- **Bear (35%):** broadband loss >2%/yr, connectivity revenue turns negative → ~$19, **−6%** incl. div (the ~14% capital return cushions the downside — not a wipeout).
- **Base (45%):** units −1-1.5%/yr offset by ARPU/mobile, ~5%/yr buybacks, mild re-rate to 8x → ~$31, **+45%**.
- **Bull (20%):** broadband stabilizes, mobile scales, Peacock breakeven, re-rate to 9.5x → ~$41, **+87%**.
- **EV ≈ +34%.** Analyst mean target $32.40 (+37%, "hold") corroborates the base.

## Action
Attractive scale-in entry at **$23-24** (below book). Not all-in — secular broadband overhang is live. **Thesis-break:** broadband losses sustained >2%/yr AND connectivity revenue negative two straight quarters, a dividend cut, or leverage >3x EBITDA.

## Caveat / what's NOT done
- Kelly size not computed (no live model pull this session) — recommend sizing **below** a clean-bargain name given value-trap risk.
- FY2025 GAAP EPS ($5.39) is inflated by the ~$8.6B Disney/Hulu payment and pre-Versant earnings; valuation here is anchored on **normalized/forward EPS ~$3.78**, which is the honest number.
