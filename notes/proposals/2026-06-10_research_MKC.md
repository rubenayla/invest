## Why it's worth a look now

McCormick — the dominant global spice/seasoning franchise (McCormick, French's, Frank's RedHot, Cholula, Old Bay, Zatarain's) — is **−34% over 12 months** and **−47% from its 2020 peak**, sitting just above its **52-week ($44.82) and 5-year ($47.37) lows**. On normalized FY2025 EPS of $2.93 it trades at **~15–16x forward, its cheapest in ~15 years**, against a 5-year-average multiple north of 24x. You're paid **~4.0%** to wait via a **Dividend Aristocrat** (~39 straight raises, ~65% payout), and beta is **0.64**.

Crucially, this is a *valuation reset, not a broken business*: revenue grew FY23→FY25 (+2.7% organic) while operating income rose +19% as inflation-era pricing stuck and input costs eased. That distinguishes it from the typical beaten-down food name (e.g. CPB's levered, organically shrinking soup business).

## Bear case (why it's not higher conviction)

Low-growth name, so EV leans on multiple re-rating that the trusted GBM models only partly credit. Live risks: tariff exposure on imported inputs (pepper, vanilla) compressing gross margin, volume stagnation as consumers trade down to private-label spices, and ~3.5x net leverage limiting buybacks. GLP-1/bond-proxy de-rating did part of the damage.

## Action

- **Entry zone:** $45–49; ideal adds below ~$46 (yield >4.2%, near 5-yr support).
- **Thesis-break:** dividend freeze/cut, gross margin sustainably <35%, or 2+ quarters of organic volume decline >3%.
- **Sizing:** defensive starter; Kelly suggests up to the ~15% cap but conviction is MEDIUM-LOW — size smaller and average down toward the lows.

Not an act-tonight distressed bargain, but a better risk/reward than most income-staple "value traps": a wide-moat compounder at a 15-year-low valuation with a covered 4% yield and low beta. Worth the owner's review against the watchlist.
