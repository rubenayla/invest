# Research Proposal: Stellantis (STLA) — speculative deep-value turnaround starter

**Date:** 2026-06-11 | **Price:** $6.57 (NYSE ADR, near 52-wk low $6.28) | **Verdict:** WATCH — lean speculative BUY LOW, conviction LOW
**Scenario EV:** ~+43% / 3yr (~13%/yr) | Full note: `notes/companies/STLA.md`

## The one-line case
Max-pessimism deep value: ~$19B market cap for a global automaker that did **$155B revenue and $18.6B net income as recently as 2023**, trading at **0.30x book / 0.12x sales** near a 52-week low — with the bad news now *printed* (a −$22.4B 2025 loss, dividend cut to zero) and **Q1 2026 confirming a real operational inflection** (return to profit, +6% revenue, +12% shipments all regions, guidance reiterated).

## Why look now (the catalyst)
2025 was a kitchen-sink year (CEO ouster, EV/transition write-downs, US dealer-inventory glut, tariff hit). The new CEO (Filosa) reset expectations low, and Q1 2026 beat the trough: **AOI €1.0B at 2.5% margin (+160bps YoY), shipments +12%, NA orders +~150% YoY** as inventory normalized, industrial FCF −€1.9B but +37% better YoY. The stock has not re-rated — it sits at the low while the operating data turns up.

## What kills it (bear case first — 40% weight)
- Balance sheet is **deteriorating, not fortifying**: equity −35% in one year ($82B→$53.5B), net cash flipped to ~$16B net debt, operating cash flow gone negative, inventory rising into falling revenue. "0.3x book" is cheap only if book stops melting.
- Recovered margins are **razor-thin (2.5%)** — one recession from another loss. Guidance is only "low-single-digit %" for 2026.
- Secular overhang unresolved: **no China**, EV transition still costly, **US tariffs** structural on Mexico/Canada-built vehicles, brand erosion (Jeep/Ram pricing, Maserati).
- **No dividend to pay you to wait** in 2026.

## Recommendation
**Small, option-like speculative starter** for deep-value/contrarian exposure — not a core position. Entry $5.50–6.75 (scale-in). **Thesis-break:** a second year of negative AOI, full-year industrial FCF worse than 2025, equity below ~$40B, or a fresh multi-billion impairment → exit (melting ice cube). **Upgrade to BUY** only on two consecutive quarters of AOI ≥4% with *positive* industrial FCF and stabilizing book value.

*Not on the watchlist currently. Quantitative models (GBM/DCF/multiples) unreliable here — negative trailing earnings/FCF — so the call rests on the normalized-earnings scenario table above, not a model output. Closer should weigh against existing beaten-down candidates; STLA is differentiated (auto, European deep value) but lower quality than the staples names.*
