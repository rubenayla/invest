## One-line thesis
Global top-3 potash + phosphate producer trading **below book (0.61x, $23.30 vs ~$38 BVPS)** and ~6.5x EV/EBITDA near its 52-week low — a genuine deep-cyclical trough on irreplaceable low-cost Saskatchewan potash reserves — but **actively burning cash** (Q1'26 net loss -$0.81/sh, 2025 FCF -$0.53B), so it's a tiny scale-in at the bottom, not a back-up-the-truck buy.

## Why it might clear the bar now
- **Asset-value floor:** 0.61x book for irreplaceable potash reserves + Brazil's #1 fertilizer distributor. Downside cushioned by replacement cost and the $20.89 52-week low.
- **Fresh, datable catalyst:** Q1'26 (2026-05-11) curtailments + a transitory **sulfur/ammonia (Persian Gulf conflict) input spike** that mechanically reverses on de-escalation. Potash is already on a **record Canpotex pace** with a falling cost curve (Esterhazy hydrofloat ramp).
- **Mid-cycle FCF power is real:** +$2.7B (2022), +$1.0B (2023) → 8–20% normalized FCF yield on $13B EV.

## Why it's only a starter / why I tried to kill it
- **Active cash bleed:** 2025 FCF -$0.53B, operating CF falling, **net debt rising** ($4.45B→$5.28B), dividend debt-funded, **buybacks paused** to zero.
- **Price-taker on volatile commodities;** phosphate is a spread business exposed to two inputs Mosaic doesn't control.
- **Open-ended geopolitical input shock** (no clear end date) and **BHP Jansen** new low-cost potash supply from 2027 caps the upside.
- **Dividend-cut risk** is live if the squeeze persists.
