## Why look now

Merck trades at a **decade-low ~13x normalized earnings** with a safe ~3% dividend. The de-rating is about the **Keytruda US patent cliff in late 2028** (~46% of revenue) — a real but, in our read, over-extrapolated risk.

**The data-backed reframe:** screens show MRK at ~23x because 2026 non-GAAP EPS guidance ($5.04–5.16) **includes a one-time $3.62/share Cidara acquisition charge** that Merck leaves inside its non-GAAP figure. Normalize it out and 2026 earnings power is **~$8.7**, essentially flat vs 2025's $8.98 → the true multiple is ~13x. Many quote feeds and casual screens miss this and make the stock look twice as expensive as it is.

## The setup

- **Healthy today:** Q1'26 beat ($16.3B rev); FY26 guide $65.8–67B (+1–3%). Keytruda still +12% to a ~$32B run-rate.
- **Launches inflecting into the cliff:** Winrevair +88% ($525M, Street peak $5–7B), Welireg +43%, Capvaxive +31%, plus Enflonsia, enlicitide (oral PCSK9, Ph3), I-DXd, Animal Health.
- **Cliff defense:** subcutaneous Keytruda QLEX targets 30–40% IV conversion *before* LOE; method-of-use/manufacturing patents extend into 2029.
- **Dense 2026 catalysts:** Welireg RCC (Jun 19 & Oct 4), Keytruda+Padcev bladder (Aug 17), I-DXd SCLC (Oct 10), Winrevair label (Sep 21).
- **Street:** median target ~$130; 18 Buy / 11 Hold / 0 Sell of 39.
