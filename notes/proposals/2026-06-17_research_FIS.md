Full note: `notes/companies/FIS.md`

## Why this clears the "look now" bar
FIS trades at its **absolute 2-year low ($39.50, –55% from the $86 high)** on **~6.3x forward adjusted EPS, ~10% FCF yield, ~6.7x EV/EBITDA, and a covered 4.3% dividend** — trough multiples normally reserved for melting businesses. But this one is **growing**: revenue $9.7B→$10.7B over FY22-25 (~5%/yr) and FY2026 guided to **~5% pro-forma organic growth**. On 2026-01-09 FIS closed a clean asset swap with Global Payments — handed over its remaining Worldpay stake, acquired GPN's **Issuer Solutions** business — finally closing the value-destroying Worldpay chapter. **Q1 2026 beat (adj EPS $1.36, +12%, ~6% above consensus; FCF more than doubled) and full-year guide reaffirmed,** yet the stock made new lows. That dislocation is the opportunity.

## The bet (and the catalyst)
At 6.3x earnings you don't need a re-rating: ~10% FCF yield + 4.3% dividend + ~5% organic growth is a low-teens return on its own. The explicit catalyst is **self-funding deleveraging** — $2.1B FCF takes net leverage from **3.6x → 2.8x in ~18-24 months**, at which point **paused buybacks resume**. Repurchases at 6x earnings are violently accretive; that's the obvious re-rating trigger. Mean analyst target $58 (+47%); even a partial re-rate to a still-cheap 8.5x is ~+35% on flat EPS.

## Why it's BUY LOW, not BUY HIGH (the honest bear case)
Management has **earned its discount**: the $43B Worldpay deal (2019) led to ~$23B of goodwill write-offs, then a sell-at-a-loss, then a re-lever to 3.6x for a *mature* business GPN was happy to sell. The equity is now **geared** (3.6x leverage onto a 6.7x EV/EBITDA enterprise), **buybacks are frozen** for 1-2 years, and Issuer Solutions carries large-bank client-concentration risk. The whole payments complex (PYPL, GPN, FI) is derating on stablecoin/real-time-payments fears, and FIS has been a value trap for four years. This is a **starter/BUY LOW**, not a max-conviction position.
