# Proposal: Global Payments (GPN) — deep-value merchant-acquirer at a 5-year low, post-Worldpay

**Date:** 2026-06-23 | **Price:** $64.28 | **Standalone rating: BUY LOW** | **Scenario EV: ~+31% (1y)**
Full note: `notes/companies/GPN.md`

## Why look now
GPN sits at its **literal 5-year low ($64.28)** — down ~65% from the 2021 peak — six months after closing the transformative **Worldpay acquisition / Issuer Solutions divestiture** (the FIS asset swap, 2026-01-12). It is now a pure-play merchant/commerce-solutions company trading at:
- **~4.6x forward adjusted EPS** ($13.80–$14.00 guided) / **~12.5x GAAP**
- **0.74x book** ($87/sh book value)
- **~11.6% FCF yield** (~$2.0B FCF on $17.6B cap), funding a **$7.5B buyback** retiring stock at <5x earnings
- Analyst mean target **$93 (+45%)**, 26 analysts, "buy"

The stock popped +16% on the February print, then round-tripped the whole move back to new lows — sentiment, not fundamentals, set the price.

## The kill case (why it's this cheap)
1. **Worldpay is a serial orphan** — failed to grow under Vantiv, FIS, and GTCR; integrating two overlapping acquiring books onto one platform is exactly where acquirers stumble.
2. **~4.5x net leverage** geared onto a sector the market prices for terminal disruption (Stripe/Adyen/Block, A2A, stablecoins).
3. **Adjusted-vs-GAAP gap is huge and recurring** — "$14 EPS" excludes ~$2.5B amortization; on GAAP it's ~$5, so the real multiple is ~12.5x, not 4.6x.
4. Repeated new lows = knife-catch risk; legacy organic growth had already decelerated.

## The asymmetry
Even on **GAAP ~12.5x** with an ~11.6% FCF yield below book, the cash flows look durable enough that mid-single-digit organic growth + a buyback at <5x earnings is a high-teens IRR before any re-rating. Scenario table:

| Scenario | Prob | Target | Return |
|----------|------|--------|--------|
| Bear (integration stumbles, de-rate) | 35% | ~$52 | −19% |
| Base (synergies land, modest re-rate) | 45% | ~$90 | +40% |
| Bull (13–15% growth proven) | 20% | ~$126 | +96% |

**EV ≈ +31%**, fat left tail → starter-size, not full.

## Suggested action
A **modest BUY LOW starter** here, with room to add **below $60**. Thesis-break: 2026 adj-EPS guide cut below ~$13, two quarters of shrinking organic merchant volume, or any leverage-driven dividend cut. Upgrade trigger: two clean integration quarters with positive Worldpay organic volume + deleveraging toward ~3.5x.

**Pair context:** FIS (already noted BUY LOW, ~6.3x) is the lower-leverage, lower-beta way to play the same deal; GPN is the higher-risk/higher-reward merchant side. If buying the payments-incumbent mispricing, decide which risk profile fits — they are the two halves of one transaction.
