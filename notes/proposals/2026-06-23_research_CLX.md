# Proposal: CLX (Clorox) — quality Aristocrat at a multi-year low, watch for sub-$85

**Date:** 2026-06-23
**Price:** $92.64
**Rating:** WATCH (lean BUY LOW)
**Expected value:** ≈ **+23% over ~2.5 years** (~+9%/yr), probability-weighted, plus a 5.35%
dividend yield and 0.55 beta.

## One-paragraph thesis
Clorox has fallen from ~$170 (2024) to $92.64, sitting at ~17% of its 52-week range, after a
Q3-FY26 guidance cut (adjusted EPS to $5.45–5.65 from $5.95–6.30; organic sales −9%). The cut
is driven largely by a **one-time ~$0.90 EPS drag** from unwinding ERP-transition inventory,
plus GOJO/Purell acquisition dilution and input inflation — headwinds that reverse or fade in
FY27. Strip them out and normalized adjusted earnings power is ~$6.50, putting the stock at
~14x with a 5.35% yield on a Dividend-Aristocrat portfolio of category-leading brands. The
catch: zero organic growth, negative book equity from buybacks + goodwill, ~$3.3B net debt,
and a dividend at ~102% of FY26 GAAP EPS — covered by normalized FCF but with no cushion this
year.

## Why it's only a WATCH at $92.64 (not a buy-now)
The probability-weighted upside (+23%/2.5yr) is decent but not a fat-pitch, and the bear case
(structural no-growth + over-levered, tight payout = yield trap) is live until FY27 guidance
confirms the air-pocket is transitory. The risk/reward tilts clearly favorable **sub-$85**
(near the $84.70 low, ~6% yield, ~13x normalized).

## Action for the owner
- **Add to watchlist; set an alert at ~$85.** That's the level where I'd want to buy a starter.
- **Key catalyst: Q4-FY26 results / first FY27 guidance (~Aug 2026)** — the clean read on
  whether the ERP unwind is done and gross margin (43.8% → toward 45%) is inflecting.
- **Thesis-break:** a dividend cut, or organic sales still falling into FY28 (a break below
  ~$70 likely coincides with the market pricing a cut).
