# Research Proposal: PepsiCo (PEP) — BUY (starter), grade B

**Date:** 2026-06-04 | **Price:** $142.54 | **Call:** BUY (starter) | **EV:** ~+18% (2y, prob-weighted) + 4% yield

## One-liner
Dividend King (53 straight raises) at a near-decade-low ~15.6x forward / ~17x normalized P/E, ~17% off its high and flat for 5 years, with a credible Elliott-driven self-help catalyst and a 4.0% yield that pays you to wait on a Frito-Lay volume/margin inflection.

## Why look now
- **De-rating to a decade low.** ~15.6x fwd / ~17x normalized core EPS (~$8.3) vs a ~22–24x decade average; EV/EBITDA ~12.7x; 4.0% yield (vs ~2.8% norm). Trailing GAAP P/E of 22x is an impairment artifact — ignore it.
- **Concrete activist catalyst.** Elliott's ~$4B stake (Sept 2025) → 8 Dec 2025 agreement: cut U.S. SKUs ~20%, reduce core-brand prices (affordability reset), close/automate Frito-Lay plants, full N.A. supply-chain review with quantified results due late 2026. Monitorable, not vague.
- **Inflection guidance + a beat.** Management guided Frito-Lay to grow volume, revenue AND margin in 2026 (calling the volume bottom). Q1 2026 (15 Apr): adj EPS $1.61 vs $1.55, revenue +8.5% to $19.44B.
- **Moat intact.** Frito-Lay ~80% U.S. salty-snack share, DSD distribution moat, ROE ~44%. The problem is self-inflicted over-pricing, which is fixable and being fixed.

## Asymmetry
- Bull (~25%): re-rate to 20x on ~$9.2 EPS → ~$184, **+37%**
- Base (~50%): 18–19x on ~$8.8 → ~$160, **+20%**
- Bear (~25%): GLP-1 + trade-down persist, 15x on ~$8.1 → ~$122, **-7%** (dividend cushions)
- Prob-weighted ≈ +17–18%; downside cushioned by the 4% yield and dividend-yield floor ~$132.

## Why only B (not A)
The growth algorithm is genuinely clouded by GLP-1 (hits salty snacks + sugary drinks, PEP's two core categories) and the stock has been dead money for 5 years for real reasons. The re-rate requires the turnaround to actually show up in volumes — a recovering-compounder bet, not a screaming mispricing. Starter size; add on confirmation.

## Entry / thesis-break
- **Entry:** ~$142 starter; add below ~$135 (toward 52w low / 4.3% yield floor).
- **Thesis-break:** Frito-Lay N.A. volume still negative through 2H-2026, OR core EPS declines in FY2026, OR dividend growth stalls. Below ~$120 *with* deteriorating fundamentals = broken, not buy-more.
