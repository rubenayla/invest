## Why this clears the "look now" bar

Nike — the #1 athletic brand globally, a dividend aristocrat (23+ years of increases) — trades at **$43.81, sitting right on its 5-year and 52-week low**, down ~73% from its $164 peak. This is a wide-moat franchise priced as if it's structurally broken, when the evidence says its earnings are *cyclically depressed by a self-inflicted, fixable clean-up.*

**The decline was strategy, not brand erosion.** The prior CEO over-rotated to DTC, abandoned wholesale partners (ceding shelf to On/Hoka/Adidas), and over-supplied classic franchises (Dunk/AF1) into the channel, killing their scarcity. Revenue fell $51.4B → $46.3B, operating income halved. A 32-year Nike insider, **Elliott Hill, returned as CEO in Oct 2024** and is reversing exactly those mistakes.

**The bottom is forming — first hard evidence.** Q3 FY2026 (reported 2026-03-31): revenue **flat YoY** (vs. prior declines), **EPS $0.35 beat $0.28 by 25%**, and **wholesale grew +5%** — direct proof the core fix is working. Gross margin fell only 130bps (decelerating). The offsetting overhang: Q4 guided down 2–4% and China expected ~−20%.

## Asymmetric risk/reward

Anchoring on normalized earnings power ($4.5–5.5B net income mid-cycle vs. ~$2.3B TTM depressed):

| Scenario | Fair Value | vs $43.81 |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Bull (turnaround + China recovery) | ~$110 | +150% |
| Base (gradual normalization by FY28) | ~$82 | +87% |
| Bear (structural share loss persists) | ~$40 | −9% |

Prob-weighted (35/45/20) ≈ **$82, +87%** over 2–3 years. The bear case lands at *today's price* — trough multiple on trough earnings — while base/bull are ~1.9–2.5x. Plus a **3.75% FCF-covered dividend** pays you to wait.

## Action

- **Entry:** ~$44, **scale in** (China overhang + weak Q4 guide argue against a full position now).
- **Thesis-break:** ~$34 on price; fundamentally, **gross margin < 38%** (lost pricing power), wholesale growth stalling, or revenue resuming >5% declines after the FY27 "clean base."
- **Changes my mind (bearish):** share loss in *running specifically*, a dividend cut, or China structurally below −20%.

## Caveats

- This is a *slow* turnaround — patience required; could be dead money for 2–4 quarters while China resets.
- It's a taste-driven business; the moat is wide but not impregnable. The key monitorable is running-category share vs On/Hoka.
