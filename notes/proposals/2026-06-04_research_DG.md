# Proposal: Dollar General (DG) — BUY (starter / scale-in)

**Full note:** `notes/companies/DG.md`

## Why look now (the mispricing)

DG reported **Q1 FY2026 on June 2 (two days ago): EPS $2.00 beat $1.89, and management RAISED FY2026 EPS guidance to $7.20–7.45** (from $7.10–7.35), reaffirming +3.7–4.2% sales and +2.2–2.7% comps. The stock **fell ~5% anyway** and sits at **$105 — 7% off its 52-week low ($95) and 34% below the 52-week high ($158)**.

This is a defensive (beta 0.28), 18.9%-ROE discount retailer trading at **~14x just-raised earnings** versus an **18–22x historical multiple**, with a **2.2% FCF-covered yield** (33% payout) paying you to wait. The de-rating was driven by analyst *skepticism about future margin recovery* (Guggenheim/Barclays target cuts) — i.e. opinion, not deteriorating results. Estimates are rising while the multiple compressed. The turnaround rally round-tripped ($98→$155→$105) even as net income recovered from a $1.13B trough back to ~$1.5B.

## Plan

- **Entry:** ~$105 starter; add toward **$95–98** or on a 2nd quarter of positive comps + flat/up gross margin.
- **Thesis-break:** **$88** price, or fundamentally — negative same-store sales, a guidance cut, or two quarters of YoY gross-margin contraction (margin-recovery engine stalled → value trap confirmed).
- **Changes my mind (bearish):** tariff escalation compressing GM with no trade-down offset; the low-end consumer cracking on both traffic and ticket; the 6–7% EBIT target being walked back.

## Risks / caveats

- The whole bull case rests on **margin recovery to 6–7% EBIT** from 5.9% — execution-dependent and the exact thing the Street doubts.
- Tariffs were a non-event in Q1 but remain a live tail risk to gross margin (imported general merchandise).
- Core customer is the most macro-fragile cohort (gas prices, SNAP cuts) — though trade-down partly offsets.
- Conviction is **MEDIUM**, not high: it's a defensive value setup with a fresh catalyst, not a deep-value steal. Size as a starter.
