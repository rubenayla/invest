# Proposal: Amdocs (DOX) — first look, BUY LOW at $55.90 (52-week low)

**Date:** 2026-06-16 | **Price:** $55.90 | **Standalone rating:** BUY LOW | **Scenario EV:** ~+24% / 2y (~+11%/yr) + 4% dividend
**Full note:** `notes/companies/DOX.md`

## Why this clears the "owner should look now" bar
A high-quality, near-debt-free cash machine is trading at a **distressed multiple for a non-distressed reason**. Amdocs runs the mission-critical billing/BSS software for the world's biggest telcos (AT&T, T-Mobile, Vodafone). It is at its exact 52-week low ($55.27), down ~37% in a year — but revenue and EPS are at all-time highs and quarterly revenue is *rising sequentially* ($1.128B → $1.172B over the last five quarters). The de-rating is sector-wide AI-disruption fear compressing the multiple from ~13-15x to ~7x forward, not a fundamentals break.

The numbers at $55.90:
- **~7x forward earnings**, ~11x trailing GAAP.
- **~10% FCF yield** ($590M FCF / $5.93B cap).
- **~11% combined shareholder yield** = 4.1% dividend (43% payout, well covered) + ~7% buyback (share count ground to ~106M).
- **Fortress balance sheet**: net debt ~$0.86B, ~1x EBITDA. No refinancing/solvency risk — rare among beaten-down names.
- ROE ~16%, margin ~12%, FY26 guide: revenue +1.5-5.5%, GAAP EPS +10-17%.

## The real bear case (why it's BUY LOW, not higher)
The secular question is legitimate: roughly half the revenue is labor-arbitrage managed services, and if GenAI truly compresses telco-IT spend and headcount, both Amdocs's volume and pricing erode. Plus AT&T concentration (~25-30%) and a CEO transition. This is *why* the multiple fell — it's not irrational. The counter: ripping out an embedded billing stack takes years and rarely happens, which is exactly why revenue grew *through* the AI scare. The moat tell is holding, for now.

~75% of probability mass is flat-to-up; downside is cushioned by the 10% FCF yield, covered dividend, and clean balance sheet. **EV ≈ +24% / 2y.**

## Action
- **Entry:** attractive at current $55-56; add aggressively below $50.
- **Thesis-break:** organic (ex-divestiture/FX) revenue actually declining YoY, a confirmed AT&T scope cut, or services gross-margin compressing toward AI-disruption levels → exit, don't average down.
- **Upgrade trigger (→ BUY MEDIUM):** two consecutive quarters of accelerating organic growth + concrete aOS revenue disclosure.
