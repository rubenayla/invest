# Research Proposal — Brown-Forman (BF-B)

**Date:** 2026-06-16 | **Price:** $26.94 | **Standalone rating: WATCH (leaning BUY LOW)**
**Scenario EV: ~+27% total over 2.5y** (~+19% price + ~8% dividends) | Full note: `notes/companies/BF-B.md`

## Why this is on the radar now

A wide-moat **Dividend Aristocrat** (Jack Daniel's, ~half of sales; 29% operating margin) trading at its **cheapest multiple in ~15 years** — 17x trailing / 16x forward vs a historical 25–35x — down ~60% from its 2022 peak (~$68 → $27), near its 52-week low ($22.61). This sits in the same premium-alcohol cluster the owner is already working (DEO, STZ both covered), so it belongs in the comparison set.

## The case in three lines

- **Quality is intact:** margins held at ~29% and FCF at ~$0.89B (7% yield) *through* a three-year revenue decline — pricing power proven, not eroded. Aged-whiskey inventory ($2.5B) is a real moat.
- **The drawdown is mostly cyclical:** post-pandemic distributor destocking + tariff fear, not (yet) evidence of secular collapse. Growth drivers still working: premium whiskey, RTD, emerging markets.
- **You're paid 3.4% to wait** on a 40-year dividend grower with a 60% payout.

## The honest catch (why it's WATCH, not BUY HIGH)

Management itself guides FY2026 to **another low-single-digit decline** — it does not yet see the inflection. At $27 you're buying the trough thesis at a *fair*, not *bargain*, price. The bear case (GLP-1 / younger-cohort drinking less = secular, plus a prolonged EU/US whiskey tariff war) is live and would make 17x too high for a shrinking franchise. Family Class-A control means no activist lever.

## Action

- **Watch, don't chase here.** Defined entry of interest: **~$23–24** (near 52-wk low, ~15x fwd, ~4% yield) — where the bear case is largely priced and the dividend cushions the wait.
- **Buy trigger:** two consecutive quarters of stabilizing/growing organic net sales (destocking over) with margins held at ~29%.
- **Avoid trigger:** organic decline *accelerating* for two more quarters, sustained Jack Daniel's US volume decline, or a prolonged EU/US whiskey tariff war.
