## Why look now

Uber fell ~30% from its $101 high to a 52-week low of $68.46 (now low $70s) **entirely on the autonomous-vehicle disruption narrative** — Tesla starting Cybercab production (April 2026) and Waymo raising $16B — while the business *accelerated*. Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-06): gross bookings **+25% to $53.7B**, Q2 guide ahead of consensus, non-GAAP EPS **+44%**, AV trips on the platform **+10x YoY**, Waymo already running **250k paid rides/week through Uber**, Uber One at 50M members. Price and fundamentals are diverging — the setup the research protocol is built to catch.

## The variant perception

Consensus: AVs disintermediate Uber's driver model and strip its best mobility trips.

Variant: **AVs are supply, and the scarce asset in robotaxi economics is demand aggregation + utilization, not the car.** An idle robotaxi loses money; Uber's 170M+ users and dispatch engine fill the seats — which is exactly why Waymo distributes through Uber and 30+ AV makers have signed on. Each aggregated AV trip *removes Uber's largest cost* (driver pay) while keeping the take. Meanwhile **~half of bookings is Delivery (AV-immune)**, advertising is a fast-growing 50%+ margin layer, and Uber is buying back **$20B (~14% of market cap)** into the fear.
