## Why look now

The world's #1 premium-spirits company (Johnnie Walker, Guinness, Don Julio, Tanqueray, Smirnoff, Captain Morgan) trades at **~12x forward earnings — a decade low — and ~60% below its January-2022 peak (~$196 → ~$79)**, sitting near its 52-week low. The reason it's interesting *today* rather than six months ago: the entire trifecta of bad news is now **announced and absorbed**, not still ahead of the stock:

1. **Dividend rebased** (~50% cut, Feb 2026) to a 30–50% payout policy — the income-holder selling is largely done, and FCF now redirects to deleveraging.
2. **Guidance reset low** (FY26 organic sales −2 to −3%) — and **Q3 already beat back to modest organic growth**, with Europe/LAC/Africa offsetting soft US/Asia.
3. **CEO change resolved** — Debra Crew out; **Sir Dave Lewis** (the operator who turned around Tesco) is in, with a **full Strategy Update on Aug 6, 2026** as a hard, datable catalyst ~2 months out.

The moat is untouched: ~60% gross margin, ~31% operating margin, and an aged-liquid inventory bank (12-year Scotch, añejo tequila) that literally cannot be replicated on demand. Revenue has held flat at ~$20B through the entire "collapse" — this is a margin/sentiment de-rating, not demand evaporation.

## The bear case (why it's only MEDIUM conviction, not a table-pounder)

- **GLP-1 + moderation + Gen-Z sobriety** could be a *structural* volume ceiling in Diageo's richest markets (US ~39%, Europe ~24%), not just a cyclical scare. If so, ~12x is fair, not cheap.
- **US tariffs** hit Diageo's cost base directly — most US revenue is imported (Scotch, tequila, Canadian whisky) with limited near-term mitigation.
- **Net debt $21.7B (~3.5x EBITDA)** — investment-grade but elevated; the dividend cut is partly a balance-sheet necessity, and the income thesis is now weaker (rebased ~3.4% yield, not the old ~4%+ progressive).
- This is a **multi-year turnaround, not a quarter**. No quick bounce.
