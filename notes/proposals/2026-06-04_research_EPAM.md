## Why this clears the "look now" bar

EPAM is at a **5-year low (~$97 vs a $673 2021 peak)**, down ~43% over the trailing year while the S&P is up ~28%. It trades at **~7.4x FY26 non-GAAP EPS guide ($12.98–$13.28) / ~11x GAAP / ~1.4x book**, is **debt-free with ~$0.75B net cash**, and is **buying back stock** (share count −5% in 3y). The market has lumped it in with the whole AI-disrupted IT-services group and priced terminal decline.

The numbers don't confirm terminal decline — they confirm a *slowdown*:
- **Q1 2026** (reported May): revenue $1.40B, **+7.6% YoY, organic constant-currency +3.7%**, at the high end of guidance.
- **AI-native revenue >$125M/qtr, +~20% sequential**, 5 straight double-digit-growth quarters, ~$600M FY26 target, plus an **Anthropic partnership**. EPAM is *monetizing* AI, not just being eaten by it.
- Management explicitly reports **no AI pricing pressure** — the drag is enterprises *deferring* IT decisions amid macro uncertainty (energy prices, geopolitics) and a ~1pp drag from the Neoris acquisition's largest client ramping down. That's cyclical, not structural — if they're right.

## The honest caveat

Services genuinely sell *hours*, and AI compresses hours — this is a more real structural risk than the software-SaaS version of the same fear (cf. the TEAM thesis, where the moat is a proprietary data corpus). So this is a **starter/scale-in sized for value-trap risk**, not a high-conviction back-up-the-truck name. The thesis-break is monitorable: two quarters of negative organic constant-currency growth combined with non-GAAP operating margin below ~13% confirms the bear case, and you exit.

**Action for owner:** Consider a starter position at ≤$100 with a second tranche reserved for $80–85. Watch next quarterly print for (a) organic cc growth staying positive, (b) AI-native revenue tracking to $600M, (c) any management mention of pricing pressure.
