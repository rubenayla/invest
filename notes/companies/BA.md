# The Boeing Company (BA)

**Sector:** Industrials | **Industry:** Aerospace & Defense
**Price:** $218.88 (2026-04-17) | **Market Cap:** $172B | **EV:** $200B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-17

## Situation Summary

Boeing is in the early innings of a multi-year industrial turnaround under CEO Kelly Ortberg (appointed Aug 2024). After the 737 MAX crashes (2018-19), the Alaska Airlines door-plug blowout (Jan 2024), a 7-week machinist strike (Sep-Nov 2024), and cumulative net losses exceeding $30B since 2019, the company delivered its first profitable year in 2025 — though net income of $2.2B was inflated by a $9.6B one-time gain from selling Digital Aviation Solutions. Operationally, Boeing delivered 600 commercial aircraft in 2025 (highest since 2018) and reached 38 737 MAX/month, with FAA approval to ramp to 42/month. The 777X is in Phase 4A certification testing with H2 2026 certification expected and 2027 entry into service. The China tariff crisis (145% US tariffs, 125% retaliatory) caused ~50 planes to be blocked from delivery, but a 90-day pause has temporarily lifted the ban. Q1 2026 earnings on April 22 will be the next major data point, with the street expecting $19.5B revenue and 130 deliveries.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Boeing is a recovering duopoly with a $682B backlog. Production is ramping, FCF turns positive in 2026 ($1-3B guided). Mean target $266 = +21%. Street rates it "Buy." The thesis is: once production normalizes at 47+/month on the 737 and 777X delivers, FCF normalizes to $10B+ and the stock re-rates toward $300+.
- **Our view:** The consensus is directionally right but underestimates two tail risks: (1) the defense segment is a $5B/year cash incinerator on fixed-price contracts that won't fully roll off until 2028-29 — this drags enterprise FCF far below what commercial alone would generate; (2) the $54B debt load at 5.9x net debt/EBITDA leaves zero margin for error — any production stumble (supplier issues, quality escapes, another strike) could force further dilutive capital raises. The $2.2B "profit" in 2025 is almost entirely a one-time asset sale; underlying operating income was still *negative* $5.4B. This is a hope trade priced for perfect execution. We do NOT have a differentiated variant perception — the market already knows the bull case and the risks. The stock at $219 is pricing in a successful ramp but not pricing in the magnitude of defense drag or the possibility of tariff-driven China order cancellations (not just delivery delays).
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (April 22) showing positive operating cash flow + 737 rate at/above 38/month + 777X production aircraft first flight (expected April 2026). If all three hit, provides evidence the ramp is real. If not, the "2026 is the year" narrative cracks.

## Inflection Point

**Partial evidence of inflection, but not yet confirmed.** The positive signals are real: 600 deliveries in 2025 (highest since 2018), FAA raised production cap from 38 to 42/month, 777X reached Phase 4A testing, Q4 2025 generated positive operating cash flow of $1.3B. Ortberg's "industrial reset" framing is culturally correct.

However, the inflection is NOT yet at the earnings/FCF level. Operating income is still deeply negative. 2025 FCF was still -$1.9B. The company guides only $1-3B FCF for 2026 — this would be the first positive FCF year since 2023 but is modest for a $172B market cap company. The inflection depends on: (1) 737 MAX sustaining 42+/month through 2026, (2) 777X achieving certification on schedule in H2 2026, (3) defense losses narrowing as fixed-price contracts roll off. All three must hit. This is observable-evidence-in-progress, not confirmed.

**Assessment:** Early-stage turnaround. The production evidence is encouraging but the financial evidence (positive operating income, sustainable FCF) has not yet arrived. We are still in the "hope" phase.

## Bull Case

- **Duopoly pricing power with massive backlog:** $682B backlog = locked-in revenue for 7+ years. Only Boeing and Airbus can build large commercial aircraft at scale. Airlines have no alternative.
- **Production ramp approaching normalized rates:** 42/month FAA cap with path to 47+/month by late 2026. Each incremental 737 MAX/month = ~$600M+ annual revenue.
- **777X certification in H2 2026:** Opens new widebody revenue stream — 540+ orders on the books. Entry into service in 2027 could be a major catalyst.
- **Defense losses peaking:** Fixed-price contract losses narrowed from $1.7B in Q4 2024 to $550M in Q4 2025. Should continue improving as legacy contracts roll off.
- **FCF normalization:** If production hits 50+/month on 737 and 777X delivers, Boeing could generate $8-12B FCF by 2028-29, justifying a $300+ stock price.

## Bear Case

- **Operating income is still negative:** The "profitable" 2025 was a mirage ($9.6B asset sale). Underlying operations lost $5.4B. There's no guarantee the production ramp translates to positive operating margins.
- **Balance sheet is a ticking time bomb:** $54B debt at Baa3 (one notch above junk). Any production stumble = potential downgrade to junk = massively higher borrowing costs = possible death spiral. $9B maturing through 2026.
- **China risk is existential for the bull case:** ~50 planes blocked by tariffs. Even with 90-day pause, permanent loss of China market (historically ~25% of Boeing's commercial deliveries) would severely impair the backlog and long-term economics. COMAC C919 is waiting in the wings.
- **777X could slip again:** Already 6 years late and $15B over budget. Phase 4A is not the final phase. Certification slipping to 2027 or later would be another credibility blow and continued cash drain.
- **Cultural rot may not be fixable:** Quality issues, the door-plug blowout, and repeated manufacturing defects suggest deep systemic problems. Ortberg has been CEO <2 years — whether the culture actually changes takes 5+ years to prove.

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

Boeing has a near-unassailable competitive moat (duopoly) and a record backlog, but the stock at $219 and 47x forward earnings is priced for a successful turnaround that has not yet materialized at the operating income or FCF level. The expected value of +0.6% offers no margin of safety. We have no differentiated variant perception — the bull case (production ramp + 777X + FCF normalization) is the consensus view. The balance sheet ($54B debt, Baa3 rating) provides zero cushion for execution misses, and the China tariff risk is a genuine existential threat to the long-term backlog. Observable production evidence (600 deliveries, FAA cap raises) is encouraging but not yet translating to financial results. This is still a hope trade.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** (1) Positive operating income for two consecutive quarters (proving the ramp translates to profits), (2) stock pullback to $175-185 range (creating ~15-20% expected value), (3) permanent resolution of China trade tensions, or (4) 777X certification achieved. Any of these would meaningfully change the risk/reward.
