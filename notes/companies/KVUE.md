# Kenvue Inc. (KVUE)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Consumer Health (Tylenol, Listerine, Neutrogena, Aveeno, Band-Aid, Zyrtec, Johnson's, Nicorette)
**Price:** $18.43 (2026-06-17) | **Market Cap:** ~$35.4B | **Revenue (TTM):** ~$15.3B | **Net margin:** ~10.6%
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-17
**Standalone rating: WATCH** (this is no longer a standalone equity — it is the *target* of the $48.7B Kimberly-Clark merger, so it trades as a thin merger-arb + a slightly-discounted, higher-break-risk way to own the future combined company. The arb spread does not compensate the deal-break tail, and a deal-bull is better served owning [[KMB]] directly.)

## Situation Summary
Kenvue is the J&J consumer-health spin-off (2023): an iconic over-the-counter and skin-health portfolio — **Tylenol, Listerine, Neutrogena, Aveeno, Band-Aid, Zyrtec, Johnson's baby, Nicorette**. At $18.43 it sits ~19% below its 52-week high ($22.87) and ~31% above its 52-week low ($14.02). The low was the **September 2025 Tylenol-autism panic** (the Trump administration / RFK Jr. publicly linked prenatal acetaminophen to autism; US Tylenol sales fell ~11% in two weeks).

Crucially, **KVUE is not a free-floating stock to value on its own merits anymore.** On **2025-11-03**, Kimberly-Clark agreed to acquire Kenvue for **~$48.7B** in cash-and-stock. Terms per Kenvue share: **$3.50 cash + 0.14625 KMB shares**. Both shareholder bases approved overwhelmingly (96%+) on 2026-01-29; the deal is in **FTC second-request review with closing targeted for H2 2026**. Post-close, former Kenvue holders own **46%** of the combined company.

So owning KVUE today = a bet that this deal closes, after which you convert into KMB stock + cash and ride the same combined-company thesis laid out in the [[KMB]] note.

## The Arb Math (the whole story)

| Input | Value |
|-------|-------|
| KVUE price | $18.43 |
| KMB price | $104.28 |
| Deal consideration | $3.50 cash + 0.14625 × KMB |
| **Implied deal value / KVUE share** | **$3.50 + 0.14625×104.28 = $18.75** |
| Gross spread to deal value | **$0.32 (~1.74%)** |
| Expected close | H2 2026 (~6-9 months) |
| Annualized gross spread | ~2.5–3.5% (unhedged of KMB moves) |

Two things follow:
1. **The spread is thin (~1.74%).** As a pure risk-arb it is unattractive: ~3% annualized for taking binary deal-break risk, and it is *unhedged* unless you short 0.14625 KMB per KVUE share — which this book does not do.
2. **Unhedged, KVUE ≈ a tracking stock for the combined company at a small discount.** You are really making the *same* call as the [[KMB]] note — is the merged tissue + consumer-health entity undervalued at ~11x? — but from the riskier seat.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Deal's basically done — 96% approved, FTC overlap is minimal, just clip the spread." True on direction, but the spread is too small to be the reason to own it, and the consensus underweights the litigation tail that could still wobble the close or the post-close multiple.
- **Why KVUE is the *worse* vehicle than KMB for a deal-bull:** If you believe the combined company re-rates (+35–45% per the [[KMB]] thesis), you capture that re-rate from *either* ticker. But KMB pays a higher yield (~5.0% vs KVUE ~4.55%), and — decisively — **KMB has a standalone downside floor (~$115–125 tissue/personal-care business) if the deal breaks, whereas a broken KVUE falls back to a litigation-hit standalone ~$14–16.** Same upside, worse downside. There is no compensating reason to prefer the target seat here.
- **Standalone (deal-break) case is only "fair," not cheap:** ~14.9x forward earnings and 4.55% yield for a portfolio with **organic declines in skin health/beauty (Neutrogena), management turnover, and the Tylenol overhang** is reasonable but not a bargain. The brands are genuinely world-class; the growth and the legal tail are not.

## Litigation & Regulatory Status (the deal-break drivers)
- **Tylenol-autism federal MDL (S.D.N.Y.):** Largely **dismissed in Kenvue's favor** — final judgment for the defendants in Feb 2024, remaining cases dismissed Aug 2024, all on appeal as of late 2024. This is the favorable, under-appreciated fact: the biggest single litigation front went Kenvue's way.
- **State AG suits:** Newer, smaller front. A Texas judge (Panola County) **rejected Kenvue's motion to dismiss** the AG Paxton consumer-protection suit in Feb 2026 (deceptive-marketing theory). Proceeding, but state consumer-protection cases are a far smaller exposure than the federal personal-injury MDL.
- **FTC:** Second-request review; tissue vs. consumer-health overlap is minimal, so clean clearance is the base case. The wildcard is the FTC's 2026 posture on consumer-staples concentration, not product overlap.
- **Net:** Deal-break probability is modest (~15–25%), and the scariest litigation headline (federal autism MDL) is already mostly resolved favorably.

**Probability-weighted EV ≈ +4–5%** (0.35×18 + 0.45×4 − 0.20×19 ≈ +4.3%, plus carry).

That is a positive but thin expected value, dominated by a fat left tail, for *the same upside you can get from [[KMB]] with a better floor.*

## Entry / Exit Levels
- **Entry (would get interesting):** below ~$16 — i.e. a meaningful discount to deal value that widens the spread to compensate the break-risk, or a litigation-headline overshoot that prices in a break that doesn't happen.
- **Thesis-break (deal-bull) price:** above ~$19.5–20 with no improvement in deal certainty — the arb is then fully closed and you are paying full freight for combined-co optionality you can get cheaper in KMB.
- **Hard stop / what would change my mind to BUY:** (1) FTC issues a clean close-timing signal *and* the spread is still >4–5% (free roll into the combined co), **or** (2) the deal breaks and KVUE overshoots to <$14 on a litigation panic that the already-dismissed federal MDL does not justify — then the standalone iconic-brand portfolio at <11x becomes a genuine value, litigation tail and all.

## Bottom Line
KVUE is a special situation, not an independent equity: a ~1.74% merger-arb spread wrapped around the same combined-company bet as [[KMB]], but from a seat with a worse downside floor and a lower yield. The spread is too thin to justify the deal-break tail on its own, and a deal-bull should simply own KMB. **WATCH** — only actionable on a wider spread (>4–5%) or a litigation-driven overshoot below ~$14–16.
