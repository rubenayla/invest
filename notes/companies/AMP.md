# Ameriprise Financial (AMP)

**Sector:** Financial Services | **Industry:** Asset Management / Wealth Management
**Price:** $434.06 | **Market Cap:** $40.3B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

Ameriprise Financial is a diversified financial services company operating in wealth management (Advice & Wealth Management), asset management (Columbia Threadneedle, $678B AUM), and retirement/protection services. The stock is trading at $434, down ~21% from its 52-week high of $550, largely driven by the broader market selloff tied to tariff uncertainty and some company-specific noise (insider sales, $50M SEC messaging fine, retirement of a key AWM leader). Fundamentally, the business just posted record results: Q4 2025 adjusted operating EPS up 16% YoY to $10.83, total AUM/AUA reached a record $1.7T (+11%), and the company returned $3.4B to shareholders (88% of operating earnings). The Huntington deal ($28B in assets, 260 advisors) is the largest institutional win in company history and signals continued organic growth in the wealth channel. The stock trades at 9.3x forward earnings and a 20% FCF yield — deeply discounted for a business of this quality.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Wall Street has a Buy consensus (10 analysts, mean target ~$560) but sees AMP as a solid-but-boring compounder with limited upside beyond the sector. The stock is perceived as sensitive to equity market levels (fee-based revenue), and the recent pullback is being attributed to macro risk (tariffs, recession fears) rather than fundamentals. Some concern about the SEC fine and insider sales creating an overhang.
- **Our view:** The market is anchoring too heavily on macro noise and missing the structural earnings power of this business. At 9.3x forward PE with 54% ROE, 20% FCF yield, net cash position, and management returning 85-90% of earnings to shareholders, AMP is priced as if earnings are about to collapse. Instead: (1) the wealth management segment has delivered 15% pretax earnings CAGR with margins expanding from 17% to 29% over the last decade — that flywheel is accelerating, not decelerating; (2) the Huntington deal adds $28B in assets and proves the institutional channel is a real growth vector; (3) the $4.5B buyback authorization through June 2027 at these depressed prices will be enormously accretive — they're buying back ~10%+ of market cap per year at these levels.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (late April) should show resilient AUA/AUM despite market volatility, Huntington onboarding progress, and continued aggressive buybacks at depressed prices. Any stabilization in equity markets removes the macro overhang entirely.

## Inflection Point

This is not a classic turnaround — AMP is a high-quality compounder that has been sold off with the broader market. The inflection is **valuation-driven**: the stock has gone from fairly valued (~14x PE) to deeply discounted (~9x PE) without a corresponding deterioration in fundamentals. The Huntington deal and continued AWM margin expansion represent incremental positive catalysts that are being ignored in the current risk-off environment.

The key inflection signal is that the company is buying back stock aggressively at these depressed levels — management clearly sees the disconnect. At $434, the $4.5B buyback covers ~10% of market cap, accelerating EPS growth mechanically.

## Bull Case

- Equity markets stabilize and AUM/AUA grows back toward $1.8T+ driving fee revenue acceleration
- Huntington integration adds ~$28B in assets and proves the institutional channel model for further bank partnerships
- Aggressive buybacks at depressed prices drive 12-15% EPS growth even with flat underlying earnings
- AWM margins continue expanding toward 30%+ as technology platform scales
- Multiple re-rates from ~9x to 12-14x as macro fears dissipate

## Bear Case

- Sustained equity market decline (20%+ drawdown) compresses AUM and fee revenue significantly
- Tariff-driven recession reduces household wealth, slowing advisor net flows
- Columbia Threadneedle faces continued active management headwinds (fee compression, passive shift)
- SEC fine is a symptom of broader compliance weaknesses that lead to further regulatory action
- Key person risk: Jim Cracchiolo (CEO since 2005) succession planning creates uncertainty
