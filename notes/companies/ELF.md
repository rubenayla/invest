# e.l.f. Beauty (ELF)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Household & Personal Products (Mass Cosmetics / Skincare)
**Price:** $51.11 (2026-06-03) | **Market Cap:** ~$3.04B | **Shares:** ~59.4M
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-03 (first note)

---

## Verdict: WATCH — fallen angel, but the fall looks largely earned, not a clean mispricing

ELF is down ~66% from its ~$151 peak and sits near its 52-week low ($49.72). On the surface it screams "broken hypergrowth darling on sale": ~15.5x forward **adjusted** EPS and ~9.6x EV/adjusted-EBITDA for a company still printing 35% headline revenue growth. Dig one layer down and the bargain mostly evaporates. The reason I am **not** flagging this as an actionable buy: the organic growth engine — the entire reason ELF ever earned a 40x multiple — has stalled, and a large slice of the "earnings" are adjusted-away real costs.

This note exists so the next session doesn't have to re-derive why the cheap-looking multiple is a trap-until-proven-otherwise.

---

## What actually happened (Q4 FY26, reported 2026-05-20)

- **Net sales +35% to $449.3M** — but **Rhode contributed $113M, i.e. ~34 of the 35 percentage points.** Strip out the acquired Hailey Bieber brand and **organic growth was ~1%.**
- **Gross margin actually expanded ~140bps to 73%** — pricing more than offset tariffs. Margins are *not* the problem.
- **GAAP net income for the quarter: −$49M** (a loss); full-year FY26 GAAP net income just **$26M, down from $112M in FY25** — collapse driven by Rhode acquisition/integration costs, intangible amortization, and interest on ~$917M of new debt.
- **FY27 guidance is the real story** and it came in below the Street:
  - Net sales growth **~12–14%** (mostly Rhode lapping + acquired base, not core).
  - **Q1 FY27 organic net sales guided DOWN high-single-digits** (lapping a heavy prior-year shipping period).
  - Adjusted EBITDA **$379–385M** (~21% margin), adjusted net income **$198–201M**, **adjusted EPS $3.27–3.32**.
  - Flagged a **$15–20M FY27 headwind** from oil prices linked to the Iran conflict.

Sell-side cut the average target from ~$153 to ~$122; Morgan Stanley to $59; Piper Sandler neutral. yfinance consensus: mean $72, low $50, high $90, 15 analysts, "buy."

## The crux

The market did not just punish a margin dip — it **re-rated ELF from a hypergrowth story to an ~low-double-digit grower whose core brand is flat-to-declining.** For years the bull case was that the e.l.f. brand kept taking mass-cosmetics share via viral/dupe marketing with Gen Z. Organic ~1% in Q4 and guided-down Q1 says that engine has, at minimum, stalled. The 12–14% total growth is now bought (Rhode), not earned.

**Quality flag:** the gap between adjusted (~$200M) and GAAP ($26M FY26) net income is enormous. It's stock-based comp (a real cost), Rhode intangible amortization, and deal costs. The "15x forward PE" is on the flattering number; on GAAP the stock is still expensive. Treat the adjusted figure with suspicion.

## Bull case

- 73% gross margin, expanding even through tariffs → genuine pricing power and brand strength.
- Rhode is performing ($113M in one quarter) and gives ELF a prestige/influencer leg beyond mass.
- International is still small and growing — a multi-year runway if execution holds.
- ~21% adjusted EBITDA margin is healthy; FCF generative.
- Down 66%; a lot of pessimism is already priced. If organic re-accelerates (new launches, international, retail-shelf gains), the multiple re-rates fast off a low base.

## Bear case

- **Core organic growth has broken** (Q1 FY27 guided down). If the e.l.f. brand is maturing/declining in the US, the whole multi-year thesis is gone and 12–14% decays once Rhode laps.
- **~75–80% China sourcing** → structural tariff exposure; pricing offsets work until they don't.
- **Adjusted ≫ GAAP** earnings — true economics are far below the headline multiple.
- **~$917M debt / ~$627M net debt** from the Rhode deal → leverage + interest drag on a now-slower grower.
- **Rhode is celebrity-dependent** (Hailey Bieber). Influencer brands can fade as fast as they spike; durability unproven.
- Mass cosmetics is brutally competitive; ELF's edge (viral dupes) is imitable and trend-sensitive.

## Scenario table (px $51.11; anchor FY27 adj EPS ~$3.30)

| Scenario | Prob | Driver | Multiple | Target | Return |
|----------|------|--------|----------|--------|--------|
| Bull | 25% | Organic re-accelerates, Rhode keeps compounding, growth back toward high-teens; FY28 adj EPS ~$4.00 | 20–22x | ~$85 | +66% |
| Base | 45% | ~12–14% total growth as guided, organic stays low; margins hold ~21% | 16–17x on $3.30 | ~$55 | +8% |
| Bear | 30% | Organic stays negative, growth decays after Rhode laps, tariff/oil margin pressure, GAAP gap re-rates it down | 12x on ~$3.00 | ~$38 | −26% |

**Expected value ≈ +9%.** Roughly balanced, not the asymmetric setup that warrants action. The right tail needs the core brand to come back to life — which the company itself just guided against for at least a quarter.

## What would change my mind (move WATCH → BUY)

1. **Organic growth inflects back positive** — a quarter where the *e.l.f. brand ex-Rhode* reaccelerates, not just total sales. This is the single most important trigger.
2. GAAP earnings converge toward adjusted (amortization/SBC roll off, debt paid down) — proving the cheap multiple is real.
3. Tariff/sourcing diversification away from China reduces the structural margin overhang.
4. Price-wise: a capitulation toward the low-$40s (sub-12x adjusted) where the bear case is largely priced and the risk/reward turns clearly asymmetric.

**Entry (if buying):** $42–46 — wait for either a fundamental organic inflection or a deeper price discount that prices the bear case. Not at $51, where it's roughly fair.
**Thesis-break (for an existing long):** organic net sales decline persists beyond the lapping quarters into mid-FY27, OR Rhode growth rolls over, OR a guidance cut to adj EPS below ~$3.00 — price-wise, a sustained break below the $42 area on fundamental deterioration.

## Data notes / caveats

- yfinance `grossMargins` (~61%) and `priceToSales` (~11x) are on a stale/quarterly basis and disagree with the company's reported 73% gross margin and ~1.9x sales — used the press-release figures.
- All per-share profitability above is on management's **adjusted** basis; GAAP is materially lower (FY26 GAAP net income $26M). Discount accordingly.
- Net debt ~$627M ($917M debt − $290M cash). EV ~$3.67B → ~9.6x FY27 adj EBITDA midpoint.
