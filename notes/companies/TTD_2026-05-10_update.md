# TTD Thesis Update — 2026-05-10

**Price:** ~$20.41 (-13% on May 7 Q1 print) | **Prior note:** 2026-03-20 BUY at $23.51

## What changed since March

Q1 2026 actuals: revenue $689M (+11.7% YoY, beat), adj EBITDA $206M at 30% margin (down from 34% prior year). **Q2 guide $750M = ~8% growth**, well below IAB CTV 13.8% and commerce media 12.1% forecasts. FY26 adj EBITDA margin guided "at least 40%" — i.e., flat YoY, not expanding. William Blair downgraded to Market Perform May 8 citing buyer survey showing TTD "losing market share."

New negatives that weren't fully in the prior note:
- **Walmart ended TTD DSP exclusivity** for Walmart Connect (was a 2021 exclusive). Walmart is now opening the roster — explicit "drive down fees" framing from agencies.
- **Amazon DSP fee disclosed at 1–2%** vs TTD's 12–15% buy-side fee plus 4.5% OpenPath publisher fee. Amazon also offers zero fees on first-party PG deals.
- **Amazon won Disney DRAX integration** (TTD no longer the only premium DSP at Disney) and signed Roku authenticated inventory deal covering 80M US households.
- **SBC still ~16% of revenue.** $164M of Q1 buybacks largely offset dilution; basic shares only down ~4% YoY.

## The moat question — answered

**The moat is genuinely weakening, not just re-rating.** This is more than a Publicis fee dispute. Three independent vectors:

1. **Price.** Amazon's 1–2% take vs TTD's ~21% effective rate is not survivable at parity feature sets. Agencies now have explicit, signed alternatives at Disney, Roku, Netflix. The "Cha-Ching Desk" nickname is sticking.
2. **Inventory access.** TTD's premium-CTV preferred-status advantage is mostly gone — Amazon DSP is now a co-equal pipe at Disney, Roku, Netflix (via Amazon-Netflix). Disney NBCU TTD Roku/Netflix all multi-DSP now.
3. **Agency relationships.** Publicis stopped recommending; Dentsu + WPP exited OpenPath. WPP CFO publicly said TTD "operates in a smaller slice of the ad market." This is structural messaging, not negotiation noise.

Kokai is making it worse, not better: higher-than-expected pricing in Performance mode is the proximate cause of difficult renewals. Margin compression (34→30%) reflects infrastructure spend (owned datacenters) plus pricing concessions, not just transitional rollout. Structural, not transitional.

## Growth decel diagnosis

Not macro. CTV market is growing 13.8% in 2026 and TTD's own video/CTV is "low 50s%" of mix — yet total growth is 12% decelerating to 8% in Q2. **TTD is underperforming the very channels it dominates.** That's share loss, full stop. The Publicis dispute amplifies it; it didn't cause it.

## Insider/capital return

CEO bought $148M at ~$24.50 (March). Still bullish signal, but he's now underwater. $500M buyback auth, $327M remaining as of 3/31. With SBC at 16% of revenue, buybacks barely run in place on share count. No 10b5-1 disclosure suggests open-window discretionary buy — high-conviction but not informationally privileged about Q1 trajectory (which he knew about).

## Verdict — DOWNGRADE to HOLD

**The $21 add-level no longer holds as a confident buy.** The thesis has materially weakened:
- Moat erosion is structural (Amazon pricing + multi-DSP at every premium CTV property), not a Publicis negotiation.
- Kokai friction looks like a permanent take-rate compression vector, not a one-quarter rollout bump.
- 8% Q2 growth in a 14% CTV market is the smoking gun for share loss.
- The CEO's buy was a thesis prop in March; at $20 he's down ~17% and Q1 confirmed the bear case is being priced in correctly.

**What I'd do with the existing position:**
- **Don't add** at $20 absent new positive data (Publicis reversal, OpenAI deal confirmation, sequential CTV reacceleration in Q2 print).
- **Hold starter position** — bear case is partly priced, and Jeff Green + balance sheet + 78% gross margin still rules out a zero. Net cash, $327M buyback left, no debt risk.
- **Cut to zero if:** (a) Q2 actual <$735M (missing the already-low guide), (b) a second top-5 agency formally derecommends, or (c) Amazon DSP take rate moves to flat 0% for CTV. Any of these = $13 base case.
- **Add aggressively if:** OpenAI deal confirmed with revenue number, OR a guide raise reversing the decel pattern. Need a positive catalyst, not just absence of negatives.

**Thesis break level revised from $15 → $17.** Below $17, you're paying for a permanently impaired DSP at ~7x EBITDA — only worth it if you believe in re-rating, and the data no longer supports that conviction.
