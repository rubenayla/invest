# SOLV — Solventum Corporation

**Price:** $75.59 (2026-06-24)
**Rating:** WATCH (lean positive) — a defensive, cash-generative medtech spun out of 3M, now de-levered
and run by a proven turnaround CEO, trading at ~11.5x 2026 adjusted EPS vs medtech peers at 13–16x. The
"orphaned-spinoff" discount has already half-closed (bottomed ~$62, now $75), so the easy money is gone
and the margin of safety at $75 is thin. Genuine re-rating optionality if Bryan Hanson executes, but the
core is a low-single-digit grower with a 3M transition-services/royalty drag. Want a sub-$66 entry (≈10x)
before paying up.
**First look:** 2026-06-24

Solventum is the **healthcare business 3M spun off in April 2024** — wound care, IV/surgical supplies,
dental/orthodontic solutions, and health information systems (clinical coding/revenue-cycle software).
~$8.3B revenue, ~$13.1B market cap on ~173M shares, EV ~$17.8B. It came public as a classic "forced
seller" orphan: index funds that held 3M for its dividend dumped the non-dividend-paying spin, 3M
retained a ~19.9% stake to sell down, and the company inherited ~$8B of debt and a web of transition
service agreements (TSAs) and supply/royalty arrangements back to the parent. The stock bottomed around
$52–62 in 2024–25 and has recovered to **$75.59**, ~14% off its 52-week high ($88.20).

## Why it's on the radar
- **Cheap-ish for the quality**: 2026 adjusted EPS guidance is **$6.40–$6.60** (tracking the high end per
  Q1'26), so ~**11.5x forward adjusted earnings** on a low-beta (0.74) defensive medtech. Peers (BDX, BAX,
  ZBH) sit at 13–16x. Trailing GAAP P/E (~9x) is flattered by the divestiture gain — use the adjusted number.
- **Balance sheet de-risked**: the **$4.1B sale of Purification & Filtration to Thermo Fisher** (closed 2025)
  put ~$3.4B net proceeds toward debt paydown, taking gross debt from ~$8B at spin to ~$5.3B (net debt
  ~$4.7B). The divestiture is *accretive* to adjusted EPS via lower interest.
- **Proven operator at the helm**: **Bryan Hanson** (CEO from May 2025) ran the Zimmer Biomet turnaround;
  his mandate here is margin expansion + portfolio pruning (SKU exits) + reigniting organic growth.
- **Consistent beats**: every quarter since the spin has beaten consensus (surprises +3.7% to +19%), a sign
  guidance is sandbagged. Analysts: 13 covering, **BUY**, mean target **$82.15** (+8.7%), range $60–$100.
- **2026 organic growth guide +2.0–3.0%** (+3.0–4.0% ex ~100 bps of deliberate SKU exits) — modest growth
  resuming after a flat-revenue stretch ($8.13B → $8.20B → $8.25B → $8.33B FY22–FY25, ~0.8% CAGR).

## The bear case (lead with it)
1. **The core is a low-single-digit grower, not a compounder.** Revenue was essentially flat for four years.
   The biggest segment (MedSurg/wound care) and Dental face share pressure; Health Information Systems is a
   slow-growth software book. +2–3% organic is the *guide*, and a chunk of that is price. Strip price and
   real volume growth is anemic. You are paying 11.5x for ~mid-single-digit EPS growth, much of it
   engineered via debt paydown and buybacks rather than the franchise getting better.
2. **The 3M umbilical cord is a real drag.** TSAs, a supply agreement, and royalty/IP arrangements back to
   3M create stranded costs and a margin headwind that only unwinds gradually. GAAP operating margin is
   depressed (~6%) by separation costs and intangible amortization; the bull case *requires* these to roll
   off on schedule — execution risk, not a given.
3. **Leverage caps the re-rating.** Net debt ~$4.7B against ~$1.8–2.0B adjusted EBITDA is ~2.4x — manageable
   but it means free cash flow goes to the balance sheet, not shareholders (no dividend, limited buyback).
   2025 reported FCF was roughly breakeven (separation/restructuring cash costs); the market wants to see
   normalized FCF conversion prove out before paying a premium multiple.
4. **Tariffs.** Management explicitly flagged tariff headwinds in 2026 guidance; a medical-supply business
   with global manufacturing has real input/import exposure.
5. **The discount has already half-closed.** This isn't a fresh washout — it's up ~20–45% off the lows. The
   asymmetric "orphan mispricing" trade was 2024–25; at $75 you're closer to fair value.

## The bull case
- **Re-rating to peers is the prize.** A defensive medtech growing EPS mid-single-digits should not trade at
  11.5x when peers fetch 13–16x. Closing half that gap on flat estimates is +15–20% with no operational hero
  ball required.
- **Hanson turnarounds are credible.** At Zimmer he took a fatigued ortho franchise and expanded margins and
  multiples. The SKU-exit + margin-expansion + bolt-on M&A playbook is exactly what he ran before.
- **Self-help, not market-dependent.** Debt paydown accretion, TSA roll-off, and stranded-cost removal are
  largely within management's control — the EPS bridge to ~$7.5–8.0 by 2028–29 doesn't need a strong end market.
- **Low beta + non-cyclical demand.** Hospital consumables and dental are recession-resistant; 0.74 beta makes
  the risk-adjusted return more attractive than the raw EV suggests.

## Entry / exit framework
- **Entry interest: sub-$66** (≈10x the 2026 guide), where the bear-case downside compresses and the
  risk/reward turns clearly positive. At $75 the margin of safety is thin.
- **Thesis-break (sell / stay away): below ~$58** on deteriorating organic growth (negative organic prints)
  or evidence the TSA/stranded-cost roll-off is slipping — that would confirm a structural low-grower, not a
  turnaround.
- **What would change my mind (to a buy):** two consecutive quarters of organic growth at the high end of
  guide *with* visible adjusted-margin expansion, plus the 3M TSAs winding down on schedule — that converts
  the re-rating from "hope" to "happening," and would justify paying up toward $80.

## What would make me more bearish
- Organic growth slips toward 0% or a segment (Dental, MedSurg) posts a negative print.
- Tariffs or FX force a guidance cut.
- FCF conversion stays weak past 2026 (separation costs were supposed to be largely behind them).
- 3M's residual ~19.9% stake overhangs the stock as it sells down.

## Bottom line
A quality, defensive, de-levered medtech at a reasonable-but-not-cheap ~11.5x, with a credible self-help
catalyst (Hanson) and consistent beats — but a structurally slow-growth core and a thin margin of safety at
$75. **WATCH (lean positive); buy the dip sub-$66, not here.**
