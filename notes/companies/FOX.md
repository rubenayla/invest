# Fox Corporation (FOX)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Entertainment
**Price:** $52.02 | **Market Cap:** $24.3B | **EV:** $27.8B

## Situation Summary

Fox Corp is trading 24% below its 52-week high ($68.18) after a mixed Q2 FY2026: EPS of $0.82 beat estimates by 67%, but EBITDA declined 11% YoY to $692M due to higher sports rights costs and digital marketing spend. The stock sold off post-earnings despite the EPS beat, and insiders have been heavy sellers — CEO Lachlan Murdoch sold $65.4M and President Nallen sold $26.5M in March 2026. The big catalyst on the horizon is the 2026 FIFA World Cup (June-July 2026) where Fox holds exclusive U.S. English-language rights for a 48-team, 104-game tournament hosted in North America — the largest sports advertising event outside the Super Bowl. Meanwhile, Tubi (Fox's AVOD platform) hit profitability for the first time and grew revenue 27% in Q1 FY2026, and the Fox One streaming service has attracted 2.3M subscribers since its August 2025 launch.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Wall Street sees Fox as a mature legacy media company with modest growth (5% revenue CAGR), facing secular cord-cutting headwinds and rising sports rights costs that compress margins. The 7 Buy / 7 Hold split reflects uncertainty. Mean target ~$65-76. The stock is cheap on paper (12x P/E, 7x EV/EBITDA) but "cheap for a reason" — the media business is structurally challenged.

- **Our view:** The market is underpricing three simultaneous tailwinds converging in the June-September 2026 window: (1) the FIFA World Cup on home soil, with knockout-stage ads priced at Super Bowl-level CPMs and inventory nearly sold out — this is a one-time revenue surge that hasn't happened for Fox since the 2022 tournament (which was in Qatar with poor U.S. time zones); (2) Tubi's profitability inflection means digital revenue growth is now accretive to margins, not a drag; (3) Fox One at 2.3M subs creates a direct-to-consumer revenue stream that recaptures cord-cutters. The current $52 price appears to embed significant worry about the margin compression seen in Q2 and insider selling, but the Q2 weakness was driven by sports rights timing and political ad comps — both temporary. The World Cup quarter (Q4 FY2026 / calendar Q3 2026) should produce Fox's strongest quarter in years.

- **Trigger:** Q3/Q4 FY2026 earnings (reporting Aug/Nov 2026) showing World Cup advertising revenue surge, Tubi margin expansion, and Fox One subscriber growth. Near-term, the World Cup itself (starting June 11, 2026) will generate headlines and analyst upgrades as ad pricing data emerges.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — multiple converging inflections:**

1. **Tubi profitability crossover:** Fox's largest growth asset just turned profitable. This changes the earnings trajectory — Tubi revenue growth now flows directly to the bottom line rather than being a drag.

2. **Fox One DTC launch:** 2.3M subscribers in 4 months, with Amazon driving 57% of sign-ups. This creates a cord-cutting hedge that didn't exist 6 months ago.

3. **FIFA World Cup on home soil (June 2026):** 104 games, knockout-stage CPMs at Super Bowl levels, inventory nearly sold out. This is the largest single-event advertising opportunity in Fox's history.

4. **Technical oversold condition:** Stock is 24% below 52-week high, 11% below 50-day MA, trading near the bottom of its range with declining short interest (-19% in February).

The observable evidence is clear: Tubi profitability is already reported, Fox One subscriber numbers are growing, and World Cup ad inventory is sold. These are not speculative — the revenue is largely locked in.

## Bull Case

- FIFA World Cup drives a $500M-1B incremental advertising revenue surge in Q4 FY2026, producing the best quarter in Fox's history
- Tubi sustains 20%+ revenue growth and expanding margins, reaching $2B+ revenue run-rate by FY2027
- Fox One grows to 5M+ subscribers by end of FY2027, partially replacing affiliate fee erosion
- Share buyback ($1.8B pace) reduces share count by 7-8% annually at current prices, amplifying EPS growth
- Stock re-rates from 10.5x forward P/E to 13-14x as the market recognizes the digital transition

## Bear Case

- NFL rights renewal (next negotiation cycle) costs escalate dramatically, compressing margins permanently
- Cord-cutting accelerates beyond expectations, and Fox One/Tubi cannot fully replace lost affiliate economics
- Insider selling accelerates — Murdoch family reducing exposure could signal lack of confidence
- Political/regulatory risk: changes in FCC regulation, advertising boycotts, or defamation exposure
- World Cup viewership disappoints due to U.S. team performance or competition from other platforms

## Verdict

**BUY** — Conviction: **MEDIUM**

Fox trades at 12.3% FCF yield and 7.2x EV/EBITDA — pricing that implies permanent decline. But three converging tailwinds (World Cup, Tubi profitability, Fox One) are specific, measurable, and largely already locked in via pre-sold ad inventory. The variant perception is that the market is treating temporary margin compression (sports rights timing, political ad comps) as structural, while underpricing a once-in-a-generation home-soil World Cup advertising event. Conviction is MEDIUM rather than HIGH because of the heavy insider selling and the structural cord-cutting headwind that makes the post-World Cup trajectory uncertain.
