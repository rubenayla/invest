## Situation Summary

Halliburton is the second-largest oilfield services company globally, with a strong position in completions and production. The company navigated a challenging 2025 marked by declining North America activity, margin compression (operating income fell ~22% YoY), and a Q3 one-time charge that cratered net income. However, Q4 2025 showed a dramatic rebound (adjusted EPS $0.69, beating estimates by 26%, FCF $875M), and the stock has rallied 60% from its October lows near $18.25. The near-term overhang is tariff risk on steel/equipment costs, weak oil prices from an oversupplied market (OPEC+ unwinding cuts), and North American E&P capital discipline. A new PETRONAS Suriname collaboration (April 2026) signals international growth, and Q1 2026 earnings on April 21 are the next major catalyst. Analyst consensus is $2.21 EPS for FY2026 (down 9% YoY from $2.42) with recovery to $2.62 in FY2027.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** HAL is a cyclical value play in a down-cycle. Wall Street expects modest EPS decline in 2026 ($2.21 vs $2.42), then recovery in 2027. 13 Strong Buys / 3 Buys / 8 Holds / 1 Sell with mean target $37.50 — i.e., already near fair value at $38.72. The market sees the stock as fairly priced after the rally.
- **Our view:** The stock has already priced in the recovery. At $38.72 with consensus 2026 EPS of $2.21, the forward PE is ~17.5x on the actual 2026 number. For a cyclical OFS company in a down-cycle, 17-18x is historically expensive — HAL traded at 10-14x forward earnings for most of the 2022-2024 upcycle. The re-rating from $18 to $39 has captured the margin recovery story. Upside from here requires either oil prices to spike (not our base case with OPEC+ adding supply) or sustained international growth that offsets NA weakness. No variant perception: consensus is roughly correct.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings on April 21 will set the narrative. If guidance disappoints on NA activity or tariff cost absorption, the stock could give back recent gains. If international revenues surprise upward, it could break $41.

## Inflection Point

**No positive inflection evident.** This is the opposite — HAL appears to be past its cycle peak:
- Revenue peaked in 2023 ($23.0B) and has been declining
- Operating margins peaked in 2023 (17.7%) and are compressing
- ROE has dropped from 28% to 12%
- NA drilling activity is declining as E&Ps cut budgets
- OPEC+ is adding supply, pressuring oil prices

The PETRONAS Suriname deal (April 2026) is a positive data point for international growth but is early-stage and won't impact revenue meaningfully for 12-18 months.

**What would constitute an inflection:** Oil prices above $85 sustained, or NA rig counts stabilizing and turning upward. Neither is visible today.

## Bull Case
- Oil prices spike on geopolitical disruption (Middle East, Russia) to $85+, driving E&P spending acceleration
- International revenue growth (Middle East, Suriname, Brazil) offsets NA weakness, reaching 65%+ of revenue
- E-frac technology gains market share, improving margins through fleet efficiency
- Buyback program ($1B/yr) provides meaningful EPS support at declining share counts
- Multiple re-rates to 18x forward earnings if market sees HAL as a quality cyclical at trough

## Bear Case
- Oil prices remain in $55-65 range as OPEC+ supply and weak China demand persist — NA activity continues declining
- Tariffs on steel/equipment raise costs 5-10%, compressing margins further
- International spending disappoints as NOCs defer projects amid lower oil prices
- BKR pulls ahead on diversification (energy transition, data centers), HAL left as pure-play commodity services
- Earnings decline further to $1.80-2.00 and stock re-rates to 10-12x = $18-24

## Verdict
