# Match Group, Inc. (MTCH)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Online dating apps (Tinder, Hinge, Match.com, OkCupid, PlentyOfFish, Meetic, Azar, Pairs, +others)
**Price:** $35.31 (2026-06-18) | **Market Cap:** ~$8.24B | **Enterprise Value:** ~$11.47B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-18
**Verdict:** WATCH (lean BUY LOW under ~$30). Conviction **LOW–MEDIUM**.

A cash-gushing portfolio of dating apps trading at **~8x forward EPS / ~10x EV/EBITDA / ~10% FCF yield**, where the market has priced Match as a melting ice cube because its largest asset (**Tinder, ~53% of revenue**) has been losing payers for two years. The variant question is whether the **two genuine positives** — Hinge compounding +25–28% and Tinder registrations turning positive YoY in March 2026 (first increase in ~2 years) — plus a new owner-operator CEO buying stock and an aggressive buyback, are enough to flip the story from "terminal decline" to "stabilize + modest growth." It is cheap and you get paid to wait (~10% FCF yield + 2.3% dividend + buyback), but revenue has been **dead flat for two years and guidance is flat again for 2026**, so this is value-with-optionality, not a fat-pitch bargain. **EV ≈ +20%** over ~2 years with a real left tail.

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Match owns the largest stable of dating apps in the West. Revenue is ~$3.49B (2025), nearly all subscription + à-la-carte ("direct revenue"). The two assets that matter:
- **Tinder** — ~$1.86B annualized direct revenue (~53% of total). Still the category's biggest brand but structurally challenged: Q4'25 payers **−8% to 8.8M**, RPP (revenue per payer) **+5% to $17.63**, direct revenue **−3%**. Monetization (RPP) is offsetting some of the payer bleed, but the trend is down.
- **Hinge** — the growth engine. Q4'25 direct revenue **$186M (+26%)**, payers **+17% to 1.9M**, RPP **+8% to $32.96**; Q1'26 **$194M (+28%)**, payers **+15% to 2.0M**. European expansion went from 0.2M MAU at launch to **3.3M MAU**, expected to add **>$100M direct revenue in 2026**. Hinge monetizes better than Tinder (higher RPP) and targets the relationship-seeking demographic that is growing while casual-swipe fatigue hits Tinder.

The rest (Match.com, Meetic, OkCupid, PoF, Azar, Pairs, evergreen brands) is a slowly-declining cash cow that funds buybacks.

## Why it's cheap (the de-rating)

1. **Tinder is shrinking.** Payers −8% YoY; the franchise that drove the IPO-era growth story has been in payer decline for ~2 years. The market extrapolates Tinder → zero.
2. **Revenue is flat, and guided flat.** 2022 $3.19B → 2023 $3.36B → 2024 $3.48B → 2025 $3.49B (**+0.2%**). FY2026 guide: **$3.41–3.535B, ~flat YoY**. No top-line growth until ~2027 at the earliest.
3. **Dating-app secular fear.** Narrative that Gen Z dates less, swipe-fatigue is real, and that AI companions / new entrants (e.g. social-first apps) erode the category.
4. **Leverage.** Net debt ~$2.95B (total debt $3.97B, cash $1.02B) ≈ **~2.7x EBITDA** — manageable but real; negative book value (−$0.93/sh) because buybacks have exceeded retained equity.

## Why it might be mispriced (the bull)

- **Cheap on cash, paid to wait.** ~$805M FCF on $8.24B mkt cap = **~9.8% FCF yield**; ~8.3x forward EPS ($4.24 est); ~10x EV/EBITDA ($1.1B EBITDA). 2026 adj-EBITDA margin guided to **37.5%** after a **13% workforce cut** (~325 jobs).
- **Tinder may be inflecting.** Management reports **Tinder registrations returned to YoY growth in March 2026 — the first increase in nearly two years** — plus improving MAU and Gen-Z engagement. Registrations → MAU → payers → revenue is a lagged chain, so this is a *leading* indicator, not yet in the numbers. If it holds, Tinder revenue decline narrows toward flat by 2027.
- **Hinge is a real second engine.** Growing 25–28% with better unit economics and a long international runway; if it reaches ~$1B+ direct revenue (from ~$700M run-rate) it materially offsets Tinder and shifts mix toward growth.
- **Capital return shrinks the equity.** Diluted shares **295M (2022) → 262M (2025) → ~233M now** (−21% in 3y, −7% YoY). $959M buyback authorization remaining (~12% of cap) + a **dividend raised 5% to $0.20/qtr (~2.3% yield)**. Even flat net income → mid-single-digit EPS growth from the buyback alone.
- **Owner-operator + activist alignment.** New CEO **Spencer Rascoff** (Zillow co-founder), installed after **Elliott** and **Anson** built stakes, is one year into a three-phase turnaround and **personally bought ~$2M of stock twice in three months** — insider conviction at the current price.

## Variant Perception

- **Bear / consensus view:** A no-growth holding company whose flagship is in secular decline, levered ~2.7x, in a category facing demographic and AI-disruption headwinds. Cheap for a reason; "registrations turning" is a vanity metric that won't convert to payer revenue; value trap that grinds sideways while Tinder bleeds.
- **Our (cautiously interested) view:** At ~8x forward EPS and ~10% FCF yield the market is pricing perpetual decline, but you have **three things that don't usually coincide in a value trap**: a profitable +25% growth asset (Hinge), a genuine leading-indicator inflection (Tinder registrations), and an aligned owner-operator buying stock into a heavy buyback. The buyback alone compounds per-share value while you wait. The setup is asymmetric *if* Tinder merely stops getting worse.
- **Why still only WATCH:** the single thing that matters — does the Tinder registration turn convert into payer/revenue stabilization — is not yet in the reported numbers, and management itself guides 2026 revenue flat. Buying before payer trends confirm is paying for an inflection on faith. The margin of safety is in the cash flow, not the growth.

## Entry / Exit Discipline

- **Entry:** lean BUY LOW **under ~$30** (≈7x FCF, near the 52-wk low, where downside is cushioned by the buyback/FCF yield). At $35 it is fair-to-slightly-cheap, not a fat pitch — a starter only.
