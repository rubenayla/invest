## Business Primer

**What they do.** Chewy is the largest US online pet retailer: ~$12.6B revenue selling pet food, supplies, and increasingly **pharmacy (Chewy Pharmacy), insurance, and vet care (Chewy Vet Care clinics)**. The structural attraction is **Autoship** — recurring subscription orders that are ~80%+ of net sales — which makes a discretionary-looking retailer behave more like a recurring-consumable subscription (pet food/meds are bought regardless of the economy). ~20M active customers, and the key metric the market watches is **NSPAC (net sales per active customer)** rising even as the customer count plateaus.

**Why it's not a fat-margin business.** It's still a retailer: ~30% gross margin, ~1.3% GAAP operating margin (~5-6% adjusted EBITDA margin). The bull case is *margin expansion* — higher-margin pharmacy/private-label mix, a sponsored-ads business (high-margin), and automation/fulfillment leverage — not revenue heroics. The proof is in the FCF: it has climbed **$119M → $343M → $452M → $562M** over four years on only ~6% annual revenue growth.

## Why it's cheap (the de-rating)

1. **Growth decelerated, hard.** Revenue grew ~mid-teens during COVID pet adoption; FY26 (ended Jan 2026) was **$12.6B, +6.2%**, and Q4 was roughly *flat* YoY. The post-pandemic pet-adoption wave reversed (fewer new pet households), so active-customer growth stalled near ~20M. The market re-rated a "growth" multiple toward a "GDP-grower" multiple.
2. **The BC Partners overhang.** Chewy is a controlled, dual-class company. Total shares ~409M but **public float is only ~62M**; sponsor BC Partners has been steadily monetizing its stake via secondary offerings. Each placement is a fresh supply dump into a thin float — a recurring, mechanical lid on the price that has nothing to do with fundamentals and won't fully clear until the sponsor is out.
3. **Consumer-discretionary fear + competition.** Macro worry about the discretionary consumer, plus Amazon and Walmart pushing into pet, feeds a "no durable moat" narrative.
4. **Down 56% with no dividend to pay you to wait** — pure capital-appreciation thesis, so patience has an opportunity cost.

## Why it might be mispriced (the bull)

- **It's a real FCF machine, cheaply priced.** ~$562M FCF (+24% YoY) on an ~$8.2B EV = **~14.5x EV/FCF**, forward P/E ~10x, and a **net-cash** balance sheet (~$879M cash vs ~$557M debt). For a business compounding FCF at >20% with 80% recurring revenue, that is not a demanding multiple.
- **Autoship recurrence dampens the cyclicality** the discretionary label implies — people don't stop feeding/medicating pets in a downturn.
- **Margin-expansion runway is structural, not hopeful:** sponsored ads, private label, pharmacy, and fulfillment automation each lift mix/leverage; the four-year FCF ramp is the receipt.
- **Buybacks** are shrinking the share count and partially absorbing the sponsor's selling.
- **Sell-side mean target ~$38.85** (vs $20.40) screams either deep value or stale targets the analysts haven't yet cut — the gap itself is a flag to watch, not to trust.
