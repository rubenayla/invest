# Walmart Inc. (WMT)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Discount Stores
**Price:** $121.98 | **Market Cap:** $972B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-19

## Situation Summary

Walmart just reported Q4 FY2026 (Jan quarter): revenue $190.7B (+5.6% YoY), EPS $0.74 (beat $0.73 estimate). Full-year FY2026 revenue crossed $713B for the first time. E-commerce surged 24% globally (27% US), and the e-commerce segment was profitable for the first time ever. Advertising revenue hit $6.4B (+46% YoY), now representing roughly one-third of operating income alongside membership fees. The company announced a $30B buyback program and a CEO transition (John Furner took over Feb 1). However, FY2027 guidance disappointed: EPS $2.75-$2.85 vs. Street at $2.96, citing "fluid backdrop" (tariffs, labor costs). The stock has pulled back ~9.4% from its 52W high of $134.69, currently at $121.98. Tariff risk is significant: ~60% of Walmart's imports come from China, and the company has been publicly pressuring Chinese suppliers for 10% discounts, drawing pushback from Chinese authorities.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Strong Buy (26 buy / 3 hold / 0 sell). Mean target ~$133-$139. The market sees Walmart as a defensive compounder transitioning into a tech-enabled retail platform with high-margin advertising and membership businesses. The narrative is that Walmart is "the next Amazon" in retail media.
- **Our view:** The quality transformation is real but the valuation already prices it in. At 44.7x trailing and 37x forward PE, this is no longer a value stock -- it trades like a tech compounder but still generates 3% net margins and 4.6% operating margins. The high-margin revenue streams (advertising $6.4B, Walmart+ membership) are growing fast but still small relative to the $713B revenue base. The FY2027 guidance miss is telling: management sees headwinds the market is dismissing. Tariff exposure is the biggest near-term risk, and Walmart's aggressive supplier squeeze could backfire if Chinese suppliers push back or reduce quality. The 0.81% dividend yield provides negligible cushion at these multiples.
- **Trigger:** Q1 FY2027 earnings (May 2026) for tariff impact visibility; any tariff escalation/resolution; e-commerce profitability sustainability confirmation.

## Inflection Point

**Partial inflection -- business model transformation.** Walmart is evolving from a pure low-margin retailer into a retail platform with high-margin revenue streams:
1. **E-commerce profitability** -- achieved for the first time in FY2026. This is structural, not cyclical.
2. **Advertising at $6.4B** -- growing 46%, with 70-90% margins. On track to be a $10B+ business within 2 years.
3. **Walmart+ membership** driving recurring revenue and customer stickiness.

However, the inflection is partially priced in -- the stock trades at 44x trailing earnings, 3-4x its historical PE range of 12-20x. The market already sees the transformation. The question is whether the high-margin businesses can scale fast enough to justify the premium.

## Final Verdict: WATCH

**Conviction: Medium**

Walmart is an exceptional business undergoing a genuine transformation from low-margin retailer to high-margin retail platform. The advertising, e-commerce, and membership businesses are real and growing fast. Management execution has been outstanding.

However, the valuation gives us pause. At 44.7x trailing and 37x forward PE, the stock prices in near-perfect execution and continued margin expansion. The FY2027 guidance miss signals management sees headwinds. Tariff risk is material and underappreciated. The expected value of +4.5% does not offer sufficient margin of safety for a new position.

**Action:** Add to watchlist. Monitor for:
1. **Better entry at $105-$115** (PE compression to 35-38x forward)
2. **Tariff clarity** -- any resolution would be a catalyst
3. **Q1 FY2027 earnings** -- key test of whether FY2027 guidance is conservative or realistic
4. **Advertising/membership trajectory** -- if these accelerate beyond expectations, the premium is justified

This is a quality compounder at a rich valuation. Wait for the market to give us a better price.
