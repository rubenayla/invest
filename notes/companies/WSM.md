# Williams-Sonoma, Inc. (WSM)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Specialty Retail (Home Furnishings)
**Price:** $192.87 (2026-04-15) | **Market Cap:** $22.95B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-16

## Situation Summary

WSM just delivered a STRONG Q4 FY25 (reported March 18, 2026): +3.2% comp, 20.3% Q4 operating margin, record full-year EPS of $8.84, ended with $1.0B cash / no funded debt, and hiked the dividend 15% (20th consecutive annual raise). Despite the beat, the stock pulled back ~12% from its February 2026 high of $214 on FY26 guidance: +2-6% comp, **17.5-18.1% op margin** (down from ~18.6% in FY25) due to ~$80M of incremental tariff costs front-loaded into H1. Goldman (Kate McShane) upgraded to Buy on 2026-04-13 with $218 PT, arguing the pullback over-discounts a best-in-class home retailer. Current price $192.87 (~+3% YTD, -13% from 52w high). Housing turnover remains near multi-decade lows (~6.2% mortgage rates, re-nesting trend). Q1 FY26 reports in late May — the first real test of tariff pass-through.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Home-furnishings is structurally impaired by frozen housing turnover; WSM's 20%+ Q4 op margin is a cyclical peak that compresses to a new normal in the 17-18% range as tariffs hit; 7 Buy / 14 Hold analyst split with $204 mean PT (~+6% upside) reflects a tepid "quality-but-expensive" view. Trades at ~22x TTM / 19x forward — premium to peers, but moderate given margin structure.
- **Our view:** **Weak variant perception.** WSM IS structurally superior (20%+ op margin vs peer 2-11%; debt-free with $1B cash; ROE >50%; vertically integrated Pottery Barn / West Elm brands with real pricing power), but this is already widely understood. The debate is about DURABILITY of the premium through a tariff + housing trough. Our differentiated view: the 17.5-18% FY26 guide is **conservative and front-loaded**. Management has a track record of sandbagging, and the tariff hit normalizes in H2 as inventory rotates. Even if margins reset permanently to ~18% (vs ~20% peak), that's STILL 2x the peer average — the quality premium is not at risk. Housing turnover recovery is optionality, not a requirement. At 19x forward on a likely-conservative number, this is priced for "good company, tough year," which is probably correct but not a meaningful mispricing.
- **Trigger:** Q1 FY26 earnings in late May 2026 — if comps track toward the upper end (4-6%) and op margin holds 17%+ despite H1 tariff concentration, the "margin reset" narrative breaks. Conversely, a comp miss or margin below 16.5% would confirm bear thesis.

## Inflection Point

No clean inflection. WSM is a **high-quality compounder at a mid-cycle valuation**, not a turnaround. Possible optionality: (a) housing turnover recovery if rates drop to ~5%, unlocking 10%+ comp tailwind; (b) tariff relief post-July 2026 if Section 122 expires cleanly; (c) B2B segment (~$1B) scaling to $2B+ over 3-5 years. None are dateable or high-conviction — they're call options, not a thesis.

## Bull Case

- FY26 guidance is sandbagged. Management consistently guides conservative; actual op margin lands 18.5-19% vs 17.5-18.1% guide.
- Tariff concentration is front-half; H2 exit-rate op margin returns to 19-20%, setting up FY27 at 19%+ full year.
- Housing turnover bottoms mid-2026 with Fed cuts, driving 2027 comp acceleration to high-single-digits.
- Continued ~3-4% share count reduction via $1B+ buyback + 1.6% dividend = 5%+ per-share compounding floor even with flat revenue.
- Multiple re-rates from ~19x forward to 22x on durability proof (Goldman's thesis).

## Bear Case

- Tariff reality is worse than guided: Vietnam/China/India sourcing (19%+16%+15% = 50%+ of imports) gets hit harder; Section 232 furniture tariffs (30-50%) can't be fully passed through; FY26 op margin ends at 16-17%.
- Housing turnover stays frozen through 2027; comps grind at 0-2%; the whole category de-rates.
- Premium reverts: market concludes 20% op margin was a COVID-pricing-power peak; steady-state is 15-16%; WSM trades to 14-15x = $135-150.
- Mean reversion in ROE as buybacks slow once cash cushion is needed for tariff-driven working capital build.

## Verdict

**PASS** — Conviction: MEDIUM

No meaningful variant perception. WSM is a best-in-class operator trading at a fair — not cheap — price for what it is. The Goldman upgrade at $218 essentially codifies the consensus "high-quality cyclical" view. My base case EV of +1.5% and expected value tilted by a fat-tail bear at -33% does not clear the hurdle for a cyclical with significant tariff and housing-turnover overhangs. GBM's +44% is partly inflated by stale prices and the general GBM tendency to extrapolate trend in mean-reverting cyclicals. I like the BUSINESS a lot more than the SETUP. A better entry would be post-Q1 earnings IF guidance holds and the stock dips to $165-175 (close to 200d MA and 15x forward) — that would improve EV materially.

**If WATCH:** Would upgrade to BUY at $165-170 (roughly -14%) with confirmation that Q1 op margin prints above 16.5% and comps positive. Would avoid adding above $210 even on good news unless FY26 margin guide is raised.
