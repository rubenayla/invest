# Zimmer Biomet Holdings (ZBH)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Medical Devices (Orthopedics)
**Price:** $88.26 (2026-06-16) | **Market Cap:** ~$17.1B | **Enterprise Value:** ~$24.2B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-16
**Standalone rating: WATCH** (cheap, defensive, but low-growth with value-trap history)

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Zimmer Biomet is one of the big-four orthopedic-implant makers (with Stryker, Johnson & Johnson's DePuy, and Smith+Nephew). The core business is large-joint reconstruction — artificial **knees and hips** — which is roughly half of revenue. Around that sits a portfolio of "SET" products (Sports medicine, Extremities, Trauma, foot & ankle, craniomaxillofacial), spine and dental (now a separate joint venture), and a growing **robotics + data** franchise built on the **ROSA** surgical robot. In FY2025 the company did ~$8.2B of revenue at a 70% gross margin.

**Why customers (surgeons/hospitals) choose them.** Implants are a trust-and-switching-cost business: a surgeon trained on a given knee system, its instrument trays, and its robotic workflow rarely switches without a strong reason. ZBH's installed base of ROSA robots pulls through implant sales (the razor-and-blade dynamic), and hospital purchasing is sticky once a system is validated. The moat is real but **narrow and contested** — it is the #2/#3 player in most categories, not the leader.

**The core problem.** Orthopedics is a low-growth, share-stable oligopoly: the end market grows mid-single-digits (aging demographics, more procedures), and ZBH's **organic** growth has chronically run *below* market — only **~2.9% organic constant-currency in Q1 2026**, with full-year organic guidance of just **2.0–4.0%**. The headline 9.3% Q1 revenue growth is mostly the **Paragon 28** foot-and-ankle acquisition plus FX, not underlying strength. Stryker's Mako robot took the robotics-share lead years ago and ZBH has been playing catch-up with ROSA ever since.

**Where they want to grow.** Management's bet is (1) faster-growing adjacencies bought in — Paragon 28 (foot & ankle), the dental/spine JV — to lift the blended growth rate above the sleepy knee/hip core; (2) robotics + ZBEdge data to defend and pull through implants; and (3) margin/FCF expansion. The strategy is essentially "buy growth, integrate, harvest cash."

## Situation Summary — why it's beaten down, and the catch

Stock is ~16% off its 52-week high and trades at **~10.4x FY2026 adjusted EPS** ($8.40–8.55 guidance, raised after a Q1 beat) — versus a ~13–15x historical range and far below Stryker's premium multiple. On the surface that's a quality medtech name on sale.

**The catch worth stating plainly:** the "cheap on forward P/E" headline is mostly the GAAP-vs-adjusted gap. **GAAP diluted EPS has *fallen* three years running: $4.88 (2023) → $4.43 (2024) → $3.55 (2025)** even as revenue rose from $7.4B to $8.2B. Adjusted EPS strips out ~$4.50/share of acquisition amortization and integration costs — aggressive add-backs for a company whose growth *is* serial acquisition. The honest anchors:
- **FCF ~$1.0–1.1B / 193M shares ≈ $5.40/sh → ~6.1% FCF yield** at $88 (the cleanest valuation peg).
- **EV/EBITDA ~9.4x**, P/B **1.35x** (limited downside — trades near book).
- **ROE 6%, ROA 4%** — low, because the acquisition-built goodwill/intangibles haven't earned their cost of capital. This is the value-trap tell: ZBH has *looked* cheap for years and stayed cheap.
- **Net debt ~$7.1B** ($7.6B debt vs $0.48B cash), levering FCF; manageable but it caps buybacks.
- Defensive: **beta 0.47**, 1.1% dividend, 25% payout (room to grow).

## Bull vs Bear

**Bull.** Demographic tailwind is durable (knee/hip volumes compound with an aging population). At 10x adjusted earnings / 6% FCF yield / 1.35x book, expectations are already low — you're paid to wait. Q1 beat + raised guidance suggests the procedure environment is healthy and Paragon 28 is additive. If ROSA robotics and foot & ankle lift organic growth toward 4–5% and integration restores margin, a re-rate to 13x on a higher EPS base is a clean double-digit return with a defensive beta.

**Bear.** ZBH is the structural laggard in a no-growth oligopoly. Organic growth of 2–3% barely beats inflation; the company masks it with debt-funded M&A that dilutes margin, balloons amortization, and crushes GAAP earnings and ROIC. Stryker keeps winning robotics; pricing pressure runs up to ~100 bps/yr; tariffs and FX are live headwinds. The stock has been "cheap" for half a decade — the multiple is low *because* the market correctly prices a serial under-earner. Buying a value trap at 10x is still buying a value trap.

## Verdict

**WATCH.** This is a textbook "cheap, defensive, low-quality-growth" medtech: real downside protection (1.35x book, 6% FCF yield, 0.47 beta), but ~2–3% organic growth, falling GAAP earnings, sub-cost-of-capital ROIC, and a competitive position that keeps it the discounted #2/#3. The ~+15% scenario EV doesn't compensate for the value-trap history versus higher-conviction ideas. Not a PASS — the asymmetry (limited downside) is attractive — but it needs a cheaper entry or evidence the organic-growth/ROIC trend has actually inflected before it's a BUY.

- **Entry of interest:** ~$76–78 (≈9x adjusted EPS, ~7% FCF yield, near book) — where downside is well-covered and a re-rate is mostly free.
- **Thesis-break / avoid:** organic constant-currency growth falling back below ~2%, or another debt-funded acquisition that re-levers without lifting ROIC — confirms value trap; pass.
- **What would change my mind to BUY:** two consecutive quarters of organic CC growth ≥4% *with* margin expansion (Paragon 28 + ROSA actually inflecting the franchise), or a price near $76 that prices in the bear case.
