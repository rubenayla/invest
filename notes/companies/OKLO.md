# Oklo Inc. (OKLO)

**Sector:** Utilities | **Industry:** Independent Power Producers (Advanced Nuclear)
**Price:** $48.57 | **Market Cap:** $8.4B | **EV:** $7.1B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-06

## Situation Summary

Oklo is a pre-revenue advanced nuclear company developing the Aurora Powerhouse, a liquid metal-cooled fast reactor (15-75 MWe per unit) targeting AI data center power demand. The company scored a transformative deal with Meta in January 2026 to develop a 1.2 GW nuclear campus in Pike County, Ohio, with first phase targeted for 2030 and full capacity by 2034. Meta prepays for power, providing early capital for fuel procurement. In March 2026, Oklo received two DOE nuclear safety design agreements (NSDA) and an NRC materials license, marking concrete regulatory progress after its original NRC application was denied in 2022. Its subsidiary Atomic Alchemy is targeting reactor criticality by July 4, 2026 under the DOE Reactor Pilot Program at INL -- a potential near-term catalyst. The stock is down 75% from its $194 all-time high but up ~180% from its 52-week low of $17.42. Sam Altman stepped down as chairman in April 2025 to avoid conflicts of interest with OpenAI, but retains a ~2.6% stake. The company ended 2025 with $1.4B in cash and marketable securities after a $1.18B capital raise.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Wall Street rates OKLO a "Buy" (14 Buy, 10 Hold, 0 Sell) with a $91 mean price target, implying ~87% upside. The consensus sees Oklo as a high-risk, high-reward bet on next-gen nuclear for AI data centers, with the Meta deal providing legitimacy. However, the market discounts heavily for: (1) zero revenue with first commercial operations not expected before 2027-2028, (2) history of NRC denial in 2022, (3) massive cash burn acceleration ($80-100M guided for 2026 OpEx alone, plus CapEx), and (4) inevitable dilution from ATM offerings ($1.5B authorized).
- **Our view:** At $48.57 ($8.4B market cap), OKLO is priced as a speculative option on the nuclear-AI thesis. The key question is not whether the thesis is right -- it almost certainly is directionally correct that AI needs nuclear power -- but whether OKLO is the right vehicle at this price. The 14 GW customer pipeline and Meta deal are real, but first revenue is 2-4 years away, the NRC COLA review will take 2-3 years, and significant dilution is baked in. The stock is trading at 5.3x book value for a company that is 100% pre-revenue. However, the Atomic Alchemy criticality milestone (July 4, 2026) is an underappreciated near-term catalyst that could validate the technology and de-risk the thesis.
- **Trigger:** (1) Atomic Alchemy/Groves reactor criticality by July 4, 2026 (binary event -- first proof of technology), (2) NRC COLA acceptance for formal review (expected late 2026/early 2027), (3) Additional hyperscaler customer announcements.

## Inflection Point

**Approaching but not yet reached.** Several potential inflection points are converging in 2026:

1. **Atomic Alchemy criticality (July 4, 2026 target):** This would be the first proof that Oklo's technology works -- a genuine technology validation inflection. The Groves isotope test reactor at INL is on track. This is a BINARY EVENT.

2. **NRC COLA submission (late 2025/early 2026):** Already submitted; NRC is reviewing. The pre-application readiness assessment was completed successfully in July 2025 with no significant gaps identified. This is the most important long-term milestone -- without NRC approval, nothing else matters.

3. **Meta deal execution (2026-2030):** Pre-construction and site characterization beginning in 2026, with the Centrus/Oklo HALEU JV at Piketon securing fuel supply. This is the commercialization inflection.

**Key risk:** The 2022 NRC denial shows these inflection points can fail. If criticality is delayed or the COLA is rejected, the stock could reprice dramatically downward.

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: **MEDIUM**

OKLO is a fascinating speculative bet on the intersection of nuclear power and AI infrastructure, backed by legitimate technology, a transformative Meta deal, and a well-funded balance sheet. However, at $48.57 ($8.4B market cap) for a company with zero revenue, a history of NRC denial, and 4+ years until first commercial operations, the risk/reward is not compelling enough for a BUY today. The expected value calculation is essentially breakeven (+0.3%) with enormous variance. The 12.2% short interest reflects rational skepticism about execution timeline and dilution risk.

The critical near-term catalyst is the Atomic Alchemy criticality target (July 4, 2026). This is a genuine binary event: success validates the technology and could trigger a significant re-rating; failure would revive the 2022 NRC denial narrative.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:**
1. Successful criticality demonstration (July 4, 2026) at a price below $60 -- this would de-risk the technology thesis
2. NRC COLA formal acceptance for review with no significant deficiencies
3. Stock pullback to $25-35 range (near book value), which would provide margin of safety for the speculative thesis
4. Any combination of additional hyperscaler deals + regulatory milestones that shorten the path to first revenue

**Would downgrade to PASS on:**
1. Criticality failure or significant delay (past 2027)
2. NRC COLA rejection or major deficiency findings
3. Cash burn exceeding $200M/year without corresponding milestone progress
4. Stock rally above $80 without fundamental de-risking (pure momentum)
