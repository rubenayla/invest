# Atlassian Corporation (TEAM)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Application Software (work-management / dev collaboration SaaS)
**Price:** $101.53 (2026-06-04) | **Market Cap:** ~$25.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-04
**Verdict:** BUY (scale-in) — quality compounder that the 2025 "AI kills seat-based SaaS" panic cut in half; its **Q3 FY26 print (May 1) refuted the thesis** — revenue *re-accelerated* to +32% (sharpest in 6 quarters) and its AI product (Rovo) is monetizing. Still ~15x EV/FCF for a 25–30% grower with an 85% gross margin and a near-monopoly franchise. Caveat: the bounce off the $57 low has already run ~+77%, so this is a scale-in at a reasonable price, not a bottom-tick.

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Atlassian builds the software teams use to plan and track work. The crown jewels are **Jira** (issue/project tracking — the de-facto standard for software engineering teams), **Confluence** (team wiki/knowledge base), and **Jira Service Management** (IT service desk / ITSM). Around these sit Bitbucket (git), Trello (lightweight boards), Loom (async video), Compass, and now **Rovo** (their AI layer / agents). The company sells almost entirely **bottoms-up** — developers and teams adopt it without a sales call — and has historically spent far less on sales & marketing than peers because the products spread organically. Revenue is ~85% gross margin, overwhelmingly subscription, and split across **Cloud** (the growth engine), **Data Center** (self-hosted, the cash cow being migrated to Cloud), and Marketplace/services.

**Why it works (the moat).** This is a **wide moat** built on switching costs and a workflow network effect. Jira and Confluence become the system of record for how an organization works — every ticket, sprint, runbook, and decision lives there, wired into hundreds of third-party integrations via the Atlassian Marketplace (one of the largest enterprise app ecosystems). Ripping it out means retraining every team and rebuilding years of accumulated process. Net revenue retention runs comfortably above 110% because existing customers keep adding seats, products, and (now) AI credits. The Marketplace itself is a moat competitors can't clone overnight.

**Why it got cheap (the de-rating).** Through 2025 the entire application-software group was repriced on a single fear: **AI coding agents (Cursor, Copilot, Claude Code, etc.) shrink developer headcount → fewer Jira/Confluence seats → seat-based SaaS revenue erodes structurally.** "Why pay per-seat for a wiki when an AI writes and reads it?" For a company priced as a 30% compounder, even a whiff of seat erosion justifies a brutal multiple reset. TEAM fell from a **$221 high (July 2025) to a $57 low (April 10, 2026)** — a ~74% drawdown — despite the actual numbers never breaking.

**The turn.** On **May 1, 2026** Atlassian reported **Q3 FY26**: revenue **$1.787B, +32% YoY** (the fastest growth in at least six quarters), Cloud revenue **$1.13B, +29%**, non-GAAP EPS **$1.75 vs ~$0.98 consensus** (an ~80% beat), and **Jira Service Management ARR crossing $1B**. Management *raised* full-year Cloud (+26.5%) and Data Center (+21.5%) growth guidance. Crucially, **Rovo** (their AI product) is compounding usage **>20% month-over-month**, and Rovo customers are growing ARR at roughly **2x** the rate of non-Rovo customers. The stock gapped from $69 to $89 (+30%) and has since drifted to $101. The feared dynamic — AI hollowing out seats — is so far showing up as the *opposite*: AI is driving credit consumption and cross-sell.

## Situation Summary

TEAM sits at **$101.53, ~+77% off its 52-week low ($57.2, April 10) but still −54% below its 52-week high ($220.9, July 2025).** The most recent print was a beat-and-raise that re-accelerated the top line and validated AI monetization, yet the stock still trades at roughly **15x EV/FCF and ~17x forward non-GAAP earnings** — multiples normally reserved for low-double-digit growers, not a 25–30% compounder with 85% gross margins. You are buying a wide-moat franchise after the bear thesis took its best shot and missed, with the multiple still sitting near the bottom of its historical range.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** AI is an existential, not incremental, threat to seat-based developer SaaS. Even if Atlassian grows now, the terminal value is impaired — agents will read/write Jira and Confluence so humans need fewer (or no) seats, net revenue retention rolls over, and a 30% grower decays into a 10% grower. Hence ~15x cash flow for a name that traded at 30–50x for most of its life.
- **Our view:** Atlassian is positioned to be an **AI beneficiary, not a victim.** Its moat is the *proprietary corpus of work data* (every ticket, doc, and workflow) that AI agents need as context — Rovo sells access to that corpus and charges for agent/credit consumption, a usage-based revenue stream layered *on top of* seats. Q3 is the first hard datapoint and it points up: growth re-accelerated, Rovo customers expand ARR ~2x faster, and Service Management (ITSM) — a seat-light, workflow-heavy category — just crossed $1B ARR. The market is paying a sub-20% grower's multiple for a re-accelerating 30% grower.
- **Trigger:** Each quarter that Cloud growth holds ≥25% and Rovo usage/credit revenue compounds rebuilds the bull narrative and re-rates the multiple. Net revenue retention staying >110% and AI agents driving *more* (not fewer) paid workflows is the proof. The Data-Center-to-Cloud migration is a multi-year ARPU tailwind independent of the AI debate.

**The honest caveat (SBC).** Headline FCF (~$1.44B) is real cash, but **stock-based compensation (~$1.36B) is nearly as large** — i.e. much of the "free" cash flow is funded by diluting shareholders. On a strict SBC-adjusted basis, owner earnings are far thinner than the 5.6% headline FCF yield implies. This is the single best bear argument and is *why* the market discounts Atlassian's cash flow. The bull case requires SBC to fall as a % of revenue over time (operating leverage on an 85%-margin base) while FCF compounds — plausible at 30% growth, but not yet proven. Size accordingly.

Anchor on non-GAAP EPS and EV/FCF, haircutting for SBC. At $101.53 the stock is ~17x forward non-GAAP EPS and ~15x EV/FCF — both near the low end of Atlassian's historical range (it spent most of its life at 30–50x FCF). Horizon: ~2–3 years (to FY28).

The Street mean target is **$143 (~+41%)** across 31 analysts (range $81–$480); the central case is roughly our base scenario, and the $480 high pin reflects how violently this re-rates if the AI-tailwind thesis is confirmed.

## Entry / Exit Framework

- **Entry:** ~$101 (current), **scale-in**. The bottom ($57) is gone and the easy post-earnings bounce is captured, so phase the position. Add toward **$80–85** (the post-earnings gap-fill / rising 200-day region) on any market-driven pullback, or after a second consecutive quarter of ≥25% Cloud growth confirms the re-acceleration is durable.
- **Thesis-break price / signals:** The thesis breaks if **Cloud revenue growth decelerates below ~18% for two consecutive quarters**, **net revenue retention drops toward/under 105%**, or **Rovo usage/credit growth stalls** — any of which would mean AI is eroding seats faster than Atlassian can monetize agents. A close back below the **pre-earnings $69 base** on deteriorating fundamentals (not just market beta) is the hard exit; treat ~$70 as the line where the bear case is winning.
- **What would change my mind (either way):** *Bullish confirmation* — Rovo disclosed as a standalone revenue line growing triple digits, SBC/revenue trending down, NRR re-accelerating. *Bearish* — management softening Cloud guidance, seat counts flattening at large accounts, or competitors (Linear, Notion, GitHub Projects + Copilot) showing share gains in net-new logos.

## Bottom Line

Atlassian is a wide-moat, 85%-gross-margin, ~30%-growing franchise that the market priced for AI-driven decline — and the first hard quarter after the panic showed *acceleration*, not erosion, with its own AI product (Rovo) monetizing visibly. At ~15x EV/FCF / ~17x forward earnings it's cheap *for the growth*, with the honest asterisk that heavy stock-based comp makes the headline cash-flow yield flatter than reality. Risk/reward at $101 is favorable (~−23% bear vs +62% weighted, +131% bull), but the run off the low argues for a **scale-in, not an all-at-once**. **BUY (scale-in), MEDIUM conviction.**
