# Automatic Data Processing, Inc. (ADP)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Software - Application (HCM/Payroll)
**Price:** $208 | **Market Cap:** $84B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-19

## Situation Summary

ADP is the world's largest payroll and HCM (Human Capital Management) provider, serving 1M+ clients globally with 67,000 employees. The stock has fallen ~37% from its 52-week high of $330, driven by (1) slowing "pays per control" volume growth (essentially flat Y/Y in Q1 FY26), (2) declining FCF margins in PEO segment, (3) macro employment weakness (ADP's own payroll report showed only +37K jobs in one recent month), and (4) AI disruption fears. Meanwhile, the underlying business remains rock-solid: FY25 revenue grew 7%, operating margins expanded for the 4th straight year to 26.3%, and the company raised FY26 guidance after a Q2 beat. ADP announced a $6B buyback and launched an AI agent marketplace in March 2026. Annual dividend of $6.80/share (3.3% yield), a 50-year Dividend King.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Hold. 14 analysts, median PT $272. Market sees a high-quality but slow-growth compounder now priced more fairly after the selloff. Concerns center on employment cycle risk, AI disruption to payroll processing, and PEO margin pressure. Short interest negligible at 2.9%.
- **Our view:** The market is OVER-penalizing cyclical slowdown in a business with structural advantages. ADP's switching costs are enormous (payroll errors are career-ending for CFOs), compliance complexity creates regulatory moats, and the company processes $3.3T+ in annual wages. The AI "threat" is actually an opportunity -- ADP's new AI Marketplace positions it as the platform layer, not the disrupted layer. At 20x trailing PE with 7-8% revenue growth, 11%+ EPS growth, and 3.3% dividend yield, this is a quality compounder at a rare discount.
- **Trigger:** Employment data stabilization (watch monthly ADP Employment Reports) + FY26 Q3 earnings confirmation of sustained margin expansion. If pays-per-control reaccelerates even modestly, the multiple re-rates quickly.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- valuation reset creating entry opportunity in a quality compounder.**

- **Multiple compression**: Stock traded at 30-35x PE for years; now at 20x trailing, 17x forward. This is the cheapest ADP has been since the COVID crash in 2020.
- **Employment cycle trough**: Pays-per-control went flat, but this is cyclical, not structural. Any stabilization in hiring = volume tailwind.
- **AI as platform play**: March 2026 AI Marketplace launch positions ADP as the hub for HR-tech AI agents, not the victim. This could expand TAM and increase ARPU.
- **FCF inflection**: FCF grew 22% Y/Y in FY25 to $4.4B. $6B buyback at these prices is highly accretive.

**Caution**: If employment deteriorates further (recession scenario), pays-per-control could decline, and PEO margins may compress further. The new $5.7B in net debt needs monitoring.

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: MODERATE

ADP is a best-in-class compounder trading at its cheapest valuation in 6 years. The business quality score of 21/25 reflects one of the widest moats in software -- switching costs, regulatory complexity, and scale that no competitor can replicate. Revenue has grown every single year, margins are expanding (not contracting), and FCF conversion is excellent. The 37% drawdown is driven by cyclical employment fears and AI narrative anxiety, neither of which threatens ADP's structural position. At 20x trailing PE with a 3.3% dividend yield and 4.1% FCF yield, you're getting a Dividend King at a recession-like discount without an actual recession in earnings.

Expected value of +17.2% with a quality floor (the dividend yield alone is compelling). The $6B buyback at these prices is a strong signal from management. Risk/reward is clearly skewed to the upside.

**Position sizing note:** Low beta (0.85), defensive characteristics. Suitable for a 4-6% portfolio allocation as a core holding.
