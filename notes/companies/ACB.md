# Aurora Cannabis (ACB)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Drug Manufacturers - Specialty & Generic
**Price:** $3.28 (2026-04-23) | **Market Cap:** $194M
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-23

## Situation Summary

Aurora Cannabis has executed a dramatic strategic pivot away from the Canadian recreational market — where it was chronically unprofitable and dilutive — into global medical cannabis (Germany, Poland, UK, and Australia). Q3 FY2026 (Feb 2026) showed record medical revenue of $76.2M (+12% YoY, +17% internationally), with adjusted EBITDA of $18.5M and $154M cash with zero cannabis business debt. The company is guiding FY2026 medical revenue of $269–$281M and consolidated EBITDA of $52–$57M. Germany's pharmacies channel post-reclassification in 2024 has been the demand catalyst; Poland is scaling quickly. This is no longer the debt-laden, dilution machine of 2020–2022 — but the share count has ballooned ~10x since IPO and the stock sits near 52-week lows even as fundamentals are improving meaningfully.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** ACB is a burned cannabis name with history of catastrophic dilution, impairments, and failed Canadian rec market bets. Market prices it as near-terminal (0.47x book, 0.52x sales). Most investors permanently written off the whole sector.
- **Our view:** The transformation to a European medical-focused business is real and underappreciated. 69% gross margins in global medical, positive EBITDA, zero cannabis debt, $154M cash vs $194M market cap — the downside is substantially covered. Market is paying essentially nothing for the medical growth optionality.
- **Trigger:** Continued Germany/Poland ramp showing sustained double-digit growth over 2-3 more quarters; or FY2026 results confirming EBITDA guidance ($52–57M vs current EV of $239M = ~4-5x EV/EBITDA at midpoint). An EBITDA number in that range would be undeniable.

## Inflection Point

Yes — but it happened already and the stock hasn't followed. FY2025 (Mar 2025) was the first year with positive EBIT ($29M), near-breakeven FCF (-$3M), and tiny net profit ($1.6M). Q3 FY2026 shows the inflection deepening: $154M cash, EBITDA ~$18.5M/quarter, 69% gross margins in medical. The inflection evidence is observable — the question is whether it sustains or if Germany regulatory shifts (cannabis reclassification reversal, pharmacy channel policy changes) disrupt the runway.

## Bull Case

- Germany/Poland medical cannabis market grows 15–20%/year through FY2028; Aurora captures disproportionate share due to first-mover advantage and EU-GMP production credentials
- FY2026 EBITDA of $52–57M confirmed → at 8x EV/EBITDA, fair EV = $416–$456M → equity ~$350M+ = $5–6/share
- Cash pile ($154M vs $194M mkt cap) limits downside; company could buyback 79% of float at current prices
- Israel/Poland/UK additional market licenses provide revenue diversification; macro tailwinds from European medical cannabis expansion
- Short interest likely elevated in cannabis names — any positive catalyst triggers outsized moves

## Bear Case

- **Disclosed but under-covered (from earnings):** Plant propagation segment (Thrive) is non-core and creates earnings noise; management has not guided when it will be divested or optimized. It suppresses consolidated margins and complicates valuation.
- Chronic dilution history means any capital need (acquisition, capacity expansion, cash burn period) would hit existing shareholders hard; share count has already grown ~10x since IPO
- Germany's cannabis regulatory environment remains fluid — if the medical-to-recreational reclassification shifts again and medical channel demand migrates to lower-margin recreational, Aurora's 69% medical margins collapse
- Canadian business is essentially a zero-growth cash drag; any resurgence of competitive pressure there could turn positive EBIT negative
- Cannabis sector permanently uninvestable for most institutional capital — lack of re-rating catalyst outside sector-specific retail buyers

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: LOW-MEDIUM

The turnaround is real and the valuation is deeply discounted (0.47x book, $194M market cap vs $154M cash, EV/EBITDA ~4-5x on guidance). But the quality score (9/25) reflects the catastrophic capital allocation history and genuine uncertainty about whether 69% medical margins are durable as Germany's cannabis regulatory picture evolves. The variant perception is sound — market hasn't credited the transformation — but the management trust deficit and sector-wide institutional avoidance cap conviction.

**Would upgrade to BUY if:** Q4 FY2026 confirms EBITDA within guided range ($52–57M), medical revenue shows sustained >10% YoY, and cash stays above $120M — validating that the business model has genuinely shifted. Entry at $3.00–3.30 range on any market weakness.

**If WATCH:** Trigger to BUY = confirmed annual EBITDA > $50M with flat/growing cash balance. Trigger to PASS = two consecutive quarters of medical revenue decline or cash burn exceeding $20M/quarter.
