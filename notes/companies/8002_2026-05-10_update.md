# Marubeni (8002.T) — Trim/Hold Decision

**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Position:** ~$6,950 (1.1M JPY), 14% portfolio, **+142.6% gain**
**Current price:** ~5,418 JPY | Market cap ~9.77T JPY

## Verdict

**TRIM 25%** — book ~$1,740 of gains, redeploy into a less crowded sizing. Keep 75% (~$5,200, ~10.5% portfolio weight) to ride the Berkshire-just-crossed-10% catalyst and the still-intact yen tailwind. This is a real catalyst week, not the time for a full exit.

This materially weakens the bear "fully priced" argument.

## Three Reasons to Trim

1. **+143% gain at 14% weight is just too much rope.** Position sizing rules (max 15%) say trim on principle alone — let the gain become a sizing problem and one bad print costs disproportionately. Kelly framework with crowded consensus + thin remaining edge argues for ~8-11% target weight.
2. **Earnings near cycle peak.** Revenue down 2.9% CAGR over 3yrs; net income growth is margin-and-buyback, not organic. Energy/grain prices soft. FY2027 (Mar 2027) guidance — the next big catalyst — is more likely flat than blowout given Q3 FY2026 EPS missed by 27%.
3. **JPY appreciation risk is now real.** BOJ is the only major central bank tightening into 2026. Even though spot is 155-156 today (intervention noise), the directional bias is yen-stronger. A move from 155 to 140 cuts USD-denominated portfolio value 10% without the stock moving an inch.

## Three Reasons to Hold (Most of It)

1. **Berkshire just crossed 10% on Marubeni and Sumitomo** (week ending May 9, 2026). That ceiling was the prior cap. Crossing it is a fresh signal of commitment from the buyer who set the floor — exactly the catalyst the prior bear case said was unlikely. Selling into this is selling into news that strengthens the thesis.
2. **Peer-relative valuation is not stretched.** 9.0x P/E and 1.13x P/B is cheap, not crowded-trade pricing. Itochu trades at 1.8x P/B with better ROE; if Marubeni's ROE keeps drifting toward 15%, the gap closes via Marubeni rising, not Itochu falling.
3. **Capital return acceleration intact.** Dividend hiked to ~115 JPY (forward yield ~2.1%), 85B JPY buyback in flight, ROE 13.9% and climbing. Shareholder yield ~3% plus mid-single-digit EPS growth from buyback mechanics gives a 6-8% baseline return even with zero multiple expansion.

## JPY Consideration

At entry vs now, a portion of the +143% is JPY translation. Yen was ~150 a year ago, ~155 today — translation is mildly negative recently, not positive. So the +143% is almost entirely price-driven (Marubeni 1,878 → 5,418 = +188% in JPY, partially offset by JPY weakening into USD). On forward basis: BOJ tightening + Fed neutral = USD/JPY compresses toward 140-145 by Q4 2026, which is a **headwind to USD-denominated returns** worth ~7-10%. This argues for trimming exposure but not eliminating it; the underlying business benefits operationally from weak yen on overseas earnings translation, so there's a partial natural hedge inside the stock itself.

## Why Not 33% or 50%

- 33%+ trim implies the thesis is broken. It isn't — Berkshire just doubled down by crossing 10%.
- 50%/full exit would be right if peer valuation showed Marubeni as the most expensive shosha. It doesn't; it's middle-of-pack to cheap.
- 25% addresses the **sizing** problem (14% → 10.5%) without overreacting to a catalyst that just got stronger.
