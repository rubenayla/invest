**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Software - Application

## Situation Summary

Intuit is the dominant US financial software platform, owning TurboTax (#1 consumer tax), QuickBooks (#1 SMB accounting), Credit Karma (consumer finance/credit), Mailchimp (email marketing), and the nascent Intuit Enterprise Suite (mid-market ERP). The stock is trading at $447, down ~45% from its $814 all-time high in late 2025. The selloff is driven by a broad software sector de-rating on AI disruption fears, analyst downgrades (Wells Fargo cut to Equal Weight), and concerns that FY2025's strong growth set an impossibly high bar. Despite the price collapse, fundamentals are excellent: FY2025 (ended Jul 2025) delivered $18.8B revenue (+15.6% YoY), $3.9B net income (+31% YoY), and $6.1B FCF. Q2 FY2026 (ended Jan 2026) beat estimates with $4.65B revenue (+17% YoY) and non-GAAP EPS of $4.15 vs $3.66 expected. Full-year FY2026 guidance of $21.0-21.2B revenue (12-13% growth) and $22.98-23.18 non-GAAP EPS (14-15% growth) was reiterated. The company has signed major AI partnerships with both Anthropic and OpenAI and is pushing aggressively into mid-market ERP, where QBO Advanced and Intuit Enterprise Suite grew 40% in Q2.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** INTU is a high-quality compounder, but the stock deserves a lower multiple in an AI-disrupted world. The fear: AI agents could eventually replace TurboTax for simple returns, and ChatGPT-style tools could erode QuickBooks' value proposition. Wells Fargo downgraded, arguing FY2025 was peak growth. Analysts still rate it Strong Buy with a ~$690 mean target, but the stock trades 35% below that target, reflecting real skepticism about whether premium software multiples are justified.
- **Our view:** The market is overcorrecting. INTU is not a passive victim of AI disruption -- it IS the AI disruptor in its categories. The Anthropic and OpenAI partnerships, Intuit Assist AI assistant, and "done-for-you" agentic tax filing are offense, not defense. The real story is the mid-market ERP expansion (40% growth, expanding sales force by 30%) which opens a massive new TAM against legacy players like Sage and NetSuite. At 17x forward P/E and 4.9% FCF yield, INTU is trading at its cheapest valuation in a decade for a business compounding revenue at 14% CAGR with 80% gross margins. The IRS Direct File program was killed in Nov 2025, removing the most credible competitive threat to TurboTax. Mailchimp is a drag (sub-10% growth) but only ~10% of revenue and expected to reaccelerate.
- **Trigger:** (1) FY2026 guidance raise as Q2 beat flows through, (2) mid-market ERP partnerships gaining visible traction, (3) broader software sector re-rating as AI panic subsides and actual AI monetization emerges.

## Inflection Point

**Partial -- AI monetization inflection beginning.** INTU is at the early stages of an AI-driven product transformation that could expand margins and TAM simultaneously. The partnerships with Anthropic and OpenAI, the launch of agentic "done-for-you" tax filing, and the AI-native ERP for mid-market are all inflection signals. The mid-market ERP push (40% growth, 30% sales force expansion, partnerships with Rehmann, Cherry Bekaert, Aprio) is a genuine new growth vector against a $50B+ TAM. However, this is early-stage -- the inflection has not yet been proven in sustained revenue acceleration. The valuation reset itself is an inflection of sorts: INTU has gone from 45x forward P/E to 17x, creating a setup where even moderate execution re-rates the stock.

## Bull Case

- AI-driven "done-for-you" experiences cannibalize the assisted tax market (H&R Block stores, CPAs for simple returns), expanding TurboTax's TAM from DIY to assisted
- Mid-market ERP grows into a $3-5B revenue line as Intuit Enterprise Suite displaces Sage, legacy QuickBooks Desktop, and competes with NetSuite
- Mailchimp reaccelerates to double-digit growth with AI-powered marketing automation
- Operating margins expand to 30%+ as AI reduces support costs and automates workflows
- Software sector re-rates as AI monetization proves accretive rather than destructive; INTU returns to 30x+ forward P/E

## Bear Case

- AI commoditizes tax filing: free AI tax agents from Google/OpenAI/startups erode TurboTax pricing power
- QuickBooks faces disruption from AI-native accounting tools that don't require Intuit's complex software
- Mid-market ERP fails to gain traction against entrenched NetSuite/Sage competitors
- Mailchimp continues to stagnate, dragging overall growth below 10%
- Revenue growth decelerates to single digits, and the market assigns a utility-like 15x multiple permanently

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: **HIGH**

INTU is a rare opportunity to buy a dominant, wide-moat software compounder at a decade-low valuation. At 17x forward P/E and 4.9% FCF yield, you are paying a below-market multiple for a business with 80% gross margins, 14% revenue CAGR, $6.1B FCF, a fortress balance sheet (0.64x net debt/EBITDA), and #1 market positions in tax and SMB accounting. The 45% drawdown is driven by macro sentiment (software de-rating on AI fears), not fundamental deterioration -- Q2 FY2026 just beat estimates on both top and bottom line. The AI disruption narrative is backwards: INTU is the disruptor, not the disrupted, with partnerships spanning Anthropic and OpenAI and an AI-native ERP pushing into mid-market. The IRS Direct File program is dead. All trusted valuation models (GBM, autoresearch) see 15-45% upside. The expected value calculation shows +27% with asymmetric upside. The main risk is permanent software multiple compression, but at 17x forward P/E, much of that is already priced in.
