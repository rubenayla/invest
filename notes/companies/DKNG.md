# DraftKings (DKNG)

**Sector:** Consumer Discretionary | **Industry:** Online sports betting (OSB) + iGaming operator (US-focused)
**Price:** $26.32 (2026-06-17) | **Market Cap:** ~$13.1B | **Shares:** ~496M | **Net Debt:** ~$0.9B (cash $1.0B / debt $1.9B)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-18
**Standalone rating: WATCH** (lean BUY only under ~$22) — a real growth-inflection business (just turned profitable, 76% gross margin) at a now-reasonable ~15x forward EPS, but facing a genuinely hard-to-handicap structural threat (CFTC-regulated prediction markets) stacked on top of rising state betting taxes and a *verified* hard deceleration in handle. Asymmetry is only mildly positive at $26 after a +27% bounce off the March low; it was far more interesting at the $20.72 trough.

## Situation Summary
DraftKings is the #2 US online sportsbook (roughly co-leader with FanDuel/Flutter, ~30%+ combined OSB share) and a fast-growing iGaming (online casino) operator. The business finally crossed into profitability in FY2025 (net income ~breakeven at +$3.7M, after losing $1.4B / $0.8B / $0.5B in 2022/23/24), on the back of revenue compounding hard: **$2.24B → $3.67B → $4.77B → $6.05B (FY2022-25)**. TTM revenue is ~$6.3B, gross margin 76.7%.

The stock has been cut roughly in half — from a **$48.23 peak (Aug 2025)** to a **$20.72 trough (Mar 2026)**, now recovering to **$26.32** (-46% off high, +27% off low). This is not a sentiment wobble; it is a sustained de-rating driven by three overlapping, *partially verified* concerns:

1. **Prediction markets (Kalshi, Polymarket, Robinhood, etc.).** CFTC-regulated event-contract platforms are pulling sports-event volume from licensed sportsbooks. Prediction-market trading volume hit **~$63.5B in 2025, 4x the $15.8B in 2024**. Their structural edge is real: federally regulated (one CFTC license ≈ all 50 states, bypassing slow state-by-state OSB licensing), and winnings can fall under **Section 1256 (60/40 cap-gains)** tax treatment vs. ordinary-income sportsbook winnings. BNP Paribas opened DKNG with a rare **Sell** (the "dramatic threat"); MoffettNathanson cut to **Neutral, $27 PT** (Apr 2026).
2. **State tax increases.** Illinois (per-wager tax), New Jersey, and others are raising betting taxes; 2026 guidance explicitly does **not** assume further hikes, so this is unhedged downside to EBITDA.
3. **A real growth cut.** FY2026 revenue guided to **$6.5-6.9B (mid $6.7B)** — *below the lowest analyst estimate* and far under the ~$7.3B consensus; shares dropped 18% after hours on the print. FY2025 ($6.1B) also missed management's own $6.3-6.6B internal target. Most damning: **January 2026 handle grew only +4% YoY, vs +11% in January 2025** — a hard, observable deceleration regardless of cause.

CEO Jason Robins counters that the prediction-market impact "has been de minimis" and concentrated in low-margin customers, and notes several states have issued cease-and-desist orders to Kalshi (i.e., the regulatory tide may turn against the disruptors).

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Prediction markets are eating sports betting's lunch, and they have a permanent tax and regulatory-arbitrage advantage. DKNG's hyper-growth story is over (handle +4%, guidance below the low end), state taxes are climbing, and the path to the once-promised multi-billion EBITDA is now in doubt. A formerly-priced-for-perfection growth stock losing its growth — avoid."
- **Our view (cautious pushback):** At $26 you are paying ~15x forward EPS and ~2.1x EV/sales for the #2 operator in a still-legalizing US market with 76% gross margins and a freshly-positive bottom line. The prediction-market threat is loud but unresolved — multiple states are actively trying to *ban* them, which (if it sticks) protects DKNG's licensed moat and could trigger a relief re-rate. iGaming (higher-margin online casino) is the bigger long-term prize and is barely legal in a handful of states; each new state legalization is upside not in the price. This is no longer a priced-for-perfection stock.
- **Why it is NOT a buy here:** The single most important number is the **+4% handle growth** — that is *verified data*, not narrative, and it says the US OSB market is maturing and/or being skimmed *right now*. The premium-multiple growth thesis that supported $48 is structurally over; the question is only whether DKNG settles as a low-teens grower (fine at 15x) or a stalling one (expensive at 15x). The prediction-market tax/regulatory edge is genuinely structural and may not be legislated away. After a +27% bounce, the easy money (the $20.72 trough) is gone, and the EV is only mildly positive with very wide bands.
- **Trigger / what re-rates it:** (a) regulatory clampdown on Kalshi/Polymarket sports contracts that visibly protects licensed-OSB volume; (b) two quarters of handle re-acceleration showing the +4% was a blip; (c) a big new iGaming state legalization (NY would be the prize). Conversely, another guide-down or handle deceleration confirms the bear.

## Bull / Bear

**Bull**
- **Profitability inflection on a high-gross-margin (77%) model** with operating leverage as promotional spend normalizes; the multi-year cash-burn era is over.
- **Reasonable, no-longer-heroic multiple:** ~15x forward EPS, ~2.1x EV/sales for the #2 US OSB operator. The $48 priced-for-perfection setup is gone.
- **iGaming optionality:** online casino is higher-margin and legal in only a few states; each new legalization (NY especially) is upside not in guidance.
- **Regulatory tailwind possible:** multiple states are moving to ban/curb Kalshi-style sports event contracts; a clampdown protects DKNG's licensed moat and could trigger a relief rally.
- **Duopoly economics:** DKNG + FanDuel dominate; scale, brand, and product depth are real switching frictions vs. thin prediction-market order books on niche sports.

**Bear**
- **Verified deceleration:** handle growth +11% → +4% YoY. The growth story that justified the premium is impaired *now*, not hypothetically.
- **Structural disruptor edge:** prediction markets enjoy single-license 50-state reach (CFTC) and Section 1256 tax treatment — advantages that may be durable and are hard to compete away.
- **Unhedged tax risk:** 2026 guidance excludes further state betting-tax hikes; Illinois/NJ-style increases flow straight through thin EBITDA margins.
- **Guidance credibility:** 2026 guide below the lowest analyst estimate, and 2025 missed management's own internal target — a pattern of optimism.
- **Thin profitability + dilution history:** net margin <1%; small revenue/cost surprises swing the bottom line, and SBC/convertibles have historically diluted holders.

## Entry / Exit Framework
- **Entry interest:** under **~$22** (toward the March $20.72 trough), where the bear case is largely priced and the risk/reward turns clearly positive. A confirmed regulatory clampdown on prediction markets *or* two quarters of handle re-acceleration would justify entry higher.
- **Thesis-break (sell/avoid) signals:** another FY guide-down; handle growth flat or negative YoY; clear evidence prediction markets are taking *high-margin* (not just low-margin) volume; a major new state authorizing prediction-market sports contracts.
- **What would change my mind (to BUY):** state-level bans on Kalshi/Polymarket sports event contracts holding up in court, or NY/large-state iGaming legalization — either re-opens the growth runway the market has written off.

## Bottom Line
A de-rated growth-inflection name that is no longer expensive, but sits on top of an unresolved, potentially-structural disruption (prediction markets) plus rising betting taxes and a *real* handle deceleration. The +12% scenario EV with −47%/+75% tails is a **WATCH**, not a buy, at $26.32 after the bounce. Revisit with conviction under ~$22 or on a clear regulatory/handle inflection.
