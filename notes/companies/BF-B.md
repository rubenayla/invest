# Brown-Forman Corporation (BF-B)

**Sector:** Consumer Staples | **Industry:** Distillers & Vintners (Premium Spirits)
**Price:** $26.94 (2026-06-16) | **Market Cap:** ~$12.4B | **Enterprise Value:** ~$14.6B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-16
**Standalone rating: WATCH** (leaning BUY LOW) — wide-moat dividend aristocrat at its cheapest multiple in ~15 years, but revenue is in its third year of decline and the catalyst is "wait for the spirits cycle to turn"

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Brown-Forman is a family-controlled (the Brown family holds the majority of Class A voting stock) American spirits house built around one of the most valuable brands in the world: **Jack Daniel's Tennessee Whiskey**, which is roughly half of company sales. Around it sit Woodford Reserve and Old Forester (premium American whiskey), **Herradura and el Jimador** (tequila), Gin Mare and Fords (gin), Glenglassaugh/Benriach/The GlenDronach (single-malt Scotch), Korbel (sparkling), and a fast-growing **Jack Daniel's ready-to-drink (RTD)** line. FY2026 (year ended 2026-04-30) did **$3.93B of revenue at a ~29% operating margin** — among the very best margins in all of consumer staples.

**Why it has a moat.** Aged whiskey is a structural barrier to entry: you cannot make 7-year Jack Daniel's without having laid down the barrels 7 years ago, and BF-B carries **$2.5B of inventory** (≈63% of equity) precisely because the product is time. On top of the supply moat sits a brand moat — Jack Daniel's commands global pricing power and shelf presence that a new entrant cannot buy. The result is a business that has raised its dividend for **40+ consecutive years** (a Dividend Aristocrat) and historically earned mid-teens-to-high ROE.

**The core problem.** Spirits are in a cyclical downturn and BF-B's revenue has fallen three years running: **$4.23B (FY23) → $4.18B (FY24) → $3.98B (FY25) → $3.93B (FY26)**. Net income fell harder, from **$1.02B (FY24) to $715M (FY26)**, dragging EPS from ~$2.18 to ~$1.52. The market responded by collapsing the multiple from ~30x to ~17x, and the stock from a 2022 peak of ~$68 to **$26.94 today — a ~60% drawdown**, near its 52-week low of $22.61.

## Situation Summary — why it's beaten down, and the catch

Three forces compounded into the de-rating:
1. **Post-pandemic destocking.** Distributors over-ordered during the 2021–22 boom and have spent two years working inventory down — a volume air-pocket on top of normalized demand.
2. **Tariff and trade-war risk.** American whiskey was hit by EU retaliatory tariffs in 2018–21 and is exposed again under the 2025 trade tensions; tequila (Mexico) and imported inputs add more tariff surface. Management explicitly flags **"changes to trade policies, tariffs, and import/export regulations"** as a live risk and is running productivity/tariff-mitigation programs.
3. **The secular bear-case overhang.** GLP-1 weight-loss drugs, "sober-curious" Gen Z, and trading-down are cited as reasons spirits demand may be structurally — not cyclically — lower. This is the question the multiple is really arguing about.

**The catch worth stating plainly:** at 17x trailing / 16x forward earnings, BF-B is no longer *expensive* — but it is not a deep-value screen-pop either. The bargain case rests entirely on **(a)** margins having held at ~29% through the downturn (they did) and **(b)** the revenue decline being cyclical destocking that reverses, rather than the front edge of a secular decline. FY2026 guidance was for a **low-single-digit decline** in organic net sales and operating income — i.e. management itself does not yet see the inflection. You are paid 3.4% to wait, but you are waiting.

## Bull vs Bear

**Bull.** This is a genuine wide-moat compounder on sale for the first time in 15 years. Jack Daniel's is an irreplaceable global brand; 29% operating margins survived a three-year revenue decline intact, proving pricing power. Destocking is a finite headwind that mechanically reverses once distributor inventories normalize, and management already points to **premium whiskey, RTD, and emerging markets** as growing. A Dividend Aristocrat with a 3.4% well-covered yield (60% payout, 7% FCF yield) pays you to hold through the trough. If revenue stabilizes and the cycle turns, EPS recovers toward ~$2.00 and the market re-rates a quality name back toward 22x — that is a 60%+ move plus dividends.

**Bear.** Revenue has fallen three straight years and management guides to a fourth year of decline — calling the bottom here is a forecast, not a fact. If the GLP-1 / younger-cohort drinking-less thesis is real, American whiskey faces secular volume pressure, and 17x is not cheap for a *shrinking* franchise — DEO and STZ (both covered) show how far premium-alcohol multiples can keep falling. A prolonged EU/US tariff war on American whiskey would hit the most profitable export volumes directly. Family control via Class A super-voting stock means minority Class B holders have little say and no activist lever. "Cheapest in 15 years" can stay cheap for years if the earnings base keeps eroding.

## Verdict

**WATCH, leaning BUY LOW.** Brown-Forman is a wide-moat, family-run quality compounder trading at its lowest valuation in ~15 years, with margins that proved their durability through a three-year revenue decline. That is exactly the kind of "quality on sale" the watchlist hunts for — but the honest read is that *management itself guides to another year of decline*, so the entry today ($27, ~19% above the 52-week low) buys the trough thesis at a fair, not bargain, price. The ~+27% total-return EV is good-not-great and leans heavily on a cyclical (not secular) read of the spirits slowdown. It belongs on the radar with a defined cheaper entry, not a chase here.

- **Entry of interest:** ~$23–24 (near the 52-week low, ~15x forward / ~4% yield) — where the bear case is largely priced and the dividend yield cushions the wait.
- **Thesis-break / avoid:** organic net sales decline *accelerating* (worse than low-single-digit) for two more quarters, sustained Jack Daniel's volume decline in core US, or a prolonged EU/US whiskey tariff war — any of these tips "cyclical destocking" into "secular decline" and 17x is then too high.
- **What would change my mind to BUY:** two consecutive quarters of stabilizing-to-growing organic net sales (destocking confirmed over) with margins held at ~29%, or a price near $23 that prices in the bear case.
