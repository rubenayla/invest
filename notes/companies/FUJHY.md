# Subaru Corporation (FUJHY)

## Situation Summary

Subaru reported a Q3 FY2026 (Oct–Dec 2025) operating loss of ¥36.4B ($233M) — its first quarterly loss in years — driven primarily by US tariffs (25% auto tariff enacted April 2025) and yen depreciation. The company now guides full-year operating profit to fall ~68% YoY and quantifies the total FY tariff impact at ¥229B (~$1.4B), up ¥44B from prior guidance. The structural vulnerability is acute: ~70% of Subaru's sales are in the US and roughly half of those vehicles are imported from Japan. Management is responding by shifting Forester and Forester Hybrid production to Indiana, but this de-risking takes 12–18 months to fully take effect. On EV, Subaru is explicitly delaying "full-scale EV mass production investment" citing hybrid demand revival — the Trailseeker BEV (co-developed with Toyota) entered production April 2026 but volumes are small. This is a value-trap candidate if tariffs persist; it becomes a deep-value opportunity if tariffs are reduced or production shifts neutralize the headwind.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Tariff-crushed Japanese automaker with limited pricing power, no EV optionality, and a binary outcome tied to US trade policy. Avoid — too much headline risk, no catalyst.
- **Our view:** The market is pricing in permanent ¥229B tariff drag, but Subaru's Indiana production shift structurally removes ~50% of the US import exposure within 12–18 months. The stock now trades at 0.64x book with $5.9B net cash — enterprise value is actually negative per yfinance — implying investors are pricing in near-zero terminal value for a profitable franchise. Even under a bear scenario of sustained tariffs, the balance sheet provides a floor.
- **Trigger:** (1) Formal US–Japan tariff negotiation progress or exemption announcement (administrations have signaled auto tariff flexibility for allies). (2) Q4 FY2026 earnings (May 2026) showing stabilized losses + Indiana line ramp confirmation. (3) FY2027 guidance restoration to profitability once local production covers Forester/Forester Hybrid volumes.

## Inflection Point

Partially yes — but timing is uncertain. The production shift to Indiana represents a real structural fix: Forester (Subaru's highest-volume US model) is already in Indiana, Forester Hybrid just started US production. Once both Forester variants are fully US-built, approximately 30–40% of the tariff headwind is neutralized organically. The remaining exposure (Outback, Legacy, Crosstrek, Impreza) is harder to localize without massive capex. The inflection trigger is not EV progress — it is the Indiana ramp rate confirmation in FY2027 guidance (expected May 2026 earnings). The stock has been near its 52-week low ($7.65) — the inflection has not yet been priced in.

## Bull Case
- US–Japan trade deal or auto tariff reduction materializes (multiple reports of negotiations in progress); ¥229B drag shrinks to ¥50–80B; EPS recovers to ¥140–160/share equivalent; stock re-rates to 8–10x P/E → $11–14.
- Indiana production ramp eliminates Forester import exposure by Q2 FY2027; operating margin recovers to 7–8%; market re-rates from trough P/B of 0.64x toward 0.9–1.0x book → $10–11.
- Yen weakening: JPY/USD moving from ~142 to ~155 adds ~¥50–70B operating tailwind; partially offsets tariff drag in JPY terms.
- **Hidden risk not in headlines (from primary source data):** FCF dropped from ¥467B to ¥227B YoY (−52%) while capex held at ¥265B — Subaru is investing heavily in factory reconfiguration and Trailseeker EV production simultaneously. This dual capex burden is not widely covered but is already depressing FCF materially, and continues through FY2027.

## Bear Case
- Tariffs persist at 25%+ through FY2027 and US–Japan talks stall; full ¥229B+ annual drag destroys operating profit; dividend is cut to preserve cash; stock drifts to 0.5x book → $5.50.
- **Primary source risk (under-covered):** Management disclosed ¥28B impairment of environmental regulation credits held — a non-cash but structural hit suggesting Subaru had been banking US ZEV credits that are now worthless under policy changes. Future emissions compliance costs in US/EU have not been quantified.
- Indiana production ramp is slower than guided (supply chain, tooling, labor) — tariff drag persists longer than 12–18 months.
- US auto market demand contracts (recession or credit tightening); Subaru's AWD niche loyal but not recession-immune; volume drop of 10–15% compounds margin pressure.
- Toyota-first EV architecture dependency: Trailseeker is a Toyota BZ platform derivative; if Toyota pivots or delays, Subaru's EV roadmap has no independent fallback.

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

Subaru is deeply cheap on a normalized earnings basis (6.8x trailing P/E, 0.64x P/B, 12.9% FCF yield, net cash balance sheet) but is in the middle of a genuine earnings trough from tariffs that could deepen before improving. The 18% expected value is real but the 30% probability bear case (-29%) is not abstract — it materializes if both the production shift AND tariff relief fail to appear within 12 months. The asymmetry is better at this price than at $10.61 (autoresearch implied entry), but the binary nature of the tariff outcome and the single-analyst OTC structure make this a watch-and-confirm rather than immediate buy.

**Upgrade to BUY if:** (1) Q4 FY2026 earnings (May 2026) confirm Indiana ramp is on schedule AND FY2027 guidance implies operating margin recovery above 4%; OR (2) credible US–Japan tariff negotiation news cuts the expected impact below ¥150B. Buy zone: $7.00–$8.50. Thesis-break signal: Indiana delay announcement or tariff escalation above 30%.
