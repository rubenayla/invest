## Business Primer

**What they do.** GE HealthCare is one of the two dominant global medical-imaging companies (with Siemens Healthineers; Philips a distant third). Spun out of GE in January 2023. Four segments on ~$21B revenue (FY25):
- **Imaging** (~$11B) — CT, MRI, X-ray, molecular imaging / PET-CT, image-guided therapy. The flagship capital-equipment franchise.
- **Advanced Visualization Solutions / AVS** (~$5B) — ultrasound and interventional/point-of-care.
- **Patient Care Solutions / PCS** (~$3B) — monitoring, anesthesia, diagnostic cardiology, maternal-infant.
- **Pharmaceutical Diagnostics / PDx** (~$2.4B) — contrast media and radiopharmaceutical tracers; highest-margin segment, consumable/recurring.

**Why it's a moat.** A massive global installed base of imaging hardware (hospitals standardize on a vendor for a decade-plus) drives high-margin recurring **service, software, and consumables** (roughly ~40–45% of revenue is recurring). Switching costs are severe — retraining staff, requalifying workflows, re-integrating PACS. GEHC also holds the **largest FDA-cleared AI-enabled-device portfolio** in the industry, positioning it for the imaging-AI upgrade cycle. ROE ~19%, gross margin ~39%, FCF ~$1.5B+.

**Why it's cheap (the reason for the de-rating).**
1. **Serial margin cuts.** FY26 adjusted-EPS guide was trimmed from **$4.95–5.15** (set at Q4'25) to **$4.80–5.00** at Q1'26 (Apr 29, 2026). The market hates a second cut in a row.
2. **Input-cost inflation.** ~$250M unexpected 2026 cost headwind (≈$0.15 EPS): **memory chips ~$100M, oil/freight ~$100M, metals ~$50M.** Price/cost actions are expected to offset >half, with the larger benefit landing in **2027**.
3. **Tariffs.** US–China trade exposure (GEHC manufactures and sources in China and sells there). Management guides 2026 tariff drag *below* 2025 and assumes **no** IEEPA refund benefit in guidance — i.e. conservatively set.
4. **China softness.** Anti-corruption drag, delayed stimulus, and rising local competition (Mindray, United Imaging). Guidance explicitly bakes a "cautious China" view.
5. **PDx supplier disruption** dinged Q1'26 GAAP profit.
6. **Leverage + token dividend.** ~$8.3B net debt (~2.4x EBITDA); the dividend is a nominal ~0.2% (4% payout) because cash goes to deleveraging — no income-investor support under the stock.

## Situation Summary

- **FY26 guidance:** **adjusted EPS $4.80–5.00** (+4.6% to +9% YoY), **organic revenue +3–4%**, **adjusted EBIT margin 15.4–15.7%** (still +10–40bps expansion *despite* the cost hit).
- **Valuation:** $62.03 / ~$4.90 midpoint = **~12.7x forward**; trailing GAAP P/E ~14.9x; EV/EBITDA ~10.4x; P/B 2.6x. Versus its own 14–18x band and medtech peers at ~18–22x, that's a clear discount.
- **Street:** mean target **$79.72 (+28%)**, range $65–98 (the *low* target sits above today's price); 18 analysts, consensus Buy.

The precise debate: *is the 2026 margin squeeze a transitory input-cost/tariff air-pocket that price actions and 2027 normalization fix — in which case ~12.7x on a moaty imaging compounder is too cheap — or is it the front edge of a structurally lower-margin, China-impaired, slow-growth business that deserves a low-teens multiple?*

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** A serial disappointer. Two guidance cuts in a row, only 3–4% organic growth, levered balance sheet, China structurally weakening, and tariff/cost overhangs with no visible end. A "quality" label doesn't earn a re-rate when management keeps walking numbers down — dead money.
- **Our view:** The market is extrapolating a *demand* collapse from what is plainly a *cost* event. Revenue grew, EBIT margin still **expanded** YoY through the worst of it, and the specific drivers — memory-chip spot prices, ocean freight, metals — are textbook transitory, with management explicitly guiding price/cost catch-up to flip positive in 2027. Guidance was set conservatively (no tariff-refund credit, cautious China). You're paying ~12.7x trough-ish earnings for the #2 global imaging installed base, the industry's largest AI-cleared device portfolio, ~19% ROE, and a multi-year deleveraging runway that eventually frees capital return. The downside is well-bounded: 5% to the 52-week low, defensive beta 0.87, and ~$1.5B FCF.
- **Trigger / what re-rates it:** (a) one *clean* quarter — guidance held or raised — to break the serial-cut narrative; (b) 2027 evidence that price actions are out-running input costs (margin inflecting up); (c) any China stabilization or stimulus-driven capex thaw; (d) deleveraging crossing ~2x → capital-return optionality. The mirror risk that breaks it: a *third* cut, China deteriorating into outright revenue decline, or tariffs re-escalating.

## Entry, Thesis-Break, and What Would Change My Mind

- **Entry:** Starter at ~$62 (within 5% of the 52-week low); scale-in toward **$55–58** (~11–12x trough EPS), where most of the bad news is priced and the risk/reward improves further.
- **Thesis-break (avoid/trim):** a sustained close **below ~$52** *driven by fundamentals* — specifically a **third** guidance cut combined with China turning to outright **revenue decline** (not just soft growth) — would signal the margin/China problems are structural, not cyclical. Price weakness to the low-$50s *without* those is an **add**, not a break.
- **What would raise conviction to BUY (scale-in, A):** (a) one quarter where guidance is **held or raised**; (b) adjusted EBIT margin inflecting clearly upward as price catches cost (2027); (c) China book-to-bill stabilizing; (d) net debt through ~2x with capital-return signaling.
- **What would change my mind to PASS:** evidence the imaging-equipment cycle is structurally de-rating GEHC's pricing (share loss to United Imaging/Mindray globally, not just in China), or margins proving permanently lower because the input-cost step-up is structural rather than transitory.

## Bottom Line

A wide-moat, cash-generative, AI-leading global #2 in medical imaging at **~12.7x forward, 5% off its 52-week low**, marked down for a **transitory input-cost/tariff margin squeeze** that management expects to offset into 2027 — while revenue keeps growing and EBIT margin keeps expanding. The serial-guidance-cut narrative and China softness are real and cap conviction at MEDIUM, so this is a **starter / scale-in (B-grade BUY LOW)**, not a max-conviction buy. Downside is bounded by the 52-week low and a defensive ~0.87 beta; upside to the Street's $79.72 mean is +28%, with a probability-weighted ~+15% scenario EV.
