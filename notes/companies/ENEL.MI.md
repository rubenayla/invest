# Enel S.p.A. (ENEL.MI)

**Sector:** Utilities | **Industry:** Utilities - Diversified

**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

Enel is Europe's largest utility by market cap, transitioning aggressively from a legacy fossil/hydro operator into a renewables-and-grids platform. The company just reported solid FY2025 preliminary results (ordinary EBITDA EUR 22.9B, in-line) and unveiled a significantly upgraded 2026-2028 strategic plan: EUR 53B in capex (+EUR 10B vs prior plan), ~EUR 20B in renewables, EUR 26B+ in grids, targeting EPS of EUR 0.80-0.82 by 2028 (6% CAGR). A EUR 1B buyback tranche has been launched. The stock has rallied ~41% from its 52-week low of EUR 6.71 to EUR 9.44, but still trades 8% below its 52-week high of EUR 10.31. Analyst consensus is split (10 buy / 10 hold / 3 sell) with a mean target of EUR 9.85, suggesting limited near-term upside from here.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Enel is a well-managed utility trading at fair value after a strong re-rating. The 2026-2028 plan is ambitious but execution risk exists given the leverage increase (net debt/EBITDA rising from 2.5x to ~3.0x). Analysts see modest upside (~4% to EUR 9.85 target), essentially a hold-for-yield story at ~5% dividend yield.
- **Our view:** The market is underappreciating the earnings acceleration from the grid RAB expansion (+22% to EUR 58B by 2028) and renewables scale-up. Over 90% of EBITDA is regulated or contracted, meaning the ~7% EPS CAGR is unusually high-certainty for a utility. The combination of 5% dividend yield + 6% EPS growth + 1-2% buyback support = 12-13% total shareholder return, which is exceptional for a beta-0.9 defensive stock. The market is pricing this as a static utility rather than a compounding machine with visible growth.
- **Trigger:** FY2025 full results release (March 19, 2026 -- just happened/imminent) should confirm the trajectory. Next catalyst is H1 2026 results showing execution on the new plan, plus visible progress on the EUR 1B buyback reducing share count.

## Inflection Point

Enel is past its 2022-2023 trough (energy crisis impairments, high leverage, asset disposals) and into a **growth acceleration phase**. The inflection evidence is strong:

- **Deleveraging complete:** Net debt/EBITDA improved from peak ~3.5x to 2.5x, creating capex headroom
- **Asset rotation done:** Non-core LatAm disposals finished, portfolio now focused on Italy, Spain, US, Brazil
- **FCF inflection:** Turned FCF positive in 2023, growing in 2024 (EUR 3.1B), enabling dividend growth + buybacks
- **Strategic shift visible:** New plan adds EUR 10B capex vs prior, signaling confidence in returns

This is not a "hope it turns around" story -- the inflection has already happened and the data confirms it. The question is whether the market gives full credit for the growth trajectory.

## Bull Case

- **Grid RAB expansion** (EUR 47B to EUR 58B by 2028) drives predictable, inflation-linked earnings growth in a monopoly business
- **Renewables scale-up** (~15 GW addition) at improving returns as technology costs decline and PPAs tighten
- **Multiple re-rating** from ~13x to ~15x forward PE as the market recognizes the quality of the earnings growth (regulated/contracted)
- **Buyback + dividend** provide 6-7% annual cash return while waiting, with DPS growing ~6% CAGR
- **Italy grid license extension** (20 years past 2030) provides long-duration earnings visibility rarely seen in utilities

## Bear Case

- **Leverage creep:** Net debt/EBITDA rising to 3.0x during investment phase -- a credit downgrade would increase funding costs materially
- **Execution risk on EUR 53B capex:** Supply chain inflation, permitting delays, or technology missteps on the renewables build-out
- **Interest rate sensitivity:** As a heavily leveraged utility, rising rates compress equity value and increase debt service
- **Italian political/regulatory risk:** Government as largest shareholder creates potential for politically motivated decisions (windfall taxes, price caps)
- **Currency risk:** Significant LatAm operations (Brazil) expose earnings to FX volatility

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

Enel offers a rare combination for a utility: visible 6-7% EPS growth backed by regulated/contracted earnings, a 5.1% dividend yield growing at 6% CAGR, and buyback support -- all at a beta of 0.9. The ~13x forward PE undervalues the growth quality relative to European utility peers at 14-16x. The variant perception is that the market is pricing Enel as a static yield play rather than a compounding machine with an identifiable growth inflection already underway. Expected total return of ~16% annually (11% capital appreciation + 5% dividend) is attractive for a defensive, low-volatility position.

The conviction is MEDIUM rather than HIGH because: (1) the stock has already re-rated significantly from 2023 lows, (2) consensus targets show only ~4% upside suggesting limited near-term catalyst surprise, and (3) the leverage increase to 3.0x during the capex phase introduces execution risk.

**Entry:** Current price of EUR 9.44 is acceptable. Stronger buy below EUR 9.00 (where FCF yield approaches 3.5% and forward PE drops to ~12.3x).
**Scale-in plan:** 50% now, 50% on pullback to EUR 8.80-9.00 or on confirmation of FY2025 full results beating guidance.
**Thesis-break:** Exit if net debt/EBITDA exceeds 3.5x, or if EPS guidance for 2027 is cut below EUR 0.70.
