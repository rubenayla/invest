# The Toronto-Dominion Bank (TD)

**Sector:** Financial Services | **Industry:** Banks - Diversified
**Price:** $99.87 (2026-04-10) | **Market Cap:** $167.5B USD
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-10

## Situation Summary

TD is Canada's second-largest bank by assets, recovering from a massive self-inflicted wound: a $3.1B settlement in Oct 2024 for BSA/AML violations that enabled ~$700M in illicit transactions. The bank now operates under an OCC-imposed US asset cap ($434B), has exited its $14.4B Schwab stake (Feb 2025) to bolster capital, and is spending ~$1B on AML remediation across 2025-2026. Despite these headwinds, Q1 FY2026 (Jan 2026) was a blowout: adjusted EPS of C$2.44 beat consensus by 8.4%, revenue grew 10.6% YoY to C$16.6B, and net income surged 45% YoY. The stock has rallied ~77% off its 52-week low and now trades within pennies of its 52-week high at $99.87.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** TD is a "show me" story. Analysts see the AML remediation as a multi-year drag, with the US asset cap unlikely to lift before 2027. Consensus price target (~$97.55 USD) implies the stock is fairly valued here. The strong Q1 was good but expected given easy comps against the fine-impacted FY2024. Most see limited upside until the US growth story resumes.
- **Our view:** The consensus is roughly correct. TD has executed well on remediation and the Canadian domestic franchise is strong (record deposits, rate stabilization benefits). However, the US asset cap is a genuine structural constraint on the growth engine that made TD unique among Canadian banks. The Schwab exit removes a valuable strategic asset. At 11x trailing earnings the bank looks cheap, but that reflects the reality: constrained US growth + remediation costs + no Schwab income. There is no clear variant perception here -- the market is pricing this reasonably.
- **Trigger:** OCC asset cap removal (not expected before H2 2027 at the earliest). Until then, catalysts are limited to incremental cost savings from the restructuring program (~$900M pre-tax in FY2026) and continued strong Canadian operations.

## Inflection Point

**Partial inflection, not yet complete.** The worst is behind TD: the $3.1B fine is paid, the Schwab sale bolstered capital, and Q1 FY2026 showed the core business is healthy. However, the true inflection -- resumption of US growth -- requires the OCC asset cap to be lifted, which management frames as a 2027+ event. The stock has already priced in much of the "recovery from crisis" narrative (up 77% from lows). The next leg requires demonstrable US remediation progress.

## Bull Case
- Canadian franchise is a compounding machine: oligopoly, record deposits, rate stabilization, and restructuring savings (~$900M in FY2026)
- At 11x trailing / 13.4x forward P/E, TD trades at a discount to Royal Bank (~13x trailing) despite similar quality
- ~$8B share buyback program (funded by Schwab proceeds) provides price support and EPS accretion
- If US asset cap lifts in 2027, TD re-rates to peer multiple (15-20% upside from multiple expansion alone)
- Wholesale banking (TD Securities) hitting record revenue after Cowen integration

## Bear Case
- US asset cap may persist longer than expected; regulatory monitors have broad discretion
- ~$500M+ annual remediation costs are a structural drag on earnings for years
- Without US growth, TD loses its key differentiator vs Canadian peers (RY, BMO have their own US strategies)
- Stock is at 52-week high -- most of the recovery is already priced in
- Canadian housing market weakness (TD slashing housing forecasts for 2026) could pressure domestic loan books
- Short interest has historically been elevated (TD was world's most-shorted bank at one point)

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

The expected value of +6.9% (plus 3.2% dividend) is modest for the risk profile. There is no clear variant perception -- the market is pricing the situation reasonably.
