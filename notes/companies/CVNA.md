# Carvana Co. (CVNA)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Auto & Truck Dealerships
**Price:** $299.60 | **Market Cap:** $65.7B | **EV:** $43.7B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23 (updated from 2026-03-20)

## Situation Summary

Carvana has completed one of the most remarkable corporate turnarounds in recent memory, going from near-bankruptcy in late 2022 to S&P 500 inclusion in late 2025. FY2025 delivered record results: $20.3B revenue (+49% YoY), $1.9B operating income, and $1.4B net income. The stock has pulled back ~40% from its January ATH of $487, driven by three factors: a $1.23B follow-on equity offering in February 2026, concerns about rising reconditioning costs pressuring unit economics, and broader market rotation out of high-beta consumer cyclicals. A 5-for-1 stock split is scheduled for May 7, 2026. Tariffs on imported new cars could be a significant tailwind, driving more consumers to used vehicles. The stock is trading at 27.6x forward earnings with analysts targeting a mean of $428.50.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Carvana is a proven turnaround story, but the easy money has been made. Analysts see ~46% upside to $428 mean target. Concerns center on (1) whether $7,000+ GPU can be maintained while scaling to 600K+ units, (2) $1.23B dilutive equity raise, and (3) EPS expected to decline 17% in 2026 due to loss of the $685M tax benefit that inflated 2025 NI.
- **Our view:** The market is too focused on the NI decline (which is a non-recurring tax benefit noise) and underappreciating two structural advantages: (1) Carvana's vertically integrated logistics (own haulers + ADESA megasites) create a cost moat that widens with volume — reconditioning cost concerns are short-term growing pains, not structural; (2) tariffs on new car imports could push used car demand significantly higher, and Carvana is the best-positioned digital platform to capture that. The 40% pullback from ATH creates a much more reasonable entry point at ~28x forward vs 45x+ at the peak.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (likely late April / early May) should show volume acceleration toward 600K annual run-rate AND stable/improving GPU despite scaling. Tariff clarity in Q2 2026 could be a secondary catalyst.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — Carvana is at a profitability/scale inflection.** The company has crossed from survival mode into profitable growth. Key evidence:
- Operating income swung from -$1.5B (2022) to +$1.9B (2025) — a $3.4B improvement
- FCF has been positive and growing for 3 consecutive years
- The company has shifted messaging from "capital preservation" to "volume growth" with 600K unit target
- The ADESA integration is mature and providing operational leverage
- The equity raise signals management confidence in deploying capital for growth

This is classic Phase 2 of a turnaround: the company has proven it can be profitable, now it's about scaling profitably. The key risk is that scaling too fast erodes unit economics.

## Bull Case
- Tariffs on new car imports drive massive demand shift to used vehicles; Carvana captures outsized share as the dominant digital platform
- GPU maintained at $6,500-7,000+ while scaling to 600K-800K units by 2027, driven by ADESA operational leverage
- Operating margins expand toward 10-12% as SG&A leverage kicks in at higher volumes
- Used car market digital penetration accelerates from <10% toward 20%+, with Carvana as the primary beneficiary
- Debt paydown continues, enabling potential share buybacks by 2027-2028

## Bear Case
- Reconditioning costs continue rising, compressing GPU below $6,000 as volume scales — the "scale vs. economics" tradeoff turns negative
- Consumer spending weakens in a tariff/inflation-driven slowdown; used car demand drops as consumers defer purchases
- Competition intensifies: CarMax and dealers accelerate digital capabilities, squeezing Carvana's differentiation
- Beta of 3.7 means a broad market downturn would devastate the stock regardless of fundamentals
- Garcia family's dual-class control (46% voting power on 2% economic ownership) is a governance risk

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

Carvana is a genuinely great business going through a real inflection, and the 38% pullback from ATH creates a much more attractive entry than two months ago. However, the expected value of +11% is modest for a beta-3.7 stock — you need to believe in the bull case to justify the risk. The forward PE of ~28x on potentially declining 2026 EPS (due to the tax benefit normalization) means you're paying for execution on the volume ramp. GBM models are strongly bullish (all 6 models positive) and analyst consensus is +43%, but DCF models flag extreme overvaluation. The thesis is sound, but the risk-reward is not compelling enough for a HIGH conviction buy at this price.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:**
1. Price below $250 (bringing EV closer to 15% FCF yield on 2027E numbers)
2. Q1 2026 earnings confirming GPU stability above $6,500 with 45%+ volume growth
3. Clear tariff catalyst materializing that drives used car demand surge
4. Any combination of two of these three conditions

**Entry plan if triggered:** Scale in with 1/3 position at $250, add 1/3 at $220 on further weakness, final 1/3 on earnings confirmation. Thesis-break stop at $150 (51-week low area).
