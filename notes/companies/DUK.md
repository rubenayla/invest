# Duke Energy Corporation (DUK)

**Sector:** Utilities | **Industry:** Utilities - Regulated Electric

## Situation Summary

Duke Energy is the largest fully-regulated US electric utility by capex plan ($103B over 5 years, an 18% increase), with operations across the Carolinas, Florida, and Indiana. The stock trades at 20.4x trailing / 19.3x forward earnings with a 3.3% dividend yield. The dominant narrative is "data center demand + grid modernization = accelerating rate base growth." Duke has ~4.5 GW of data center ESAs under contract with a ~9 GW total pipeline, mostly from hyperscalers building in the Carolinas. However, Duke has filed for a 15% rate increase in NC ($1.7B combined for DEC and DEP), with NCUC hearings in spring 2026 and a decision expected late 2026 — a significant regulatory overhang. The stock is 4.4% below its 52-week high of $134.49 and has outperformed the market YTD, benefiting from rate-cut expectations and the AI power demand theme. Q1 2026 earnings on May 5.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Duke is a core "AI power demand" play among regulated utilities — safe dividend plus data center optionality equals a defensive compounder. Analysts are moderately bullish (7 Buy / 7 Hold, avg target $137-140, ~8% upside). The market prices ~5-7% EPS growth through rate base expansion funded by the $103B capex plan.
- **Our view:** No clear variant perception. The data center demand is real (4.5 GW contracted, moving toward 10% of sales by 2030 from <1% today), but it is **already priced into the premium valuation** and the $103B capex plan. The regulatory risk on the NC rate case is underappreciated — a material haircut to the $1.7B revenue ask would compress near-term earnings growth. Meanwhile, the massive capex program requires continuous equity issuance and debt growth (net debt/EBITDA already 5.3x), creating dilution risk. The 19x forward PE already reflects the growth story; there is no mispricing to exploit.
- **Trigger:** NC rate case decision (late 2026) is the key event — a favorable outcome would de-risk the growth plan, but an adverse one could compress the multiple. No clear catalyst for upside re-rating from here.

## Inflection Point

**Partial inflection — data center demand is real but not yet an earnings inflection.** Data centers are <1% of current peak demand but projected to be ~10% of sales by 2030. This is a genuine secular tailwind, but the earnings impact is back-end loaded (2028+) because the capex must be deployed first and then approved via rate cases. The NC rate case decision in late 2026 is the near-term catalyst that either validates or challenges the growth plan. This is not a turnaround or hidden-growth story — it's a consensus theme with execution risk.

## Bull Case

- NC rate case approved at or near requested levels, de-risking the $103B capex plan
- Data center demand exceeds projections; ESA pipeline converts to 8-10 GW of connected load by 2030
- Interest rates decline, reducing financing costs on $90B+ debt and expanding the PE multiple
- EPS growth accelerates to 7-8% as rate base compounds, dividend grows in lockstep
- Nuclear fleet provides clean baseload advantage as grid reliability becomes premium

## Bear Case

- NCUC significantly haircuts the rate increase (30-50% reduction), compressing 2027-2028 EPS growth
- Data center demand disappoints — hyperscalers slow buildouts, shift to on-site generation, or regulatory backlash forces data centers to bear more infrastructure costs
- Rising interest rates increase financing costs on $91B debt, compressing spreads and earnings
- Equity dilution from financing $103B capex program erodes per-share growth
- Florida or Indiana regulatory outcomes less favorable than modeled
- Extreme weather events (cold snaps, hurricanes) create unrecoverable costs

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

Duke Energy is a high-quality regulated utility with a genuine data center demand tailwind, but there is no variant perception to exploit. The market already prices 5-7% EPS growth from the $103B capex plan. The +42% autoresearch signal likely reflects the GBM models' favorable view of utility momentum in a falling-rate environment, but the most calibrated models (gbm_1y at +12.6%, gbm_3y at -2.1%) suggest fair value is near current price. The expected value of +5.8% (roughly matching the dividend yield plus modest capital appreciation) is adequate for a bond substitute but does not justify active allocation over better opportunities. The NC rate case in late 2026 is the key swing factor.

**If WATCH:** Would upgrade to BUY on:
1. A pullback to $115-118 (forward PE ~17x), which would provide a meaningful margin of safety
2. Favorable NC rate case outcome (>80% of requested increase approved), confirming the growth plan
3. A significant market correction that compresses utility valuations broadly — DUK would be a high-quality name to own through turbulence
