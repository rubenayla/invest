# Zoetis Inc. (ZTS)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Animal Health (drugs, vaccines, diagnostics)
**Price:** $77.59 (2026-06-04) | **Market Cap:** ~$32.5B (~419M shares)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-04
**Verdict:** BUY (starter / scale-in) — the global #1 animal-health company has de-rated ~55% from its early-2025 high to ~11x forward earnings (a decade low vs a historical ~30–35x), after a single ugly Q1'26 print exposed simultaneous competitive pressure across dermatology and parasiticides. The business is *decelerating*, not declining (revenue still +2–5% organic, livestock +12%, EPS still grew in Q1), and the franchise economics — 72% gross margin, 68% ROE, 37% operating margin — remain intact. Conviction MEDIUM: the bear case (permanent erosion of companion-animal pricing power) is genuinely live, so this is a scale-in, not a back-up-the-truck.

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Zoetis is the world's largest pure-play animal-health company — medicines, vaccines, diagnostics and biodevices for both **companion animals** (dogs, cats, horses) and **livestock** (cattle, swine, poultry, fish). Spun out of Pfizer in 2013. ~$9.5B revenue, sold in >100 countries. The crown jewels are the **companion-animal franchises**: dermatology (Apoquel, Cytopoint), parasiticides (Simparica/Simparica Trio), and the osteoarthritis-pain monoclonal antibodies (Librela for dogs, Solensia for cats).

**Why it's been a premium compounder (the moat).** Animal health is structurally attractive: no government price-setting (owners pay cash or pet insurance, not Medicare), no patent-cliff cliff-edges like human pharma (brands persist long after patent expiry because generics are slow and vets are sticky), recurring spend, and a multi-decade pet-humanization tailwind. Zoetis has the broadest portfolio, the deepest vet relationships, and category-defining products. The result historically: high-single/low-double-digit organic growth, 70%+ gross margins, mid-30s operating margins, ~60–70% ROE, and consistent dividend growth — which is why the market paid 30–40x earnings for a decade.

**What went wrong (the reason it's cheap).** On **May 7, 2026** Zoetis reported Q1'26 and cut full-year guidance; the stock fell ~25% in a day (from ~$111 to ~$83) and has since drifted to ~$77 (-55% off the early-2025 ~$171 high). The problem is concentrated in the high-margin **US companion-animal** book, which fell ~11%, with three core franchises declining at once:
- **Dermatology −11% ($347M):** Apoquel hit by "price-driven competitive pressure"; Cytopoint by lower clinic traffic. New rivals arrived — **Merck's Numelvi** (oral JAK inhibitor, US-approved Feb 2026) and **Elanco's Zenrelia** (daily JAK), the latter reaching blockbuster run-rate in under two years.
- **Parasiticides −1% ($385M, Simparica):** softer vet traffic + heightened promo; **Elanco's Credelio Quattro** (all-in-one + tapeworm coverage) is taking share.
- **OA-pain mAbs −8% ($140M, Librela/Solensia):** macro/clinic-traffic headwinds, plus the lingering shadow of earlier Librela adverse-event reports — though US Librela rose sequentially for the first time in six quarters, a tentative green shoot.

CEO Kristin Peck framed it as **four converging pressures**: sustained vet-clinic price increases, rising pet-owner price sensitivity, intensified competition, and new entrants who "have not yet expanded the overall market" (i.e. they're splitting the existing pie, not growing it). Livestock, by contrast, was strong (+12% organic across cattle, poultry, swine, fish), partly cushioning the miss.

## Situation Summary

ZTS sits at **$77.59, ~55% below its 52-week high of $171.52** and right at the 52-week low ($72.38). This is a textbook premium-compounder-breaks-its-narrative de-rating:

- **Revenue is still growing.** Q1'26 +2% reported / flat organic; FY26 guide is **+2–5% organic**. This is deceleration, not contraction.
- **EPS still grew** +3% YoY in Q1 to $1.53 (missed the ~$1.62 whisper, hence the reaction).
- **Guidance cut, but modestly:** FY26 revenue to **$9.68–9.96B** (from $9.825–10.025B in Feb) and **adjusted EPS $6.85–7.00**.
- At $77.59 against the **$6.925 guidance midpoint that's ~11.2x forward earnings** (~10.5x on Street's ~$7.42); trailing P/E ~12.7x. Decade average was ~30–35x.
- Margins and returns are **undamaged**: 72% gross, 37% operating, 68% ROE, 33% payout, 2.78% dividend yield, beta ~0.86.

The precise debate: *is the simultaneous derm + parasiticide competition a permanent impairment of Zoetis's pricing power (justifying a structurally lower multiple), or a cyclical air-pocket — soft vet traffic + a competitive wave that the broadest portfolio in the industry digests — that hands you a category leader at a once-a-decade price?*

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** The moat is cracking. For the first time well-funded rivals (Merck, Elanco) have genuinely competitive products in Zoetis's two biggest cash franchises *at the same time*, US companion (the highest-margin growth engine) is shrinking 11%, deceleration has run four straight quarters, and management itself concedes new entrants aren't expanding the market — i.e. share loss. If derm and parasiticides become competitive markets, the premium pricing that earned a 30x multiple is gone for good, and ~11x is the new normal, not a bargain.
- **Our view:** The de-rating overshoots. (1) Zoetis is *still growing* revenue and EPS through the worst of the competitive wave — this is a margin/pricing scare, not a demand collapse. (2) Livestock (+12%) and emerging markets diversify the companion-animal weakness. (3) The JAK competitors carry safety baggage (Zenrelia launched with a boxed warning), and Apoquel/Cytopoint retain strong labels and vet loyalty — share loss is likely to be gradual and partial, not a cliff. (4) The economics that define the moat (72% gross margin, 68% ROE) are fully intact; nothing about the cost structure broke. (5) At ~11x for the global #1 with a growing 2.78% dividend and ongoing buybacks, even a partial re-rating toward a still-discounted 16–20x on a stabilized ~$7–8 EPS is a 60–120% move.
- **Trigger:** Two clean quarters showing US companion-animal declines *moderating* (less negative than −11%) and gross margin holding would confirm the cyclical read and start the re-rate. The mirror risk that breaks it: US companion declines *worsen*, derm/parasiticide share loss accelerates visibly, or FY26 EPS gets cut again — that would validate the structural-erosion bear case.

## Entry / Exit Discipline

- **Entry:** Scale in starting here (~$77). It is sitting on the 52-week low; add on further weakness toward ~$68–70 (where forward P/E approaches ~10x and the dividend yield tops 3%).
- **Thesis-break / stop:** Below ~$60 *accompanied by deteriorating fundamentals* — i.e. US companion-animal declines worsening beyond −11% for two more quarters, visible gross-margin compression, or a *second* FY26 EPS guidance cut below ~$6.50. A price drop on stable/improving fundamentals is an add, not a stop; a price drop confirming erosion is an exit.
- **What would change my mind (bull → bear):** Sustained acceleration of US companion declines; clear derm share migrating to Numelvi/Zenrelia in Q2/Q3 data; parasiticide share visibly bleeding to Credelio Quattro; management cutting guidance again. Conversely (bear → bull): two quarters of moderating companion declines with intact gross margin.
