# Stryker Corporation (SYK)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Medical Devices
**Price:** $338.38 (2026-04-17) | **Market Cap:** $129.6B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-17

## Situation Summary

Stryker is 16% off its 52-week high ($405) amid broad tariff fears hitting medtech -- CEO Lobo flagged ~$400M tariff impact for 2026 (vs $175M in 2025), concentrated in H1. Despite the macro headwinds, operational execution remains stellar: FY2025 delivered 10.3% organic growth and 11.2% total revenue growth to $25.1B, with operating income CAGR of 17.6% over 3 years. The big product story is Mako RPS, a handheld robotic system for total knee that began limited market release in Feb 2026, targeting ASCs and surgeons who currently do manual procedures -- this opens a new addressable segment beyond the 3,000+ traditional Mako installed base. Q1 2026 earnings are due April 30. Analysts remain overwhelmingly bullish (27 analysts, consensus Buy, mean target $419).

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** High-quality compounder growing ~10% organically, temporarily pressured by tariffs. Fair value $380-420 range. Multiple will recover as tariff clarity emerges. Mako RPS is incremental but small near-term.
- **Our view:** The market is underestimating the TAM expansion from Mako RPS and the ASC shift. Currently 2/3 of US knees are done on Mako, but only in large hospital ORs. Mako RPS at ~1/3 the footprint and cost unlocks the other 75% of ORs that don't have robotic access today. The $400M tariff headwind is manageable against $25B revenue and Stryker's pricing power -- they've historically passed through costs. The real risk is execution on the RPS rollout timeline, not tariffs. However, this is a well-known thesis and the stock is only modestly below fair value -- there's no truly contrarian angle here.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (April 30) -- first read on tariff pass-through effectiveness and Mako RPS early adoption metrics. Full commercial launch H2 2026 will be the real inflection data point.

## Inflection Point

**Moderate inflection -- Mako RPS is a new product cycle, not a turnaround.** Stryker is not broken; it's a compounder potentially entering an accelerated growth phase. The inflection thesis:
- Mako RPS (limited release Feb 2026, full launch H2 2026) opens the ASC market and manual-surgery segment
- 75% of US ORs don't have robotic access -- Mako RPS at lower cost/footprint addresses this gap
- If adoption tracks like original Mako (which went from ~300 to 3,000+ units), the revenue impact could be material by 2027-2028
- However, this is a 2-3 year story, not an immediate catalyst

## Bull Case

- Mako RPS full launch exceeds expectations, driving 15%+ ortho revenue growth and new customer acquisition in ASCs
- Tariff impact comes in below $400M guidance as mitigation strategies (pricing, dual sourcing) prove effective
- Operating margins continue expanding toward 22-23% as robotic platform scales
- International Mako penetration accelerates (currently underpenetrated vs US)
- Aging demographics drive structural procedure volume growth for a decade+

## Bear Case

- Tariff escalation beyond $400M estimate if China/EU trade deteriorates further
- Mako RPS cannibalization: steals from higher-margin traditional Mako placements rather than growing the market
- Competitive response from Zimmer Biomet ROSA, Smith+Nephew CORI, J&J Velys intensifies pricing pressure
- Orthopedic procedure volumes plateau as GLP-1 drugs reduce obesity-related joint deterioration
- M&A integration risk if Stryker pursues another large deal to sustain growth

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

Stryker is an A-tier medtech compounder with a genuine product cycle catalyst in Mako RPS. The 16% pullback from highs creates a reasonable entry, and the ~16% EV is attractive for a company of this quality (21/25). However, there is no true variant perception here -- the Mako RPS thesis is well-known to every analyst, and the stock still trades at 22.6x forward earnings, which is not cheap enough to compensate for the lack of edge. The tariff overhang ($400M) creates near-term uncertainty but is manageable.

**Would upgrade to BUY if:** (1) Stock pulls back to $310-315 range (~20x forward P/E) on tariff fears around Q1 earnings, providing a genuine margin of safety, OR (2) Q1 earnings on April 30 show Mako RPS initial adoption metrics exceeding expectations, confirming the TAM expansion thesis before consensus fully prices it in. At current price, the risk/reward is good but not exceptional for a consensus long.
