# DocuSign, Inc. (DOCU)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Application software — e-signature / agreement management (e-sign core + IAM "Intelligent Agreement Management" platform)
**Price:** $42.58 (2026-06-18) | **Market Cap:** ~$8.13B | **Enterprise Value:** ~$7.85B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-18
**Verdict:** WATCH (lean BUY LOW under ~$38). Conviction **LOW–MEDIUM**.

A highly cash-generative, net-cash software franchise trading at a **~13% headline FCF yield (~6x EV/FCF)** at its **52-week low ($42.58 vs 52wk range $40.16–$86.65)** — but the headline cheapness is **half-illusion**: stock-based compensation runs **$622M/yr (~19% of revenue)**, so on GAAP it is **~26x trailing earnings** and on SBC-adjusted owner-earnings only a **~6–7% yield**. The variant question is whether the **verified inflection** — dollar net retention up to 102% for **six consecutive quarters** of improvement, and the **IAM platform exploding from 2.3% → 10.8% of ARR in one year** — re-accelerates a business whose core e-signature product has decelerated to **~4–6% billings growth**. You are paid to wait (real FCF + a $1.4B buyback that more than offsets dilution), and the downside is cushioned by net cash, but this is **reasonably-priced-quality-with-optionality, not a deep-value bargain**. **EV ≈ +19%** with a real left tail if commoditization wins. Most of the asymmetry needs IAM to convert promise into reported growth.

## Business Primer

**What they do.** DocuSign is the category leader in electronic signature. Revenue ~$3.22B (FY26, ended Jan 2026), almost entirely subscription. The company is mid-pivot from a single-product e-sign vendor to a broader **IAM (Intelligent Agreement Management)** platform — AI-assisted contract creation, negotiation, storage, and analytics — repositioning the agreement workflow around its installed base.

- **Core e-signature** — the cash cow, ~89% of ARR. Mature, decelerating: FY26 ARR $3.27B (+8.0% YoY), but **billings growth has slowed to ~4–6%** (Q1 FY27 billings +4.2%, below 5.2% consensus). This is the source of the de-rating.
- **IAM** — the growth engine and the entire bull case. IAM ARR exceeded **$350M, 10.8% of total ARR as of Jan 2026, up from 2.3% a year earlier**. FY27 guidance targets **IAM >$600M ARR and ~18% of total** — i.e. management expects the mix shift to accelerate, not fade.

**Quality markers.** Gross margin ~80%, GAAP operating margin ~13%, net cash (cash ~$602M vs debt ~$185M → ~$417M net cash, plus short-term investments push total liquidity higher), FCF ~$1.06–1.25B.

## Why it's cheap (the de-rating)

1. **Growth has stalled to single digits.** Billings — the leading indicator — grew just **4.2% in Q1 FY27**, missing consensus, and management **cut full-year billings growth guidance to ~6.5%** (from ~7.0%), citing early renewals plus "macro caution." The market reads this as a saturating core with pricing pressure.
2. **Commoditization risk is real.** E-signature is increasingly a feature, not a product: Adobe Acrobat Sign, Dropbox Sign, and a long tail of cheaper/free alternatives pressure price and differentiation. The bear thesis is that DocuSign's moat is eroding and IAM is a defensive bolt-on, not a new growth platform.
3. **Serial value trap.** The stock has been "cheap" the entire way down from ~$310 (2021 pandemic peak) to $42. Being cheap has not been a catalyst for four years.
4. **The SBC overhang.** See below — this is the most important and least-discussed item.

## The SBC reality check (why "6x EV/FCF" overstates the bargain)

- Headline FCF ~$1.06B (FY26) on an $8.1B market cap looks like a **~13% FCF yield / ~6x EV/FCF** — screaming cheap for 80%-gross-margin software.
- **But stock-based compensation is $622M/yr — ~19% of revenue.** FCF does not subtract SBC; GAAP earnings do. On GAAP, net income is only ~$309M (9.6% margin) → **trailing P/E ~26x**, and **SBC-adjusted owner-FCF is ~$440–600M → a ~6–7% owner-earnings yield**, not 13%.
- The redeeming detail: DocuSign repurchased **$869M of stock in FY26** (buyback authorization now **$1.4B**), which *more than offsets* the $622M of SBC dilution — net share count is shrinking ~3%/yr. So the dilution is being neutralized with cash, but at the cost of most of the "free" cash flow. The honest read: **owner-earnings yield ~6–7% on an ~8% grower** = fairly valued, with the buyback compounding per-share value while you wait.

## Why it might be mispriced (the bull)

- **A genuine, verified inflection — not a vanity metric.** Dollar net retention rose to **102%, improving for six consecutive quarters** (101% → 102%). NRR turning up after years of decline is the single hardest thing for a maturing software company to fake, and it is happening.
- **IAM is scaling fast.** **2.3% → 10.8% of ARR in one year**, $350M+ ARR, guided to $600M+/18% next year. If IAM keeps compounding, total growth re-accelerates from the ~8% floor and the AI-platform narrative supports multiple expansion.
- **Q1 FY27 beat on profit.** Adjusted EPS **$1.09 vs $1.00 consensus** on revenue +8.7% — the deceleration is in *billings/bookings*, not yet in P&L; margins are holding.
- **Capital return + balance sheet cushion.** Net cash, $1.4B buyback (~17% of cap), buying ~$700–870M/yr at trough prices. Even flat earnings produce mid-single-digit EPS growth from share shrinkage.
- **Cushioned downside.** Net cash + real (even SBC-adjusted) cash generation means the equity is unlikely to be a zero; the left tail is "dead money," not "wipeout."

## Variant Perception

- **Bear / consensus view (rec = Hold):** A maturing e-sign vendor whose core is commoditizing, decelerating to ~5% billings growth, propped up by a non-GAAP/FCF optics story that hides 19%-of-revenue SBC. On GAAP it's 26x for ~8% growth — not cheap. IAM is a "me-too" AI bolt-on that won't outrun the core's decay. Classic value trap; cheap for four years and counting.
- **Our (cautiously interested) view:** The market is pricing perpetual decay, but two verified facts cut against that — **NRR up six straight quarters** and **IAM at 10.8% of ARR and accelerating**. At an honest ~6–7% owner-earnings yield with net cash and a dilution-neutralizing buyback, you are paid a fair coupon to own a quality franchise with a credible re-acceleration option. The risk/reward is *acceptable*, not a fat pitch.
- **Why only WATCH:** the bargain is an illusion until SBC normalizes (it should fall as % of revenue as the company matures, but hasn't yet), and the entire upside leans on IAM converting ARR mix into reported total-growth re-acceleration — which guidance promises but the billings line does not yet show. Buying the full thesis today is paying for an inflection on management's word. The margin of safety is the cash flow and net cash, not the growth.

## Entry / Exit Framework

- **Entry:** Current $42.58 is acceptable for a starter; the asymmetry improves materially **under ~$38** (closer to the 52wk floor, where owner-earnings yield approaches ~8%). Prefer to scale in on confirmation rather than commit full size on the IAM promise.
- **Thesis-break / stop-watching:** **Dollar net retention rolling back below 100%** (would kill the inflection thesis), **IAM growth stalling** materially below the $600M FY27 guide, or **SBC failing to decline as a % of revenue** over the next 2–4 quarters. Sustained price below ~$34 on deteriorating billings = thesis broken, not a bargain.
- **What would change my mind (to BUY):** one or two more quarters of NRR improvement *plus* total revenue growth re-accelerating toward double digits as IAM mix passes ~15% — that converts the optionality into a trend and would justify upgrading from WATCH.
