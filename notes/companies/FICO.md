## Situation Summary
FICO is the credit-score monopolist being publicly attacked by the FHFA (Director Bill Pulte) for doubling mortgage-score royalties from ~$5 to $10 for 2026 — a single hike that adds ~$500M of cost to the mortgage industry. The FHFA has greenlit VantageScore 4.0 as a competitor for Fannie/Freddie-backed mortgages (July 2025), and Fannie Mae began accepting VantageScore 4.0 on Nov 17, 2025. However, LLPA tables required for actual secondary-market execution are not expected until late 2026. The stock peaked near $2,217 in 2025 and has collapsed ~54% to $1,030 on the political/regulatory overhang. Q1 FY26 (reported Jan 28, 2026) was strong: revenue +16% to $512M, Scores +29%, B2B mortgage +36%, EPS $7.33 beat $7.07. FY26 guide: $2.35B revenue (+18% YoY). Next earnings: May 6, 2026 (Q2 FY26).

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** The 30-year FICO monopoly in GSE mortgage is structurally broken. VantageScore 4.0 is a credible alternative now blessed by FHFA, and Pulte has made lowering credit-score costs a political priority. The double-hike to $10 is the last gasp of pricing power and will either be rolled back or circumvented. Apply a growth-software multiple de-rating from ~45x to ~20x forward EPS.
- **Our view (weak variant):** The market correctly priced in real regulatory/competitive risk, but likely overshoots in the near term because (1) LLPA publication is delayed to late 2026 / mid-2027, meaning 2026 revenue still captures the full $10 price; (2) 70-80% of resellers have already signed FICO's Direct Licensing Program, locking in distribution; (3) FICO 10T is the better technical product and will co-exist with VantageScore 4.0, not be replaced; (4) mortgage is only ~20-25% of Scores revenue — the rest (auto, card, personal loans) is untouched by FHFA. However, this is NOT a clean variant perception — the bear case is directionally correct, the timing is what's uncertain.
- **Trigger:** Q2 FY26 earnings (May 6, 2026) — if Scores revenue continues +25-30% YoY, it proves the price hike is sticking through mortgage-origination channels despite the noise. Conversely, any guidance cut or sign of lender revolt kills the thesis.

## Inflection Point
**Negative inflection in progress.** The monopoly narrative is cracking in real-time. However, the FINANCIAL inflection is still +6 to +18 months out — VantageScore 4.0 can't be used for GSE-sold loans until LLPAs are published (late 2026 or 2027). 2026 financials will likely look great. The question is whether the stock re-rates ahead of actual revenue impact or waits for it. No positive inflection evident — this is a defensive holding at best. Not a "buy the bottom" setup because the structural narrative is unresolved.

## Bull Case
- LLPA delays stretch VantageScore adoption into 2027-2028, giving FICO 1-2 more years of monopoly pricing — EPS compounds 20%+ while the clock ticks.
- FICO 10T wins technical bake-offs and co-exists with VantageScore as dual standard — loss of share < 20% rather than > 50%.
- Mortgage is only ~25% of Scores; auto/card/personal (the bigger bucket) continues growing at double-digit rates untouched.
- Political threat recedes under next administration, or Pulte moves on — pricing power intact, stock re-rates to 30-35x forward EPS.
- Aggressive buybacks on the dip accelerate EPS growth (share count -6%/year possible).

## Bear Case
- Pulte and CFPB escalate: direct price caps, forced licensing terms, or designation as "public utility" for GSE-backed scoring.
- Lenders rapidly adopt VantageScore 4.0 for non-GSE loans (portfolio loans, jumbos) as a bargaining chip, normalizing the alternative across the market.
- FICO walks back the $10 price under political pressure — 2026 guide gets cut, forward multiple compresses further.
- Pace of CEO selling signals he doesn't believe pricing power is sustainable.
- Negative book equity + $3B debt limits downside optionality in a cyclical mortgage slowdown.
- DCF models calling $400-500 fair value turn out to be closer to truth as pricing normalizes.

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: **MEDIUM**

The sell-off is partly justified — this is a real regulatory attack on the most profitable leg of a software monopoly, not a misunderstanding. The expected value is modestly positive (+20%), but the scenario dispersion is wide (-32% to +75%) and there is no clear variant perception: consensus bear case is directionally correct and hard to disprove. The CEO's heavy selling without a single buy during the crash is a significant negative signal from the person with the best information. GBMs say +30% upside, DCFs say -50% — that model disagreement should lower conviction, not raise it. The May 6 earnings print is a dateable catalyst that will either validate (Scores growth holds) or invalidate (guide cut) the pricing-power thesis cheaply.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** (a) price below $900 with GBM signals intact, OR (b) May 6 earnings showing Scores +25%+ and FY26 guide reaffirmed/raised — that would prove 2026 pricing is banked and you have 12-18 months of monopoly economics to ride before LLPAs arrive. **Would downgrade to PASS on:** FY26 guide cut, or public rollback of $10 price, or FICO walking back Direct Licensing economics.
