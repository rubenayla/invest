# Monolithic Power Systems (MPWR)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Semiconductors
**Price:** $1,353 (2026-04-15) | **Market Cap:** $66.5B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-16

## Situation Summary
MPWR is the market leader in power management ICs (PMICs) for AI accelerators, historically sole-sourced into NVDA's Hopper/Blackwell platforms. Starting mid-2025, Edgewater Research reported NVDA was reallocating Blackwell power management to Infineon (60-70% share) and Renesas — crushing the stock from ~$900 peak to ~$500 lows in mid-2025, recovering to $1,023 in March 2026. The Feb 5 2026 Q4 print was a clean beat ($751M vs $740M), followed by a 28% dividend hike and aggressive Q1 2026 guidance ($770-790M vs $732M consensus) with Enterprise Data 2026 growth raised from "30-40%" to a "50% floor." Stock has ripped +32% in six weeks to $1,353 — near 52-week high of $1,378 — heading into April 29-30 earnings.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** Post-dual-sourcing scare, MPWR has proven Enterprise Data resilience (Feb guide raise) but still faces secular share loss to Infineon on Rubin. 15 of 16 analysts rate Buy with a $1,328 PT — stock is now *above* the analyst consensus. Narrative has flipped from "share loss" to "AI picks-and-shovels back on."
- **Our view:** No variant perception. The thesis has already played out — the snapback rally from $500 to $1,353 (+170%) ate all the obvious edge. At 52x forward P/E and 24x sales, the "dual-sourcing was overblown" story is fully priced. Insiders sold $197M in the last 3 months with ZERO buys — the people closest to customer allocations are unloading into strength. On Rubin (shipping late 2026/2027), Infineon really does appear to have won majority share; MPWR's "50% Enterprise Data growth" guide for 2026 partly reflects Blackwell tail-end demand that does NOT repeat.
- **Trigger:** Rubin customer allocation disclosures during 2026 H2 earnings calls will quantify whether MPWR retains a meaningful (>30%) share or gets relegated to a minority second-source. If share drops below 30%, the 50x multiple cannot hold.

## Inflection Point
**Negative inflection already occurred** (mid-2025 NVDA reallocation) and MPWR has partially recovered. The "Enterprise Data 50% growth floor" guide is the positive data point. But the crucial inflection — Rubin share — does not get resolved until H2 2026 calls. This is a post-inflection, mid-rally name, not an entry-point setup.

## Bull Case
- Enterprise Data 50% growth floor materially above Street; if Q1'26 delivers $790M+ and raises again, multiple holds at 55x
- Non-AI (auto, comm, storage) grew 40% in 2025 — diversification reducing NVDA dependency
- Pricing power: Q1 gross margin guide 55.2-55.8% — Infineon competition hasn't compressed MPWR's ASPs
- Debt-free, $1.26B cash funds buybacks and dividend growth
- If MPWR retains even 40% of Rubin, current growth compounds for 3-5 years

## Bear Case
- **Insider selling $197M, zero buys** — management knows the Rubin allocations
- Infineon winning 60-70% of Blackwell power management already documented; Rubin likely similar or worse
- At $66B mcap, 52x forward, 24x sales — ZERO margin of safety; any multiple compression is brutal
- Stock already +170% off 2025 lows, +32% in 6 weeks — all the easy money is gone
- Customer double-ordering risk: 50% guide could include inventory build that reverses in H2'26
- DCF/RIM consensus points to fair value $100-300 range — not literal, but flags that earnings power cannot scale with revenue if competition compresses margins

## Verdict

**PASS / WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM (on the negative)

There is no variant perception here: the "dual-sourcing was overblown" thesis is fully baked into a 170% rally. At 52x forward and 24x sales, the stock is priced for MPWR to retain NVDA share it has demonstrably already lost on Blackwell and likely on Rubin. Insider selling pattern ($197M, zero buys) is the loudest signal in the data — people who know customer allocations are dumping into strength right before earnings. Expected value is modestly negative (-6%) with asymmetric downside if the Rubin narrative hardens.

**If WATCH:** Would flip to BUY on (1) pullback to $900-1,000 (40x forward, pre-snapback level), (2) explicit Rubin design-win disclosure securing >40% socket share, OR (3) insider buying resuming. Otherwise pass — AI picks-and-shovels exposure is better expressed via NVDA itself, AVGO, or KLAC at less stretched multiples.

**If PASS reason:** Wrong timing, not permanently uninvestable. Company is high-quality; setup is terrible.
