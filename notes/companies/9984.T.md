# SoftBank Group Corp. (9984.T)

**Sector:** Communication Services / Investment Holding | **Industry:** Telecom / AI & Tech Investing

**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

SoftBank Group is at a critical juncture: having bet the company on AI through a $41B OpenAI investment (11% stake), the $500B Stargate data center JV, and its 90% ownership of ARM Holdings, the stock has sold off ~49% from its October 2025 high of 6,924 JPY to 3,558 JPY. The selloff was driven by AI spending concerns (Oracle guidance miss), Stargate execution delays (disputes with OpenAI/Oracle over locations and financing), and tariff-related economic uncertainty. PayPay's US IPO priced below range. Meanwhile, ARM's royalty revenue from AI data centers is growing 100%+ YoY, and SoftBank booked a $4.2B gain on its OpenAI stake. The stock now trades at a ~21% discount to self-reported NAV of ~25.7T JPY. CEO Masayoshi Son has concentrated 60% of assets into "ASI-oriented investments."

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** SoftBank is an overleveraged AI bet with massive execution risk on Stargate, ARM overvaluation (60x PE), and unclear path to returns on $41B OpenAI commitment. The 49% drawdown reflects justified skepticism that Son's AI vision will pay off.
- **Our view:** The market is treating SoftBank as if Stargate will fail AND ARM will de-rate, pricing in a worst-case scenario. But even in a bear case, ARM's royalty model is a genuine structural winner in AI compute (energy efficiency + architecture dominance), and the OpenAI stake alone could be worth $30B+ at next funding round. The NAV discount at 21% is modest by SBG historical standards (has traded at 40-60% discounts) but the quality of assets is higher than during Vision Fund 1.0 era. However, the FCF is deeply negative and leverage is high — this is a thesis that requires AI capex to produce returns, which remains unproven.
- **Trigger:** (1) Q4 FY2026 earnings May 7 showing improved NAV and Stargate progress; (2) ARM earnings showing continued AI revenue acceleration; (3) OpenAI monetization milestones / next valuation mark-up.

## Inflection Point

**Yes, but unclear direction.** SoftBank is at an inflection — the company has transformed from a diversified tech conglomerate into a concentrated AI bet. The question is whether this is a POSITIVE inflection (ARM becomes the Nvidia of edge AI, OpenAI dominates, Stargate builds the AI infrastructure backbone) or a NEGATIVE one (AI spending slows, ARM de-rates, Stargate stalls, and SBG is left with $100B of debt and depreciating assets).

Observable evidence for the positive case: ARM data center royalties growing 100%+ YoY, OpenAI valuation increasing, AI capex still accelerating industry-wide.

Observable evidence for the negative case: Stargate disputes/delays, Oracle guidance miss spooking AI sentiment, FCF deeply negative, massive financing commitments.

**Verdict:** The inflection has begun but the outcome is binary. This is not a "recovery with observable evidence" — it's a bet on the future of AI infrastructure.

- ARM de-rates from 60x to 30x PE as growth disappoints, destroying ~$70B of SBG NAV
- Stargate JV collapses due to financing disputes, regulatory issues, and partner disagreements — SBG left with stranded commitments
- OpenAI faces competitive pressure from open-source models, valuation plateaus or declines
- Leverage becomes unsustainable: margin loans on ARM shares face margin calls if ARM drops 40%+
- Son's concentration in AI repeats Vision Fund 1 mistakes — conviction without discipline

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: LOW

SoftBank Group is a fascinating but highly speculative investment. The bull case is compelling — ARM's AI royalty growth is real, the OpenAI stake provides massive optionality, and the stock is technically oversold after a 49% drawdown. At 6.7x trailing PE and 1.3x book, it looks cheap. But the low PE is misleading (one-time investment gains), FCF is deeply negative, leverage is elevated at 4.8x net debt/EBITDA, and the entire thesis depends on an AI infrastructure buildout that faces execution risk (Stargate disputes), financing risk (margin loans on ARM shares), and cycle risk (AI capex could peak). The expected value of +8% does not compensate for the binary risk profile. Management quality is the biggest concern — Son's track record includes Alibaba genius but also Vision Fund disasters.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** (1) Stargate financing resolved and construction begins, (2) ARM earnings showing sustained 40%+ growth with path to profitability justifying multiples, (3) Stock reaching 2,500-2,800 JPY range providing wider margin of safety (30%+ NAV discount), OR (4) Clear evidence of AI monetization proving the capex cycle is sustainable.

**Would downgrade to PASS on:** ARM dropping below $100B market cap, Stargate JV formally restructured with SBG losses, or credit rating downgrade on SBG debt.
