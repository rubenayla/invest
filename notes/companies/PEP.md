# PepsiCo Inc. (PEP)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Beverages & Snacks (non-alcoholic)
**Price:** $142.54 (2026-06-04) | **Market Cap:** ~$195B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-04
**Call:** BUY (starter) — Dividend King at a near-decade-low multiple with a real activist catalyst; paid 4% to wait on a Frito-Lay turnaround

## Business Primer

**What they do.** PepsiCo is a two-engine consumer-staples giant. (1) **Snacks/foods** — Frito-Lay (Lay's, Doritos, Cheetos, Ruffles, Tostitos) and Quaker Foods. Frito-Lay North America is the crown jewel: ~80% share of the U.S. salty-snack category and operating margins in the high-20s%. (2) **Beverages** — Pepsi, Mountain Dew, Gatorade (the dominant U.S. sports drink), Tropicana stakes, bubly, Rockstar, plus a large international bottling/distribution footprint. FY2025 (ended Dec) net revenue was ~$93.9B, up ~2% reported. Net income was $8.24B, depressed by intangible-brand impairments (Rockstar, bare, Be & Cherry); core EPS was ~$8.1–8.2. Trailing GAAP EPS $6.38 reflects those write-downs, not run-rate earnings — use the ~$8.2 core figure when valuing it.

**Why it's a moat.** Frito-Lay's edge is **direct-store-delivery (DSD)**: PepsiCo's own drivers stock the shelf, control facings, and place displays in ~200k+ U.S. outlets several times a week. No private-label or challenger snack brand can replicate that route density economically, which is why Frito-Lay has held ~80% salty-snack share for decades and prices with little resistance. Gatorade has a similar lock on the sports-drink shelf. Beverages is a weaker #2 to Coca-Cola but throws off cash. The combined business is asset-light at the brand level (ROE ~44%), with pricing power that let PepsiCo push through two years of double-digit list-price increases post-COVID.

**Where they want to grow.** International (snacks penetration in emerging markets is a multi-decade runway), "permissible"/better-for-you snacks and functional beverages (the **Good Warrior** / health-forward portfolio pivot, protein, the Poppi prebiotic-soda acquisition), and—most importantly right now—**margin and volume recovery in Frito-Lay North America** after two years of price-driven volume declines.

**What could go wrong (business risks).** (1) **GLP-1 / Ozempic structural demand risk** — the single biggest overhang. Appetite-suppressant drugs disproportionately hit salty snacks and sugary drinks, PepsiCo's two core categories. This is the bear's whole thesis and it is not imaginary. (2) **Volume elasticity** — PepsiCo over-pushed price 2022–2024; U.S. consumers traded down to private label and cut units, so volumes went negative even as revenue grew on price. (3) **Consumer trade-down** in a soft low-end economy. (4) **Net debt ~$42B** and FX translation on ~40% international revenue. (5) **Turnaround execution risk** — activist deals can disappoint and a supply-chain overhaul is multi-year.

**How to think about it.** Same bucket as Coca-Cola, Mondelez, Hershey — a wide-moat consumer staple with durable pricing power, here de-rated to a decade-low multiple because (a) volume went negative and (b) GLP-1 fear caps the terminal-growth assumption. The debate is not about quality; it is whether GLP-1 + trade-down permanently impairs the snack/soda growth algorithm, or whether this is a cyclical price-elasticity air-pocket that an affordability reset + activist margin discipline repairs. See sibling staples notes [HSY.md](HSY.md) and [KO.md](KO.md).

## Situation Summary

PepsiCo is roughly flat over five years ($135 in 2021 → $142.54 now), ~17% below its 52-week high of $171.48 and just off a 52-week low of $127.60. It has been dead money while the S&P compounded — a genuine value-investor setup *if* the growth algorithm isn't broken. Three things changed the picture in the last year:

1. **Elliott's ~$4B activist stake (Sept 2025).** One of the world's most effective activists targeted exactly the right thing: bloated North-American cost structure and a sprawling SKU count. On **8 Dec 2025** PepsiCo formalized an agreement to **cut its U.S. product lineup ~20%**, reduce prices on core brands (affordability reset), close/automate Frito-Lay plants, cut U.S./Canada headcount, and run a full North-America supply-chain review with results due **late 2026**. This is a concrete, monitorable margin catalyst, not a vague "review."
2. **Frito-Lay inflection guidance.** On the Q4 call management was unusually explicit: it *expects Frito-Lay to grow volume, net revenue, AND operating margin in 2026* — i.e. it is calling the bottom on the volume decline via the affordability reset.
3. **Q1 2026 beat (15 Apr 2026).** Adjusted EPS **$1.61 vs $1.55** consensus; revenue **+8.5% to $19.44B** vs $18.89B expected. Early evidence the reset is landing.

Wall Street is lukewarm — 21 analysts, mean rating **Hold**, mean target **$171** (≈+20%), low $132 (≈-7%), high $195. The cautious consensus is the opportunity: nobody is excited, so you are not paying for excitement.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** "Quality but structurally challenged staple; GLP-1 caps growth, turnaround is show-me. Hold, fairly valued, ~$171 if it executes." Reflected in the decade-low multiple.
- **Our view:** The market is pricing a *permanent* growth impairment (GLP-1 + trade-down) into a franchise that, on the evidence, is having a *cyclical* price-elasticity air-pocket. PepsiCo created its own volume problem by over-pricing; the fix (cut SKUs, cut price on core, restore affordability) is squarely within management's control and is exactly what Elliott is forcing. Frito-Lay's ~80% share and DSD moat are undamaged. You are buying a Dividend King (53 consecutive annual raises, +5% in 2025) at ~15.6x forward / ~17x normalized core EPS vs a ~22–24x decade average, with a **4.0% yield** that pays you to wait and an activist holding management's feet to the fire. GLP-1 is a real but slow, partial headwind — it bites mix and unit growth at the margin over years, not a step-change that justifies a permanent staple de-rate.
- **Trigger:** 2H-2026 Frito-Lay volume turning positive (confirming the Q4 inflection call), the late-2026 supply-chain review delivering a quantified margin target, and 2–3 more "beat + volume recovering" prints. Each unwinds the structural-impairment discount.

## Entry / Exit

- **Entry:** Current ~$142 is a reasonable starter. Add aggressively below ~$135 (toward the 52-week low / dividend-yield floor ~4.3%).
- **Thesis-break price / what changes my mind:** Frito-Lay North America volume stays negative through 2H-2026 (the affordability reset failing), OR core EPS *declines* in FY2026, OR dividend growth stalls/freezes. Any of those breaks the "recovering King, paid to wait" thesis. A drop below ~$120 *with* deteriorating fundamentals = thesis broken, not a buy-more.
- **Disconfirming evidence to watch:** GLP-1 prescription data showing accelerating snack/soda category contraction (not just PepsiCo-specific share loss); private-label salty-snack share gains continuing despite the price cuts.

## Bottom Line

**BUY (starter), grade B.** A wide-moat Dividend King at a near-decade-low multiple, with a credible self-help + activist catalyst and a 4% yield that pays you to wait — but the growth algorithm is genuinely clouded by GLP-1 and it has been dead money for five years, so this is a recovering-compounder bet, not a screaming bargain. Size as a starter and add on weakness / confirmation of the Frito-Lay inflection. Not an A: the re-rate requires the turnaround to actually show up in volumes, and the structural (GLP-1) overhang is real, capping both terminal growth and conviction.
