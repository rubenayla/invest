# Snap Inc. (SNAP)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Internet Content & Information
**Price:** $4.62 | **Market Cap:** $7.8B | **EV:** $9.1B

## Situation Summary

Snap is in the early stages of a profitability inflection after years of heavy losses, with FY2025 adjusted EBITDA of $689M (+35% YoY) and FCF of $437M on $5.93B revenue (+11% YoY). The stock trades at $4.62, down 56% from its 52-week high of $10.41, near its all-time post-IPO lows. Q4 2025 earnings beat expectations (EPS $0.03 vs -$0.03 est, revenue $1.72B vs $1.70B est), but Q1 2026 guidance of $1.50-1.53B disappointed vs $1.55B consensus, triggering a selloff. Key upcoming catalysts include the consumer launch of Specs AR glasses (spun off into Specs Inc. subsidiary in January 2026 after $3B+ cumulative investment), Snapchat+ reaching 24M subscribers (+71% YoY), and a new $500M buyback program. Headwinds include FTC regulatory action over children's privacy (AI chatbot complaint referred to DOJ), COPPA compliance deadline in April 2026, and Snap still operating at a GAAP net loss (-$460M in 2025). The stock carries a consensus Hold rating from 36 analysts with a mean target of ~$8.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** SNAP is a structurally challenged social media company that cannot compete with Meta/TikTok on advertising scale, has never generated sustained GAAP profitability, and the Specs AR bet is a speculative money pit. The Hold rating and stock trading near lows reflects skepticism that the profitability trajectory will sustain. Market sees ongoing regulatory risk (FTC, children's privacy) and competitive ad market headwinds. Forward P/E of 7.8x looks cheap but the market doesn't trust the forward earnings estimates.
- **Our view:** The profitability inflection is real but fragile. Adjusted EBITDA grew 35% to $689M while revenue grew only 11%, demonstrating genuine operating leverage. FCF went from $55M (2022) to $437M (2025) -- a nearly 8x improvement in 3 years. The subscription business (Snapchat+ at 24M subs, +71% YoY, "Other Revenue" +62% YoY to $232M in Q4 alone) is providing a diversification away from pure ad dependency. However, the gap between GAAP losses (-$460M) and adjusted metrics ($689M EBITDA) is mostly stock-based compensation (~$1.1B annually), which is real dilution. The Specs spin-off could unlock hidden value but is pre-revenue and high-risk. The variant perception is moderate -- the turnaround is priced in somewhat at 7.8x forward P/E, but not fully if adjusted EBITDA continues its trajectory toward $1B+.
- **Trigger:** (1) Q1 2026 earnings (late April) proving adj EBITDA acceleration continues, (2) Specs consumer launch (H2 2026) demonstrating product-market fit, (3) Snapchat+ subscriber growth sustaining above 50% YoY, (4) first GAAP profitable quarter would be a psychological milestone.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- profitability inflection is underway but early.** Snap crossed into positive adjusted EBITDA in 2024 and accelerated to $689M in 2025 (+35%). FCF went from near-zero to $437M. The key question is whether this trajectory continues to GAAP profitability. Evidence the inflection is real:
- Adj EBITDA flow-through of 32% (every $1 of incremental revenue drops $0.32 to adj EBITDA)
- Infrastructure costs guided flat in 2026 ($1.6-1.65B) while revenue grows -- pure operating leverage
- SBC as % of revenue declining (27% in 2022 -> 19% in 2025)
- Subscription revenue providing higher-margin, recurring diversification

**Risk:** This is still early -- Snap has NOT yet posted a GAAP profitable quarter. The inflection could stall if ad market weakens or Specs investment burns cash faster than expected.

## Bull Case

- Adj EBITDA reaches $900M-$1B in 2026 as operating leverage accelerates with flat infrastructure costs and 10%+ revenue growth
- Snapchat+ subscribers pass 30M by year-end 2026, subscription revenue exceeds $1B annually (vs ~$700M run-rate now)
- Specs consumer launch generates product buzz and opens hardware/AR revenue stream; Specs Inc. attracts minority investment valuing AR division at $2-5B
- First GAAP profitable quarter in H2 2026, triggering institutional re-rating (many funds can't hold loss-making companies)
- $500M buyback at current prices retires ~6% of market cap, reducing dilution from SBC

## Bear Case

- Q1 2026 guidance miss indicates ad revenue deceleration; digital ad market softens amid macro uncertainty
- FTC/DOJ children's privacy action results in consent decree, content restrictions, or fines that constrain user engagement
- Specs consumer launch flops (Meta Ray-Ban dominance, high price point, limited developer ecosystem) -- $3B sunk cost with no return
- SBC remains elevated, diluting shareholders faster than buyback offsets -- share count doesn't decrease despite buyback
- TikTok (if US ban reversed) or Meta Reels continue stealing young user attention, DAU growth stalls

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: **LOW-MEDIUM**

SNAP has a genuine profitability inflection underway (adj EBITDA +35%, FCF 8x in 3 years) and trades at a cheap forward P/E of 7.8x with 5.6% FCF yield -- metrics that look like a deep value setup. The trusted GBM models unanimously see 20-170% upside. However, business quality is mediocre (13/25), the company has NEVER posted a GAAP profit, SBC dilution is aggressive at $1.1B/yr, and the Specs AR glasses bet is a speculative $3B investment competing against Meta's dominant position. The FTC children's privacy action adds regulatory overhang on Snap's core young-user demographic. The variant perception exists (market underpricing the profitability trajectory) but is not strong enough to overcome the quality concerns.
