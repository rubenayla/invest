# Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Sector:** Financial Services | **Industry:** Insurance - Diversified
**Price:** $92.30 | **Market Cap:** $33.5B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

Arch Capital delivered a strong Q4 2025 (EPS $2.98 vs $2.34 est, net income $1.2B, combined ratio 80.6%) and full-year record operating income. AM Best upgraded its credit rating to "aa" in December 2025. However, the reinsurance pricing cycle has turned decisively: Jan 2026 renewals saw property cat rates decline 14-20%, the sharpest drop since 2014, with further softening expected through 2026. CEO Papadopoulo sold $2.1M in shares on March 10 at $96.31, adding to $5.7M+ of insider selling with no insider purchases. Q1 2026 earnings due April 28-29 will be the first quarter reflecting soft renewal pricing. The CFO at the RBC conference (March 11) stated capital returns are "front and center" as reinsurance pricing softens.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Moderate Buy -- 19 analysts, mean target $109 (+18%). Wall Street sees ACGL as a quality compounder at a reasonable valuation, expecting gradual cycle normalization with Arch's discipline outperforming peers.
- **Our view:** The consensus is approximately right, which means there is no edge. The 8x trailing P/E is optically cheap but appropriate for a late-cycle insurer facing rate declines. ROE has declined from 28% to 19.5% and will likely settle at 14-16% as the cycle normalizes. The 14-20% cat reinsurance rate decline is a significant headwind that hasn't fully flowed through earnings yet. GBM models (most calibrated) suggest near fair value at current price.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (April 28) will reveal the extent of rate softening impact. If combined ratio deteriorates above 85%, the stock re-rates lower. If Arch demonstrates pricing discipline by shrinking premiums but maintaining margins, the quality premium holds.

## Inflection Point

**Negative inflection is underway.** The reinsurance pricing cycle has turned. Jan 2026 renewals saw the sharpest rate decline since 2014. This is not a catastrophic downturn but a clear shift from tailwind to headwind.

Evidence:
- Property cat rates down 14-20% at Jan 2026 renewals
- Combined ratio ex-cat already at 79.5% (little room to improve)
- ROE declining: 28% -> 22% -> 19.5% over 3 years
- Revenue growth decelerating: 38% -> 27% -> 14% -> 8.5%
- CFO at RBC (March 11): capital returns are "front and center" as reinsurance pricing softens -- management is signaling fewer deployment opportunities

This is NOT a buying inflection. It's a late-cycle normalization where the question is "how bad does it get?"

## Bull Case

- **Optically cheap**: 7.6x trailing P/E, 1.41x book, 18.3% FCF yield -- deep value metrics if earnings hold
- **Buyback machine**: No dividend, $6B+ FCF enables 5-8% annual share count reduction, supporting EPS even if earnings dip
- **AM Best upgrade**: "aa" rating allows Arch to win business from lower-rated competitors in a soft market
- **Cycle discipline**: 20-year management track record of pulling back when pricing deteriorates
- **Analyst consensus $109**: 18% upside from consensus with $125 high target

## Bear Case

- **Pricing cycle turned**: 14-20% cat rate decline is just the beginning. Abundant capital is flooding the reinsurance market. CFO confirms margins "under pressure."
- **ROE normalization**: Could settle at 12-15% through the soft cycle, making 1.4x book look expensive (peers trade at 1.0-1.2x in soft markets)
- **Cat risk**: $547M wildfire loss in Q1 2025 shows vulnerability. Climate-driven losses structurally increasing.
- **Insider selling, no buying**: CEO sold $2.1M on March 10; multiple insiders sold $6M+ recently. No insider purchases.
- **Mortgage segment risks**: Housing market sensitivity remains; higher-for-longer rates cut origination volume.

## Verdict

**PASS** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

ACGL is an excellent business at a fair price, which is not a compelling investment thesis. The most reliable valuation models (GBM) suggest near-zero upside from current levels. The reinsurance pricing cycle has turned with Jan 2026 renewals showing 14-20% rate declines, and this headwind will increasingly pressure earnings and ROE over the next 12-18 months. Expected value of +3.1% is well below equity market base rates. Consistent insider selling with zero insider buying adds caution.

**Would upgrade to WATCH if:** Stock pulls back to $75-80 (1.1-1.2x book), where bear case is priced in and cycle-trough ROE of 12-14% is already reflected. At that level, the quality of the franchise creates asymmetric upside for the next hard market turn.
