# ITOCHU Corporation (8001.T)

**Sector:** Industrials | **Industry:** Conglomerates (Sogo Shosha)

## Situation Summary

Itochu posted record net profit for FY2025 (¥880B, +10% YoY), driven by its consumer/non-resource franchise — food wholesale, convenience retail (FamilyMart), apparel, and IT services — which now accounts for ~70% of segment profits. Unlike peers Mitsubishi and Mitsui (more resource-heavy), Itochu's non-resource tilt insulates earnings from commodity cycles. The stock has pulled back ~10% from ¥2,111 (April 8 model run) to ¥1,930 today, likely reflecting tariff-driven macro fears and yen appreciation risk. Berkshire Hathaway owns 8.53% as of early 2025 and has not sold — providing a well-known quality floor. Analyst consensus is buy, with mean target ¥2,429 (+26% upside from current).

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Itochu is a safe, Buffett-endorsed sogo shosha, fairly valued at ~14-15x earnings. Market views Japan trading houses as resource-commodity plays — rates them on commodity cycle exposure. Some discount for Japan structural slow growth.
- **Our view:** The market is mispricing Itochu as a commodity trader when it is actually a consumer/retail conglomerate with durable recurring earnings. FamilyMart alone (one of Japan's largest CVS chains) is a hidden compounder. At ¥1,930 and 14.5x trailing P/E with ¥770B FCF, FCF yield is 5.7% — historically the floor for re-rating.
- **Trigger:** FY2025 full-year results (May 2026) + continued buyback execution. If FY2026 guidance shows net profit growth, the market may finally separate Itochu's consumer profile from the commodity-cycle narrative.

## Inflection Point

Partially present. Itochu is not a deep turnaround — it has been compounding steadily. The inflection is in narrative re-rating: the stock pulled back from ¥2,200+ highs (late 2024) to ¥1,930 on tariff/macro fears and yen strength. This is a valuation reset opportunity in a quality compounder. The FY2025 full-year earnings release (expected May 2026) with buyback update is the near-term observable catalyst. Japan corporate governance reform (shareholder return pressure) continues to structurally improve capital allocation across trading houses.

## Bull Case

- FamilyMart domestic comps recover as real wage growth boosts consumer spending; Itochu's non-resource franchise re-rated to 17-18x P/E (from 14.5x)
- Yen stabilizes or weakens, boosting yen-translated overseas earnings
- Berkshire Hathaway increases stake or positive commentary in annual letter amplifies narrative
- Buyback execution reduces share count 2-3% annually, compounding per-share earnings growth above net income growth
- China/Asia business recovery — Itochu has significant food/textile exposure to Asian consumer

## Bear Case

- Yen appreciation vs USD significantly compresses overseas earnings translation (this is the primary management-disclosed risk, larger than headlines suggest)
- **Under-covered primary risk:** Itochu's retail segment (FamilyMart) faces Japan demographic headwinds — shrinking working-age population reduces foot traffic at urban convenience stores; management guides for domestic CVS growth slowing to low single digits
- Commodity prices (energy, metals) spike and compress Itochu's commodity procurement margins without corresponding price pass-through
- China economic deterioration hits Itochu's textile/apparel supply chain — ~20% of procurement flows through China
- Japan governance reform stalls, multiple stays compressed at 14x vs 18-20x global consumer conglomerate peers
