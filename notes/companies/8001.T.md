## Situation Summary

Itochu is one of Japan's "big five" general trading houses (sōgō shōsha) — a diversified conglomerate with the most consumer-facing tilt of the group (FamilyMart convenience stores 100%-owned, Dole foods, textiles, machinery, energy, finance, ICT). The stock trades at ¥1,926, 16% off its 52w high of ¥2,287 and ~3% above its 200d MA of ¥1,863, reflecting a recent pullback from the parabolic move that took peer Marubeni (8002.T) to all-time highs. **FY2026 full-year earnings are due May 1, 2026 (4 days away)** — the next dateable catalyst. Berkshire Hathaway holds 8.53% (March 2025 disclosure), increased after Itochu and the other trading houses relaxed the 10% ownership cap; Greg Abel (Buffett's successor) is reportedly committed to retaining the stake. The trading-house re-rating story is well-known, so the question is no longer "will Wall Street notice" but "is the price still reasonable for a high-quality compounder."

Compared to 8002.T which is at 52w high after a +155% run, Itochu is mid-range — better entry point in the same basket.
- **Trigger:** May 1, 2026 FY26 results. Watch for: (1) operating profit growth, (2) shareholder return policy update (buyback expansion or dividend hike), (3) any commentary on Berkshire dialogue under Abel, (4) FY27 guidance.

## Bull Case

- May 1 FY26 earnings beat with raised buyback authorization → re-rate to 17–18x P/E
- Berkshire under Abel formally announces increased stake (relaxed ownership cap allows up to ~15%)
- Yen weakens further → overseas earnings (Dole, etc.) translate higher
- FamilyMart international expansion (Asia) accelerates — segment becomes a real growth driver
- Japan-wide capital efficiency reforms (TSE pressure) drive further buybacks across the basket

## Bear Case

- Yen strengthens (BOJ further tightening) — overseas earnings translate lower; trading houses are heavily international
- China demand slowdown — Itochu has meaningful textile, machinery, food exposure to China
- FamilyMart competitive pressure (7-Eleven dominance, Lawson resurgence) — consumer segment underperforms
- Berkshire halo fades if Abel signals trimming or shifts focus → de-rating of the entire basket
- **Hidden risk (not in the news headline):** D/E rising from ¥3.92T to ¥4.62T over 3 years. If Japanese rates continue rising (BOJ hiked in 2024-2025), interest expense compresses earnings even at constant operating profit. The "low rates forever" assumption that supported trading-house leverage is fading.

## Verdict
