# Mazda Motor Corporation (MZDAY)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Auto Manufacturers
**Price:** $3.38 (2026-04-16) | **Market Cap:** ~$4.3B (ADR) / ¥700B (7261.T)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-16
**Note:** MZDAY is the unsponsored ADR of 7261.T (Tokyo). 7261.T ¥1,110.5; ratio ≈ 1 ADR per 0.25 common share.

## Situation Summary
Mazda is in the teeth of a tariff crunch: US imposed 27.5%/15% on Japan exports and 25% on Mexico exports, costing ~¥97B in FY2026 and driving an H1 operating loss of ¥53.9B. FY2026 (ending Mar-26) guidance was cut to ¥20B net income (vs ¥114B in FY2025, -82%), on 1.28M units (-5% YoY) and revenue ¥4,820B (-4%). Company returned to profitability in Q3 FY26 via volume cuts, SUV mix shift, and hidden cost-outs on the new CX-5. Strategic bright spot is the Changan JV (EZ-60 pre-orders >40K, global export push as Mazda 6e / CX-6e starting 2026). Stock is down ~21% over the last 2 months (from $4.28 in late Feb to $3.38).

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** Sub-scale Japanese automaker squeezed by US tariffs, lagging BEV transition, dependent on China JV for EVs. Analyst target ¥1,336 on 7261.T implies ~+20% but recommendation is "Hold" (4 buy / 1 sell / rest hold).
- **Our view:** Consensus is roughly right. The cheap multiples (P/B 0.19 ADR, P/E forward looks broken) reflect genuine ROE collapse (0.5% TTM), structural US exposure via Japan/Mexico, and lack of a hybrid lineup to match Toyota. The Changan JV is real but small relative to 1.2M global units. We do NOT see variant perception vs Toyota/Honda, both of which are also cheap, larger, better-capitalized, and hybrid-leading.
- **Trigger:** None dateable in next 6 months that we can handicap favorably. CX-5 launch is incremental; tariff relief depends on Japan–US negotiations with uncertain timing; Changan JV ramp gradual.

## Inflection Point
**No clear positive inflection.** Company is EARLY in the tariff hit, not recovering from it. Q3 FY26 back to profitability is a weak signal; FY27 depends on (a) tariff negotiations, (b) new CX-5 reception, (c) Changan JV exports scaling. None are observable catalysts with dates. Price action (-21% over 2 months) suggests the market is still digesting bad news.

## Bull Case
- Tariff relief via Japan–US trade deal re-rates Japanese autos collectively
- Changan JV EZ-60 / Mazda 6e global rollout adds 100-200K units in 2027
- New CX-5 + 2027 hybrid variant restores US mix
- Multiple expansion from P/B 0.2 to Toyota-level ~1.0 if ROE recovers to 6-8%

## Bear Case
- Tariffs become permanent; Mazda's Mexico/Japan-heavy production is structurally disadvantaged
- Hybrid gap vs Toyota/Honda widens — Mazda hybrid only arrives 2027
- China EV JV gets caught in geopolitics (export restrictions)
- Dividend cut (payout >100% unsustainable) removes retail/yield support
- Sub-scale vs Toyota means less R&D per unit → widening tech gap
- Sub-$3 revisit if FY27 guidance disappoints

## Verdict

**PASS** — Conviction: MEDIUM

No variant perception. The GBM/ML models say "cheap, mean reverts" but they don't know about the structural tariff regime; the earnings-based DCF/RIM models are appropriately bearish. Mazda shares the "cheap Japanese automaker" trade with Toyota and Honda, who are both larger, better-capitalized, hybrid leaders, and less tariff-exposed. Mazda-specific positives (Changan JV, new CX-5) are real but too small to differentiate. The ~5% dividend yield is at risk given 103% payout ratio. Expected value +15% is not enough compensation for a 9/25 quality company with a fragile dividend and no dateable catalyst.

**Would upgrade to WATCH if:** Stock drops below $2.80 (back to 52w low) AND Q4 FY26 results (May-26) show tariff-adjusted operating income stabilizing, OR if Japan–US strikes a specific tariff deal dropping rates below 10%. A better Japan-auto expression is Toyota (TM) for quality or Honda (HMC) for similar cheap valuation with stronger balance sheet.
