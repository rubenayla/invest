# Dollar General Corporation (DG)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Discount Retail (small-box rural)
**Price:** $105.09 (2026-06-04) | **Market Cap:** ~$23.2B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-04
**Verdict:** BUY (starter / scale-in) — defensive cash machine at a 52-week low that just beat-and-raised (June 2) and was shrugged off; trades at ~14x trough-ish earnings vs a 18–22x history, with a 2.2% FCF-covered yield paid while you wait.

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Dollar General runs ~20,600 small-box discount stores (roughly 7,400 sq ft each), overwhelmingly in **rural and small-town America** where there is often no Walmart, no Aldi, and no grocery competition within a 20-minute drive. ~80% of sales are **consumables** (food, snacks, cleaning, paper, HBA) at low price points; the rest is seasonal, home, and apparel. The model is *convenience + price for the low-income household* — the store is close, the basket is small, the trip is frequent. New formats: **DG Fresh** (self-distributed produce/perishables), **pOpshelf** (a higher-margin discretionary banner), and a growing **digital/marketplace** push.

**Why it works (the moat).** Not a wide moat, but a real **scale-and-location** advantage: DG's rural footprint is a distribution network a competitor cannot economically replicate store-for-store, and its purchasing scale on consumables lets it hit price points smaller chains can't. The customer is sticky by geography and income, not brand love. Beta is **0.28** — earnings barely flex with the cycle, and a weak economy is partly a *tailwind* (trade-down from Walmart/grocery into dollar stores).

**What went wrong (why it's cheap).** 2023–2024 was ugly: over-expansion, supply-chain cost bloat, rampant **shrink (theft)**, labor/wage pressure, and a stressed core consumer crushed operating margin from ~10% (2021) to ~5–6%. Net income fell from $2.42B (FY2021) to a $1.13B trough (FY2023). Management (CEO **Todd Vasos returned Oct 2024**) launched "Back to Basics": cull SKUs, fix in-stocks, attack shrink with self-checkout rollbacks, slow new-store growth, and rebuild store labor. FY2024–25 showed the recovery taking hold (net income back to ~$1.5B).

**The round-trip.** The turnaround rally ran the stock from ~$98 (Oct 2025) to **$155 (Feb 2026)** — then it round-tripped all the way back to **$105** by June 2026 as analysts (Guggenheim, Barclays) cut targets on *margin-recovery skepticism* and investors de-risked into the June 2 print and the late-May shareholder vote. The fundamentals kept improving while the multiple deflated.

## Situation Summary

DG sits at **$105.09, ~7% above its 52-week low ($95.11) and 34% below the 52-week high ($158.23)**. The most recent print (**Q1 FY2026, reported June 2 2026 — two days ago**) was a **beat-and-raise the market ignored**: EPS **$2.00 beat the $1.89 consensus**, and management **raised FY2026 EPS guidance to $7.20–$7.45** (from $7.10–$7.35), reaffirming net sales growth of **+3.7–4.2%** and same-store sales of **+2.2–2.7%**. Critically, the 10-Q states tariffs "did not materially impact" Q1 results. Yet the stock fell ~5% over the following sessions. You are buying a defensive retailer, on fresh positive estimate revisions, at a multiyear-low multiple, when the tape is washed out.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** DG's margin recovery to its 6–7% long-term EBIT target depends on gross-margin expansion the company can't reliably deliver in a hostile environment — tariffs, a broke low-end consumer, rising gas prices, SNAP cuts, and competition from Walmart/Aldi/Dollar Tree. With comps near the ~3.5% breakeven needed for margin leverage, it's a value trap; hence the target cuts and the round-trip.
- **Our view:** The de-rating overshot the fundamentals. Estimates are *rising*, not falling — guidance was just raised, not cut — and the business is structurally defensive with a location moat and a counter-cyclical trade-down kicker. At ~14x current-year guidance for a 2.2%-yielding, 18.9% ROE retailer with a clean balance sheet (excl. leases) and 33% payout, the bear case ("margin never recovers") is largely *already in the price*, while the base case (margin grinds toward 6.5%) maps to the analyst mean of $131.
- **Trigger:** Each quarter that comps stay positive (guide +2.2–2.7%) and gross margin holds/expands YoY rebuilds the path to the 6–7% EBIT target. Shrink moderating and DG Fresh/pOpshelf scaling are the margin levers. A confirmed tariff pass-through with no comp damage removes the biggest overhang.

## Valuation — Scenario Analysis

Anchor on the **margin-recovery path**, not the trough. DG earns ~$7.07 TTM EPS on a 5.9% operating margin; the company targets **6–7% EBIT**. On ~$43–46B of sales, each 100bps of operating margin is ~$330–340M pre-tax (~$0.50–0.55B net, ~$2.30–2.50 EPS). Historically DG traded at **18–22x**; it sits at ~14x today.

The asymmetry is favorable: the bear case (−20%) lands roughly at the 52-week low the stock already kissed, while base/bull are +25–62%. Analyst mean target is **$131** (29 analysts, range $90–$175) — the Street's central case is the base scenario, ~25% above today.

## Entry / Exit Framework

- **Entry:** ~$105 (current), **starter then scale-in**. Buy a starter now into the post-print washout; add toward **$95–98** (the 52-week low / Oct-2025 base) if the tape gives it, or on a second consecutive quarter of positive comps + flat-to-up gross margin.
- **Thesis-break price / signal:** ~**$88** (≈ −16%, decisively below the 52-week low). More important are the *fundamental* breaks: **same-store sales turning negative**, a **guidance cut** (reverses the June-2 raise), or **gross margin contracting YoY** for two quarters (the margin-recovery engine has stalled → it really is a value trap, not a trough).
- **What would change my mind (bearish):** a tariff escalation that visibly compresses gross margin with no offsetting trade-down comp benefit; the core consumer cracking hard enough that traffic *and* ticket fall together; or the 6–7% EBIT target being formally walked back.
- **What would confirm (bullish):** two quarters of comps ≥3% with YoY gross-margin expansion, shrink commentary turning clearly positive, pOpshelf/DG Fresh contributing margin mix, and a resumed buyback — any of which supports a re-rate back toward the high-teens multiple.

## Bottom Line

A boring, defensive, 0.28-beta discount retailer trading at ~14x just-raised earnings, near a 52-week low, with a safe 2.2% yield and ~25% to the analyst mean. The market punished it for *skepticism about future margin* while the company posted a beat-and-raise. The bear case is largely priced; the base/bull cases pay you 1.25–3x the downside, with the dividend funding the wait. **BUY (starter), scale in lower.**
