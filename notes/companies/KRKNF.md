## Situation Summary

Kraken Robotics is a Newfoundland-based subsea-tech pure play (synthetic aperture sonar / SAS, KATFISH towed sonar, SeaPower pressure-tolerant batteries, ToughBoy data buoys). 2025 was a record year — **revenue $102M CAD (+12% YoY), adj. EBITDA $25M (24% margin), $82M net cash**. The catalyst of the year is the **Covelya acquisition** announced 2026-03-03: $615M EV at 9.7x EBITDA, expected to close Q2 2026, funded by a $150M credit facility and a $402.5M subscription-receipt offering. Pro-forma the deal: 2025E combined revenue $364M, adj. EBITDA $88M, net leverage 0.3x, share count rising from 321.8M (FD pre) to 385.1M (FD post-deal). Stand-alone 2026 guidance is $165-175M revenue (+62-71% YoY) plus an additional ~$200M from Covelya. The stock has retraced ~27% from a $10.72 CAD 52w high to $7.84 CAD as the equity raise dilution and integration risk filtered through. 4 analysts cover (mean PT $11.13 CAD, range $6.50-$14.00, "hold").

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Pure-play on subsea defense / undersea-cable security / AUKUS / unmanned underwater vehicles. Anduril relationship pulls Dive-LD battery demand. Compounder thesis with accretive M&A. The story is well-known on FinTwit and the stock has tripled off lows ($2.24 → $7.84).
- **Our view:** Three things that are underappreciated:
  1. **GAAP profitability collapsed in 2025 ($20M → $2.9M net income)**, mostly because (a) 2024 had a one-time -$8.1M tax benefit and (b) SG&A nearly doubled ($21M → $39.5M, +88% YoY) ahead of revenue (+12%). The "operating leverage will inflect" thesis requires SG&A growth to slow — there's no proof of that yet.
  2. **FCF is structurally negative right now** (-$28.8M in 2025) because of the $30.3M Nova Scotia battery-plant capex. They're funded by serial equity issuance: $109.8M issued in 2025, $67M in 2024, $402.5M coming for Covelya. Per-share returns will lag revenue per share.
  3. **The Covelya deal is binary in the short term.** Subscription receipts unwind if the deal isn't closed by 2026-12-31; the $402.5M raise is conditional on close. That's a hard deadline and a real risk.
- **Trigger:** Covelya close (Q2 2026 expected). Clean close with on-track integration commentary unlocks the post-deal multiple compression (~9x EV/Sales pro-forma, ~34x EV/EBITDA on combined $88M). Slip past Q3 derates the stock back to $5-6 CAD.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — but it's a deal-driven inflection, not an organic one.** The Covelya transaction more than triples revenue in one stroke, diversifies away from pure-Kraken concentration risk, and turns the combined business into a $364M-revenue, 24%-margin defense-tech mid-cap. The inflection is binary: deal closes clean → multiple compresses to defensible levels and earnings ramp. Deal slips → stock retests $5-6.

## Bull Case

- Covelya closes Q2 2026 clean; pro-forma Q3-Q4 reports validate the $364M / $88M EBITDA pro-forma; multiple stays put or expands modestly.
- Anduril Dive-LD wins additional Navy CAMP/XLUUV funding → step-function in SeaPower battery orders.
- Operating leverage finally shows up on stand-alone Kraken: SG&A growth slows below revenue growth in 2026; gross margin holds at 60%+; GAAP NI inflects positive.
- TSX uplisting (mentioned by tweet author) brings index inclusion → forced passive buying.
- AUKUS submarine + EU subsea cable defense narratives keep institutional inflows tilted toward subsea pure-plays.

## Bear Case

- **Subscription-receipt unwind risk** (disclosed in deck): if Covelya isn't closed by 2026-12-31, the $402.5M raise unwinds and the deal collapses. Stock derates to $5-6.
- **SG&A keeps outpacing revenue** in 2026; operating leverage thesis fails to materialize; market loses patience with "adj. EBITDA story."
- **Equity-issuance dependence**: another raise within 18 months for organic growth or a follow-up deal further dilutes per-share returns.
- **Anduril concentration**: Anduril is private and not contractually obligated; if a US Navy XLUUV procurement slips right or Anduril's Dive-LD program loses funding, Kraken's battery revenue takes a step-down.
- **Defense procurement reset**: a US/Canadian budget squeeze or peace-related defense de-rating shaves 30%+ off the multiple.
- **Underweight in headlines, on-record in deck:** Covelya integration risk including "ability to retain Covelya talent," "potential undisclosed liabilities," "increased indebtedness post-acquisition." Bull pitches gloss over these.
