# Analog Devices (ADI)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Semiconductors
**Price:** $312.19 | **Market Cap:** $152B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

Analog Devices is in the early-to-mid stages of a cyclical recovery after the 2024 downturn (FY24 revenue fell 23% to $9.4B). Q1 FY26 revenue of $3.16B was up 30% YoY, and Q2 guidance of $3.5B (+/- $100M) implies accelerating momentum with ~20% sequential industrial growth. The company implemented 10-30% price increases across its product line starting Feb 2026, partly in response to tariff/inflation pressures, which is boosting near-term revenue (~1/3 of Q2 sequential growth is price-related). Data center revenue is a new growth vector, up ~50% in FY25 and accelerating into FY26, driven by power delivery and optical connectivity for AI infrastructure. The stock has nearly doubled from its 52-week low of $158.65 but remains 14% below its 52-week high of $363.20.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** ADI is a high-quality analog semiconductor company in recovery mode. 21 buys, 2 holds, 0 sells. Mean target $385. The market views this as a straightforward cyclical recovery play with some AI optionality. Priced at ~27x on FY26 run-rate earnings, the recovery is mostly priced in.
- **Our view:** The consensus is largely correct -- this is a quality compounder, but at current prices the recovery is well-discounted. The stock has already re-rated significantly from the trough. The AI data center angle is real but ADI's share of that TAM is modest compared to pure-play AI semiconductor companies. The 10-30% price increases provide a one-time revenue boost but may create demand elasticity risk if the industrial cycle softens. At 27x forward earnings with mid-single-digit long-term revenue growth, the risk/reward is balanced rather than compelling.
- **Trigger:** Q2 FY26 earnings (late May 2026) -- if industrial momentum sustains above guide and pricing sticks without demand destruction, the stock re-rates toward $350+. Conversely, any sign of order softening or channel inventory buildup would be negative.

## Inflection Point

ADI is in a **cyclical recovery inflection** -- the trough was FY24 at $9.4B revenue, and FY26 is tracking toward $13-14B (above prior peak). This is not a structural inflection but a classic analog semiconductor cycle. The recovery is now OBSERVABLE and well-documented (4 consecutive quarters of above-seasonal growth). The AI data center angle adds a modest secular growth layer (~50% data center revenue growth) but is not transformational for a company this size. The price increase initiative is a one-time margin inflection -- once absorbed, it becomes the new baseline.

## Bull Case

- Industrial recovery accelerates beyond expectations; FY26 revenue reaches $14B+ as channel restocking drives upside
- AI data center becomes a $1B+ revenue segment with 40%+ growth rates, forcing a premium multiple
- Price increases stick with no demand destruction, permanently elevating margins to 48%+ adjusted operating
- Automotive segment recovers in H2 FY26 as tariff uncertainty clears, adding another growth leg
- Buybacks + dividends return significant capital; stock re-rates to 30x+ forward earnings

## Bear Case

- Industrial recovery plateaus as price increases cause demand elasticity; customers find substitutes or delay projects
- Automotive continues to soften, offsetting industrial strength
- AI data center growth decelerates as hyperscaler capex normalizes
- Tariffs escalate further, compressing margins despite price increases (raw material and logistics costs outpace pricing)
- At 27x forward earnings, significant downside if growth disappoints -- this is not a cheap stock on any metric

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

ADI is a high-quality analog semiconductor business (19/25) in an observable cyclical recovery with a genuine AI data center kicker. However, the stock at $312 already prices in much of the recovery (27x forward earnings), the consensus is overwhelmingly bullish (21/2/0 buy/hold/sell), and the expected value of +9.3% is modest for the risk profile of a cyclical semiconductor at this stage of the cycle. There is no clear variant perception -- the market sees exactly what we see.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** A pullback to $270-280 (providing 15%+ expected value), or if Q2 earnings demonstrate sustainable above-consensus acceleration that shifts the FY27 EPS trajectory toward $14+. Also would upgrade if AI data center revenue growth accelerates to suggest a structural re-rating rather than cyclical recovery.
