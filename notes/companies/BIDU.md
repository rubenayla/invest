# Baidu, Inc. (BIDU)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Internet Content & Information
**Price:** $137.68 (2026-05-20) | **Market Cap:** ~$46.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-05-20

## Business Primer

**What they do today.** Baidu runs the dominant general-purpose search engine in mainland China — the place a Chinese internet user types a question into when they want a list of web pages, the same way an English-speaking user types into Google. China's internet is effectively walled off from the global one: Google search has been blocked there since 2010, so a domestic search engine had room to grow. Baidu makes money mostly by selling text ads against those search results: a hospital, training school, e-commerce shop, or local services business pays Baidu to appear at the top of the page when a user searches for "back pain doctor Shanghai" or "English tutoring." That advertising business — called Online Marketing Services in the filings — was Baidu's historical engine but is now shrinking: Q1 2026 ad revenue was RMB 12.6 billion, down 22% year-on-year. Around the search engine, Baidu also operates Baidu Map (the most-used mapping app in China), Baidu Wenku and Baidu Baike (a paid document/knowledge repository and a Chinese-language encyclopedia analogous to Wikipedia), and an "AI Cloud" arm that rents out graphics-processing-unit compute and a proprietary large-language-model called ERNIE to Chinese enterprises that cannot easily buy from AWS, Azure, or OpenAI. Finally, Baidu owns Apollo Go, a driverless robotaxi service that as of Q1 2026 ran 3.2 million paid rides in the quarter across cities including Wuhan, Chongqing, and Beijing — passengers hail a car with no human driver via an app, the same model as Waymo in the United States. iQiyi (a Netflix-style long-form video site Baidu controls) is listed separately and not the focus here.

**Why customers choose them (the value proposition).** Three distinct buyer groups, three distinct reasons. (1) Chinese end-users open Baidu instead of WeChat search or Douyin search because Baidu has indexed the open Chinese-language web for two decades — it returns web pages, PDFs, encyclopedia entries, forum threads, and academic articles, where WeChat only searches inside WeChat's walled garden of articles and chats, and Douyin returns short videos. If you want a textual answer about a medical condition, a legal procedure, a software bug, or a homework problem, Baidu still returns the broadest set of indexed sources, and it owns the Wenku/Baike content people end up on. (2) Advertisers pay Baidu instead of buying Douyin or WeChat ads because a user typing "lawyer divorce Beijing" into Baidu has explicit purchase intent — they are looking for that exact service right now — which converts to a phone call or visit at a much higher rate than someone passively scrolling short videos. Baidu also lets advertisers geo-target down to district level and bid by keyword. (3) Enterprise cloud customers (banks, government bureaus, manufacturers) buy AI Cloud and ERNIE access from Baidu because the United States restricts Chinese firms from buying NVIDIA's most advanced GPUs and from using OpenAI/Anthropic APIs, leaving a small set of domestic providers — Baidu, Alibaba, Tencent, Huawei — as the only places to rent large-language-model capacity that runs on hardware approved for Chinese soil. Baidu was the first Chinese firm to ship a ChatGPT-equivalent product (ERNIE Bot, March 2023) and has accumulated the longest training-data history in Chinese, which matters for legal, medical, and government use cases.

**Where they want to grow.** Management has explicitly pivoted the company around three bets. First, AI Cloud: in Q1 2026 the AI-powered portion of the core business crossed 50% of revenue for the first time (RMB 13.6 billion, +49% year-on-year), with AI Cloud Infrastructure revenue +79% and GPU-cloud rental +184% — the bet is that Chinese enterprises will rent ERNIE and Baidu-managed GPUs the way American firms rent OpenAI and AWS. Second, Apollo Go robotaxi: 3.2 million paid rides in Q1, 22 million cumulative, "unit economics breakeven" achieved in the largest operational city — the bet is to scale to dozens of Chinese cities and eventually expand internationally (UAE, Hong Kong, and Singapore pilots are underway), turning autonomy into a multi-billion-revenue line. Third, embedding generative AI into the search results page itself, so search becomes a conversation rather than ten blue links — this is meant to defend the consumer franchise against Douyin and Tencent Yuanbao and eventually monetize through AI-native ad formats.

**What could go wrong (business risks).** The legacy ad business is genuinely cracking. Mobile users increasingly default to Douyin's in-app search (roughly 20 billion monthly queries against Baidu's 120-150 billion) and to Tencent's WeChat search, so Baidu's share of search-intent is eroding. Chinese advertisers facing weak consumer spending are cutting performance-marketing budgets first, and AI-generated answers on Baidu's own results page cannibalize paid clicks. Apollo Go ride economics are heavily subsidized — fares per ride are well below Didi's human-driver prices and below current operating cost, so the path to profitability depends on a vehicle-cost decline that hasn't been independently verified. AI Cloud growth is real but operating margin compressed sharply in 2025 (full-year net income collapsed from RMB 23.8 billion in 2024 to RMB 5.6 billion in 2025) because Baidu is spending aggressively on GPUs and free GPU subsidies. Capex absorption: free cash flow turned sharply negative in 2025 (-RMB 16.4 billion versus +RMB 13.0 billion in 2024). And there is the structural overhang: BIDU is an American Depositary Receipt (ADR) representing a Cayman-Islands shell that holds Variable Interest Entity (VIE) contracts on the Chinese operating company. If the Holding Foreign Companies Accountable Act (HFCAA) standoff between the US Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB) and Beijing re-escalates, or if Chinese courts ever invalidate the VIE contracts, US-listed shares could be delisted or made unenforceable.

**How to think about it.** A still-cash-generative Chinese search-and-ads incumbent in slow decline, financing an aggressive pivot into AI infrastructure and autonomous mobility — trading at a depressed multiple because investors weight the decline risk and ADR/geopolitical risk more heavily than the AI optionality. Closest mental model: Yahoo in 2012 if Yahoo had simultaneously owned a credible OpenAI competitor and Waymo, with a 30% conglomerate-and-geopolitics discount stapled on.

## Situation Summary

Q1 2026 (reported May 2026): total revenue RMB 32.1 billion (~$4.65B), down 2% QoQ. The narrative is a tale of two businesses. Core AI revenue +49% YoY to RMB 13.6B (52% of general business — crossed the half mark for the first time); AI Cloud Infra +79%, GPU Cloud +184%. Apollo Go did 3.2M driverless rides in Q1 (+120% YoY), achieved unit-economics breakeven in its biggest city. Offsetting that: Online Marketing (ads) -22% YoY to RMB 12.6B, "Legacy Business" -29% to RMB 10.2B. GAAP operating margin 10%, net income RMB 3.4B. $172M in Q1 buybacks. Full-year 2025 net income collapsed 76% (RMB 23.8B to 5.6B) on AI investment and ad weakness; FCF turned -RMB 16.4B. Street narrative: "AI pivot story working but ad core leaking faster than AI can fill"; consensus analyst rating "strong buy" (33 analysts, target $176).

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Baidu is a melting-ice-cube search business with optionality on AI Cloud and robotaxi. The market prices it at ~8x forward earnings, sub-1x sales, and 1.2x book — a "show me" stock where forward PE of 15 reflects no confidence that net income recovers. Strong-buy ratings from sell-side, but institutional ownership is thin (21%) and the stock trades 17% below 52-week high. Effectively: nobody trusts the AI line items will compound, and everyone is afraid of ADR/China-tariff tail risk.
- **Our view:** The Q1 2026 print is the first quarter where AI-powered revenue is structurally larger than legacy ad revenue (52% vs 48% of general business), AI Cloud Infra is growing at 79% with GPU cloud at 184%, and Apollo Go is at breakeven economics in its biggest city with 22M cumulative rides. That is observable inflection evidence, not narrative. A company with $46B market cap, ~$25B cash and short-term investments, 42% gross margins, and AI Cloud growing at +79% is mispriced if even half the AI bull case plays out. The forward PE of 15 implies essentially zero credit for any of this.
- **Trigger:** Three potential triggers within 6-12 months: (a) Q2/Q3 2026 prints showing AI Cloud crossing 60% of general business and ad revenue stabilizing (decline rate compressing from -22% toward -10%); (b) Apollo Go fleet milestone — formal announcement of breakeven across 3+ cities, or international expansion contract (UAE/Singapore); (c) PCAOB-China audit cooperation re-affirmation removing the delisting overhang. Any one of these can re-rate from 15x forward to 18-22x.

## Inflection Point

Yes, with observable evidence: Q1 2026 was the **first quarter** AI-powered revenue exceeded legacy ad revenue inside the general business (52% vs 48%). Apollo Go hit unit-economics breakeven in its largest city. These are dateable, disclosed milestones — not narrative. The thesis is that we are 1-2 quarters into the composition flip, before the market has re-rated. Risk: aggregate revenue is still flat because the ad melt is fast enough to offset AI growth in the short run; the inflection in *composition* precedes the inflection in *aggregate revenue* by 2-4 quarters.

## Bull Case

- AI Cloud Infrastructure compounds at 60%+ for 2-3 more years as Chinese enterprises that cannot buy AWS/OpenAI consolidate on Baidu/Alibaba/Huawei; ERNIE wins meaningful share of the regulated/government segment.
- Apollo Go scales from 1 breakeven city to 5-10 cities by 2027, achieving net profitability and announcing a multi-year fleet expansion plan; market starts assigning a separate sum-of-parts value to the autonomy unit.
- Online marketing decline rate decompresses from -22% toward -5% as macro stabilizes and AI-native ad formats start monetizing.
- Buybacks ($172M Q1 = ~$700M annualized at 1.5% yield) continue or accelerate with $25B in cash on balance sheet.
- PCAOB-China cooperation holds, ADR discount narrows from current "double-digit Chinese-stock discount" toward parity with global peers.

## Bear Case

- Ad decline accelerates past -25% YoY as Douyin/WeChat in-app search take more high-intent queries; AI growth cannot offset and aggregate revenue inflects negative.
- **AI Cloud growth is propped up by GPU rental subsidies and customer concessions, not durable demand** — this is the risk under-covered by news headlines, surfaced only in margin commentary (operating margin compression from 16% to 10% in one year). If the GPU-cloud +184% growth was "bought" via below-cost pricing, the headline number is a vanity metric and AI Cloud margin compresses further before any plateau.
- Apollo Go breakeven economics depend on fares that may not be sustainable; subsidy-led rides are not durable unit economics — Didi competition and regulatory pricing intervention could push fares lower.
- HFCAA / PCAOB cooperation breaks down under renewed US-China tariff tension; BIDU forced into Hong Kong primary listing, ADR trades at 30-50% discount or is delisted.
- Robin Li capital allocation: continued aggressive AI capex keeps FCF negative for 2-3 more years, balance sheet still strong enough but earnings stay depressed and the multiple compresses further.

## Verdict

**BUY** — Conviction: MEDIUM

Three things converge: (1) clear variant perception — the market is pricing the legacy melt but not the AI composition flip that just crossed 50% for the first time; (2) observable inflection evidence — Q1 2026 was the first 52%-AI quarter and Apollo Go hit breakeven economics in its biggest city, both disclosed not narrated; (3) asymmetric setup — $25B cash and short-term investments (>50% of market cap), forward PE 15, 7-of-7 GBM models say +28% to +165%, scenario EV +33%. Conviction is MEDIUM rather than HIGH because profitability collapsed in 2025 (op margin 16% to 10%, FCF -¥16B), AI Cloud margin sustainability is genuinely unknown, and the ADR/geopolitical tail is real and non-diversifiable.

**If BUY:** Entry zone $130-145 (current $137.68 is fine). Scale-in: half-position now, second half on Q2 2026 print if AI Cloud holds >60% growth and ad decline doesn't accelerate past -25%. Thesis-break price: $95 (would imply Q2 AI Cloud slowdown OR ADR overhang materializing — at that point structural concerns have validated, not opportunity). Hard size cap given China/ADR concentration risk and unproven Apollo Go unit economics.
