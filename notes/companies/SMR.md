# NuScale Power Corporation (SMR)

**Sector:** Industrials | **Industry:** Specialty Industrial Machinery (Nuclear SMR)
**Price:** $10.29 | **Market Cap:** $3.27B | **EV:** $1.92B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-06

## Situation Summary

NuScale Power is a pre-revenue nuclear technology company with the first and only NRC-certified small modular reactor (SMR) design in the United States -- the VOYGR power module (77 MWe per module). The stock has collapsed ~80% from its October 2025 high of $57.42 to ~$10.29 following a catastrophic Q3 2025 earnings report where NuScale disclosed a $495M payment to ENTRA1 Energy (a 3-year-old company with 3 employees and no operational history) under a "Partnership Milestone Agreement" for a non-binding TVA deal. This triggered multiple securities fraud class action lawsuits (lead plaintiff deadline April 20, 2026). The company's original CFPP project in Idaho was cancelled in November 2023 after costs ballooned from $5.3B to $9.3B. NuScale has pivoted its strategy toward data center power and the ENTRA1/TVA 6-GW deployment, but has zero binding orders, minimal revenue ($31.5M in 2025, down 15% YoY), and is burning ~$460M/year in cash. The silver lining: $1.3B in liquidity after aggressive ATM dilution ($750M in Q4 2025 alone), NRC-certified design advantage, and massive secular tailwinds from AI data center power demand and bipartisan nuclear policy support.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Wall Street is split. 13 analysts with a $20.73 mean target (roughly +100% from current), but recommendations range from Strong Buy to Strong Sell. The bull narrative centers on: only NRC-certified SMR design = unassailable first-mover advantage, 6 GW TVA pipeline = massive future revenue, data center power demand creates urgency for nuclear buildout, and the stock is "cheap" after the 80% drawdown. Bears see a company that paid $495M to a shell company, has no binding contracts, is burning cash at $460M/year, faces class action lawsuits, and doubled authorized shares for further dilution.
- **Our view:** The market is STILL too optimistic on execution timeline and too dismissive of the ENTRA1 red flags. The NRC certification is a real moat, but it is a moat around an empty castle -- NuScale has no revenue pipeline that converts to cash in the next 3-5 years. The TVA/ENTRA1 deal is a non-binding MOU with a company that Guggenheim Securities described as "an entity supporting the activities of a single individual." The $495M milestone payment for a non-binding agreement is either terrible capital allocation or something worse. Even if legitimate demand exists, NuScale's VOYGR economics are unproven: the Idaho CFPP cost escalated from $58/MWh to $89/MWh before cancellation, and competitors (GE Vernova BWRX-300, TerraPower Natrium, Oklo) are catching up on the regulatory front. The variant perception: the 80% drawdown has NOT made this stock cheap -- at 104x P/S with no path to profitability before 2030+, the stock is still a speculative option on nuclear policy execution, not a value play.
- **Trigger:** Next earnings report (likely May 2026) to see Q1 2026 cash burn rate and any new binding agreements. Resolution or progression of the class action lawsuits. Any actual binding contract (not MOU) with a creditworthy counterparty would be a genuine catalyst.

## Inflection Point

**No observable inflection. This is a company that had an ANTI-inflection in 2025.**

- The Idaho CFPP cancellation (Nov 2023) was the original thesis-breaker -- the one real project died due to cost overruns
- The ENTRA1/TVA announcement (Sep 2025) was supposed to be the new inflection, but has been exposed as a non-binding deal with a questionable counterparty
- The $495M ENTRA1 payment and subsequent class action lawsuits represent a NEGATIVE inflection -- trust in management has been severely damaged
- R&D spending has been CUT (from $156M to $46M), suggesting the company is pulling back from development, not pushing forward
- Revenue declined in 2025 -- the trend is backward, not forward

**For a real inflection to emerge, NuScale needs:** (1) a binding contract with a creditworthy utility/data center operator (not ENTRA1), (2) a clear deployment site with regulatory approvals and financing secured, and (3) evidence that VOYGR economics work at scale ($60-80/MWh without massive federal subsidies). None of these exist today.

## Bull Case

- **Only NRC-certified SMR design** provides 3-5 year head start over competitors; certification took 10+ years and is a genuine barrier to entry
- **AI data center power demand** is creating unprecedented urgency for new baseload generation; nuclear is the only carbon-free option that can provide 24/7 power at scale
- **Bipartisan nuclear policy support:** ADVANCE Act, IRA tax credits ($30/MWh production tax credit), DOE loan guarantees all favor SMR deployment
- **$1.3B cash runway** means the company can survive until the market matures; no near-term bankruptcy risk
- **TVA/ENTRA1 6 GW deal**, even if only 10-20% materializes, would be transformative (600 MW-1.2 GW = $3-6B in reactor orders)
- **International interest:** Romania, South Korea, UK, and other markets are evaluating NuScale's design

## Bear Case

- **ENTRA1 is a shell company** and the $495M payment is a massive capital allocation failure that raises governance questions; the class action lawsuit could result in management changes, settlements, or worse
- **Zero binding contracts** after 17 years of development; NRC certification hasn't translated to a single commercial reactor order
- **VOYGR economics are unproven and possibly uncompetitive:** Idaho CFPP escalated from $58/MWh to $89/MWh before cancellation; at $89/MWh, SMRs are more expensive than solar+storage ($40-60/MWh) and utility-scale wind ($30-50/MWh)
- **Competition is real and accelerating:** GE Vernova BWRX-300 has a committed customer (OPG), TerraPower has DOE funding and a demo site, Rolls-Royce has UK government backing
- **Dilution will continue:** $1B ATM program, 662M authorized shares (vs 319M outstanding). At current burn rates, NuScale needs to raise $100-200M/year minimum, diluting existing shareholders 5-10% annually
- **Deployment timeline is decade-long:** Even the optimistic TVA timeline has first power in the early 2030s. That's 4-7 more years of cash burn with no product revenue
- **Management credibility is damaged:** Class action lawsuits, insider selling, questionable ENTRA1 partnership all suggest governance issues
- **26% short interest** reflects broad institutional skepticism

## Verdict

**PASS** -- Conviction: HIGH

NuScale Power has a genuinely valuable asset in its NRC-certified SMR design -- the only one in the United States -- and the secular tailwinds for nuclear power from AI data center demand and bipartisan policy support are real. These are not disputed.

However, the investment case collapses under scrutiny of execution, management quality, and valuation:

1. **No binding contracts after 17 years of development.** The Idaho CFPP was cancelled. The TVA/ENTRA1 deal is a non-binding MOU with a 3-employee company that Guggenheim called "an entity supporting the activities of a single individual."
2. **The ENTRA1 payment is disqualifying.** Paying $495M to a shell company for a non-binding agreement -- regardless of whether it's legally defensible -- demonstrates either terrible judgment or worse. This is a management team that cannot be trusted with shareholder capital.
3. **The math doesn't work at current prices.** At 104x P/S on declining $31.5M revenue, with $460M/year cash burn, guaranteed further dilution, and no product revenue for 4-7 years, the risk/reward is deeply unfavorable. Even after an 80% decline, the stock is not cheap.
4. **Competition is closing the gap.** GE Vernova's BWRX-300 has a committed customer and is advancing through NRC review. TerraPower has Bill Gates' backing and DOE funding. NuScale's first-mover advantage is a depreciating asset.
5. **The 26% short interest tells you what the smart money thinks.** This is one of the most heavily shorted stocks in the market.

The bull case requires believing that (a) ENTRA1/TVA converts to binding orders, (b) VOYGR economics work at scale despite the CFPP failure, (c) NuScale beats competitors to deployment despite having no active construction site, and (d) dilution doesn't destroy shareholder value over 4-7 years of waiting. Each assumption individually is uncertain; collectively, they're a low-probability parlay.

**Would reconsider as WATCH if:**
1. NuScale secures a binding contract with a creditworthy counterparty (not ENTRA1) -- a Fortune 500 data center operator, a major utility, or a government entity
2. Class action lawsuits resolve without revealing fraud or serious governance failures
3. Stock approaches cash value (~$4-5/share after accounting for continued burn)
4. A credible new CEO/management team is installed with a clean break from the ENTRA1 episode
5. GE Vernova BWRX-300 fails NRC certification, preserving NuScale's monopoly on certified SMR designs
