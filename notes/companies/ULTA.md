# Ulta Beauty, Inc. (ULTA)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Specialty Retail (Beauty)
**Price:** $467 (2026-06-03) | **Market Cap:** ~$20.4B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-03

## Business Primer

**What they do today.** Ulta Beauty is the largest specialty beauty retailer in the United States — ~1,521 US stores plus 87 company-operated international locations (Mexico), and a meaningful e-commerce business. Its differentiator is that it sells mass *and* prestige beauty under one roof (drugstore brands next to Estée Lauder, Dior, Chanel-adjacent prestige), plus a salon/services business inside most stores. The economic engine is the Ultamate Rewards loyalty program — ~45M active members driving roughly 95% of sales — which gives Ulta a first-party data and repeat-purchase advantage that pure wholesale or pure-online competitors lack. The model is high-ROE specialty retail: ~43% gross margin, ~14% operating margin, very high inventory turns, and a 47% return on equity.

**Where they want to grow.** Three levers. First, the core US store fleet still has runway (management targets ~1,800 US stores long-term) plus remodels and the newer small-format/wellness concepts. Second, international and new channels — the Mexico build-out, a Ulta Beauty Marketplace (third-party digital assortment), and a media network (retail advertising, high-margin). Third, capital return at scale: Ulta is a buyback machine, repurchasing ~$900M–$1B/year historically and accelerating to **$555M in Q1 FY2026 alone**, shrinking the share count from ~50M (2022) to ~43.6M now (~13% in three years).

**What could go wrong (business risks).** The competitive set has intensified materially. Amazon is now the largest beauty retailer by volume; Sephora's shop-in-shop inside Kohl's directly attacks Ulta's mass-prestige niche; TikTok Shop is taking share in impulse/viral beauty; and Target — which has ~74% store overlap with Ulta — is building out its own beauty assortment. Ulta's own shop-in-shop partnership with Target was not renewed and winds down by **August 2026** (small revenue, but a strategic retrenchment signal). Operating margin has already drifted down from a ~16% post-COVID peak (FY ended Jan 2024) to ~14% as Ulta reinvests to defend share. Tariffs are an emerging 2026 H2 overhang for the beauty supply chain, and the category itself is normalizing after a multi-year post-COVID boom.

**How to think about it.** A dominant, loyalty-moated specialty retailer with fortress unit economics (47% ROE, ~$1B FCF, net cash ex-leases) that is reinvesting through a more competitive phase — so margins compress modestly while the franchise stays healthy. The bet is that the loyalty + mass-prestige-under-one-roof model is durable enough that mid-single-digit comps and a shrinking share count compound earnings even without margin expansion. The bear bet is that Amazon/Sephora/TikTok structurally erode share and margins, turning Ulta into a low-growth retailer that deserves a low-teens multiple permanently.

## Situation Summary

ULTA ran from ~$365 (mid-2024) to a ~$685 peak in February 2026, then sold off ~32% to ~$467 by early June 2026. The drawdown came in two legs: (1) March 2026 — conservative full-year FY2026 guidance (comps +2.5–3.5%, "only modest leverage" on margins) disappointed after the run-up; (2) **June 3, 2026 — the stock fell ~6% (from $495 to $467) the day after Q1 FY2026 results**, despite a clear beat-and-raise. Q1 (ended May 2, 2026): net sales **$3.16B (+11.1%)**, **comps +5.3%** (well above the 2.5–3.5% full-year guide), operating income +11.6% to $448M, diluted **EPS $7.74 (+15.5%)**, and $555M of buybacks executed in the quarter. Management *raised* full-year EPS guidance to **$28.36–$28.80** but kept the comp and sales ranges unchanged — implying expected H2 deceleration (tariffs, tougher compares, Target wind-down). The market sold the cautious framing, not the actual print.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Beauty's post-COVID boom is over; Amazon, Sephora/Kohl's, and TikTok Shop will grind down Ulta's share and margins; "only modest leverage" confirms the golden-margin era is gone; a beat-and-raise that the market *sells* is a sign the franchise has peaked. Fair value is low-teens earnings, i.e. roughly current price.
- **Our view:** The Q1 print contradicts the structural-decline narrative — +5.3% comps and +11% sales are *acceleration*, not erosion, and management is being deliberately conservative on the back half. You are paying ~16x this year's *raised* guidance and ~14.7x next year's consensus EPS for a 47%-ROE, ~$1B-FCF franchise that is retiring ~10% of its shares per year at depressed prices. The buyback is the swing factor: at the Q1 pace (~$2B annualized) the share count shrinks fast enough to grow EPS double-digits on flat net income.
- **Trigger:** The June 3 overreaction itself is the entry catalyst. Confirmation comes from the next two prints — if H2 comps hold ≥3% and operating margin stabilizes ≥14%, the multiple re-rates toward its 18–20x history.

## The Call

**WATCH → BUY (starter / accumulate on weakness).** Quality franchise, fresh ~6% overreaction to a beat-and-raise, near 52-week lows, at a below-historical multiple, with an accelerating buyback. Not a deep bargain at $467 (16x is fair, and the margin-erosion risk is real), so size as a starter and add into weakness.

- **Entry:** Starter ~$467; preferred accumulation **below $450** (~15x FY26 guide, near/under the 52-week low of $452).
- **Thesis-break (downgrade / exit):** comparable sales turn **negative**, operating margin breaks **below ~13%**, or management **cuts** FY2026/FY2027 EPS guidance — any of these signals structural competitive erosion rather than cyclical normalization.
- **What would change my mind (to higher conviction BUY):** two consecutive quarters of comps ≥3% with operating margin holding ≥14%, confirming the competition is being absorbed without margin damage.
