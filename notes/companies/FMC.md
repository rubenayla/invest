# FMC Corporation (FMC)

**Sector:** Basic Materials | **Industry:** Agricultural Inputs
**Price:** $17.42 (2026-04-16) | **Market Cap:** $2.18B | **EV:** $5.82B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-16

## Situation Summary
FMC fell 72% in 2025 as the global ag-chem destocking cycle extended into a third year, compounded by the imminent expiration of core Rynaxypyr (chlorantraniliprole) patents — historically 30-40% of revenue. 2025 results were brutal: revenue -18% to $3.47B, GAAP net loss of -$2.24B (goodwill/intangible impairments), negative FCF (-$177M), debt/equity 200%, and an S&P downgrade to junk. In February 2026, the board authorized a formal strategic review "including but not limited to a sale" and set a $1B debt paydown target via asset sales (India commercial business sale, binding bids Q2'26). 2026 guide: revenue $3.60-3.80B (-5% YoY), adj. EBITDA $670-730M (-17%), EPS $1.76 (-41%) — Q1 was preliminarily weak (adj. EBITDA -58% YoY). Stock has rallied ~45% off the $12 low on sale-process speculation and is now at $17.42, essentially at consensus target of $17.06.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** Transition year, patent cliff priced in, strategic review may yield a partial sale or LBO; hold rating; target ~$17; destocking bottoming but recovery uncertain.
- **Our view:** NO meaningful variant. Sale optionality is real but (a) already partly priced in after the 45% rally off lows, (b) identified suitors (Syngenta, Bayer, PE) each have constraints that make a premium takeout unlikely at $6B+ EV, and (c) even in a base-case organic recovery, 2026 EBITDA of ~$700M against $4.2B debt leaves the equity highly levered with little margin for error. GBM models (+22-122%) extrapolate mean-reversion from peak earnings that may never return; DCF (-27%) captures the structural margin compression better.
- **Trigger:** Sale announcement (2H26 at earliest, uncertain) OR Q2/Q3 destocking inflection in volumes. Neither is imminent or high-probability.

## Inflection Point
**Ambiguous — two potential inflections, neither confirmed:**
1. Destocking bottom: management says "sequential improvement" through 2026 but Q1 came in weaker than expected. No hard volume data confirming the turn yet.
2. Strategic review outcome: sale, carve-out, or "no transaction" — decision window likely 2H26. A clean sale at EV > $6B would unlock ~$30+ per share; a "no transaction" outcome likely resets stock back toward $12-14.

The stock has already rallied 45% off the $12 low on hope. The inflection is priced, not yet proven.

## Bull Case
- Strategic review concludes with a sale at 8-10x FY27E EBITDA ($750M) = EV $6-7.5B, implying equity value $2.5-4.0B or $20-32/share.
- Destocking truly ends by Q3'26, volumes snap back, 2027 revenue recovers to $4.0B+ with new AIs kicking in ($400-500M contribution), EBITDA mean-reverts to $900M+.
- Asset sales (India, licensing) deliver $1B debt paydown on schedule, de-risking the balance sheet, multiple re-rates from distressed to normal.

## Bear Case
- "Strategic review" concludes with no bidder or an unappealing private offer at small premium; stock rerates back to $12 on disappointment.
- Destocking extends into 2027 (as it has repeatedly surprised to the downside); Q1 weakness bleeds through 2026.
- Covenant flexibility expires end of 2026; if EBITDA falls below $600M, leverage breaches 6x, triggering either dilutive equity raise or forced asset sales at distressed prices.
- Rynaxypyr generic erosion is faster/deeper than -mid-single-digit guide (Chinese generics in LatAm, Asia).
- $500M October 2026 bond refinancing at elevated junk-rated coupon (8-10%) adds $25-40M/yr interest expense.
