## Situation Summary

Zscaler is the zero-trust cloud security leader, 60% below its 52-week high of $337, trading at the cheapest valuation in years (6.6x EV/Revenue, 29x forward P/E). Q2 FY2026 (Feb 2026) beat estimates with $816M revenue (+26% YoY) and raised full-year guidance to $3.31-3.32B (+24%). The AI security portfolio surpassed its $400M ARR target three quarters early and is tracking to exceed $500M by FY26 end. However, BTIG downgraded ZS to Neutral on April 9 citing competitive pressure from Cloudflare and Netskope, and KeyBanc cut its target from $220 to $160. Organic net new ARR growth (ex-Red Canary) was only 7% YoY -- a deceleration that the market is pricing in harshly.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Growth is decelerating, the SASE market is maturing, and competition from Palo Alto, CrowdStrike, Cloudflare, and Netskope is intensifying. ZS is a "good company, bad stock" -- the premium multiple is compressing to reflect slowing organic growth.
- **Our view:** The market is over-extrapolating one quarter of organic net new ARR softness (7% ex-acquisitions). Three structural tailwinds are underappreciated: (1) AI agent security is a genuinely new TAM -- Zscaler's inline architecture is uniquely positioned to secure machine-to-machine traffic, and $500M+ AI security ARR by FY26 end represents a ~15% portfolio mix that didn't exist 2 years ago; (2) Federal CMMC 3.0 mandates hitting all DoD contracts by Oct 2026 create a compliance-driven spending wave where ZS serves 14/15 cabinet agencies with FedRAMP High authorization; (3) FCF margin at 34% and expanding means ZS can self-fund growth while approaching GAAP profitability, reducing the "profitless growth" discount. At $134, you're paying 33.5x FY26 non-GAAP EPS for a company growing 24%+ with 34% FCF margins -- this is cheaper than CrowdStrike and Palo Alto on a growth-adjusted basis.
- **Trigger:** Q3 FY2026 earnings (late May 2026) showing organic net new ARR re-acceleration above 10% and AI security ARR crossing $500M would shatter the "deceleration" narrative. Federal CMMC contract awards beginning mid-2026 are also a near-term catalyst.

## Inflection Point

**Profitability inflection -- in progress.** ZS is crossing from GAAP losses to near-breakeven while maintaining 24%+ growth. Non-GAAP operating margins have gone from ~15% to 22% in two years. FCF margins at 34% are already elite. The profitability story is real and well underway.

**AI security inflection -- early innings.** The AI agent security TAM is genuinely new (didn't exist 2 years ago). ZS's inline architecture is uniquely positioned to inspect and secure AI-to-AI traffic. $500M+ ARR growing 80%+ is not a rounding error -- it's becoming a meaningful growth vector.

**Risk:** Organic customer acquisition is slowing. The inflection needs to come from expanding wallet share in existing accounts + AI upsell, not new logo growth.

## Bull Case

- AI security ARR exceeds $600M in FY27 as agentic AI adoption forces enterprises to secure machine-to-machine traffic -- ZS's inline architecture is the natural chokepoint
- Federal CMMC 3.0 mandates (Oct 2026) drive $200M+ of incremental federal ARR over 2-3 years; ZS serves 14/15 cabinet agencies
- Non-GAAP operating margins expand to 25%+ as scale leverage kicks in; GAAP profitability achieved in FY27
- Enterprise zero-trust penetration still <15%; the market isn't saturated, it's barely started for mid-market and international
- Stock re-rates from 7x to 10x EV/Revenue as profitability improves and growth re-accelerates, implying $200+

## Bear Case

- Organic net new ARR deceleration continues as Palo Alto (Prisma SASE), CrowdStrike (Falcon for IT), and Cloudflare (SSE) win competitive deals at lower price points
- Tariff-driven IT budget compression delays enterprise security platform consolidation decisions, extending deal cycles
- AI security revenues prove sticky but commoditize quickly as competitors replicate inline inspection capabilities
- SBC remains high (~15-18% of revenue), diluting actual shareholder returns despite strong FCF
- Macro recession causes enterprise customers to delay renewals or downgrade tiers, compressing NRR further below 115%

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

Zscaler at $134 is the cheapest it has been relative to fundamentals in its public history: 6.6x EV/Revenue, 4.7% FCF yield, 33.5x FY26 EPS for a company growing 24% with 34% FCF margins. The variant perception rests on AI agent security being a structurally new TAM (not just relabeled product) and federal CMMC mandates creating non-discretionary demand through Oct 2026. The risk is real -- organic net new ARR at 7% is concerning and competitive pressure from Palo Alto and CrowdStrike is intensifying. But at this valuation, the bar is low: even the bear case only implies 11% downside. EV of +40% with asymmetric risk/reward favors buying.
