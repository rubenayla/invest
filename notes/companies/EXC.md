## Situation Summary

Exelon is the largest regulated utility in the US by customer count, operating six utilities (ComEd, PECO, BGE, Pepco, Delmarva, ACE) across the Mid-Atlantic and Midwest. The company reported strong FY2025 results with adj. EPS of $2.77, beating expectations, and guided 2026 EPS to $2.81-$2.91 (+2-5% YoY). The major growth narrative centers on a massive $41.3B four-year capex plan targeting 7.9% rate base growth, driven by grid modernization and a rapidly expanding 18 GW data center pipeline (with 47 GW in potential future additions). FERC just approved ComEd's data center transmission service agreements in March 2026. The stock trades near the middle of its 52-week range ($41.71-$50.65) and carries a consensus Hold rating with a $50.88 mean target. The "Exelon Promise" initiative ($60M customer relief fund) addresses political risk around rising utility bills.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** EXC is a stable, fairly valued regulated utility offering ~3.6% dividend yield with predictable 5-7% EPS growth. It deserves a market-average utility multiple. Rate case outcomes are the key swing factor. Hold-rated stock with limited upside to $50-51.
- **Our view:** The market is underappreciating the magnitude of the data center load growth opportunity. With 18 GW in high-probability pipeline and 47 GW in potential additions, Exelon's service territories (particularly northern Virginia, Maryland, Illinois) are ground zero for hyperscaler buildout. This isn't just incremental demand; it's a structural step-change in load growth that could push EPS toward the high end of guidance ranges for years. However, the heavy capex required creates equity dilution risk ($3.4B planned), and negative FCF means the company is reliant on capital markets.
- **Trigger:** Successful execution on 2026 rate cases (Illinois multi-year grid plan filing) and conversion of data center pipeline from "high probability" to contracted load. Next earnings (likely late April 2026) will provide an update on pipeline conversion rates.

## Inflection Point

**Moderate inflection — data center demand is structural but gradual.** Exelon is not at a dramatic turning point but is positioned at the leading edge of a secular shift in electricity demand driven by AI/data centers. The 18 GW high-probability pipeline growing from 16 GW a year ago shows acceleration. FERC approval of ComEd TSAs in March 2026 removes a regulatory overhang. However, the revenue impact is gradual — this is a multi-year buildout, not a step-function change.

The more meaningful near-term inflection would be favorable Illinois multi-year rate case outcomes, which would de-risk the capex plan and potentially trigger multiple expansion.

## Bull Case

- Data center pipeline converts at higher-than-expected rates, driving load growth above 3% and supporting capex recovery through rate base growth
- Illinois regulatory environment normalizes; multi-year grid plan approved with constructive ROEs
- Interest rates decline, compressing utility yields and pushing EXC toward 18-19x forward P/E
- "Exelon Promise" neutralizes political risk around customer bill increases
- 5-7% EPS growth sustained through 2029, dividend grows in lockstep

## Bear Case

- Illinois regulatory risk re-emerges; adverse rate case outcomes force further capex cuts
- $3.4B equity issuance dilutes EPS growth below 5% annual target
- Data center pipeline stalls due to permitting delays, power market constraints, or hyperscaler pullback
- Rising interest rates compress utility multiples; 3.6% dividend yield becomes uncompetitive vs. bonds
- Balance sheet leverage (5.5x) limits financial flexibility if capex needs increase

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

Exelon is a high-quality regulated utility with a genuine data center growth differentiator and above-average rate base growth (7.9%). At 15.3x forward earnings with a 3.6% yield, it offers reasonable value but not a compelling entry. The variant perception on data center load growth is valid but becoming increasingly consensus. The negative FCF and $3.4B equity issuance dilution risk are real headwinds. Total expected return of ~14% (including dividends) is adequate but not exceptional for the risk.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** (1) A pullback to $42-43 (14x forward P/E) providing better margin of safety, (2) a constructive Illinois multi-year rate case outcome that de-risks the capex plan, or (3) evidence of data center pipeline conversion accelerating beyond current expectations (e.g., pipeline growing to 22+ GW). Interest rate cuts would also be a tailwind for the entire sector.
