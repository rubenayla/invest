# General Mills (GIS) — First Look

**Price:** $32.17 (2026-06-04)
**Call:** BUY (starter / scale-in) — decade-low de-rated staple, deliberate reset-year trough
**Conviction:** B (solid, starter-sized; no sharp catalyst, volume-repair thesis unproven, leverage)

General Mills at $32 is back to its 2018 price and **−56% from the 2022 peak of ~$73** — a
genuine decade-low for a 126-year dividend payer. The market is pricing a structural decline.
The bull case is that fiscal-2026 is a *deliberate* investment/reset trough — management gave
pricing back to repair volume, and is guiding the bad news itself — not the start of a terminal
slide. You are paid **~7.4% to wait** while that plays out.

## The setup: a deliberate reset year

GIS fiscal year ends end-May; FY2026 is nearly complete (Q4 reports late June 2026).

- **FY2026 guidance (reaffirmed at Q3, Mar-2026):** organic net sales **−1.5% to −2%**, adjusted
  operating profit and adjusted diluted EPS **−16% to −20% constant currency**. On ~$4.21 FY25
  adjusted EPS that implies a **~$3.37–$3.54 trough**, so the stock at $32 is ~9–9.5x *trough* earnings.
- **Q3 FY2026 (reported Mar-2026):** net sales $4.4B (−8% y/y on volume + divestitures), organic
  sales −3%, adjusted EPS **$0.64 vs ~$0.75 consensus** (a miss), adj operating profit −32% c.c.
- **Why down on purpose:** management reset front-of-store pricing/promo to rebuild volume and
  household penetration, lapped divestitures (yogurt, etc.), and ate unfavorable timing comps in
  Q1–Q3. The earnings air-pocket is largely self-inflicted reinvestment, not pure demand loss.
- **Evidence the repair is working:** held or gained pound share in **7 of its top 10 US
  categories** in Q3 despite the optics. Management guides Q4 FY26 to a *step-up* in organic-sales
  trend and a **return to earnings growth** (favorable comps + 53rd week), and FY2027 to "improved
  organic sales results with the price-investment work behind it."

## Bull / Bear

**Bull**
- Decade-low price, 7.4% covered yield, ~9x trough earnings, ~7.5x EV/EBITDA — deep value for a
  wide-moat staple (Cheerios, Blue Buffalo, Häagen-Dazs, Pillsbury, Old El Paso, Totino's).
- The earnings reset is *in the guidance already*; FY26 is the kitchen-sink year.
- Share gains in 7/10 top categories say the volume-repair playbook is landing.
- FCF $2.29B comfortably covers interest (~$0.5–0.6B) + dividend ($1.3B), leaving ~$1B for
  buybacks/debt paydown. Dividend is safe even on trough EPS (~71% payout).
- Any re-rate toward a still-cheap 12–14x on a normalized ~$3.70 EPS is +40–80%.

**Bear**
- Center-store packaged food faces secular volume erosion: private-label share gains + GLP-1
  appetite suppression are real, durable headwinds, not one cycle.
- Pet (Blue Buffalo) — the designated growth engine — has stumbled in mainstream channels.
- Net income has fallen **three straight years** ($2.71B → $2.59B → $2.50B → $2.30B). A reset year
  can become reset *years*; the "price-investment behind us" promise has slipped before.
- **$14.5B net debt / ~3.5x leverage** limits flexibility and caps buyback firepower; the equity is
  the thin slice ($9.2B) under a large, rate-sensitive debt stack.
- Classic value-trap risk — KHC-style: cheap, high-yield, and cheaper every year.

Asymmetry is the point: a 7.4% yield + trough multiple cushion the downside to roughly flat even in
the bear case, while normalization pays +50–90%. Reward/risk skews favorably.

## Entry / thesis-break / what would change my mind

- **Entry:** $32 is attractive; scale-in given no near-term catalyst. Add on weakness toward high-$20s.
- **Thesis-break (sell/avoid):** FY26 Q4 (late June 2026) shows **no organic-sales step-up** *and*
  management cuts the FY27 "return to growth" outlook → value-trap confirmation. A break below ~$28
  on deteriorating fundamentals (not just market beta), or any **dividend freeze extending into a
  cut**, breaks the thesis.
- **What would change my mind (bull→out):** continued pound-share *losses* in core US categories,
  Blue Buffalo share erosion deepening, or net-debt/EBITDA pushing above ~4x.

## Bottom line

A decade-low, wide-moat staple at ~9x deliberately-trough earnings and ~7.5x EV/EBITDA, paying a
safe ~7.4% to wait on a volume recovery management is already executing (7/10 categories gaining
share). It is not a screaming A — leverage is high, the recovery is unproven, and center-store food
has genuine secular headwinds — but the asymmetry (≈flat downside vs +50–90% on normalization) earns
a **B, BUY starter/scale-in**. Watch the late-June FY26 Q4 print for the promised organic-sales
step-up: that is the tell on whether the reset is working.
