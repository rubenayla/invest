# Everest Group, Ltd. (EG)

**Sector:** Financial Services | **Industry:** Insurance - Reinsurance
**Price:** $315.96 | **Market Cap:** $12.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

Everest Group is in the middle of a major strategic transformation -- shedding its underperforming retail commercial insurance book (sold to AIG for ~$2B in renewal rights) and pivoting to a pure-play reinsurance and wholesale/specialty model. Q4 2025 results showed the reinsurance segment performing well (91.7% combined ratio) while insurance dragged the group (114.6% combined ratio), validating the strategic exit. The stock trades at 0.83x book value despite $380 book value per share, depressed by the transition noise, a Q4 EPS miss, and sector-wide concerns about reinsurance pricing softening in 2026. Management is aggressively buying back shares below book value ($400M+ YTD) and expects capital release from the AIG transaction to accelerate buybacks in H2 2026.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Everest is a mediocre reinsurer that just posted a 10.5% ROE (below peers like RNR at 25.9% and ACGL at 17.1%), is losing premium from the insurance exit, faces a softening reinsurance market in 2026, and deserves its sub-book discount. Analysts have a tepid "moderate buy" with $365-385 mean targets, implying limited upside.
- **Our view:** The market is pricing Everest on its blended 2025 results, which include the drag of a terrible insurance book that is being eliminated. Post-transformation (H2 2026+), Everest becomes a focused reinsurance platform with 91% combined ratios, releasing significant capital from run-off insurance reserves. The forward PE of 5.1x implies $62 EPS -- achievable if the reinsurance book maintains discipline and capital freed from insurance fuels massive buybacks (reducing share count at 0.83x book = instant value creation). The 0.83x P/B is anomalous for a company with a 91.7% reinsurance combined ratio.
- **Trigger:** H2 2026 earnings showing clean reinsurance-only results, capital release from AIG run-off becoming visible, and continued aggressive buybacks at below-book prices. New CFO Elias Habayeb starts May 2026 and could catalyze a re-rating via clearer capital return framework.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- strategic transformation inflection.** Everest is transitioning from a blended insurer/reinsurer (with an underperforming insurance drag) to a focused reinsurance and wholesale/specialty platform. Observable evidence:
- AIG deal closed, policies transitioning Jan-Q1 2026
- Insurance combined ratio of 114.6% being eliminated from future results
- Capital release from insurance run-off expected H2 2026
- Aggressive buybacks at sub-book prices (accretive to remaining shareholders)
- New CFO appointment signals fresh capital allocation framework

This is not a turnaround of a broken business -- the reinsurance core has always been strong. It is a "reveal the hidden quality" inflection where removing a bad segment exposes the real earnings power.

## Bull Case

- Post-insurance exit, clean reinsurance platform commands 1.2-1.5x book (RNR trades at ~2x, ACGL at ~1.8x), implying $456-$570 per share
- Forward EPS of $62+ materializes as insurance losses disappear and buybacks reduce share count
- Capital release from AIG run-off funds $500M+ incremental buybacks at sub-book prices, creating a value flywheel
- Catastrophe losses remain contained (2025 was $757M, below 2024's $810M), supporting sub-92% combined ratios
- Reinsurance pricing softens less than feared, with Everest's discipline on underwriting preserving margins

## Bear Case

- Reinsurance market softens significantly in 2026-27, compressing combined ratios toward 96-98%
- Major catastrophe event (hurricane, earthquake) causes outsized losses -- Everest has large property cat exposure
- Wholesale/specialty pivot proves more expensive and slower than expected, keeping expense ratios elevated
- ROE remains stuck at 10-12%, well below peers, and the P/B discount persists or widens
- Reserve development from legacy insurance book creates unexpected charges
- New CFO transition creates execution risk or signals different capital priorities
