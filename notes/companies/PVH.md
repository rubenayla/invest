# PVH — PVH Corp. (Calvin Klein, Tommy Hilfiger)

**Price:** $72.71 (2026-06-24)
**Rating:** BUY LOW (lean) / WATCH — deep-value apparel: two iconic global brands at ~5.8x
forward adjusted EPS, ~0.68x book and a ~16% free-cash-flow yield being plowed into an
aggressive buyback (~27% of shares retired in 4 years). The catch is a no-growth top line and a
fresh ~$480M brand impairment, so the bet is that per-share value compounds via buybacks even if
the business stays flat. Want sub-$65 for a real margin of safety.
**First look:** 2026-06-24

PVH owns Calvin Klein and Tommy Hilfiger — two of the most recognized apparel brands globally —
plus its remaining Heritage licensing. Roughly **$8.95B revenue**, ~$656M operating income, run
on the multi-year "PVH+" plan (DTC/digital growth, demand-driven supply, brand-building). The
stock has fallen from ~$160 in 2021 and ~$110 in 2024 to **$72.71**, sitting at only **~32% of
its 52-week range ($59.60–$100.75)** — a genuine washout, not a dip. The question is whether
this is a cheap brand-and-buyback compounder or a classic value trap.

## Why it's on the radar
- **~5.8x forward adjusted EPS** (consensus FWD EPS ≈ $12.5 on a $72.71 stock). Sub-6x for two
  global brands is a low bar to clear.
- **Trades below book**: P/B ~0.68 (stated equity ~$4.79B, ~$104/share book).
- **~16% FCF yield**: ~$538M FY26 free cash flow (FY25 $582M, FY24 $725M) vs a $3.35B market cap.
- **The buyback is the whole engine and it is real**: ordinary shares 62.7M → 57.6M → 53.2M →
  **45.8M** over four years (~27% retired), funded by ~$520–580M/yr of repurchases — i.e. they
  buy back ~15–17% of the *entire current market cap* every year, at <0.7x book.
- Analyst mean target ~$92 (≈ +27%), rating "buy" — and beta 1.72, so this moves hard both ways.

## The bear case (lead with it — this is the crux)
1. **No growth, for years.** Revenue has gone nowhere: FY23 $9.02B → FY24 $9.22B → FY25 $8.65B →
   FY26 $8.95B. These are mature brands; PVH has not durably grown the top line in a decade. A
   no-growth apparel maker deserves a low-single-digit-to-mid multiple — which is exactly what
   it has. Cheap can stay cheap.
2. **A ~$480M impairment just told you the brands lost carrying value.** FY26 GAAP net income
   **collapsed to $25.3M** (from $598.5M) on **–$479.5M of unusual items** — a non-cash goodwill/
   intangible write-down. Operating income only dipped (to $656M from $731M), so cash earnings are
   intact, but management writing down brand value is a real signal, not just an accounting quirk.
3. **Margins have compressed.** Operating margin ~5.8% (FY26) vs ~9% a few years ago. North
   America wholesale is soft, Europe is sluggish, and the consumer is the swing factor with a
   1.72 beta into a possibly weakening cycle.
4. **Tariff / sourcing exposure.** Heavy Asia (incl. China) sourcing makes PVH a direct loser
   from tariff escalation — a margin headwind it only partly controls.
5. **Leverage + negative tangible book.** ~$4.3B total debt (incl. capitalized leases) vs ~$592M
   cash; **tangible book is negative (–$326M)** because ~$2.0B of the equity is goodwill. The
   "below book" cushion is brand goodwill, not hard assets. If FCF erodes, the buyback (and the
   thesis) stops.
6. **Value-trap history.** PVH has *looked* cheap for years while compounding mostly via buyback,
   not business growth. The risk is that buybacks merely offset slow erosion rather than add value.

## The bull case (what would have to go right)
- **The math works even flat.** At ~16% FCF yield retired into stock at <0.7x book, per-share
  intrinsic value compounds even with zero revenue growth — the buyback is accretive precisely
  *because* the stock is this cheap. You don't need a turnaround; you need FCF to hold.
- **Cash earnings are stable.** Operating income (~$656M) and FCF (~$538M) held through the
  impairment — the write-down was non-cash. Strip it out and adjusted EPS is ~$12.5, growing on a
  shrinking share count.
- **PVH+ optionality is free.** Any traction in DTC/international/digital, or margin recovery back
  toward 7–8%, is upside you are not paying for at 5.8x.
- **Re-rating leverage.** From 5.8x, even a partial move to a still-cheap 8x on flat earnings is
  ~+40%; the buyback does the rest. Two brands of this stature rarely trade below book for long
  absent a genuine balance-sheet scare.

## Valuation — my own scenario build (PVH not in the dashboard model universe; done by hand)
Anchor on normalized **adjusted EPS ≈ $12.5** and the buyback compounding it. Horizon ~2.5 years.

| Scenario | Prob | What happens | Adj EPS | Multiple | Price | Return |
|----------|------|--------------|---------|----------|-------|--------|
| Bull | 25% | Consumer recovers, PVH+ gains traction, margins → 7–8%, buyback continues | ~$16 | 9x | ~$145 | **+100%** |
| Base | 40% | Flat-ish revenue, margins hold ~6%, ~12–15% of shares retired/yr, modest re-rate | ~$14.5 | 7.5x | ~$105 | **+45%** |
| Bear | 35% | Consumer recession + tariffs, margins → 4%, multiple stays 5x, buyback slows | ~$9.5 | 5x | ~$47 | **–35%** |

**Probability-weighted EV ≈ +31%** (0.25·100 + 0.40·45 + 0.35·(−35)).

The distribution is wide (this is a 1.72-beta deep-value cyclical), and the bear is weighted as
heavily as the base on purpose — the impairment + value-trap history are real. But the asymmetry
is favorable: you're buying global brands at <6x and below book, with a self-funding ~15%/yr
buyback as the value engine.

## Entry / exit framework
- **Entry:** lean BUY LOW here at $72.71; **add aggressively sub-$65** (≈5x forward / ~0.6x book,
  a clearer margin of safety). Position small given the beta.
- **Thesis-break price (down):** below ~$55 *on deteriorating fundamentals* (FCF falling, buyback
  paused, leverage covenant stress) — that's the business eroding, not just the market. A drop to
  $55 on pure multiple compression with FCF intact is an *add*, not a stop.
- **Trim/exit (up):** ~$105–115 (≈8x, top of base case) — re-rated, no longer a deep-value bargain.
- **What would change my mind (bullish→more):** two straight quarters of stable-to-up adjusted EPS
  with the buyback intact and net leverage flat or falling. **(bearish→out):** a buyback pause to
  defend the balance sheet, a second impairment, or operating margin breaking below ~4%.

## Bottom line
A cigar butt with two world-class brands. The business is ex-growth and just impaired, but cash
earnings are stable and ~16% of the market cap is being retired yearly at below book — that
compounds per-share value even if the top line never moves. Standalone **BUY LOW (lean) / WATCH**,
EV ≈ **+31%**, best bought sub-$65. Size small; the 1.72 beta and elevated leverage mean the bear
case is a real −35%+, not a formality.
