# Celanese (CE) — Refresh

**Price:** $53.77 (2026-06-16) — **down 17% from the 2026-04-16 note ($64.73)**
**Prior call:** PASS (MEDIUM), EV +6.6% at $64.73
**Updated call:** **WATCH — upgraded from PASS.** The de-rating restored real margin of
safety and one of the prior note's three upgrade conditions (price toward <$50) is nearly met.
**Updated scenario EV (2-yr):** ~**+16%** (bear −40% / base +15% / bull +86%)

## What changed since April
1. **Price −17%** ($64.73 → $53.77). The April note explicitly said "would upgrade on… price
   back to <$50 offering real margin of safety." We're now $54, not $65 — the overbought
   "buying consensus-bullish near a 52-week high" objection is gone. The cheap-entry argument
   has partly reasserted itself.
2. **The feared Q1 2026 catalyst has passed without a blow-up.** Q1 2026 operating EBITDA
   came in at **$455M** (~$1.8B annualized, *above* the ~$1.6B trailing trough). Q2 2026 is
   guided as the year's peak quarter. So the cycle is **stabilizing, modestly up off the
   bottom** — not the further-deterioration bear feared in April.
3. **Deleveraging guidance is now concrete:** FCF guide **$700–800M for 2026**, plus a further
   **$500M of divestitures by 2027**, on top of the closed Micromax sale. Near-term maturities
   were refinanced. The immediate liquidity-wall tail is lower than it looked in April.

## What did NOT change (why it's WATCH, not BUY)
- **Still ~7x net-levered.** Net debt ~$11.3B on ~$1.6–1.8B EBITDA. The equity remains a thin
  call option on a $17.5B EV; the multiple is elevated *because* EBITDA is depressed.
- **Acetyls inflection still unconfirmed.** Stabilization ≠ recovery. The April upgrade
  conditions #1 (acetyls pricing confirmed to inflect in Q2/Q3 data) and #2 (second $1B
  divestiture at ≥10x) are still open.
- **Deleveraging is slow** — debt barely moved 2024→2025 in absolute terms; it needs both cycle
  help and continued asset sales.

improvement is the lower entry price against roughly-unchanged (slightly firmer) fundamentals.

## The honest read
This is still a low-quality balance sheet (junk-rated, 7x levered, management that overpaid
for M&M at the peak). It does not become a *quality* compounder at $54. But the **risk/reward
has improved enough to move it from PASS to WATCH**: you're now paying ~$54 for the same
deleveraging-call-option that cost $65 in April, with the Q1 catalyst de-risked. The bear is
−40% (vs −46%) and the base/bull pay more.

**Buy trigger (would upgrade to BUY LOW):** any one of — (a) price <$48 with no fundamental
deterioration, or (b) confirmed acetyls pricing inflection in Q2/Q3 2026 prints, or (c) the
second ~$500M–$1B divestiture announced at ≥10x EBITDA. Two of three → BUY MEDIUM.
**Thesis-break (exit/avoid):** net debt rising, a dilutive equity raise, operating EBITDA back
below ~$1.4B annualized, or another credit downgrade.
