# Nebius Group N.V. (NBIS)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Internet Content & Information (AI Infrastructure / Neocloud)
**Price:** $117.62 | **Market Cap:** $29.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-22

## Situation Summary

Nebius is the former Yandex N.V., reborn as a pure-play AI infrastructure company after divesting all Russian assets in mid-2024. The stock has surged ~540% from its 2024 lows on the back of explosive revenue growth (479% YoY in 2025 to $530M), a landmark $27B five-year infrastructure deal with Meta signed March 2026, a $2B strategic investment from NVIDIA, and a $4B convertible notes raise. CEO Arkady Volozh has renounced Russian citizenship, severing final geopolitical ties. The company is guiding for $3.0-3.4B revenue in 2026 and $16-20B in capex to build >1.2 GW of data center capacity — an enormously ambitious buildout that is either transformational or reckless depending on AI demand trajectory.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Wall Street is decisively bullish (7 buy / 1 hold / 0 sell, mean PT $167). The narrative: Nebius is "the next CoreWeave" but with better unit economics (NVIDIA partnership = front-of-line GPU access, Meta backstop = guaranteed revenue). The stock has already run 47% YTD and 200%+ since 2024.
- **Our view:** The consensus is largely correct on demand — hyperscaler outsourcing of AI compute is a real structural trend. However, the market may be underpricing execution risk on the $16-20B capex plan. Building >1 GW in a single year has never been done at this speed. The convertible notes add $4B of dilution/debt on a $30B market cap. The bull case requires near-perfect execution on a buildout that is logistically unprecedented. Conversely, the $27B Meta contract with $15B backstop provides downside protection that the market may not fully appreciate — the backlog ($27B+) already exceeds the market cap.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (likely May 2026) showing capacity deployment on schedule + first evidence of Meta contract revenue recognition. Also: any additional hyperscaler contract announcements would validate the multi-customer thesis and reduce concentration risk.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — Nebius is at a clear inflection.** This is a company transitioning from a post-restructuring startup phase to a hyper-growth infrastructure giant in real-time. Key inflection markers:

1. **Revenue breakthrough:** $21M (2023) -> $118M (2024) -> $530M (2025) -> $3.0-3.4B guided (2026). This is not incremental — it's a step-change.
2. **Profitability inflection:** Company is guiding 40% adj. EBITDA margin for 2026, crossing from cash burn to meaningful cash generation.
3. **Contract validation:** The $27B Meta deal with backstop provisions is the strongest demand signal in the neocloud space.
4. **NVIDIA partnership formalization:** $2B strategic investment transforms a vendor relationship into a structural advantage.

The inflection has clearly begun — this is not speculative. The question is whether execution can match the ambition.

## Bull Case

- Meta $27B contract + Microsoft deal provide $30B+ backlog against a $30B market cap — backlog-to-EV ratio >1x suggests severe undervaluation if contracts are executed
- Revenue of $3.0-3.4B in 2026 at 40% EBITDA margins = $1.2-1.4B EBITDA, implying ~22-25x EV/EBITDA — reasonable for a hyper-grower
- NVIDIA partnership ensures access to next-gen chips (Vera Rubin), keeping Nebius at the technological frontier
- AI compute demand is still in early innings — hyperscaler outsourcing trend has years to run
- Additional hyperscaler contracts (Google, Amazon) would diversify customer base and re-rate the stock

## Bear Case

- $16-20B capex in a single year is logistically unprecedented — construction delays, permitting issues, or supply chain problems could derail the timeline
- Customer concentration: Meta + Microsoft = vast majority of backlog. Loss of either would be catastrophic
- $4B in convertible notes = meaningful dilution (conversion prices not fully public) on a $30B market cap
- AI demand cyclicality: if hyperscaler capex budgets contract (as happened in 2022-23), Nebius's fixed-cost infrastructure becomes a liability
- Geopolitical tail risk: despite Volozh's Russian citizenship renunciation, the Yandex heritage could become a political liability in a worsening geopolitical environment
- CoreWeave and other neoclouds are competing aggressively — margin compression possible

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

The business story is compelling — Nebius has arguably the strongest demand signal in the neocloud space with $27B+ in contracted backlog, an NVIDIA strategic partnership, and a proven management team. The inflection is real and observable. However, the stock has already priced in much of this good news (47% YTD, near 52w highs), the 20% short interest signals genuine skepticism worth respecting, and the $16-20B capex plan introduces unprecedented execution risk. At 56x trailing P/S and ~10x forward P/S, the margin of safety is thin.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:**
- A meaningful pullback to $85-95 range (~6-7x 2026E revenue), which would provide a better risk/reward entry
- Q1 2026 earnings showing capacity deployment ahead of schedule with EBITDA margin trajectory toward 40%
- Announcement of a third major hyperscaler contract, reducing customer concentration risk
- Short interest declining below 10%, suggesting skeptics are capitulating on the execution thesis
