# Carrefour SA (CA.PA)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Grocery Stores
**Price:** EUR 15.30 | **Market Cap:** EUR 10.8B | **EV:** EUR 26.2B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

Carrefour just delivered mixed FY2025 results: revenue rose 2.8% LFL to EUR 91.5B, but net income collapsed 56% to EUR 319M due to EUR 210M of Cora/Match integration costs and a EUR 516M French tax charge. Operating profit of EUR 2.16B beat expectations but was still down 5.4% YoY. The company unveiled a new "Carrefour 2030" strategic plan focusing on three core markets (France, Spain, Brazil), exiting Italy (sold in 2025) and Romania (EUR 823M sale pending). France market share hit 22%, highest since 2015. Brazil is flat due to high local interest rates. The stock has rallied ~32% off its 52-week low of EUR 11.58 (hit mid-2025), now sitting near the 52-week high of EUR 16.19. Analyst consensus target is EUR 14.78 -- below the current price -- with a neutral/hold consensus.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Market sees Carrefour as a low-growth, structurally challenged European grocer. Analysts target EUR 14.78, implying ~3% downside. The 2030 plan's 3.5% operating margin target is considered ambitious (Morningstar projects only 2.9%). Net income collapse in 2025 spooked income investors despite being largely one-off driven.
- **Our view:** The quantitative models show wide divergence (DCF/enhanced models flag +166-173% upside vs GBM_3y showing -10%). The DCF models are likely over-optimistic -- they are capitalizing strong FCF (EUR 2.4B) at a discount rate that doesn't reflect grocery sector structural challenges. The real story: Carrefour generates enormous free cash flow (22% FCF yield) and trades at 5.6x EV/EBITDA, cheap even for grocery. But the net income trend is sharply negative, the 2025 ROE of 2.9% is dreadful, and the operating margin of 2.5% leaves almost no room for error. The 2030 margin expansion plan from 2.5% to 3.5% requires 90bps improvement over 4 years in one of the most price-competitive grocery markets in the world.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 results (April 23, 2026) showing France LFL momentum + margin recovery post-Cora/Match integration. Romania disposal completion (H2 2026) providing EUR 823M and funding a special dividend. Proof of 2030 plan execution by year-end 2026.

## Inflection Point

**Possible but not confirmed.** The case for an inflection rests on:
1. Cora/Match integration costs (EUR 210M) rolling off in 2026-2027, providing a mechanical earnings boost
2. Romania sale (EUR 823M) simplifying the portfolio and providing capital for shareholder returns
3. France market share at 22% (highest since 2015) suggesting competitive positioning is improving
4. 2030 plan providing a roadmap for margin expansion

**Against:** Net income has deteriorated three years running. The integration costs are real but the underlying operating profit trend (EUR 2.34B -> EUR 2.24B -> EUR 2.24B -> EUR 2.12B) is also declining. Brazil is flat. The "inflection" requires execution on ambitious margin targets in a structurally challenged sector.

**Verdict:** This is more of a "cheap because it deserves to be cheap" situation than a clear inflection. The stock needs to prove margin recovery in 2026 before calling it a turning point.

## Bull Case

- Cora/Match integration costs (EUR 210M) disappear in 2026, mechanical earnings recovery to EUR 700M+ net income
- France market share continues expanding; 22% is dominant and creates procurement leverage
- 2030 plan margin expansion to 3.0%+ operating margin by 2028 proves achievable (even partial delivery = re-rating)
- Romania sale + special dividend + 6.2% regular yield = total shareholder return > 10% annually
- Extremely cheap (5.6x EV/EBITDA, 0.98x P/B, 22% FCF yield) means limited downside in a defensive sector
- Atacadao Brazil accelerates if BRL stabilizes and interest rates decline

## Bear Case

- Grocery price war in France intensifies (Lidl/Aldi expansion) -- operating margin stays at 2.5% or compresses
- Brazil remains stagnant: high interest rates, BRL depreciation eat into profits and translate poorly to EUR
- Net income decline reflects structural earnings deterioration, not just one-offs -- the 2023 EUR 1.66B was the anomaly, not 2025's EUR 0.32B
- Debt load (EUR 15.6B total) limits financial flexibility; no buyback in 2026 is a signal
- 2030 plan targets (3.5% operating margin) are Morningstar-confirmed ambitious -- market may never re-rate on aspirational targets
- Digital/e-commerce investments (EUR 3B over 2022-2026) may not generate adequate returns in low-margin grocery

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: LOW

The quantitative models flag massive upside (+138% weighted average), but this is driven by DCF models that are clearly mispricing grocery-sector risk. The reliable GBM models show roughly flat returns. Carrefour is genuinely cheap (5.6x EV/EBITDA, 6.2% dividend yield, below book value), but it is cheap for reasons: structurally thin margins (2.5% operating), collapsing net income, flat revenue, and ambitious 2030 targets that Morningstar calls unrealistic. The 22% FCF yield is attractive but must be weighed against the dreadful ROE of 2.9%. The stock has already rallied 32% from its lows and trades above analyst consensus targets.

**No clear variant perception.** The bull case requires believing in margin expansion that even Morningstar doubts, in one of the most competitive grocery markets globally. The defensive characteristics (dividend, low beta 0.69, consumer staple) provide downside protection but not upside edge.

**Would upgrade to BUY if:**
- Q1 2026 results (April 23) show operating margin above 2.7% and positive France LFL
- Stock pulls back to EUR 13.00-13.50 range (200DMA), restoring a margin of safety
- Evidence of 2030 plan execution: margin improvement in H1 2026 results
- Brazil inflects positively on interest rate cuts
