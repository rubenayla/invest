# Mondelez International, Inc. (MDLZ)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Confectioners
**Price:** $57.50 (2026-04-23) | **Market Cap:** $73.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-23

## Situation Summary

Mondelez reported Q4 2025 revenue of $10.50B (+9.3% YoY), beating estimates, but gross margin collapsed more than 10 percentage points to 28.4% from 39.1%, as record high cocoa hedges locked in 2024-2025 crush through all of 2026. Management guided 2026 organic sales growth to only 0-2% (vs analyst consensus of ~3.8%) and EPS growth flat to 5% (vs ~8% expected), deferring meaningful margin recovery to 2027 when hedges reset to spot prices. A $1B one-time inventory valuation charge will hit Q1 2026, marking the accounting reset as high-cost cocoa inventory rolls off. Developed markets (North America, Europe) face structural softness with US biscuits volumes down 3-4% and Northern European chocolate requiring repeated price/pack adjustments; emerging markets remain the bright spot at high-single-digit growth.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** MDLZ is stuck in a prolonged cocoa-cost trap with weak developed-market volumes, GLP-1 secular headwind, and no near-term catalyst; analysts largely rate it buy but with downgrades on 2026 EPS cuts. Target mean $66.
- **Our view:** The market is pricing in permanent margin impairment, but cocoa spot has already collapsed to ~$3,000/ton — roughly pre-crisis levels — while MDLZ's hedges remain locked until 2027. This is a **time-value mis-pricing**: the stock is depressed on 2026 earnings that are artificially suppressed by hedge accounting, not structural earnings power. 2027 free cash flow is likely to surge as $2B+ of annual cocoa cost pressure reverses. The dividend (3.6% yield at current price) is well-covered by $3.2B FCF even in the bad year.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (May 2026) will book the ~$1B inventory charge — the last major accounting hit — and management will likely guide 2027 with explicit margin recovery commentary. That is the "clearing event" where forward earnings normalize.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — classic deferred-recovery setup.** The inflection is mechanical and calendrically identifiable: MDLZ's cocoa hedges roll off into 2027, and spot prices have already normalized to ~$3,000/ton (vs hedge cost ~$8,000+/ton in 2025). The $1B inventory write-down in Q1 2026 is the accounting "kitchen sink" moment. FCF in 2027 should recover ~$2B of the annual cost headwind, pushing normalized EPS back toward $3.50-4.00 vs 2026 suppressed ~$2.50-3.00. This is observable and dateable — not speculative.

The risk is not "will cocoa normalize" (it already has). The risk is "will volume recover in developed markets" — and that is the uncertain variable.

## Bull Case
- Cocoa hedge roll-off in 2027 restores ~$2B/year of gross profit; EPS re-rates from ~$2.80 to ~$3.80, multiple expands from 17x forward to 20-22x
- Emerging markets sustain HSD organic growth (India, China, Brazil underpenetrated); EM now ~40% of revenue and growing
- North America biscuit volumes stabilize as consumer confidence recovers with rate cuts; pricing/promotional investment bears fruit in H2 2026
- Dividend stability (3.6% yield) attracts income investors as bond yields soften, supporting multiple
- Management executes on cocoa supply chain diversification (Latin America direct agreements), reducing volatility structurally

## Bear Case
- Developed market volume declines are structural, not cyclical: consumer habit change (GLP-1, health awareness, private-label trade-down) means volumes don't recover even as prices normalize
- Competitor deflation risk: if major European chocolate makers (Nestle, Ferrero) pass cocoa cost savings through price cuts, MDLZ is forced to cut prices while still hedged at high cost — **double margin squeeze not in headlines**. Management flagged "unexpected competitive reactions" but gave no concrete guidance on this scenario.
- FX headwind from EM currencies (Argentina, Turkey, Nigeria) offsets volume growth; EM growth in USD terms disappoints
- 2027 margin recovery already priced in at current levels, making this a "show me" story with downside if execution slips
- Debt/EBITDA at 3.9x leaves limited flexibility if EBITDA remains depressed longer than expected; refinancing risk in higher-rate environment

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

The investment thesis is real: mechanical 2027 margin recovery from cocoa hedge roll-off is dateable and identifiable, the brand moat is exceptional, and FCF at $3.2B even in the worst year demonstrates underlying resilience. However, the variant perception has limited edge — "buy depressed consumer staples before cost cycle reversal" is not an uncrowded idea, and the developed-market volume trajectory (structural vs cyclical) is the genuine uncertainty that market has not yet resolved. The Q1 2026 earnings report (May 2026) and the associated $1B write-down will be the definitive clearing event; post-charge, if management guides 2027 EPS recovery with specifics, this upgrades to BUY.
