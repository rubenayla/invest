**Price:** $111.52 (2026-06-17) | **Market Cap:** ~$6.8B | **Shares:** ~61.0M | **Total Debt:** ~$2.46B (net debt ~$1.6B, ~1.4x EBITDA)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-17
**Standalone rating: WATCH (lean BUY LOW)** — a beaten-down housing cyclical trading at **0.81x book value, ~11x forward EPS, ~5.9x trailing EV/EBITDA**, with earnings inflecting up (Q1 2026 net income +61% YoY) off a depressed base. Cheap and improving, but the quality is mediocre (5% ROE, no dividend, 3 straight years of declining revenue) — buy the cycle and the asset value, not a compounder.

## Situation Summary
Mohawk trades at **$111.52, ~22% below its 52-week high ($143)** and roughly **half its 2021 peak ($210)**. It is the largest flooring manufacturer in the world, with three segments — Global Ceramic (tile), Flooring North America (carpet, laminate, LVT, sheet vinyl), and Flooring Rest of World (laminate/LVT/insulation, mostly Europe). The business is deeply tied to two slow markets: **residential remodeling** (the bigger driver, ~repair/remodel) and **new-home construction**, with a smaller commercial slice. Both have been suppressed for three years by high mortgage rates freezing housing turnover (people don't re-floor a house they aren't moving into or out of) and by post-COVID demand payback.

On the screen this is a classic deep-cyclical-value setup:
- **0.81x price/book** ($137 book value vs $111.52 price) — paying less than stated equity.
- **~11.3x forward EPS** (consensus ~$9.90) and ~16.7x trailing EPS ($6.68).
- **~5.9x trailing EV/EBITDA** (EV ~$8.45B / TTM EBITDA ~$1.43B) — trough-cycle multiple.
- **Clean balance sheet:** net debt ~$1.6B, ~1.4x EBITDA, interest expense only ~$18M in 2025 (well covered). CapEx cut from $613M (2023) to $440M (2025), protecting cash.
- **No dividend** (suspended years ago; capital return is via buybacks — share count down from ~71M (2023) to ~61M).

The reason it's cheap: **revenue has declined three years running** ($11.74B 2022 → $11.14B 2023 → $10.84B 2024 → $10.79B 2025) and **ROE is just ~5%** — these are trough earnings on a commodity-ish manufacturer with real pricing pressure (cheap LVT imports, soft volumes). 2023 even printed a GAAP loss (−$449M, goodwill impairment).

What's changing: **earnings are inflecting up off the bottom.** Q1 2026 net income was **$117.1M vs $72.6M in Q1 2025 (+61% YoY)**, continuing a recovery (FY2024 net income $515M, FY2025 $370M was dragged by restructuring but operating cash flow stayed >$1.1B). Management has been restructuring (plant consolidation, cost-out) and the operating leverage is high — when volume returns, incremental margins are large.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Cheap for a reason. Flooring is a low-return, capital-intensive, cyclical commodity business whose end-markets are frozen until mortgage rates fall — and even then carpet is in secular decline as consumers shift to hard surfaces. Revenue has fallen three years straight, ROE is 5%, there's no dividend to pay you to wait. A value trap that re-rates only on a housing recovery nobody can time."
- **Our view (partial pushback):** You're buying the largest flooring maker in the world **below book value, at ~6x EV/EBITDA, with a 1.4x-levered balance sheet**, at what looks like the trough of a housing cycle — and earnings have already turned up (+61% YoY in Q1 2026). Operating leverage cuts both ways: the same volume sensitivity that crushed earnings on the way down delivers outsized EPS recovery on the way up. With ~$440M maintenance-level capex against >$1.1B operating cash flow, free cash funds steady buybacks that shrink the share count ~3%/yr while you wait. You're paid in asset value and per-share accretion, not yield.
- **Why it is NOT a slam-dunk:** ROE of ~5% says the *normalized* returns here are mediocre — this is not a quality compounder, it's a cyclical you must buy cheap and sell dear. The recovery is entirely rate-/housing-dependent and could stall for years; "below book" is not a hard floor (it traded to ~0.55x book in the 2023 panic, $76). Carpet's secular share loss is real, and LVT (the growth category) is the most import-exposed, lowest-moat product. No dividend means no carry while you wait out a long cycle.
- **Trigger / what re-rates it:** Falling mortgage rates reviving existing-home sales and remodeling activity; two or three quarters of YoY revenue growth + expanding incremental margins; EPS pushing back toward $11-13. Analysts' mean target is **~$120 (+8%)**, with a "buy" consensus (15 analysts) — modest, reflecting a "show me the volume" stance.

## Bull / Bear

**Bull**
- **Below book ($137), ~6x EV/EBITDA, ~11x forward EPS** for the world's #1 flooring maker at a cyclical trough — asset-value downside cushion plus a cheap earnings multiple.
- **Earnings inflecting:** Q1 2026 net income +61% YoY; high operating leverage means a housing-turnover recovery flows disproportionately to EPS (toward the $11-14 range seen in better years).
- **Clean balance sheet:** net debt ~1.4x EBITDA, ~$18M interest expense, capex cut to ~$440M — free cash funds ~3%/yr buybacks at below book (violently accretive).
- **Self-help:** multi-year restructuring/plant consolidation lowers the cost base; margins expand as volume returns even without much pricing.
- **Rate-cut optionality:** the single biggest swing factor (mortgage rates → existing-home sales → remodeling) is a "when," and any easing is a direct demand tailwind.

**Bear**
- **Structurally low returns:** ~5% ROE even after recovery began — this is a capital-intensive commodity manufacturer, not a compounder; mid-cycle economics are unexciting.
- **Three years of revenue decline** with no confirmed inflection in the top line yet; the recovery is entirely rate-/housing-dependent and could be pushed out years.
- **No dividend** — you are not paid to wait through a long, uncertain cycle.
- **"Below book" is not a floor:** traded to ~0.55x book ($76) in the 2023 stress; a recession would re-test those levels.
- **Secular/competitive pressure:** carpet losing share to hard surfaces; LVT (the growth product) is the most import-exposed and lowest-moat; tariff/FX swings hit the global footprint.

## Entry / Exit Discipline
- **Entry:** attractive sub-$110 (≤0.8x book); aggressive sub-$95 (back toward the lower cyclical band, ~0.7x book), where the asset-value margin of safety widens materially.
- **Thesis-break / re-evaluate:** a drop below ~$85 on a *deteriorating* fundamental trend (not just market panic), OR a fourth consecutive year of revenue decline with margins still compressing — that would signal the cycle is not turning and the operating leverage thesis has failed.
- **What would change my mind (bullish):** two consecutive quarters of YoY revenue growth with expanding incremental margins, and EPS tracking toward $11+ — at which point re-rate the position from cyclical-value to recovery-momentum.
- **Trim/exit:** approaching ~1.2-1.3x book ($165-180) with EPS near mid-cycle — cyclicals are sold into strength, not held for re-rating beyond fair value.
