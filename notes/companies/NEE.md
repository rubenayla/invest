## Situation Summary

NextEra Energy is the world's largest utility by market cap, operating through Florida Power & Light (FPL, largest rate-regulated electric utility in the US, 12M people served) and NextEra Energy Resources (NEER, world's largest wind/solar generator). The stock trades at $89.50, up ~45% from its 2024 low of $62 but below its $95.91 52-week high. FY2025 adjusted EPS came in at $3.71, up 8%+ YoY, beating guidance. Management raised long-term guidance to 8%+ EPS CAGR through 2032 (and same through 2035). In the last 30 days: Trump approved NEE to develop up to 10 GW of natural gas generation in Texas and Pennsylvania, the company raised $2.3B via equity units, EUR 1.75B in hybrid debentures, and $600M in junior subordinated notes (March 20). A 10% dividend increase was declared in February. The company is aggressively positioning for the AI/data center power boom with a 30 GW renewable backlog and 6 GW of potential new nuclear capacity at existing sites.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** NEE is the premier "quality utility" — a core holding for income investors with 30+ years of consecutive dividend growth, best-in-class renewable platform, and visible regulated earnings. At 20.4x forward P/E and $93-96 mean analyst target (22 analysts), the stock is seen as fairly valued with modest upside. The narrative has shifted from "clean energy stock" to "AI infrastructure play," which has already driven the 45% rally from lows. Institutional ownership at 84-85%. Short interest only 2.5%.
- **Our view:** NEE is a quality compounder fairly valued — not cheap, not expensive. The 8%+ EPS growth guide is credible given (1) FPL's Florida rate base growth (population + data center demand + first-in-state large load tariff), (2) NEER's 30 GW renewable backlog with hyperscaler PPAs, (3) the 10 GW natural gas approval providing near-term dispatchable capacity, and (4) potential 6 GW nuclear expansion at existing sites. However, the stock has already re-rated from $62 to $90 (+45%), the consensus target sits only ~5% higher, and the capital intensity is extreme — $9.3B/yr CapEx funded partly by equity dilution ($2.3B offering, plus hybrid/sub debt). Net Debt/EBITDA at 5.8x is high. Management flagged $500M in added interest costs for 2026. The expected total return of ~9% (price appreciation + 2.8% dividend) is adequate but not compelling for new money at these levels.
- **Trigger:** (1) Meaningful pullback to $75-80 creating better entry, (2) GW-scale data center PPA announcements for NEER, (3) FPL rate case approval with favorable terms, (4) interest rate cuts making the 2.8% yield more attractive vs. bonds, (5) Q1 2026 earnings (April 2026).

## Inflection Point

**Mild positive inflection — AI/data center demand is a new growth vector, but execution is steady-state.** NEE is not at a dramatic inflection like a turnaround. Rather, the company benefits from an accelerating secular tailwind: AI-driven data center electricity demand boosts both FPL (Florida data center siting with sales tax exemption for >100 MW facilities) and NEER (renewable PPAs for hyperscalers). The 10 GW natural gas approval from Trump adds a near-term dispatchable generation vector. The raised guidance to 8%+ through 2035 reflects management's confidence. However, this is incremental — NEE has executed the same renewables + regulated utility playbook for 15+ years. The stock's recovery from $62 to $90 already reflects market recognition.

**Key policy risk:** IRA repeal odds at ~25% per prediction markets. If transferability of tax credits is repealed, NEE plans to rely on tax equity relationships, but project returns would compress. NEE has navigated supply chain disruptions and solar tariffs without material impact historically.

## Bull Case

- Data center power demand accelerates; NEER signs multiple GW-scale PPAs with hyperscalers (Meta, Amazon, Google, Microsoft) — 30 GW backlog converts faster than expected
- FPL rate case approved with favorable terms; Florida population growth + data center demand drives rate base expansion above 8%
- Interest rates decline, making NEE's 2.8% yield and 8% growth profile more attractive vs. bonds; utility sector re-rates
- 10 GW natural gas + 6 GW nuclear expansion provide additional earnings growth vectors beyond renewables
- EPS growth exceeds 8% guidance; stock re-rates to 25x+ forward earnings ($110-120)

## Bear Case

- Rising interest rates compress utility valuations; NEE de-rates from 21x to 16-17x forward P/E. $500M added interest cost in 2026 squeezes margins.
- AI/data center power demand disappoints (efficiency gains reduce needs, permitting delays)
- FPL rate case denial or unfavorable outcome; Florida hurricane season causes major outage costs
- Balance sheet stress: $96B debt + continuous equity/debt issuance erodes per-share value. Credit downgrade risk.
- IRA renewable tax credit repeal (25% odds) compresses NEER project returns
- NextEra Energy Partners (NEP) distribution sustainability questioned again, creating overhang

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: **MEDIUM**

NEE is a best-in-class utility with a genuine moat (FPL regulated monopoly + NEER scale), 30+ years of dividend growth, and a credible 8%+ EPS growth runway powered by Florida demographics, renewable buildout, AI/data center demand, and now natural gas + nuclear optionality. Business quality scores 19/25 — among the highest-quality utilities globally.

However, the stock is **fairly valued at $89.50**. At 20.4x forward P/E and 3.4x P/B, it trades at a persistent premium to peers that is justified but leaves limited margin of safety. The analyst consensus target of ~$94 implies only ~5% upside. The expected total return of ~11% (price + dividend) is adequate but not compelling for a stock with 5.8x net leverage, $500M incremental interest costs in 2026, and ongoing equity dilution. The 45% rally from lows has already priced in the AI/data center narrative.

**Entry:** $75-80 range (15-18% pullback; ~18-19x forward P/E) for new positions
**Thesis-break:** Below $55 (implies rate case failure + growth deceleration + rate shock)
**Target:** $100 base case (12-month), $120 bull case (18-month)
