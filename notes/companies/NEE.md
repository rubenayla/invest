# NextEra Energy (NEE)

**Sector:** Utilities | **Industry:** Regulated Electric + Renewables
**Price:** $90.96 | **Market Cap:** $189.5B | **EV:** $295.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-19

## Situation Summary

NextEra Energy is the world's largest utility by market cap, operating through two segments: Florida Power & Light (FPL), the largest rate-regulated electric utility in the US serving 6+ million customer accounts, and NextEra Energy Resources (NEER), the world's largest generator of wind and solar energy. The stock trades at $91, up ~47% from its 2024 low of $62 but still below its $96 52-week high. FY2025 revenue grew 10.7% to $27.4B, with net income of $6.84B (EPS $3.30). The company raised long-term EPS growth guidance from 6-8% to 8% annually, declared a 10% dividend increase, and announced a $2B equity unit offering to fund its massive renewable pipeline. Analyst consensus is Buy with a $93 mean target (22 analysts). The forward P/E of 20.8x prices in solid growth, but the question is whether data center power demand and FPL's rate base expansion justify the premium over utility peers.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** NEE is the "quality utility" — a core holding for income investors with 30 years of consecutive dividend growth, best-in-class renewable energy platform, and visible regulated earnings growth from FPL. The concern is that at 20.8x forward P/E and 18.4x EV/EBITDA, the stock is richly valued for a utility, leaving limited upside vs. the $93 mean price target (only 2.7% above current price). The 2024-era selloff (to $62) stemmed from rising rates and NEP concerns, which have largely been resolved.
- **Our view:** NEE is a quality compounder fairly valued — not cheap, not expensive. The 8% EPS growth guide is credible given (1) FPL's Florida rate base growth (population migration + data center demand), (2) NEER's enormous renewable backlog in a world accelerating decarbonization, and (3) the structural tailwind from AI/data center power demand. However, the stock has already re-rated from $62 to $91 (+47%), much of the good news is priced in, and the analyst consensus price target sits only 2.7% higher. Net Debt/EBITDA at 5.8x is high even for a utility, and the $2B equity offering signals ongoing capital needs. This is a HOLD — own it if you have it, but the entry point for new money is not compelling at $91.
- **Trigger:** (1) Meaningful pullback to $75-80 range creating better entry, (2) data center PPA announcements for NEER specifically, (3) FPL rate case approval with favorable terms, (4) acceleration in backlog-to-revenue conversion.

## Inflection Point

**Mild positive inflection — AI/data center demand is a new growth vector, but execution is steady-state.** NEE is not at a dramatic inflection like a turnaround or new business launch. Rather, the company is benefiting from an accelerating secular tailwind: AI-driven data center electricity demand is boosting both FPL (Florida data center siting) and NEER (renewable PPAs for hyperscalers). The raised guidance from 6-8% to 8% EPS growth reflects management's confidence in this tailwind. However, this is incremental — NEE has been executing the same renewables + regulated utility playbook for 15+ years. The stock's recovery from $62 to $91 already reflects the market recognizing this.

## Bull Case

- Data center power demand accelerates faster than expected; NEER signs multiple GW-scale PPAs with hyperscalers (Meta, Amazon, Google, Microsoft)
- FPL rate case approved with favorable terms; Florida population growth continues driving rate base expansion above 8%
- Interest rates decline, making NEE's 2.74% dividend yield and growth profile more attractive vs. bonds; utility sector re-rates
- IRA tax credits remain intact and provide above-expected returns on NEER solar/wind/battery projects
- EPS growth exceeds 8% guidance as both segments outperform; stock re-rates to 25x+ forward earnings

## Bear Case

- Rising interest rates compress utility valuations; NEE de-rates from 21x to 16-17x forward P/E as bond yields rise
- AI/data center demand disappoints (efficiency gains reduce power needs, permitting delays for new capacity)
- FPL rate case denial or unfavorable outcome; Florida hurricane season causes major outage costs
- Balance sheet stress: $96B debt + continued equity issuance erodes per-share value; credit downgrade risk if CapEx doesn't moderate
- Political risk to IRA renewable tax credits under future administrations; NEER project returns compress
- NextEra Energy Partners (NEP) remains an overhang — if NEP's distribution sustainability is questioned again

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: **MEDIUM**

NEE is a best-in-class utility with a genuine moat (FPL monopoly + NEER scale), 30 years of dividend growth, and a credible 8% EPS growth runway powered by Florida demographics, renewable energy buildout, and AI/data center demand. Business quality scores 19/25 — one of the highest-quality utilities globally.

However, the stock is **fairly valued at best**. At $91, it trades at 20.8x forward P/E and 18.4x EV/EBITDA — a premium to utility peers that is justified but leaves limited margin of safety. The analyst consensus target of $93 implies only 2.7% upside. The gbm_3y model with 0.76 confidence puts fair value at $91.13 — essentially current price. The expected total return of ~9% (price + dividend) is adequate but not compelling for a stock with 5.8x net leverage and ongoing equity dilution.

The DCF models are broken (negative FVs), the RIM/ratios models undervalue it, and the GBM models suggest fair-to-modest upside. This is not a BUY at current levels — it is a quality compounder to own on pullbacks.

**Entry:** $75-80 range (15-20% pullback; ~18x forward P/E)
**Thesis-break:** Below $55 (implies rate case failure + growth deceleration + rate shock)
**Target:** $100 base case (12-month), $120 bull case (18-month)
