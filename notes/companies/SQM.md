# Sociedad Quimica y Minera de Chile (SQM)

**Sector:** Basic Materials | **Industry:** Specialty Chemicals (Lithium, Iodine, Fertilizers)
**Price:** $71.16 | **Market Cap:** $20.3B | **EV:** $24.7B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

SQM swung to a $588M net profit in 2025 (from a $404M loss in 2024), driven by record lithium volumes of 233,000 MT and resilient iodine pricing. The transformative Codelco JV ("Nova Andino Litio") was finalized in January 2026, extending SQM's access to the Atacama salar through 2060 and targeting 300,000 MT LCE by 2030. Lithium prices are recovering — Deutsche Bank raised its 2026 lithium price forecast 37% to $15.4/kg — and SQM is ramping capacity aggressively (240K MT carbonate + 100K MT hydroxide by end-2026). However, the stock has already doubled from its $29 low in mid-2025, raising the question of how much recovery is already priced in. Analysts are split (40% Buy, 40% Hold) with a consensus target of ~$68-77, roughly where it trades today.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Lithium has bottomed, SQM is a quality recovery play, but most of the re-rating has happened. Analysts see fair value in the $68-$77 range with moderate upside. The Codelco deal is positive but adds execution risk.
- **Our view:** The market underestimates the combined earnings power of SQM's three pillars — lithium volume ramp into a tightening market (Bernstein sees 99% utilization in 2026), iodine's structural scarcity (41% market share, $38K/ton pricing, growing medical/PFAS demand), and SPN stability. Forward PE of 13x implies ~$5.44 EPS for 2026, which looks conservative if lithium prices reach $15-17/kg. However, this is a WEAK variant perception — the bull case is well-known and the stock has already re-rated significantly.
- **Trigger:** Lithium carbonate price crossing $15/kg sustainably in H2 2026, which would drive 2026 EPS above $6 and justify $80+ pricing.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — early-stage earnings inflection.** SQM has crossed back into profitability after the 2024 lithium trough. The evidence is clear: Q4 2025 revenue +23% YoY, gross profit +53% YoY, lithium volumes at records. The inflection has BEGUN but is not yet fully realized — lithium prices are recovering but still well below the $15-17/kg needed for a full earnings ramp. The question is whether this is the start of a sustained upcycle or a dead cat bounce. The supply-demand balance (Bernstein: 99% utilization in 2026) supports a sustained recovery, but Chinese oversupply risk remains.

## Bull Case

- Lithium prices rally to $17-20/kg as demand growth (25%+) outpaces supply, driving 2026 EPS to $7-8+
- Iodine remains structurally tight with prices at or above $38K/ton, contributing $1B+ in annual gross profit
- Nova Andino Litio execution unlocks 300K MT target ahead of schedule, cementing lowest-cost position
- BESS (battery energy storage) demand creates a second structural demand driver beyond EVs
- Multiple re-rates to 15-18x forward PE as earnings growth becomes visible

## Bear Case

- Lithium oversupply persists — Chinese lepidolite and African spodumene projects flood the market, keeping prices at $10-12/kg
- Codelco JV terms become increasingly onerous as government ownership ramps (51% by 2030), compressing SQM's economic share
- Capex remains elevated ($800M+/yr) while FCF generation disappoints, straining balance sheet
- Chilean royalty/tax regime tightens further, eroding after-tax margins
- Global EV demand growth slows on trade war / tariff headwinds, reducing lithium demand growth to single digits
