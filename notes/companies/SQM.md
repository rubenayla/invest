## Situation Summary

SQM has rallied to a fresh 52-week high of $95.31 (+21% in 8 trading days, +225% from its $29.36 52-week low) as lithium carbonate spot prices spiked from ~$13,000/ton in December 2025 to ~$22,000–26,000/ton by April 2026 — a 95%+ move in two months. The rally is driven by (1) Zimbabwe's Feb 25, 2026 suspension of raw lithium concentrate exports (~7% of global supply), (2) SQM's own management calling a $15–18/kg price floor for 2026, (3) demand growth of 14–25% from EV + BESS, and (4) supply response lag (2+ year restart lead time on idled projects). The Codelco/NovaAndino JV is fully operational with a 2026 production target of 270,000 tons LCE and a projected $2.1B contribution to the Chilean state. Q4 2025 missed EPS ($0.64 vs $0.80 consensus) on Kwinana cost pressures despite record volumes; Q1 2026 earnings report (~May) is the next major catalyst, followed by the April 23 AGM and a 50% payout dividend vote.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — but the inflection has now been RECOGNIZED by the market.** The lithium supply/demand inversion that we flagged on April 8 has accelerated dramatically: spot prices doubled in two months, Zimbabwe cut exports, management called a price floor. Three-structural-tailwind narrative is now fully in play:

1. **Supply discipline + shocks**: Zimbabwe export ban, high-cost mines still shuttered, restart lead times 2+ years.
2. **Tariff tailwinds**: 25% U.S. tariffs on Chinese batteries, export rebate removal — persistent.
3. **Demand acceleration**: BESS as second demand pillar, 14–25% growth.

The question for the stock is no longer "has it inflected?" but "how long can lithium stay at $22–26/kg before new supply comes online?" Historical commodity cycles suggest 18–24 months of elevated prices before supply catches up. If we're 3 months into that cycle, SQM has time. If we're 15 months in, we're near the top.

## Bull Case

- Lithium stabilizes $20–25/kg through 2026–2027 as supply response lags demand; SQM 2026 EPS hits $9–11, 2027 EPS $12+
- Zimbabwe export ban persists or expands; Chinese lepidolite economics stay broken at sub-$25K
- BESS demand adds 15–20% incremental demand pillar beyond EVs
- Q1 2026 earnings beat decisively, triggering analyst target revisions to $110–130
- Iodine business contributes $1.2B+ gross profit as PFAS/medical demand tightens market further

## Bear Case

- Lithium spike is classic commodity overshoot — prices mean-revert to $13–15/kg by H2 2026 as Chinese lepidolite restarts and new African supply hits; EPS falls back to $4–5
- Q1/Q2 realized prices lag spot due to contract repricing delay — market realizes "record spot" ≠ SQM earnings
- Kwinana cost problems persist; margins disappoint despite volumes
- NovaAndino terms become increasingly onerous: 70% margin to state through 2030, 85% post-2030 — SQM is a minority economics partner in its own asset. This was priced into valuations at low lithium; becomes more painful at high lithium because SQM captures less of the windfall
- Chilean political risk — royalty/tax regime tightens further as state sees $2B+ contribution and wants more
- Technical setup at 52-week high is vulnerable; any macro risk-off (tariff news, rate shock) triggers profit-taking from the +225% YTD crowd
