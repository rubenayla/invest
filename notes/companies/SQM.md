## Business Primer

**What they do today.** SQM is a Chilean specialty chemicals and mining company organized around four distinct lines that share a common feedstock base in the Atacama Desert. Lithium (roughly half of revenue, the dominant earnings driver) — SQM is the world's #2 lithium producer behind Albemarle, extracting lithium carbonate and hydroxide from brine at the Salar de Atacama, the lowest-cost lithium resource on the planet (cash costs in the $3-5/kg range vs $7-12/kg for Australian hard-rock spodumene). Specialty Plant Nutrition — potassium nitrate, sodium nitrate and specialty fertilizers used in high-value crops (berries, citrus, tomatoes, leafy greens), where SQM is the global leader; customers are agricultural distributors across Europe, Latin America, North America and Asia. Iodine and derivatives — SQM is the world's largest iodine producer (~30% global share), serving X-ray contrast media, LCD polarizing film, biocides and pharma. Industrial chemicals and potash — smaller legacy lines (industrial salts, potassium chloride). All four come from the same Atacama brine and caliche-ore operations, which is why SQM is fundamentally a low-cost integrated chemicals producer rather than a pure lithium play.

**Where they want to grow.** Lithium volume is the explicit growth bet: management is guiding ~260,000 metric tons LCE (lithium carbonate equivalent) production in 2026, up from ~234,000 in 2025, with Q4 2025 alone topping 66,000 tons (more than 50% year-over-year growth). The signature corporate event is the Codelco joint venture for the Salar de Atacama post-2030, signed in 2024, which extends SQM's brine concession rights through 2060 in partnership with the Chilean state copper company — securing the asset that the market feared would be nationalized. SQM is also bringing Mt Holland (Australia, hard-rock spodumene, joint venture with Wesfarmers) into production to diversify geography. Specialty plant nutrition is positioned as a steady cash-generating "second leg" with mid-single-digit volume growth, and iodine pricing has been the unsung hero — iodine prices roughly tripled from 2020 lows on supply discipline and structural demand from X-ray contrast media. Salar Futuro (the post-2030 Atacama operating plan using seawater pipelines and direct lithium extraction technology) is the long-dated growth thesis; the environmental application is planned for mid-2026.

**What could go wrong (business risks).** Lithium-price cyclicality is the dominant risk — realized prices collapsed from over $60/kg in 2022 to ~$9-10/kg in 2024-2025 as Chinese supply and Australian hard-rock expansion overshot electric-vehicle demand; SQM's earnings swing violently with this. Political/contractual risk in Chile: the Codelco JV resolved the near-term concession question but means SQM is now an operating partner in a state-controlled structure post-2030, with profit-sharing skewed toward Codelco at higher lithium prices and a state-aligned governance overlay. Competition from Argentine brine producers (Allkem-Livent merger as Arcadium, now Rio Tinto), Chinese lepidolite and African hard-rock projects keeps marginal supply growing faster than EV demand in some years. Chinese demand variance — China is both the largest converter and consumer of lithium chemicals, and inventory cycles there drive realized-price swings independent of underlying EV growth. Execution risk on Mt Holland ramp-up and Salar Futuro permits, which require Chilean environmental approvals that have historically slipped. Iodine pricing power could erode if Japanese or Chinese supply restarts.

**How to think about it.** SQM is a low-cost lithium brine producer with an iodine and specialty-fertilizer cash cushion — a commodity producer with a pair of high-quality specialty businesses bolted on, NOT a tech/EV growth proxy. The right mental model is "ultra-low-cost Saudi Aramco of lithium" plus a global-leader specialty chemicals business, where the lithium leg dominates earnings beta and the iodine/SPN legs anchor through-cycle cash flow. Compared to Albemarle, SQM has better cost position and dividend discipline but higher single-asset (Chile) and political risk. The bull case requires lithium prices to normalize above ~$15/kg; the bear case is a multi-year extension of the current $8-12/kg world combined with Codelco JV terms turning out worse than expected.

## Situation Summary
SQM is operating in a still-depressed lithium price environment (~$10/kg realized in Q4 2025) but with a clear volume-growth runway — 2026 guidance is ~260,000 tons LCE, a 10-15% volume step-up, and Q1 prices are signaled "substantially above" Q4. The 2024 Codelco JV approval removed the existential overhang of nationalization, replacing it with a long-dated profit-sharing structure that the market is still digesting. Iodine continues to print near-record prices, and specialty plant nutrition margins are normalizing higher post-2024 destocking. The stock has rebounded from sub-$35 lows in 2024 to the low $80s as investors price in both volume growth and a lithium-price floor; sentiment is shifting from "Chile-risk pariah" to "low-cost cyclical with a path."

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** Lithium oversupply persists into 2027; SQM is a beta play on Chinese EV demand and lithium price. Sell-side targets cluster $79 (mean of 18 analysts: $40 low, $106 high), recommendation: "buy" but with wide dispersion. Codelco JV terms widely seen as a "tax" that caps upside.
- **Our view:** Three things the market under-weights. (1) Iodine and specialty plant nutrition together generate over $700M EBITDA at structurally higher pricing than 2018-2020, providing a hard cash-flow floor independent of lithium — the market still models these as commodity-like. (2) The lithium cost curve is steepening as marginal Chinese lepidolite producers exit at sub-$10/kg, which should put a floor on prices; SQM's $3-5/kg cash cost means it earns through any realistic cycle bottom. (3) The Codelco JV is a *risk-removed* event, not a tax — the prior implied probability of partial nationalization at unfavorable terms was high enough that the announced 50/50-ish JV structure is actually a positive expected outcome the stock has only partially absorbed.
- **Trigger:** Q2 2026 earnings (August 2026) showing Q2 realized lithium price above $11/kg + iodine maintaining record pricing + Salar Futuro environmental application filed on schedule — three observable, dateable proofs in a single quarter.

## Inflection Point
Yes — three converging signals. (1) Lithium price has bottomed: Chinese lepidolite high-cost supply is exiting, realized prices ticked up 14% QoQ in Q4 2025, and Q1 2026 is guided "substantially above" Q4. (2) Volume inflection: Q4 2025 lithium volume was up 50% year-over-year, 2026 guide is another 10-15%. (3) Codelco JV de-risked the existential overhang. The pattern matches a textbook cyclical-trough exit — observable evidence the inflection has begun, not betting on a bottom.

## Bull Case
- Lithium realized price recovers to $13-15/kg by 2027 as marginal Chinese supply exits; SQM volume grows to 280,000+ tons LCE; lithium EBITDA returns to $2.5B+
- Iodine pricing holds at $60+/kg through 2027; specialty plant nutrition margins expand with mix shift to high-value crops; non-lithium EBITDA settles structurally at $800M+
- Mt Holland ramps to full ~50,000 tons LCE capacity diversifying geographic risk
- Salar Futuro environmental approval clears mid-2026, extending Atacama reserve life materially
- Codelco JV reframed as "concession secured" rather than "nationalized" — multiple re-rates to 8-10x mid-cycle EBITDA

## Bear Case
- Lithium price stays at $8-10/kg through 2027 as Argentine and African projects ramp faster than EV demand absorbs — SQM trough cash flow persists, dividend cut becomes plausible
- **Codelco JV economic terms turn out worse than market understands**: SQM's earnings disclosures already flag "complexity in dividend allocation linked to 33,500 tons allocated to Codelco" — this is a real ongoing earnings haircut the headline narrative under-covers (this is the news-headline-under-covered risk that primary-source filings flag explicitly)
- Salar Futuro environmental permit slips past 2027 — multiple compresses on stranded-asset fear
- Iodine pricing normalizes lower if Japanese restarts or Chinese capacity expansion occurs
- China EV demand decelerates faster than expected if subsidy framework changes; battery-chemistry shift toward sodium-ion at the low end accelerates
