# Deckers Outdoor Corporation (DECK)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Footwear & Accessories
**Price:** $104.54 | **Market Cap:** $15.2B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-19

## Situation Summary

Deckers is a premium footwear powerhouse riding two exceptional brands: HOKA (performance running, $2.2B+ revenue, growing 18%+ in Q3 FY26) and UGG ($4.7B+ revenue, growing mid-single digits). The stock has been cut roughly in half from its September 2024 highs (~$133 post-split adjusted) and trades at just 15x trailing / 15x forward earnings -- an absurdly cheap multiple for a company with 58% gross margins, 38% ROE, net cash of $1.6B, and aggressive $1B+ annual buybacks. Q3 FY26 (Dec quarter) was a beat-and-raise: revenue $1.96B (+7.1% YoY), EPS $3.33 vs $2.77 consensus (+20% beat), with FY26 guidance raised to $5.4-5.43B revenue and $6.80-6.85 EPS. The main overhang is tariffs: ~75% of production is in Vietnam (20% tariff), creating an estimated $110M FY26 COGS headwind, which management expects to offset through price increases and cost-sharing. HOKA growth is decelerating (from 24% in FY25 to mid-teens in FY26) but remains exceptional. The 6-for-1 stock split in Q2 FY26 and $2.5B buyback authorization signal management confidence.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Analysts are bullish (21 analysts, mean target $128.50, range $90-$184). The market sees DECK as a quality compounder but worries about HOKA growth deceleration and tariff margin compression. Short interest is low at 3.4%. The consensus expects mid-to-high single digit revenue growth going forward with some margin pressure.
- **Our view:** The market is dramatically underpricing DECK. At 15x forward PE, the stock prices in ZERO growth premium -- this is a mature staples multiple, not appropriate for a company growing EPS at 33% CAGR over 3 years with a runway brand (HOKA) still in early innings internationally. Key variant: HOKA's deceleration from 24% to mid-teens is being treated as "peak growth" when in reality the brand is barely penetrated internationally (only 15% of revenue from international in running), has huge white space in lifestyle/trail, and is still gaining share from Nike. The tariff overhang (~$110M or ~2% of revenue) is real but manageable given 58% gross margins and pricing power. The buyback ($1B+/yr at 15x PE) is massively accretive -- they're buying back 6-7% of market cap annually.
- **Trigger:** Q4 FY26 earnings (May 2026) for tariff impact clarity; FY27 guidance will be the key re-rating catalyst if HOKA sustains 15%+ growth; any tariff relief on Vietnam.

## Inflection Point

**No major inflection -- steady compounder with favorable setup.** DECK is not at a business inflection but rather at a valuation inflection. The stock has de-rated from 30x+ PE to 15x PE over the past year despite continued execution, primarily due to: (1) HOKA growth deceleration concerns, (2) tariff fears, and (3) broader consumer discretionary rotation. The business fundamentals remain intact and improving (margin expansion, DTC shift, international growth). This is a classic "multiple compression on decelerating but still excellent growth" setup that historically creates buying opportunities.

The tariff impact (~$110M) is real but manageable at ~2% of revenue, and management has a clear mitigation plan (selective price increases + vendor cost-sharing). Gross margins in Q3 FY26 were actually 59.8%, only 50bps below last year, showing pricing power is real.

## Bull Case
- HOKA sustains 15-20% growth as international expansion (Europe, China, Japan) offsets US deceleration; brand extends successfully into lifestyle/trail categories
- Tariff resolution or mitigation limits gross margin impact to <100bps; pricing power preserves margins
- Aggressive buybacks ($1B+/yr) at 15x PE drive 6-7% annual share count reduction, turbocharging EPS growth
- Multiple re-rates from 15x to 20-25x as market re-prices the growth durability, driving $140-170+ stock price
- DTC mix continues expanding toward 60%+, driving operating margin toward 25-28%

## Bear Case
- HOKA growth decelerates to single digits as max-cushion trend fades and On/New Balance/Nike compete aggressively
- Tariff escalation on Vietnam (currently 20%) compounds with consumer weakness, compressing gross margins 300-400bps
- UGG enters secular decline as fashion cycle turns; warm winters reduce seasonal demand
- Consumer discretionary spending weakens in a recession; premium $150-200 shoes face demand destruction
- International expansion costs more than expected and dilutes margins without proportional revenue scale

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: HIGH

DECK is a rare quality compounder trading at a deep value multiple. At 15x trailing PE (or ~13x ex-cash), you're getting a company with 58% gross margins, 38% ROE, 16.5% revenue CAGR, $1.6B net cash, and $1B+ annual buybacks -- essentially for free. The HOKA deceleration narrative is overblown: mid-teens growth for a $2.5B brand with massive international white space is excellent, not disappointing. The tariff headwind is real but manageable (~2% of revenue) and management is demonstrating pricing power (59.8% gross margin in Q3 despite tariffs).

The expected value of +30% significantly exceeds cost of equity. The GBM models unanimously see 25-147% upside. The analyst consensus target of $128.50 implies +23% from here. The risk/reward is highly asymmetric: the bull case ($176, +68%) is larger than the bear case ($90, -14%), and the probability-weighted expected value is exceptional.

**Entry now at $104.54** -- no need to wait for a better price. This is already cheap on every metric that matters.
**Add aggressively below $95** -- approaches net cash + 1yr FCF on an EV basis.
**Thesis-break price:** Below $75 (implies multiple compression AND earnings decline, suggesting brand impairment).
