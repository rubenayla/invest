## Situation Summary
Nutrien is the world's largest potash producer (low-cost Saskatchewan brine/conventional mines) and the largest North American agricultural retailer (Nutrien Ag Solutions), plus a top-tier nitrogen producer. It is a textbook recovering cyclical: GAAP diluted EPS collapsed from a war-spike peak of **$14.18 (2022)** to a trough of **$1.36 (2024)**, then rebounded to **$4.66 (2025)**. Yet the stock has *fallen* ~27% from its 52-week high ($85) to near the 52-week low ($53), sitting at $62 — earnings recovering while the price de-rates. Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-06) printed $139M net earnings / **$1.11B adjusted EBITDA** on **record potash sales volume (>3.5M tonnes)**, and management **reaffirmed full-year 2026 guidance** across every segment. The market's worry is not this year's numbers — it's the medium-term potash supply wall (BHP's Jansen mega-mine) and weak farm economics pressuring the retail arm.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** Sell-side mean target **$81.8 (+31%)**, generally constructive — "cheap integrated ag-input leader, potash demand at record, dividend safe, buy the cycle dip." 17 analysts.
- **Our view:** More cautious than the sell-side. The valuation *looks* cheap (fwd P/E 11.6, EV/EBITDA 7.7, P/B 1.19) but this is a **price-taker on three volatile commodities** with **$13.2B net debt (~2.2x mid-cycle EBITDA)**. "Low P/E on a cyclical" is a classic value-trap tell when earnings are *above* trough — and 2025–26 EPS sits above the 2024 trough, not at it. The genuine edge here is **cost-curve position** (NTR is bottom-quartile potash cash cost, so it survives a glut that kills marginal Russian/Belarusian/greenfield tonnes) plus a **book-value + dividend floor**. That makes the *downside* shallower than a typical commodity name, not the upside larger. Net: a moderately positive-EV cyclical, not a fat pitch at $62.
- **Trigger / catalyst:** (1) Middle East conflict is **tightening global nitrogen & phosphate** (lifting prices/costs) — a near-term tailwind NTR flagged. (2) Potash contract settlements (China/India) and Jansen ramp pace set the 2027+ price floor. (3) Retail EBITDA guide ($1.75–1.95B) holding through a soft farm-income year is the swing factor on the bull case.

## Bull Case
- **Irreplaceable potash franchise**: bottom-quartile cash cost, decades of reserves, record 2026 shipment volumes. Survives a price war that bankrupts marginal supply.
- **Integrated retail moat**: Nutrien Ag Solutions is the largest N. American ag retailer — recurring proprietary-product and services EBITDA ($1.75–1.95B guided) that smooths the commodity cycle.
- **Cheap + shareholder returns**: fwd P/E 11.6, EV/EBITDA 7.7, 3.5% dividend at 44% payout, buybacks resuming ($551M in 2025). FCF covers the dividend ~2x.
- **Near-term tailwind**: Middle East conflict is tightening nitrogen/phosphate, lifting realized prices.
- **Secular demand**: global food demand + soil nutrient depletion underpin multi-decade fertilizer consumption growth.

## Bear Case (the kill attempt)
- **Pure price-taker** on potash/nitrogen/phosphate — zero pricing power. EPS swung $14 → $1.36 → $4.66 in three years. You cannot underwrite this with confidence.
- **BHP Jansen supply wall**: a new low-cost mega-mine ramping into 2027+ adds structural potash supply — the single biggest reason potash could stay range-bound-to-lower even as demand grows. NTR's volume growth may be met with flat/lower prices.
- **Weak farm economics**: low corn/soy prices crimp farmer input budgets → retail volume & margin pressure, the exact segment the bull case leans on. Retail has disappointed and taken impairments before.
- **Leverage amplifies the cycle**: $13.2B net debt means a glut hits equity harder than EBITDA.
- **Capital-allocation scar tissue**: heavy buybacks at $90+ in 2022, stock now $62 — management bought high.
- **"Cheap P/E" mirage**: low multiple on above-trough cyclical earnings is the wrong signal; the safe entry is when the P/E looks *high* on trough EPS, which is not now.

## Entry / Exit Discipline
- **Entry (BUY LOW tier):** ~$53–55 — the 52-week low and ~1.0x book, where downside compresses to the dividend + cost-curve floor and EV asymmetry turns clearly favorable. Not compelling enough at $62 for high conviction.
- **Thesis-break / reduce:** sustained potash benchmark breaking below ~$250/tonne FOB on confirmed Jansen oversupply, OR retail adjusted EBITDA guided below $1.6B (the moat eroding), OR net debt/EBITDA pushing past ~3x.
- **What would change my mind (to a clear BUY):** a price dip to book (~$52) with guidance intact, or evidence Jansen ramp is delayed/curtailed and potash contract prices firm — either flips the bear leg of the scenario table and lifts EV toward +35%.

EV ~+22% over two years is positive but not a fat pitch at $62; the asymmetry becomes attractive ~15% lower. Worth owning on weakness, not chasing here.
