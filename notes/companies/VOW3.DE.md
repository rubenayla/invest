# Volkswagen AG (VOW3.DE)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Auto Manufacturers
**Price:** EUR 85.68 | **Market Cap:** EUR 43.0B | **EV:** EUR 258.3B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

Volkswagen is in the middle of a painful restructuring after 2025 operating profit collapsed 53% to EUR 8.9B, driven by EUR 5B in Porsche EV strategy charges, EUR 3B in US tariff costs, and EUR 1B in restructuring. The company announced 50,000 job cuts by 2030 (35K attrition + 15K additional) targeting EUR 15B annual savings. US tariffs remain a headwind: the EU-US deal reduced auto tariffs from 30% to 15%, but a new Greenland-linked tariff of 25% on European nations kicks in June 1, 2026. The stock trades at EUR 85.68, near its 52-week low of EUR 81.68, at a forward P/E of 3.3x and 0.23x book value — pricing in severe permanent impairment. Q1 2026 results on April 30 will be the first test of whether cost cuts are translating to margin recovery.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Volkswagen is a structurally declining legacy automaker that cannot compete with Chinese EVs, is overexposed to tariffs, and will see margins stay depressed for years. The preferred shares deserve a conglomerate discount, and the massive debt (EUR 196B including financial services) makes it uninvestable at any P/E.
- **Our view:** The market is confusing cyclical trough earnings (loaded with one-off charges) with the new normal. Strip out the EUR 9B in extraordinary charges and 2025 underlying operating profit was ~EUR 18B (5.6% margin), consistent with guidance of 4-5.5% going forward. At EUR 43B market cap, the stock trades at 2.4x normalized earnings. The EUR 15B savings program is real — VW has labor agreements locked in, and Q1 2026 will show early evidence. The conglomerate houses Porsche, Audi, Lamborghini, Bentley, and a financial services arm worth more than the entire market cap. The 5.95% dividend yield is covered (48% payout ratio). Price-to-book of 0.23x means you're buying EUR 188B in equity for EUR 43B.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 results (April 30) showing margin improvement toward 5%+ guidance range. Any progress on tariff negotiations (EU-US deal revision). Restructuring savings becoming visible in H1 2026 numbers.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — early-stage turnaround.** Multiple signals:

1. **Cost restructuring underway:** 50,000 job cuts agreed, wage freeze 2025-2026, 700K unit capacity reduction. These are concrete, contractually locked measures, not aspirational targets.
2. **Charge cycle ending:** 2025 was loaded with EUR 9B in one-offs (Porsche pivot, tariff costs, restructuring). 2026 should see dramatically cleaner earnings.
3. **Guidance implies recovery:** 4-5.5% operating margin target for 2026 vs 3.4% in 2025. Even the low end (4%) on EUR 325B revenue = EUR 13B operating profit, vs EUR 10.8B reported in 2025.
4. **New tariff regime priced in:** Market has had months to digest tariff reality. Incremental bad news (Greenland tariffs June 1) is partially priced.

**Timing:** The inflection is BEGINNING but not yet confirmed. Q1 2026 (April 30) is the first data point. This is the "enter after initial recovery starts" window described in the framework — the stock is near 52-week lows, restructuring is locked in, but margin improvement hasn't been proven yet.

## Bull Case

- EUR 15B annual savings by 2030 drives operating margins to 6-7%, translating to EUR 20-23B operating profit on flat revenue
- Porsche, Audi, Lamborghini luxury brands alone worth EUR 50B+ (more than current market cap)
- EV transition succeeds — SSP platform competitive, software improves, China JVs stabilize
- Tariff situation resolves (EU-US deal improves, or VW shifts production to US/Mexico)
- Dividend yield of 6% provides downside floor and attracts income investors as earnings stabilize
- Multiple re-rates from 3.3x forward P/E toward 6-8x as market recognizes trough earnings

## Bear Case

- China market share continues eroding — BYD, NIO, Xiaomi eating VW's lunch; China is 40% of VW profits
- Tariff escalation: 25% Greenland tariffs from June 2026 hit harder than expected, EU retaliates
- Restructuring savings delayed by German labor laws, union resistance, political pressure
- EV transition requires sustained EUR 25B+ annual capex for years, destroying FCF
- Porsche and Audi brand values decline as Chinese EVs move upmarket
- "Value trap" — cheap for a reason, stays cheap for years

## Verdict

The variant perception is clear: the market is extrapolating one-off-laden 2025 earnings as the new normal, while EUR 15B in locked-in restructuring savings and the end of the charge cycle should drive significant margin recovery.
