# Lasertec Corporation (6920.T)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Semiconductor Equipment & Materials

**Analysis Date:** 2026-05-28

## Business Primer

**What they do today.** Lasertec makes the machines that *inspect* photomasks for the most advanced computer chips. A photomask is the stencil that lithography machines (ASML's EUV scanners) use to print circuit patterns onto silicon wafers — think of it as the master negative in photography. If the mask has a tiny defect, every single chip printed from it inherits that defect, so chipmakers must verify masks are flawless before mass production. Lasertec sells the inspection tools that do this verification for Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) lithography — the cutting-edge process used at the 5-nanometer, 3-nanometer and below nodes by Intel, Taiwan Semiconductor (TSMC) and Samsung. Roughly three-quarters of revenue comes from these semiconductor-related inspection systems (the rest is flat-panel-display inspection, laser microscopes, and a small electronics/energy business). Customers pay tens of millions of dollars per tool, plus recurring service and upgrade revenue. FY2025 (year ended June 2025) revenue was 251.5B JPY.

**Why customers choose them (the value proposition).** EUV uses 13.5-nanometer light, a wavelength so short it behaves differently from the visible/deep-ultraviolet light older inspection tools used. To find defects that will actually print, you have to inspect the mask *using the same EUV wavelength the scanner uses* — this is called "actinic" inspection. Lasertec is the only company in the world selling a commercial actinic EUV mask-blank inspection tool (the ABICS line) and the only volume supplier of actinic patterned-mask inspection. The alternative — using conventional optical or e-beam inspection — physically cannot "see" certain phase defects the way EUV light does, so it misses defects that then ruin production wafers. The concrete benefit: a chipmaker that inspects masks actinically catches killer defects before they scrap thousands of wafers, where each advanced wafer is worth thousands of dollars. There is effectively no substitute that does the job, which is why Lasertec holds ~90%+ share of this niche.

**Where they want to grow.** Three bets. (1) Ride the EUV adoption wave — every new advanced node (TSMC N2, Intel 18A/14A, Samsung gate-all-around) needs more masks, more inspection, and the coming High-Numerical-Aperture (High-NA) EUV transition needs tighter defect specs and new tool generations (the A200HiT and next-gen blanks testers are already selling). (2) Expand the addressable market with adjacent products — the ACTIS patterned-mask tool, ABICS E320 blank inspector, and a silicon-carbide (SiC) power-wafer inspection system (SICA) that targets the electric-vehicle power-semiconductor market. (3) Grow recurring service revenue off the installed base. Success in 3-5 years looks like sustaining a ~250-300B JPY annual order run-rate as High-NA EUV ramps globally.

**What could go wrong (business risks).** The business is brutally cyclical and lumpy — orders collapsed 61% in FY2025 (to 105B JPY) when customers paused EUV capex, and a single deferred multi-tool order swings a quarter. Customer concentration is extreme: essentially three buyers (Intel, TSMC, Samsung) and only a few dozen installed EUV inspection units worldwide, so one customer's capex delay or yield problem hits hard. The monopoly invites attack: ZEISS is developing competing EUV mask-inspection capability (AIMS EUV 3.0 line) and could erode the premium if Lasertec stumbles on the High-NA transition. There is also a lingering governance overhang from the June 2024 Scorpion Capital short report alleging accounting irregularities; the market dismissed it after strong results, but it remains a tail risk.

**How to think about it.** A toll-booth monopoly on a critical chokepoint of advanced-chip manufacturing — but a *cyclical* toll booth whose traffic (orders) swings violently with the semiconductor capex cycle. Quality of the franchise is exceptional; predictability of the earnings is not.

## Situation Summary

The big change since the last analysis (2026-03-18): Lasertec reported Q3 FY2026 (quarter ended March 2026) on April 30, 2026, and **raised its full-year order guidance to 200-240B JPY (from 170-220B)** while raising the FY2026 P&L guidance — revenue to 220B (from 200B), operating income to 100B at a 45.5% margin (from 85B), and net profit to 72B (from 60B, implying EPS ~800 vs the 665 assumed in March). Crucially, management said it expects orders to sustain the H1 CY2026 level — roughly **150B JPY per half / ~300B annualized** — through H1 CY2027. This confirms the order-recovery inflection that was only a hope in March: orders bottomed at 105B in FY2025 and are now climbing back toward a 250-300B run-rate, driven by A200HiT and blanks-testing tool demand. The Q3 quarter *itself* looked weak optically (sales -44% QoQ, operating profit -58% QoQ, EPS missed estimates by ~26%) because revenue recognition lags orders — but that is the trailing trough, not the forward story. The stock has continued higher to 40,110 JPY (a new 52-week high of 46,720 was set intra-period), and analyst forward EPS estimates have re-rated up to ~1,092 JPY.

- **Our view:** We largely *agree* with consensus now — and that is precisely the problem. In March the variant view was "the recovery is being front-run." Today the recovery is confirmed AND the stock has risen further, so the cheap-relative-to-confirmed-inflection window has mostly closed. - **Trigger:** Continued order strength at/above the 150B-per-half run-rate at the FY2026 full-year report (~early August 2026) would extend the rally; a quarter where orders slip back below ~120B, or a credible ZEISS actinic-inspection design win, would break the upcycle narrative and de-rate the multiple.

## Inflection Point

**Positive inflection — CONFIRMED (this is the key update vs March).** In March the order recovery was a hope being front-run; the April 30 report turned it into observable evidence: order guidance raised to 200-240B (more than double the FY2025 trough of 105B), FY2026 P&L guidance raised across the board, and a stated ~150B-per-half order run-rate through H1 CY2027. The cyclical bottom (FY2025 orders) is now clearly behind. Revenue recognition lags, so the trailing income statement still looks soft (Q3 sales -44% QoQ), but the leading indicator (orders) has turned up decisively. The catch: this inflection is now well understood by the market and reflected in a 40,110 JPY price and rising consensus estimates — you are entering *after* the recovery is confirmed and the stock has run, which reduces value-trap risk but also caps the reward.

## Bull Case

- Order recovery is confirmed and accelerating: 200-240B FY2026 order guidance and a ~300B annualized run-rate signal through H1 CY2027, more than doubling off the FY2025 trough.
- High-NA EUV transition (ASML's new scanners ramping 2026-2030) drives a fresh wave of mask-format and inspection-spec changes that only Lasertec can serve at the EUV wavelength.
- New products (A200HiT, ABICS E320, ACTIS, SiC wafer inspection) cited by management as drivers of the guidance raise — real TAM expansion, not just cycle recovery.
- World-class economics: ~46-49% operating margins, 44% ROE, debt-free, growing FCF funds dividends/buybacks.
- Monopoly intact — ZEISS still not shipping volume actinic inspection tools.

## Bear Case

- **Valuation leaves no margin of safety:** ~50x current-year guided EPS (~800), ~37x forward consensus EPS (~1,092), ~16x book, ~14x sales — priced for a smooth upcycle that the order history says is anything but smooth.
- **Cyclicality is the franchise's real flaw, and it's under-covered:** orders fell 61% in FY2025; management's own H1-CY2026 run-rate guidance (~150B/half) is a *forecast*, and a single deferred multi-tool order from one of three customers can swing a quarter. The Street is extrapolating the recovery linearly. (Primary-source risk the headlines under-cover: the FY2025 backlog already fell 31.6% to 315.9B, so the order recovery has to be sustained just to stop backlog erosion — not yet a backlog-rebuild story.)
- **ZEISS competitive entry:** AIMS EUV 3.0 development continues; a credible actinic-inspection design win — even an announcement — would compress the monopoly premium that justifies the multiple.
- **Customer concentration:** essentially Intel/TSMC/Samsung and a few dozen installed units; Intel's roadmap execution risk and Samsung yield issues are live concerns.
- **Scorpion Capital governance overhang:** dormant, not resolved; any new evidence or regulatory inquiry would hit a 37-50x multiple hard.

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM *(was PASS on 2026-03-18 — upgraded, because the order-recovery inflection the prior thesis doubted is now confirmed by two FY2026 guidance raises.)*

Lasertec remains a genuinely exceptional franchise — the sole actinic EUV mask-inspection monopoly, ~46-49% operating margins, 44% ROE, debt-free (quality 20/25). The reason to PASS in March (a not-yet-confirmed recovery being front-run) has been resolved: order guidance was raised to 200-240B and management guides a ~300B annualized run-rate through H1 CY2027. But the stock rose into that confirmation, so it now trades ~50x current-year and ~37x forward EPS with an expected value of only +1.8% — the upside (+24.7% bull) and downside (-35.2% bear) are roughly symmetric, and the longest-horizon GBM model is flat. There's no variant edge left; the monopoly and the recovery are both consensus. Not cheap enough to BUY into a violently cyclical order book, not broken enough to PASS on quality.

**Would upgrade to BUY at:** ~30,000 JPY (~37x current-year EPS / ~27x forward — a price that pays for the monopoly while leaving margin of safety against the cyclicality), OR on a pullback below ~33,000 with order run-rate still tracking 150B/half.
**Would downgrade to PASS if:** orders slip below ~120B/quarter (recovery stalling) or a credible ZEISS actinic design win emerges.
