## Business Model

Prudential operates across three core segments:

- **Life Insurance & Annuities:** Individual and group life, disability, and annuity products. Revenue from premiums and spread income.
- **Retirement Solutions:** Institutional and individual retirement products including pension risk transfer. Benefits from aging demographics and corporate de-risking trends.
- **PGIM (Asset Management):** One of the world's largest asset managers with ~$1.4T AUM. Revenue from management fees — high-margin, capital-light, and recurring. Provides meaningful diversification from insurance underwriting risk.

Revenue composition: insurance premiums, net investment income, and asset management fees.

## Key Risks

1. **Interest rate risk:** Significant investment portfolio in fixed income; rate moves affect both book value and spread income.
2. **Reserve adequacy:** Actuarial assumptions on mortality, morbidity, and longevity can diverge from realized experience.
3. **Credit risk:** Corporate bond and structured product exposure in the investment portfolio.
4. **Regulatory risk:** Insurance is state-regulated (US) and internationally regulated; capital requirements can shift.
5. **PGIM AUM flows:** Asset management is exposed to market drawdowns and institutional client redemptions.

## AI Disruption Assessment

**Risk level: LOW-MODERATE**

AI is more likely to be a tool Prudential adopts than a threat that disrupts it:

- **Underwriting:** AI-enhanced risk models could improve pricing accuracy and reduce adverse selection — a potential competitive advantage for incumbents with proprietary data, not a disruptive threat.
- **Actuarial / reserving:** Heavily regulated with legal requirements for licensed actuaries; pure-AI substitution faces structural barriers.
- **PGIM (asset management):** Faces moderate risk as quantitative strategies become more accessible to smaller competitors. However, PGIM's scale, relationships, and multi-asset capabilities provide defensibility.
- **Distribution:** Digital/robo-advisory platforms could pressure margins on simpler products, but complex insurance and institutional relationships are sticky.

Net assessment: PRU is more likely to benefit from AI-driven operational efficiency than to be disrupted by it.
