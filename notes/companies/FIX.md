# Comfort Systems USA, Inc. (FIX)

**Sector:** Industrials | **Industry:** Engineering & Construction (MEP Contractor)

**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-23

## Situation Summary

Comfort Systems USA is the dominant US mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP) contractor, now operating at a very different scale than two years ago. FY2025 revenue reached $9.1B (+30% YoY), EPS came in at ~$24.00, and Q4 2025 alone beat consensus by 39% on EPS ($9.37 vs. $6.73). Backlog surged to $11.94B — up 99% year-over-year — driven primarily by data-center construction. Technology verticals now represent ~45% of revenue (up from 33% in 2024), and gross margins expanded to 24.1% in FY2025 vs. 21.0% in 2024 and 17.9% in 2022. Management guided mid-to-high-teens same-store revenue growth for 2026, weighted toward H1, and is expanding modular fabrication capacity from ~3M sq ft to ~4M sq ft. The stock has already re-rated sharply — up ~+42% YTD in 2026 and near its 52-week high of $1,799.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** FIX is a high-quality data center contractor with a huge backlog; consensus "buy" at ~$1,722 mean target (only 4 analysts cover it). The market is broadly aware of the data-center tailwind and has already substantially priced it in. The stock trades at 61x trailing PE, 40x forward PE, and 6.8x EV/sales — rich even by mega-cap tech standards for a contractor.
- **Our view:** The consensus is right on the business quality but wrong on the current risk/reward. The stock's 5x run from ~$355 (52-week low, though that may be a split artifact) to $1,763 has front-loaded 3-4 years of earnings growth. Forward PE of 40x for a construction services company that still has execution risk, cyclical labor cost exposure, and 45% concentration in one volatile vertical (hyperscaler capex) is stretched. The incremental return at current prices is modest unless data-center capex accelerates further beyond already elevated 2026 guidance — and hyperscaler CapEx is beginning to face scrutiny (AI ROI questions, trade policy uncertainty on semiconductors).
- **Trigger:** A hyperscaler announcing a pause or reduction in data-center buildout (already heard from some, e.g., Amazon/Microsoft CapEx caution), a guidance miss on bookings in Q1/Q2 2026, or labor cost inflation eating into the unprecedented 14.4% operating margins. Insider selling noted (per news search) after record results — another caution signal. Conversely, an upgrade catalyst would be Q1 2026 backlog sustaining at $12B+ with margin guidance raised.

## Inflection Point

FIX has already passed its inflection — it completed the transition from regional HVAC contractor to national MEP platform with data-center specialization over 2023–2025. The inflection is well-documented, priced in, and reflected in a 5x+ run. This is now a "stay at elevated valuation if the thesis continues" story, not a fresh inflection opportunity. Any future inflection would require modular manufacturing achieving structural manufacturing margins (not just fabrication for own projects) or international expansion, neither of which is visible yet.

## Bull Case

- Data-center buildout accelerates further: AI infrastructure spending grows beyond 2025 levels, backlog sustains at $12–15B, 2026 revenue reaches $11B+ with margins holding at 24%+
- Modular capacity expansion (to 4M sq ft by end-2026) enables margin improvement as in-house fabrication grows as a % of revenue
- Labor market easing lets FIX execute more projects simultaneously without wage inflation eating margins
- FIX consolidates more regional MEP players at accretive multiples, extending its geographic moat
- Re-rating to 45x forward PE if data-center cycle proves longer and deeper than expected → $2,300–$2,500

## Bear Case

- **Primary source risk not in headlines:** Management disclosed that same-store guidance is "weighted to H1" — implying they expect deceleration in H2 2026. This is not widely discussed in the bullish news coverage. If H2 bookings disappoint, the high backlog may mask an underlying slowdown in new orders.
- Hyperscaler CapEx moderation: Microsoft, Amazon have signaled willingness to pause data-center commitments; any major cancellation hits FIX's 45%-concentrated backlog
- Labor cost inflation: MEP labor is tight nationally; wage pressures could compress the 14.4% operating margin back toward historical 8-10%
- At 40x forward PE, any guidance miss reprices the stock violently — 2020-style drawdowns of 30-50% are plausible if EPS expectations are cut
- Insider selling: management and insiders have been selling after the record run, suggesting they view the stock as fully valued

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

FIX is an exceptional business — best-in-class MEP operator with a transformational data-center exposure that has compounded earnings at 60%+ for three years. The problem is the price: at $1,763, the stock has already priced in most of the good news. GBM models cluster +6% to +34% upside at today's price (vs. +35–57% when they ran at $1,425 two weeks ago), and the full scenario EV is essentially flat (+0.8%). The 40x forward PE is rich for a cyclical services business with 45% concentration in a single volatile vertical. Insider selling after record results is a caution signal. Entry point was $900–$1,200 (now past); watch for a 20–30% pullback on any hyperscaler deceleration to create an attractive entry.

**If WATCH:** Would upgrade to BUY at $1,200–$1,350 (25–30x forward earnings), which would provide ~35–40% upside to bull-case scenarios with downside protection. Specific re-entry trigger: backlog sustains above $10B after any drawdown, confirming data-center thesis intact despite price correction.
