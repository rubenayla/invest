## Business Primer

**What they do today.** Planet Labs sells **Earth observation as a subscription**. They operate the largest fleet of imaging satellites ever built — about 200 "Dove" satellites that photograph the entire landmass of Earth, every day, at roughly 3-meter resolution, plus around 20 "SkySat" satellites for higher-resolution tasking shots. Customers pay an annual subscription to access the imagery through an application programming interface (API) or web platform. Revenue splits into three buckets: **Defense and Intelligence** (59% of revenue, growing 50%+) — the United States National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency, NATO members, allied governments; the Ukraine war has accelerated this significantly. **Civil government** — agriculture ministries (crop yields), forestry, disaster response. **Commercial** (declining) — insurance, mining, energy; most commercial customers struggle to justify the price for raw imagery.

**Where they want to grow.** Three explicit bets. First, **sovereign data contracts**: large multi-year deals with national governments that want their own monitoring capability without launching satellites — the $230M National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency End-of-Life Contract (EOCL) and similar NATO deals are the template. Second, **next-generation constellations**: Pelican (high-resolution, replacing SkySat) and Tanager (hyperspectral — detects chemical composition, methane leaks, mineral types) open new use cases at higher price points per image. Third, **artificial intelligence-derived insights instead of raw pixels** — selling "here are the changes and alerts we detected" instead of "here's a photo, you figure it out." Much higher margin and stickier customer relationships.

**What could go wrong (business risks, not stock risks).** Customer concentration is the biggest one — a handful of government contracts drive most growth, and one non-renewal or political shift (US administration cuts intelligence budget) and the bull case implodes. Stock-based compensation runs at 41% of revenue, meaning "adjusted earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization" (adjusted EBITDA) hides real economic dilution; share count grew 16% year over year. Governance: founder Will Marshall holds Class B shares with 10x voting rights and no sunset clause; an earnout mechanism just minted ~6.3M extra shares when the stock hit $21 in February 2026. Next-generation satellites (Pelican, Tanager) cost more per unit, so if revenue per satellite doesn't scale proportionally, the just-achieved free-cash-flow breakeven reverses. Competition is intensifying: Maxar (sharper), BlackSky (faster revisit), ICEYE (radar, sees through clouds), and increasingly cheap launch lets anyone deploy imaging satellites. Planet's moat is the time-series archive — eight years of daily imagery of Earth that competitors can't replicate — but "good-enough" sensors with newer technology can erode that edge.

**How to think about it.** This is **data SaaS riding on infrastructure** (their satellites), not a launch or manufacturing business. Once the constellation is up, gross margins should expand like software (~80%+ at maturity), and revenue is subscription-like and recurring. Success looks like "becoming the default geospatial data layer for governments and AI applications." Closer cousins are Palantir (defense data) and Snowflake (data platform), not Rocket Lab or Maxar.

## Situation Summary

Planet Labs PBC (NYSE: PL) operates the world's largest commercial Earth-observation (EO) constellation: ~200 SuperDoves (daily 3 m global coverage) plus the new Pelican high-resolution (~50 cm, target 30 cm Gen-2) and Tanager hyperspectral platforms. After ten years burning cash, fiscal 2026 (ended 2026-01-31) was the company's first profitable year on adjusted EBITDA ($15.5M) and free cash flow ($52.9M). Revenue grew 26% to $307.7M, Q4 accelerated to +41% Y/Y, and management guided FY27 to $415–440M (+39% mid) with capex $80–95M. The growth re-acceleration is real and is almost entirely defense-driven: Defense & Intelligence = 59% of FY26 revenue and grew >50% Y/Y, fueled by NATO, a €240M Germany contract, a 10-Pelican JSAT (Japan) deal, Swedish Armed Forces, NRO EOCL renewal, and Luno-B from NGA. Commercial/agriculture revenue is **declining**; civil government is flat. The stock has gone parabolic on the European-sovereign-EO-demand narrative and trades at 48x sales and 35x next-year sales — pricing in flawless execution through FY29.

## Variant Perception (Steinhardt's 4 Qs)

1. **The Idea.** Planet is a structural beneficiary of sovereign-EO demand (NATO build-out, post-Ukraine European rearmament, Pacific theater monitoring). Defense backlog now provides 2+ years of revenue visibility and pulls the model from "story stock" to "validated infrastructure."
2. **Consensus.** Strong Buy (7 buys / 3 holds / 0 sells; 22 analysts); mean PT ≈ $33–36 — i.e. **below the current $41.61**. Short interest 9–11% of float. Bull narrative: $900M backlog × 39% growth × FCF-positive = "Palantir of space." Bear narrative: hyper-cyclical defense ramp + dilution + commercial rot.
3. **Where I differ.** Consensus is converging on a 35–50x sales multiple as the "new normal" given backlog visibility, but: (a) the entire revenue surprise is one-time signing bonuses and prepayments from sovereign deals being recognized as RPO, not recurring run-rate; (b) SBC at $126M is **41% of revenue** — adjusted EBITDA is a fiction, GAAP operating margin remained −30%; (c) commercial/ag rot is being papered over by defense, hiding a real business deterioration that will reappear if defense spending slows; (d) analyst PTs trail the spot price, meaning the marginal buyer is momentum, not fundamental. Variant perception: **the multiple, not the business, is the trade.**
4. **Dateable trigger (0–6 mo).** Q1 FY27 print (June 2026) — first quarter where defense comparison normalizes and any commercial drag becomes visible without the European-deal cover; also first Pelican Gen-2 revenue contribution. A guide-raise prints to $50+; an in-line or commercial-soft print could trigger 25–35% multiple compression.

Quantitative models are uninformative here. Anchor instead on **sales multiple normalization**. Framework: FY27 revenue $430M, FY28 revenue $580M (35% growth, decelerating). A "mature space-data SaaS" multiple is 10–14x sales (Palantir at 60x is the outlier, not the comp). Maxar pre-take-private traded at 2–3x sales. Average defense primes trade at 1.5–3x. Planet's "fair" multiple at scale is probably 8–12x — implying FY28 fair value of $14–20/sh once the multiple resets, even with 35–40% growth. The bull's "$50 target" assumes Planet sustains 30x+ sales **past FY28**, which has no historical precedent for an EO/data business at scale.

## Business Quality (15/25)

- **Moat (3/5):** Constellation scale and 10-year imagery archive are real (~3,000 daily images of every land point) and hard to replicate, but Maxar (better resolution), BlackSky (faster revisit), and proliferating sovereign constellations (China, Korea, India) all chip at it.
- **Management (3/5):** Co-founder Will Marshall is technically credible; team executed the COGS pivot. But repeated dilution events, earnout share issuance just before the rally crested, and dropping customer-count disclosure all show shareholder-friendly is not the operating instinct.
- **Profitability (2/5):** First positive adjusted EBITDA year; GAAP still deeply negative because SBC is 41% of revenue. FCF positive but boosted by sovereign prepayments — true sustainable FCF likely 30–50% lower.
- **Balance sheet (3/5):** $178M net cash, but $462M convertible due 2028 will be addressed in equity if shares stay above conversion — more dilution latent.
- **Growth runway (4/5):** Sovereign EO TAM is genuinely expanding; Defense & Intelligence growth >50% is sustainable for 18–24 months on backlog alone; AI-derived insights (in-space compute partnership with Google/NVIDIA) optionality is real if early.

## Inflection Point

The company **already inflected** in FY26 (first profitable year) — the bull case has played out. What's left is execution on the Pelican Gen-2 ramp (30 cm imagery is the threshold for direct Maxar competition), Tanager hyperspectral commercialization (a wholly new revenue stream for methane/mineral exploration), and the FY27 backlog conversion. Risk is asymmetric: Pelican delays or Tanager monetization lag don't show in numbers until FY28 — but a single quarter of commercial revenue staying flat-to-down past FY27 would unmask that the "growth" is sovereign signing bonuses pulled forward.

## Bull Case (probability 30%)

- Sovereign EO demand sustains through 2030 as China/Pacific tensions accelerate; Germany €240M deal is a template replicable across 10+ European nations.
- Tanager hyperspectral opens a $1B+ TAM in carbon-credit verification, methane regulation, and mining — Planet is the only commercial supplier of full-band hyperspectral globally.
- Pelican Gen-2 (30 cm) takes EOCL share from Maxar/BlackSky.
- AI-derived insights (in-space compute with NVIDIA/Google) flips PL from imagery vendor to platform.
- FY29 revenue $850M, 65% gross margin, 25% EBITDA margin = $215M EBITDA × 35x = $7.5B EV, plus $400M cash → market cap $7.9B + Tanager/SunCatcher optionality. **Target $60–75** at peak.

---

## Bear Case (probability 30%)

- Defense backlog is front-loaded with one-time signing bonuses; FY28 revenue grows only 15–20% as Germany/JSAT contracts move from prepayment recognition to steady-state burn rate.
- Commercial/agriculture revenue keeps declining; commercial is a structural loser — small farmers won't pay subscription for imagery they get from public Landsat/Copernicus.
- SBC stays at $120M+ → GAAP losses persist → shares grow another 8–12% in dilution by FY28 (convertible note conversion + ongoing RSU grants).
- Multiple resets to 10–15x sales as growth decelerates → FY28 revenue $580M × 12x = $7B EV ÷ ~360M shares = **$19/sh.**
- **Under-covered risk (the one news doesn't cover):** **co-founder Class B supervoting share structure plus the earnout dilution mechanism.** The company just issued ~5.7M new Class A and 584K Class B shares in Feb 2026 because the stock hit $21, and the founders' Class B retains 10:1 voting control with no sunset. Public investors are minority-economic and effectively disenfranchised on governance — every earnings call decision (Tanager capex, SBC policy, M&A) is unilaterally founder-controlled. This combined with the disclosure quality slipping (customer-count metric is being retired) is the structural risk media coverage entirely misses.

## Verdict

**WATCH — MEDIUM conviction — Expected value −18%.**

Real business, wrong price. The fundamental thesis (sovereign EO demand → backlog → FCF) is validated, but the stock has discounted three more years of perfect execution into 18 months of multiple expansion. The path to losing 25–40% on a single miss is shorter than the path to another 50% upside.

- **Existing holders:** trim above $40 if the position has run hard; let the rest ride with a mental stop near $30 (loss of $33 analyst-PT support).
- **Non-holders:** wait for Q1 FY27 print (June) and either a 20%+ pullback or hard evidence the commercial segment has stabilized. Best risk/reward entry is near $25–28.
- **Re-rate to BUY** if: commercial revenue returns to growth, Pelican Gen-2 wins direct Maxar replacement contract, or stock pulls back to <12x FY28 sales.
- **Re-rate to PASS** if: Q1 print shows defense growth decelerating below 35% Y/Y, or another large dilutive equity raise is announced.

**Thesis-break levels:** sustained >$50 means multiple expansion is more durable than assumed (re-rate up); sustained <$25 means commercial rot has won (re-rate down).
