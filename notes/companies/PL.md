## Situation Summary

Planet Labs has had a spectacular run -- up ~1,000% over the past 12 months from ~$2.79 to $35.88 -- driven by a triple catalyst: (1) accelerating revenue growth (FY26 +26% YoY, Q4 +41% YoY), (2) first full fiscal year of positive adjusted EBITDA ($15.5M) and FCF ($53M), and (3) a massive defense pivot anchored by its SHIELD IDIQ prime selection ($151B ceiling) and multi-country government contracts ($900M backlog, +79% YoY). The NVIDIA partnership for GPU-native AI processing of satellite imagery adds a narrative tailwind. However, the stock now trades at 40x trailing revenue and 29x forward revenue -- pricing in years of flawless execution on a company that still loses $95M/yr on a GAAP operating basis. The $460M convertible notes offering (Sept 2025) funded Pelican constellation expansion but increased leverage significantly.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Planet Labs is a "new space" AI + defense darling with the world's largest daily earth imaging constellation. The market expects ~39% revenue growth in FY27 (guided $415-440M), continued government contract wins, and an eventual path to GAAP profitability. Analysts have a Buy consensus ($35 mean target, range $18-$40) -- but the stock is already above the mean target. Retail and momentum investors have piled in on the ASTS/RKLB "new space" narrative.

- **Our view (SpaceX Starship thesis):** The Starship angle is real but **already largely priced in** at 40x revenue. Cheaper launches will help Planet deploy more Pelican and next-gen satellites, but: (a) Planet currently launches on Falcon 9, not Starship -- Starship benefit is speculative and years away for PL specifically, (b) satellite manufacturing is the bottleneck, not launch cost -- Pelican production just ramped, (c) the stock has already re-rated from $3 to $36 on the defense/AI narrative. The marginal upside from Starship is incremental, not transformative, for a company whose core constraint is converting a $900M backlog into recurring revenue. The real question is whether Planet can achieve operating leverage and reach GAAP breakeven before the multiple compresses.

- **Trigger:** FY27 quarterly earnings showing GAAP operating margin improvement (moving from -31% toward breakeven). If they can demonstrate SBC discipline and operating leverage, the multiple could sustain. Conversely, any revenue miss or margin regression will trigger a violent de-rating from these heights.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- Planet is at a genuine profitability inflection.** After years of cash-burning growth, FY26 was the first year of positive adj. EBITDA and FCF. The operating loss is narrowing rapidly (-$176M -> -$167M -> -$111M -> -$95M over 4 years). The $900M backlog and 39% revenue growth guidance for FY27 suggest operating leverage should accelerate as satellite constellation costs are largely fixed.

**However**, the inflection is already aggressively priced. The stock is up 1,000% in 12 months. The question is not whether an inflection exists (it clearly does) but whether the stock price already discounts the next 3-5 years of this inflection.

**Starship-specific inflection:** The SpaceX Starship thesis is a second-order benefit. Cheaper launches would reduce Planet's capex for constellation replenishment and expansion ($83M capex in FY26, rising). But Planet currently uses Falcon 9, and the benefit of Starship is 2-4 years out for non-Starlink payloads. This is a "nice to have" optionality, not a near-term catalyst.

## Bull Case

- FY27 revenue beats the high end of guidance ($440M+) as SHIELD task orders and international defense contracts ramp faster than expected
- Pelican Gen-2 (30cm resolution) closes the gap with Maxar, opening high-value NGA/NRO tasking contracts worth $50M+/yr incremental
- NVIDIA AI partnership creates a new "analytics-as-a-service" revenue stream (higher margin than raw imagery)
- Operating leverage accelerates: SBC as % of revenue declines as the company matures, pushing toward adj. EBITDA margins of 15-20%
- Starship reduces constellation refresh costs by 30-50% in 2028+, driving structural margin improvement

## Bear Case

- At 40x revenue, any execution stumble triggers 30-50% drawdown -- the stock has zero margin of safety
- SBC continues at $110M+/yr, meaning real economic profitability is years away despite "non-GAAP profit" claims
- SHIELD IDIQ is a framework, not a contract -- actual task orders may be small relative to $151B ceiling; Planet competes with established primes for every dollar
- Defense spending is politically cyclical -- a budget sequestration or pivot could slow government bookings
- Maxar (higher resolution), BlackSky (faster delivery), and new entrants erode Planet's medium-resolution daily imaging niche
- $460M convertible notes dilution overhang through 2030; warrant exercises in April 2026 add ~13M shares
- CEO selling shares while stock is near ATH

## Verdict

**PASS** -- Conviction: HIGH

Planet Labs is a genuinely good business at a genuinely terrible price. The company has real competitive advantages (largest daily imaging constellation, 10+ year data archive, deep government relationships), is at a legitimate profitability inflection, and has strong secular tailwinds (defense intelligence, climate monitoring, AI-powered analytics). The $900M backlog and NVIDIA partnership are meaningful.

However, at 40x trailing revenue and 29x forward revenue, the stock prices in 5+ years of flawless 35%+ compounding -- for a company that still loses $95M/yr on a GAAP basis and dilutes shareholders 5% annually through SBC. The Starship thesis is interesting optionality but already priced in and 2-4 years from being material. Expected value from the scenario table is deeply negative (-25%) because the asymmetry is unfavorable: limited upside from already-premium levels vs. significant downside if execution falters.

The 12.4% short interest and parabolic chart add to the risk of violent corrections. CEO insider selling is a yellow flag.

**Would upgrade to WATCH at:** $18-22 range (~15-18x FY27 forward revenue), where the risk/reward becomes more balanced and the growth story doesn't need to be perfect to work.

**Would upgrade to BUY at:** $12-15 range (~10-12x FY27 forward revenue), where the margin of safety compensates for the still-unprofitable GAAP business.
