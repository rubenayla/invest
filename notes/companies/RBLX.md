# Roblox Corporation (RBLX)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Electronic Gaming & Multimedia
**Price:** $54.55 (2026-04-23) | **Market Cap:** $39.0B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-23

## Situation Summary

Roblox just reported its best quarter on record: Q4 2025 revenue +43% YoY to $1.4B, bookings +63% to $2.2B, and DAUs exploding 69% YoY to 144M — crossing 100M for the first time. The growth was heavily driven by a viral hit (likely Dress to Impress), which management explicitly said they are *not* assuming repeats in 2026 guidance of 22-26% bookings growth. Full-year 2025 FCF reached $1.35B (from near-zero in 2022), marking a clear profitability inflection. The stock has pulled back ~5% from the $57.50 model base price to $54.55 today despite these results. The key unresolved tension: massive engagement growth but monetization per user remains low (~$1.50/DAU/quarter), and the platform still loses money on a GAAP basis ($1.07B net loss in 2025).

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** RBLX is a "show me" story — analysts believe in the engagement (mean target $103.60 from 35 analysts, "buy" recommendation) but worry about monetization per user lagging peers, negative GAAP earnings, and regulatory/safety overhangs. The stock trades at ~8x EV/Revenue, premium but not extreme.
- **Our view:** The market underweights two things: (1) the FCF inflection is REAL and accelerating — FCF went from -$58M (2022) to +$1.35B (2025), a $1.4B swing in 3 years at 30% revenue CAGR; (2) the age-verification rollout (45% global DAU penetration) is quietly building a 18+ cohort that monetizes 40% higher. The market prices RBLX as a kids platform with ceiling economics; we see an expanding adult demographic unlocking advertising and premium monetization.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (likely May 2026) will show whether the DAU base stabilizes post-viral-hit or continues growing organically. Any evidence of adult DAU retention + bookings-per-user expansion triggers re-rating toward $80-100.

## Inflection Point

YES — clear and observable. FCF went from -$58M (2022) to +$1.35B (2025), a $1.4B swing in 3 years. The inflection is driven by: (1) bookings growth outpacing creator payouts and infrastructure scaling, (2) maturing infrastructure cost structure, (3) emerging advertising business. Management guided ~26% FCF growth in 2026 at the midpoint. This is not a speculative future inflection — it has already happened and is continuing. GAAP profitability likely 2-3 years out.

## Bull Case

- Age-verification rollout enables 18+ advertising at scale; ARPU for verified adults is 40% higher and growing — could meaningfully shift blended monetization by 2027
- Bookings growth sustains above 20% even without viral hits as organic DAU growth (international, older demographics) compounds
- FCF hits $2B+ in 2026, compressing EV/FCF to ~20x and triggering a quality re-rating
- Creator economy flywheel: $1.5B paid to creators in 2025 drives more content → more engagement → more payers (94% growth in monthly unique payers YoY)
- **Primary source risk not in headlines:** Management disclosed that the creator payout rate increase (8.5%) effective September 2024 is structural — it permanently raises the cost of revenue floor, limiting gross margin expansion even as revenue scales. This compresses the path to GAAP profitability and is underappreciated in sell-side models.

## Bear Case

- Post-viral-hit normalization: Q4 2025 DAU surge was partially driven by a single viral title; if DAUs retreat in Q1 2026, the monetization narrative collapses
- Monetization per DAU structurally low (~$1.50/DAU/quarter vs $4-8 for mobile gaming peers) — users treat Roblox as free entertainment, cultural habit too embedded to change
- Regulatory risk: child safety legislation (KOSA in US, DSA in EU) could mandate stricter age verification, limit data collection for ads, or impose fine exposure that dwarfs current cash flows
- **Primary source risk underweighted by media:** Management confirmed the infrastructure cost structure is high and NOT expected to scale down significantly near-term — bandwidth/compute costs scale roughly linearly with engagement hours, not with monetization. This means the 88% YoY growth in engagement hours also means proportionally higher infrastructure spend, and gross margin improvement requires bookings to outpace engagement. The news focuses on DAU growth, not this cost dynamic.
- Competition from Fortnite Creative, Minecraft, and emerging UGC platforms could draw creators/users without Roblox's weak economic incentives

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

The FCF inflection is real and impressive, and the business quality has meaningfully improved. However, the Q4 2025 DAU surge (69% YoY) is partially viral-hit-driven, and management explicitly said 2026 guidance does not assume a repeat — meaning the key question of sustainable organic DAU growth won't be answered until Q1 results (~May 2026). At $54.55, the stock is not cheap on traditional metrics (8.4x EV/Rev, negative GAAP earnings) but is reasonable on FCF trajectory. The variant perception — adult demographic monetization + FCF inflection underappreciated — is credible but needs Q1 confirmation.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** Q1 2026 DAUs holding above 125M organically + bookings tracking at or above guidance midpoint. At that point, entry around $50-55 with thesis-break at $40 (below bear case target).
