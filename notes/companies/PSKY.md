# Paramount Skydance Corporation (PSKY)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Entertainment
**Price:** $11.67 (2026-04-16) | **Market Cap:** $12.97B | **EV:** $26.2B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-16

## Situation Summary
David Ellison's Skydance closed its merger with Paramount Global in Aug-2025 and has moved aggressively: cost-cut target raised from $2B -> $3B+ run-rate (>$1.4B banked by end-2025, +$1B by end-2026), 600 employees took RTO buyouts, DTC segment hit full-year profitability in 2025 with Paramount+ at 79M subs. In Feb-2026 PSKY announced a **$110B EV acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery** ($31/share cash), outbidding Netflix after a bidding war. **WBD shareholder vote is April 23, 2026 (7 days away)** — Glass Lewis recommends FOR. Funding locked: $47B of new Class B equity from Ellison/RedBird at $16.02/share, $24B pledged by Gulf sovereigns (PIF/Qatar/L'imad), $3.25B rights offering. Stock is down ~30% YTD / ~33% from 6-months ago — the market is pricing deal-execution risk, pro-forma leverage fear (4.3x fully-synergized, potentially 6-7x peak), and dilution from the $16.02 raise that is 37% above current spot.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Empire-building leveraged bet from an inexperienced CEO. Pro-forma leverage approaches $100B net debt, integration is brutal, streaming is structurally disadvantaged vs. Netflix, linear TV is in secular decline. Wells Fargo cut PT to $8 (-31%). Mean analyst PT $12.93 implies +11%. Rating: Hold. Michael Burry publicly said he 'doesn't trust the buyer.'"
- **Our view:** Three things the consensus under-weights. (1) **Ellison's operational execution is already visible** — $3B cost target hit in ~6 months post-close vs. $2B original, DTC profitability pulled forward, aggressive paywall/price hikes actually sticking. This is not a "promise" anymore, it's in the Q4 print. (2) The **WBD deal is cash-to-WBD-holders, not stock-for-stock** — PSKY shareholders are NOT being diluted at $11.67 (the dilution was via $47B at $16.02, a 37% premium to spot, meaning incoming equity holders are paying UP). Current holders get exposure to $6B+ synergies against an already-discounted base. (3) **The event calendar is extremely dense in next 90 days** — April 23 vote, May 4 Q1 earnings (first post-merger-announcement), likely DOJ/FCC positioning, Q3-close countdown — any of which can compress the discount. Our edge: the market is conflating "leveraged and complex" with "impaired," but operational trajectory in streaming + cost execution is quantifiably improving at the current valuation (0.45x sales, 4.0x EV/EBITDA 2026E).
- **Trigger:** April 23 WBD shareholder vote. FOR vote (Glass Lewis-endorsed, WBD board unanimous) removes the largest overhang, converts the story from "bidding risk" to "execution story" with a clear 6-Bn-synergy anchor. Even a NO vote (lower probability) arguably relieves the overhang the other direction — PSKY becomes a cleaner standalone at 4x EBITDA with visible cost momentum. Either resolution compresses uncertainty.

## Inflection Point
**YES — multiple overlapping inflections:**
1. **New management with a clear, public, dateable cost plan** (already hitting targets ahead of schedule — Fisher/Steinhardt checkbox).
2. **DTC profitability crossed in 2025** — now growing from a profitable base instead of bleeding cash.
3. **WBD merger vote in 7 days** — largest M&A event overhang in media since AOL-TW.
4. **Price structurally down 30% YTD / 33% in 6 months** into the event; classic compressed-entry setup.

This is a textbook "corporate event + new management with credible plan" inflection per Step 5 of the protocol.

## Bull Case
- WBD vote passes April 23 -> deal closes Q3 2026 -> combined entity targets $6B synergies, #2 global streaming scale (200M subs), re-rates toward Netflix-light multiple (6-7x EV/EBITDA vs. 4x now).
- $3B cost takeout continues to beat, pulling 2026 EBITDA toward $4.0-4.2B vs. $3.8B guide.
- Paramount+ price hike (Jan-2026) sticks with <2% churn — ARPU acceleration visible in Q1 print (May 4).
- Larry Ellison's capital backstop + Gulf sovereign financing = practical zero bankruptcy risk even in stress.
- Stock is the cheapest major-studio equity on EV/Sales (0.45x) despite owning CBS/Paramount+/Pluto/Nickelodeon.

## Bear Case
- WBD vote FAILS or deal gets blocked by DOJ on antitrust — PSKY trades down on "empire lost" narrative, but ironically this bear case isn't that bad (cleaner standalone entity).
- WBD deal CLOSES but integration is a disaster: 200M subs is a management bandwidth problem, Zaslav-era WBD had hidden debt/litigation, Max+Paramount+ cannibalize rather than combine. Pro-forma leverage strands equity for 5+ years.
- Linear TV decline accelerates beyond the 5% affiliate drag; political ad cycle disappoints in 2026; streaming ARPU hike causes 5%+ churn.
- Regulatory: Trump DOJ or FCC uses merger approval as political leverage on CBS News coverage — already rumored as a conditional concession.
- Dilution continues: rights offering + future raises at sub-$15 if stock stays weak.
