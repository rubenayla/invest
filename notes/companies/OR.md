## Situation Summary

OR Royalties (formerly Osisko Gold Royalties, renamed May 2025) delivered a record "triple crown" year in 2025: revenues up 45% to $277M, operating cash flow up 54% to $246M, net income surging from $16M to $206M — entirely on gold price appreciation while GEOs were flat at ~80,800. The company closed 2025 debt-free with $142M cash. Q1 2026 was even more dramatic: revenue hit $102.8M (+87% YoY) driven by gold prices near all-time highs. The 2026 GEO guidance of 80,000–90,000 at ~97% cash margin is essentially unchanged volumetrically, meaning ALL earnings growth this year is gold-price leverage. The long-term story is 50% GEO volume growth by 2030, driven by fully-financed new mine builds (Windfall, Hermosa/Taylor, Cariboo) starting production in 2027+. Stock has pulled back from ~$45+ highs to ~$39, even as gold remains above $3,200/oz — the price dislocation from the company's underlying earnings power is the opportunity.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** OR is a quality gold royalty compounder, fairly valued at current gold prices; upside depends on gold price remaining elevated; some concern about Canadian Malartic concentration (~50%+ of GEOs from one mine)
- **Our view:** The market is pricing OR at a 15-20% discount to peers (Royal Gold, Sandstorm) on EV/GEO and hasn't fully repriced for the step-change in earnings power at $3,000+ gold. The autoresearch model ($53.50 FV) was calibrated at $40/share with gold lower — at $39.22 today after a gold run-up, the embedded discount has actually widened. Furthermore, consensus undervalues the 2027-2030 GEO growth optionality from 6 development-stage royalties; these are funded by others (Agnico, Gold Fields, South32) so OR bears zero capex risk.
- **Trigger:** (1) Q1 2026 earnings call (May 2026) showing annualized EPS run rate ~$0.45–0.50/quarter at current gold = ~$1.80 fwd EPS, re-rating forward PE from 23x toward peers at 30x; (2) first production update from Windfall or Hermosa confirming 2027 GEO step-up; (3) any gold price leg above $3,500 is pure earnings leverage with no incremental cost.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — dual inflection underway:**

1. **Earnings power inflection (now):** 2025 EPS was $1.10 vs $0.09 prior year. Q1 2026 revenue of $102.8M implies ~$0.40+ EPS/quarter at current gold. The market hasn't fully repriced to this new earnings regime. Forward PE compresses from 35x trailing to ~22x forward at current gold.

2. **Volume inflection (2027):** First of the "class of 2027" development projects (Windfall, Hermosa/Taylor) entering production creates a second earnings step-up independent of gold price. This is a classic "hidden option" that doesn't show up in consensus 12-month models.

The first inflection is already observable (Q4 2025 earnings, Q1 2026 revenue). The second begins to crystallize in 12-18 months.

## Bull Case

- Gold sustains $3,000+ through 2027 (supply constraints, central bank buying, de-dollarization). At $3,200 gold, OR earns ~$1.80/share annualized. At 30x (Royal Gold's multiple), that's $54.
- 2027 GEO step-up materializes: Windfall (Gold Fields) and Hermosa (South32) begin production on schedule, driving GEOs toward the 100K+ range. This forces upward estimate revisions.
- Debt-free balance sheet enables one or two accretive royalty acquisitions in 2026, adding future GEO streams at today's deal prices.
- Canadian Malartic mine life extension/expansion — Agnico has been drilling extensions; any resource upgrade accretes directly to OR at zero cost.
- Re-rating toward Franco-Nevada / Royal Gold multiples (30-35x fwd earnings) — historically OR has traded at a discount due to smaller size; as market cap grows past $8B, more institutional mandates can include it.

## Bear Case

- **Gold price mean-reversion to $2,200 (management's primary undisclosed risk):** Management flagged commodity price volatility prominently. At $2,200 gold (2023 levels), revenue falls ~35% to ~$180M. This is the single biggest risk and it is almost entirely absent from the current news narrative, which focuses on gold's "structural" bull case.
- **Windfall/Cariboo development delays (primary source, underreported):** Management specifically named permitting delays as a key risk. Windfall (Gold Fields) is in Quebec with complex Indigenous consultation requirements. A 12-18 month delay pushes the 2030 GEO target out and removes a key re-rating catalyst.
- **Canadian Malartic concentration:** ~50% of GEOs from one mine. Any operational disruption at Malartic (weather, labor, geotechnical) causes an outsized earnings hit. This is in all analyst reports but worth keeping front of mind.
- **Currency risk (CAD/USD):** OR reports in USD but most underlying royalties are on Canadian mines. A strong CAD raises effective royalty cost basis for operators, potentially reducing production economics.
- **CSA transition impact (disclosed in guidance):** Management specifically flagged "transitional impacts at the CSA operation" as a 2026 GEO headwind — this was not in the news headlines but appears in the guidance decomposition.
