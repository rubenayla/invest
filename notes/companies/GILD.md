# Gilead Sciences, Inc. (GILD)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Drug Manufacturers - General
**Price:** $129.67 (2026-05-19) | **Market Cap:** $161.0B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-05-19

## Business Primer

**What they do today.** Gilead is a large-cap biopharmaceutical company anchored by an HIV franchise that generates roughly two-thirds of revenue. Biktarvy — a once-daily single-tablet treatment regimen — is the cornerstone product at ~$3.4B/quarter and holds >50% U.S. HIV treatment share. Descovy is the legacy daily oral pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) drug, and Yeztugo (lenacapavir) is the newly launched twice-yearly injectable for HIV prevention. Beyond HIV, Gilead sells Veklury (remdesivir, COVID-19 antiviral, now a runoff business), a Liver Disease portfolio (hepatitis C and B drugs plus Livdelzi for primary biliary cholangitis), an Oncology franchise built around Trodelvy (a Trop-2 antibody-drug conjugate for triple-negative and HR+ breast cancer), and Cell Therapy (Yescarta and Tecartus, CAR-T treatments for blood cancers, sold via the Kite subsidiary). Customers are payers, hospitals, specialty pharmacies, and government programs globally. Q1 2026 revenue was $7.0B (HIV $5.0B, Oncology $810M, Liver $767M, Veklury $144M).

**Where they want to grow.** Management's central strategic bet is converting HIV from a mature franchise into a growth platform via lenacapavir — the only twice-yearly long-acting injectable for PrEP, branded Yeztugo (approved 2025). Q1 2026 Yeztugo sales hit $166M (+72% sequentially) and full-year guidance was raised to $1B, putting it on a blockbuster trajectory in year one. The follow-on Bictegravir/Lenacapavir (BIC/LEN) combo for HIV treatment has a PDUFA date of August 27, 2026 — this would be the long-acting heir to Biktarvy. Gilead has also laid out seven new HIV launches through 2033 to keep franchise share post-Biktarvy generics. Outside HIV, the priorities are scaling Trodelvy in oncology (+37% Q1 2026, $402M) and rebuilding the cell therapy pipeline through three major 2026 acquisitions — Arcellx (CAR-T for myeloma), Ouro, and Tubulis (next-gen antibody-drug conjugates) — driving the $11.5B in acquired IPR&D charges flowing through 2026 earnings.

**What could go wrong (business risks).** The Biktarvy patent cliff was extended from 2033 to April 2036 via 2025 generic settlements, but the cliff is real and structural — when generics hit, ~$13B+ of high-margin revenue erodes fast unless lenacapavir-based regimens have already taken share. Lenacapavir launch execution is the bet: payer access reached 85% in three months (ahead of schedule), but uptake outside the U.S. specialty channel, real-world adherence to twice-yearly dosing, and competitive response from ViiV (GSK/Pfizer) cabotegravir long-acting must all cooperate. Cell therapy is shrinking — Yescarta -14% in Q1 on in-class and out-of-class competition (bispecifics) and is unlikely to recover. Trodelvy carries impairment history and faces antibody-drug conjugate competition (Enhertu, datopotamab). Drug pricing reform — Inflation Reduction Act Medicare negotiation, most-favored-nation pricing proposals, Affordable Care Act subsidy expiration — is a recurring tail risk for the HIV book where Medicare exposure is meaningful. Veklury is in terminal decline (-52% Q1). The 2026 GAAP EPS guidance cut to a $(3.25)-$(2.85) loss (from $6.75-$7.15) reflects $11.5B in M&A write-downs and signals aggressive deal-making with uncertain payback.

**How to think about it.** This is an HIV-franchise cash machine repositioning itself around lenacapavir long-acting therapeutics — bet on lenacapavir transforming HIV from mature to growth. Gilead is not a generic large-cap pharma — it is a category-leader in one therapy area (HIV: >50% U.S. share, durable, high-margin) trying to extend that moat by another decade through a paradigm shift (twice-yearly injection replacing daily pills) while building optionality in oncology. The investment thesis hinges almost entirely on whether the lenacapavir platform compounds revenue faster than Biktarvy erodes — a single-drug, single-franchise wager, not a diversified pharma bet.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** GILD is a "transition story" — HIV cash cow extending Biktarvy to 2036, optionality from lenacapavir, but oncology disappointing and pricing reform a chronic overhang. Analyst mean target $158 / "buy" recommendation. Market is currently anchored on the GAAP EPS cut and skeptical that M&A spending will earn its keep.
- **Our view:** The market is conflating GAAP optical noise (one-time IPR&D write-downs from M&A) with underlying operational deterioration, but the operations are accelerating. Yeztugo's payer access curve (85% in three months, tracking to 90% by mid-2026) is materially ahead of plan, and the BIC/LEN PDUFA in August likely opens a multi-billion-dollar long-acting HIV treatment market that the Street is under-modeling. Biktarvy's three-year patent extension to 2036 is also still being absorbed in long-dated DCFs — most sell-side models still discount from a 2033 cliff. The combination of (a) accelerating Yeztugo, (b) August PDUFA optionality, (c) extended patent runway, and (d) compressed multiple from M&A noise creates an asymmetric setup.
- **Trigger:** August 27, 2026 PDUFA decision on bictegravir/lenacapavir for HIV treatment. Approval reframes the HIV franchise from "defending Biktarvy" to "owning the long-acting treatment and prevention category" — a multiple re-rate catalyst.

## Inflection Point

Yes — clear inflection underway. (1) Lenacapavir/Yeztugo launched mid-2025 and is exceeding plan on payer access and sequential growth. (2) Patent litigation settled in 2025 pushing Biktarvy exclusivity from 2033 to 2036 — three additional years of $13B+ revenue. (3) BIC/LEN PDUFA target August 27, 2026 is a near-term catalyst that, if approved, opens the long-acting HIV treatment market and converts the strategic narrative from "manage decline" to "compound long-acting platform." (4) The 2024 trough year ($480M net income on $3.9B IPR&D) is now in the rear-view mirror; 2025 net income recovered to $8.5B and 2026 underlying earnings are guided higher ex-IPR&D noise.

## Bull Case

- **Yeztugo (lenacapavir) becomes a $5-10B drug by 2030** as twice-yearly dosing converts PrEP-naive populations, displaces Descovy daily oral, and global access programs (PEPFAR/Global Fund) expand reach.
- **BIC/LEN approval August 2026** triggers re-rating; long-acting HIV treatment is a structurally larger TAM than long-acting prevention.
- **Biktarvy patent extension to April 2036** removes the largest valuation overhang; three extra years at peak revenue is worth ~$40B in cumulative FCF.
- **Pricing reform less severe than feared** — Biktarvy already in Medicare negotiation pipeline, but lenacapavir-based regimens reset the franchise outside of small-molecule LOE pressure.
- **Operating leverage and buybacks** — $9.5B annual FCF supports continued ~$2-3B repurchase pace plus 2.5% dividend yield.

## Bear Case

- **M&A overhang isn't just optics — capital allocation is becoming the bear thesis.** The $11.5B IPR&D charge from Arcellx/Ouro/Tubulis is a real cash outlay disguised as a write-down. If these deals underperform like Immunomedics did, Gilead has compounded the Kite/Immunomedics pattern: paying premium prices for oncology assets that don't earn their cost of capital. *(This is the risk management disclosed but news headlines under-cover — they led with "EPS guidance cut" framing rather than capital allocation track record.)*
- **Lenacapavir competitive response from ViiV** (GSK/Pfizer JV) — cabotegravir long-acting is already on market for treatment, and ViiV has a follow-on PrEP candidate. Duopoly compression possible.
- **Drug pricing reform (IRA Medicare negotiation, most-favored-nation pricing, ACA subsidy expiration)** could cut U.S. HIV franchise pricing by 10-20% over the next 3-5 years.
- **Cell therapy continued decline** — Yescarta -14%, Tecartus -4%, bispecifics gaining share. Kite acquisition write-downs not over.
- **Real-world lenacapavir adherence risk** — twice-yearly injection requires patients to show up; if retention falls short of clinical trials, the value proposition vs. daily oral compresses.
