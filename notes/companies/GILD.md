# Gilead Sciences (GILD)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Drug Manufacturers - General
**Price:** $137.21 | **Market Cap:** $170B

## Situation Summary

Gilead Sciences is a large-cap biopharma anchored by a dominant HIV franchise ($20.8B in 2025 sales, 52% US market share) and an expanding oncology portfolio. The company just reported strong FY2025 results ($29.4B revenue, $8.5B net income, $9.5B FCF) and issued constructive 2026 guidance ($29.6-30.0B product sales, $8.45-8.85 non-GAAP EPS). Lenacapavir (Yeztugo), a twice-yearly HIV injectable, is a paradigm-shifting product now on the market. The $7.8B Arcellx acquisition (closing Q2 2026) adds anito-cel, a differentiated BCMA CAR-T for multiple myeloma with a Dec 2026 PDUFA date. Three major FDA decisions are stacked in H2 2026 (lenacapavir oral combo, anito-cel, Trodelvy 1L TNBC), creating a catalyst-rich setup. The stock has pulled back ~12% from its 52-week high of $157.29, trading at $137.21 -- below the analyst consensus target of $157-160.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Gilead is a stable, mature biopharma cash cow trading at a reasonable valuation. HIV franchise is well-managed but growth is decelerating. Oncology (Trodelvy) has been disappointing. The stock is a defensive dividend payer, not a growth name. Most analysts rate Buy with ~$160 targets, but the market is pricing it at a discount to large-cap pharma peers.
- **Our view:** The market is underappreciating three things: (1) lenacapavir transforms HIV from "mature" to "growth" -- the twice-yearly injectable + 7 planned oral combos through 2033 effectively neutralize the Biktarvy patent cliff (2033) and extend the franchise to 2036+; (2) the oncology portfolio is at an inflection with three FDA catalysts in 6 months; (3) the EPS trajectory is genuinely inflecting upward ($0.38 -> $6.78 -> $8.65 guided) and the 15x forward PE doesn't reflect this growth. FCF yield of 5.6% is too high for a business of this quality.
- **Trigger:** Three stacked FDA decisions in H2 2026: lenacapavir oral combo, anito-cel PDUFA (Dec 23, 2026), and Trodelvy 1L mTNBC. Any one of these can re-rate the stock; all three would be transformative.

## Inflection Point

**YES -- Gilead is at a multi-dimensional positive inflection:**

1. **Lenacapavir platform launch:** Transforms HIV from "mature cash cow" to "growth franchise." Twice-yearly injectable (Yeztugo) is on market; oral combos (bictegravir/lenacapavir) showed strong Phase 3 ARTISTRY data. Seven new products planned through 2033, effectively pre-empting the Biktarvy 2033 patent cliff.
2. **Oncology de-risking:** Trodelvy ($1.4B in FY2025, growing 6% YoY) moving to 1L TNBC (larger market). Anito-cel adds CAR-T optionality in multiple myeloma (multi-billion dollar market). Yescarta label expansion to CNS lymphoma approved Feb 2026.
3. **EPS inflection:** FY2024 was trough GAAP earnings ($0.38) due to Trodelvy impairment. FY2025 bounced to $6.78. FY2026 guided $8.45-8.85 non-GAAP -- a clear upward trajectory. Operating leverage visible as revenue grows faster than costs.
4. **Narrative shift:** From "ex-HCV story" to "innovative HIV + oncology platform." Analyst upgrades accelerating (Jefferies $180 PT, Scotiabank raised PT 26% to $177).

**Observable evidence:** Revenue inflecting up, three FDA decisions imminent, management reiterating multi-year growth guidance, institutional accumulation evident.

## Bull Case

- Lenacapavir oral combo approved H2 2026, captures significant share of $30B+ HIV market, extending franchise to 2036+
- Anito-cel approved Dec 2026, enters $15B+ multiple myeloma market with differentiated safety profile (no parkinsonism)
- Trodelvy wins 1L mTNBC, expanding TAM from ~$1.4B to potential $3-4B peak sales
- Multiple re-rating from 15x to 18-20x forward PE as market recognizes growth inflection
- FCF enables continued buybacks ($1.9B/yr) + dividend growth (3.8% increase announced) + bolt-on M&A

## Bear Case

- Oncology setbacks: Trodelvy 1L TNBC fails to differentiate, anito-cel delayed or rejected at PDUFA
- Lenacapavir uptake slower than expected due to pricing/access challenges, particularly in resource-limited settings
- Arcellx integration complexity; $7.8B deal dilutes near-term earnings before anito-cel launches
- Biktarvy competitive pressure from ViiV's long-acting cabotegravir (Cabenuva) and generic risk post-2033
- Broader market sell-off disproportionately hits large-cap pharma (though 0.37 beta provides insulation)
