## Situation Summary

Cigna is a diversified healthcare services company built on two pillars: Evernorth Health Services (pharmacy benefits/Express Scripts, specialty pharmacy, care services — ~80% of revenue) and Cigna Healthcare (medical insurance — ~20% of revenue). The company sold its Medicare Advantage business to HCSC in 2025, deliberately de-risking from the segment most exposed to the Trump administration's near-flat Medicare rate proposal that hammered UNH and HUM. Revenue grew 11% in 2025 to $275B, and adjusted EPS grew 9% to $29.84. The company guided 2026 adjusted EPS of at least $30.25. CEO David Cordani retires July 1, 2026, succeeded by COO Brian Evanko — an orderly transition. PBM reform legislation (100% rebate pass-through, FTC-mandated cost-plus reimbursement, end of spread pricing) is the key overhang, but Cigna has proactively launched a rebate-free model at Express Scripts, positioning ahead of regulation. BofA named CI its top managed care pick. Bernstein upgraded to Outperform on March 12. The stock trades at 8x forward earnings — a deep value multiple for a company generating $8-9B in annual FCF.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — PBM model transition creates a clearing event.**

- **PBM reform already priced in:** The stock has de-rated from 15x+ earnings to 8x over the past 2 years as PBM reform fears mounted. At 8x forward, the market assumes meaningful earnings erosion that guidance contradicts.
- **Proactive model shift:** Express Scripts' rebate-free, cost-plus model launched in 2026 — Cigna is AHEAD of regulation, not reacting to it. If Q1 2026 results show Evernorth margins holding, it proves the transition works.
- **Medicare divestiture complete:** Sold to HCSC, removing the #1 source of political/regulatory risk in managed care. CI is now the "cleanest" large-cap managed care name.
- **Biosimilar wave:** Multi-year tailwind as patent cliffs hit blockbuster biologics. PBMs capture spread/admin fees on biosimilar substitution regardless of rebate model.

**Risk:** If PBM reform goes further (forced divestiture of pharmacy operations per Warren/Hawley bill), the thesis breaks. Current probability: low (~15%), as the bill faces industry lobbying and constitutional challenges.
