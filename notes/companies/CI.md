# The Cigna Group (CI)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Healthcare Plans (Managed Care / PBM)
**Price:** $265 | **Market Cap:** $71B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-19

## Situation Summary

Cigna is a diversified healthcare services company built on two pillars: Evernorth Health Services (pharmacy benefits/Express Scripts, specialty pharmacy, care services — ~80% of revenue) and Cigna Healthcare (medical insurance — ~20% of revenue). The company sold its Medicare Advantage business to HCSC in 2025, deliberately de-risking from the segment most exposed to the Trump administration's near-flat Medicare rate proposal that hammered UNH and HUM. Revenue grew 11% in 2025 to $275B, and adjusted EPS grew 9% to $29.84. The company guided 2026 adjusted EPS of at least $30.25. CEO David Cordani retires July 1, 2026, succeeded by COO Brian Evanko — an orderly transition. PBM reform legislation (100% rebate pass-through, FTC-mandated cost-plus reimbursement, end of spread pricing) is the key overhang, but Cigna has proactively launched a rebate-free model at Express Scripts, positioning ahead of regulation. BofA named CI its top managed care pick. Bernstein upgraded to Outperform on March 12. The stock trades at 8x forward earnings — a deep value multiple for a company generating $8-9B in annual FCF.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Cigna is a well-run managed care name but faces structural PBM headwinds from reform legislation. 24 analysts, consensus "strong buy" with $339 mean target (+28%). Short interest negligible at 2.6%. Market prices in PBM risk as a permanent margin headwind.
- **Our view:** The market is OVERWEIGHTING PBM reform risk for Cigna specifically. By divesting Medicare Advantage (the segment most hurt by rate freezes) and proactively adopting rebate-free/cost-plus models at Express Scripts, Cigna has positioned itself as the WINNER of PBM reform — not the victim. The new model trades lower gross margins for stickier, more transparent client relationships. At 8x forward earnings with $8B+ FCF and aggressive buybacks, the stock is priced as if earnings will decline — but guidance says they'll grow. The CEO transition is a non-event (Evanko has been running operations).
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (late April/early May) will be the first clean quarter showing how the rebate-free Express Scripts model impacts Evernorth margins. If Evernorth pre-tax income tracks toward the $6.9B+ guide, it proves the model transition works and the stock re-rates.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — PBM model transition creates a clearing event.**

- **PBM reform already priced in:** The stock has de-rated from 15x+ earnings to 8x over the past 2 years as PBM reform fears mounted. At 8x forward, the market assumes meaningful earnings erosion that guidance contradicts.
- **Proactive model shift:** Express Scripts' rebate-free, cost-plus model launched in 2026 — Cigna is AHEAD of regulation, not reacting to it. If Q1 2026 results show Evernorth margins holding, it proves the transition works.
- **Medicare divestiture complete:** Sold to HCSC, removing the #1 source of political/regulatory risk in managed care. CI is now the "cleanest" large-cap managed care name.
- **Biosimilar wave:** Multi-year tailwind as patent cliffs hit blockbuster biologics. PBMs capture spread/admin fees on biosimilar substitution regardless of rebate model.

**Risk:** If PBM reform goes further (forced divestiture of pharmacy operations per Warren/Hawley bill), the thesis breaks. Current probability: low (~15%), as the bill faces industry lobbying and constitutional challenges.

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

Cigna at 8x forward earnings is a mispriced managed care compounder. The market is treating all PBM-exposed names as equally vulnerable, but Cigna has proactively restructured (Medicare divestiture, rebate-free model, cost-plus pharmacy reimbursement) to be the WINNER of reform, not the victim. At 12% FCF yield with $5B+ annual buybacks, you're getting paid handsomely to wait for the re-rating. The V-shaped earnings recovery from the 2024 trough ($12.12 GAAP EPS -> $22.18) demonstrates operating leverage. Expected value of +18% with favorable risk/reward (66% bull vs -28% bear).

The key risk is legislative — if PBM forced divestiture passes, the thesis breaks. But at 8x earnings, significant bad news is already priced in. The biosimilar wave is a powerful multi-year tailwind that the market isn't crediting.
