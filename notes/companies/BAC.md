# Bank of America Corporation (BAC)

**Sector:** Financial Services | **Industry:** Banks - Diversified
**Price:** $46.83 | **Market Cap:** $342B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-19

## Situation Summary

Bank of America is the second-largest U.S. bank by assets ($3.4T), riding a multi-quarter NII recovery after the 2023 rate-shock trough. FY2025 was strong: revenue $113B (+7% YoY), net income $30.5B (+13%), EPS $3.81 (+19%). NII bottomed in Q1 2025 at $14.4B and has expanded sequentially every quarter since, reaching $15.75B in Q4 2025. Management guides 5-7% NII growth for 2026, with ~200bps of operating leverage.

The stock currently trades at $46.83, down 19% from its 52-week high of $57.55 (Dec 2025). The pullback is driven by three factors: (1) Berkshire Hathaway sold ~50% of its BAC stake (from 1.03B shares to ~515M) between mid-2024 and end-2025, with Buffett exiting at a premium to book; (2) tariff uncertainty and recession fears hitting bank stocks broadly in Q1 2026; (3) the stock trading below both its 50-day ($52.10) and 200-day ($50.65) moving averages, indicating a technical downtrend.

The Q1 2026 earnings report (expected April 15) will be the next major catalyst. Credit quality has been solid: net charge-off ratio 0.44% in Q4, provision for credit losses declining, and CET1 ratio at 11.4% (well above the 10% regulatory minimum).

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Moderate Buy. 18 analysts: 83% Buy/Strong Buy, 17% Hold, 0% Sell. Average PT $62.19 (33% upside). Market expects steady NII recovery, operating leverage, and capital returns (buybacks + dividends). Forward P/E of ~9.5x on $4.95 consensus EPS for 2026 is cheap for a mega-cap bank. The concern is macro uncertainty (tariffs, potential credit deterioration).

- **Our view:** The consensus is directionally right but underweights two things: (1) BAC's exceptional deposit franchise and scale moat make NII recovery more durable than peers -- they have 69M consumer customers and sticky low-cost deposits; (2) the Berkshire selling overhang is nearly complete (Buffett sold for valuation reasons at 1.4x+ book, not a quality call). At 1.22x P/B and 12.3x trailing P/E, the stock is cheaper than it was when Buffett was buying. The key risk the consensus may be underweighting is a tariff-induced recession scenario where credit losses spike and NII guidance gets pulled.

- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (~April 15). If NII comes in at $16B+ (confirming the growth trajectory) and credit remains benign, the stock re-rates toward $52-55. If credit deteriorates or NII disappoints, the stock tests $40 support.

## Inflection Point

**Moderate positive inflection in progress.** BAC is in the early-to-mid stages of an NII recovery cycle. Net interest income bottomed in Q1 2025 ($14.4B) and has grown sequentially every quarter since, reaching $15.75B in Q4 2025. This trend should continue through 2026 as the fixed-rate asset portfolio reprices higher and deposit costs stabilize.

The Berkshire selling overhang appears to be winding down -- Buffett has halved his position and retired. The stock has already absorbed most of that selling pressure. Once the overhang clears, the valuation discount should narrow.

The negative inflection risk is a tariff-induced recession. If unemployment rises significantly, credit losses would spike and NII guidance would be at risk. The market is pricing some of this risk at 9.5x forward P/E, but a full recession would push the stock to $35-38.

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: MEDIUM-HIGH

Bank of America at $46.83 represents a compelling risk/reward for a high-quality, wide-moat mega-cap bank. The investment case rests on three pillars:

1. **NII recovery is real and accelerating.** Four consecutive quarters of sequential NII growth, with management guiding 5-7% for FY2026. The fixed-rate asset repricing tailwind has years to run.

2. **Capital return is exceptional.** $24B in buybacks + $8B in dividends = ~$32B returned to shareholders in FY2025 (9.4% of market cap). Even if earnings flatten, the buyback machine drives mid-single-digit EPS growth.

3. **Valuation is attractive.** At 9.5x forward P/E and 1.22x P/B, you're paying below historical averages for an improving earnings trajectory. The Berkshire selling overhang is largely digested.

The main risk is a tariff-induced recession that spikes credit losses, but BAC's CET1 of 11.4% and diversified franchise provide resilience. The expected value of +15% with favorable asymmetry clears our hurdle rate.

**Entry strategy:** Buy a half position now at ~$47. Add the other half on either: (a) a dip to $42-43 (0.9% of 52-week range, ~11x P/B), or (b) confirmation of strong Q1 earnings in April. Size per Kelly criterion.
