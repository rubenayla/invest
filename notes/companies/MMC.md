## Situation Summary

Marsh McLennan is the world's largest insurance broker and risk advisor, recently rebranded simply as "Marsh" in January 2026. The company just reported Q1 2026 with 8% revenue growth ($7.6B) and 8% adjusted EPS growth ($3.29), but GAAP EPS missed due to a $425M Greensill litigation charge. The stock has pulled back ~26% from its 52-week high of $235.78 to ~$175, creating the most attractive valuation (17x forward PE) in years. The McGriff acquisition ($7.75B, closed Nov 2024) is fully integrated, and the Thrive efficiency program targets $400M in annualized savings over 3 years. Leadership is being reshuffled with CFO McGivney elevated to COO, signaling multi-year succession planning. The hard insurance market is softening (commercial rates -5% Q1), but organic growth remains solid at 4% underlying.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Quality compounder temporarily dinged by Greensill charge and softening insurance pricing. Analysts rate it Hold (14/21) with $203 mean target. BofA downgraded to Sell citing growth deceleration. Market fears: (1) insurance rate cycle turning negative, (2) McGriff integration drag on margins, (3) recession risk hitting consulting segments.
- **Our view:** The pullback overprices near-term headwinds. The Greensill charge is a one-time non-recurring event ($425M) now provisioned, removing overhang. The Thrive program ($400M savings) + McGriff revenue synergies are NOT yet in consensus numbers — the market is extrapolating rate softening into a growth story that is actually accelerating its efficiency transformation. At 17x forward PE, MMC trades at a historic discount to its 25-30x norm. The business model generates recurring fee income (not underwriting risk), so insurance rate direction matters less than volume/complexity of risk transfer. Additionally, 5.8% FCF yield on a 28% ROE compounder is anomalous.
- **Trigger:** Q2 2026 earnings (July) showing Thrive savings flowing to margins + McGriff revenue contribution above expectations. Greensill resolution removing litigation overhang entirely.

## Inflection Point

**Moderate inflection — valuation reset + efficiency program.** This is not a turnaround but a quality compounder hitting a temporary valuation dislocation. The Greensill charge created a GAAP EPS miss that spooked momentum holders. The Thrive program ($400M savings) represents a genuine step-change in operating leverage starting 2H 2026. The stock has not traded at 17x forward PE since the COVID crash. If Thrive delivers and organic growth sustains 4%+, the multiple should mean-revert toward 22-25x.

## Bull Case
- Thrive program delivers ahead of schedule, driving 200bp+ margin expansion by 2027
- McGriff cross-selling accelerates middle-market organic growth to 6-7%
- Hard-to-soft insurance cycle transition actually increases brokerage volumes as clients shop more
- Greensill resolution removes last litigation overhang
- Multiple re-rates from 17x to 22-25x (historical norm) as EPS growth reaccelerates

## Bear Case
- Insurance rate softening accelerates, organic growth declines to 2-3%
- Consulting segments (Mercer, Oliver Wyman) hit by corporate spending cuts in recession
- Thrive program restructuring costs ($500M) front-loaded with delayed savings
- Additional Greensill-related liabilities surface beyond the $425M provision
- Rising interest expense ($960M) from McGriff debt weighs on earnings growth

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

MMC is a best-in-class compounder trading at a historically cheap multiple (17x forward) due to transient factors (Greensill charge, insurance rate softening fears, tariff/recession overhang). The variant perception is real — the Thrive program and McGriff synergies are not fully reflected in consensus estimates, and the market is conflating insurance *underwriting* cycle risk with *brokerage* fee risk. However, without a clearer near-term catalyst and with macro uncertainty elevated, this is a WATCH rather than BUY. The risk/reward is attractive but not compelling enough for a high-conviction position.

**Would upgrade to BUY if:** (1) Stock dips below $165 (15x forward, 6.5%+ FCF yield), or (2) Q2 earnings confirm Thrive savings flowing to margins with maintained organic growth, or (3) Greensill litigation fully resolved. At $165, the margin of safety is sufficient for a quality compounder with 10%+ normalized total return potential.
