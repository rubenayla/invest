## Situation Summary

IREN is a former Bitcoin miner undergoing a radical transformation into an AI cloud infrastructure provider, anchored by a landmark $9.7B, 5-year contract with Microsoft to deploy NVIDIA GB300 GPUs at its Childress, Texas campus. The company reported Q2 FY26 revenue of $185M (missed $224M estimate) and a $155M net loss, driven by $219M in unrealized losses, $32M mining impairments, and $58M in SBC -- reflecting the messy middle of a capital-intensive pivot. The stock is trading at $41, down 46% from its 52-week high of $77, as the market digests execution risk, construction delays, and a massive revenue forecast cut (FY26/27 estimate slashed from $1.3B to $569M). Management guides to 140K GPUs deployed and $3.4B ARR by year-end 2026, with ~$2.3B already contracted, but credibility is being tested by delivery timelines.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Wall Street sees IREN as a high-potential but high-risk AI infrastructure play. 8 Buy / 2 Hold / 2 Sell ratings. Mean target ~$76, but Goldman at $39 (near current price) reflects skepticism about execution timeline. The revenue forecast cut from $1.3B to $569M spooked many. Market is pricing in significant execution/delay risk.
- **Our view:** The market is over-discounting construction delays while under-appreciating the contractual visibility. $9.7B Microsoft contract with 20% prepayment + $3.6B GPU financing at <6% covers ~95% of GPU capex. Unlike speculative AI plays, IREN has a named counterparty (Microsoft), contracted revenue ($2.3B ARR locked), and a clear delivery schedule. The key question is WHEN, not IF, the revenue arrives. Current price implies the market expects meaningful contract slippage or cancellation -- an overly pessimistic assumption given Microsoft's strategic need for GPU capacity.
- **Trigger:** Q3 FY26 earnings (May 13, 2026) showing material GPU deployment progress and AI Cloud revenue ramp. Sweetwater 1 energization in Q2 2026. Each milestone de-risks the thesis and narrows the gap between contracted and delivered revenue.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- IREN is at a major inflection.** The company is transitioning from a money-losing Bitcoin miner to a contracted AI infrastructure provider with $9.7B in Microsoft commitments. The inflection evidence is OBSERVABLE:

1. Revenue grew from $75M (FY23) to $501M (FY25) -- 6.6x in 2 years
2. First profitable year in FY25 ($87M net income)
3. $9.7B Microsoft contract signed (Nov 2025) -- the largest single contract in the company's history
4. GPU financing secured ($3.6B at <6%) -- eliminates the "how do they fund it?" concern
5. 810MW of operating data centers available

**However**, the inflection is still early-stage. Q2 FY26 revenue miss ($185M vs $224M expected) and the massive forecast cut ($1.3B to $569M) suggest the ramp is slower than initially expected. We are in the "messy middle" where the old business is being wound down but the new business hasn't fully ramped. This creates maximum uncertainty -- and potentially maximum opportunity.

## Bull Case

- GPU deployments accelerate in H2 2026, reaching or approaching the 140K target, and AI Cloud ARR tracks toward $3.4B
- Microsoft exercises expansion options beyond the initial $9.7B contract, validating IREN's platform
- Operating leverage kicks in as GPU fleet scales -- gross margins already at 68%, and incremental GPU deployments should be highly profitable
- 4.5GW power pipeline positions IREN as one of the largest independent AI infrastructure providers; additional hyperscaler contracts possible
- Renewable energy positioning becomes a competitive advantage as ESG requirements tighten for data centers

## Bear Case

- Construction delays and GPU supply chain issues cause significant slippage in the $3.4B ARR target -- revenue ramp takes 12-18 months longer than guided
- Microsoft renegotiates or slows drawdown of the $9.7B contract if AI spending moderates or excess capacity emerges
- Dilution risk: with $964M in debt, FCF deeply negative, and massive capex ahead, further equity raises are possible (shares already went from ~200M to 330M+)
- GPU-as-a-service commoditizes as Core Scientific, Cipher Mining, Applied Digital, and hyperscalers themselves build out capacity -- margin compression
- Bitcoin mining wind-down creates ongoing impairment charges and losses that obscure the AI transition economics

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

IREN has a compelling long-term thesis: $9.7B contracted revenue with Microsoft, funded GPU capex, massive power pipeline, and a first-mover advantage among Bitcoin-to-AI converts. The variant perception (market over-discounting delays while under-appreciating contractual certainty) has merit. However, the Q2 FY26 miss and the dramatic revenue forecast cut ($1.3B to $569M) signal that the ramp is genuinely slower than management initially guided. At $41, the stock is not cheap enough to compensate for the execution uncertainty -- forward P/E of 53x and P/S of 18x demand near-flawless execution.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** (1) Q3 FY26 earnings showing meaningful GPU deployment acceleration and AI Cloud revenue above estimates, (2) Sweetwater 1 energization on schedule, or (3) stock pullback to $30-35 range where risk/reward becomes asymmetric. Entry at $32-35 would offer 40-50% margin of safety vs. base case.

**Key monitoring:** May 13 earnings report is the next decision point. Watch for GPU deployment numbers, AI Cloud revenue run-rate, and any updates on construction timelines.
