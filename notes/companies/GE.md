# GE Aerospace (GE)

**Sector:** Industrials | **Industry:** Aerospace & Defense
**Price:** $304 | **Market Cap:** $321B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-16

## Situation Summary

GE Aerospace is riding the strongest aerospace cycle in decades. Q4 2025 results were exceptional: orders surged 74% YoY to $27B, backlog hit ~$190B, LEAP deliveries exceeded 1,800 units (record), and FY2025 revenue grew 21% to $42.3B with operating income up 28%. Management guided 2026 to $7.10-$7.40 EPS (mid-teens growth) and $9.85-$10.25B operating profit, hitting its 2028 targets two years early. The stock dropped ~7% post-earnings on "guidance deceleration" concerns (growth slowing from 38% EPS growth in 2025 to ~15% in 2026) and has pulled back 13% from its 52-week high of $345 to $304. The company just announced a $1B U.S. manufacturing investment to ramp LEAP production, expand MRO capacity, and strengthen defense output — partly a tariff mitigation play. Tariffs remain a $500M headwind but are being managed through pricing and cost cuts.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** GE Aerospace is a premium-quality compounder in a structural aerospace upcycle. Wall Street unanimously rates it Strong Buy with a ~$363 mean target (+19%). The market prices it at ~42x forward guided EPS, implying it expects double-digit earnings growth for many years. It is a consensus institutional long (81% institutional ownership) and a "must-own" industrials name.

- **Our view:** The consensus is largely correct on quality — this is a genuinely excellent business with a $190B backlog providing multi-year visibility. However, the market may be underestimating two risks: (1) The valuation already discounts near-perfect execution on a massive production ramp, leaving no margin of safety if supply chain issues (titanium, specialized labor) cause delivery slippage; (2) At 42x forward PE with decelerating growth (from 38% to 15%), any guidance miss could trigger a sharp de-rating. Conversely, the market may be underpricing the aftermarket/services moat — as the installed LEAP fleet grows, the recurring high-margin services revenue becomes an increasingly powerful annuity. The variant perception here is weak; this is a well-covered, well-understood story. The edge, if any, is in timing a better entry rather than discovering hidden value.

- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (late April) will be the first real test of whether 2026 guidance is achievable. LEAP delivery rates and services revenue growth will be the key metrics. Any tariff escalation or supply chain disruption (particularly titanium sourcing) could create a buy-the-dip opportunity.

## Inflection Point

GE's inflection already happened (2022-2024: spin-offs, transformation into pure aerospace). We are now in the **execution/harvesting phase**, not the inflection phase. The stock has already re-rated dramatically (up 67% over the past year, ~400%+ from 2022 lows). The remaining question is whether the aftermarket services ramp as the LEAP fleet ages represents a second-order inflection — higher-margin, recurring revenue that the market may still be underpricing on a 3-5 year view. But this is a consensus view, not a hidden insight.

**No new inflection point is evident.** This is a "continuation of excellence" story, not a turning-point story.

## Bull Case

- LEAP installed base grows to 10,000+ engines, creating a massive aftermarket annuity that drives margins above 25% operating
- Defense spending accelerates globally; GE's military engines (T901, XA100) win major programs
- Supply chain execution exceeds expectations; LEAP deliveries hit 2,000+ units in 2026
- Services revenue compounds at 15%+ for 5 years, justifying premium multiple sustainably
- Share buybacks at current FCF levels meaningfully reduce share count, boosting EPS growth

## Bear Case

- Supply chain disruptions (titanium, specialized castings, labor shortages) cause LEAP delivery misses — backlog is meaningless if you can't deliver
- Tariff escalation increases costs by $500M+ with limited ability to pass through to airlines already under margin pressure
- Valuation de-rating: at 42x forward PE, any miss triggers institutional selling; 5 turns of PE compression = -12% even with earnings growth
- Boeing/Airbus production delays reduce near-term engine delivery needs, creating a temporary revenue headwind
- RISE next-gen engine program ($10B+ development) absorbs FCF in late 2020s with uncertain returns
- Legacy liabilities (pensions, LTC insurance) resurface as a capital drain

The math is sobering. At $304, the risk/reward is roughly symmetric to slightly negative. The bull case requires everything to go right AND the market to pay an even higher multiple. The base case suggests the stock treads water as earnings growth gets offset by modest PE compression. The bear case has meaningful downside because the starting valuation leaves no cushion.

**Thesis breaks if:** LEAP deliveries miss targets for 2+ consecutive quarters, services revenue growth drops below 10%, or management lowers 2026 EPS guidance below $7.00.

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

GE Aerospace is an outstanding business — 21/25 quality score, dominant market position, massive backlog, improving margins, and strong management. However, outstanding businesses can still be mediocre investments at the wrong price. At $304 (42x guided forward EPS), the stock prices in near-perfect execution with no margin of safety. The expected value from the scenario table is approximately -3%, meaning risk/reward is unfavorable at current levels. There is no clear variant perception — the market understands the LEAP/services story well, and this is one of the most crowded institutional longs in industrials.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** A pullback to $250-$270 range (33-37x forward PE), which would provide a meaningful margin of safety and improve expected value to +10-15%. Alternatively, would upgrade if Q1 2026 results show services revenue accelerating beyond guidance (indicating the aftermarket ramp is faster than expected) even at current prices, as that would suggest the market is underpricing the annuity value. A broader market correction that drags GE down with beta (1.37) would be the most likely entry opportunity.

**If management raises 2026 guidance materially on Q1 earnings**, re-evaluate immediately — that would signal the growth deceleration narrative is wrong.
