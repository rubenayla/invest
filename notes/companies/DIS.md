# Walt Disney Company (DIS)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Entertainment
**Price:** $99.07 | **Market Cap:** $178B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-18

## Situation Summary

Disney just completed a CEO transition TODAY (March 18) — Josh D'Amaro, the architect of the $60B parks expansion, replaces Bob Iger. Q1 FY2026 (ended Dec 2025) was strong: revenue $26B (+5% YoY), parks hit a record $10B quarterly revenue, and streaming (Disney+/Hulu) posted $450M operating income (+72% YoY) — now firmly profitable. The company guides double-digit EPS growth for FY2026 with $19B in operating cash flow and $7B in buybacks. However, the stock has barely moved (+4.5% from our entry) as the market worries about tariff impacts on the $60B parks capex, linear TV erosion, and consumer spending fatigue. 2026 movie slate is blockbuster-heavy: Avengers: Doomsday, Toy Story 5, Moana live-action.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Disney is a turnaround that already happened — streaming is profitable, parks are strong, but growth is now mid-single-digits and the stock is fairly valued at 14x trailing PE. Analysts are bullish (93% buy/strong buy) with a $130 mean target (+31%), but the stock hasn't moved, suggesting the market disagrees or sees headwinds.

- **Our view:** The market is underappreciating three things: (1) The **streaming profitability inflection is early innings** — Q1 had $450M operating income, Q2 guides $500M, and the annual run-rate should reach $2.5-3B by FY2027 as content amortization stabilizes. This was a $4B annual loss segment just 2 years ago. (2) The **FCF improvement is dramatic** — from $1B (FY22) to $10B (FY25), a 10x increase in 3 years. At 5.7% FCF yield, Disney is cheaper than the S&P 500 on a cash-generation basis. (3) **New CEO D'Amaro is a parks operator**, not a content executive — he'll lean into the highest-ROIC segment (parks at 33% operating margin vs. 8% for streaming). The $7B buyback at these prices is highly accretive.

- **Trigger:** Q2 FY2026 earnings (~May 2026). If streaming operating income hits the guided $500M and parks maintain momentum despite tariff fears, the narrative shifts from "turnaround complete, now what?" to "compounding machine at 14x PE." Secondary trigger: box office performance of summer 2026 slate (Avengers: Doomsday June 2026).

## Inflection Point

**Yes — profitability inflection, not revenue inflection.** Evidence:

1. **Streaming crossed into sustainable profitability** — from -$4B annual loss (FY22) to ~$1.8B annual profit run-rate. Biggest P&L swing in Disney history.
2. **FCF 10x'd in 3 years** — $1B to $10B. Structural shift, not one-time.
3. **CEO transition to parks operator** — D'Amaro will lean into highest-ROIC business.
4. **$7B buyback at 14x PE** — management buying at historically low multiples.

The inflection is OBSERVABLE and underway. The question is whether it's already priced in.

## Bull Case

- Streaming operating income reaches $2.5-3B in FY2027 as content costs stabilize
- Parks maintain 7-10% revenue growth through pricing power and new attractions (cruise fleet doubling by 2031)
- 2026 movie slate delivers 3+ billion-dollar films (Avengers: Doomsday, Toy Story 5)
- D'Amaro accelerates capital returns — buyback expanded, dividend growth begins
- Stock re-rates from 14x to 18x forward PE on FCF durability recognition -> target $126-140

## Bear Case

- Tariffs inflate parks capex costs (steel, aluminum for $60B expansion), squeezing ROIC
- Consumer spending pullback hits park attendance and per-capita spending — recession risk
- Linear TV erosion accelerates (ESPN cord-cutting), more than offsetting streaming gains
- Movie slate underperforms (franchise fatigue)
- Competition from Netflix, Amazon, Apple forces higher content spend and margin compression

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: **LOW**

Disney is a decent company at a fair price, but the expected value is near zero. The profitability inflection is real and underway, but may already be reflected in the 14x forward PE. There's no strong variant perception — the market knows streaming is profitable and parks are strong. The tariff/consumer spending risks create a fat bear tail (-29%).

Compared to other holdings, DIS has the lowest expected value in the portfolio. SQM (+52%), TSLA (+75%), GOOG (+45%), MOH (+70%) all offer significantly more upside.

**DIS is the correct sell candidate to free capital for AVGO.**
