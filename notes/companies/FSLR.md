## Situation Summary

First Solar is the only vertically integrated, US-based thin-film solar manufacturer at scale, giving it a unique moat under IRA domestic content rules and tariff protections. The stock has pulled back ~30% from its 52-week high of $286 amid flat 2026 revenue guidance ($4.9B-$5.2B vs Street's $6B), policy uncertainty under Trump, and $125-135M in tariff charges. However, the 45X manufacturing tax credit survived the "One Big Beautiful Bill" largely intact with phase-out not starting until 2030, removing a key overhang. The company delivered strong 2025 results ($5.2B revenue, $14.21 EPS) and is turning FCF-positive ($1.2B in 2025) as the heavy capex cycle for US factory buildout winds down. Q1 2026 earnings are expected around April 23-28.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** First Solar is a good company in a bad policy environment. Flat 2026 guidance disappointed. Market worries about IRA rollback, permitting delays, and potential tariff volatility under Trump. Analysts say "Moderate Buy" but price targets have been cut.
- **Our view:** The market is over-discounting policy risk. The 45X credit is safe until 2030 (law signed July 2025). At 14x trailing / 8x forward earnings, the stock prices in significant earnings erosion that won't materialize. FSLR is the *beneficiary* of tariffs and domestic content requirements, not a victim. As the only US-made option qualifying for the 10% domestic content bonus, they have structural pricing power that actually *increases* under protectionist policy. The transition from heavy capex ($1.5B in 2024) to maintenance levels means FCF will explode in 2026-2027.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (late April) confirming bookings strength and margin resilience; continued tariff enforcement on SE Asian imports; new utility-scale contract announcements leveraging domestic content advantage.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- FCF inflection.** First Solar is transitioning from a heavy capex phase (building 4 US factories, spending $1.4-1.5B/yr in 2023-2024) to a mature cash generation phase. FCF went from -$785M (2023) to -$308M (2024) to +$1.19B (2025). With capex guided at $0.8-1.0B for 2026 and EBITDA at $2.6-2.8B, FCF should be $1.5-2.0B. This is a classic "capex cliff" inflection where the market still values the company based on the investment phase rather than the harvest phase.

Additionally, the South Carolina finishing facility (opening H2 2026) will reduce freight costs and increase domestic content qualification, potentially improving margins further.

## Bull Case

- Forward P/E of 8x is absurdly cheap for a company with $2B+ annual FCF generation, net cash balance sheet, and policy moat
- 45X credits ($2.1-2.2B annually) are locked in until 2030, providing massive margin support
- Tariffs on Chinese/SE Asian imports continue tightening, strengthening FSLR's pricing power
- Utility-scale solar demand accelerates as AI data centers drive massive power needs
- Capex cycle ending means FCF could reach $2B+ by 2027, supporting aggressive buybacks or dividends

## Bear Case

- 2026 guidance is genuinely flat -- revenue $4.9-5.2B vs $5.2B in 2025; growth stalls
- Permitting delays under Trump administration slow utility-scale project starts
- 45X credit phase-out starts 2030 -- market begins discounting this by 2027-2028
- Competition from Chinese manufacturers intensifies if tariff enforcement weakens
- Solar demand cyclicality -- if power prices drop or natural gas stays cheap, project economics weaken
- $125-135M tariff charges on imported components eat into margins
