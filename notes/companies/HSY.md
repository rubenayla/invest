# The Hershey Company (HSY)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Confectioners (chocolate + salty snacks)
**Price:** $183.20 (2026-06-04) | **Market Cap:** ~$37B (~203M shares: ~150M Common + ~53M Class B)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-04
**Verdict:** BUY (starter / scale-in) — down ~24% from its high while a record cocoa-cost spike *halved* 2025 earnings; with cocoa now collapsed to ~$3,100–4,100/t (from a ~$12,000 peak) and management reaffirming a +30–35% adjusted-EPS recovery for 2026, you are buying a dominant US confection franchise at the low end of its multiple on *normalized* earnings. Conviction MEDIUM — the recovery is largely consensus, so the edge is in the durability of low cocoa into 2027, not in a hidden surprise.

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Hershey is the #1 US chocolate maker (~45% category share) — Reese's, Hershey's, Kisses, Kit Kat (US license), Jolly Rancher, Twizzlers — plus a salty-snacks arm built by acquisition (SkinnyPop, Dot's Homestyle Pretzels, Pirate's Booty). North America Confectionery is the profit engine (~80%+ of operating income); International and Salty Snacks are smaller.

**Why customers choose them (the moat — wide-ish).** Iconic brands, ~85% of revenue from the US where shelf position, scale, and a century of brand equity are extremely hard to dislodge. Reese's alone is the best-selling US candy brand. Pricing power is real: Hershey has pushed list prices repeatedly through the inflation cycle and held volume reasonably well. Control is anchored by the **Hershey Trust**, which holds the super-voting Class B stock — this entrenches long-term stability (and has historically blocked takeovers, e.g. the 2016 Mondelez approach).

**What went wrong (the reason it's cheap).** A single input — **cocoa** — broke the P&L. Cocoa futures spiked to a record ~$12,000/tonne in 2024 on West African crop failures, roughly 3–4x normal. As Hershey's hedges rolled to those prices through 2025, **gross margin collapsed from ~47% to ~34%** and **net income fell from $2.22B (2024) to $883M (2025)** — GAAP EPS roughly halved (~$10.9 → ~$4.4). The stock de-rated from ~$239 to the $160s. Secondary worries piled on: GLP-1 weight-loss drugs as a long-term threat to confection demand, price-elasticity pushback from stretched consumers, a new CEO (Kirk Tanner, ex-Wendy's, started 2024), and tariffs.

**The reset.** Cocoa has since crashed — consecutive surplus seasons and soft demand pushed ICE NY cocoa to ~$3,100–4,100/t in 2026, *below* even the $5,500–7,000 the market projected at the start of the year. As high-cost hedged inventory clears, margins inflect. Management's FY2026 guide (reaffirmed at Q1, reported April 30 2026): **+4–5% net sales, ~400bps gross-margin recovery, +30–35% adjusted EPS (+79–89% reported), with double-digit EPS growth weighted to H2** as costs normalize.

## Situation Summary

HSY sits at **$183.20, ~24% below its 52-week high of $239** and ~14% above the 52-week low of $160. The setup is the textbook cyclical-cost trough:

- **Revenue grew** through the crisis ($11.2B 2024 → $11.7B 2025) — this was never a demand collapse, it was a cost shock.
- **Earnings were the casualty:** net income $2.22B → $883M; gross margin 47% → 34%.
- **2026 is the inflection:** Q1'26 came in strong and management reaffirmed the full-year recovery; the margin turn begins Q2 as cheap cocoa flows through COGS.
- TTM GAAP EPS ~$5.38; **forward EPS ~$9.92**, i.e. the market already pencils in most of the snap-back → **fwd P/E ~18.5x**.

So the debate is precise: *is the cocoa relief durable enough to deliver a clean ~$10 earnings year in 2026–2027, and does a wide-moat staple deserve to re-rate back toward its historical 22–25x once it does?*

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** The recovery is known and already priced — fwd P/E 18.5x isn't a screaming bargain, GLP-1 is a structural demand overhang, volumes are soft on elasticity, debt is elevated (net debt ~$4.8B), and the payout ratio is temporarily >100% on depressed earnings. A fair-value staple, not a bargain.
- **Our view:** The market is anchoring on the 2026 recovery but under-weighting **2027 — the first *full clean year* at structurally lower cocoa**, where EPS can exceed the 2024 ~$11 peak. At $183 you are paying ~18.5x 2026 GAAP / ~23x 2026 *adjusted* for a franchise that historically commands 22–26x, with a safe-on-normalized-earnings 3.15% dividend (15+ years of raises) and beta ~0.07 (genuine portfolio ballast). The GLP-1 fear is real but slow-moving and already discounted; confection has shown more resilience than the bear case assumed.
- **Trigger:** Each 2026 quarterly print that confirms the **~400bps gross-margin recovery and double-digit H2 EPS** rebuilds the normalized-earnings case and argues for re-rating toward 22–24x on ~$10–11 EPS. The mirror risk that breaks it: cocoa re-spikes, or volumes crack mid-single-digits as price elasticity / GLP-1 finally bite.

## Entry / Exit Discipline

- **Entry:** $183 is a reasonable *starter*. Prefer to scale in on weakness toward **$165–175** (closer to the 52-week low), where the risk/reward improves to ~3:1.
- **Thesis-break (price):** a sustained break below **~$155** (under the 52-week low of $160) on deteriorating fundamentals signals the recovery is failing — re-underwrite.
- **Thesis-break (fundamental):** if the guided **~400bps FY26 gross-margin recovery does not show up in the Q2/Q3 2026 prints**, or volumes decline mid-single-digits on elasticity, the normalized-EPS thesis is wrong regardless of price.
- **What would change my mind (bullish):** cocoa holding <$4,000/t through 2027 + stable volumes → 2027 EPS clears the 2024 peak and the stock re-rates to 23–25x.

## Risks

- **Cocoa re-spike** — the entire thesis hinges on input normalization; West African weather/disease could reverse it.
- **GLP-1 / secular confection demand** — slow-moving structural headwind to "indulgence" categories.
- **Price elasticity** — repeated list-price hikes may finally erode volume.
- **Leverage + payout** — net debt ~$4.8B and a >100% payout on trough earnings leave less cushion if recovery slips (covered comfortably on normalized EPS).
- **CEO transition execution** — Kirk Tanner is still relatively new; the salty-snacks strategy is unproven at scale.
