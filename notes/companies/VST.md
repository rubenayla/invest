# Vistra Corp (VST)

**Sector:** Utilities | **Industry:** Independent Power Producers
**Price:** $164.33 | **Market Cap:** $55.7B | **EV:** $77.5B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-18

## Situation Summary

Vistra is the largest competitive power generator in the US, operating ~41 GW of capacity including a 6.4 GW nuclear fleet across Texas (Comanche Peak) and PJM (Ohio, Pennsylvania). The stock is trading at $164, down ~25% from its $220 all-time high in late 2025, after a Q4 2025 EPS miss ($2.13 vs $2.33 expected). However, the fundamental story is stronger than ever: Vistra signed landmark 20-year nuclear PPAs with both Meta (~2,600 MW) and AWS (~1,200 MW), totaling ~3,800 MW of contracted nuclear capacity for AI data center power. The company also announced the $4.7B Cogentrix acquisition adding ~5,500 MW of modern gas generation. FY2025 adjusted EBITDA hit a record $5.91B, and 2026 guidance of $6.8-7.6B EBITDA (ex-Cogentrix) implies ~22% year-over-year growth. The stock has hedged 100% of 2026 generation volumes, providing near-term earnings visibility.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** VST is a "picks and shovels" AI power play, but the pullback from $220 reflects concern that (1) the trailing P/E looks absurd at 75x due to 2025's depressed net income from non-cash items, (2) the Cogentrix deal adds $4.7B of debt to an already leveraged balance sheet, and (3) nuclear uprates/relicensing carry execution risk. Analysts still rate it Strong Buy with a $234 mean target but near-term momentum is negative.
- **Our view:** The market is anchored on backward-looking metrics (trailing P/E, 2025 net income) while ignoring the massive forward earnings inflection. On 2026 guidance midpoint, VST trades at 10.8x EV/EBITDA and ~14x forward P/E — cheap for a company with 20-year contracted nuclear revenue from hyperscalers, 100% hedged near-term volumes, and a credible path to $7+ billion EBITDA. The nuclear PPAs are not fully reflected in the price because delivery ramps through 2034. The Cogentrix deal, while adding leverage, adds dispatchable gas capacity into a power market with structurally rising demand.
- **Trigger:** (1) Cogentrix deal close (mid-to-late 2026) proving accretive, (2) first nuclear PPA deliveries starting late 2026, (3) Q1/Q2 2026 earnings beating guidance and demonstrating the EBITDA ramp is real.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — VST is at a demand-driven inflection.** The hyperscaler nuclear PPAs represent a structural shift: for the first time, nuclear plants have long-term contracted revenue from creditworthy counterparties (Meta, Amazon), eliminating merchant price risk on ~3,800 MW. This is happening as AI/data center power demand creates a structural supply-demand imbalance in US electricity markets. The Cogentrix acquisition doubles down on dispatchable capacity at exactly the right point in the cycle. The inflection has clearly begun — the PPAs are signed, delivery starts late 2026, and 2026 EBITDA guidance confirms the ramp.

## Bull Case

- 2026 EBITDA comes in at high end of guidance ($7.6B) as power prices remain elevated and nuclear PPAs begin contributing
- Cogentrix closes accretively and adds $500M+ EBITDA; leverage declines faster than expected
- Additional hyperscaler PPA announcements (Google, Microsoft) for remaining uncontracted nuclear/gas capacity
- Nuclear uprates and license extensions proceed on schedule, adding 500+ MW of zero-cost capacity
- Power market tightens further as coal retirements outpace new renewable/gas builds, supporting higher merchant prices

## Bear Case

- Cogentrix integration stumbles or power market softens, making the $4.7B price look too high
- AI/data center buildout slows (DeepSeek-style efficiency gains reduce power demand growth)
- Nuclear uprate/relicense faces NRC delays or cost overruns
- Rising interest rates pressure the leveraged balance sheet (D/E already 3.9x, will increase with Cogentrix)
- Regulatory intervention in power markets (price caps, co-location restrictions like the 2024 FERC order) limits upside
