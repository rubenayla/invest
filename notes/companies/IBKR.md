# Interactive Brokers Group (IBKR)

**Sector:** Financial Services | **Industry:** Capital Markets
**Price:** $67.69 | **Market Cap:** $115B (all classes) | **Class A Market Cap:** ~$30B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

Interactive Brokers is riding a secular wave of global retail/institutional trading growth, having added 1M+ net new accounts in 2025 (to 4.4M total, +32% YoY) and crossing $6B net revenue for the first time. The stock joined the S&P 500 in 2025 and hit an all-time high of $79.18 in February 2026 before pulling back ~14% to $67.69. The company is pursuing a bank charter via OCC (expected by end of 2026), expanding crypto in Europe, and investing in AI features. Despite falling interest rates, IBKR grew net interest income 20% YoY thanks to growing client equity ($780B, +37% YoY). Market narrative: consensus bull, "best-in-class compounder" — 8/9 analysts rate it Buy with mean target $80.56.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** IBKR is a high-quality compounder with a dominant platform, but the easy gains from the rate cycle are over. Valued fairly at 25-30x forward earnings. Growth will moderate as account additions normalize.
- **Our view:** The market underappreciates three things: (1) the bank charter is a transformational event that unlocks mutual fund/ETF custody — a massive new revenue stream that competes with Schwab/Fidelity on their home turf, (2) IBKR's 77% pre-tax margin and automation moat mean incremental revenue drops almost entirely to the bottom line — operating leverage is extreme, and (3) international expansion (Brazil, Taiwan, UAE, crypto Europe) opens TAM that domestic-focused competitors cannot access. The RIM model's known anti-growth bias (noted in memory) means the 1 bearish model out of 11 is likely the RIM systematically undervaluing this asset-light business.
- **Trigger:** Bank charter approval (expected H2 2026) + Q1 2026 earnings on April 21 showing continued 25%+ account growth and DARTs momentum.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — bank charter is a potential inflection.** If approved (expected H2 2026), IBKR can custody mutual funds and ETFs, opening a multi-trillion dollar market currently dominated by Schwab, Fidelity, and State Street. This transforms IBKR from "best electronic broker" to "full-service financial infrastructure."

Secondary inflection: crypto expansion in Europe going live in Q1 2026, plus ForecastX prediction exchange gaining traction, adds new revenue streams that don't exist in current numbers.

**Evidence of inflection beginning:** Application filed with OCC, CEO publicly stated approval expected by year-end. Account growth accelerating (32% YoY). Client equity growing faster (37% YoY) than accounts, meaning higher-value clients are joining.

## Bull Case

- Bank charter approved in H2 2026, unlocking mutual fund/ETF custody and attracting billions in new AUM
- Account growth sustains 25%+ as international expansion (Brazil, Taiwan, UAE, crypto Europe) opens new markets
- Operating leverage drives EPS growth of 30%+ as incremental revenue drops to bottom line at 77% margins
- Market re-rates from 25x to 30-35x forward PE as bank charter transforms the business model
- DARTs continue growing (+27% YoY in Jan 2026) as global trading activity remains elevated

## Bear Case

- Interest rate cuts accelerate, compressing net interest income (currently ~50% of net revenue) faster than commission growth can offset
- Bank charter denied or significantly delayed beyond 2026, removing key catalyst
- Account growth decelerates as competition from Schwab, Robinhood, and international platforms intensifies
- Thomas Peterffy succession risk — he is 81 years old; while Milan Galik is a capable CEO, Peterffy's strategic vision is irreplaceable
- Regulatory risk: increased capital requirements or trading restrictions could pressure margins
