# Humana Inc. (HUM)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Managed Care (Medicare Advantage-centric health insurer)
**Price:** $364.46 (2026-06-11) | **Market Cap:** $43.8B | **Enterprise Value:** $36.2B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-11
**Standalone rating: PASS (lean) — beaten-down name that has already round-tripped; recovery is priced in and then some**

## Situation Summary
Humana is the most Medicare-Advantage-concentrated of the big managed-care insurers, and it is in the second year of a self-described "Medicare Advantage reset." Adjusted EPS collapsed from **~$22 (2022) → $20 (2023) → ~$10 (2024) → ~$9.8 (2025)** as elevated senior medical-cost (utilization) trend, the **v28 risk-model phase-in** (which lowered risk-adjusted revenue), and a **Star Ratings disaster** hit at once. Revenue kept *growing* ($93B → $130B) the whole time — this is a **margin collapse, not a demand collapse**. Profit margin is at a literal trough of **0.8%**, ROE 6.3% (vs a normal ~15-20%).

The Star Ratings problem is the single biggest swing factor. Only ~25% of Humana's MA members are in 4-star-or-higher plans for Bonus Year 2026 (vs the ~94% the company had historically), a **~$3.5B revenue/bonus headwind**. Humana sued CMS to vacate the 2025 Star Ratings; on **2026-10-14 the court rejected the challenge**, so the BY2026 hit is locked in. FY2026 GAAP EPS guidance is "**at least $8.89**" (~$9.00 adjusted) — i.e. management does not expect 2026 to be the recovery year either.

The recovery is a **2027-2028 story**: management targets *doubling* individual-MA margin in 2026 (normalized for Stars), v28's final phase-in year is 2026 (headwind then rolls off), and improved Star Ratings for Bonus Year 2027 would restore much of the lost bonus revenue. Sell-side models that, with **FY2027 EPS consensus of ~$15.71 (+78% y/y)**.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus / bull view:** "Trough earnings, self-help margin recovery, v28 headwind ending, Stars bonus comes back in 2027 → EPS doubles to ~$16 and normalizes toward $20+. A low-beta (0.77) franchise insurer at 23x *trough-recovery* earnings is a recovery compounder."
- **Our view:** **The stock has already priced that recovery, and the market is now ahead of its own analysts.** HUM has more than **doubled off its $163 52-week low to $364 (near the $369 52-wk high)** while reported numbers are still at trough. At $364 you are paying **~41x FY2026E ($8.84)** and **~23x FY2027E ($15.71)** — and the FY2027 number is a *forecast of a contingent recovery*, not earnings in hand. The tell: the **sell-side mean target is $296 — ~19% BELOW the current price** (range $195–$441, 24 analysts, "Hold"). When a beaten-down name trades a fifth above the consensus target on trough margins, the easy money has been made and you are underwriting execution at full price.
- **Trigger:** 2026-07-29 Q2 earnings (note the **+1 quarter EPS estimate is slightly negative, −$0.21**, a deliberately weak print), and the autumn 2026 Star Ratings release that sets Bonus Year 2028 — the next binary on whether the margin recovery compounds or stalls.

## Valuation — normalized earnings, not the trough

- Pre-crisis normalized EPS power was **~$20-22 (2022-23)**. Even haircutting for permanently lower MA margins (V28 reset, tighter CMS, membership pruning), a reasonable **normalized EPS is ~$16-18** — essentially what FY2027 consensus already assumes.
- Managed care historically trades **~15-18x** forward (UNH-era premium multiples are gone post-2024 sector de-rating). On normalized **$16-17 × 16-17x ≈ $260-290** — right at the analyst mean target ($296), and **~20% below today's $364.**
- To justify $364 you need normalized EPS to reach the high teens *and* hold an 18-20x multiple — i.e. full pre-crisis recovery *plus* a premium re-rate, with no further Stars or utilization setback. That is the bull scenario priced as the base case.

This is the textbook signature of a recovery story that has **already re-rated past fair value before the recovery shows up in the numbers**.

## Bull vs Bear
**Bull (real, but priced):** #1 pure-play Medicare Advantage franchise riding a secular demographic tailwind (10,000 Americans age into Medicare daily); revenue never stopped growing; v28's final phase-in is 2026 so that headwind ends; management is explicitly executing margin recovery (exiting unprofitable plans/markets, repricing 2026 bids) and targets doubling individual-MA margin; CenterWell (pharmacy + primary care) adds a growing care-delivery leg. If Stars and utilization both cooperate, EPS doubles and the stock can hold $360+.

**Bear (the case to respect):** Every leg of the recovery is **contingent and partly out of management's control** — Star Ratings are CMS-scored (and HUM *lost* its lawsuit), medical-cost trend across the whole MA industry has surprised to the high side for two straight years, and CMS rate/risk-model policy keeps tightening. The FY2027 EPS range ($11.57–$20.00) is enormous precisely because the path is unknowable. Meanwhile the stock already doubled off the lows and trades ~20% **above** the analyst mean target. You are paying a full multiple for a forecast, with a fat downside tail if any one leg slips.

## Entry / Exit
- **Entry of interest:** **~$260-290** (≈16-17x normalized ~$16-17, at/below consensus target) — where the contingent recovery is no longer fully priced and the risk/reward turns favorable. **Not at $364.**
- **Thesis-break / avoid:** medical-cost trend re-accelerating in 2026 quarterlies; the autumn 2026 Star Ratings release showing the Bonus-Year-2028 bonus pool failing to recover; 2026 EPS guidance cut below ~$8.50; or individual-MA margin failing to inflect toward the "doubling" target.
- **What would change our mind to the upside:** a clean Star Ratings recovery confirmed for BY2027/2028 *plus* MA medical-loss-ratio normalizing back toward management's targets — at which point the path to $18-20 EPS becomes credible and a pullback toward $290 would be a genuine BUY LOW.
