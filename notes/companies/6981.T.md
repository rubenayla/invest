# Murata Manufacturing Co., Ltd. (6981.T)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Electronic Components (MLCC)
**Price:** ¥4,813 (2026-04-23) | **Market Cap:** ¥8,761B (~$55B)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-23

## Situation Summary

Murata is the global #1 MLCC (multilayer ceramic capacitor) maker with ~40% world share, plus RF modules, sensors, and acoustic wave filters. FY2026 Q3 (nine months to Dec 2025) showed bifurcated performance: server/AI-driven MLCC revenue up +10.1% YoY, automotive +4.1%, but smartphone/RF modules down -12.0% due to iPhone RF volume declines and pricing pressure. A ¥43.8B goodwill impairment on the SAW (surface acoustic wave) filter business hit operating profit hard — operating profit fell -13.3% despite revenue growing +2.9%. Full-year FY2026 guidance was revised: sales up to ¥1.80T but operating profit cut to ¥270B. The bright spot: book-to-bill hit 1.12x in Q3 with orders up +11% QoQ, signaling a genuine MLCC demand inflection led by AI servers. The SAW impairment is a one-time item obscuring an underlying recovery.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Market sees Murata as a structurally declining smartphone RF play, penalized by Apple's in-housing of RF components and ongoing MLCC cyclicality. Trailing PE of 47x on compressed earnings looks expensive. Analyst mean target ¥4,532 is actually *below* current price — consensus is mild sell/hold.
- **Our view:** The SAW impairment is masking an MLCC cycle turn driven by AI server demand that is structurally larger and stickier than smartphone demand. Server MLCC content per unit is 10-30x a smartphone. Murata's ¥565B net cash position (~6.5% of market cap) provides buyback/dividend optionality. Book-to-bill at 1.12x is the observable inflection signal the market hasn't fully priced.
- **Trigger:** FY2026 full-year results (May 2026) and FY2027 guidance issuance — if AI server MLCC growth offsets smartphone RF weakness, earnings revision cycle turns positive and forward PE compresses toward 20-25x on higher earnings.

## Inflection Point

**YES — observable, but early stage.** The inflection signal is:
1. Book-to-bill 1.12x in Q3 FY2026 (vs 1.0x in Q2) — demand outstripping supply
2. Orders +11% QoQ in Q3 — leading indicator for revenue 1-2 quarters ahead
3. Server MLCC revenue +10.1% YoY for nine months — the AI content cycle is real
4. FCF recovering: ¥80B trough in FY23 → ¥253B FY24 → ¥259B FY25

The thesis requires FY2027 guidance (issued May 2026) to show AI/server driven earnings acceleration offsetting continued smartphone RF weakness. Do not buy ahead of that confirmation — risk of a guidance disappointment is real if Apple accelerates RF in-housing.

## Bull Case
- AI server MLCC demand grows 30%+ annually through FY28; Murata captures disproportionate share due to high-layer-count capability
- Book-to-bill sustains above 1.1x for 3+ quarters, driving ASP recovery and 500-700bps op margin expansion back toward 20%+
- Net cash deployed in buybacks (management has ¥565B to work with), boosting EPS and ROE above cost of capital
- Yen depreciation tailwind: ~50% of revenue is overseas, USD/JPY at 150+ amplifies JPY-reported earnings
- **Risk NOT in headlines (from primary source):** Management disclosed that multilayer resin substrate revenue (used in high-end smartphones) declined — this is an Apple design-win dependency risk that goes beyond the headline "RF modules" story. If Apple shifts resin substrate suppliers, it would cut a second revenue stream that analysts aren't tracking separately.

## Bear Case
- Apple continues in-housing RF/filter components (already costing Murata smartphone revenue -12% YoY); accelerates to Murata's high-frequency module business
- AI server MLCC demand proves one-time (data center CapEx pause) and smartphone recovery doesn't materialize, leaving Murata in a prolonged trough
- Yen appreciation (USD/JPY back to 130) crimps overseas earnings on translation
- Management fails to deploy ¥565B cash productively — continues to accumulate dead balance sheet weight, depressing ROE
- China MLCC producers (Yageo, Samsung Electro-Mechanics, domestic Chinese) gain share in mid-tier applications, forcing further ASP declines
