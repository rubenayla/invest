# AAR Corp. (AIR)

**Sector:** Industrials | **Industry:** Aerospace & Defense
**Price:** $118.51 (2026-04-17) | **Market Cap:** $4.7B | **EV:** $5.6B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-17

## Situation Summary

AAR Corp is the largest independent MRO provider in North America, riding a powerful aerospace aftermarket cycle driven by Boeing/Airbus delivery delays and aging fleet dynamics. Q3 FY2026 (Feb 2026) was a blowout: $845M revenue (+25% YoY, 14% organic), adj EPS $1.25 beating consensus by $0.09, and adjusted operating margin expanding to 10.2%. Management raised FY2026 guidance to ~19% total sales growth. The company is integrating two significant acquisitions (HAECO Americas for $78M and ADI American Distributors), both expected margin-accretive by late 2026. A $450M U.S. Air Force pallet contract adds defense optionality. However, the stock has run 136% off its 52-week low and now trades at 24x FY2026E earnings -- the "under-followed small-cap" thesis is fading as the stock approaches mid-cap status with consensus "Strong Buy" ratings.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** MRO supercycle is well-understood. Street expects ~15-16% EPS growth into FY2027. Six analysts cover with Strong Buy consensus, mean target $131 (10% upside). The market is pricing in continued execution on acquisitions and organic growth. Short interest is low (3.3%) -- nobody is betting against this.
- **Our view:** The MRO supercycle thesis is mostly priced in at 24x current-year earnings and 21x forward. The real question is whether AAR can compound at 15%+ EPS CAGR through FY2028-2029 as HAECO/ADI fully integrate and the government segment accelerates. The $450M USAF contract and 55% organic government sales growth are underappreciated -- defense aftermarket is a multi-year tailwind largely independent of commercial aviation cycles, providing earnings diversification. Also, FCF is poised to inflect: TTM FCF of only $36M (0.8% yield) is suppressed by heavy working capital investment in inventory (+$109M YoY). As revenue growth moderates and inventory normalizes in FY2027-2028, FCF should converge toward $150-200M, dramatically changing the valuation picture.
- **Trigger:** May 12, 2026 Investor Day in NYC -- management will present long-term financial targets. If they guide to sustained 10%+ adj operating margin and meaningful FCF inflection, the stock re-rates. Also Q4 FY2026 results (July 2026).

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- FCF inflection.** AAR is at the transition from "acquisition-fueled growth with negative FCF" to "integrated platform generating substantial free cash flow." Evidence:
- Q3 FY2026 FCF was $41.7M (strongest quarter in 2+ years)
- HAECO integration ahead of schedule, workforce adjustments complete
- Management expects Q3 to be "margin low point" with sequential improvement
- Inventory build should moderate as organic growth decelerates from 14% to 6-8%
- Interest expense likely to decline as revolver paid down

If FCF normalizes to $150-200M (operating income ~$280M less $80M capex, $50M interest, $50M taxes), that's a 3-4% FCF yield vs current 0.8%. That would be the re-rating catalyst.

## Bull Case
- HAECO and ADI fully integrated by late 2026, driving adj operating margins to 11-12%
- FCF inflects to $150M+ in FY2027, enabling share buybacks and debt paydown
- Government services becomes a material growth engine ($450M USAF contract + rising defense budgets)
- MRO supercycle extends through 2028-2029 as Boeing/Airbus delivery backlog remains massive
- Investor Day May 12 reveals ambitious long-term targets, attracting new institutional buyers

## Bear Case
- MRO demand normalizes faster than expected if Boeing/Airbus ramp production successfully
- Integration risk: HAECO/ADI margins don't reach AAR levels, diluting returns on invested capital
- Interest expense remains elevated if rates stay high, capping EPS growth despite strong operating performance
- CEO selling 86K shares post-earnings could signal insider view that stock is fairly valued
- Valuation already reflects consensus growth: 24x earnings for a low-margin services business is not cheap
- Working capital could remain a persistent cash drain as business scales

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

AAR Corp is a genuinely good business riding real secular tailwinds (aging fleet, MRO supercycle, rising defense budgets). The government segment growth is underappreciated and the FCF inflection story is compelling. However, at 24x current-year earnings with only 6 analysts giving Strong Buy and the stock already up 35% in 3 months, the easy money has been made. Expected value of +9.3% does not compensate for execution risk on integrations and high interest costs. The MRO supercycle thesis is increasingly consensus, not contrarian.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** (1) Pullback to $100-105 range (18-19x FY2027E EPS), which would boost expected value to 20%+, OR (2) Investor Day on May 12 reveals FY2028 targets materially above current consensus (adj EPS $7+, FCF $200M+), providing conviction on the next leg of earnings growth, OR (3) Material debt paydown that reduces interest expense burden.
