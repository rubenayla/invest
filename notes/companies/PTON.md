# Peloton Interactive Inc. (PTON)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Leisure / Connected Fitness
**Price:** $3.84 | **Market Cap:** $1.63B | **EV:** $2.40B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

Peloton is deep in a turnaround after its post-COVID collapse, trading near its all-time low at $3.84 (52-week range $3.65-$9.20). Q2 FY2026 (Feb 5) was a mixed bag: revenue missed at $657M (-3% YoY) with subscriber churn rising to 1.9%, but adjusted EBITDA beat at $81M (+39% YoY) and FCF guidance was raised. CFO Liz Coddington departed effective March 27, 2026; no replacement named yet. Sarah Robb O'Hagan was appointed Chief Content & Member Development Officer on March 17. The Peloton Commercial Series (announced March 16) is the first gym-grade connected equipment leveraging the Precor acquisition, shipping late 2026 in six countries -- the stock spiked 7% on the announcement but has since given it back. The term loan prepayment penalty expires May 2026, enabling a potential refinancing that could save ~$29M/year in interest. Revenue guidance was cut by $71M for the year to $2.40-2.44B while EBITDA guidance was raised to $450-500M -- classic "shrinking to profitability."

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** PTON is a shrinking business with a broken growth story. Revenue declining, subscribers churning, and the at-home fitness market is permanently smaller post-COVID. Short interest at 16.7% reflects significant bearish conviction. Analysts have a Buy consensus but have been cutting targets aggressively (Goldman $12.50 to $7, JPM $9 to $6).

- **Our view:** The market is over-anchoring on the top-line decline and missing that (1) PTON's revenue is now roughly at its pre-COVID trendline (~$2.4-2.5B), meaning the "decline" is actually normalization, not structural death; (2) the profitability transformation is real -- from -$2.8B net income in FY2022 to approaching breakeven, with FY2025 FCF of $324M; (3) at 5.3x EV/Adj EBITDA the stock prices in terminal decline, but the Commercial Series + Precor's 60-country distribution could reopen a growth channel into the $16B (growing to $36B by 2033) commercial fitness market; and (4) the term loan refi catalyst in May/June 2026 is underappreciated as a margin expansion event.

- **Trigger:** Two near-term catalysts: (1) Term loan refinancing announcement (May/June 2026) -- if achieved at SOFR+300bps, saves ~$29M/year and signals credit market confidence; (2) Q3 FY2026 earnings (May 13) -- management guided for revenue inflection toward YoY growth in back half of FY2026. Any quarter showing flat-to-positive revenue would force a re-rating. The Commercial Series shipping in late 2026 is a medium-term catalyst.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- early-stage profitability inflection, but revenue inflection not yet confirmed.**

Evidence the inflection has begun:
- First operating profit ever in FY2025 (+$62M vs -$406M prior year)
- First profitable quarter (Q2 FY2025)
- FCF swung from -$86M to +$324M in one year
- Adj EBITDA: ~$4M (FY2024) -> ~$475M (FY2026E) -- extraordinary operating leverage
- Net leverage dropping rapidly (was 14x, now 1.6x)
- Commercial Business Unit growing 10% YoY

Evidence the inflection is NOT yet complete:
- Revenue still declining (-3% YoY)
- Subscribers still churning (1.9% monthly, up from 1.4%)
- Revenue guidance lowered by $71M
- Hardware refresh (Cross Training) flopped with existing users
- No insider buying

**Assessment:** This is a turnaround at the profit inflection point -- costs have been fixed but revenue hasn't stabilized. The classic "shrinking to profitability" pattern. The key question is whether the Commercial Series and Peloton IQ can stabilize/grow the top line. Current price likely reflects the market's bet that they can't.

## Bull Case

- **Revenue stabilizes in H2 FY2026 as guided**, with Commercial Series and Peloton IQ driving growth from FY2027. Revenue returns to pre-COVID trendline growth of 6-8%.
- **Term loan refinancing in May/June 2026** saves $29M/year in interest, immediately flowing to bottom line. Signals credit market confidence.
- **Commercial expansion via Precor's 60-country network** opens a $16B+ TAM. 10% YoY commercial growth accelerates as Commercial Series ships in late 2026.
- **Short squeeze potential:** 16.7% short interest + improving fundamentals + catalyst proximity = forced covering drives stock higher.
- **EV/EBITDA re-rates from 5.3x to 8-10x** as the market prices in "stable" rather than "declining" -- stock doubles to $8-10.

## Bear Case

- **Revenue decline accelerates** as subscription churn continues. $44/month is too expensive vs gym memberships ($25/mo) and Apple Fitness+ ($10/mo). At-home fitness market structurally smaller post-COVID.
- **CFO departure signals deeper problems.** New CFO search takes too long, execution stumbles during transition.
- **Commercial Series fails to gain traction.** Peloton's cost structure (15.7% G&A, 23.2% S&M) is 2x too high for commercial equipment margins. Technogym/Life Fitness are entrenched.
- **Debt burden becomes unmanageable** if EBITDA erodes. $120M/year interest on shrinking revenue = cash drain. Refinancing terms disappoint.
- **No insider buying** despite stock near all-time lows. Management doesn't believe in their own turnaround enough to put money in.

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: **MEDIUM**

The risk/reward is favorable at $3.84 with an expected value of +34%. PTON trades at 5.1x EV/Adj EBITDA for a business generating $324M in FCF (19.9% yield) with two near-term catalysts: term loan refinancing (May/June) and Q3 earnings showing potential revenue inflection (May 13). The variant perception is that the market prices in permanent decline, but revenue is roughly at the pre-COVID trendline and the Commercial Series opens a large new growth channel. The turnaround is real on the cost side (EBITDA went from $4M to ~$475M in two years), and the question is solely about revenue stabilization. At sub-$4, you're getting paid to wait for the answer. CFO departure is a headwind but manageable -- the cost playbook is already in motion.

**Entry:** Current price ($3.84) is attractive -- only 5% above the 52-week low of $3.65. Scale in now, add at $3.50 if support breaks. Add aggressively below $3.00 (3x EBITDA with cash covering most debt).
