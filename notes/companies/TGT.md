## What happened to the price

$230 (Nov 2021 COVID-discretionary peak) → $137 (2y ago) → $96 (1y ago) → **$82 low** → $128 today. A ~44% drawdown from the peak, but the bottom was set during the 2025 trough (DEI-rollback consumer boycott + tariff fear + discretionary recession) and the stock has already rallied a third off it. This is **not** a fresh dislocation — it is a recovery in progress that you are arriving at mid-move.

## The fresh fact that changes the stale narrative (verify this — it's the whole thesis)

For three years Target was a textbook melting retailer: revenue $109B (FY23) → $107.4B → $106.6B → **$104.8B (FY25)**; EPS $8.94 → $8.86 → **$8.13**; operating income $5.7B → $5.1B. Share lost to Walmart and Amazon, discretionary mix punished.

**Q1 FY2026 (quarter ended 2026-05-02, reported 2026-05-20) broke the trend** — verified across the SEC 8-K, TradingView, StockTitan and the earnings-call transcript:
- **Comparable sales +5.6%**, driven by **traffic +4.4%** (not just ticket — bodies through the door) and +1.1% average transaction.
- Net sales **+6.7% to $25.4B**.
- **Gross margin 29.0%**, up 80bps from 28.2%.
- GAAP/Adjusted EPS **$1.71 vs $1.30** prior-year adjusted (+31.6%), beating ~$1.41 consensus by ~17%.
- Digital comps +8.9%; same-day delivery +27% (Target Circle 360); Roundel ad business non-merch revenue +24.6%.
- **Guidance raised**: FY26 net sales +4% (was +2%), EPS near the high end of $7.50–$8.50.

That is a genuine, broad-based inflection — the kind a "declining retailer" screen would miss entirely. New CEO Michael Fiddelke's "clarified strategy" is, on one quarter, working.

## The bear case (lead with this — be skeptical of one quarter)

1. **Easy comp, flattering the print.** Q1 FY26's +5.6% laps a deeply depressed Q1 FY25 (comps were negative, EPS $1.30 during the boycott/tariff trough). The two-year stack is only mildly positive. One quarter off a bombed-out base is not a trend — it is what every dead-cat bounce looks like in month one.
2. **Guidance is quietly conservative.** "EPS near the high end of $7.50–$8.50" → ~$8.30–$8.50, i.e. roughly **flat-to-+5% vs FY25's $8.13**. Management is not guiding to a growth ramp; they are guiding to stabilization. A +5.6% comp quarter that produces ~0–5% full-year EPS growth tells you the comparisons get much harder from Q2.
3. **Structural share loss is unresolved.** Walmart (grocery-anchored, trades 37x) and Amazon keep taking general-merchandise share; Target's ~40%+ discretionary mix is the most cyclically and secularly exposed slice of US retail. One good quarter doesn't reverse a decade of relative decline.
4. **Margins are structurally thin and tariff-exposed.** 4.5% operating margin (vs 6%+ pre-2022); import-heavy private-label sourcing leaves Target squarely in the tariff blast radius. The Q1 gross-margin gain can reverse fast if it has to absorb tariffs or discount to hold the new traffic.
5. **You already missed the cheap entry.** At $82 (10x, ~5.5% yield) this was a fat pitch. At $128 (15.4x, 3.6% yield) it is fairly priced for the modest growth being guided.

## The bull case (what would have to go right)

1. **Cheapest large-cap general retailer.** ~15x forward vs Walmart ~37x, Costco ~50x, Dollar General ~18x. If the turnaround merely holds, a re-rate to 17–18x on $8.50 EPS = $145–153 — and you collect a covered 3.6% Dividend-King yield while you wait.
2. **Traffic, not just ticket, is inflecting.** +4.4% traffic is the hardest metric to fake and the leading indicator that the "clarified strategy" (assortment, value, same-day delivery) is pulling guests back. If it persists into Q2/Q3, the multiple should follow.
3. **High-margin flywheels growing fast.** Roundel (retail-media ads, +24.6%) and Target Circle 360 membership are structurally higher-margin than merchandise; mix-shift here lifts the through-cycle margin ceiling.
4. **54-year Dividend King with covered payout.** ~$2.1B paid vs $2.8B FCF; the dividend pays you to wait for confirmation and is unlikely to be cut.

## Levels

- **Entry (BUY LOW zone):** ~$108–112 — ~13x forward, ~4.2% yield. That price pays you for the risk that one quarter was a head-fake and gives real margin of safety.
- **Thesis-break / sell:** Q2 or Q3 FY26 comps turn negative again, or full-year EPS guided below $7.50 — confirms the bounce was an easy-comp artifact. Structural break below ~$95 on deteriorating fundamentals.
- **What flips me bullish:** a **second** consecutive quarter of positive comps *and* positive traffic (Q2 FY26, reported ~Aug 2026) with gross margin held ≥29%. That converts "one easy comp" into "the turnaround is real" and would justify chasing toward $145+.

## Bottom line

The useful, non-obvious finding: **Target genuinely inflected positive in Q1 FY26** after three years of decline — traffic up 4.4%, margins up, guidance raised — which the lazy "dying retailer" narrative completely misses. But the market already knows: +33% off the low, 15x forward, ~flat EPS guided. The fat-pitch entry was $82. At $128 it is a **WATCH** — own the confirmation (a second positive quarter) or own the pullback (~$110), not the bounce.
