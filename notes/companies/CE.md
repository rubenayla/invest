## Situation Summary
CE is a classic post-peak, over-levered turnaround. After paying $11B for DuPont's M&M business at the top of the 2022 cycle, it got hit by a synchronized acetyls + auto/industrial downturn and is now carrying ~$12.9B of debt against a collapsed EBITDA base. Q4 2025 missed badly ($0.67 vs $0.89 consensus) and included a $1.1B goodwill impairment on Engineered Materials; FY2025 GAAP net loss was ‑$1.17B on $9.54B revenue. Both Moody's (Ba1) and S&P (BB) have now cut CE to junk. Management cut the dividend 95%, closed Lanaken, sold Micromax for $500M (Feb 2026, proceeds to debt), and guided Q1 2026 EPS to $0.70–$0.85. The stock has ripped +44% in the last 90 days on hopes of cyclical bottom and deleveraging traction, now sitting near a 52‑week high ($68.65).

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Deleveraging is on track — junk rating is fully priced, acetyls will rebound in 2H26/2027, $2B net debt reduction by YE27 gets CE back to IG." Sell-side mean target $68.6 (≈6% upside), rating "Hold". Mizuho at $58 Neutral.
- **Our view:** **No clear variant perception.** The bull case (cycle bottom + deleveraging) is the consensus case after a 44% run. The bear case (acetyls stays weak, covenant stress, equity becomes residual claimant) is also well-known. Sell-side target is effectively at today's price. Without a differentiated edge on either acetyl chain margins or divestiture valuations, this is a coin-flip on cycle timing, not an informational advantage.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (mid-May). Either confirms the Q1 guide (which is already below consensus) or shows further deterioration. Neither outcome gives a structural re-rating — the stock already rallied 44% on the setup.

## Verdict

**PASS** — Conviction: MEDIUM

No variant perception. The deleveraging narrative is consensus, already +44% priced into the stock, and the sell-side price target ($68.6) is essentially at today's price. GBM +53-67% signal is real but reflects mean-reversion optimism, not an edge — the DCFs correctly flag that if acetyls stays weak, debt service eats equity. Business quality (9/25) is in red-flag territory, balance sheet risk is the dominant variable, and near-term catalyst (Q1 earnings) is guided BELOW consensus into an overbought technical position. Expected value of +6.6% is not worth the left-tail risk (‑46% bear case) on a junk-credit, cycle-exposed equity.

**Dominant risk:** Balance sheet, unambiguously. $11.7B net debt vs $1.2B cash vs $110-230M segment adj-EBIT run-rate on Acetyl Chain means any refi disruption or covenant bump cascades. Deleveraging is progressing but is not yet "on track" — it's on probation, dependent on both asset-sale execution AND cycle cooperation.

**Would upgrade on:** (1) acetyls pricing confirmed to inflect in Q2/Q3 2026 data, AND (2) second $1B divestiture announced at ≥10x EBITDA, AND (3) price back to <$50 offering real margin of safety. Absent all three, no edge.
