# Starbucks (SBUX)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Restaurants
**Price:** $105.11 (2026-07-15) | **Market Cap:** $119.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-07-16

## Situation Summary
Brian Niccol left Chipotle to run Starbucks in September 2024 and launched the "Back to Starbucks" plan — menu simplification, faster service, restored barista staffing hours, technology, and a ~$500M investment in workforce and store upgrades. FY2025 (ended Sept 2025) was the cost of that reset: GAAP diluted EPS collapsed from $3.31 to $1.63 (−51%), operating income fell from $5.11B to $3.58B (−30%), and net income halved to $1.86B — all while revenue grew only ~2.8% to $37.2B. The market looked through the earnings trough: the stock recovered ~37% off its ~$77 low to $105, near its 52-week high ($107).

The turnaround is now showing real traction. **Q2 FY2026 (ended Mar 29, 2026): global comparable sales +6.2%, US comps +7.1% (second straight positive quarter), revenue +9% to $9.5B — and the growth is transaction-led (+3.8% comparable transactions), the healthiest kind.** Management raised FY2026 guidance: global/US same-store sales to at least +5% (from +3%), and adjusted EPS to $2.25–$2.45 (from $2.15–$2.40). Separately, Starbucks sold a 60% stake in its China business to Boyu Capital (closed start of fiscal Q3 2026), deconsolidating a market that had been dragging results (China Q2 comps just +0.5%, discount-driven).

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "The turnaround is working — buy the momentum." Street recommendation is Buy, mean target ~$106 (i.e. no upside from spot). Bulls point to two straight quarters of positive, transaction-led US comps, raised guidance, and China risk offloaded.
- **Our view:** *Right story, wrong price.* The recovery is genuine but it is already in the stock. At $105 you pay **~44x the midpoint of FY2026 adjusted EPS ($2.35)** and **~35x FY2027 consensus (~$3.00)** — a premium multiple for a business whose durable growth is now mid-single-digit US comps plus margin recovery. The China stake sale cuts both ways: it removes a drag *and* gives up the consolidated long-term growth story that historically justified a 28–33x multiple; SBUX keeps 40% of the JV (licensing/royalty economics) but is now structurally more of a mature US + licensing model. The dividend ($2.44/yr) exceeds current GAAP EPS — a >100% payout ratio only covered because FCF ($2.44B FY25) still runs ahead of GAAP net income. Equity is negative (−$8.1B), a structural artifact of years of debt-funded buybacks, not a solvency issue but a reminder there's no book-value floor.
- **Trigger for a re-look:** a pullback to the low-$80s (≈27x FY27 EPS with turnaround runway intact), OR evidence the margin recovery is running ahead of plan (op margin back toward 15%+ faster than guided). At $105 the risk/reward is unattractive — you need the bull case just to earn a normal equity return.

## Valuation — Scenario Framework
No GBM/DCF model runs exist in the DB (first note on this name), so this is a scenario-based fair value on a ~2.5-year horizon (to ~FY2028), anchored on adjusted EPS recovery × exit multiple, plus ~$6 cumulative dividends.

| Scenario | Prob | FY28 adj EPS | Exit P/E | Price | +Divs | Total vs $105 |
|----------|------|--------------|----------|-------|-------|---------------|
| Bear — momentum fades, comps flatten, margin recovery stalls | 30% | ~$2.75 | 22x | $60 | $66 | **−37%** |
| Base — mid-single comps hold, op margin recovers to ~15% | 45% | ~$3.50 | 26x | $91 | $97 | **−8%** |
| Bull — full execution, margins toward FY23 peak | 25% | ~$4.20 | 30x | $126 | $132 | **+26%** |

**Probability-weighted expected value ≈ −8%.** The base case is roughly flat-to-negative; only the bull case earns an equity return. That is the signature of a good company at a full price.

## Bull / Bear
**Bull:**
- US comps positive two straight quarters, transaction-led — the turnaround has inflected, not just stabilized.
- Niccol has a proven QSR turnaround record (Chipotle, Taco Bell); "Back to Starbucks" is coherent and measurable.
- China drag deconsolidated; SBUX retains 40% upside via the JV with none of the operating headache.
- Earnings troughed in FY25; operating leverage on recovering comps could snap EPS back to $3.50–$4.00 by FY27–28.
- Elite brand, pricing power, high ROIC franchise model, reliable dividend growth history.

**Bear:**
- Valuation fully reflects the recovery: 44x FY26 adj / 35x FY27 for a mid-single-digit grower.
- The US comp reacceleration is partly a "sugar high" from restored staffing hours and promotions — margin-dilutive, and its durability past the easy comparisons is unproven.
- China sale removes the long-term growth optionality that justified SBUX's historical premium; the remaining business is a mature, saturated US market.
- Dividend exceeds GAAP EPS and ~all of FCF; limited room to fund both dividend growth and store investment without leaning on the balance sheet (already $24B debt, negative equity).
- Any single quarter of comp deceleration compresses a 40x multiple violently.

## What Would Change My Mind
- **More bullish:** a pullback to low-$80s; or FY26 op margin recovering meaningfully ahead of the guided path; or FY27 US comps holding +4–5% into tougher comparisons (proves durability, not a sugar high).
- **More bearish (thesis-break):** US comps turn negative again in any FY26–27 quarter, or margin recovery reverses — either invalidates the entire "EPS recovers to $3+" premise the multiple rests on.

## Rating
**WATCH** — the turnaround is real and executing, but the stock has fully caught up to it. Negative probability-weighted EV (≈ −8%) at $105; entry only attractive in the low-$80s. Good company, wrong price. This is not a bargain and does not clear the "act now" bar.
