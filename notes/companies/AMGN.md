## Situation Summary

Amgen is one of the world's largest independent biotech companies, with a diversified portfolio spanning osteoporosis (Prolia, EVENITY), cardiovascular (Repatha), inflammation (Enbrel, Otezla, TEZSPIRE), oncology (BLINCYTO, KYPROLIS, IMDELLTRA), and rare diseases (TEPEZZA, KRYSTEXXA, UPLIZNA). FY2025 revenue came in at $36.75B (+10% YoY), beating estimates, with Q4 2025 revenue of $9.87B (vs $9.47B consensus) and EPS of $5.29 (vs $4.73 expected). However, Amgen is at an inflection point defined by two opposing forces:

**Headwind — Patent cliff:** Prolia and Xgeva (combined ~$6B+ in FY25 sales) face imminent biosimilar erosion in 2026. CVS is already swapping to biosimilars starting April 2026. Enbrel revenue declined 48% in Q4 2025 alone. Otezla took a $1.2B impairment due to IRA price setting. Management guides 2026 revenue of $37B-$38.4B, implying only ~1-3% growth — a sharp deceleration from 10% in 2025.

**Tailwind — Growth portfolio + MariTide:** Repatha (+36% FY25), TEZSPIRE (+52% FY25), EVENITY, and the Horizon rare disease portfolio (Tepezza, Krystexxa) are growing strongly. MariTide, a once-monthly GLP-1/GIP obesity drug, showed up to 20% weight loss in Phase 2 and is now in six Phase 3 trials (MARITIME program) with readouts expected early 2027. If successful, MariTide could be a multi-billion-dollar franchise. The stock also has a significant FCF catalyst: consensus expects FCF to jump from $8.1B (2025) to ~$13.4B (2026) as one-time Horizon integration and R&D charges roll off.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Amgen is a defensive large-cap biotech trading at ~15x forward P/E with a 2.9% dividend yield. The bull case is MariTide optionality and growth product momentum, but the bear case is significant: Prolia/Xgeva biosimilar erosion ($6B+ at risk), massive debt from the Horizon acquisition ($55B), and MariTide Phase 2 data that "disappointed Wall Street" with high discontinuation rates and vomiting concerns. Analysts are split: 13 Buy, 17 Hold, 3 Sell, median target $351.50 — essentially flat from current price.
- **Our view:** The market is fairly pricing the base case but may be underweighting two things: (1) the FCF inflection from $8.1B to $13.4B in 2026 as one-time costs roll off, which improves the deleveraging story dramatically; and (2) MariTide's differentiated monthly dosing could carve out a meaningful share of the $100B+ obesity market even if it's not best-in-class on weight loss (20% vs 22-25% for Lilly/Novo next-gen). The key risk is that the patent cliff could be worse than guided if biosimilar uptake is faster, and MariTide Phase 3 failure would remove the main growth catalyst.
- **Trigger:** (1) MariTide Phase 3 readouts (early 2027) — binary event that could move the stock 20%+; (2) 2026 FCF delivery proving the $13B+ thesis; (3) Prolia/Xgeva biosimilar erosion trajectory in H1 2026 — faster than guided = bearish.

## Inflection Point

**Mixed — patent cliff headwind meets growth portfolio crossover.** Amgen is at the trough of its patent cliff cycle: Enbrel is in terminal decline (-48% Q4), Prolia/Xgeva biosimilar erosion begins in earnest in 2026, and Otezla was impaired. The growth portfolio (Repatha, EVENITY, TEZSPIRE, rare disease) is scaling but not yet large enough to fully offset the declines. The true inflection is MariTide-dependent: if Phase 3 succeeds (readouts early 2027) and launches by 2028-2029, Amgen transitions from a declining-growth pharma to a high-growth obesity/cardiometabolic platform. The FCF inflection ($8.1B to $13.4B in 2026) is a near-term positive that could accelerate deleveraging and re-rate the stock.

## Bull Case

- MariTide Phase 3 succeeds with clean safety profile and 18-20% weight loss on monthly dosing — differentiated convenience drives $5B+ peak sales
- FCF inflection to $13B+ in 2026 accelerates deleveraging; debt drops below $45B by 2028
- Growth products (Repatha, EVENITY, TEZSPIRE) continue 20-50% growth, more than offsetting biosimilar erosion
- Prolia/Xgeva erosion slower than feared (biosimilar adoption in biologics historically slower than small molecules)
- Additional MariTide indications (ASCVD, HF, sleep apnea) expand addressable market to $200B+
- Forward P/E expands from 15x to 18-20x on obesity pipeline de-risking

## Bear Case

- MariTide Phase 3 fails or shows inadequate weight loss vs Lilly/Novo next-gen (retatrutide, amycretin achieving 25%+)
- Prolia/Xgeva biosimilar erosion faster than guided — CVS swap sets precedent for other PBMs
- Debt load constrains strategic flexibility; rising rates increase interest expense on $55B debt
- IRA drug price negotiation hits more products (Enbrel, Otezla already impacted)
- Revenue stagnates at $37-38B for 2-3 years as patent cliff and growth portfolio roughly cancel out
- Trump drug pricing framework introduces new uncertainty for pharma margins

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: **MEDIUM**

Amgen is a high-quality defensive biotech at a fair price with a big binary catalyst ahead. The 15x forward P/E is reasonable but not cheap enough to provide a margin of safety given the patent cliff headwinds and execution risk. The expected value of +3.4% is below our hurdle rate for a new position. The real question is MariTide — if Phase 3 succeeds (early 2027 readouts), the stock likely re-rates to $450-500 on obesity optionality. If it fails, the stock drops to $270-280 as the growth narrative collapses.

The right play is to **wait for one of two entry signals:**
1. **MariTide Phase 3 readout (early 2027):** If data is positive, buy the dip on any "sell the news" reaction. The obesity market is $100B+ and monthly dosing is differentiated.
2. **Price dip to $290-310 range:** At 12-13x forward P/E, you get paid for the patent cliff risk and MariTide is free optionality.

The FCF inflection to $13B+ in 2026 is genuinely interesting and supports the deleveraging thesis, but it's a one-time catch-up from Horizon integration costs rolling off, not a structural improvement. The 2.9% dividend provides some downside protection.
