# Kering SA (KER.PA)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Luxury Goods
**Price:** EUR 232.60 | **Market Cap:** EUR 28.5B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

Kering is in the early stages of a sweeping turnaround under new CEO Luca de Meo (ex-Renault), who joined in late 2025. Gucci, which drives ~50% of group revenue, has seen sales decline 22% in 2025 after a botched creative transition under Sabato de Sarno. New creative director Demna (ex-Balenciaga) debuted his first Gucci collection in late February 2026 to mixed-but-positive reviews (Bernstein: 7.6/10 -- "not a home run but an improvement"). The group sold its beauty division to L'Oreal for EUR 4B (closing H1 2026) to deleverage and refocus. Q4 2025 comparable sales fell 3% group-wide but beat expectations, sparking a 12-14% stock pop in February. Capital Markets Day on April 16 will present the full strategic roadmap. The stock trades at EUR 232.60, down ~34% from its 52-week high of EUR 354 and up ~55% from the 52-week low of EUR 150.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Kering is a broken luxury franchise in early-stage recovery. Analysts are predominantly Hold (12 hold, 4 buy, 7 sell out of 23). The market believes Gucci's turnaround will take 2-3 years with uncertain outcome, and that Kering's elevated leverage and compressed margins make it a high-risk bet vs. peers like LVMH or Hermes. Consensus target ~EUR 302 implies ~30% upside but with wide dispersion (EUR 190-400).

- **Our view:** The market is pricing Kering as if the turnaround has a low probability of success, but three structural changes are undervalued: (1) the L'Oreal beauty sale cuts net debt/EBITDA from 2.2x to ~1.1x, giving meaningful financial flexibility; (2) Demna's appointment brings genuine creative firepower -- his track record at Balenciaga (revenue 5x under his tenure) is the strongest creative CV in luxury; (3) FCF yield of 8% provides a margin of safety even if the turnaround is slow. At forward PE of 22x on trough earnings, you're not paying for recovery -- you're paying for "more of the same." If Gucci margins recover even halfway to 2022 levels, this reprices significantly.

- **Trigger:** Capital Markets Day on April 16, 2026 is the near-term catalyst -- management will present brand strategies, financial targets, and organizational changes. Secondary catalyst: Demna's first full season of Gucci products hitting stores in H2 2026 will show whether commercial traction follows the creative reset.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- early-stage turnaround with observable evidence.** Three inflection signals:

1. **Management change:** De Meo as CEO brings turnaround expertise (Renault precedent). First CEO from outside the fashion world signals willingness to rethink operations.
2. **Creative reset at Gucci:** Demna's appointment is the single most important hire in luxury. His first collection received positive-enough reviews (7.6/10) to sustain the narrative. Commercial products won't hit stores until H2 2026 -- the real test.
3. **Balance sheet deleveraging:** L'Oreal beauty sale (EUR 4B, closing H1 2026) transforms leverage from stretched to comfortable, freeing investment capacity.

**Timing:** We are past the absolute bottom (stock up from EUR 150 low) but still early in the turnaround. Q4 2025 results showed deceleration in decline (Gucci -10% vs. -25% earlier quarters). The inflection has begun but is not yet confirmed by revenue growth.

## Bull Case

- Demna reignites Gucci's creative identity, driving mid-single-digit organic growth by 2027 (his Balenciaga track record supports this)
- Operating margins recover to 20%+ within 3 years as revenue grows against a right-sized cost base (operating leverage kicks in)
- Beauty sale proceeds + FCF fund store network refresh and marketing reinvestment without adding debt
- China luxury demand recovers in H2 2026, providing a macro tailwind
- Capital Markets Day (April 16) delivers credible medium-term targets, triggering re-rating

## Bear Case

- Demna's aesthetic doesn't translate to Gucci's customer base -- he's known for avant-garde streetwear, Gucci needs broad commercial appeal
- China luxury market stays depressed through 2026-2027 as structural spending shift persists
- YSL and Bottega Veneta can't compensate for continued Gucci weakness
- Interest expense burden becomes problematic if rates stay high and EBITDA doesn't recover
- Fashion turnarounds historically have low success rates -- Burberry took 5+ years to stabilize under new creative direction

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

Kering offers asymmetric risk/reward at current levels. The 8% FCF yield provides a floor, trusted valuation models (GBM, autoresearch) unanimously see 28-179% upside, and the stock is uncrowded with near-term catalysts (CMD April 16). The variant perception is clear: the market is pricing Gucci's turnaround as unlikely, but the combination of Demna's proven creative track record + de Meo's operational turnaround experience + balance sheet deleveraging creates a credible path to re-rating. The key risk is that fashion turnarounds are inherently uncertain -- hence medium rather than high conviction.

**Entry:** Scale in starting at EUR 230-240 (current levels), add on weakness toward EUR 200 or on CMD confirmation.
**Position size:** Moderate (3-5% of portfolio) given turnaround uncertainty.
**Thesis-break:** Exit if Gucci comparable sales haven't inflected positive by Q1 2027, or if net debt/EBITDA exceeds 3x.
