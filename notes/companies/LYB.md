# LyondellBasell Industries (LYB)

**Sector:** Basic Materials | **Industry:** Chemicals (Commodity — Olefins & Polyolefins, Intermediates, Refining)
**Price:** $65.15 (2026-06-11) | **Market Cap:** $21.0B | **Enterprise Value:** $32.9B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-11
**Standalone rating: WATCH (value-trap risk; lean PASS at this price)**

## Situation Summary
LYB is one of the world's largest commodity polyolefin (polyethylene / polypropylene) producers, caught in the deepest, longest petrochemical down-cycle in decades. After earning $11.81 EPS in 2022, the chain has compressed every year since — $6.46 (2023) → $4.15 (2024) → **‑$2.34 (2025, a GAAP net loss of ‑$745M)**. Revenue has fallen from $50.5B (2022) to $30.2B (2025). The proximate cause is a global oversupply of ethylene/PE driven by a wave of new Chinese and Middle-Eastern capacity that has structurally depressed integrated polyolefin margins, plus weak European demand.

Management has responded like a company that expects an extended trough: in early 2026 it **cut the quarterly dividend ~50%, from $1.37 to $0.69** (first cut in LYB's history as a public company), and it has launched a "Strategic Assessment" of its European Olefins & Polyolefins assets (likely divestiture/closure). The dividend cut is the single most important fact here — it confirms management does not see near-term cash generation covering the old payout.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Trough is in; 2026-27 is a cyclical recovery. Forward EPS ~$7.5, so at $65 you're paying 8.6x recovery earnings for a low-beta (0.33) cash machine with a now-sustainable ~4.2% dividend." Sell-side mean target **$79.88** (range $63–98, 17 analysts), rating **Hold**.
- **Our view:** The "8.6x" is on an *analyst-hoped recovery year*, not normalized power. On a China-haircut **mid-cycle EPS of ~$6**, LYB trades at ~11x and roughly its own normalized fair value — it is **not cheap**, it is *fairly priced for a structurally lower mid-cycle*. The cut dividend confirms the trough is not a quarter-or-two event. There is no informational edge: both the cycle-recovery bull case and the China-overcapacity bear case are widely known, and the sell-side target sits only ~23% above a price that already reflects the cut.
- **Trigger:** Outcome of the European O&P strategic review and 2026 polyolefin spread direction. Neither is likely to produce a structural re-rating from here; a value-destructive European exit is a real downside catalyst.

## Valuation — mid-cycle, not single-year

Commodity-chemical DCF/forward-P-E on trough or recovery-year numbers is unreliable; normalized earnings power is the right frame.

- Full-cycle EPS (2017–2024, excl. 2021 super-peak and 2025 trough) averaged ~$8. **Haircut ~25% for structurally lower China-era polyolefin margins → normalized ~$6.**
- Commodity chemicals with leverage and an overcapacity overhang deserve ~9–10x. **$6 × 9.5 ≈ $57 fair value.** Even the analyst recovery EPS of $7.53 × 9 ≈ $68.
- So fair value spans roughly **$54–68 (mid ~$61)** vs $65 today → fairly valued to slightly rich on normalized power; only "cheap" if you fully credit a 2026 snap-back to $7.5 EPS.

That is a mediocre risk-adjusted profile — the textbook signature of a deep-cyclical trading *near* fair value rather than *below* it.

## Bull vs Bear
**Bull:** World #1-2 polyolefin scale, US-Gulf-Coast cost advantage (cheap natgas/ethane feedstock), low beta 0.33, P/B 2.1x, reset dividend now ~4.2% and likely covered, huge operating leverage to any cycle upturn, management actively pruning weak European assets. If the cycle turns, $80+ is reachable.

**Bear (the case to respect):** Chinese + Middle-East ethylene/PE capacity has likely *structurally* lowered mid-cycle margins — "normalized" may be permanently below the 2017-19 baseline, so the recovery the consensus prices may never fully arrive. Net debt $9.5B at 4.3x trough EBITDA constrains the balance sheet; the dividend cut signals management agrees the trough is durable. A European O&P divestiture could crystallize losses. This is the classic value-trap setup: optically cheap on recovery earnings, fairly-to-richly valued on a structurally lower mid-cycle.

## Entry / Exit
- **Entry of interest:** ~$50 (≈1.5x book, ~7-8x normalized, ~5.5% reset yield) — where the cyclical option is cheap enough to pay for the structural risk. **Not at $65.**
- **Thesis-break (avoid / exit):** a *second* dividend cut; net-debt/EBITDA breaching ~5x with no spread inflection; a European O&P exit at a clearly value-destructive price; or polyolefin spreads making new cycle lows in 2026.
- **What would change our mind to the upside:** evidence of Chinese capacity *rationalization* (plant closures, slowed additions) lifting integrated PE spreads, or LYB executing the European exit accretively while holding the dividend.

## Bottom Line
A genuinely beaten-down, high-quality operator — but the beating has already taken it roughly *to* fair value on a realistically haircut mid-cycle, not below it. The dividend cut and European review confirm an extended trough rather than signaling capitulation-bottom. Expected return is ~+3-4%/yr, dominated by dividend carry, with a meaningful structural-overcapacity downside tail. **WATCH; revisit near $50 or on clear evidence of cycle inflection. Lean PASS at $65.**
