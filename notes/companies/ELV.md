# Elevance Health, Inc. (ELV)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Healthcare Plans (Managed Care)
**Price:** $295 | **Market Cap:** $65.1B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-19

## Situation Summary

Elevance Health (formerly Anthem) is the second-largest U.S. managed care company by enrollment, with ~46M medical members across Medicaid, Medicare Advantage, and commercial lines. The stock has been crushed -- down 36% from its 52-week high of $459 -- by a triple headwind: (1) CMS threatened to suspend enrollment in all ELV Medicare Advantage plans effective March 31, 2026, over years of risk-adjustment data submission failures (flash drives instead of electronic systems); (2) Medicaid margins are deeply negative (~-1.75% operating margin in 2026) as post-redetermination acuity is higher than state rates reflect; (3) the entire managed care sector is repricing as medical cost trends run 7-10% vs. initial 5% assumptions. Revenue grew 13% in 2025 to $199B but net income declined and FCF has deteriorated sharply ($7.2B -> $3.2B over 3 years). The company guided for $25.50+ adjusted EPS in 2026 (down from ~$30 adjusted in 2025) and recently restructured leadership, consolidating Carelon under the CFO. The stock trades at 11.6x forward earnings -- a historically cheap multiple for a Blue Cross franchise.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** ELV is a quality franchise in a cyclical trough, but near-term visibility is poor. CMS sanctions create headline risk. Medicaid losses are a drag. 20 analysts, consensus "buy" with $381 mean target (+29%). Short interest negligible at 1.9%.
- **Our view:** The market is over-discounting the CMS sanction risk -- the enrollment freeze does NOT affect existing 1.9M MA members and stems from a pre-2023 data submission issue that ELV has already corrected. The real risk is the Medicaid margin trough (-1.75%) extending into 2027, but management has a credible path to 12%+ EPS growth from 2027 onward via rate catches, membership normalization, and cost controls. At 11.6x forward PE with a 2.3% dividend yield for a company with a Blue Cross license moat, the risk/reward is compelling -- this is the kind of "hated sector, quality franchise" setup that generates alpha.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (likely April) will be the key catalyst. If the benefit expense ratio comes in at/below 90.2% guidance and CMS sanctions are resolved or de-escalated, the stock re-rates. Conversely, if medical cost trends accelerate further, the trough extends.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- cyclical trough with multiple reversion catalysts.**

- **Medicaid rate catch-up**: State Medicaid rates lag actual acuity by 12-18 months. 2026 is the trough year at -1.75% operating margin; rates should begin reflecting true costs in 2027.
- **CMS sanctions resolution**: The underlying issue (pre-2023 data submission) has been corrected. Sanctions are punitive but fixable -- resolution removes a ~$20-30 overhang.
- **Medical cost trend stabilization**: The sector is repricing 2026 products for 7-10% medical inflation. If actual trends stabilize at these levels, margins recover as pricing catches up.
- **Leadership restructuring**: Consolidating Carelon under the CFO and Health Benefits under one leader simplifies execution.

**Caution**: ACA subsidies expiring could increase the uninsured population and shift costs. Medicaid cuts from the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" could reduce enrollment further. These are structural headwinds, not just cyclical.

## Bull Case
- CMS sanctions resolved quickly (Q2 2026), removing headline risk and enabling MA enrollment growth
- Medicaid operating margins improve from -1.75% toward breakeven as state rates catch up in 2027
- Benefit expense ratio comes in at/below 90.2% target, proving management can manage cost trends
- Carelon services division scales, diversifying revenue and improving enterprise margins toward 5-6% target
- Stock re-rates from ~12x to 14-15x forward PE as sector sentiment improves
- Buybacks continue at $2.5B+/yr pace, compressing share count

## Bear Case
- CMS sanctions extended or expanded, permanent loss of MA market share in affected states
- Medicaid losses deepen as states cut rates further under federal pressure (OBBBA)
- Medical cost trends accelerate beyond 10%, benefit expense ratio exceeds 91%
- ACA subsidy expiration drives adverse selection in individual market
- FCF continues deteriorating, forcing debt-funded buybacks or dividend cuts
- Sector de-rating as political rhetoric targets managed care profits

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: MODERATE

Elevance Health is a high-quality managed care franchise trading at a cyclical trough valuation (11.6x forward PE, 4.9% FCF yield) after a 36% drawdown driven by fixable regulatory issues and sector-wide cost headwinds. The Blue Cross license moat in 14 states, $199B revenue scale, and investment-grade balance sheet provide downside protection. The CMS sanctions are a headline risk but not an existential one -- the underlying data issue was corrected in 2023, and existing 1.9M MA members are unaffected.

The key bet is that 2026 is the Medicaid trough year and medical cost trends stabilize at current repriced levels, enabling the 12%+ EPS growth trajectory management has guided for starting in 2027. Expected value is +12.3% with a skew toward the bull case as multiple catalysts converge.

**Position sizing:** Kelly sizer recommends 15% (max cap) of portfolio, reflecting high model agreement on upside. Given the regulatory overhang and FCF deterioration, a more conservative 5-8% starting position with a plan to add on Q1 earnings confirmation or CMS resolution is prudent.

**Would downgrade to WATCH on:** (1) CMS sanctions expanded beyond MA-PD enrollment, (2) Q1 benefit expense ratio >91%, (3) Medicaid operating margin guidance worsens below -2%.
