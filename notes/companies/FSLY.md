## Situation Summary

Fastly just reported record Q4 and full-year 2025 results: revenue $624M (+15% YoY), Q4 accelerating to +23% YoY, FCF turning positive ($48M full year) for the first time in company history, and RPO surging +55% YoY to $353M — a strong forward revenue indicator. The stock rallied ~31% on the print, then pulled back sharply; as of today the stock trades at $24.28, well below the post-earnings spike and notably below where quantitative models were calibrated ($31.60 — models must be recalculated). Management guided 2026 to $700–720M revenue with non-GAAP operating income of $50–60M, implying continued operating leverage. However, the consensus analyst target is only $19.86 (7 analysts, "hold"), suggesting the Street remains deeply skeptical despite the beat-and-raise. The critical underappreciated datapoint from the primary source: security revenue was up 32% YoY and reached 20% of total revenue — a mix-shift story analysts have not yet priced in.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Fastly is a structurally challenged CDN commodity player competing against Cloudflare and Akamai; still unprofitable on GAAP; a COVID-darling that lost TikTok revenue and hasn't recovered; analyst mean target ($19.86) is below current price, implying overvalued even at $24.
- **Our view:** The consensus is anchored on trailing GAAP losses and the old CDN narrative. In reality, Fastly has quietly inflected: (1) FCF turned positive in 2025 ($48M), (2) security/edge-compute now 20% of revenue growing 32%, (3) RPO +55% signals customers are locking in multi-year deals, (4) operating leverage is accelerating (EBITDA -$29M in 2025 vs -$120M in 2022). The market is applying a "broken growth stock" discount to a company that is no longer broken.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (expected late April/early May 2026) — if Fastly posts FCF positive again and security revenue mix continues expanding past 20%, the narrative shifts from "CDN commodity" to "edge security platform." A second consecutive FCF-positive quarter is the catalyst.

## Inflection Point

**YES — this is a clear FCF inflection story.** The financial data tells an unambiguous story:
- FCF: -$150M (2022) → -$32M (2023) → -$21M (2024) → +$48M (2025)
- EBITDA: -$120M (2022) → -$57M (2023) → -$79M (2024) → -$29M (2025)
- Gross margin: 48% (2022) → 53% (2023) → 54% (2024) → 57% (2025)

The inflection has begun and is observable in reported numbers. Revenue re-accelerated from 15% full-year to 23% in Q4. RPO +55% provides 2026 visibility. This is not a speculative inflection — it's in the data.

## Bull Case
- Security revenue growing 32% and now 20% of revenue; if this reaches 35%+ of mix, FSLY re-rates from "CDN commodity" to "security platform" (much higher multiple)
- RPO +55% to $353M provides exceptional 2026 revenue predictability; 2026 guide of $700-720M (+12-15%) is likely conservative given Q4 momentum
- FCF positive and growing: if FCF reaches $100-150M in 2026-27, at even 20x FCF that's $2-3B enterprise value — near or above current EV of $3.9B on FCF alone, with the optionality of edge compute upside
- Operating leverage from fixed-cost network infrastructure means incremental revenue is very high-margin; non-GAAP OpInc guidance of $50-60M in 2026 is a step-change
- **Primary-source risk NOT in headlines:** NRR of 110% and enterprise customer count at 628 — the existing customer base is expanding spend. This is a land-and-expand working, not a net-new customer acquisition story

## Bear Case
- Cloudflare (NET) outcompetes on product (Workers, Zero Trust, R2, AI gateway) while trading at 20x revenue vs FSLY's 6x — talent and engineering will follow NET; FSLY is the smaller, less-loved platform
- Analyst consensus price target ($19.86) is BELOW current price — 7 analysts all see downside; if consensus is right about underlying business quality, multiple contraction from ~6x sales to 4x = $16/share
- GAAP profitability still distant; stock-based compensation dilutes shareholders materially; total debt $430M against $181M cash creates risk if revenue growth stalls
- CDN commoditization continues: Cloudflare Free tier, AWS CloudFront pricing, and Akamai are all structural headwinds to CDN pricing. FSLY's differentiation is in programmable edge, which requires customer adoption inertia
- **Primary-source risk underreported in news:** The 2026 guidance calls for only +12-15% revenue growth (vs 23% Q4 exit rate) — management is guiding deceleration. RPO growth of 55% likely includes lumpy enterprise contracts that may not recur at the same rate

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

The inflection is real and confirmed in the data (FCF positive, margins expanding, revenue re-accelerating, RPO +55%). However, the Q1 2026 earnings report is imminent and will confirm or deny whether the trend is durable. The setup is attractive: 12.9% short interest, analyst targets below current price (negative sentiment), stock has pulled back from the post-earnings spike, and GBM models show very strong upside ($41-87 fair value vs $24.28).

The primary concern is that Cloudflare is building a better product at a much faster pace, and FSLY's moat is genuinely weak (2/5). The +27% expected value is decent but not compelling enough to act ahead of Q1 confirmation.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** Q1 2026 earnings confirming: (1) FCF positive again, (2) security revenue growing >25% YoY, (3) NRR stable at or above 110%. At that point, the "CDN narrative" is dead and a re-rate is justified.

**Entry target if confirmed: $22-25** (current levels). Scale-in position — not full size given Cloudflare competitive risk.
**Thesis-break signal:** Q1 FCF negative, or security revenue growth decelerates below 20% YoY, or NRR drops below 105%.
