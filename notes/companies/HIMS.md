# Hims & Hers Health (HIMS)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Drug Manufacturers - Specialty & Generic
**Price:** $24.16 | **Market Cap:** $5.51B | **EV:** $6.05B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

DTC telehealth platform that just resolved its Novo Nordisk lawsuit and entered a partnership (Mar 9) to sell branded Wegovy/Ozempic. Previously attempted to sell compounded GLP-1s, but FDA cracked down in Feb 2026. Acquiring Eucalyptus for $1.15B for international expansion. Stock is down 70% from its ATH of $70.43. 46% short interest. SEC investigation and securities class actions are pending. Q4 revenue $618M, FY2025 $2.35B (+59% YoY). Management guided 2026 revenue of $2.7-2.9B. 52-week range: $13.74 - $70.43. Beta: 2.61.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Hold. Mean analyst target $23.69 (13 analysts), range $16-$30. The market sees HIMS as a high-growth DTC platform that successfully pivoted from compounded GLP-1s to a Novo partnership, but is weighed down by legal overhangs, margin compression, and aggressive balance sheet moves.
- **Our view:** No edge. The Novo partnership is a positive development but appears already priced in at $24. The stock faces a wall of overhangs — SEC investigation, class action lawsuits, gross margin compression (82% to 74%), SBC exceeding net income, and $970M in new debt to fund an unproven international acquisition. The risk/reward is asymmetric to the downside at current prices.
- **Trigger:** Would need to see: (1) SEC investigation resolution or dismissal, (2) gross margin stabilization above 70%, (3) early evidence of Eucalyptus integration success and international revenue contribution.

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: LOW

Negative expected value (-5.7%) at current prices. The Novo partnership is a genuine positive development, transforming HIMS from a regulatory adversary into a distribution partner for branded GLP-1s. However, the stock has already re-rated to reflect this, and the overhang list is long: SEC investigation, class action lawsuits, gross margin compression from 82% to 74%, SBC exceeding net income, $970M in new debt, and a $1.15B acquisition commitment for unproven international expansion.

The bull case requires multiple things to go right simultaneously (legal resolution + margin recovery + Eucalyptus success), while the bear case only needs one or two things to go wrong. At 46% short interest, the stock is a coiled spring in both directions — but the fundamental risk/reward does not justify a position.

**Entry:** Would consider at $16 or below, where the risk/reward improves meaningfully and the bear case is partially priced in.
**Thesis-break stop:** $30 (if legal clears and margins stabilize, the bull case is playing out and the stock is a buy, not a short).
