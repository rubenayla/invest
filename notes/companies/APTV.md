## What APTV is now (RemainCo)
Aptiv shed the labor-intensive, low-margin wiring-harness business (EDS → Versigent). What's left is
the **higher-margin, higher-tech** half:
- **Engineered Components / Signal & Power** — connectors, electrical centers, high-voltage
  electrification (the "nervous system" of the vehicle).
- **Advanced Safety & User Experience (ASUX)** — ADAS, software-defined vehicle, compute platforms.

RemainCo: ~$13.6B clean revenue (2027E), operating margin ~10%, vs the blended ~$20B/8% pre-spin.
The spin is the classic "keep the brains, spin the harness" move and should support a structurally
higher multiple than the old conglomerate.

## Bull case
- **Post-spin re-rating + forced selling.** Index funds and harness-focused holders dumped RemainCo
  mechanically into the spin (price bottomed ~$54 six weeks after distribution). RemainCo deserves a
  higher multiple than the old blended business; 9x forward is below auto-tech peers (~11–14x).
- **Higher-quality mix.** Connectors + high-voltage + software are the secular-growth, pricing-power
  parts of the vehicle. Content-per-vehicle keeps rising (electrification, ADAS, compute) even when
  unit volumes are flat.
- **Analysts onside.** Strong-buy consensus, $78 target (+27%), 2027E EPS $6.82 growing.
- **~8% FCF yield** funds buybacks at a depressed multiple.

## Bear case (try to kill it)
- **Auto supplier = structural value trap.** The sector trades 8–12x *for a reason*: cyclical,
  capital-intensive, customer (OEM) pricing power squeezes margins, China local competition in
  connectors/electronics. "9x forward" may just be a fair multiple, not a discount.
- **Revenue is shrinking near-term.** 2027E clean revenue ($13.6B) is *below* 2026E ($14.5B); global
  vehicle production is soft and EV adoption slowed, denting the high-voltage growth story.
- **Leverage into a cyclical.** Net debt ~$5–6B (~2x EBITDA) on a business tied to global auto builds;
  a real downturn compresses both EPS and the multiple at once.
- **ASUX competitive pressure.** ADAS/compute is contested by Mobileye, Nvidia, Qualcomm — Aptiv is not
  the clear winner; software margins are a hope, not yet a proof.
- **Spin dis-synergies.** Splitting from EDS removes scale/purchasing leverage and adds standalone cost.

## Verdict — BUY LOW (lean)
A genuine post-spin special situation: the apparent "−31% crash" is mostly the VGNT distribution, and
what's left is the better half of Aptiv at ~9x forward earnings with a strong-buy consensus and ~8% FCF
yield. EV ~+25% is attractive. But this is a **leveraged auto-cyclical** in a sector that earns its low
multiple, with near-term revenue actually shrinking — exactly the profile that turns into a value trap
if vehicle production rolls over. So the right posture is to **buy weakness, not chase strength**:
accumulate near the post-spin low (~$54–56, ~P/B 1.25x), not at $61+.

- **Entry:** $54–56 (near post-spin low / ~1.25x book)
- **Thesis-break:** sustained 2027 revenue guide cut + net leverage >2.5x EBITDA, or multiple stuck <9x
  through a full year of "growth" (signals value trap, not dislocation) → PASS
- **What would change my mind (to BUY MEDIUM):** clean RemainCo EBITDA-margin expansion (>12%) confirmed
  in two prints, leverage trending down via FCF/buybacks, and ASUX bookings showing software pull-through
