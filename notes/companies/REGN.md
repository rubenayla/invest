## Situation Summary

Regeneron is navigating a pivotal transition: its legacy Eylea franchise is eroding under biosimilar pressure (Amgen's Pavblu launched Nov 2024; Sandoz, Alvotech/Teva, and Celltrion biosimilars all entering by end-2026), while Dupixent continues to be a growth engine at $17B+ annualized revenue with 21% growth and expanding indications (AFRS approval Feb 2026). The company is building a new revenue pillar in oncology with Lynozyfic (linvoseltamab, FDA accelerated approval Jul 2025 for multiple myeloma) and entering the massive obesity market via olatorepatide (Phase 3 data showing 19% weight loss in China; global Phase 3 to start by year-end). The stock has pulled back ~10% from its 52-week high of $821, trading at just 14x forward earnings -- cheap for a large-cap biotech with this pipeline breadth and Dupixent's growth trajectory. Management signaled double-digit revenue growth for 2026 despite Eylea headwinds, and is investing $2B in new manufacturing capacity.

## Inflection Point

**Partial inflection identified -- obesity pipeline reframes the investment case.**

Regeneron is transitioning from a two-product company (Eylea + Dupixent) to a multi-franchise platform. The inflection has three legs:

1. **Eylea HD stabilization:** EYLEA HD is recapturing share from biosimilars with convenience dosing (every 12-16 weeks vs 4-8). Q4 2025 HD sales of $393M growing 16% sequentially suggests the transition is working.
2. **Oncology launch:** Lynozyfic approval unlocks a new therapeutic area. Early but trackable -- watch Q1/Q2 2026 launch metrics.
3. **Obesity entry:** Olatorepatide Phase 3 data from China (19% weight loss, superior tolerability) is the biggest optionality. Global Phase 3 initiation in H2 2026 is the next milestone.

The inflection is NOT yet fully observable in financials -- operating margins are still compressing. This is a "plant the seeds now, harvest in 2027-2028" situation. The 14x forward PE suggests the market is valuing Regeneron on current-year earnings with little pipeline premium.

## Bull Case

- Dupixent exceeds $20B in 2027 with AFRS, COPD, and food allergy expansions; consensus currently models ~$18B
- Olatorepatide Phase 3 shows best-in-class tolerability, reframing REGN as a credible obesity player and unlocking >$100B TAM
- Eylea HD stabilizes at $2B+ annual run-rate, biosimilar erosion less severe than feared
- Lynozyfic captures meaningful myeloma share ($600M+ peak); combination studies expand into earlier lines
- Forward PE re-rates from 14x to 18-20x as market assigns pipeline value

## Bear Case

- Eylea franchise collapses faster than expected as 3+ biosimilars flood the market in late 2026; HD conversion stalls
- Olatorepatide global Phase 3 fails to replicate China data or shows safety signal, killing the obesity narrative
- Dupixent faces emerging IL-13 competition (e.g., from AstraZeneca's tezepelumab) that slows growth to single digits
- Operating margin compression continues as R&D spend escalates without proportional revenue from new launches
- Forward PE compresses to 12x on growth deceleration fears, stock trades toward $550-600
