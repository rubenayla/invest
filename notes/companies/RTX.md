# RTX Corporation (RTX)

**Sector:** Industrials | **Industry:** Aerospace & Defense
**Price:** $204.56 | **Market Cap:** $275B | **EV:** ~$307B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-19

## Situation Summary

RTX is the world's largest aerospace and defense company by revenue ($88.6B in 2025), operating three segments: Collins Aerospace ($30.2B), Pratt & Whitney ($32.9B), and Raytheon ($28.0B). The stock has rallied ~82% from its 52-week low of $112.27 to $204.56, driven by a global defense spending super-cycle (NATO rearmament, US arsenal replenishment), record commercial aerospace aftermarket demand, and a $268B backlog providing multi-year revenue visibility. FY2025 was a strong year: revenue +10% YoY, adjusted EPS $6.29 (+10%), and FCF of $7.9B (+$3.4B YoY). The company is guiding 2026 for $92-93B revenue (5-6% organic growth), $6.60-6.80 adjusted EPS, and $8.25-8.75B FCF.

The key overhang remains the Pratt & Whitney GTF engine powder metal recall, which has grounded ~835 aircraft at peak. However, aircraft-on-ground are now down 20% from highs, MRO output is up 26% YoY, and the GTF Advantage variant is entering service. The recall is expected to normalize by late 2026. Meanwhile, Raytheon is a primary beneficiary of the global rearmament cycle, with a $50B umbrella contract for Patriot systems and ramping Tomahawk/interceptor production.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** RTX is a core defense holding benefiting from the global rearmament cycle. Analysts rate it Buy (13 Buy, 6 Hold, 3 Sell) with a median target of ~$225. The stock is seen as fairly valued at 31x forward P/E and 21x EV/EBITDA given the backlog visibility and secular tailwinds. Concerns center on (1) the GTF recall overhang, (2) whether the defense budget super-cycle is already priced in after the 82% rally, and (3) DOGE-driven Pentagon efficiency cuts potentially delaying some programs.

- **Our view:** The market is correctly pricing RTX as a quality defense compounder, but underappreciating two things: (1) the FCF inflection -- $7.9B in 2025 rising to $8.5B+ in 2026 as the GTF recall winds down and aftermarket margins expand, and (2) the backlog-to-revenue conversion kicker as supply chain constraints ease. At $204, the stock trades at 27x forward P/E ($7.51 forward EPS) which is rich for a defense name but justified if the FCF ramp continues. The risk/reward from here is more balanced than exciting -- most of the rearmament narrative is priced in. The stock is near its 52-week high of $214.50 with limited margin of safety.

- **Trigger:** (1) GTF recall fully normalized by late 2026 removing the P&W margin drag, (2) FY2026 FCF coming in above $8.75B high end of guide, (3) incremental international Patriot/LTAMDS orders from European NATO expansion.

## Inflection Point

**Partial — the GTF recovery is a margin inflection, not a revenue inflection.** The biggest positive inflection was in 2025 when FCF nearly doubled as GTF recall cash outflows peaked and began declining. The next inflection comes as P&W operating margins normalize (currently ~2.4% vs 5-7% potential), which could add $1B+ to operating income by 2027. On the defense side, the rearmament super-cycle is real but already reflected in the backlog and stock price. This is not a "hidden catalyst" situation — it's a "steady compounder executing on known catalysts" situation.

## Bull Case

- GTF recall fully resolved by late 2026; P&W margins expand from 2.4% toward 6%, adding $1B+ operating income
- European rearmament accelerates: additional Patriot/LTAMDS orders from Germany, Poland, Romania, Japan
- FCF exceeds $9B in 2027 as GTF drag fully fades and aftermarket grows
- Defense budget remains elevated ($1T+) through decade; backlog converts faster as supply chains normalize
- Multiple re-rates to 35x forward P/E on quality premium and defense scarcity value

## Bear Case

- GTF recall costs escalate or timeline extends beyond 2026, dragging P&W profitability
- DOGE/Pentagon efficiency drives delay or cancel programs (though core platforms like Patriot are protected)
- Defense spending cycles down after the current rearmament wave; backlog growth stalls
- Geopolitical de-escalation reduces urgency for allied military spending
- At 27x forward P/E, any earnings miss triggers a sharp de-rating (limited margin of safety)

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: **MEDIUM**

RTX is an excellent business — arguably the highest-quality defense franchise in the world with a wide moat, $268B backlog, and a clear path to margin expansion as the GTF recall fades. The problem is the price. At $204 and 27x forward P/E, the stock has re-rated dramatically from its 2023 lows and is now trading at a significant premium to historical defense multiples. The expected value of +1.75% offers no margin of safety. The GBM models with highest confidence (gbm_1y at $234, gbm_lite_1y at $204) confirm the stock is approximately fairly valued. Most of the rearmament tailwind and GTF recovery are already priced in.

This is a "great company, fair price" situation. Not a buying opportunity.
