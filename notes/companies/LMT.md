# Lockheed Martin Corporation (LMT)

**Sector:** Industrials | **Industry:** Aerospace & Defense
**Price:** $627 | **Market Cap:** $145B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-22

## Situation Summary

Lockheed Martin is the world's largest defense contractor, riding a global defense spending upcycle driven by Ukraine/Russia, Middle East escalation, NATO rearmament, and US bipartisan support for higher military budgets. Revenue grew 5.6% in FY2025 to $75B with a record backlog of ~$194B (2.5x annual revenue), providing exceptional long-term visibility. However, net income has declined two years running ($6.9B -> $5.3B -> $5.0B) due to F-35 cost overruns, supply chain inflation, and program charges. The stock is up 41% from its 52-week low but trades 7% below its 52-week high of $692. Analysts expect a major EPS inflection in 2026 ($30.19E, +40% YoY) as program charges normalize and pricing power improves. Key risk: DOGE-driven defense budget scrutiny and potential F-35 production slowdowns. Space Force halted some rocket launches at Lockheed's space division. Competition from Anduril and new-gen defense tech firms intensifying. Next earnings April 21, 2026.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** "Hold" — 14 analysts, mean target $587 (8.7% DOWNSIDE from current price). Market sees LMT as a steady defense compounder but believes the defense spending rally is largely priced in at 30x trailing PE. Short interest negligible at 1.4%. Beta of 0.20 makes it a defensive holding.
- **Our view:** The consensus is too bearish on the EPS recovery. The 2023-2025 earnings decline was driven by one-time F-35 lot charges and supply chain normalization — NOT structural margin deterioration. With $194B backlog, 6% revenue growth, and analysts projecting $30+ EPS in 2026, forward PE is actually ~21x, which is reasonable for a defense monopolist. The "missile defense supercycle" thesis is real — Middle East escalation + NATO 2%+ GDP targets = multi-year demand tailwind. DOGE risk is overstated; defense is politically untouchable in both parties. The concern is that at $642, the market already reflects much of the EPS recovery — limited margin of safety.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (April 21) will confirm or deny the EPS inflection narrative. If LMT guides to $30+ EPS for 2026 and backlog stays above $190B, re-rating to $680-700 is likely. If program charges persist or DOGE rhetoric escalates, pullback to $560-580.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — earnings inflection expected in 2026.**

- **EPS recovery:** Analysts project $30.19 EPS in 2026 (+40% YoY) as F-35 program charges normalize. This is the biggest single-year earnings improvement in LMT's recent history.
- **Backlog acceleration:** $194B and growing at ~10%/yr signals sustained revenue growth for years.
- **Missile defense supercycle:** Middle East escalation is driving unprecedented demand for THAAD, PAC-3, and integrated air defense — high-margin Missiles & Fire Control segment.
- **NATO rearmament:** European allies committed to 2%+ GDP defense spending, with many now targeting 3%+. LMT is the primary F-35 supplier for allied air forces.
- **DOGE headwind:** Potential for defense budget scrutiny, though bipartisan support makes deep cuts unlikely. More of a sentiment risk than fundamental threat.

## Bull Case
- F-35 cost issues resolve, program charges end, operating margins expand to 12%+ by 2027
- EPS reaches $30+ in 2026 as guided, forward PE compresses to 20x = $600+ justified
- Missile defense supercycle drives Missiles & Fire Control segment to 25%+ of revenue at higher margins
- NATO rearmament creates 5+ year international order book growth
- Backlog continues growing to $200B+, providing unmatched visibility
- Buybacks continue reducing share count 2-3%/yr, amplifying EPS growth

## Bear Case
- DOGE or new administration targets defense spending, creating budget uncertainty
- F-35 program charges continue into 2026, delaying the EPS recovery
- Supply chain inflation persists, keeping margins compressed at 10%
- Space segment underperforms (rocket launch halts, SpaceX competition)
- At 30x trailing PE, any earnings disappointment triggers 10-15% correction
- Anduril and new-gen defense tech captures increasing share of future programs
- Geopolitical de-escalation (Ukraine ceasefire, Middle East peace) reduces urgency for defense spending

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MODERATE

Lockheed Martin is a best-in-class defense franchise with an unassailable moat, record backlog, and a genuine earnings inflection coming in 2026. The quality score of 19/25 reflects a durable, well-managed business. However, at $642 (30x trailing, 21x forward), the stock is approximately fairly valued — the defense spending rally has already been largely priced in, and the analyst consensus target of $587 implies modest downside. Expected value is slightly negative at -3.6%.

The key question is whether the EPS recovery to $30+ materializes and whether the missile defense supercycle drives above-consensus growth. If it does, there's 10-12% upside. If not, the downside is meaningful (-26% in bear case).

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** (1) Pullback to $540-560 range providing 15%+ margin of safety on forward earnings, OR (2) Q1 2026 earnings confirming $30+ EPS guidance with backlog growth above 10%, OR (3) Major new international F-35 orders or missile defense contracts expanding the backlog beyond $200B.
