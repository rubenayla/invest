## Situation Summary

DoorDash delivered FY2025 revenue of $13.7B (+28% YoY), achieved its first year of GAAP profitability ($935M net income), and grew Marketplace GOV 39% YoY to ~$115B. The Q4 2025 EPS of $0.48 slightly missed consensus estimates, but the underlying momentum — 903M orders (+32% YoY), accelerating international unit economics, and a Deliveroo integration beating EBITDA targets — was strong. Management guided Q1 2026 GOV of $31-31.8B and EBITDA of $675-775M, implying continued scaling of operating leverage. Grocery/retail unit economics are expected to turn positive in 2H 2026, representing the next major margin catalyst. Q1 2026 earnings are due May 6, 2026.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** DASH is a maturing food delivery platform facing slowing US growth, rising competition from Uber Eats/Instacart, and a stretched valuation (EV/EBITDA ~52x). Most bulls anchor on US delivery durability; bears focus on grocery profitability delays.
- **Our view:** The market is anchoring on US food delivery saturation and missing two underpenetrated vectors: (1) DASH is building a global convenience commerce platform via Wolt + Deliveroo at a stage where unit economics are already inflecting positively internationally while still in heavy investment mode; (2) the FCF story is structurally better than GAAP earnings suggest — FCF jumped from $21M in 2022 to $1.83B in 2025, a genuine operating leverage story. At 23x forward PE and 2.4% FCF yield, the market is not pricing in durable 25%+ revenue growth for 3+ more years.
- **Trigger:** May 6, 2026 Q1 results — if GOV growth reaccelerates and grocery/retail economics show early progress, the re-rating from ~23x to 30x+ forward PE is plausible. Deliveroo full integration milestones in H2 2026 are a secondary catalyst.

## Inflection Point

Yes — this is a GAAP profitability inflection story. The key turning points:
1. **FCF inflection complete**: $21M (2022) → $1.83B (2025). This is done and durable.
2. **GAAP profitability inflection**: 2025 was the first GAAP profitable year ($935M net income). Operating leverage is now self-evident.
3. **Grocery/retail inflection pending**: Unit economics expected to turn positive 2H 2026 — this is observable but not yet priced in.
4. **International margin inflection in progress**: Wolt/Deliveroo unit economics improving YoY and QoQ despite investment phase.

The inflection is clearly underway (not a value trap risk), but the grocery/retail and international phases are still ahead.

## Bull Case

- Grocery/retail unit economics turn positive on schedule (2H 2026), expanding DoorDash's addressable basket from ~$450B food to ~$1.5T US convenience commerce
- International markets (Wolt, Deliveroo) scale toward Wolt's demonstrated ~30% GOV CAGR, driving 40%+ international GOV growth through 2027
- Agentic AI ordering (DoorDash AI agents enable autonomous re-ordering for consumers) creates new engagement loops reducing CAC
- DashPass subscriber monetization deepens — ARPU expansion from $120/yr toward $200/yr as platform adds categories
- Operating margin expands to 12-15% by 2028 on scale; re-rates to 35-40x forward PE

## Bear Case

- **Grocery margins stay negative longer than 2H 2026 guidance** — management disclosed this as an ongoing investment drag; news media has under-covered how much grocery is suppressing consolidated EBITDA margins (this came from primary Q4 data, not headlines)
- Uber Eats closes market share gap in suburban US, triggering promotional intensity that erodes US GOV growth and unit economics
- Deliveroo integration complexity (UK regulatory environment, labor reclassification risk) delays international profitability by 12-18 months
- Consumer spending downturn in a recession scenario: food delivery (avg ticket ~$35) is discretionary; DoorDash showed during COVID that it can accelerate in lockdowns but macro weakness reverses this
- SBC dilution: stock-based comp has been 6-8% of revenue historically; at scale this becomes a meaningful headwind to EPS even with strong FCF
