# The Mosaic Company (MOS)

**Sector:** Materials | **Industry:** Agricultural Inputs (potash + phosphate fertilizer)
**Price:** $23.30 (2026-06-04) | **Market Cap:** ~$7.4B (~318M shares) | **EV:** ~$13.0B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-04
**Verdict:** BUY LOW (starter only, conviction LOW) — borderline with WATCH. Mosaic trades at **0.61x book** ($23.30 vs ~$38 BVPS) and ~6.5x EV/EBITDA near its 52-week low ($20.89), a genuine deep-cyclical trough on a global top-3 potash + phosphate producer with multi-decade, low-cost Saskatchewan potash reserves. But it is **actively bleeding cash right now**: Q1'26 (reported 2026-05-11) was a **net loss of -$0.81/share**, 2025 free cash flow was **-$0.53B**, net debt is rising, and buybacks were paused. The asset value is real and the potash franchise is performing, but a fresh Persian-Gulf-conflict input-cost shock (sulfur/ammonia) has impaired phosphate margins, so this is a tiny scale-in at the bottom of the range, not an actionable back-up-the-truck bargain.

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Mosaic is one of the world's largest producers of two of the three primary crop nutrients: **potash** (K — Saskatchewan and New Mexico mines, marketed offshore through the Canpotex export cartel with Nutrien) and **phosphate** (P — Florida and Louisiana, plus **Mosaic Fertilizantes** in Brazil, the country's #1 fertilizer distributor). Nitrogen (the third nutrient) it largely buys/blends rather than makes. ~$12B revenue, ~13,000 employees. Spun out of Cargill in 2004/2011.

**Why it can be a good business.** Potash is the crown jewel: Mosaic's Esterhazy and Belle Plaine complexes sit on some of the lowest-cost, longest-life potash reserves on earth (cash cost ~$84/tonne in Q1'26, trending lower as the **Esterhazy "hydrofloat" project** ramps to full rate). Potash is a 4-producer global oligopoly (Nutrien, Mosaic, Belaruskali, Uralkali/K+S), demand is inelastic (you cannot grow food without K), and Canpotex pools North American export volume to manage price. Brazil distribution (Fertilizantes) gives a captive, growing emerging-market demand channel. At mid-cycle the company throws off real cash — FCF was **+$2.7B in 2022** and **+$1.0B in 2023**.

**Why it's a hard business (the reason it's cheap).** Mosaic is a **price-taker on a volatile commodity** with no control over its top line, and phosphate is structurally worse than potash: phosphate margins are a *spread* between finished-product price and two volatile input costs — **sulfur and ammonia** — neither of which Mosaic controls. Capex is heavy and relentless (~$1.25–1.4B/yr), so trough FCF turns negative even when the income statement shows a small profit. Returns at the trough are abysmal (2024 ROE was 0.6%, 2025 net income $0.54B on $12.1B equity).

## Situation Summary

MOS sits at **$23.30, -39% below its 52-week high ($38.23) and ~12% off the 52-week low ($20.89)**. The earnings path tells the cyclical story — and a fresh negative inflection:

| Year | Revenue | Net Income | Free Cash Flow |
|------|---------|------------|----------------|
| 2022 (peak, Ukraine-war spike) | $19.1B | $3.58B | +$2.69B |
| 2023 | $13.7B | $1.16B | +$1.00B |
| 2024 (trough) | $11.1B | $0.17B | +$0.05B |
| 2025 | $12.1B | $0.54B | **-$0.53B** |
| Q1'26 (reported 2026-05-11) | — | **-$0.26B (-$0.81/sh)** | — |

The 2025 income line *looked* like a recovery off the 2024 trough, but **cash went the wrong way**: operating cash flow fell to $0.82B while capex held at $1.36B, so FCF was -$0.53B, total debt rose ($4.45B → $5.28B), the dividend ($0.28B) was effectively debt-funded, and **buybacks went to zero** (after $1.67B in 2022, $0.76B in 2023, $0.24B in 2024). That is a company conserving cash at the bottom of a cycle, not one inflecting up.

**The Q1'26 shock.** A Persian Gulf conflict spiked **sulfur and ammonia** prices, compressing phosphate stripping margins *below variable cost on a marginal basis*. Mosaic responded by **curtailing ~half the capacity at its Bartow (FL) and Louisiana phosphate plants**, idling SSP at Araxá/Patrocínio in Brazil, cutting 2026 capex guidance by $250M to $1.25B, and launching a $50M workforce-reduction program. The bright spot: **potash is on a record pace** — Canpotex fully committed through June, and unit costs set to fall as Esterhazy hydrofloat reaches full production. So the franchise splits cleanly: **potash strong, phosphate in an input-cost vise.**

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view (Street "hold," mean target ~$26.82, +15%):** A cheap-but-going-nowhere cyclical. You're at 0.6x book for a reason — the company is loss-making this quarter, burning FCF, raising debt, and the phosphate input shock is open-ended geopolitics nobody can underwrite. Potash faces **new low-cost supply from BHP's Jansen mine** ramping from 2027, which caps the upside even if the trough is in. Wait for cash generation to actually turn before touching it.
- **Our view:** The market is pricing the *current* phosphate squeeze as permanent when it is a transitory input spike layered on a genuine asset-value floor. (1) At **0.61x book**, you're paying ~$13B EV for irreplaceable Saskatchewan potash reserves + Brazil's #1 fertilizer distributor — assets that would cost far more to replicate. (2) The phosphate margin problem is a *spread* problem (sulfur/ammonia), and curtailments + eventual input normalization mechanically restore it; this is not lost demand (Q1'26 phosphate volumes were the highest in five years). (3) Potash — the better half — is firing on all cylinders with falling unit costs. (4) Mid-cycle FCF power is real ($1–2.7B in 2022–23); at ~$13B EV that is a 8–20% FCF yield on normalized output. The honest caveat: you are catching a falling knife with active losses, so size it as a deep-value option, not a core position.
- **Trigger (confirms the buy):** Sulfur/ammonia roll over as the Gulf conflict de-escalates, phosphate curtailments reverse, and Esterhazy hydrofloat drops potash cash cost toward ~$75/tonne — two quarters of positive FCF and a return to net profit. **Thesis-break (kills it):** the Gulf input shock persists or worsens into 2027, phosphate stays curtailed, net debt keeps climbing toward a leverage problem, **the $0.88 dividend gets cut**, or BHP Jansen supply collapses potash pricing just as Mosaic needs it. Any of those validates the value-trap read.

## Rating Rationale

Standalone: **BUY LOW (starter only), conviction LOW — a coin-flip with WATCH.** The pull toward BUY is the 0.61x book asset floor, a performing potash franchise with a falling cost curve, and a clean transitory explanation (sulfur/ammonia spread) for the phosphate pain, giving a positive ~+23% scenario EV at a genuine 52-week-low entry. The pull toward WATCH/PASS is everything cash: a Q1'26 net loss, negative 2025 FCF, rising debt, a debt-funded dividend at risk, paused buybacks, and an open-ended geopolitical input shock with no clear end date. Net: worth a *tiny* deep-value starter at the bottom of the range for a cyclical-tolerant book, or a watchlist add with a lower trigger (~$20 / clear sign sulfur is rolling over) for everyone else. Not an actionable back-up-the-truck bargain tonight.
