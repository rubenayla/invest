# Nike, Inc. (NKE)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Footwear & Apparel (athletic)
**Price:** $43.81 (2026-06-04) | **Market Cap:** ~$65B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-04
**Verdict:** BUY (scale-in) — wide-moat franchise at a 5-year low with early stabilization signals; turnaround is real but slow, China is the live overhang.

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Nike designs and sells athletic footwear, apparel, and equipment under the Nike, Jordan, and Converse brands — the #1 athletic brand globally by revenue (~$46B FY2025). It does almost no manufacturing itself; production is outsourced to contract factories in Vietnam, China, and Indonesia, and Nike captures the brand margin. Two routes to market: **wholesale** (Foot Locker, Dick's, JD Sports, etc. — ~$6.5B/quarter) and **Nike Direct** (its own stores + Nike.com app + SNKRS — ~$4.5B/quarter, higher gross margin but higher operating cost).

**Why customers choose them (the moat).** Nike's moat is brand and scale, not technology. Decades of athlete endorsements (Jordan, the swoosh, "Just Do It"), franchise sneaker lines (Air Force 1, Air Jordan, Dunk, Pegasus), and the largest marketing budget in the industry create pricing power and shelf dominance that a new entrant cannot replicate cheaply. The Jordan brand alone is a multi-billion-dollar franchise with cult pricing power. Scale also buys the best factory capacity and the deepest retail distribution. The moat is wide but **not impregnable** — it is a fashion/taste business, and taste shifts.

**What went wrong (the reason it's cheap).** Under the prior CEO (John Donahoe, 2020–2024), Nike over-rotated to DTC, pulled back from wholesale partners (ceding shelf space to On, Hoka, New Balance, and a resurgent Adidas Samba/Gazelle), and over-supplied its classic franchises (Dunk, AF1) into the channel — killing their scarcity and forcing margin-destroying clearance. Revenue fell from a $51.4B peak (FY2024) to $46.3B (FY2025), operating income halved ($6.3B → $3.7B), and the stock fell ~73% from its $164 peak to ~$44.

**The turnaround.** Elliott Hill — a 32-year Nike veteran who ran the commercial business — returned as CEO in **October 2024**. His playbook: (1) re-engage wholesale partners to recapture shelf space, (2) cut supply of over-distributed classics to restore scarcity/pricing, (3) refocus product on **sport/performance innovation** (running, basketball, training) rather than lifestyle retro, and (4) clear excess inventory. This is a multi-year clean-up: you eat lower revenue and margins now (clearance, China reset) to rebuild a healthier base.

## Situation Summary

NKE sits at **$43.81, essentially on its 5-year low ($42) and 52-week low ($41.35)**, down ~73% from the $164 peak and ~45% from the 52-week high of $80. The most recent print (**Q3 FY2026, ended Feb 28 2026, reported Mar 31 2026**) is the first concrete stabilization signal: revenue **$11.3B, flat YoY** on a reported basis (−3% currency-neutral) vs. the steady declines that preceded it, and **EPS $0.35 beat the $0.28 consensus by 25%**. Critically, **wholesale grew +5% reported** — direct evidence Hill's channel re-engagement is working — while Nike Direct fell −4% (the intentional DTC pullback). The offsetting bad news: management guided **Q4 sales down 2–4%** (worse than the +1.9% Street estimate) and warned of an expected **~20% decline in China** in the quarter. So: stabilization in the core (North America/EMEA wholesale) is fighting a still-deteriorating China.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Nike is a structurally challenged former leader losing a generation of consumers to On and Hoka (running) and Adidas (lifestyle), with China in secular decline and tariffs permanently impairing margins. The turnaround will take years, guidance keeps getting cut, so the stock is a value trap near its lows.
- **Our view:** The earnings are *cyclically depressed by a self-inflicted, fixable clean-up*, not structurally broken. The brand, the Jordan franchise, and the distribution moat are intact — what broke was strategy (DTC over-rotation, franchise over-supply), and the new CEO is reversing exactly those mistakes with early, measurable success (wholesale +5%, revenue flat YoY, EPS beat). You are buying a wide-moat, dividend-aristocrat (23+ years of increases) cash machine at a 5-year low on trough earnings, where the downside is largely already priced and the normalization upside is ~2x.
- **Trigger:** Each quarter that wholesale keeps growing and gross margin stops falling (it was 40.2%, down only 130bps YoY — decelerating decline) rebuilds the case that earnings normalize toward $4–5B net income. China stabilizing (or simply lapping the weak comps) removes the last overhang. FY2027 (starting June 2026) is the "clean base" year management has signaled.

## Valuation — Scenario Analysis

Anchor on **normalized earnings power**, not trough. Nike earned $5.7B net (FY24) and $6.0B (FY22) at the top, ~$4.0B pre-pandemic (FY19). Mid-cycle normalized is plausibly **$4.5–5.5B** on ~1.2B shares = **$3.75–4.60 EPS**. Current TTM EPS is ~$1.90 (depressed).

The asymmetry is the point — the bear case lands roughly at *today's price* because the stock already trades at a trough multiple of trough earnings, while base/bull are ~1.9–2.5x. Analyst mean target is $60.8 (range $23–$120); the Street is in the "base, discounted for time" camp.

## Entry / Exit Framework

- **Entry:** ~$44 (current), **scale in**. The China overhang and weak Q4 guidance argue against a full position up front — buy a starter, add on either (a) a China stabilization signal, or (b) a further dip toward the low $40s/high $30s.
- **Thesis-break price / signal:** ~**$34** (≈20% below, under the 52-week low). More important than price are the *fundamental* breaks: **gross margin breaking below ~38%** (brand has lost pricing power → structural, not cyclical), **wholesale growth stalling/reversing** after FY26 (Hill's core fix isn't taking), or **revenue resuming >5% declines** in FY2027 after the "clean base" reset. Any of those flips this from "cyclical trough" to "value trap."
- **What would change my mind (bearish):** sustained share loss in *running specifically* (Nike's heartland — losing it to On/Hoka would be structural), a dividend cut (signals FCF stress and management capitulation), or China declining beyond the guided reset into a structural −20%+ run-rate.
- **What would confirm (bullish):** two consecutive quarters of positive currency-neutral revenue growth, gross margin inflecting upward, and SNKRS/Jordan re-establishing scarcity pricing on key franchises.

## Bottom Line

A wide-moat, dividend-aristocrat franchise trading at a 5-year low on self-inflicted, fixable, cyclically-depressed earnings, with a credible insider CEO reversing the exact mistakes that caused the decline — and the first hard evidence (wholesale +5%, revenue flat YoY, EPS beat) that the bottom is forming. Risk/reward is asymmetric (~−9% bear vs ~+87% base vs +150% bull) and you are paid 3.75% to wait. The live risk is China and the slow pace; size accordingly — **starter position, scale in.**
