# Lyft, Inc. (LYFT)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Software - Application (Rideshare)
**Price:** $13.34 | **Market Cap:** $5.3B | **EV:** $4.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

Lyft reported record Q4 and full-year 2025 results (gross bookings +19% YoY in Q4, adj. EBITDA +37% YoY to $154M), yet shares sold off 15% on slightly soft active rider numbers (29.2M vs 29.5M expected) and Q1 2026 guidance that merely met expectations ($4.86-5.0B bookings, $120-140M adj. EBITDA). The stock is now trading near 52-week lows at $13.34, down ~48% from its 52w high of $25.54, punished alongside growth/tech names in a risk-off environment. Lyft is aggressively pivoting toward autonomous vehicles — Waymo partnership launching in Nashville in 2026, Agentic AI integration with NVIDIA DRIVE Hyperion announced March 16 — while executing a $1B buyback authorization. The market narrative treats Lyft as a permanent #2 to Uber with existential AV risk; the financials tell a different story of accelerating profitability and massive FCF generation.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Lyft is a structurally disadvantaged #2 player that will be disrupted by autonomous vehicles. Uber's first-mover AV partnerships (Waymo in multiple cities) and superior scale make LYFT a "melting ice cube." The ~17% short interest reflects genuine bearish conviction.
- **Our view:** The market is mispricing Lyft's FCF inflection. At $1.1B FCF on a $5.3B market cap (20.9% FCF yield), the stock is pricing in FCF *decline*, but the trend is sharply upward (from -$352M in 2022 to +$1.1B in 2025). The Waymo-Nashville partnership and NVIDIA AV platform position Lyft as an AV *beneficiary*, not victim — Lyft becomes the demand aggregation platform for autonomous fleets. The $1B buyback at these prices is enormously accretive (~19% of market cap). The net income figure of $2.84B in 2025 includes a large one-time gain (likely tax asset revaluation), which inflates trailing P/E, but the underlying adj. EBITDA trajectory ($528.8M, up 38% YoY) is real.
- **Trigger:** (1) Waymo Nashville launch on Lyft network (mid-2026) proving the platform model works. (2) Q1 2026 earnings in May showing continued bookings acceleration and buyback execution. (3) Additional AV partnership announcements expanding the fleet-as-a-service narrative.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — profitability inflection is real and underappreciated.** Lyft crossed from cash-burning to generating >$1B FCF in just 3 years. The operating leverage is kicking in as the fixed cost base is now spread over a much larger ride volume (945M rides in 2025, +14% YoY, 11th consecutive quarter of double-digit growth). The AV pivot represents a potential *second* inflection — from ride-hailing operator to autonomous fleet management platform — but this is early-stage and the Nashville launch will be the first real test.

The $1B buyback at current prices (~19% of market cap) is itself an inflection catalyst — at this FCF yield, the math is powerfully accretive.

## Bull Case

- FCF continues to compound: $1.1B in 2025, potentially $1.4-1.6B by 2027 as ride volumes grow mid-teens and margins expand
- $1B buyback at $13-15 retires ~15% of shares outstanding, driving significant EPS accretion
- Waymo Nashville success leads to multi-city AV fleet management deals; Lyft becomes the "AWS of autonomous mobility"
- Market re-rates from distressed-tech multiple (4.8x FCF) toward platform multiple (12-15x FCF), implying $35-45 per share
- United Airlines partnership and other corporate deals drive higher-value ride mix

## Bear Case

- Uber's dominant scale and multi-product ecosystem (delivery, freight) permanently cap Lyft's growth and margin potential
- AV disruption actually hurts Lyft — Waymo/Cruise/Tesla build their own demand platforms, disintermediating Lyft entirely
- Revenue growth stalls below 10% as rideshare matures; investor exodus from low-growth story
- Regulatory risk in key markets (California gig economy laws, insurance costs)
- Management burns FCF on acquisitions or overpays for AV technology rather than returning capital

## Verdict

**BUY** — Conviction: MEDIUM

Lyft is a rare case where the FCF inflection is dramatic and real ($1.1B on a $5.3B market cap = 21% yield) but the stock is priced for permanent decline due to AV disruption fears and #2 player discount. The variant perception is that Lyft is an AV *beneficiary* through fleet management partnerships, not a victim, and the $1B buyback at these levels is enormously accretive. Conviction is MEDIUM rather than HIGH because the growth runway is genuinely narrower than Uber's and AV platform economics remain unproven.

**Entry:** Scale in at $12.50-13.50 (current levels). Add aggressively below $11.
**Thesis-break:** Exit if FCF drops below $700M/year or AV partnerships dissolve. Hard stop at $8.
