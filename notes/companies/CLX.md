# CLX — The Clorox Company

**Price:** $92.64 (2026-06-23)
**Rating:** WATCH (lean BUY LOW) — quality staple near multi-year lows with a defined one-time
catalyst, but no-growth + leverage + a tight payout cap the upside; want a sub-$85 entry.
**First look:** 2026-06-23

Clorox is a defensive consumer-staples company: bleach and cleaning (Clorox, Pine-Sol,
Tilex), trash bags (Glad), charcoal (Kingsford), dressings (Hidden Valley), water filtration
(Brita), cat litter (Fresh Step), natural personal care (Burt's Bees), and — newly — hand
hygiene (Purell, via the GOJO acquisition). Roughly $7.1B revenue, ~80% U.S., a Dividend
Aristocrat with ~47 straight years of payout increases. The stock has fallen from ~$195 six
years ago and ~$170 in 2024 to $92.64 today, sitting at only ~17% of its 52-week range
($84.70–$132.03). The question is whether a one-time earnings air-pocket is being mistaken
for permanent impairment.

## Why it's on the radar
- Down ~30% from its 52-week high, ~3% off the 52-week low. Genuine multi-year washout, not a dip.
- 5.35% dividend yield (vs a ~3% historical norm) — the market is pricing stress, which on a
  47-year Aristocrat usually means either a bargain or an impending cut.
- Low beta (~0.55), defensive end-markets. The kind of name you want cheap before a risk-off turn.
- Analyst mean target ~$105 (≈ +13%), rating "hold" — lukewarm, not capitulation.

## The bear case (lead with it — this is the crux)
1. **No organic growth, and FY26 is going backwards.** Revenue has been flat at ~$7.1B for
   five years (FY22 $7.11B → FY23 $7.39B → FY24 $7.09B → FY25 $7.10B). FY26 guidance is for
   net sales **down ~6%** and **organic sales down ~9%**. A staple that can't grow volume is
   worth a low-teens multiple, not a premium.
2. **The Q3-FY26 guidance cut was ugly.** Management slashed adjusted EPS to $5.45–5.65 (from
   $5.95–6.30) and GAAP diluted EPS to $4.78–4.98, and widened the gross-margin decline to
   250–300 bps (from 50–100 bps). Drivers: a ~$0.90 EPS drag from unwinding ~2 weeks of
   inventory shipped ahead of demand during the fiscal-2025 ERP transition, GOJO acquisition
   dilution/integration costs, and sticky input-cost/tariff inflation.
3. **Balance sheet is stretched and the dividend is tight.** ~$4.49B debt vs ~$1.19B cash
   (net debt ~$3.3B); EV ~$14.7B on an $11.2B cap. Book equity is **negative** (years of
   buybacks plus GOJO goodwill) — ROE is not meaningful here. The $4.96 dividend is **~102%
   of FY26 GAAP EPS** and ~89% of adjusted EPS this year. It is covered by normalized free
   cash flow (~$700–800M FCF vs ~$610M dividend), but FY26 has almost no cushion, and the
   GOJO debt raises the bar.
4. **ERP/transition execution has been self-inflicted.** This is the same company that took a
   2023 cyberattack hit; the demand-shaping distortions are management's own doing, which
   dents the "well-run defensive compounder" framing.

## The bull case (what would have to go right)
- **The ~$0.90 ERP drag is explicitly one-time and reverses in FY27.** Strip it out and
  normalized adjusted earnings power is ~$6.30–6.70, on which today's price is ~14–15x — cheap
  for a brand portfolio of this quality, against a 20–24x historical multiple.
- **Gross margin is recovering off a supply-chain trough** (43.8% now vs ~44–46% historically);
  management's multi-year program targets continued expansion. Margin is the main earnings lever
  for a flat-revenue staple, and it's pointed up over a 2–3 year arc.
- **Purell/GOJO adds a genuinely growing category** (hand/surface hygiene) to an otherwise
  ex-growth portfolio, and should turn from dilutive to accretive once integrated.
- **Capital return does real work**: 5.35% yield while you wait, funded by FCF, plus buybacks
  that shrink the (already buyback-heavy) share count. You don't need growth heroics — margin
  normalization + a partial re-rating gets you a respectable return.

## Valuation — my own build (classic models run DB-wide, not per-ticker; done by hand)
Normalized FY27 adjusted EPS ≈ **$6.50** (FY26 ~$5.55 midpoint + ~$0.90 ERP reversal, modest
organic recovery, GOJO turning accretive). Horizon ~2.5 years; ~$12.5 of dividends collected.

the base case modest, while the 5.35% yield and one-time-catalyst reversal support a floor.

## Entry / thesis-break
- **Buy interest: sub-$85** (near the $84.70 52-week low, ~6%-yield, ~13x normalized) — that's
  where the risk/reward tilts clearly favorable. At $92.64 it's a WATCH, not a back-up-the-truck.
- **Thesis-break: a dividend cut, or organic sales still declining into FY28.** Either would
  confirm the bear "structural decline + over-levered payout" read rather than a transitory
  air-pocket. A break below ~$70 would likely coincide with the market pricing a cut.
- **What would change my mind (to the upside):** FY27 guidance showing organic sales back to
  flat/positive and gross margin recovering toward 45%, with the dividend reaffirmed — that
  validates the normalized-$6.50 earnings power and the re-rating case.

## Catalysts to watch
- Q4-FY26 results / FY27 initial guidance (~Aug 2026) — the cleanest read on whether the ERP
  unwind is truly behind them and margins are inflecting.
- GOJO/Purell integration milestones and accretion timing.
- Any commentary on the dividend at the FY27 raise decision (the Aristocrat streak is at stake).
