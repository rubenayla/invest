## Situation Summary

Qnity Electronics is a freshly independent DuPont spinoff (late 2025) supplying specialty chemicals and materials for semiconductor manufacturing — CMP pads/slurries, photoresists, advanced coatings, and interconnect materials. On its first earnings call as a standalone company (Q4/FY2025, Feb 26 2026), CEO Jon Kemp reported the "seventh consecutive quarter of strong organic growth," with FY2025 net sales of $4.75B (+10% YoY) and Adjusted Pro Forma EBITDA of $1.4B (29.5% margin). 2026 guidance is $4.97–$5.17B revenue and $3.55–$3.95 adjusted EPS. The market is still discovering the name post-spinoff, analyst coverage is thin (8 opinions), and the stock trades near consensus target — but the primary-source risk the news underplays is a planned 9%-of-sales CapEx spike in 2026 (vs. the ~6% normalized run-rate), compressing near-term FCF while the company builds IT independence from DuPont and funds local-for-local manufacturing.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Thin analyst coverage (~8 opinions), price target $140.50 implying flat upside. Market appears to price Qnity as a slow-growth specialty chemical supplier — "strong buy" rating but no re-rating catalyst expected.
- **Our view:** The market does not yet appreciate (a) the structural AI/advanced-node tailwind embedding in both segments (AI chip demand driving CMP intensity), (b) the recurring-revenue nature of consumable semiconductor materials (high switching costs, long qualification cycles), and (c) that the CapEx spike in 2026 is a one-time IT separation cost masking true normalized FCF power of ~$1B+/yr. GBM models show 30–120% upside; the wide spread reflects uncertainty about whether this is a cyclical recovery story or a secular compounder.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (May 12, 2026) — first standalone quarter with full guidance reaffirmation and margin visibility will be the first data point for re-rating.

## Inflection Point

Yes — **double inflection:**
1. **Corporate birth**: Just became a standalone public company. Spinoffs systematically underperform in the first 6–12 months as forced sellers (index funds, parent company shareholders) exit, then recover as focused management and pure-play premium emerge.
2. **Earnings recovery**: Operating income grew 54% from 2023 trough to 2025. This is not a mature flat business — it's a cyclical recovery in semiconductor materials aligned to the AI buildout secular tailwind. Observable evidence: seven consecutive quarters of organic growth, FCF up 52% over two years.

The 2026 CapEx spike is a one-time drag, not a structural inflection reversal. Normalized FCF power is ~$1B/yr.

## Bull Case
- AI-driven advanced node adoption accelerates CMP and photoresist volume throughput — semi materials intensity per wafer increases with each node
- Post-spinoff re-rating: pure-play semiconductor materials commands 30–35x EV/EBITDA vs. conglomerate discount embedded in DuPont valuation
- Non-fluorine photoresist (R&D 100 Award winner) gains share in leading-edge fabs, opening a new product cycle
- CapEx normalization post-2026 (from 9% back to 6% of sales) boosts FCF yield from ~3.3% to ~4.5%+
- Analyst coverage expansion (currently only 8 analysts) drives institutional discovery and multiple expansion

## Bear Case
- **Primary-source risk underplayed in news:** 2026 CapEx guided at 9% of net sales (~$450M) vs. normalized ~6% — this is a ~$150M FCF headwind in 2026 that depresses the "true earnings power" narrative. If IT systems separation from DuPont runs over budget or takes longer, this drag extends.
- Simple_ratios model shows -37% downside: at 42x trailing P/E and 6.2x P/S, the stock is priced for continued execution. Any earnings miss punished severely at this multiple.
- Semiconductor materials is cyclical — the 2022→2023 revenue drop from $4.76B to $4.0B (-16%) happened in one year. If AI capex moderates, volumes compress quickly.
- Very low insider ownership (0.05%) suggests management does not have strong financial alignment with shareholders.
- Analyst consensus target ($140.50) implies zero upside from here — 8 analysts see no margin of safety.

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

Qnity is a high-quality semiconductor materials compounder at a pivotal moment: freshly independent, riding the AI advanced-node buildout, with clear FCF improvement and 7 quarters of organic growth. The business quality is real (moat: 4/5, profitability: 4/5). However, the stock has already appreciated ~19% since models were last run ($119 → $141), analyst consensus implies zero further upside, and the expected value from scenarios is only +13% — insufficient for a BUY at this multiple (42x trailing, 32x forward P/E). The CapEx-heavy 2026 is a near-term FCF drag that limits the catalyst to earnings reaffirmation rather than an upside surprise.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** Q1 2026 earnings (May 12) beat + 2026 guidance raise that demonstrates the AI volume tailwind is flowing through faster than modeled, combined with a stock pullback toward $120–125 (where EV-based GBM models have more margin of safety). At ~$125, the risk/reward improves materially with FCF yield ~3.9% and EV <30% above normalized FCF.

**Entry price if upgrading:** $120–$130 range. Thesis-break at $95 (would imply semi cycle downturn materializing).
