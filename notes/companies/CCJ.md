# Cameco Corporation (CCJ)

**Sector:** Energy | **Industry:** Uranium
**Price:** $105.14 (2026-05-19) | **Market Cap:** ~$45.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-05-19

## Business Primer

**What they do today.** Cameco is the largest Western (non-state) uranium producer and a vertically integrated nuclear fuel supplier. Roughly 84% of revenue comes from the Uranium segment: mining and milling U3O8 (yellowcake) at three Tier-1 Saskatchewan assets — Cigar Lake (50% operated stake), McArthur River / Key Lake (70%) — plus a 40% stake in the JV Inkai in-situ recovery mine in Kazakhstan (operated with state-owned Kazatomprom). The other ~16% is the Fuel Services segment, which converts U3O8 into uranium hexafluoride (UF6) and uranium dioxide (UO2) at Port Hope and Blind River — Cameco operates the only commercial UF6 conversion plant in North America. Customers are nuclear utilities globally (US, Europe, Asia) on long-term contracts. Production runs ~19.5–21.5 million pounds U3O8 (Cameco's share) per year. On top of the mining/conversion business, Cameco owns 49% of Westinghouse Electric Company (the other 51% is Brookfield's), acquired in late 2023; Westinghouse sells reactor services, fuel assemblies, plant automation, and is the lead Western reactor OEM (AP1000 large reactors plus the AP300 small modular reactor in development). Westinghouse contributes via equity earnings, currently lumpy and seasonally back-end-weighted.

**Where they want to grow.** Three explicit bets. First, ride the term-contract repricing cycle: utilities are dramatically under-contracted relative to reactor lifetimes, and Cameco is layering new commitments at market-related prices with floors and ceilings well above the prices baked into pre-2022 legacy contracts — average annual delivery commitments are now over 28 million pounds per year through 2030. Second, ramp idled and constrained capacity (McArthur River back to full nameplate, JV Inkai output recovery in Kazakhstan, restart optionality on McClean Lake and Rabbit Lake) as prices justify it — this is supply discipline, not growth at any cost. Third, monetize Westinghouse: the joint venture is co-leading an ~$80 billion US reactor build-out narrative with Brookfield and the US government, with optionality on AP1000 builds in Poland, Bulgaria, Ukraine, plus an AP300 SMR pipeline. Success in 3–5 years looks like realized uranium prices in the US$80–100/lb range (vs. ~$66/lb realized in Q1 2026), Westinghouse equity earnings stepping up to a $400–600M/yr run rate, and a handful of named AP1000/AP300 reactor commitments.

**What could go wrong (business risks).** Uranium is a brutal commodity. Spot prices have whipsawed from $20/lb (2016) to over $140 (2007 peak) to $107 (early 2024) to ~$84 today; a Fukushima-style demand shock or a sustained Kazakh oversupply event (Kazatomprom controls ~40% of global supply via in-situ recovery mining) collapses the thesis. Production risks are real and specific: Key Lake mill is undergoing an extended Q3 2026 maintenance shutdown with potential overruns; Cigar Lake remains technically demanding (deep, high-grade, water-sensitive); JV Inkai has flagged sulfuric acid supply issues, supply chain disruptions, transportation, and construction delays. The Kazakh JV is also geopolitically exposed — both to Russia (transit corridors) and to Kazatomprom's strategic choices. Westinghouse execution is the wildcard: reactor builds are historically over-budget, over-schedule, and politically dependent — the segment posted a $46M net loss (Cameco's share) in Q1 2026 with management warning H1 2026 would be the "weakest" period. There's an open Canada Revenue Agency transfer-pricing dispute involving up to $559M of cash and letters of credit. And the stock trades at ~97x trailing P/E, ~55x forward P/E — valuation already prices in a meaningful nuclear renaissance.

**How to think about it.** Cameco is the largest Western uranium pure-play with embedded reactor-services optionality via Westinghouse — the cleanest public-market vehicle for the long-term thesis that utility uranium term-contract prices reprice meaningfully higher as the nuclear renaissance, AI data center power demand, and an ex-Russia, ex-Kazakhstan supply squeeze converge. It is not a value stock; it is a multi-decade option on Western nuclear capacity expansion, priced at expensive earnings multiples but defensible replacement-value/NAV multiples.

## Situation Summary

Cameco delivered a strong Q1 2026: revenue $845M (+7% YoY), adjusted EBITDA $509M (+44%), adjusted EPS more than doubled, and management reaffirmed full-year guidance of 19.5–21.5M lbs U3O8 production and 13–14M kgU fuel services. Average realized uranium price was US$66.21/lb (+6% YoY) while spot sat at ~$84/lb — the gap reflects legacy contracts still rolling off. Term contract book stands at >28M lbs/year average through 2030 with elevated 2026–2028 volumes. Westinghouse posted a $46M net loss (Cameco's share) but $122M adjusted EBITDA, with management explicitly flagging H1 2026 as the seasonal weak point. Operating cash flow swung to a $22M use (vs. $110M source prior year) from working capital and tax payments. Analyst consensus is Buy with mean target ~$138 (~31% upside); the stock has roughly doubled in the past year and trades at ~97x trailing earnings — a consensus-long, fully-priced setup in a structurally bullish secular story.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** "Nuclear renaissance plus AI data center power demand drives sustained uranium term repricing; Westinghouse is a hidden gem; mean analyst target ~$138 implies ~30% upside; Buy." The thesis is well-understood, broadly held by long-only growth and ESG-energy mandates, and reflected in a ~95x trailing P/E.
- **Our view:** The secular thesis is correct but the consensus underestimates two near-term frictions. First, the *pace* of contract repricing is slower than market implies — Cameco's own realized price was $66/lb in Q1 while spot was $84, meaning legacy contract roll-off lags by 2–3 years and analyst forward EPS models routinely overshoot. Second, Westinghouse near-term equity earnings will disappoint in H1 (management told us so) and reactor newbuild contracts are notorious for slipping right. The variant: Cameco is a high-quality multi-decade compounder, but the *entry price is wrong today*. A 10–15% pullback on a Q2/Q3 Westinghouse miss or any uranium spot dip below $75 creates the actual buying setup, not chasing at ~$105.
- **Trigger:** Q2 2026 earnings (early August) — if Westinghouse equity earnings miss again as guided, or if uranium spot dips on Kazakh production headlines, expect a 10–15% drawdown; that is the BUY trigger. Conversely, named AP1000 contract awards (Poland follow-on, Bulgaria, Ukraine) within 6 months would re-rate higher.

## Inflection Point

Yes — a multi-year inflection is in progress and observable:
- 2022–2024: Spot uranium broke out from $30/lb to $100+ on Sprott Physical Trust accumulation and end-of-Russian-imports policy.
- 2023: Westinghouse acquisition closed (Cameco 49% / Brookfield 51%).
- 2024–2025: Major utility term contracting pickup; US AP1000 newbuild momentum (Poland, Bulgaria); SMR roadmaps activated.
- 2026: AI data center power demand begins to flow into utility nuclear planning (Microsoft–Three Mile Island, Amazon–Talen, Oracle–small modular announcements).

The inflection is *not in question*; the question is at what price you participate. The stock has already moved from ~$25 (2022) to $105 (today), so much of the "inflection rerate" is in.

## Bull Case
- Uranium term-contract prices reprice durably to US$90–110/lb as utilities scramble to lock in supply for both existing fleet life-extensions and new AP1000/SMR builds.
- AI data center power demand creates a step-function in projected nuclear capacity additions, pulling Western utilities to contract 2030–2040 supply now.
- Westinghouse wins named AP1000 builds in Poland (Choczewo follow-on), Bulgaria (Kozloduy 7–8), Ukraine, with AP300 SMR commitments — Cameco's 49% stake produces $500M+/yr equity earnings by 2028.
- Tier-1 Saskatchewan reserves give Cameco a 30+ year production runway at low cost.
- Disciplined supply: management does not chase volume into a top; protects realized prices.

## Bear Case
- **Underreported risk from primary source:** Key Lake mill extended Q3 2026 maintenance shutdown is flagged by management as a real downside scenario for 2026 production; reporters have not picked this up but it could shave 1–2M lbs from guidance and pressure realized cash costs.
- Westinghouse equity earnings disappoint repeatedly — reactor builds slip, AP300 SMR timeline stretches; segment becomes a drag rather than a kicker.
- Kazakh oversupply: JV Inkai is constrained today by sulfuric acid, but Kazatomprom retains the option to flood the market; ~40% of global supply originates there.
- Uranium spot retraces to $60–70/lb (it has done so multiple times in the modern era); Cameco's realized price stalls in the $60s for several years and EPS estimates get cut 30%+.
- Valuation: 97x trailing P/E, 55x forward, EV/EBITDA ~39x — any thesis crack triggers a fast multiple compression. Crowded long with limited valuation cushion.
- Canada Revenue Agency transfer-pricing dispute (up to $559M exposure) — adverse ruling tail risk.
