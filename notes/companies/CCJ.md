# Cameco Corporation (CCJ)

**Sector:** Energy | **Industry:** Uranium Mining / Nuclear Fuel Cycle
**Price:** $116.45 | **Market Cap:** $49.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-30

## Situation Summary

Cameco is the western hemisphere's largest uranium producer (Cigar Lake, McArthur River/Key Lake, Inkai JV in Kazakhstan) and -- via the 49% Westinghouse stake acquired with Brookfield in late 2023 -- a vertically integrated nuclear fuel cycle and reactor services name. The stock has tripled in 18 months (from ~$40 in late 2024 to $116 today), riding the AI-power thesis: Microsoft/Constellation TMI restart, Amazon/Talen, Meta nuclear RFPs, plus Trump-era executive orders pushing US uranium independence and SMR licensing acceleration. Operationally, FY2025 was a step-change: revenue CAD$3.48B (+11% YoY), adjusted EBITDA CAD$1.93B (+26%), realized U3O8 price CAD$87/lb (+9%), and Westinghouse swung from a CAD$218M segment loss to a CAD$58M profit on Dukovany construction milestones and global services growth. The contract book stands at ~230M lbs U3O8, with ~28M lbs/yr committed 2026-2030. The catch: at $116, CCJ trades at 118x trailing P/E and ~26x EV/EBITDA, pricing in years of margin expansion that the contract book caps in the near term.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Strong Buy with average target ~$124-130 (RBC C$160, Stifel C$180, Scotia C$150). The Street treats CCJ as the cleanest large-cap proxy on a structural uranium deficit -- 78GW of reactors under construction, restart pipeline, SMR optionality -- combined with a Westinghouse asset that is now compounding rather than dragging. Term U3O8 at $90/lb (highest since 2009) is read as the leading indicator that contracted realizations will mark up over time.
- **Our view:** The bull thesis is real but the marginal buyer at $116 is paying for a 2028-2030 outcome today. Cameco's contract book is heavily base-escalated and market-referenced with caps, which means the upside to a $120-150/lb spot environment leaks through to the P&L over 3-5 years, not 1-2. Realized price of CAD$87 against spot $85 and term $90 already shows the contract book is *not* trailing badly -- it's roughly at-market -- so the "hidden upside as legacy contracts roll" argument is weaker than it looks. Westinghouse is the swing factor: if Dukovany-style EPC wins multiply (Czech, Poland, Bulgaria, possibly UK SMRs), the equity income contribution can double. If they don't, the multiple compresses.
- **Trigger:** A US DOE uranium reserve award to Cameco in 2026, a second AP1000 EPC win post-Dukovany, or spot U3O8 breaking $100/lb on a supply disruption (Inkai, Niger, Russian sanctions tightening). Conversely, a Kazatomprom production beat, SPUT redemptions at NAV discounts, or a Westinghouse cost overrun on Dukovany would compress the multiple.

## Inflection Point

**Yes, the industry is mid-inflection -- but Cameco's stock has already participated meaningfully.** The structural drivers:

- **AI/data-center power demand:** Microsoft-Constellation TMI restart (2026 target), Amazon-Talen, Meta nuclear RFP, Google-Kairos SMR PPA. This is the new marginal demand on a market that was already short.
- **Policy alignment:** Trump executive orders on uranium independence (May 2025); Russian uranium import ban grandfathering expires; UK, France, Czech, Poland advancing reactor builds; Japan restart pace accelerating.
- **Term price signal:** $90/lb is the highest term price since 2009. Term price leads spot historically.
- **Western fuel cycle scarcity:** Kazatomprom production guidance cuts (2024, 2025), Niger junta disruption to Orano supply, Russian conversion/enrichment displacement.

**However:** CCJ is up ~180% in 18 months. The "uranium is undiscovered" framing is several years stale. Sprott Physical Uranium Trust trades at NAV, generalist money is in, sell-side is at Strong Buy. The inflection in the *commodity* may continue; the inflection in *the stock relative to fair value* has largely happened. What's NOT priced in is multi-year Westinghouse EPC pipeline + spot above $120 sustained. What IS priced in is the base case that everyone agrees on.

## Bull Case

- Spot U3O8 breaks $100/lb on a supply disruption (Inkai outage, Niger escalation, tightened Russian sanctions). Term price follows to $110+, Cameco's market-related contract slice marks up materially.
- Westinghouse wins a second AP1000 EPC contract (Poland, Bulgaria, or UK) within 18 months; equity income contribution doubles toward CAD$500M+.
- US DOE uranium reserve program awards Cameco a multi-year offtake at premium pricing, validating the "western premium" thesis.
- SMR licensing accelerates -- Westinghouse AP300 gets first deployment commitment, adding LR option value.
- Cameco sanctions Millennium or restarts mothballed capacity, signaling growth runway beyond 2030.
- Indian, Chinese, and Middle Eastern long-term contracting accelerates (India already locked 22M lbs through 2035 in 2025).

## Bear Case

- Spot U3O8 fades to $70-75/lb on Kazatomprom production beat or financial-buyer (SPUT) selling pressure. Term price softens, future contract realizations compress.
- Westinghouse Dukovany cost overruns or schedule slip; equity income reverts toward breakeven.
- A high-profile reactor incident (any country) freezes the policy momentum.
- Multiple compression: 26x EV/EBITDA mean-reverts to 15-18x peer average -- a 30-40% multiple haircut even on flat EBITDA.
- Generalist rotation out of "AI power" trade -- CCJ correlates with CEG/VST/OKLO and gets sold as a basket if the AI capex narrative cools.
- Inkai JV operational risk: Kazakhstan transport bottleneck, geopolitical disruption, or Russia-Kazakhstan friction affecting export routes.
- Currency: USD weakening vs CAD compresses USD-denominated EPS (CCJ books in CAD, sells in USD).

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM-LOW

CCJ is a quality business in a real structural bull market, but the stock has already absorbed most of the easily-modeled upside. At $116, the math says: base-case fair value ~$120 (3% upside), bear case $75 (-36%), bull case $165 (+42%). Probability-weighted EV is +3% -- below the cost of capital and not enough premium for commodity volatility. The user's framing -- "underpriced uranium fuel cycle as a less-crowded subset of the AI-power trade" -- is half right and half stale. *Less crowded than CEG/VST?* Yes. *Underpriced?* Not at 26x EV/EBITDA with the contract book where it sits. The Westinghouse stake is the genuinely interesting embedded asset, but at this price you're paying for it twice -- once in the multiple and once in the bull-case Dukovany roll-out assumption.

If the user wants the "uranium fuel cycle" thesis with a better R/R profile, alternatives worth considering: BWXT (already in the watchlist -- Naval reactors + HALEU + medical isotopes, defense-anchored cash flow), or directly via SPUT/URNM/URA basket if the view is that the commodity outperforms the equities given the latter's multiple compression risk.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:**
- Pullback to $85-95 range (multiple compression to ~18x EV/EBITDA, downside well-supported by contract book)
- Spot U3O8 closing above $105/lb with term following to $100/lb
- Second Westinghouse AP1000 EPC contract (any geography) confirming the EPC pipeline thesis
- US DOE uranium reserve award at a premium to spot

**Would downgrade to PASS on:**
- Spot U3O8 breaking below $75 with Kazatomprom guiding production above plan
- Westinghouse Dukovany schedule slip or cost overrun disclosure
- Equity raise (highly unlikely given fortress balance sheet, but a tell)
- Stock above $135 without confirmed Westinghouse EPC catalyst -- pure multiple expansion at that point
