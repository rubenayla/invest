# Hormel Foods (HRL) — First Look

**Price:** $23.13 (2026-06-04)
**Call:** BUY LOW (starter / income-while-you-wait) — de-rated Dividend King at a generational-low ~5% yield with a fresh, evidence-backed turnaround print
**Conviction:** MEDIUM (B−) — turnaround is real but young; earnings power has been flat for 4 years and DCF models say flat FCF is worth far less than today's price

Hormel at $23 is **−38% from its 2022 peak (~$37)** and sitting just above its 3-year low (~$20).
For a **60-year Dividend King**, the stock now yields **~5.0%** — roughly **2.5× its historical
~2% yield** — which is the kind of mean-reversion signal that has marked durable entry points in
quality staples before. The bull case is that fiscal-2025 was the earnings trough (margin
compression + one-off impairments), and the multi-year **"Transform & Modernize" (T&M)** program
is now visibly working: **Q2 FY2026 (reported 2026-05-28) beat with adjusted EPS $0.40, +14% y/y,
segment profit +13%, and all three segments growing.** You get paid ~5% to wait while it plays out.
The bear case is that earnings have gone *nowhere* since 2018, center-store packaged protein faces
real secular pressure, and the cash-flow DCFs say a no-growth Hormel is worth ~$11.

## The setup: FY2025 trough → T&M-driven recovery

Hormel's fiscal year ends late October. FY2026 is ~halfway done (Q2 reported 2026-05-28).

- **FY2025 (ended Oct 2025):** net sales **$12.1B** (organic +2%), **adjusted EPS $1.37**, but
  **GAAP EPS only $0.87** after **$234M of Q4 non-cash impairments** (an Indonesia minority
  investment + Retail intangibles — *not* a fresh Planters writedown). Op margin compressed on
  persistent meat/commodity input inflation.
- **FY2026 guidance:** adjusted EPS **$1.43–$1.51 (+4% to +10%)**, net sales $12.2–12.5B, organic
  +1% to +4%. GAAP EPS guide trimmed to $1.28–$1.37 *solely* for a $61M whole-bird-turkey
  divestiture loss — an accounting drag, not an operating miss.
- **Q1 FY2026 (Feb-2026):** adj EPS $0.34, organic +2%, guidance reaffirmed (in-line).
- **Q2 FY2026 (2026-05-28):** **adj EPS $0.40, +14% y/y — beat (~$0.35 consensus)**; net sales
  ~$2.97B (+2.5%, organic +3%); **segment profit +13% with all three segments growing**; China
  drove International (+20% segment profit on SPAM exports). Adj EPS guidance reaffirmed. Stock
  popped on the print. This is the freshest, most concrete evidence the turnaround is landing.
- **Transform & Modernize (T&M):** multi-year supply-chain / network / portfolio overhaul launched
  Q4 FY2023, targeting **~$250M annual run-rate benefit by FY2026** and a return toward **~10% adj
  operating margin** (from ~8.4%). Architected by John Ghingo, now President.
- **Whole-bird turkey sale (FY2026):** Hormel exited the lowest-margin, most avian-flu-exposed
  commodity turkey business (booking a $61M loss). Jennie-O *value-added* turkey stays. This
  structurally de-risks the most volatile piece of the model — a genuine quality improvement.
- **CEO:** Jeff Ettinger (CEO 2005–2016) returned as **interim** CEO 2025-07-14 for ~15 months;
  permanent CEO targeted ~Oct 2026. Ghingo is the leading internal candidate. Leadership overhang.

## Bull case

- **Generational-low valuation for a 60-year Dividend King:** ~5% yield vs ~2% historical, ~15.7x
  fwd P/E vs a 20–24x history, ~9.9x EV/EBITDA vs ~13–15x. A staple this de-rated has usually
  been a decent entry if earnings merely stop falling.
- **Turnaround is showing in the numbers, not just the deck:** Q2 FY2026 beat, +14% adj EPS, all
  segments growing, T&M savings flowing. This is the catalyst, and it's fresh (2026-05-28).
- **Mix is improving:** shed commodity turkey, growing branded/value-added (SPAM exports to China,
  Planters recovering post-recall, Applegate/Justin's/Columbus premium brands, Foodservice strength).
- **Fortress balance sheet:** ~1.7x net leverage, A-rated, low refinancing risk. The Hormel
  Foundation's 47% stake makes a dividend cut almost unthinkable culturally — strong income floor.
- **Earnings leverage:** every 100bps of op-margin recovery toward the ~10% target is meaningful on
  $12.4B of sales; a re-rate *and* margin recovery both hitting is the bull payoff.

## Bear case (lead with this)

- **Earnings power is flat-to-down, structurally.** Adj EPS: FY2022 ~$1.82 → FY2023 ~$1.45 →
  FY2024 ~$1.47 → FY2025 $1.37 → FY2026 guide ~$1.47. Revenue has been ~$12B for **four straight
  years**. This is not a growth compounder anymore; the old premium multiple was a bull-market
  artifact and **15x may be the new fair multiple**, not a discount.
- **The dividend didn't cover itself in FY2025:** FCF $0.53B < dividends $0.63B. FY2024 covered
  1.65×, so FY2025 looks like a working-capital/earnings anomaly — but if recovery stalls, the
  "60-year streak" survives only as token ~1% raises (already the case), i.e. a stagnant yield trap.
- **The cash-flow DCFs are damning:** the repo's `dcf`, `dcf_enhanced`, and `multi_stage_dcf` all
  land near **~$11 fair value** — roughly half today's price — because a no-growth ~$0.55–0.85B
  FCF stream discounts to far less than $12.7B of market cap. If the secular-decline bears are
  right, that's the number, not $27.
- **Secular headwinds are real:** private-label share gains in center-store, GLP-1 demand erosion
  for processed/packaged protein (narrative, not yet quantified by Hormel), commodity/avian-flu
  volatility, and soft consumer trading down.
- **Leadership vacuum:** interim CEO until ~Oct 2026; the permanent pick could reset strategy/guide.
- **Thin tangible book:** $7.9B equity is mostly goodwill from the (widely-criticized, ~$3.35B)
  Planters deal; PB ~1.6x overstates asset protection — tangible book is only ~$1.3B.

## Entry, thesis-break, what would change my mind

- **Entry:** starter at/below ~$23; add on weakness toward the ~$20 3-year low (yield ~5.8% there),
  where the income floor and Foundation backstop make the risk/reward more asymmetric.
- **Thesis-break (sell/avoid):** (a) a quarter where T&M margin gains *reverse* and adj op margin
  slips back below ~8%; (b) adj EPS guidance cut below ~$1.40 for FY2026; (c) FCF fails to re-cover
  the dividend in FY2026 (two straight uncovered years = the streak becomes a balance-sheet drain);
  (d) the permanent CEO (~Oct 2026) resets guidance materially lower.
- **What would raise conviction to BUY MEDIUM:** two consecutive quarters of T&M margin expansion
  *and* FY2026 FCF comfortably re-covering the dividend (>1.3×), confirming the trough is in.
- **What would kill it:** confirmation the DCF bears are right — organic volume turning persistently
  negative as private label / GLP-1 erode the franchise, making ~$11 the anchor rather than ~$26.

## Bottom line

A quality, investment-grade Dividend King at a genuine multi-year-low valuation and a ~5% yield,
with a *fresh and concrete* turnaround datapoint (Q2 FY2026 beat, 2026-05-28) and a de-risked
portfolio (turkey exit). That's a real BUY-LOW starter for an income-oriented value sleeve — you're
paid well to wait and the downside is cushioned by the Foundation's near-religious dividend
commitment. But four years of flat earnings, an FY2025 dividend that didn't self-fund, and a stack
of DCFs pointing at ~$11 keep conviction at **MEDIUM** and the size **small**: this is a "let the
turnaround prove itself one or two more quarters" name, not a back-up-the-truck bargain. Most of the
return here is the yield plus a modest re-rate, not a deep-value double.
