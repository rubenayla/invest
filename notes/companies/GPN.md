## Situation Summary
Global Payments is the merchant-acquiring counterpart to the FIS asset swap (see `notes/companies/FIS.md` — the other side of the same transaction). On **2026-01-12 GPN closed its transformative deal**: it acquired **Worldpay** from FIS and GTCR (~$24B) and simultaneously divested its own **Issuer Solutions** card-processing business to FIS (~$13.5B). The result is a **pure-play commerce/merchant-solutions company** — the plumbing that lets merchants accept card and digital payments, plus the software and embedded-payments layer around it. Management frames it as serving "the full spectrum, from the corner store to the global enterprise."

The market hates it. The stock is **$64.28 — its absolute 5-year low**, down ~65% from the $184 2021 peak and below its prior-cycle troughs. On the screen it is extraordinarily cheap: management guides **$13.80–$14.00 adjusted EPS for 2026 → ~4.6x forward adjusted earnings**, the stock trades at **0.74x book** ($87/sh book value), **~12.5x GAAP** earnings, and a **~11.6% free-cash-flow yield** (~$2.0B FCF on a $17.6B cap). It even popped **+16% on 2026-02-19** when it reported the integration starting and a large buyback — then round-tripped the entire move back to new lows. Analysts' mean target is **$93 (+45%)**, range $60–$194, 26 analysts, consensus "buy".

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Legacy merchant acquirer being disintermediated. The old card-present, ISO-channel acquiring model is melting under Stripe, Adyen, Block/Square, Toast and increasingly account-to-account and stablecoin rails. GPN just re-levered to ~4.5x net to bolt on **Worldpay — a perennial orphan asset** that has destroyed value through three owners (Vantiv → FIS → GTCR → now GPN). 'Adjusted' EPS of $14 is a fiction that excludes ~$2.5B of recurring intangible amortization; GAAP EPS is only ~$5. Cheap for a reason. Avoid."
- **Our view (partial pushback):** You are paying ~12.5x *GAAP* earnings and ~11.6% FCF yield, **below tangible-adjusted book**, for a business that still throws off ~$2B of real cash and is guided to **13–15% adjusted EPS growth** with explicit cost/revenue synergies. The disruption thesis is real but overstated for the enterprise/integrated-software segments Worldpay strengthens — global e-commerce acquiring is *growing*, not declining. At this multiple you do not need a re-rating to win: high-single-digit organic growth + an ~11% FCF yield (much of it now directed to a **$7.5B buyback** retiring shares at <5x earnings) is a high-teens IRR on its own. The asymmetry vs. the FIS side of the trade is similar but with *more* operational leverage to integration success.
- **Why it is NOT a slam-dunk (kill case first):** (1) **Worldpay is a serial underperformer** — every prior owner failed to grow it; merging two giant, overlapping merchant-acquiring books while migrating to one ("Genius") platform is exactly where acquirers stumble. (2) **Leverage ~4.5x net** geared onto a business facing secular questions means any organic-volume stall hits the equity disproportionately, and a recession in consumer spending flows straight through. (3) The **adjusted-to-GAAP gap is enormous and recurring** — a serial acquirer's amortization is arguably a real cost, so "4.6x earnings" flatters the true ~12.5x. (4) It has made **repeated new lows** — classic knife-catch. (5) Organic growth at legacy GPN had already decelerated to low-single-digits before the deal.
- **Trigger / what re-rates it:** Two or three quarters proving (a) Worldpay **organic merchant volumes are growing**, not shrinking, (b) Genius-platform migration and the $70–80M of 2026 synergies are landing on schedule, and (c) net leverage marching back toward ~3x on FCF + buyback accretion. Even a re-rate from ~4.6x to a still-cheap 6.5x adjusted EPS is ~+40%; the analyst mean ($93) implies +45%.

## Levels
- **Entry:** Already attractive at $64 (5y low, 0.74x book). Scale in; add aggressively only **below $60** (below the 52-week low / prior support), which would also be a tell that the disruption/leverage narrative is winning and demands re-underwriting.
- **Thesis-break / exit:** A **cut to 2026 adjusted EPS guidance below ~$13**, or two quarters of **shrinking organic merchant volumes**, or any **leverage-driven dividend cut / covenant stress**. Any of these confirms the value trap → move to PASS.
- **What would change my mind (upgrade to BUY MEDIUM):** Two consecutive quarters of (a) positive Worldpay organic volume growth, (b) synergies tracking ahead of the $70–80M plan, and (c) net leverage falling toward ~3.5x — at which point ~5x earnings with a proven integration is too cheap.

## Relationship to the FIS note
GPN and FIS are the **two sides of the January 2026 asset swap**. FIS took the stable, mature **Issuer Solutions** book (and is the lower-beta, lower-leverage way to play the deal — rated BUY LOW at ~6.3x). GPN took **Worldpay** and the merchant/commerce-solutions future — **higher operational leverage, higher debt, higher reward if integration works, and a worse outcome if it doesn't.** If you believe the payments incumbents are mispriced, FIS is the safer expression and GPN the more aggressive one.
