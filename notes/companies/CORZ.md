# Core Scientific, Inc. (CORZ)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** AI/HPC Data-Center Colocation (formerly Bitcoin mining)
**Price:** $24.37 (2026-05-21) | **Market Cap:** $7.7B (basic) / ~$12.3B (fully diluted, ~505M shares)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-05-21

## Business Primer

**What they do today.** Core Scientific owns large industrial buildings full of electrical capacity — data centers wired to the power grid with substations, transformers, and cooling. Historically it filled those buildings with Bitcoin-mining computers and earned money by mining crypto. It is now in the middle of a hard pivot: instead of running its own mining rigs, it rents the buildings, power, and cooling to companies that run artificial-intelligence computing. This rental model is called "colocation" — the customer brings (or pays for) the expensive graphics-processing-unit servers, and Core Scientific provides the powered, cooled space and operates the facility. In Q1 2026, colocation revenue was $77.5M of total revenue of $115.2M, and the remaining self-mining revenue is being deliberately wound down (management expects only one or two mining sites left by year-end). The single anchor tenant today is CoreWeave, an AI cloud provider, under a 12-year take-or-pay lease projected at roughly $10B of total revenue across about 590 megawatts of capacity.

**Why customers choose them (the value proposition).** An AI company that wants to deploy thousands of GPUs has two choices: build its own data center, or rent powered space from someone who already has it. Building from scratch means waiting 5-7 years just to get the electric utility to connect a new large load to the grid — interconnection queues are the binding constraint in the United States right now. Core Scientific already has roughly 1.3 GW of *energized* grid capacity at sites in Texas, Georgia, North Carolina, and Oklahoma, built out during the mining era. So the concrete benefit to a customer like CoreWeave is time: they get hundreds of megawatts of live, grid-connected, cooled power in 12-14 months instead of half a decade, at sites with cheap electricity (Core Scientific specializes in low-cost power regions). The customer pays a fixed monthly rent for that capacity (take-or-pay means they pay whether or not they fully use it), which converts Core Scientific's volatile mining income into predictable, contracted, long-term cash flow.

**Where they want to grow.** Management's explicit plan is to become a pure AI/HPC infrastructure landlord and shed mining entirely. They have laid out a roughly 3 GW expansion pipeline: ~520 MW at existing sites, 285 MW from the Hunt County, TX acquisition, 330 MW at Muskogee, OK (regulatory approval expected Q3 2026), ~370 MW of unannounced opportunities, plus ~900 MW of behind-the-meter potential and ~600 MW in load studies. The two flagship expansion sites, Pecos (TX) and Muskogee (OK), each target ~1.5 GW of total gross power. Critically, the prior exclusivity that tied new capacity to CoreWeave has expired, so management says it is now in active discussions with three hyperscalers plus chipmakers, AI labs, and "neocloud" providers to fill the uncontracted pipeline. Success in 3-5 years looks like 1-2 GW billing across several investment-grade tenants, not just CoreWeave.

**What could go wrong (business risks).** First, customer concentration: today essentially 100% of colocation revenue is CoreWeave, and CoreWeave is itself an unprofitable, highly-leveraged company whose own filings state Core Scientific is ~100% of *its* HPC colocation supply — a mutual dependency that cuts both ways. Second, execution: operating Tier III/IV data centers with 99.99% uptime guarantees for hyperscalers is a fundamentally harder business than running mining containers, and the pipeline requires multibillion-dollar construction against transformer/switchgear supply-chain bottlenecks. Third, capital intensity: 2026 capex is ~$2B, funded with a new $3.3B project bond at 7.75% — high rates compress returns and pile on leverage against a company with negative book equity. Fourth, the pipeline beyond CoreWeave is *uncontracted* — if new tenant deals don't materialize, the 3 GW story is just empty land and the stock is overvalued. Fifth, GPU/AI overcapacity: if hyperscalers overbuild their own data centers, third-party landlords like Core Scientific get squeezed.

**How to think about it.** This is an *infrastructure landlord riding the AI build-out* — closer to a specialized data-center REIT than a software company — that emerged from bankruptcy in 2024 and is converting a stranded Bitcoin-mining asset base into contracted AI colocation. It sits in the "Bitcoin-miner-to-AI-datacenter pivot" cohort (peers: IREN, WULF, HUT, RIOT, CIFR). The thesis is no longer "cheapest asset in the cohort" — after a 132% twelve-month run and large share dilution, it has re-rated; the question now is whether the *uncontracted* pipeline fills with new investment-grade tenants fast enough to justify a price that already credits more than the CoreWeave contract alone.

## Situation Summary

The defining event is that CoreWeave's $9B all-stock bid to acquire Core Scientific was **rejected by Core Scientific shareholders on 2025-10-30** (203M shares against vs. 21M for) and the merger was terminated. Activist Two Seas Capital (still a top-7 holder at ~5%) led the revolt, arguing the fixed exchange ratio undervalued Core Scientific's low-cost power, data-center expertise, and pipeline. Core Scientific is therefore now a **standalone company** again, free of CoreWeave's exclusivity. Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-06) showed the transition inflecting: revenue $115.2M (colocation $77.5M now covering operating costs), 243 MW fully billing to CoreWeave, but a $347M net loss driven by a $266.5M non-cash impairment and a $30.8M warrant/CVR revaluation; adjusted EBITDA was barely positive at $4.4M. The company closed a $3.3B project bond at 7.75% (~$2.9B net) to fund a ~$2B 2026 capex plan, and is targeting 450 MW billing by summer and 590 MW by early 2027. The Wall Street narrative is bullish (strong_buy, 14 analysts, mean target ~$29.8) and the stock trades near its 52-week high after a 132% one-year run.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Bullish — analysts rate it strong_buy with a ~$29.8 mean target (~22% upside). The market narrative is "independent CORZ is now free to fill its 3 GW pipeline with multiple hyperscalers at peer EV/MW, the CoreWeave rejection unlocked value, and the project bond de-risks the buildout." The thesis cohort treats it as the executing value play.
- **Our view:** The "deep value at $4M/MW" framing that justified the original thesis is **stale and largely closed**. After the 132% run plus heavy dilution (basic 318M → fully-diluted ~505M shares including 187M from warrants/converts/awards), fully-diluted EV is ~$13.5B. The contracted CoreWeave book (590 MW, ~$850M annualized revenue, ~$570M full-ramp EBITDA at the 82.5% midpoint, reached only in early 2027) supports roughly $11-15/share at 12-15x EV/EBITDA. To reach today's $24 you must either pay ~25x for the contracted cash flow *or* credit the entire ~3 GW pipeline at peer multiples — i.e. the market is already pricing in tenant wins that are not yet signed. The variant perception is therefore **bearish-vs-consensus on valuation**: the stock now needs the *uncontracted* pipeline to convert, against an unprofitable, mutually-dependent anchor tenant and 7.75% debt — the asset-value margin of safety the thesis was built on is gone.
- **Trigger:** A signed new-hyperscaler colocation contract (re-rates up) OR a CoreWeave covenant/cash stress event or a quarter where the uncontracted pipeline visibly stalls (re-rates down). Next dateable: Q2 2026 earnings (~early Aug 2026) and Muskogee regulatory approval (expected Q3 2026).

## Inflection Point

**Yes — an operational inflection is underway and observable.** Colocation revenue ($77.5M in Q1'26) has crossed the level where it covers operating costs; adjusted EBITDA turned positive (+$4.4M); 243 MW is fully billing with a clear path to 450 MW by summer and 590 MW by early 2027. The mining-revenue trough (FY2025 -37.5%) is being replaced by contracted colocation. This is the "profitability inflection as operating leverage kicks in" pattern. **However**, the *valuation* has already run ahead of the inflection — the stock is +132% over twelve months and near its 52-week high, so the easy re-rating from "distressed miner" to "AI landlord" has largely happened. The remaining upside requires a *second* inflection (new tenants on the uncontracted pipeline) that is not yet evidenced by signed contracts.

## Bull Case

1. **Pre-energized capacity is the scarce asset** — ~1.3 GW grid-connected today, 3 GW pipeline; interconnection queues of 5-7yr make this hard to replicate, and AI power demand is structurally short.
2. **Contracted, take-or-pay revenue** — ~$10B / 12yr from CoreWeave at 80-85% gross margin once 590 MW fully bills (early 2027), with the $3.3B project bond lockbox securing those cash flows.
3. **Exclusivity expired = optionality unlocked** — free to sign additional hyperscalers; management says three are in active discussions for Pecos/Muskogee. Any signed deal re-rates the uncontracted pipeline from option to revenue.
4. **Independence preserved at a premium** — shareholders rejected a $9B buyout judged too cheap; the standalone path lets them capture the pipeline value CoreWeave wanted for itself.
5. **Operational inflection is real and visible** — colocation covering opex, adj EBITDA positive, MW billing ramping on schedule.

## Bear Case

1. **Valuation has closed the gap** — the original "$4M/MW deep value" thesis is stale. Fully-diluted EV ~$13.5B requires ~25x EV/EBITDA on contracted cash flow *or* paying for unsigned pipeline. Margin of safety is gone.
2. **Mutual single-customer dependency** — ~100% of colo revenue is CoreWeave, and CoreWeave's own filings say Core Scientific is ~100% of *its* HPC colo supply. CoreWeave is unprofitable and highly leveraged; any covenant/cash stress there directly threatens CORZ's cash flow and the bond lockbox.
3. **Uncontracted pipeline is the swing factor and it's unsigned** — most of the 3 GW is land + load studies, not revenue. The market is pricing tenant wins that haven't happened. **(Under-covered risk vs. headlines:** management itself flagged that exclusivity *expired* — bullishly framed in news as "free to sign others," but it equally means CoreWeave is no longer obligated to take new capacity, leaving CORZ to find demand in a market where hyperscalers increasingly self-build.)**
4. **Heavy dilution overhang** — fully-diluted share count ~505M (187M from warrants/converts/equity awards) vs. 318M basic; per-share value is structurally diluted ~37% from the headline.
5. **High-cost leverage on a pre-profit base** — $3.3B bond at 7.75%, negative book equity, FCF -$461M, current ratio 0.55. Rising power costs (~$51/MWh, +8.5% in 2026) and transformer lead times pressure the buildout.

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

The operational story is genuinely inflecting (colocation covering opex, adjusted EBITDA positive, MW billing ramping on schedule), and the energized-power asset is real and scarce. But the variant perception is that the valuation has already closed the deep-value gap the original thesis was built on: at ~$24 with ~505M fully-diluted shares, the price requires either ~25x EV/EBITDA on the contracted CoreWeave book (which doesn't fully ramp until early 2027) or that the *unsigned* 3 GW pipeline gets credited at peer multiples. Expected value is only ~+5%, with a -47% bear leg anchored on a mutually-dependent, unprofitable single tenant and 23% short interest. That is not an attractive risk/reward at the 52-week high.

**If WATCH:** Would upgrade to BUY on (a) a pullback to ~$15-17 (where the contracted book alone provides support and the EV/MW is back toward deep-value), or (b) a signed new investment-grade hyperscaler contract on the uncontracted pipeline that converts optionality into revenue — at which point paying up for the pipeline becomes justified rather than speculative. Existing holders sitting on the 132% run: reasonable to trim into strength near the 52-week high given the thin EV and high short interest.
