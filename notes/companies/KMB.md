# Kimberly-Clark Corporation (KMB)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Household & Personal Products (Huggies, Kleenex, Scott, Cottonelle, Kotex, Depend, Poise; tissue + personal care)
**Price:** $104.28 (2026-06-16) | **Market Cap:** ~$34.6B | **Shares:** ~332M | **EV (standalone):** ~$41B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-17
**Standalone rating: WATCH** (cheap defensive Dividend King at a ~14x trough multiple and 5% yield, but a transformative, dilutive, litigation-laden $48.7B Kenvue merger has turned it into a special situation — lean BUY LOW near the 52-week low ~$92-95)

## Situation Summary
Kimberly-Clark is a global tissue and personal-care staple (Huggies diapers, Kleenex, Scott, Cottonelle, Kotex/U by Kotex, Depend/Poise adult care). The stock has fallen **~24% from its 52-week high ($137.46) to $104.28**, sitting just ~13% above its 52-week low ($92.42). On the surface it screens as a defensive bargain: **~13.8x forward earnings, a 4.96% dividend yield from a 53-year Dividend King, and ~13% net margins**. KMB rarely trades this cheap — its long-run multiple is ~18-20x.

The cheapness is not random — it dates almost entirely to **November 3, 2025**, when KMB announced it would acquire **Kenvue** (the J&J consumer-health spin-off: Tylenol, Listerine, Neutrogena, Aveeno, Band-Aid, Zyrtec) in a **~$48.7B cash-and-stock deal** ($40B equity). The market hated it: KMB dropped ~15% on the news and has stayed depressed. This is less an "acquisition" than a near-merger — post-close KMB holders own **54%** of the combined company, former Kenvue holders **46%**, meaning KMB is issuing **~281M new shares (~85% dilution)** plus ~$6.7B cash. Both shareholder bases approved overwhelmingly (96%+ KMB) on 2026-01-29; the deal is now in an **FTC second-request review with closing targeted for H2 2026**.

So KMB today is two assets in one ticker: (1) a stable, cash-generative tissue/personal-care franchise worth ~$115-125 standalone, and (2) a contentious bet that bolting on Kenvue's struggling, litigation-exposed consumer-health portfolio creates value. The ~$104 price implies the market is assigning **negative value (~$10-20/share) to the Kenvue deal.**

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "KMB overpaid ~$48.7B at a premium for a deteriorating Kenvue (organic declines in skin health, a Tylenol franchise under political/legal attack), voluntarily inheriting **Tylenol-autism and talc litigation tail risk**, while diluting holders ~85% and levering the balance sheet to ~3.5-4x to do it. A boring tissue compounder just blew up its identity. Avoid — value trap."
- **Our view (partial pushback):** The **standalone tissue/personal-care business alone is worth roughly the current price or more** (~$7.5 forward EPS × 16x ≈ $120), so at $104 you are being *paid* to take the deal optionality rather than paying up for it. If the merger merely *doesn't destroy value* — synergies of $2.1B run-rate land even partially, litigation gets capped via settlement — the combined entity earns ~$9-10 adjusted EPS on ~613M shares and re-rates from ~11x toward a normal staples 14-15x. Meanwhile you collect a ~5% Dividend-King yield management has explicitly committed to protect. The risk is real but it is *compensated* here in a way it was not at $137.
- **Why it is NOT a clear buy yet:** The Tylenol-autism litigation is a genuinely unquantifiable, politically amplified tail (the Trump administration publicly linked Tylenol to autism in late 2025; US Tylenol sales fell ~11% in two weeks). Talc claims linger too. J&J spent **$10B+** resolving talc — a comparable outcome would swamp the synergy math. Layer on ~85% dilution and integration risk on a $40B deal, and the left tail is fat. This is a special situation, not a clean compounder.
- **Trigger / what re-rates it:** (1) **FTC clears the deal cleanly** (tissue + consumer-health have minimal overlap, so clearance is likely — removes the binary), (2) the **first one or two integration quarters show synergies on track** and Kenvue organic growth stabilizing, and (3) a **litigation settlement framework** that caps Tylenol/talc exposure. Any two of these and a re-rate from ~11x toward 14x on ~$9.5 EPS is +35-45%.

## Bull / Bear

**Bull**
- **Standalone downside floor:** even if you assign zero value to the deal, the tissue/personal-care franchise at ~$7.5 forward EPS × 16x ≈ $120 — *above* the current price. You're paid to wait.
- **Dividend King at a 5% yield:** 53 consecutive years of increases; ~68% payout on forward adjusted EPS; management explicitly committed to maintaining the dividend through the integration. Strong income floor.
- **Cheap multiple:** ~13.8x forward vs. ~18-20x historical and vs. staples peers — most of the de-rate is deal-fear, not fundamental deterioration.
- **Real synergy + scale story:** $2.1B run-rate synergies on a ~$32B combined revenue base; broader consumer-health/wellness platform with global distribution overlap.
- **FTC clearance likely:** tissue and OTC consumer-health barely overlap, so the regulatory binary probably resolves favorably, removing an overhang.

**Bear**
- **Tylenol-autism litigation is an unquantifiable, politically charged tail** — the sitting administration publicly endorsed the autism link; US Tylenol sales dropped ~11% in two weeks. A talc-style outcome (J&J paid $10B+) would dwarf synergies.
- **~85% dilution + ~3.5-4x leverage:** holders are diluted nearly in half and the balance sheet goes from pristine to stretched; deleveraging freezes buybacks and caps dividend *growth* for years.
- **Kenvue is itself struggling:** organic declines in skin health/beauty (Neutrogena), management turnover, and a brand (Tylenol) under active reputational assault. KMB may be buying a fixer-upper at a premium.
- **Integration risk on a $40B near-merger:** different culture (pharma-adjacent consumer health vs. tissue), the synergy/timeline is management's optimistic case, and Morningstar flagged "sizable integration risk," raising its uncertainty rating to High.
- **Loss of identity / forced holders:** index and "boring tissue compounder" holders may sell the transformed entity, a technical overhang.

## Verdict — WATCH (lean BUY LOW)
A genuinely cheap, high-yield Dividend King with real standalone downside protection (~$120 floor), wrapped in a contentious, dilutive, litigation-laden $48.7B merger that the market is pricing as value-destructive. At **$104** the risk/reward is roughly balanced with a fat left tail — a WATCH, not a buy-now. The setup gets *attractive* near the **52-week low (~$92-95)**, where the ~5% yield and standalone floor better compensate the Kenvue/litigation risk, or after a **de-risking catalyst** (clean FTC clearance + one solid integration quarter + a litigation-cap framework).

- **Entry (start buying):** ≤ ~$95 (near 52-wk low), or higher on a confirmed de-risking catalyst.
- **What would change my mind to BUY:** FTC clears unconditionally + first post-close quarter shows synergies on track and Kenvue organic stabilizing + a Tylenol/talc settlement framework that caps exposure.
- **Thesis-break (value-trap confirmation):** a dividend cut, net leverage stuck >3x past 2027, an adverse Tylenol-autism bellwether verdict, or sustained break below ~$85 on deteriorating fundamentals.
