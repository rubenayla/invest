# Centene Corporation (CNC)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Managed Care (Medicaid + ACA Marketplace–centric government insurer)
**Price:** $63.68 (2026-06-22) | **Market Cap:** $31.4B | **Book Value/sh:** $43.40 (P/B 1.47)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-22
**Standalone rating: WATCH (lean PASS) — the $25 dislocation has fully round-tripped; at $63.68 near the 52-wk high you pay ~14x for a government-dependent insurer walking into a scheduled ACA-subsidy headwind**

## Situation Summary
Centene is the largest U.S. Medicaid managed-care organization and the largest ACA Marketplace (exchange) insurer, with ~$190B of revenue that is **~100% government-paid** (Medicaid, Medicare, ACA exchanges). It is the lowest-margin, highest-government-risk name in the managed-care group.

The stock is a completed crisis round-trip. In **July 2025** Centene **pulled its full-year guidance** when its ACA Marketplace risk-adjustment true-up came in catastrophically against it (its membership was healthier-relative-to-market than priced, so it owed money into the risk pool) on top of Medicaid medical-cost trend running ahead of state rates. The stock collapsed from ~$60 to a **$25.08 52-week low**. FY2025 closed with a **GAAP net loss of −$6.67B** (−$13.05 EPS) on $194.8B revenue — the disaster year.

Since then it has recovered all of it: $26 (Jul-25) → $33 (Mar-26 second dip) → **$63.68 today, just under the $66.55 52-wk high (+154% off the low).** The recovery is driven by (1) Medicaid margins normalizing as states finally grant catch-up rate increases — Q1-26 Medicaid HBR 93.1%, −50bps y/y — and (2) a composite **+4.5% Medicaid rate increase** for 2026. Q1-26 adjusted EPS was **$3.37** (a swing to ~$1.5B profit), and management raised FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance to **">$3.40"** — a deliberately conservative floor that implies a near-breakeven back half as the ACA book shrinks.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus / bull view:** "Trough is behind us. Medicaid margins are recovering on rate catch-up, Q1 already beat, and the franchise scale ($190B revenue, #1 in Medicaid) is intact. Normalized EPS power rebuilds toward $6+ → a low-teens multiple gives a mid-$70s+ stock." Sell-side is "buy," 17 analysts, but mean target **$61.35 — slightly BELOW the current $63.68.**
- **Our view:** **The asymmetry has inverted.** The free money was buying the July-2025 to March-2026 dislocation at $25–35. At $63.68 you are back to pre-crisis levels paying **~14.3x consensus 2026 EPS ($4.45)** and ~18x the company's own ">$3.40" floor — full multiples — right as the single largest scheduled headwind hits: the **ACA enhanced premium tax credits expired at end-2025.** Centene is the most exchange-exposed insurer in the country, and management itself guides to a **~35%+ industry-wide ACA enrollment decline** and is losing **~2 million Marketplace members** in 2026. Marketplace was Centene's biggest recent growth engine; it is now shrinking by design. You are paying a full price for a business whose growth driver is in structural decline, offset only by a Medicaid-margin recovery that is already well-telegraphed and partly priced.
- **Tell:** the analyst mean target sits *below* the price after a 154% rally — the same "stock has run past its own bulls" signature as [[HUM]] and [[EL]]. The fat left tail ($25 just twelve months ago) is fresh, not theoretical.

## Valuation — normalized earnings, not the trough

- Pre-crisis normalized EPS was **~$6.40 (2024)**, with management's old long-term target ~$7.50. Both are now stale: the ACA subsidy expiry permanently shrinks the highest-growth book, so a realistic **normalized 2027–28 EPS is ~$5.25–6.25**, not $7.50.
- Managed care trades **~10-13x** forward post-2024 sector de-rate; Centene, as the lowest-quality/most-government-exposed name, deserves the **low end (~10-12x).**
- On normalized **$5.75 × 11x ≈ $63** — i.e. today's price already fully credits the recovery. On the conservative 2026 floor (>$3.40) the stock is ~18x; on consensus $4.45 it is 14.3x. None of these is cheap.
- Downside anchor: book value $43.40. In another risk-adjustment or Medicaid-rate-pushback shock, the stock would compress toward ~1.0x book (~$43) or worse — exactly the regime it traded in twelve months ago.

## Rating rationale
- **WATCH, leaning PASS.** Not a short — the Medicaid recovery is genuine and scale is a real moat — but the risk/reward is unattractive *at this price.* You are buying near the 52-week high, after a 154% rally, into a known, scheduled ACA-subsidy headwind, at ~14x with a fresh memory of a $25 print.
- **Where it gets interesting:** below **~$45** (≈8x normalized EPS, ~1.0x book) the asymmetry flips back to favorable — that is the BUY-LOW zone, not $64.
- **Thesis-break / what would change my mind (to bullish):** Medicaid HBR continuing to fall toward ~88-89% *and* effectuated ACA enrollment data (summer 2026) showing the membership loss is materially less than the −35% feared, with no new risk-adjustment surprise. Either would lift normalized EPS toward the bull case.
- **Thesis-break (to more bearish):** Medicaid HBR re-accelerating above ~94%, a second ACA risk-adjustment true-up against them, or state pushback that holds 2027 rates below medical-cost trend. Any of these re-opens the path back toward book.
- **Catalyst:** Q2-26 earnings (late July 2026) plus the summer 2026 effectuated-enrollment release that reveals the true depth of the ACA membership loss — the next binary.
