# Merck & Co. (MRK)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Pharmaceuticals (large-cap, oncology-led)
**Price:** $114.70 (2026-06-03 close) | **Market Cap:** ~$288B (~2.51B shares)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-04
**Verdict:** BUY (starter / scale-in) — a wide-moat pharma at a **decade-low ~13x normalized earnings** with a ~3.0% dividend, marked down because Keytruda (~46% of sales) loses US exclusivity in late 2028. The optical "23x" you see on screens is an artifact: 2026 non-GAAP EPS guidance ($5.04–5.16) **includes a one-time $3.62/share Cidara charge** that Merck (unusually) leaves inside non-GAAP — normalized 2026 earnings power is ~$8.7, roughly flat vs 2025's $8.98. ## Business Primer

**What they do.** Merck is a top-3 global pharma. The crown jewel is **Keytruda** (pembrolizumab), the world's best-selling drug at a ~$32B annual run-rate (Q1'26 $8.03B, +12% YoY), an anti-PD-1 immuno-oncology backbone used across dozens of cancer indications. Around it: a fast-scaling launch portfolio (Winrevair, Welireg, Capvaxive, Enflonsia), the Gardasil HPV-vaccine franchise, a large Animal Health business (~$6B), and a deep oncology/cardiometabolic/vaccine pipeline.

**Why it's a wide moat.** Patent-protected biologics with entrenched clinical-guideline positions, an oncology salesforce and trial infrastructure that are extremely hard to replicate, and pricing power inside the world's most defensible drug category. Free cash flow runs ~$18–20B/yr, funding a growing dividend (~$3.40/yr, raised annually) and serial business development.

**Why it's cheap (the reason for the de-rating).** Two overhangs:
1. **The Keytruda cliff.** US composition-of-matter exclusivity lapses **late 2028**. Keytruda is ~46–48% of total revenue, so the market fears a revenue air-pocket in 2029–2031 that the pipeline can't fully backfill — the classic "pharma patent cliff" de-rating.
2. **Gardasil China.** HPV-vaccine shipments to China were paused/cut on channel-inventory glut, knocking a previously-reliable growth engine and souring sentiment through 2025.
Layer on macro pharma fears (US drug-pricing/IRA Medicare negotiation, threatened pharma import tariffs) and the multiple compressed to the low end of its decade range.

## Situation Summary

MRK sits at **$114.70**, in the lower third of its multi-year range, having de-rated from the ~$130s. The numbers say the franchise is healthy *today* — the discount is entirely about 2028+:

- **Q1'26 beat:** revenue $16.3B (vs ~$15.9B est). Headline non-GAAP EPS was a **$(1.28) loss**, but only because of a **$3.62/share Cidara acquisition charge** booked inside non-GAAP — back it out and underlying quarterly EPS was ~+$2.3.
- **FY26 guidance:** revenue **$65.8–67.0B (+1–3%)**; non-GAAP EPS **$5.04–5.16 as reported**, i.e. **~$8.66–8.78 normalized** (adding back the Cidara charge), essentially flat vs 2025's **$8.98**.
- **Launches are inflecting into the cliff:** **Winrevair** (sotatercept, pulmonary arterial hypertension) $525M, **+88%** — run-rate ~$2.1B, Street peak $5–7B; **Welireg** $199M (+43%); **Capvaxive** (pneumococcal vaccine) $142M (+31%); plus Enflonsia (RSV) launching.
- **Valuation:** $114.70 / ~$8.7 normalized = **~13.2x**, vs a historical ~14–17x and peer large-pharma ~14–16x. Dividend ~$3.40 → **~3.0% yield**, ~39% payout on normalized EPS (very safe).
- **Street:** median target ~$130 (range $100–150); 18 Buy / 11 Hold / 0 Sell of 39 analysts.

The precise debate: *does the launch portfolio + subcutaneous-Keytruda conversion + pipeline cushion the 2028 cliff enough to keep normalized EPS from collapsing — and if so, is ~13x with a 3% yield too cheap for the franchise?*

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** A melting ice cube. Keytruda is ~half of revenue and ~half of profit; when US biosimilars arrive in 2029 the company could shed double-digit-billions of high-margin sales, and no single pipeline asset replaces a $30B+ drug. Cheap for a reason; dead money until the cliff resolves.
- **Our view:** The cliff is real but the bear case over-extrapolates a *gradual, partly-defendable* erosion into an *overnight collapse*. Three things blunt it: (1) **Keytruda QLEX**, the patent-protected subcutaneous formulation, is being pushed to convert ~30–40% of IV volume *before* LOE — that share is defended past 2028, and method-of-use/manufacturing patents extend into 2029; (2) US is only part of global Keytruda, and ex-US timing differs; (3) the **launch + pipeline stack is unusually deep** — Winrevair alone could add ~$4–5B, plus Welireg, Capvaxive, Enflonsia, oral PCSK9 **enlicitide** (potential multi-billion cholesterol drug, Phase 3), I-DXd, and Animal Health (spinoff-optionable). Meanwhile you are paying a *decade-low* multiple on *normalized* earnings while the screen shows a misleading charge-inflated 23x. You get a safe 3% dividend and ~$18B+ FCF (ample BD firepower) to bridge the gap.
- **Trigger / what re-rates it:** A dense 2026 catalyst calendar — Welireg RCC decisions (Jun 19 & Oct 4), Keytruda+Padcev bladder (Aug 17), I-DXd small-cell lung (Oct 10), Winrevair label expansion (Sep 21) — plus a Gardasil-China stabilization and any needle-moving BD deal. Each de-risks the post-2028 revenue base and argues for re-rating toward 15–16x. The mirror risk that breaks it: subcutaneous conversion disappoints, biosimilar erosion is faster/deeper than modeled, or Gardasil China stays structurally impaired.

## Entry, Thesis-Break, and What Would Change My Mind

- **Entry:** Starter at ~$115; scale-in on weakness toward **$100–105** (~11–12x normalized, where the dividend yield approaches 3.3% and most of the cliff is arguably priced).
- **Thesis-break (sell/avoid):** a sustained close **below ~$85** on *fundamental* deterioration — i.e. subcutaneous-Keytruda conversion clearly failing AND a key pipeline readout (enlicitide / I-DXd / Welireg) disappointing — would mean the cliff is winning and the pipeline isn't backfilling. Price alone below $100 without those is an *add*, not a break.
- **What would raise conviction to BUY scale-in (A):** (a) Keytruda QLEX conversion tracking >35% with durable pricing; (b) Winrevair clearly on a $5B+ trajectory; (c) a positive enlicitide Phase 3; (d) Gardasil-China restocking. Two-plus of these and the 2029 trough looks shallow → re-rate justified.
- **What would change my mind to PASS:** evidence that biosimilar Keytruda erosion will mirror the steep small-molecule cliffs (e.g. >50% in year one) with subcutaneous conversion stalling, leaving a true earnings air-pocket the pipeline can't fill until the mid-2030s.

## Bottom Line

A high-quality, cash-generative pharma at a **decade-low ~13x on normalized earnings** with a safe ~3% dividend, where the screen's optical 23x is a charge artifact most quote services don't normalize. The Keytruda 2028 cliff is real and keeps this a *starter*, not a max-conviction buy — but you're paid to wait, the launch portfolio is inflecting hard, and the 2026 catalyst calendar is dense. **B-grade: BUY starter / scale-in.**
