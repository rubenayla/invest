## 2) Medicaid redetermination tail — still bleeding

Attrition guide raised from 2% → 6%, year-end membership ~4.5M (from ~4.8M). Six points on ~$36B of FY Medicaid premium is **~$2B of revenue evaporating**, partially offset by the healthier-cost-trend cohort exiting first (favorable mix in Q1 cost data). But the rest of 2026 carries adverse selection: state actuaries lag in acuity-adjusting capitation, so MCR pressure from sicker remaining members hits Q2-Q3 before July rate resets catch up. This is why the FY guide is worse than Q1.

## 3) Marketplace — the unhedged tail risk

ACA enhanced premium tax credits expired Jan 1 2026 (the FY2022 reconciliation sunset). Average subsidized premiums going from $888 → $1,904. MOH already cut its 2026 service area by ~20% and raised prices ~30%. Marketplace membership guide: 305K → 250K by year-end (~18% shrinkage on top of footprint cuts). **Marketplace is roughly 7% of premium today and falling.** Bull side: MOH is de-risking voluntarily. Bear side: if the 2026 reconciliation package doesn't restore enhanced PTCs, the segment shrinks further into 2027 and the 79.5% MCR doesn't survive adverse selection (the people who keep paying triple are sick).

## 5) Lawsuit

Hindlemann v. Molina, C.D. Cal. 2:25-cv-09461. Class period Feb 5 – Jul 23 2025. Allegation: misrepresented medical cost trend assumptions, concealed rate/cost mismatch, attributed near-term growth to "unusually low" use of behavioral health, pharmacy, inpatient/outpatient. Stock dropped 16.84% on July 24 2025 print. Lead plaintiff deadline Dec 2 2025. **Read:** this is more substantive than boilerplate — they're alleging specific disclosure inadequacy on cost trend, which is the exact issue management has since acknowledged. But the damages window (a stock that's already down from $360 → $182) limits settlement size to plausibly <$200M, immaterial to the thesis. Real risk is if discovery surfaces evidence of internal warnings ignored in 1H 2025 — that opens the door to executive turnover, not bankruptcy.
