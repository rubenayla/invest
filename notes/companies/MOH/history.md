# MOH history

Dated log: catalysts hit, surprises, thesis revisions. Newest first.

## 2026-04-28 — Risks/opportunities cataloged from MCR research

Risks to the rate-cycle timing call:
- Medicaid attrition guide tripled at Q1 2026 (2% → 6%): revenue mix risk if
  redetermination headwind persists into 2H 2026 even as MCR improves.
- ELV and UNH Q1 2026 commentary still flag elevated Medicaid trend / negative
  Medicaid segment margins through 2026 — MOH's clean Q1 print may be ahead
  of the sector, not the sector average.
- State politics: DOGE-era federal FMAP cuts could force states to underfund
  their Medicaid share, squeezing managed-care capitation rates.
- Risk-adjustment under-credit: as redeterminations remove healthy members,
  the remaining pool is sicker. If state actuaries don't fully credit this,
  MCR stays high even with headline rate increases.
- Cost-trend persistence: GLP-1s, behavioral health, hospital labor — even
  +5% rate hikes don't close the gap if trend doesn't decelerate.
- Carrier-mix shift: states can re-allocate contract share to lower bidders
  (rate up but membership down).

Opportunities (lag → mispricing):
- State Medicaid rates lag claims by 12-18 months by construction (actuaries
  use prior-year data). The market consistently treats trough earnings as
  run-rate, so the recovery is mechanically forecastable from the rate cycle.
- MOH peak Q4 2025 → rates re-priced July 2026 → MCR should normalize by
  mid-2027 → stock re-rates 2027-28. ~2-3 year setup.
- Operating leverage: every 100bps MCR improvement ≈ $4.50 EPS. The stock
  trades at trough P/E on trough EPS; both compress on recovery, which is
  why historical recoveries deliver 100%+ moves.
- Leading indicators in order of when they arrive:
  1. Sector cost trend (peer Q1/Q2 prints) — already softening.
  2. State actuarial reports (often public).
  3. **Preliminary July rate notices (May-June 2026)** — the real "tell".
  4. July 1, 2026 effective rate increases.
  5. Q3 2026 earnings — when the market actually believes it.
- Most of the price move happens between #3 and #5. Currently between #1
  and #3.

## 2026-04-23 — Q1 2026 earnings: thesis on track

- Adj EPS $2.35 (beat $2.17 cons). Stock +11% on day, +17% intra-week.
- Consolidated MCR 91.1%; Medicaid 92.0%; Medicare 89.8%; Marketplace 84.0%
  (~79.5% ex-prior-year items).
- 250bps better than Q4 2025's 94.6%. Well below the 93% thesis-break
  threshold for Q1.
- Reaffirmed $42B premium / $5+ EPS guide.
- One smudge: Medicaid attrition guide raised from 2% → 6% (continuing
  redetermination headwind), offset on cost this quarter.
- Sell-side moves post-print:
  - Morgan Stanley → PT $146 (EW), 2027E EPS lifted to $9.00
  - Mizuho → PT $195
  - Barclays → PT $161 (still UW, 12x 2027E)
  - Consensus PT still ~$144 — Street has not capitulated to the bull case.
- Sources: investors.molinahealthcare.com Q1 2026 release; Investing.com
  earnings call transcript; IBTimes / Yahoo coverage of stock reaction.

## 2026-04-06 — CMS CY2027 MA Final Rate Notice: +2.48%

- Net rate +2.48% (~$13B), vs +0.09% advance notice from Feb 2026.
- CMS did not adopt full risk-model changes.
- Less direct relevance for MOH (exiting MAPD by 2027), but signals
  regulator is responding to industry pressure — read-through positive
  for the broader managed-care rate cycle.
- Sources: AHA news; Crowell client alert.

## Upcoming catalysts (watch list)

- **May-June 2026** — Some states publish preliminary Medicaid rate
  notices ahead of July 1 effective date. Earliest tell on whether the
  rate cycle delivers the 4-6% renewals the thesis assumes.
- **Late Apr 2026** — MOH 10-Q filing (segment detail, reserve commentary).
- **July 1, 2026** — State Medicaid rate renewals (FL, CA, TX). The
  original thesis trigger. Bear-case threshold: <3% increases combined
  with Q1 MCR >93% — the latter is already off the table.
- **Late July 2026** — MOH Q2 2026 earnings. First clean quarter to test
  sequential MCR improvement and any updated 2027 framing.
- **Sep-Oct 2026** — MA OEP positioning data (peer-relevant); 2027 state
  Medicaid rate previews.
- **Q3 2026 earnings (Oct/Nov 2026)** — first full quarter under new
  July rates. The print where the market typically starts believing.
