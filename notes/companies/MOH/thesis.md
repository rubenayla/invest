## Situation Summary

Molina Healthcare is a Medicaid-focused managed care company trading at $142, down 60% from its 52-week high of $360, after a catastrophic Q4 2025 ($2.75 adjusted loss vs. expected profit) driven by medical care ratio spiking to 94.6%. Management has guided 2026 as the "trough year" with adjusted EPS of at least $5 (down from $11.03 in FY2025), burdened by $2.50/share of one-time drags ($1.50 from new Florida CMS Medicaid contract implementation, $1.00 from Medicare Advantage underperformance). The company is exiting Medicare Advantage Part D by 2027 and focusing on its core Medicaid/Marketplace franchise. The entire Medicaid managed care sector is under pressure from rate-trend imbalance, with 2026 widely viewed as the industry margin trough before rate restoration in 2027.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Wall Street rates MOH a "Hold" (75% hold, 17% buy). Mean price target $151 -- essentially flat from here. The market sees a company stuck in a margin crisis with uncertain recovery timing, burdened by the Florida contract startup and Medicare exit costs. Consensus 2026 EPS ~$5 implies a ~28x forward P/E, which looks expensive on trough earnings.
- **Our view:** The market is pricing MOH on trough 2026 earnings and failing to look through to normalized 2027-2028 profitability. Key mispricing: (1) Management says only 130bps of Medicaid rate improvement is needed to restore 4.5% target margin, and each 100bp MCR improvement = ~$4.50 incremental EPS. (2) The $2.50/share of one-time burdens (Florida + MAPD) drops off in 2027. (3) Normalized EPS of $15-20 (management's embedded earnings trajectory) on the current share count puts the stock at 7-10x normalized earnings. (4) The company has $4.2B cash, a 1.7x current ratio, and survived the 2016 Medicaid crisis -- this is not a balance sheet risk.
- **Trigger:** July 2026 Medicaid rate renewals (FL, CA, TX) showing 4-6% increases that close the rate-trend gap; Q1/Q2 2026 earnings showing MCR stabilization below 92%; April 2026 final CMS Medicare Advantage rates potentially better than the 0.09% advance notice.

## Inflection Point

**YES -- this is a classic cyclical trough with identifiable recovery catalysts.** The inflection thesis:

1. **Rate-trend gap closing:** Medicaid rates lagged medical cost inflation by 12-18 months. July 2026 renewals should bring 4-6% rate increases vs. 5% cost trend. Management says only 130bps of improvement needed to hit 4.5% target margin.
2. **One-time burdens falling away:** The $2.50/share drag (Florida implementation + MAPD) is a 2026-only headwind. By 2027, Florida should be contributing positively, and MAPD will be exited.
3. **Embedded earnings:** Management has identified $4-6/share of incremental EPS from new contracts commencing 2027-2029.
4. **Observable evidence:** Insider buying at $125 (Feb 2026). Management's confident "trough year" framing. Industry peers (CNC, ELV) also calling bottom.
5. **Historical precedent:** Molina went through a similar crisis in 2016-2017 (marketplace losses, ACA uncertainty). Stock fell 60%, then recovered 400%+ over the next 3 years as margins normalized.

**Timing:** We are NOT trying to buy the absolute bottom. The stock bottomed at $121 in early March. The recovery has begun ($142 now). The risk of being early is mitigated by the net cash balance sheet.

## Bull Case
- Medicaid rate increases of 5%+ in July 2026 renewals close the rate-trend gap, MCR normalizes to 88-89% by mid-2027
- Normalized EPS of $18-20 by 2028 (management's embedded earnings path), stock re-rates to 15x = $270-300
- Florida CMS contract (large new state) becomes a significant earnings contributor by 2027
- $1B+ annual buyback capacity at depressed prices accelerates per-share earnings recovery
- S&P 500 re-inclusion as earnings normalize creates index fund buying pressure

## Bear Case
- Medical cost inflation accelerates further (GLP-1 drugs, behavioral health), MCR stays above 92% through 2027
- State Medicaid budgets are under political pressure (DOGE/federal spending cuts), rate increases fall short of actuarial need
- Florida contract implementation costs exceed $1.50/share estimate, or the contract underperforms
- Further membership attrition as states continue aggressive eligibility redeterminations
- Management's "trough year" call is premature -- what if 2027 is also a trough?
