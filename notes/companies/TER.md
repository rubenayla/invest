# Teradyne, Inc. (TER)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Semiconductor Equipment & Materials
**Price:** $303.92 | **Market Cap:** $47.6B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

Teradyne is riding an AI-driven supercycle in semiconductor test equipment, with Q4 2025 revenue up 45% YoY in its Semiconductor Test segment and AI now accounting for 60%+ of revenue (guided to 70% in Q1 2026). The stock has surged ~362% from its 52-week low of $65.77, pricing in massive forward earnings growth. Q1 2026 guidance of $1.15-1.25B in revenue (75% YoY jump) is a record. However, the robotics segment (Universal Robots) remains a headwind with ~10% revenue decline and layoffs, while the company faces ongoing China/tariff risks and Advantest competition in AI memory testing. The stock trades at 88x trailing earnings and 37x forward, reflecting consensus belief that AI test demand is durable and expanding.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Teradyne is an AI infrastructure winner with durable multi-year tailwinds. The test equipment duopoly (TER + Advantest) captures growing complexity in AI chip validation. Analysts see $6.30 EPS in 2026 with further upside. The market prices this as a high-growth compounder deserving a premium multiple.
- **Our view:** The consensus is largely correct on the direction but the valuation already prices in 2-3 years of perfect execution. At 37x forward earnings, the stock needs to deliver $8+ EPS to justify current prices on a reasonable 35x multiple, and any AI capex slowdown or share loss to Advantest would compress the multiple sharply. The robotics segment, while small (~15% of revenue), is a drag masking how expensive the core test business really is. Revenue CAGR over 3 years is only 0.4% -- the AI boom is recent, not a long track record.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (late April) will be the next major re-rating event. If revenue exceeds $1.25B and management raises full-year guidance, the stock breaks higher. If they flag any AI demand normalization, the premium compresses fast.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- AI test demand inflection is real and observable.** Q4 2025 was the breakout quarter with 45% YoY semi test growth. AI now 60-70% of revenue vs. likely <30% a year ago. This is not speculative -- the revenue is showing up. The question is duration and magnitude, not whether the inflection exists.

However, the stock price has already moved dramatically (+362% from 52-week low), pricing in much of this inflection. The easy money has been made. From here, returns depend on whether AI test demand ACCELERATES further or merely sustains.

## Bull Case

- AI chip complexity continues to drive test intensity higher (more test time per chip, more chip designs)
- Teradyne gains share from Advantest in AI memory testing (HBM4, next-gen DRAM)
- Robotics segment finally inflects to growth with new Detroit hub and manufacturing reshoring trend
- Revenue hits $5B+ in 2026 with 35%+ op margins, driving EPS above $8
- Multiple sustains at 35-40x as market treats TER as a structural AI winner

## Bear Case

- AI capex cycle normalizes in H2 2026 as hyperscalers digest capacity -- test equipment demand is inherently cyclical
- Advantest continues to win share in the highest-value AI test segments
- China export restrictions tighten further, reducing addressable market
- Robotics segment continues to bleed money and distract management
- At 88x trailing PE, any earnings miss triggers 20-30% correction as momentum reverses

## Verdict

**PASS** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

Teradyne is a high-quality business riding a real AI inflection, but the stock has already priced in an extremely optimistic scenario. At 88x trailing and 37x forward earnings, with a 0.9% FCF yield and 3yr revenue CAGR of just 0.4%, the risk/reward is unattractive. The expected value from our scenario analysis is -17%. The consensus is overwhelmingly bullish (80% buy ratings, 98%+ institutional ownership), leaving no margin for disappointment. The AI test thesis is valid, but the entry point is wrong.
