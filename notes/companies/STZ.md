# Constellation Brands, Inc. (STZ)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Alcoholic Beverages (beer-led)
**Price:** $135.40 (2026-06-04) | **Market Cap:** ~$23.3B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-04
**Call:** WATCH (lean PASS) — optically cheap (~11.7x guided-down EPS, near 52w low) but the core beer franchise has turned volume-negative, FY27 EPS is guided lower, and Berkshire just dumped 95% of its stake. The discount is a value trap until depletions inflect.

## Business Primer

**What they do.** Constellation is, in practice, a U.S. beer company with a shrinking wine/spirits tail. The crown jewel is its **exclusive U.S. rights to the Mexican-import beer portfolio** — Modelo Especial, Corona Extra/Premier, Pacifico, Victoria, and the Modelo Chelada line (rights acquired from AB InBev in the 2013 antitrust-forced divestiture, brewed in Mexico, sold only in the U.S.). Beer is ~85% of revenue and ~90%+ of operating profit, with best-in-class **~38% segment operating margins**. The **Wine & Spirits** segment is a secularly declining afterthought — Constellation sold its mainstream/value wine brands to **The Wine Group (and earlier Gallo)** to refocus on the premium tail (The Prisoner, Meiomi, Kim Crawford, High West, Casa Noble). FY2026 (ended Feb 2026) revenue was **$9.14B, down ~11% YoY** — most of the drop is the wine divestiture, but beer also stopped growing.

**Why it's (was) a moat.** Modelo Especial is the **#1 beer by dollar sales in the U.S.**, and Constellation's beer business was the category's #1 dollar-share gainer again in FY2026 (+0.4 share points, 4 of the top-15 share-gaining brands). The import rights are a genuine, legally-protected franchise: a competitor cannot replicate Modelo/Corona's brand equity or the Mexico-brewed authenticity. For a decade this drove high-single to low-double-digit volume growth with fat margins — a rare combination in staples.

**What broke.** Two things, one cyclical and one structural:
1. **The core Hispanic consumer pulled back.** Roughly half of Constellation's beer volume is bought by Hispanic consumers. Intensified U.S. immigration enforcement in 2025–2026 reduced on-premise (bar/restaurant) traffic, store visits, and discretionary spend in exactly this demographic. FY2026 beer **depletions fell 2.1%** (Modelo −3%, Corona Extra −7%), only partly offset by Pacifico (+15%) and Victoria (+16%), which are still too small to carry the franchise.
2. **Aluminum tariffs hit margins.** Mexican-brewed beer cans take a direct hit from 2025–2026 aluminum tariffs; FY2026 beer operating margin fell ~170bps to 38.0% on tariff COGS plus unfavorable fixed-cost absorption on lower volume.

**How to think about it.** This is a high-quality franchise being tested on whether its volume engine is *cyclically* stalled (immigration-fear air-pocket + tariff cost spike that both fade) or *structurally* impaired (demographic headwind persists, beer category matures, GLP-1/moderation chips at volumes). The stock is priced cheap, but cheap on *declining* forward earnings is a different bet than cheap on trough earnings. Compare the staples-at-a-low debate in sibling notes [DEO.md](DEO.md) (spirits, also volume-challenged) and [PEP.md](PEP.md).

## Situation Summary

STZ is at **$135.40, ~5% above its 52-week low ($126.45) and ~24% below its high ($178.14)** — a multi-year low, down from ~$260 in 2024. The bull setup that drew attention in 2024–2025 has materially deteriorated in the last six months:

1. **Berkshire dumped it.** Berkshire initiated STZ in late 2024 and built it to **7.6% (13.4M shares) by Nov 2025** — the marquee smart-money endorsement. The **13F filed 15 May 2026 showed Berkshire cut the position ~95%** (down to ~0.4%), under new CEO Greg Abel's portfolio overhaul. Whatever the original thesis was, Berkshire abandoned it near these lows. That is a strong negative tell, not a contrarian buy signal.
2. **FY2027 guidance is for a *decline*.** Management guided FY2027 (issued Feb 2026): **comparable EPS $11.20–$11.90** (midpoint $11.55, below FY2026), **beer organic net sales −1% to +1%** (flat), enterprise operating margin **32–33%**, beer margin **37–38%**. The growth algorithm is, by the company's own guidance, gone for the year.
3. **Q4 FY2026 (reported 2 Jun 2026) missed** on a GAAP basis (large wine/intangible impairment dragged reported EPS); Q4 depletions barely positive (+0.6%). Aluminum-tariff relief is *expected* in FY2027 but is not yet in the numbers.
4. Next catalyst: **Q1 FY2027 results on 30 Jun 2026** — the first read on whether beer depletions are stabilizing or still sliding.

Wall Street mean target is **~$176 (+30%)** with a "buy" skew, but those targets lag the deterioration — analysts have not fully marked to the flat-to-down guide.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus / bull view:** "Best-in-class beer margins, #1 U.S. share gainer, Modelo is the top beer in America, ~11.7x earnings and a 3% yield near a multi-year low — a wide-moat franchise on sale; tariffs and the Hispanic pullback are transitory." Target ~$176.
- **Our (skeptical) view:** The cheapness is *earned*. The volume engine is negative (depletions −2.1%, Modelo −3%, Corona −7%), and the cause — a structural pullback in the core Hispanic demographic under sustained immigration enforcement — is **not datably transitory** the way a sulfur-price spike or an inventory destock is. Management itself guides beer to flat and EPS *down* in FY2027. Add ~$11.1B net debt (~3x EBITDA), a secularly shrinking wine tail, GLP-1/moderation as a slow terminal-growth drag, and the single most-watched holder (Berkshire) selling 95% near the low. At ~11.7x *declining* EPS you are not being paid enough for a franchise whose growth has stalled with no clear inflection date. This is value-trap shaped: a great business, a deteriorating tape, and a multiple that can keep compressing while EPS flatlines.
- **What would flip it to a buy:** (1) two consecutive quarters of beer depletions inflecting back toward flat-to-positive (watch Modelo/Corona specifically, not just Pacifico/Victoria); (2) confirmed aluminum-tariff relief flowing through to a beer-margin re-expansion above 39%; (3) price closer to **~$110** (≈9.5x trough EPS, ~3.7% yield) to build in a real margin of safety; or (4) evidence the Hispanic-consumer pullback is normalizing (on-premise traffic recovering).

## Entry / Exit Discipline

- **Entry trigger:** ~$110 (≈9.5x trough EPS, ~3.7% yield) **OR** two quarters of stabilizing beer depletions — whichever comes first. Q1 FY2027 (30 Jun 2026) is the next data point.
- **Thesis-break (avoid / stay out):** beer depletions worsen beyond −4%, FY2027 EPS guidance cut again, beer margin slips below 36%, or net leverage rises toward 3.5x. Any of these confirms structural impairment.
- **What changes my mind (to bullish):** Modelo + Corona depletions return to positive with tariff relief flowing through to margin — that converts "stalled franchise" back to "wide-moat compounder at a discount."
