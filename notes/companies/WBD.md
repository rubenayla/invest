# Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Entertainment
**Price:** $27.63 | **Market Cap:** $68.6B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

WBD is now a merger arbitrage play. Paramount Skydance announced a definitive agreement on Feb 27, 2026 to acquire WBD for $31.00/share in cash ($81B equity, $110B enterprise value). The shareholder vote is scheduled for today (March 20, 2026). The HSR antitrust waiting period expired without DOJ challenge, and the FCC chair has publicly stated the deal is "cleaner" and will be approved "quickly." DOJ antitrust chief denies fast-tracking but Bloomberg reports a challenge is "unlikely." Expected close is Q3 2026, with a $0.25/share/quarter ticking fee if closing slips past Sept 30. The stock trades at $27.63, implying a 12.2% spread to the $31.00 deal price.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** The deal closes at $31.00 in Q3 2026. Analyst targets have converged to the deal price ($26-$31.25 range). Short interest is low at 2.8%. The market assigns some probability of regulatory risk (California AG investigation, EU/UK reviews) or deal break, hence the 12% spread.
- **Our view:** The spread is attractive for a merger arb but the fundamental thesis has changed. This is no longer a value play on WBD's media assets or streaming growth — it is a binary bet on deal closure. The $7B reverse termination fee from Paramount provides strong downside protection even in a deal-break scenario. Upside is capped at $31.00 (plus ticking fees). The pre-deal "value play with 92% model upside" thesis is superseded by the acquisition.
- **Trigger:** Shareholder vote today (March 20), followed by regulatory clearances. Q3 2026 expected close.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — but it's a corporate event, not an operational turnaround.** The Paramount acquisition IS the inflection. The standalone business was showing early signs of operational improvement (first positive net income, streaming subscriber growth, massive debt reduction) but was still struggling with secular linear TV decline. The $31/share deal price represents Paramount's view that the combined entity (with $6B+ synergies) unlocks more value than WBD standalone.

The streaming momentum (140M+ subs expected Q1, 150M+ by year-end) and UK/Ireland launch are real operational inflections — but WBD shareholders won't participate in that upside post-acquisition.

## Bull Case (Merger Arb)

- Deal closes on time in Q3 2026, capturing full $31.00 spread (+12.2% in ~5-6 months, ~25% annualized)
- If delayed past Sept 30, ticking fee of $0.25/quarter adds incremental return
- $7B reverse termination fee provides massive downside floor if deal breaks ($2.82/share to WBD shareholders)
- All major regulatory hurdles appear manageable: HSR expired, FCC chair supportive, DOJ challenge "unlikely"
- Shareholder vote today should pass easily (no major opposition reported)

## Bear Case

- DOJ or state AGs (California) block the deal on antitrust grounds — streaming market concentration concerns
- EU/UK regulators impose remedies that delay or unwind the deal
- Paramount financing collapses (though $47B equity is fully committed by Ellison/RedBird, and $54B debt from BofA/Citi/Apollo)
- If deal breaks, WBD reverts to standalone valuation — stock could drop to $15-20 range (where it traded pre-deal speculation)
- Macro shock (recession, credit crisis) disrupts $54B debt financing

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

This is a clean merger arbitrage with a 12.2% spread to the $31.00 deal price, likely closing in Q3 2026 (~25% annualized return). Regulatory risk is low but not zero: HSR expired without action, FCC is supportive, DOJ challenge unlikely per Bloomberg. The $7B reverse termination fee (~$2.82/share) provides a meaningful floor. However, the expected value of +6.7% reflects the 15% deal-break probability where the stock could drop to ~$20. For a dedicated merger arb portfolio, this is attractive. For a fundamentals-based portfolio, the upside is capped at $31 and the pre-deal "92% model upside" thesis no longer applies.

**Would upgrade to BUY if:** (1) Stock dips to $26 or below on temporary deal uncertainty (widening the spread to 19%+), or (2) DOJ formally clears the deal, removing the largest remaining risk. At current price, risk/reward is decent but not compelling enough for a non-arb position.
