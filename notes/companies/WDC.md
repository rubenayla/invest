# Western Digital Corporation (WDC)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Computer Hardware (Pure-play HDD post SanDisk spinoff)
**Price:** $294.79 | **Market Cap:** $100.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

Western Digital completed its separation from SanDisk (NAND/SSD) in February 2025 and is now a pure-play HDD company operating in a duopoly with Seagate. The stock has surged from ~$29 (52-week low) to $295 as AI data center demand has created a structural shortage in nearline HDDs -- WDC's CEO confirmed all HDD capacity is sold out through calendar 2026 with firm POs from the top 7 customers and LTAs extending to 2027-2028. Q2 FY2026 beat expectations with $3.02B revenue, 46.1% non-GAAP gross margins (up 770bps YoY), and Q3 guidance of $3.2B at ~47.5% margins. UltraSMR has crossed 50% of nearline exabytes and HAMR qualification was pulled forward by half a year.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Wall Street is overwhelmingly bullish (20 Buy, 6 Hold, 0 Sell) with a $325 median target. The narrative is "AI storage supercycle" -- WDC is a direct beneficiary of hyperscaler capex with locked-in revenue visibility. Consensus expects continued margin expansion as UltraSMR mix increases and pricing power persists.
- **Our view:** The consensus is largely correct on the demand side, but the stock at $295 (near its $320 all-time high) already prices in much of this cycle. The real question is: how much further can margins expand, and what happens when capacity additions eventually catch up with demand in 2027-2028? At 22x forward earnings for a cyclical hardware company, the market is pricing in sustained peak margins. The risk is that this is a mid-cycle momentum trade, not a deep value opportunity. However, the sold-out capacity through 2026 and LTAs through 2028 provide unusual earnings visibility for a cyclical.
- **Trigger:** Q3 FY2026 earnings (April 23, 2026) -- if margins hit 48%+ and HAMR qualification progresses, the re-rating continues. Conversely, any sign of demand softening or hyperscaler capex pullback would de-rate quickly given the elevated multiple.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- WDC is at a major inflection.** The company has transformed from a struggling conglomerate (NAND losses dragging on HDD profits) into a focused, high-margin duopoly benefiting from AI-driven demand. Key evidence:

1. **Post-spinoff margin expansion**: Gross margins jumped from 28% (FY24) to 46% (Q2 FY26) and guiding higher
2. **Sold-out capacity**: Revenue visibility through 2026-2028 via LTAs is unprecedented for HDD
3. **Technology inflection**: UltraSMR crossing 50% of exabytes, HAMR qualification pulled forward -- both drive higher capacity per drive and better margins
4. **Balance sheet repair**: Debt cut from $7.4B to $4.7B, with further deleveraging from FCF

The inflection has clearly BEGUN and is well-recognized by the market (stock up 10x from lows). The question is how much upside remains.

## Bull Case

- AI storage demand exceeds current estimates; hyperscalers need 2-3x more exabytes than projected through 2028
- Gross margins reach 50%+ as UltraSMR and HAMR drives dominate the mix (higher capacity, lower cost per TB)
- Supply discipline maintained in duopoly -- neither WDC nor Seagate adds excess capacity
- FY27 EPS reaches $15+ as revenue crosses $14B with 50% gross margins
- Multiple sustains at 25x+ on secular AI storage narrative -> $375+

## Bear Case

- AI capex cycle peaks in 2026-2027; hyperscaler spending pulls back, leaving overcapacity
- SSD cost-per-TB crossover happens faster than expected, threatening nearline HDD demand at 20TB+
- Pricing power erodes as LTAs roll off and customers renegotiate in a softer market
- Cyclicality reasserts: margins mean-revert toward 35-40%, EPS drops to $8-10
- Multiple compresses to 15x as market treats it as a cyclical at peak -> $120-150

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

WDC is a genuinely transformed business riding a real AI-driven demand cycle with sold-out capacity and expanding margins. The quality is solid (18/25) and the inflection is undeniable. However, at $295 the stock has already re-rated massively (10x from lows, near all-time highs), trades at 22x forward for a cyclical hardware company, and expected value from our scenario analysis is slightly negative (-8%). The variant perception is weak -- this is a consensus long with 20 Buy ratings and 0 Sells. The best entry was in the $100-150 range when the post-spinoff story was still being recognized.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:** A pullback to $220-240 (15-18x forward EPS), which would provide a more favorable risk/reward. Also would upgrade if Q3 earnings show margins exceeding 48% AND management announces new capacity expansion commitments, signaling the cycle has further to run. Entry at current levels only justified with a 3+ year horizon and conviction that AI storage demand is being structurally underestimated.
