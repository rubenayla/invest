## Inflection Point

**Yes — SMFG is at a structural inflection.** After three decades of near-zero rates crushing Japanese bank profitability, the BOJ rate normalization cycle represents a generational turning point. SMFG is compounding this macro tailwind with:

1. **Rate cycle inflection**: Each 25bp hike adds ¥100-150B to NII. With rates at 0.75% and heading to 1.0-1.5%, there's significant further NII upside.
2. **Fee income transformation**: Olive platform and NISA account growth are building recurring fee streams that reduce rate sensitivity over time.
3. **Capital return inflection**: Management has shifted from hoarding capital to actively returning it (buybacks + growing dividends), compressing the share count and boosting EPS.

**Observable evidence**: Three consecutive years of record profits, mid-year guidance raise, aggressive buybacks. This is not speculative — the inflection is already underway.

- JPY strengthening hurts international earnings (40%+ of profits from overseas)
- Global recession reduces corporate lending demand and increases credit costs
- Japan's shrinking population limits long-term domestic loan growth
- Competition from fintech/digital banks erodes deposit franchise (low probability near-term)
