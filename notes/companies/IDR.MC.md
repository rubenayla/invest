# Indra Sistemas (IDR.MC)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Defense & IT Services
**Price:** EUR 48.68 | **Market Cap:** EUR 8.5B | **EV:** EUR 9.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

Indra is Spain's national defense champion undergoing a structural transformation from a mid-tier IT services company into a European defense-tech consolidator. FY2025 results were outstanding: revenue up 13% to EUR 5.46B, net income up 57% to EUR 436M, and a staggering 140% surge in order intake to EUR 12.8B, pushing the backlog to EUR 16.1B (book-to-bill 2.34x). The stock has pulled back ~26% from its EUR 66.15 high (52-week), likely on profit-taking after the European defense euphoria cooled and debt concerns from acquisitions (net debt jumped to EUR 583M from net cash). The 2026 guidance is aggressive: revenue >EUR 7B, EBIT >EUR 700M, with Spain's EUR 14B Special Modernization Programs (PEM) providing multi-year visibility.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Indra is a clear European defense beneficiary, but the easy money has been made. The stock tripled from EUR 20 to EUR 66 in 12 months on the defense spending narrative. At forward PE ~16x, it's priced for the growth already. Debt from acquisitions (TESS Defence, ITP Aero stake) adds risk. Analysts have a mean target of EUR 55-63 but the stock is well below that, suggesting uncertainty.
- **Our view:** The market is anchoring on the recent pullback and missing three things: (1) the EUR 16B backlog provides 2.9x revenue coverage, the highest in company history and far above European defense peers -- this is not a cyclical peak, it's a structural step-change; (2) the 2026 guidance implies ~35% EBIT growth (EUR 700M vs EUR 517M) which would push 2026 EPS to ~EUR 3.37, putting the stock at just 14.4x 2026E earnings -- cheap for a defense grower; (3) Spain's consolidation of its defense industry under Indra's leadership (including potential EM&E acquisition) creates a national champion with NATO-driven secular tailwinds for a decade.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 results (expected May 2026) should confirm the massive backlog is converting to revenue. Additional PEM contract awards and the potential EM&E/Escribano acquisition announcement would also serve as catalysts.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- Indra is at a clear structural inflection.** The company is transforming from a mixed IT services / defense electronics company into Spain's consolidated national defense champion. Three inflection drivers:

1. **Spain's PEM programs** (EUR 14B) provide a decade-long order pipeline that didn't exist 2 years ago
2. **European defense spending surge** post-Ukraine/Trump withdrawal concerns -- spending rising from 1.3% to 2%+ of GDP across NATO Europe
3. **Consolidation role** -- Indra is the designated platform for Spanish defense industry consolidation (acquiring companies, not being acquired)

The backlog jump from EUR 7.2B to EUR 16.1B in one year is observable proof the inflection has begun.

## Bull Case

- Q1 2026 confirms massive backlog conversion; revenue run-rate toward EUR 7B+ guidance
- EBIT margins expand toward 11%+ as higher-margin defense mix increases (defense grew 23% vs Minsait 5%)
- EM&E acquisition completes, adding land vehicles and ammunition capabilities
- European defense budgets continue rising; Indra wins cross-border contracts (Diehl partnership, ELT Group JV)
- Stock re-rates toward European defense peer multiples (Rheinmetall ~25x, Leonardo ~18x)

## Bear Case

- Acquisition integration challenges (TESS Defence, potential EM&E) dilute margins
- Spanish government budget politics delay or reduce PEM spending
- Backlog conversion slower than expected -- defense program delays are common
- Corporate governance concerns around Escribano's dual role (CEO + potential EM&E seller) deter institutional investors
- European defense spending enthusiasm fades if geopolitical tensions ease

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: **MEDIUM-HIGH**
