## Situation Summary

Solaria Energía is a Spanish pure-play solar IPP (Independent Power Producer) that owns and operates 3.1 GW of photovoltaic plants across Spain, Italy, Portugal, Uruguay, and Greece, with a 14.2 GW pipeline. The company just posted record FY2025 results: revenue €303M (+27%), EBITDA €266M (+32%, beating guidance by 6%), and net income €137M (+55%). It is now pivoting from pure solar generation into a solar-plus-storage-plus-data-center platform, having signed two large PPAs with Merlin Properties for data center power (438 MW combined) and launched Gravyx, a 14 GWh battery storage JV with Stoneshield Capital. The stock has surged 307% from its 52-week low of €6.09 to €24.79, now near its 52-week high of €24.99, trading well above the analyst consensus target of ~€17-19. Spain's solar market faces a structural risk from electricity price cannibalisation — over 693 hours of negative/zero wholesale prices in 2025 — but Solaria's 80% PPA-backed revenue provides partial insulation.

## Variant Perception

- **The Idea:** Solaria is a high-growth European solar IPP trading at the cheapest EV/EBITDA multiple among quality renewables peers, with a credible path to double EBITDA by 2028 via aggressive capacity expansion (3.1GW to 9GW) and a differentiated data center/storage pivot.

- **Consensus View:** Analysts are divided but lean cautiously bullish — 7 Buy, 6 Hold, 2 Sell across 15 analysts. Mean target of €18.95, implying 24% DOWNSIDE from current price. The consensus acknowledges strong earnings growth but fears: (1) Spain's electricity price cannibalisation from solar oversupply, (2) heavy debt load (4.7x Net Debt/EBITDA), and (3) execution risk on the €2.5B capex plan. Most analysts set targets when the stock was €14-18 and have not fully updated for the data center narrative.

- **Variant Perception:** The market may be underappreciating two things. First, the data center pivot transforms Solaria from a commodity solar generator into an infrastructure platform selling 40-year PPAs at premium rates — this deserves a higher multiple than a pure solar IPP. Second, the family-controlled ownership (Díaz-Tejeiro family holds ~23%, insiders 46%) and CEO share purchases signal skin-in-the-game alignment rarely seen in European utilities. HOWEVER, the stock at €24.79 has already priced in much of this re-rating. The variant perception was valid at €10-15; at €25, it is largely played out. The stock trades at 16.3x EV/EBITDA (2025) vs the sector average of 10-12x, suggesting the market has already given Solaria credit for its growth story.

- **Trigger:** Successful execution of the Merlin Properties data center PPAs and Gravyx battery JV, plus achieving the 2026 EBITDA target of €331M. Additional triggers: signing of the 1.1 GW hybrid PPA pipeline currently in negotiation, and any announcements of Tier 1 hyperscaler data center contracts. On the downside, a spike in Spanish pool electricity prices (due to storage buildout or demand growth) would be a positive catalyst, while further negative price hours would be bearish.

**Revenue and earnings trends are genuinely strong.** Revenue has grown at ~30% CAGR over 3 years, EBITDA at ~22%, and net income at ~15% (slower due to rising interest costs on growing debt). The EBITDA margin of 88% reflects Solaria's asset-light operating model — minimal COGS (€2M on €197M energy revenue), with most costs being D&A (€48M) and SG&A (€2.3M). This is project finance debt secured against specific solar assets, not corporate debt, which provides some comfort — but 4.7x Net Debt/EBITDA is at the high end of the range for utility companies.

**FCF is deeply negative** (-€260M in 2025) because capex of €398M far exceeds OCF of €138M. This is a deliberate growth investment phase (€2.5B capex planned for 2026-2028). The company must continue accessing debt markets to fund this expansion. If credit conditions tighten or electricity prices collapse, this becomes the key vulnerability.

## Inflection Point

There IS an inflection underway, and it is maturing:

**Evidence of inflection:**
- Revenue, EBITDA, and net income all hit record highs in 2025, with EBITDA beating guidance
- Installed capacity grew 82% YoY to 3.1 GW
- Data center pivot secured with 438 MW in signed Merlin PPAs (40-year contracts)
- Gravyx JV launched with 14 GWh battery pipeline, providing storage optionality
- Stock up 307% from 52-week lows — the market is recognizing the transformation
- CEO actively buying shares, family increasing commitment

**But risks to the inflection:**
- FCF remains deeply negative (-€260M) — the company is spending far more than it earns
- Leverage is increasing (4.7x Net Debt/EBITDA, up from 4.3x in 2024)
- Spain's wholesale electricity price cannibalisation (693 hours negative/zero in 2025) threatens the 20% merchant revenue
- Most of the 9 GW target and data center pipeline is still in development, not operational
- The stock has already priced in significant execution success

**Assessment:** The inflection is real and increasingly proven (record earnings, signed data center contracts, expanding capacity). But the stock price has moved ahead of the fundamentals — buying now means betting on continued perfect execution of a €2.5B capex program in a challenging electricity price environment.

## Bull Case

- **Data center re-rating:** Solaria becomes a recognized European data center power infrastructure platform. The 40-year Merlin PPAs are just the beginning — 1.1 GW hybrid PPA pipeline in negotiation + 500 MW additional data center talks could make this a multi-billion revenue stream by 2028-2030. Market re-rates from utility multiple (10-12x EV/EBITDA) to infrastructure/data center multiple (15-20x)
- **Execution beats targets:** Installed capacity reaches 4.3 GW in 2026 and 6.9 GW in 2027 on schedule. EBITDA hits or beats €331M target (2026), €456M (2027), €521M (2028). At 14x EV/EBITDA on 2028E EBITDA, EV = €7.3B, equity value ~€6.0B = €48/share
- **Battery storage optionality:** Gravyx JV captures value from the cannibalisation problem — store cheap daytime solar, sell at evening premiums. Batteries turn a headwind into a tailwind
- **European energy policy tailwinds:** EU Green Deal, REPowerEU, and Spain's energy transition create favorable regulatory environment for renewables and data center power
- **Interest rate cuts:** ECB rate cuts reduce financing costs on the €1.5B debt pile, boosting net income

## Bear Case

- **Electricity price collapse:** Spain's solar cannibalisation worsens — negative price hours expand from 693 to 1,500+ per year, crushing merchant revenue and forcing PPA renegotiation at lower rates. Solar PPA prices in Spain already fell to €32.50/MWh in Q4 2025
- **Debt spiral:** €2.5B capex plan requires continued debt issuance. If credit markets tighten or Solaria's leverage ratio exceeds debt covenants, growth stalls. Rising interest costs (already €48M, up 126% from 2022) eat into net income. Net Debt/EBITDA could reach 5-6x if EBITDA growth disappoints
- **Execution failure:** Permitting delays, grid connection bottlenecks, construction overruns derail the 9 GW target. Spain's solar queue is already congested with oversupply risk. Italy/Portugal regulatory changes add uncertainty
- **Data center hype fades:** If hyperscaler capex pulls back or data center demand shifts to other regions, the Merlin PPAs remain just two contracts rather than a platform story. Multiple compresses back to pure utility levels (10-12x EV/EBITDA)
- **Regulatory/tax risk:** Italy's Meloni government has already signaled plans to lower electricity prices, which could affect Italian operations. Spain could follow with windfall taxes or price caps on renewables
- **Valuation already stretched:** At 16.3x EV/EBITDA (2025) and P/E of 22.5x, the stock is priced for growth perfection. Any earnings miss triggers a de-rating

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MODERATE

Solaria Energía is a genuinely good business (16/25 quality score) with a compelling long-term thesis: founder-led solar IPP with a differentiated data center/storage pivot, exceptional margins, and a scarce development pipeline. The record FY2025 results and data center PPAs validate the strategy. Management alignment (46% insider ownership, active CEO buying) is excellent.

However, the stock at €24.79 is priced for perfection:

1. **Negative expected value (-7.0%).** The scenario table shows more downside risk than upside potential from current prices.
2. **Extreme technical extension.** Up 307% from lows, within 1% of 52-week high, 60% above 200-day MA. Analyst targets lag at €18.95 (24% downside).
3. **Heavy leverage.** 4.7x Net Debt/EBITDA with €2.5B more capex needed. Deeply negative FCF (-€260M). Execution-dependent on continued capital market access.
4. **Cannibalisation headwind.** Spain's 693 hours of negative electricity prices in 2025 is a structural problem that will worsen as solar capacity grows from 32GW to potentially 50GW+.
5. **Data center story is early.** Only 2 signed Merlin PPAs. The 1.1 GW hybrid pipeline and 500 MW data center talks are promising but unconfirmed.

**Would upgrade to BUY at:** €14-16 (10-11x EV/EBITDA on 2026E EBITDA), which would provide adequate margin of safety and a positive expected value. This would require a ~35-40% pullback from current levels.

**Would upgrade to STRONG BUY if:** (1) Stock at €12-14, AND (2) data center pipeline expands beyond Merlin to hyperscaler customers, AND (3) Gravyx JV deploys first 1 GWh+ of battery storage, AND (4) Net Debt/EBITDA stabilizes below 4.5x.

**Key differentiator vs peers:** Solaria's 256-employee team managing 3.1 GW of capacity is extraordinarily lean (12 MW per employee). This operational efficiency, combined with the family ownership structure and data center pivot, makes it a higher-quality asset than most European solar IPPs. The thesis is right — the price is just wrong (too high today).
