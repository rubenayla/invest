# IAMGOLD Corporation (IAG)

**Sector:** Basic Materials | **Industry:** Gold
**Price:** $16.50 (2026-05-28) | **Market Cap:** $9.5B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-05-28

## Business Primer

**What they do today.** IAMGOLD digs gold out of the ground and sells it. It runs three operating mines: **Cote Gold** in Ontario, Canada (its newest and most important asset, ~70% owned with Sumitomo as junior partner); **Essakane** in Burkina Faso, West Africa (an older, large open-pit mine); and **Westwood** in Quebec, Canada (a smaller underground mine). In Q1 2026 it produced 183,600 attributable ounces of gold and sold it at an average of $4,850 per ounce, generating over $1 billion of quarterly revenue. The "customer" is essentially the global gold market — refiners and bullion buyers — at the prevailing spot price. There is no negotiation: IAMGOLD is a price-taker. Its job is to mine each ounce for less than gold sells for, and pocket the spread.

**Why customers choose them (the value proposition).** Gold is a fungible commodity, so IAMGOLD has no "product" customers prefer — an ounce of IAMGOLD gold is identical to an ounce of Barrick gold. The real "value proposition" is for *investors*: IAMGOLD offers leveraged exposure to the gold price with a very large per-ounce cash margin right now. Its all-in sustaining cost (AISC, the fully-loaded cost to mine and sustain an ounce including royalties and sustaining capex) was about $2,124/oz in Q1 2026, while gold sold for $4,850/oz. That roughly $2,700/oz gross margin means each $100 move in gold flows almost directly to cash flow. Compared with simply owning physical gold or a gold ETF (which just tracks the metal), a low-cost miner like IAMGOLD amplifies gold-price moves — its stock has a beta of ~2.2 to the metal. The catch: that leverage cuts both ways.

**Where they want to grow.** Management's explicit bets all center on Cote Gold. (1) A **Cote expansion study** due Q4 2026 targeting throughput of 50,000-55,000 tonnes per day (up from ~36,000 nameplate today), which would lift annual production materially. (2) A **Cote resource update** in Q2 2026 targeting 20 million measured-and-indicated ounces, extending mine life. (3) The **Nelligan** project (a deposit near Cote with 4.3M measured/indicated and 7.5M inferred ounces), with a preliminary economic assessment due H1 2027. In parallel they are returning huge cash to shareholders — $350M of buybacks since December 2025 (18M shares retired) — and have eliminated net debt. Success in 3-5 years looks like a growing, low-cost, debt-free Canadian-anchored producer throwing off free cash flow.

**What could go wrong (business risks).** First and largest: the **gold price**. The entire thesis rests on gold staying high; a drop to $4,000 would cut the per-ounce margin by roughly a third and crush earnings. Second: **Burkina Faso geopolitical risk** — Essakane sits in a coup-prone Sahel state with a military government, rising resource-nationalism, and security threats; record Q1 output there could reverse on a tax hike, export ban, or shutdown. Third: **Cote execution** — the mine is barely two years into full production; Q1 saw a conveyor failure and crusher constraints that capped throughput, and the big expansion is a multi-year capital project that could run over budget. Fourth: **cost inflation** — gold-linked royalties add ~$115/oz of cost for every $1,000 rise in gold, and oil/labor costs keep AISC creeping up, eroding the margin even when gold is flat.

**How to think about it.** This is a **deleveraged, post-construction mid-tier gold producer at a free-cash-flow inflection** — it just finished spending $2B+ building Cote and has flipped from cash-burner to cash-machine. It trades like a high-beta gold call option with a cheap multiple and a clean balance sheet, not a durable compounder. The variant question is whether the market is paying for the de-risked balance sheet and growing reserve base, or just renting the gold-price beta.

## Situation Summary

IAMGOLD delivered an exceptional Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-06): revenue over $1B, adjusted EPS of $0.67, adjusted EBITDA of $666M, and $525M of mine-site free cash flow. It reached a **net cash position** (vs. $800M+ net debt a year earlier), repaid another $100M of its facility, and bought back $260M of stock in the quarter ($350M since December). Essakane posted record production (111,900 oz). The catch: AISC came in at $2,124/oz consolidated — well above the ~$1,900 the prior thesis assumed — driven by gold-linked royalties (~$115/oz per $1,000 of gold) and one-off Cote conveyor/crusher constraints that limited throughput to 9.2M tonnes. Meanwhile gold has pulled back from its January 2026 peak of ~$5,589 to ~$4,700-4,750 spot. The stock has *fallen* from ~$19.64 (prior analysis) to $16.50 even as forward EPS estimates rose to ~$2.51, pushing the forward P/E down to 6.6x. Key dateable catalysts: Cote resource update (Q2 2026) and Cote expansion technical report (Q4 2026).

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** IAG is a high-beta gold play (~2.2 beta) that already ran from $5 to $25; analysts are constructive (9 Buy / 3 Hold, mean target ~$26) but treat the stock as primarily a leveraged bet on the gold price, with AISC creep and Burkina Faso risk capping the multiple at ~7-10x. The recent pullback to $16.50 reflects gold's retreat from its January peak.
- **Our view:** The market is conflating a *lower gold price* with a *worse business* and has overshot. Two things changed in IAG's favor that the falling stock price masks: (1) forward EPS estimates rose to ~$2.51 (Q1 alone annualizes to $2.68) even as the share price fell, so the stock is now genuinely cheap at 6.6x forward / ~8.8% FCF yield with a *net-cash* balance sheet and an active buyback retiring ~7% of shares annually; (2) the high Q1 AISC was inflated by *non-recurring* conveyor/crusher items that management expects to fade after the May conveyor replacement and secondary-crusher commissioning, so the cost trajectory should improve, not worsen. At today's price the stock implies gold falling well below $4,500 and staying there — yet even TD/JPM base cases see gold averaging $4,800-5,000+ in 2026.
- **Trigger:** (1) Q2 2026 Cote resource update (targeting 20M M&I oz) — a tangible reserve-life extension; (2) Q4 2026 Cote expansion technical report (50-55k tpd) — re-rates IAG from "ramped" to "growing"; (3) Q2/Q3 earnings showing AISC stepping down post-conveyor-fix, proving the Q1 cost spike was transitory; (4) continued aggressive buybacks compounding per-share FCF.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — IAG is past the construction-to-production inflection and now in the harvest-plus-reinvest phase.** The capital-destruction era (negative FCF 2022-2024 totaling ~-$1.16B building Cote) is over. Confirmed evidence: FY2025 FCF +$841M; Q1 2026 mine-site FCF $525M; net debt of $800M+ a year ago converted to a *net cash* position; $350M of buybacks since December. The *new* inflection to watch is the **cost normalization** — Q1's elevated AISC reflected one-off Cote conveyor/crusher constraints, and the May conveyor replacement plus secondary-crusher commissioning should drive AISC down over the next two quarters. If that plays out, it's an observable, near-term re-rating trigger separate from the gold price.

## Bull Case

- Gold holds $4,800-5,000+ (J.P. Morgan/TD base cases; central-bank buying ~800t/yr, Fed cuts) while IAG annualizes ~$2.60-2.80 EPS — at even 9-10x that's $24-28.
- Q1 AISC spike proves transitory: conveyor fix (May) + secondary crusher phase out the contracted aggregate crusher, AISC steps back toward ~$1,900, restoring operating leverage.
- Cote expansion study (Q4 2026) confirms 50-55k tpd economics and resource update lifts reserves to 20M oz — re-rates IAG as a *growing* producer, not a one-mine ramp.
- Buyback compounds: ~$350M+/yr retiring ~7% of shares lifts per-share FCF independent of gold.
- Multiple re-rates from 6.6x toward the peer 10-13x as the market credits the net-cash balance sheet.

## Bear Case

- Gold corrects to $4,000/oz: per-ounce margin compresses ~25-30%, EPS falls toward $1.50-1.80, and the 2.2 beta amplifies the move — stock to $10-12.
- **Royalty / cost squeeze (under-covered by headlines):** gold-linked royalties add ~$115/oz per $1,000 of gold, so a *higher* gold price actually inflates AISC and dampens the upside leverage investors assume — and if the Q1 conveyor/crusher costs prove *not* one-off, AISC stays above $2,100 and FCF disappoints even at flat gold. This is the primary-source risk management quantified that the bullish narrative glosses over.
- Burkina Faso: Essakane (record Q1, ~40-45% of attributable production) is in a military-junta state with rising resource nationalism — a tax hike, export restriction, or security event could gut a large slice of cash flow.
- Cote expansion overruns: a multi-year ~50-55k tpd build is exactly where miners destroy value; capex creep or schedule slip would sour the growth re-rating.
- Stock already had a huge multi-year run; even with a clean balance sheet, gold-sentiment reversal drives institutional profit-taking (74% institutional ownership).
