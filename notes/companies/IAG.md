# IAMGOLD Corporation (IAG)

**Sector:** Basic Materials | **Industry:** Gold
**Price:** $19.64 | **Market Cap:** $11.7B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-16

## Situation Summary

IAMGOLD just delivered a transformational 2025: revenue nearly tripled to $2.85B, operating cash flow exceeded $1.1B, and FCF swung to +$841M after years of heavy capex on the Cote Gold mine. Cote achieved nameplate capacity (36,000 tpd) in mid-2025 and produced 280k attributable oz in its first full year. The company aggressively deleveraged — repaying a $400M high-cost term loan and closing a legacy gold prepay — while initiating $100M in buybacks. Gold sits at ~$5,000/oz, well above the $1,900 AISC, providing enormous per-ounce margins. The stock has run from $5.35 to nearly $20 in 12 months but still trades at just 9.2x forward earnings vs. the gold miner industry average of ~13x.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** IAG is a mid-tier gold producer that successfully brought Cote online, but the stock's 3x run-up over the past year already reflects the operational turnaround. Analysts are split (6 Buy, 2 Hold) with a wide target range ($13-$34). The market treats IAG as a high-beta gold play (~2.2 beta) with limited re-rating potential beyond gold price moves.
- **Our view:** The market is underpricing the FCF inflection. IAG just transitioned from a capital-destruction phase (negative FCF 2022-2024 totaling -$1.16B) to a capital-return phase ($841M FCF, buybacks, debt paydown). At 9.2x forward P/E and 7.2% FCF yield, it trades at a ~30% discount to mid-tier gold peers. The forward EPS of $2.14 assumes gold near current levels — if gold averages $5,000+ in 2026 (consensus), IAG could deliver $2.50+ EPS. The Cote expansion (potential Phase 2 to 45,000 tpd) is optionality the market isn't yet pricing.
- **Trigger:** (1) Q1 2026 earnings (~May 2026) showing sustained FCF generation and further debt reduction; (2) Cote Phase 2 expansion announcement, which would add 50-80k oz/year; (3) Gold continuing above $5,000 forces further analyst upgrades on all gold miners.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — IAG is at a clear positive inflection.** The company just crossed from its capital-intensive construction phase (Cote Gold build, $2B+ spent) into the harvesting phase. Evidence:
- 2025 was the first year of positive FCF since 2021
- FCF swung from -$73M (2024) to +$841M (2025) — a $914M improvement
- Debt reduced by $391M in a single year
- Share buybacks initiated ($100M in Dec 2025 + early 2026)
- Cote hit nameplate capacity ahead of schedule

This is the textbook "construction-to-production" inflection that creates the most value in mining. The question is how much of this is already priced in given the 3x stock run.

## Bull Case

- Gold sustains $5,000+/oz through 2026-2027 (central bank buying, geopolitical demand, inflation hedging), driving EPS toward $2.50-$3.00
- Cote Phase 2 expansion approved, adding 50-80k oz/year at low incremental cost, re-rating IAG as a growing mid-tier producer
- Continued aggressive capital returns: buybacks reduce share count 5-10%, potential dividend initiation
- Multiple expansion from 9x to 12-13x forward P/E as market recognizes IAG is now a FCF machine, not a construction story
- Essakane continues to outperform in Burkina Faso (340-380k oz attributable guidance)

## Bear Case

- Gold corrects to $4,000/oz — per-ounce margins compress 32%, EPS could fall to $1.00-$1.20, stock re-rates to $10-$12
- Cote operational issues: the mine is only 1 year into full production; processing problems, grade variability, or cost overruns could emerge
- Burkina Faso political instability threatens Essakane (45-50% of 2026 production) — coups, tax hikes, or operational disruptions
- AISC inflation: labor, energy, and royalties continue creeping higher, especially as gold-linked royalties eat into margins
- Stock has already tripled — institutional profit-taking could create sustained selling pressure even if fundamentals are fine

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

IAG is a legitimately good story: a successful mine build, a clean balance sheet, and massive FCF generation at current gold prices. The variant perception (market underpricing the FCF inflection) has merit — 9.2x forward P/E and 7.2% FCF yield for a growing gold producer is genuinely cheap vs. peers at 13x. However, two factors prevent a BUY today: (1) the stock has already tripled in 12 months, meaning much of the construction-to-production re-rating is done; (2) the thesis is overwhelmingly a gold price bet — at $4,000 gold, this is a -54% drawdown candidate with beta of 2.2. The expected value of +13% is positive but not compelling enough for the volatility.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:**
- Pullback to $16-17 (15-20% lower), which would put forward P/E at ~7.5x and FCF yield near 10%
- A confirmed Cote Phase 2 expansion announcement with credible economics
- Gold sustaining above $5,000 for another quarter while IAG continues to underperform peers (creating a valuation gap)

**Entry price if buying:** $16.50 (7.7x forward EPS, ~10% FCF yield)
**Thesis-break signal:** Gold below $4,000 sustained, or Cote production guidance cut >20%, or Essakane force majeure
