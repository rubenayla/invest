## Situation Summary
DexCom makes continuous glucose monitors — the small skin-worn sensors (G6 / G7 / the OTC **Stelo**) that stream glucose readings to a phone every few minutes, replacing fingersticks. It is one of two global leaders in CGM alongside **Abbott** (FreeStyle Libre). FY2025 revenue was **$4.66B (+16% YoY)** with operating margin ~19.5%, diluted EPS **$2.09**, and free cash flow **$1.08B** (~23% FCF margin) on a **net-cash** balance sheet.

The stock peaked near **$90** within the last year and now trades **$68.92, ~23% off that high** and roughly mid-range of its $54–$90 52-week band. The de-rating is not about the current numbers — those are strong and accelerating — it is about the **forward growth durability** of the CGM category in a GLP-1 (Ozempic/Mounjaro) world, plus a 2024 sales-force-reorganization stumble that broke the company's long streak of beat-and-raise quarters and shook the "secular compounder" narrative.

Despite the drawdown, the Street is still constructive: **24 analysts, "strong buy", mean target $85.2 (+24%)**, range $65–$112. Forward consensus EPS ~**$3.08** (FY26), implying ~22x forward.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus / bull view:** "CGM is one of the great secular MedTech growth stories — penetration in insulin-using diabetics is still incomplete, and the **Type-2 non-insulin** and **prediabetes/wellness** TAMs are an order of magnitude larger and barely tapped. Stelo (cleared OTC, cash-pay, no prescription) opens a brand-new consumer channel. GLP-1 is a *tailwind* not a threat: CGM improves drug adherence and outcomes, and the diabetic/obese population keeps growing. Mid-teens growth + margin expansion + net cash + buybacks = compounder. The 23% drawdown is a gift."
- **Bear view (kill case first):** (1) **GLP-1 erosion is real at the margin** — as Type-2 patients lose weight and normalize glucose on Mounjaro/Ozempic, a meaningful slice needs *less* monitoring, capping the very Type-2 non-insulin TAM the bull case leans on. (2) **Abbott is bigger, cheaper, and aggressive** — Libre outsells Dexcom in units globally and is winning the price-sensitive Type-2/OTC segment Dexcom needs for its next leg; CGM is commoditizing toward a two-player price war. (3) **Growth has already decelerated** from 20%+ to mid-teens, and the 2024 sales-force reorg proves execution is not bulletproof. (4) **Valuation has not actually de-rated to cheap** — 33x trailing / 22x forward earnings for a low-/mid-teens grower is PEG ~1.5–2.2; the drawdown removed froth, not created value. (5) Stelo monetization (low ASP, wellness churn) is unproven and may dilute, not lift, blended economics.
- **Our read:** This is a genuinely high-quality franchise — durable, net cash, real FCF, secular tailwind — that the repo's value discipline still can't call cheap. The bear's strongest point is #4: at $69 you are paying a full quality multiple, so the **expected value is good-not-great with a fat left tail** if the growth/GLP-1 debate breaks bearish. You don't need much to go wrong for a 22x-forward name to see 15x. Wait for the price to do the work.
- **Trigger / what re-rates it up:** Two or three quarters showing (a) **Type-2 non-insulin + Stelo new-patient adds re-accelerating** organic growth back toward ~+15–18%, (b) gross/operating **margin expansion holding** despite Abbott pricing, and (c) explicit evidence GLP-1 cohorts **retain** CGM rather than churn off. Any of those flips this to BUY LOW.

## Levels
- **Entry:** No edge at $69 — fully valued. **Build interest below ~$58** (forward PE ~19x, near the lower half of the 52-week band, where the bear tail compresses). Aggressive only **sub-$54** (the 52-week low), which would signal the growth-scare is winning and require re-underwriting the Type-2/Stelo thesis.
- **Thesis-break / avoid:** Two consecutive quarters of **organic growth below ~+8%**, a **guidance cut** citing GLP-1 attrition or Libre price competition, or **gross-margin compression** below the mid-50s%. Any of these confirms the bear's commoditization/erosion case → PASS.
- **What would change my mind (upgrade to BUY LOW/MEDIUM):** Re-acceleration of new-patient adds (Type-2 non-insulin + Stelo) with margins intact for 2–3 quarters, *or* the price simply de-rating to ~17x forward (~$52) where quality-at-a-discount math finally works.
