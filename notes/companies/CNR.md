## Situation Summary

Canadian National Railway is North America's only transcontinental railway, connecting three coasts (Pacific, Atlantic, Gulf of Mexico). The dominant near-term narrative is tariff headwinds: US-Canada trade tariffs cut CN's 2025 revenues by ~$350M (roughly 2% of total), and management guided 2026 volumes as "flattish" with continued forest-product weakness. In response, CN cut capex by ~$600M for 2026 (down to $2.8B) and is trimming $75M in management labor costs. Q1 2026 results are scheduled for April 29 — the first clean post-tariff read. ## Inflection Point

**No clear positive inflection.** CN is in a defensive phase — cutting capex, cutting jobs, managing through tariff headwinds with "flattish" volume guidance. The potential inflection catalyst is: (1) US-Canada tariff resolution allowing cross-border volume recovery (32% of traffic), or (2) trade diversion to Canadian ports (Prince Rupert, Halifax) increasing in CN's favor as shippers avoid US-China tariffs. Neither is imminent. The Q1 2026 call (April 29) may provide color on trade diversion volumes. This is a "wait for inflection evidence" situation, not an entry point.

## Bull Case

- US-Canada tariffs resolve within 12 months; cross-border volumes recover; OR improves back toward 59-60%; earnings power normalizes at CAD $8+ EPS
- Prince Rupert port volumes surge as US importers reroute away from US West Coast to avoid China tariffs — CN uniquely positioned as the only railway serving Prince Rupert
- CN's $130M+ in new sales wins (from new CCO) continue to compound and offset tariff volume losses
- Capex cut to $2.8B reduces FCF pressure; FCF recovers to $4B+; buybacks accelerate at low share count
- Premium re-rating to 22-23x earnings as trade normalization drives estimate revisions

## Bear Case

- US-Canada tariff standoff persists through 2026-2027; forest products and cross-border volumes remain depressed; OR stays above 62%; EPS stagnates at CAD $7-7.50
- **Primary-source risk not in headlines:** Debt trajectory is a hidden risk — CN has added $5.7B in debt over 3 years while revenues are flat. If EBITDA compresses further and capex must rise for network maintenance, free cash flow could drop below dividend coverage, forcing balance sheet repair over buybacks
- Q1 2026 volumes disappoint vs. already-modest guidance on April 29, triggering an earnings cut and multiple compression
- Housing and industrial production weakness in North America extends; auto/lumber/chemicals volumes remain soft
- The stock has re-rated from $126 to $155 (+23%) in 6 weeks while fundamentals have not changed — if tariff optimism doesn't materialize in hard numbers, mean-reversion to $130-135 range
