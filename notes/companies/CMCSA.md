# Comcast Corporation (CMCSA)

**Sector:** Communication Services | **Industry:** Cable broadband, wireless (MVNO), media (NBCUniversal, Peacock), theme parks
**Price:** $23.66 (2026-06-17) | **Market Cap:** ~$84.5B | **Shares:** ~3.57B | **Total Debt:** ~$99B (net debt ~$89B, ~2.5x EBITDA)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-17
**Standalone rating: BUY LOW** (lean) — ~6.3x forward EPS, P/B 0.96, EV/EBITDA 4.8x, ~14% shareholder yield (5.6% dividend + ~8% buyback) at a 2-year low. The bear case — secular broadband subscriber erosion — is real and *deepening*, so this is a value-vs-trap debate, not a clean bargain. Buy the cash return, underwrite the broadband floor, not a re-rating.

## Situation Summary
Comcast trades at **$23.66, a hair above its 52-week low ($23.13)** and down ~31% from its 52-week high ($34.36). On the screen it is one of the cheapest large-caps in the market: **~6.3x forward EPS** (consensus FY-forward EPS ~$3.78), **trailing P/B 0.96** (below book), **EV/EBITDA ~4.8x**, and a **5.6% dividend** ($1.32/sh) that is only ~26% of GAAP earnings. Add ~$7B/yr of buybacks (which shrank the share count from 4.43B to 3.57B — ~19% — in four years) and total shareholder yield is **~14%**.

The reason it's this cheap: **the core domestic broadband business is losing subscribers and the losses are widening.** Q4 2025 saw **−181,000 domestic broadband customers**, the worst quarterly result on record, as fixed-wireless access (T-Mobile / Verizon 5G home internet) and fiber overbuilders (AT&T, frontier) take share. Broadband is Comcast's profit engine — high-margin, the anchor of every bundle — so the market is pricing terminal decline.

Against that, the rest of the story is quietly improving:
- **Wireless (Xfinity Mobile, an MVNO on Verizon's network):** +364k lines in Q4, a record, to **9.3M+ lines** — the convergence lever that raises broadband ARPU and cuts churn.
- **Peacock:** 44M paid subs; Q4 loss widened to $552M YoY but **full-year adjusted-EBITDA losses improved >$700M** and revenue grew 10% to $5.4B — the trajectory is toward breakeven.
- **Experiences:** **Epic Universe** (new Orlando theme park) opened May 2025, a multi-year attendance/revenue ramp.
- **Portfolio cleanup:** the **Versant** spinoff (NBCUniversal's declining cable networks — USA, CNBC, MSNBC, E!, Golf Channel, plus digital assets) **completed in late 2025**, leaving a cleaner "connectivity + premium content + experiences" story (and explaining why forward EPS ~$3.78 sits below FY2025's reported $5.39 — 2025 was inflated by the ~$8.6B Disney/Hulu put payment and still included Versant earnings).

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Melting ice cube. Broadband — the only thing that mattered — is in structural decline as FWA and fiber eat the cable moat, and the losses are *accelerating*, not stabilizing. Media is secularly challenged, Peacock burns cash, and theme parks are cyclical. Cheap for a reason; a classic value trap that's been dead money for years. Avoid."
- **Our view (partial pushback):** You are not paying for growth — you're paying **6.3x earnings and below book** for a business throwing off ~$12-13B of normalized free cash flow and returning ~14% of the market cap every year. At this multiple the broadband base does **not** need to grow; it needs to *not collapse*. Domestic broadband **revenue is still rising** even as units fall, because ARPU growth + Xfinity Mobile + business services more than offset sub losses — connectivity revenue is flat-to-up. If subscriber losses stay in the ~1%/yr range while the company retires ~5% of shares annually, EPS grinds higher and the 14% capital return compounds in your favor. You're buying a low-beta (0.66) cash machine at a trough multiple.
- **Why it is NOT a slam-dunk:** The bear case is not a narrative — it's in the unit numbers, and they're getting *worse* (−181k is a record loss). FWA is a genuine, structurally cheaper substitute for a large slice of light-usage households, and fiber overbuild is permanent capacity, not a cycle. $99B of debt geared onto a declining-unit business amplifies any EBITDA stall. This has been cheap-and-getting-cheaper for years; multiple compression can continue even as the FCF holds.
- **Trigger / what re-rates it:** Two or three quarters of **broadband sub losses decelerating** (back toward <100k/qtr) while ARPU and mobile keep connectivity revenue growing, Peacock crossing into breakeven, and continued aggressive buybacks at <0.7x book. Even a re-rate from 6.3x to a still-cheap 8x on flat EPS is ~+27%; analysts' mean target is **$32.40 (~+37%)**.

## Bull / Bear

**Bull**
- **Trough multiple + below book:** 6.3x forward EPS, 0.96x book, 4.8x EV/EBITDA for a business with ~$35B EBITDA and a broadband moat. Melting-ice-cube pricing on something that isn't melting at the top line.
- **~14% shareholder yield:** 5.6% dividend (26% payout, safe) + ~8% buyback. At <0.7x book the buybacks are violently accretive; ~5%/yr share-count shrinkage alone offsets a lot of unit decline.
- **Connectivity revenue still grows despite unit losses:** ARPU growth + 9.3M-and-rising Xfinity Mobile lines + business services keep the segment flat-to-up; convergence bundling lowers churn.
- **Self-help underway:** Peacock losses narrowing >$700M/yr toward breakeven; Epic Universe ramping; Versant spin removes the worst secular-decline asset, leaving a cleaner story.
- **Low beta (0.66), investment-grade, $33B operating cash flow** — defensive cash machine, not a speculative turnaround.

**Bear**
- **Broadband losses are accelerating, not stabilizing:** −181k in Q4 2025 is a *record* loss. FWA and fiber overbuild are structural, permanent substitutes — this is the entire thesis and it is deteriorating.
- **Broadband is the profit engine:** if unit erosion outruns ARPU/mobile offsets, connectivity revenue turns negative and the high-margin core compresses — the cheap multiple then re-rates *down*, not up.
- **$99B debt** geared onto a declining-unit business: any sustained EBITDA stall hits equity holders disproportionately; refinancing at higher rates pressures FCF.
- **Media/streaming structurally challenged:** Peacock still loses money; content economics are brutal; theme parks are cyclical and capital-hungry.
- **Value trap track record:** the stock has been "cheap" for years and kept falling. A low multiple is not a floor.

## Entry / Exit
- **Entry:** attractive here at **$23-24** (within ~3% of the 52-week low, below book). Scale-in name given the secular overhang — this is a "buy the yield + buyback, dollar-cost into weakness" position, not an all-in.
- **Thesis-break (fundamental):** domestic broadband subscriber losses sustained **above ~2%/yr** AND **connectivity revenue (broadband + wireless + business services) turning negative YoY for two consecutive quarters** — that means the profit engine is eroding faster than capital return can compensate. Exit on confirmation regardless of how "cheap" the multiple looks.
- **Thesis-break (price/risk):** a break below ~$22 *on accelerating fundamental deterioration* (not just market beta); a dividend cut (would signal FCF stress and break the income thesis); leverage drifting above ~3x EBITDA.
- **What would change my mind (bullish):** two straight quarters of broadband losses decelerating below ~100k/qtr with ARPU intact → re-underwrite toward the bull case.
