# IQVIA Holdings (IQV)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Diagnostics & Research (Contract Research Organization + Healthcare Data/Analytics)
**Price:** $182.17 (2026-06-11) | **Market Cap:** $30.4B | **Enterprise Value:** $44.6B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-11
**Standalone rating: WATCH (quality-at-a-discount, but lean wait for a better entry / leverage + catalyst risk)**

## Situation Summary
IQVIA is the world's largest contract research organization (CRO) bolted onto the leading healthcare commercial-data and analytics franchise. Two engines:
- **R&D Solutions (R&DS, ~60% of revenue)** — runs clinical trials for pharma/biotech (the CRO business). Cyclical: bookings track biopharma R&D funding.
- **Technology & Analytics Solutions (TAS, ~35%) + Contract Sales (~5%)** — proprietary real-world data assets, prescription/claims data, and commercial analytics sold to pharma. Stickier, higher-margin, a genuine data moat.

The stock has been derated with the whole CRO group (ICLR, MEDP, CRL, Fortrea): it sits at **$182, ~26% below its 52-week high of $247 and near the bottom third of its range ($153–247)**. Revenue keeps growing (+8.4% in 2025 to $16.6B), but **GAAP net income has been flat at ~$1.36B for three straight years (2023–2025)** despite that growth — the market's core worry. Forward P/E of ~12.8x looks cheap, but that figure is on *analyst adjusted* EPS (~$14); trailing GAAP P/E is 23x. The real multiple is ~14–15x adjusted earnings / **14.9x EV/EBITDA** — depressed vs IQVIA's own high-teens history.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Best-in-class CRO + irreplaceable data moat, growing mid-single-digits, ~$2B FCF, buying back ~3% of shares a year at a trough multiple. 20 analysts, **strong_buy**, mean target $228 (+25%), range $195–287. Biotech funding is bottoming; when it turns, bookings reaccelerate into operating leverage."
- **Our view:** The franchise quality is real and the multiple is genuinely below history — but two things keep this a WATCH not a buy at $182. (1) **Earnings have stalled for three years** while revenue grew: margin/mix pressure plus a rising interest bill on $16.2B of debt is eating the growth. The "cheap forward P/E" rests on adjusted EPS that strips heavy intangible amortization + stock comp — a quality-of-earnings flag, not a clean 12.8x. (2) The bull catalyst — **biotech funding recovery — has been "six months away" for over two years.** Small/emerging biopharma (a big slice of R&DS bookings) is still capital-starved post-2022 rate shock. You are paid ~14x to wait, with no dividend, on a leveraged balance sheet.
- **Trigger to act:** Two consecutive quarters of R&DS book-to-bill >1.2 with improving cancellation rates, *plus* net-debt/EBITDA trending down — that combination would confirm the cycle turn and justify paying up.

## Bull / Bear
**Bull:**
- Genuine quality at a below-history multiple (~14–15x adj. / 14.9x EV/EBITDA vs high-teens historically).
- TAS data moat is hard to replicate and is arguably an **AI beneficiary** — proprietary real-world/claims data is the fuel for pharma's AI commercial and trial-design tooling.
- ~$2B FCF + relentless buyback shrinking shares ~3%/yr at a trough price.
- Operating leverage if R&DS bookings turn; analyst strong_buy with 25% mean upside.

**Bear:**
- **Three years of flat GAAP earnings** despite revenue growth — margin/mix pressure + interest drag.
- **4.7x net-debt/EBITDA** in a higher-rate world; refinancing and rate sensitivity; no dividend cushion.
- CRO end-market: small/mid biotech funding still weak; the "recovery" has been perpetually delayed.
- 2026–2028 large-pharma **patent-cliff wave** (Keytruda, Eliquis, etc.) could prompt R&D-budget caution among the biggest customers.
- **AI tail risk both ways:** if AI genuinely compresses clinical-trial cost/timelines, IQVIA's labor-leveraged CRO model could be disrupted rather than helped.
- Large GAAP-vs-adjusted gap (intangible amort + SBC) — the "cheap" multiple is partly an adjusted-earnings artifact.

## Entry / Thesis-break
- **Entry zone:** prefer **$160–170** (~12–13x adj., near the recent $153 low) for a real margin of safety against the leverage. At $182 the risk/reward is only modestly positive (EV +14%) — hence WATCH, not buy.
- **Thesis-break / stop-think:** below **~$145** (signals the CRO downturn is structural, not cyclical), or if **net-debt/EBITDA climbs toward 5.0x**, or R&DS **book-to-bill falls below 1.0** for two quarters.
- **What would change my mind (more bullish):** two consecutive quarters of R&DS book-to-bill >1.2, biotech funding indicators turning up, and visible debt paydown. **(More bearish):** large-pharma R&D-cut announcements, rising trial cancellations, or evidence AI is compressing CRO pricing.

## Calibrated numbers
- Scenario-weighted **EV ≈ +14%** (2-yr). Sell-side mean target +25%.
- No Kelly run (modest EV + high leverage argue for a sub-full size if bought; would size small at $182, larger only in the $160s).
- Standalone: **WATCH** — quality franchise at a fair-to-cheap multiple, but stalled earnings + heavy leverage + a perpetually-delayed catalyst mean the margin of safety isn't compelling until a lower price or a confirmed booking inflection.
