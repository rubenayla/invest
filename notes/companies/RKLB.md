# Rocket Lab USA, Inc. (RKLB)

**Sector:** Industrials | **Industry:** Aerospace & Defense

**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-22

## Situation Summary

Rocket Lab is the West's most credible commercial alternative to SpaceX, operating across launch services (Electron) and a fast-growing Space Systems division (satellite manufacturing, components, ground software). The company posted $602M revenue in 2025 (+38% YoY) with record gross margins of 34.4% GAAP and a $1.85B backlog (+73% YoY), anchored by an $816M SDA contract. The stock has surged from ~$15 to $67 over the past year (4.5x), driven by accelerating revenue, improving margins, and enthusiasm for the medium-lift Neutron rocket. However, Neutron's first launch was pushed to Q4 2026 after a stage 1 tank failure, and the company remains unprofitable with -$198M net income and -$322M FCF in 2025. At 63x P/S and $38B market cap, much of the optimism is already priced in.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Rocket Lab is the premier SpaceX alternative with a clear path to becoming a vertically integrated "space prime." Wall Street sees 33% upside to $90 average target. The narrative centers on Neutron as a game-changer that will unlock the medium/heavy launch market and drive profitability inflection. 10 of 12 analysts rate BUY, 0 SELL.
- **Our view:** The consensus is largely right on the business quality but wrong on valuation. At 63x trailing revenue for a company burning >$300M/yr in cash, the stock prices in flawless Neutron execution and rapid profitability -- neither of which is assured. Neutron has already slipped multiple times, and even when operational, it will compete against SpaceX's Falcon 9 (which can undercut on price) and Starship (which renders medium-lift economics obsolete for many payloads). Space Systems is genuinely strong but margins there (lower than launch) will dilute as it grows as a share of revenue. The gap between "great company" and "great investment at this price" is wide.
- **Trigger:** Neutron first launch success/failure in Q4 2026 will be the defining re-rating event. A successful launch could drive another leg up; a failure or further delay could trigger a 30-40% correction given the valuation premium.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- approaching profitability inflection, but not there yet.**

- **Gross margin trajectory** is the strongest signal: 9% (2022) -> 21% (2023) -> 27% (2024) -> 34% (2025). If this continues toward 40%+ (management targets non-GAAP 40%+), operating leverage kicks in.
- **Neutron R&D peak:** Management says Q1 2026 is peak Neutron R&D spend. If true, R&D as % of revenue should decline from here, driving operating margin improvement.
- **Backlog conversion:** $1.85B backlog with 37% converting in next 12 months ($685M) provides strong revenue visibility.
- **Risk:** The inflection is EXPECTED but not yet OBSERVABLE in bottom-line numbers. Net losses widened in 2025. The market is trading on the anticipated inflection, not proven results.

## Bull Case

- Neutron launches successfully in Q4 2026, opening the $10B+ medium/heavy launch market and driving a revenue step-change to $1.5-2B by 2028
- Space Systems becomes a dominant satellite prime, winning additional SDA tranches and commercial constellation contracts
- Gross margins expand to 45%+ as Electron matures and Space Systems scales, driving EBITDA breakeven by late 2027
- Defense spending tailwinds (HASTE hypersonic testing, national security launches) provide durable, high-margin revenue
- Potential SpaceX IPO re-rates the entire space sector upward

## Bear Case

- Neutron suffers further delays or technical failures, pushing first launch to 2027+ and burning through cash reserves
- SpaceX undercuts on price with Falcon 9 and Starship, making Neutron's unit economics uncompetitive at $55M/launch
- Valuation compression: at 63x P/S, even modest disappointment triggers severe multiple contraction (see: other high-multiple growth stocks in 2022)
- Space Systems margins disappoint as government contracts carry lower margins than expected, diluting overall profitability
- Additional equity raise needed if FCF burn continues at $300M+/yr, diluting existing shareholders
- Peter Beck key-man risk -- company narrative is heavily tied to founder

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

Rocket Lab is a genuinely excellent company building something rare and valuable -- a vertically integrated space prime that is the only credible Western alternative to SpaceX. The business quality score of 18/25 reflects real competitive advantages, impressive execution on margins, and a massive growth runway. However, at 63x trailing P/S and negative expected value from the scenario table, the stock is priced for perfection. The entire Neutron thesis is priced in before the rocket has left the pad. The risk/reward is unfavorable: upside requires flawless execution on an already-delayed rocket program, while downside from multiple compression alone could be severe.

**Would upgrade to BUY if:**
1. Stock pulls back to $35-45 range (25-35x P/S), providing margin of safety
2. Neutron successfully completes key milestones (full stage test, static fire) de-risking the Q4 2026 launch
3. Company demonstrates path to EBITDA breakeven with concrete timeline
4. Any combination of the above that brings expected value positive

**Entry price for BUY:** $40-45 (approximately 30x trailing P/S, pricing in strong growth but not perfection)
**Would revisit after:** Neutron static fire test or Q1 2026 earnings (May 2026)
