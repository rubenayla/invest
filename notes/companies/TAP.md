# Molson Coors Beverage Company (TAP)

**Sector:** Consumer Defensive | **Industry:** Beverages — Brewers (beer, plus growing "beyond beer": non-alc, flavored, energy)
**Price:** $40.60 (2026-06-17) | **Market Cap:** ~$7.6B | **Shares:** ~175M | **EV:** ~$13.9B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-17
**Standalone rating: WATCH** (deep-value optics + ~13% total shareholder yield, but real secular volume decline and 2026 EPS guided *down* — lean BUY LOW near $34-36)

## Situation Summary
Molson Coors is the #2 US brewer (Coors Light, Miller Lite, Coors Banquet, Blue Moon, Peroni, Madri; plus Canada/EMEA/APAC). The stock has fallen ~36% from its 5-year high (~$64) to $40.60, sitting just above its 52-week low ($38.04) and barely off its 5-year low ($37.34). On the screen it looks like deep value: **0.76x book, ~8x forward earnings, ~5.4x EV/EBITDA, a 4.7% dividend (only ~35% payout), and a ~13-14% free-cash-flow yield**, with management retiring ~7-8% of the share count per year via buybacks.

But the cheapness is *earned*, not accidental. FY2025 printed a headline net **loss of -$2.14B (-$10.75 EPS)** — that is almost entirely a **~$3.6B non-cash goodwill impairment** (goodwill fell $5.58B → $1.94B), i.e. management itself writing down the value of brands it acquired. Underneath, the business still generated ~$1.07B of free cash flow. The real problem is the top line: revenue $11.70B (2023) → $11.63B (2024) → **$11.14B (2025, -4.2%)**, US beer industry volumes ran **-5% in 2025**, and 2026 guidance is for underlying **EPS to *decline* 11-15%** on aluminum (Midwest Premium) cost inflation and continued volume/share pressure. This is a *trough* year being guided to, not a recovery year.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** "Structurally declining beer maker. Gen-Z drinks less, GLP-1 weight-loss drugs suppress alcohol consumption, Coors Light's 2023 Bud-Light-boycott share gains have faded, and now tariff-driven aluminum costs are crushing margins. The $3.6B impairment proves the brands are worth less. Cheap for a reason — a melting ice cube. Avoid."
- **Our view (partial pushback):** The *cash machine* is more durable than the *brand value*. At a ~13-14% FCF yield with a fortress-ish investment-grade balance sheet (~2.3x net-debt/EBITDA), Molson Coors returns ~$1.0B/yr to shareholders (≈$376M dividend + ~$648M buyback = **~13% total shareholder yield**). If volumes merely *stabilize* near -2%/yr instead of -5%, the buyback alone compounds per-share value meaningfully and the multiple re-rates off a trough. You do not need growth to make money — you need the decline to *decelerate*. The bet is on management's claim that 2026 is the trough and US industry volume improves vs. the -5% of 2025.
- **Why it is NOT a clear buy yet:** Unlike a multiple-compression story (where fundamentals are fine and only the multiple fell), here the *fundamentals are actually deteriorating* and management is guiding them *lower* for 2026. The impairment is a real signal, not noise. The secular drivers (GLP-1, younger cohorts drinking less, premiumization-to-spirits) are structural, not cyclical. A 13% shareholder yield does not protect you if the underlying free cash flow itself erodes 4-5%/yr indefinitely — you are then just being handed back your own melting capital.
- **Trigger / what re-rates it:** A quarter or two showing US volume decline decelerating to low-single-digits + "beyond beer" / premium portfolio (Madri, Peroni, non-alc, Zoa energy) growing fast enough to offset core-lager erosion + margin stabilization as aluminum costs anniversary. Even a re-rate from ~8x to ~10x forward on stabilizing EPS is +25-35%.

## Bull / Bear

**Bull**
- **Valuation floor:** 0.76x book, ~8x forward EPS, ~5.4x EV/EBITDA, ~13-14% FCF yield. Most of the bad news (impairment, 2026 trough guide) is on the table and the stock sits at multi-year lows.
- **13% total shareholder yield:** 4.7% dividend (only ~35% payout — very safe and growing) + ~8% buyback. Shrinking the share count ~7-8%/yr at <book value is highly accretive *if* FCF holds.
- **Defensive, low beta (0.42):** holds up in risk-off; beer demand is recession-resilient even if secularly soft.
- **Optionality in "beyond beer":** premium (Madri, Peroni), non-alcoholic, flavored/RTD, and energy (Zoa) are growing off a small base and carry better margins; success here changes the terminal-decline narrative.
- **Trough-year setup:** 2026 is explicitly guided as the trough (aluminum cost peak). A 2027 normalization + any volume deceleration re-rates a single-digit multiple quickly.

**Bear**
- **Secular volume decline is real and possibly accelerating:** US beer industry -5% in 2025; GLP-1 drugs, sober-curious Gen-Z, and a generational shift toward spirits/cannabis are structural, not cyclical. The $3.6B impairment is management *conceding* the core brands are worth less.
- **2026 EPS guided *down* 11-15%:** this is a melting-ice-cube, not a recovery. The market may be right to pay only 8x.
- **Margin squeeze:** Midwest Premium aluminum (tariff-driven) inflation hits all of 2026; pricing power is limited when volumes are already falling.
- **Share loss:** US volume share -60bps in Q1 2026 — the post-Bud-Light-boycott tailwind has fully reversed.
- **Value-trap risk:** a 13% shareholder yield funded by declining FCF is just an orderly return of your own eroding capital. The buyback at "cheap" prices is value-destructive if the business keeps shrinking.

## Entry / Exit Discipline
- **Entry idea (BUY LOW): ~$34-36** — i.e. the 5-year low zone, where the FCF yield pushes toward ~16-17% and you are unambiguously over-paid to wait. At $40.60 today the margin of safety is thin given the *down* EPS guide.
- **Thesis-break / avoid-or-trim:** US beer industry volume decline staying at or worse than -5% into 2027 (no deceleration), OR a dividend/buyback cut (signals FCF stress), OR net leverage rising above ~3x EBITDA. Any of these flips it from "cheap defensive cash machine" to "value trap — exit."
- **What would change my mind to BUY:** two consecutive quarters of decelerating US volume decline (toward -2%) **plus** visible beyond-beer/premium mix lifting margins — confirming 2026 was the trough.

## Bottom Line
TAP is a genuine deep-value, high-shareholder-yield, low-beta defensive name trading at multi-year lows and below book — but it is cheap *because* volumes are in real secular decline and management is guiding 2026 earnings *down* 11-15%. The ~13% capital return is attractive only if free cash flow stabilizes; if it keeps eroding 4-5%/yr, the yield is just a managed return of melting capital. **WATCH at $40.60; would turn BUY LOW around $34-36 or on evidence the volume decline is decelerating.** Not an "act now" bargain — a watchlist name to buy lower or on confirmation.
