## Situation Summary

Centrus is the only US-public-listed uranium enricher and the only domestic producer of HALEU (high-assay low-enriched uranium, 5-20% U-235) — the fuel for next-gen advanced reactors (TerraPower Natrium, X-Energy Xe-100, Oklo, Kairos). The thesis has three layers stacked on top of each other: (1) the Prohibiting Russian Uranium Imports Act (signed 2024) bans Russian LEU outright by 2028, creating a forced reshoring of ~25% of US enriched uranium supply; (2) the DOE awarded LEU a $900M task order in 2025 to expand the Piketon, Ohio enrichment complex (+$170M options = $1.07B total), on top of an existing HALEU production contract extended through June 2026 with up to 8 additional option years; (3) advanced reactor designs need HALEU starting 2027-2030 and Centrus is currently the only domestic source. The company has built a $3.8B total backlog (LEU $2.9B incl. $2.3B contingent on capacity, Tech Solutions $0.9B) extending to 2040, and raised $390M via ATM in Nov-2025 at $269/share to partially fund the buildout. But the operating reality lags the narrative: 2026 revenue guidance is $425-475M (flat YoY), the first new commercial cascade does not come online until **2029**, capex is $350-500M for 2026 alone, and the stock has already round-tripped from $66 to $464 to $195 in the last 12 months. Q4 2025 missed EPS by 44% and the stock dropped 20% on Feb-11 2026. At $195 it still trades at ~50x TTM EPS / ~9x sales on a business whose enrichment capacity story is a 2029-2032 event.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Sell-side is bullish — 12-analyst average price target $238-265 (median ~$250, +25-35% upside), consensus rating Buy. The narrative is "national champion, structural Russian-ban tailwind, only domestic HALEU producer, $3.8B backlog locked to 2040." Generalist energy and uranium-thesis funds see LEU as the highest-beta way to play the nuclear renaissance + Trump-era domestic-energy theme.
- **Our view:** The structural thesis is real but the entry price isn't. Three hidden flaws: (a) **the cash flow gap is enormous** — first new commercial cascade 2029, $350-500M of 2026 capex against ~$80M run-rate net income, and capex compounds for 3-4 more years before any new SWU production; (b) **"backlog" is $2.3B contingent** on Centrus actually building capacity that doesn't exist yet — these are forward commitments, not booked revenue; (c) **the dilution risk is structural**, not one-off. The Nov-2025 ATM raised 17% of the share count at $269 — at $195 the next raise will be more dilutive, and project capex through 2029 likely needs another $1-2B beyond current cash. The market has priced in optionality on capacity that will not generate revenue until 2029 at the earliest, while ignoring 4 years of dilution + capex drag.
- **Trigger:** A signed long-term LEU offtake contract at locked-in pricing that converts $2.3B of contingent backlog into definitive (would justify the multiple). On the downside: any DOE waiver denial for 2026/2027 TENEX imports, an equity raise priced below current spot, or a cost overrun announcement on the Piketon expansion.

## Inflection Point

**The thesis inflection is structural (Russian ban + HALEU demand) but the financial inflection is multi-year out.** Centrus is the right company on the right thesis at the wrong moment in the capex cycle. Between now and 2029 the income statement does not improve materially — 2026 guidance is flat. The first commercial cascade comes online in 2029; meaningful HALEU revenue from the new $900M DOE expansion is 2029-2030; the contingent $2.3B backlog converts (or doesn't) over the 2027-2030 window. The catalysts that could move the stock up are **announcements** (definitive offtakes, additional DOE option exercises, customer name reveals) rather than financial results. The risks that could move it down are **execution slips** (cascade timeline pushed right, cost overruns, dilutive equity raises). The 12-month risk/reward is asymmetric in the wrong direction at $195: limited upside from "more good announcements" because PT is already $250, and large downside from "any execution stumble" because there's no near-term FCF cushion to defend the multiple.

## Bull Case

- DOE exercises full $170M of options on $900M HALEU expansion task order, then awards a follow-on contract for additional cascades (raising total federal commitment toward $2B+)
- Centrus signs 2-3 named long-term commercial offtakes (e.g., a major US utility consortium, KEPCO/KHNP follow-on, or a TerraPower/X-Energy multi-year HALEU contract) that converts $1B+ of contingent backlog into definitive
- SWU prices stay above $180/SWU through 2027, locking in high-margin TENEX-replacement contracts at favorable economics
- Trump administration further restricts Russian uranium imports (e.g., earlier ban or no 2027 waivers granted to anyone but Centrus), driving SWU prices toward $250/SWU
- Piketon first commercial cascade comes online on time in 2029; cost-to-build is at or below $1.5B/cascade plan
- Company avoids further dilutive equity raises by securing DOE loan guarantees or DOD/national-security-backed financing
- Re-rating to "national champion strategic asset" — multiple expands toward 12-15x sales on 2030 revenue ($1B+) — supports $400+ stock by 2027

## Bear Case

- **Cash burn forces dilutive equity raise at sub-$200 prices.** Capex through 2029 likely needs $1-2B beyond current cash; an ATM at $150 would meaningfully dilute. Nov-2025 raise was at $269 — next one is much worse.
- DOE chooses to multi-source HALEU (awards parallel contracts to a competitor like Orano USA or Urenco) — Centrus loses its monopoly on US HALEU as the strategic justification for the premium
- Russia retaliates further on TENEX exports OR the US grants 2026/2027 import waivers to other parties (not just Centrus), undercutting the structural shortage thesis
- Q1/Q2 2026 results disappoint — guidance midpoint $450M implies Q1 ~$110M; the Zacks consensus of $74M for Q1 (down 24% over 60 days) suggests analysts are already cutting
- SWU prices fall back to $120-150/SWU as Urenco expands capacity in Europe and Russian material continues to flow to non-US markets
- Piketon expansion suffers a cost overrun or 1-2 year delay (history of US enrichment megaprojects is poor — see USEC's American Centrifuge bankruptcy in 2014)
- Advanced reactor demand (Natrium, Xe-100, Oklo) slips by 2-3 years vs current schedule, pushing HALEU revenue ramp from 2029 to 2031+
- Multiple compression from 50x TTM P/E to 20-25x on a flat 2026 — that alone takes the stock to $80-100 even with modest earnings growth
- $2.3B contingent backlog stays contingent — no major customer signs to firm-fixed terms by year-end 2026

## Verdict

**PASS / WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

Centrus has the right strategic position on the right thesis — it really is the only US-public enricher and the only domestic HALEU producer at a moment when the US is structurally forced to reshore ~25% of its enriched uranium supply. The DOE has put real money behind the company ($900M task order + $170M options + extension of HALEU operations contract through 2026 with 8 option years). The $3.8B backlog to 2040 is impressive on paper.

But the math at $195 is **almost exactly the same math the buyers got at $266** (where the Nov-2025 ATM cleared) — flat 2026 revenue, $350-500M of capex, first commercial cascade in 2029, $2.3B of backlog that's still contingent, and a likely future equity raise to bridge the cash gap. The +80% bull case requires definitive offtake conversion AND DOE follow-on AND no cost overruns AND no further dilution — that's four plies of execution risk priced as a near-given. Expected value is approximately flat (+0.2%); the asymmetry is mildly bear-skewed because the bear case ($90, -54%) has a real near-term catalyst (Q1 2026 earnings May 5, dilutive raise), while the bull case requires multi-quarter announcement flow.

This is a "wait for the dilution and the disappointment, then re-engage" setup, not a "buy the structural thesis at any price" setup. The stock has already 10x'd from the 2023 lows of ~$30 — that move is done. The next 5x will come from the 2029-2032 cascade ramp, and the path from here to there goes through capex, dilution, and quarter-to-quarter execution risk.

**Action if not held:** Pass. Add to watchlist with re-engage triggers below.

**Action if currently long with cost basis below $100:** Consider trimming 30-50% to lock in gains; ride the rest. Cost basis above $200: hold with a stop in the $150 zone, or trim aggressively into any rally above $230.

**Would re-engage as long on:**
- Pullback to $130-150 (cash-plus-backlog floor zone) where the asymmetry flips back to favorable
- Definitive offtake announcement converting $500M+ of contingent LEU backlog to firm
- DOE awards a second HALEU task order or exercises the full $170M option on the existing one
- Confirmed Piketon cascade construction milestones on schedule (2027 mechanical completion, 2028 commissioning)

**Would short / aggressive avoid on:**
- Equity raise priced below $180 (signals balance sheet strain)
- DOE announces parallel HALEU award to Orano USA, Urenco, or a new entrant
- Q1 or Q2 2026 revenue falls below $90M (vs ~$112M/qtr implied by guidance)
- SWU spot drops below $150/SWU sustained for 2 months
- Any Piketon cost overrun or schedule slip past 2030 for first cascade
