# SJM — The J.M. Smucker Company

**Price:** $112.96 (2026-06-18) | **Market cap:** ~$12.0B | **Fwd P/E:** ~10.5x | **Div yield:** 3.8% ($4.40) | **Beta:** 0.26

**Standalone rating: WATCH (lean PASS at $113). BUY LOW under ~$95.**

First look. Packaged-food staple (Uncrustables, Folgers/Dunkin'/Café Bustelo coffee, Jif, Smucker's, Meow Mix, Milk-Bone, and the 2023 Hostess sweet-baked-snacks acquisition). Classic "cheap defensive that fell" name — but it has already recovered off its lows, and the forward guide is for *declining* sales. The discount looks largely earned, not a mispricing.

## The setup
- Trades at ~10.5x forward EPS with a safe-looking 3.8% yield and very low beta (0.26) — screens cheap for staples.
- But it is **near its 52-week high** ($113 vs. range $88–$119), not at the lows. The easy bounce already happened.
- Trailing GAAP EPS is −$11.78 because of **~$2B+ of goodwill/intangible impairments** tied to the Hostess deal. This is what breaks the DCF/RIM models below — ignore their fair values.

## Bear case (lead with it)
1. **FY2027 guidance is a decline**: management guides net sales **−3% to −4%** and adjusted EPS **$9.75–$10.25** (roughly flat-to-down vs FY2026's ~$10.6). A staple guiding to shrinking sales does not deserve multiple expansion.
2. **Hostess was value-destructive.** Bought for ~$5.6B in 2023, then impaired heavily; as of 2026-01-31 the brand was **reclassified from indefinite- to finite-lived** — i.e. management now expects it to decline and will amortize it. Sweet baked snacks face GLP-1 / better-for-you secular pressure.
3. **Leverage is elevated.** Total debt ~$7.1B vs. cash ~$0.06B; EV ~$19.4B on EBITDA ~$1.9B → EV/EBITDA ~10x and **net debt/EBITDA ~3.7x**. Deleveraging competes with the dividend and buybacks for the ~$1.0B of FCF.
4. **Coffee cost whip.** Record green-coffee prices pressure Folgers/Bustelo margins; pricing actions risk volume. Coffee is the biggest segment and the swing factor.

## Bull case
- **Uncrustables** is a genuine growth engine (frozen sandwiches, capacity expansions) inside an otherwise low-growth portfolio.
- Dividend is well-covered on a *forward* basis: $4.40 vs ~$10 EPS = ~44% payout, ~$0.47B cost vs ~$1.0B FCF (~2x). Yield + low beta make it a defensive ballast.
- If coffee costs normalize and Hostess stabilizes, a re-rate from ~10.5x toward a staples-typical 13x on ~$11 EPS is ~$143.
- Recent print was fine: Q4 FY2026 net sales $2.3B (+6%), adjusted EPS $2.77 (+20%). The problem is the *forward* guide, not the trailing quarter.

## Verdict
**WATCH, lean PASS at $113.** Cheap multiple and a safe dividend, but management is guiding sales *down*, leverage is high, Hostess is a confirmed misstep, and the stock has already recovered off its lows. There is no margin of safety here — EV is dominated by the dividend, not by upside.

- **Entry (BUY LOW):** under ~$95 (back toward the 52-week low), where the 3.8%+ yield, ~9.5x multiple, and defensive low beta give a real cushion and the bull case skews EV positive.
- **Thesis-break (avoid / would change mind to negative):** a dividend cut, net leverage rising above ~4x, or coffee margins compressing into a second guidance cut. Any of these and even the dividend thesis fails.
- **What would make it a BUY now:** evidence coffee green-bean costs have peaked AND FY2027 sales guide is revised up to flat-or-better — that flips the base case toward the bull and justifies the re-rate.
