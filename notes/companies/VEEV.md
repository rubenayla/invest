# Veeva Systems Inc. (VEEV)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Health Information Services
**Price:** $167.69 (2026-04-17) | **Market Cap:** $27.6B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-17

## Situation Summary

Veeva is the dominant vertical SaaS platform for life sciences, holding ~80% CRM market share in pharma. The stock is down 46% from its 52-week high of $310.50, driven primarily by the Salesforce competitive scare: management tempered expectations from 18/20 top pharma on legacy CRM to 14/20 committing to Vault CRM. FY2026 (ended Jan 2026) was strong -- $3.2B revenue (+16% YoY), $909M net income (+27% YoY), operating margin expanding to 28.7%. The Vault platform migration from Salesforce infrastructure is ahead of schedule (end-of-support pulled forward to Dec 2029), ~140 customers live. FY2027 guidance of $3.59B revenue (+12.4%) and $8.85 non-GAAP EPS implies the stock trades at just 18.9x forward earnings -- historically cheap for a 15%+ growth compounder with 75% gross margins.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Veeva is a great business, but Salesforce competitive threat creates uncertainty on CRM retention. Stock deserves a lower multiple than its historical 40-50x P/E until Vault CRM migration is fully proven. Growth is decelerating (16% -> 12%) and the premium is no longer warranted.
- **Our view:** The market is over-weighting the Salesforce CRM fear and under-weighting the Vault platform expansion story. The real growth engine is not CRM (which is mature) but Vault's expansion into clinical (eTMF, CTMS, CDMS), safety (replacing Oracle Argus -- multi-billion TAM), quality, and regulatory. These are mission-critical, compliance-heavy workflows where Veeva's integrated platform and validated status create massive switching costs. At 18.9x forward P/E and 14.8x EV/FCF, the market is pricing Veeva like a mature, low-growth enterprise SaaS company, ignoring the $6B revenue run-rate target by FY2030 (17% CAGR needed). The GLP-1/obesity drug boom is a net positive -- it's driving a wave of pharma R&D spending ($30B+ in GLP-1-related deals in 2024 alone), which means more clinical trials, more regulatory filings, more Vault seats.
- **Trigger:** Q1 FY2027 earnings (May/June 2026) showing Vault CRM migration acceleration to 200+ customers. AI agent monetization rollout (Safety/Quality agents April 2026, Clinical/Regulatory August 2026). Any top-20 pharma commitment announcement would re-rate the stock.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- Vault platform expansion is a genuine inflection, but it is mid-cycle, not early.** The off-Salesforce migration removes platform risk and creates a one-time migration revenue wave (FY2026-2029). More importantly, it enables cross-selling: every CRM customer is now a candidate for Vault Clinical, Safety, Quality, and Regulatory modules. The AI agent rollout (April-December 2026) could be a monetization accelerant. However, this is not an early-stage inflection -- it's an established compounder transitioning from a single-product (CRM) to a multi-product platform. The inflection evidence is observable (140 Vault CRM customers, expanding margins, $6B target) but not yet proven at scale for the newer Vault modules.

## Bull Case

- Vault CRM migration reaches 200+ customers by May 2026, top-20 commitments rise from 14 to 16+, killing the Salesforce narrative
- Vault Safety captures meaningful share from Oracle Argus (multi-billion TAM), driving re-acceleration to 18%+ growth
- AI agent monetization (Safety, Clinical, Regulatory agents in 2026) adds incremental ARPU with minimal cost
- GLP-1/obesity drug boom drives sustained pharma R&D spending increase, expanding Veeva's addressable market
- Multiple re-rates from current 18.9x back to historical 30x+ as competitive fears dissipate

## Bear Case

- Salesforce wins more top-20 accounts beyond the current 3, expanding from CRM into adjacent Vault categories
- Vault Safety/Clinical adoption stalls -- pharma companies reluctant to consolidate all mission-critical systems on one vendor
- Growth decelerates below 10% as CRM matures and Vault expansion fails to compensate
- Macro/tariff-driven pharma budget cuts reduce IT spending, elongating sales cycles
- AI disruption: horizontal AI tools commoditize some Vault workflows (regulatory document generation, clinical data management)

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

Veeva is a rare 5/5 moat business trading at its cheapest valuation in years (18.9x forward P/E, 14.8x EV/FCF). The Vault platform expansion is a legitimate growth driver, and the Salesforce competitive fear looks overdone (80% CRM share intact, 14/20 top pharma committed). However, there is no clear variant perception with a timing edge: the market knows Veeva is high-quality, the selloff is driven by reasonable uncertainty about growth trajectory, and the Q1 earnings catalyst is 5-6 weeks away. The "great SaaS, premium multiple, just hold forever" thesis is valid but offers no informational edge. The stock is cheap enough to be interesting, but not cheap enough to be a screaming buy without confirming Vault adoption acceleration.

**Would upgrade to BUY if:** (1) Stock revisits $148-155 range on broader market weakness, providing a wider margin of safety, OR (2) Q1 FY2027 earnings confirm Vault CRM at 200+ customers and/or Vault Safety wins a top-10 pharma account, OR (3) Management raises FY2027 guidance above $3.65B, signaling re-acceleration. At $150 or below, the risk/reward shifts to BUY with high conviction.
