# MP Materials Corp. (MP)

**Sector:** Materials | **Industry:** Rare Earth Mining & Magnets
**Price:** $62.50 | **Market Cap:** $11.1B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-30

## Situation Summary

MP Materials operates Mountain Pass in California (the only producing rare-earth mine in the US) and is vertically integrating downstream into NdPr metal and sintered NdFeB permanent magnets at the Independence facility in Fort Worth, TX. The investment story changed in mid-2025 when the Department of Defense bought $400M of convertible preferred (becoming the largest shareholder), guaranteed a $110/kg NdPr price floor for 10 years via a Price Protection Agreement (PPA), and committed 100% offtake on the next 7,000 MT/yr of magnet capacity over 10 years (the planned "10X" facility in Northlake, TX, financed with a $1B JPMorgan/Goldman commitment). MP simultaneously stopped all REO concentrate sales to China in July 2025. The stock ran from ~$18 in early 2025 to a $100.25 high in late 2025/early 2026 on the policy news, then de-rated to $62.50 -- a 38% drawdown but still up 245% from the 2024 trough. Q4 2025 was the inflection: Materials segment adj. EBITDA flipped to +$40.3M (vs -$1.3M YoY) almost entirely due to $51M of PPA income. The Magnetics segment generated $66.9M in revenue and $26.4M adj. EBITDA for FY25 from precursor metal/alloy sales, with first GM-bound automotive magnet trial production at Independence and commercial ramp targeted for late 2026.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** "Strong Buy" with $75 average target (~20% upside). Sell-side narrative: MP is "the only US rare-earth play" with a Pentagon-backed floor that converts a commodity miner into a regulated-utility-like cash machine. Wedbush published $90 target. Bull thesis emphasizes China export controls (April 2025 dysprosium/terbium licenses, dysprosium +105% YTD to $930/kg) repricing the entire Western supply chain.
- **Our view:** The PPA is genuinely transformational -- it converts ~70% of revenue (the upstream NdPr stream) into a contract with the US government rather than a commodity exposure. That deserves a higher multiple than a typical miner. BUT the $11B market cap on $224M of FY25 revenue (~50x sales), -$111M EBITDA, and -$0.50 EPS already prices in flawless execution of the magnet ramp AND continued policy support AND no slippage at Independence/10X. The stock has already done the easy work: it priced in the Pentagon deal. From here the math demands real magnet shipments, not headlines.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (May 7) -- look for first commercial magnet revenue from Independence, NdPr metal production volumes, capex guidance for 10X, and updated PPA mechanics. A clean print with named GM volume commitments could re-test $80-90. A magnet ramp delay or NdPr volume shortfall would crack the policy-premium thesis and re-test $45-50.

## Inflection Point

**Yes, MP is at a genuine policy + operational inflection -- but it is partly priced in.**

Three structural changes happened in 2025 that justify a different multiple than MP carried as a pre-2025 commodity miner:
1. **PPA price floor** -- $110/kg NdPr for 10 years converts the upstream business from cyclical to contract-revenue. The Q4'25 print ($51M PPA income, Materials seg. adj. EBITDA +$40M vs -$1.3M YoY) is the proof.
2. **Magnet shipments started** -- first commercial NdPr metal production at Independence; trial automotive-grade NdFeB sintered magnets running; GM allocated 1,000 MT initial offtake; commercial ramp late 2026.
3. **DoD as anchor customer + shareholder** -- 100% offtake on 7,000 MT of incremental magnet capacity over 10 years; DoD now the largest shareholder. This is unusually deep alignment.

**However**, the stock already moved from $18 -> $100 on this thesis before pulling back to $62. The 38% drawdown from the high is not "underpriced"; it is "less crazy." From here the upside requires Independence to actually ship volume to GM, 10X to come in on budget, and the Pentagon umbrella to hold through future administrations. None of that is guaranteed.

## Bull Case

- Independence reaches commercial production by Q4 2026 with GM volumes ramping smoothly to 1,000 MT/yr -- adds ~$200-250M revenue at 30-40% gross margin
- 10X facility breaks ground in 2026, on track for first production by 2028; DoD offtake guarantees the entire 7,000 MT
- China export controls escalate (the suspension expires November 2026; could become permanent restrictions); Western dysprosium/terbium prices stay 3-4x Chinese benchmark, which MP captures via heavy-rare-earth separation expansion (the $150M DoD loan)
- PPA income normalizes into a predictable annuity -- the market re-rates the upstream business to a 12-15x EBITDA contract-revenue multiple instead of a 5-8x miner multiple
- Defense magnet demand (F-35, drones, missiles) provides a higher-margin niche that doesn't compete with the GM volume -- structurally higher blended margins than expected

## Bear Case

- Independence magnet ramp slips into 2027 or beyond -- yield issues on automotive-grade sintered NdFeB are non-trivial and China has 30+ years of process knowledge MP doesn't
- 10X capex blows out from $1.25B to $2B+ as construction inflation and complex chemistry catch up; MP has to raise equity at lower prices
- NdPr market price stays at ~$108/kg, meaning the PPA delivers little incremental income (the floor only matters when prices fall below $110); the bull thesis on PPA underwhelms
- A future US administration unwinds or weakens the DoD partnership terms; the policy premium evaporates
- Heavy rare earth separation (Dy/Tb) lags -- MP remains structurally dependent on Chinese heavies for any high-temperature magnet (defense, EV traction motors), undercutting the "supply chain independence" narrative
- Recession kills EV demand -- GM cuts magnet allocation, Independence runs at 30-40% utilization
- At $11B market cap on $224M revenue (50x sales), any execution stumble triggers a violent re-rating to $35-45

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

MP is a real story with a real moat, but the stock is not "underpriced" in the strict sense. The Pentagon partnership genuinely repriced the business (a contract-revenue floor that other miners don't have), and the magnet ramp is on track. But $11B market cap on $224M of FY25 revenue and -$111M EBITDA bakes in significant successful execution. The +5-10% expected value from the scenario table reflects balanced risk/reward, not a clear edge. The "underpriced rare-earth magnet supply chain" claim is half right -- the THEME is underpriced (most investors haven't figured out how strategic this is), but MP as the single-name expression has already done much of its repricing.

**Where the math actually breaks down for the bull thesis:** the PPA only matters when NdPr is below $110/kg. NdPr is already ~$108/kg and rising on China controls. In a world where market prices stay above $110, the PPA pays nothing and you're just owning a miner + magnet ramp at 50x sales. The PPA is a put option that only pays in the bear scenario for prices -- the asymmetry is real but not the windfall the headline number suggests.

**Would upgrade to BUY on:**
- Pullback to $45-50 range (-25-30% from here) where bear case downside is better compensated
- Q1 2026 earnings showing first commercial magnet revenue from Independence with named GM volume
- Heavy rare earth separation (Dy/Tb) commercial production confirmed -- closes the last China-dependency gap
- 10X facility groundbreaking with capex confirmed at or below $1.25B

**Would downgrade to PASS on:**
- Independence magnet ramp delayed to 2027+
- Equity raise at current prices to fund 10X (would imply capex blowout)
- DoD partnership terms renegotiated or weakened by future administration
- Major China-US rare earth diplomatic deal that removes the supply scarcity premium

**Underpriced rare-earth supply chain -- where to look instead:**
The MP-specific math is fully fair. If the user wants deeper underpricing in the same theme, look at: (a) USA Rare Earth (USAR), (b) Energy Fuels (UUUU) -- has Donald monazite optionality, (c) Lynas Rare Earths (LYC.AX) -- the only at-scale ex-China integrated player, (d) heavy-rare-earth-specific juniors like Ucore Rare Metals or Ionic Rare Earths. None have MP's Pentagon umbrella, but several still trade closer to commodity miner multiples without the policy premium fully priced in.
