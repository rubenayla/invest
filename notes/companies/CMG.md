# Chipotle Mexican Grill (CMG)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Restaurants
**Price:** $35.25 (2026-04-16) | **Market Cap:** $46.6B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-16

## Situation Summary
CMG split 50-for-1 in June 2024 and has since fallen ~40% from the low-$60s post-split peak. Brian Niccol left for SBUX in Aug 2024; Scott Boatwright (ex-Arby's COO, internal promotion) inherited a brand that had just posted its first full-year same-store-sales decline since 2016 (-1.7% in 2025, Q4 comps -2.5%, traffic down all four quarters of 2025). Management guided 2026 comps flat vs. Street at +1.8%, triggering an ~8% sell-off on the Feb 3, 2026 print. Boatwright's "Recipe for Growth" plan leans on 350-370 new units (vs. record 334 in 2025), the equipment upgrade package (350 stores installed, showing "hundreds of bps" of comp outperformance), rewards relaunch, LTOs (Chicken al Pastor return), and international expansion. Next catalyst is Q1 2026 earnings on Apr 29, 2026.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus view:** Growth-stock-turned-broken-story. Street has Buy rating with $44-$48 mean target, but the stock trades at 31x trailing / 26x forward PE with flat comps — multiple compression continues until traffic inflects. Bears: premium multiple is unsustainable without comp growth; Chipotle-ization of the menu/value gap vs. QSR peers is structural, not cyclical.
- **Our view:** Weakly differentiated. The setup is closer to "reasonable expectations reset" than a genuine mispricing. Unit economics are still elite (47% ROE, 15% op margins, $3M+ AUV, $1.45B FCF). But at 26x forward and a 3.1% FCF yield, you're paying a quality premium that requires the 2027+ comp recovery to materialize. Models diverge wildly: DCF variants say $7-13 (−78%), RIM says $77 (+127%), GBMs cluster $44-88 (+30 to +166%). GBMs pattern-match to historical recoveries from growth-stock drawdowns — plausible but path-dependent. The "variant" view would require a conviction that the 2027 comp inflects to +3-5% on pricing + equipment-package flow-through — there's no proprietary insight here that the Street doesn't also have.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings Apr 29 (comps +/- direction, full-year reiteration vs. raise); equipment package comp lift data at quarterly updates; loyalty relaunch traction. But these are WELL-KNOWN and telegraphed — the trigger is not asymmetric.

## Inflection Point
**Not yet.** Traffic has declined four consecutive quarters and management guided flat 2026 comps — the inflection is announced but not observed. Recipe for Growth is a plan, not a result. The equipment package comp lift ("hundreds of bps") is encouraging but covers only 350 of ~3,900 stores. Entering before observable evidence = trading catalyst risk for reduced upside (stock has already fallen 40%+ from peak, compressing the reward). Per protocol: "Don't try to buy absolute bottoms. Look for observable evidence that the inflection has begun."

## Bull Case
- Q1 2026 (Apr 29) surprises with comps inflecting positive on Chicken al Pastor + equipment rollout + rewards relaunch.
- Equipment package scales from 350 → 1,500+ stores in 2026, delivering 200-300bps system comp lift by 2027.
- International (Middle East, Europe expansion) adds a meaningful growth vector post-2027.
- Multiple re-rates from 26x → 32x forward PE once traffic inflects; earnings grow 15% = 2-year total return of ~50-70%.

## Bear Case
- Flat-comp 2026 becomes negative, exposing a structural value problem vs. QSR; brand premium erodes.
- Multiple compresses from 26x → 18-20x forward PE (McDonald's-like) as growth story dies; stock at $22-25.
- Boatwright forced into promotional spiral (BOGOs, LTOs are already starting) that compresses margins 150-200bps.
- Unit economics decline as new stores cannibalize existing; 334 openings/year look like empire-building.
- GLP-1 drag on QSR traffic bites harder than expected.
