# V.F. Corporation (VFC)

**Sector:** Consumer Discretionary | **Industry:** Apparel & Footwear (multi-brand: The North Face, Vans, Timberland, Dickies, Altra)
**Price:** $17.03 (2026-06-22) | **Market Cap:** $6.7B | **Enterprise Value:** ~$10.9B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-06-22
**Standalone rating: WATCH — the turnaround is real and de-risking, but the stock has already run ~55% off its lows and near-term guidance is muted. Not a fresh bargain at $17.**

## Situation Summary
VF Corp is two-plus years into a self-help restructuring ("Reinvent") under CEO Bracken Darrell (ex-Logitech, hired October 2023). The crisis was self-inflicted *and* cyclical: a bloated cost base, an over-leveraged balance sheet (the 2020 Supreme acquisition at the top), and a collapsing Vans brand drove two straight years of GAAP losses (**−$969M FY24, −$190M FY25**) and forced a deep dividend cut (the payout went from ~$2.00/yr to **$0.36/yr**, a ~80% cut).

The FY2026 result (fiscal year ended 2026-03-31, reported May 2026) is the inflection:
- **Net income back to +$255M** (first profitable year since FY23) and the **first annual sales growth in three years**; management says ~70% of the business is now growing.
- **Operating margin expanded ~220bps to ~7%** (adjusted), with FY27 guided to **~8%**.
- **Net debt cut from $5.8B to $2.7B** (Supreme sold to EssilorLuxottica for ~$1.5B in 2024 plus FCF paydown), taking leverage from **5.1x to ~2.0x** — the single most important de-risking event in the story.

This is a margin/balance-sheet repair, not a demand story. Revenue is roughly flat-to-slightly-up (~$9.6B continuing); the thesis is entirely operating leverage as the cost base shrinks and the brand mix improves.

## Variant Perception
- **Consensus / bull view:** "Trough is in. Debt halved, margins inflecting, The North Face (+7%) and Altra (+45%) are humming, and Vans has finally returned to growth in the Americas. A storied brand house at 12x forward earnings with a clear path from 7% → low-teens operating margin is an early-innings compounder; pre-crisis VFC ran 13–15% margins and traded $80–90."
- **Our view:** **The easy money — the off-the-lows bounce — is already made, and the next leg needs Vans to actually work.** VFC has roughly doubled off its ~$11 52-week low to $17 (mid-range; 52-wk $11.06–$22.27) *before* the operating recovery is proven. At $17 you pay ~12x forward EPS ($1.39) for a thin-margin (7% operating) apparel house whose **flagship Vans franchise — still ~30% of revenue — fell 5% globally in Q4**. FY27 guidance is deliberately muted (**revenue +1–2% constant-currency, operating margin ~8%**), i.e. management is *not* promising a hockey stick. The tell: the sell-side is "Hold," mean target **$20.21** (+19%) with a wide $14–$40 range, and the GBM 3-year model + autoresearch (the repo's most-trusted signal) returns **no edge** — Kelly says don't size it.
- **Triggers:** Quarterly Vans global comp (the swing factor — Americas growth must broaden to EMEA/APAC), FY27 margin progression toward 8%+, continued net-debt paydown below ~$2.5B, and apparel tariff exposure on Asian sourcing (a live 2026 cost headwind).

## Valuation — models & scenarios

Classic models are largely **broken on VFC** due to the leverage and recent losses, so weight them accordingly:
- **simple_ratios:** fair value ~$29.5 (+70%) — peer-multiple based, optimistic; assumes margin normalization is achieved.
- **RIM:** ~$2.1 (−88%) — unreliable here (known anti-leverage / anti-asset-light bias; anchors on thin book value).
- **DCF / dcf_enhanced:** negative/failed fair values — the high debt load and volatile FCF break the discounting; ignore.
- **GBM 3y + autoresearch (trusted):** **no edge** — Kelly position size = 0.
- **Analyst mean target:** $20.21 (+19%), range $14–$40, "Hold," 21 analysts.

## What would change my mind / actionability
- **Entry of interest:** ~$13–14 (near the analyst low, ~10x forward, ~25–30% downside cushion) **or** clear evidence Vans is growing *globally* (not just Americas) — either would convert WATCH → BUY LOW.
- **Thesis-break (sell/avoid):** operating margin stalls below 7%, Vans global decline re-accelerates, **or net leverage creeps back above ~3x**. Any of those means the repair has stalled and the leverage tail reopens.
- **Bottom line:** A genuinely improving turnaround that has already been substantially rewarded. The franchise (The North Face especially) has real value and the balance-sheet repair is the highest-quality part of the story, but at $17 you're paying ~12x forward for a flat-revenue, thin-margin apparel house whose biggest brand is still shrinking. Watch for a pullback or a Vans global inflection.
