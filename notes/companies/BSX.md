# Boston Scientific (BSX)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Medical Devices
**Price:** $71.28 | **Market Cap:** $106B | **EV:** $116B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-18

## Situation Summary

Boston Scientific delivered a blockbuster FY2025 -- $20.1B revenue (+19.9% reported), adj. EPS $3.06 (+22%), FCF $3.4B -- yet the stock has crashed 35% from its $109.50 52-week high to ~$71. The selloff was triggered by Q4 2025 earnings (Feb 4): despite beating estimates, management guided 2026 organic growth of 10-11%, well below the ~25% U.S. EP segment growth the Street had baked in. The FARAPULSE pulsed-field ablation franchise, BSX's star product, saw U.S. sequential growth flatten as competitors (Medtronic's PulseSelect, J&J's VARIPULSE) entered. A securities fraud class action was filed alleging management misrepresented the sustainability of U.S. EP volumes (lead plaintiff deadline May 4, 2026). Meanwhile, BSX announced the $14.5B Penumbra acquisition (Jan 15) to enter neurovascular/thrombectomy, expected to close H2 2026, ~$0.06-0.08 dilutive in year one. Next earnings: April 22, 2026.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** The market fears FARAPULSE's U.S. growth is decelerating from hyper-growth to normal growth as PFA competition intensifies. The Penumbra deal adds leverage and is near-term dilutive. The class action lawsuit creates headline risk. Analysts still rate it Strong Buy (mean PT ~$104) but PTs are being cut (UBS $120->$105, Citi $102->$94). The stock has de-rated from ~35x forward to ~20.6x forward earnings.
- **Our view:** The market is over-extrapolating the FARAPULSE deceleration. EP is one segment -- BSX has diversified growth across Cardiology (WATCHMAN, ICDs), Endoscopy, Urology (eCoin acquisition), and Neuromodulation. Organic growth of 10-11% in medtech is still elite-tier. The stock at 20.6x 2026E EPS prices in near-zero growth above current guidance, ignoring the operating leverage story (op margins went 16% -> 20% in 3 years). FCF of $4.2B guided for 2026 on a $106B market cap = 4% yield, attractive for a double-digit grower.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (April 22) showing EP stabilization or better-than-feared growth in non-EP segments. Penumbra deal close removing overhang. Resolution or dismissal of the class action (these ambulance-chaser suits rarely succeed).

## Inflection Point

This is **not a turnaround** -- BSX is a high-quality compounder that hit a speed bump. The inflection is in the **valuation reset**: the stock has de-rated from ~35x to ~20.6x forward PE while fundamentals remain strong. This creates a classic "growth at a reasonable price" entry point.

The key question is whether FARAPULSE deceleration stabilizes. If U.S. EP growth normalizes at 15-18% (vs. the prior 25%+ pace) with margins continuing to expand, the stock re-rates higher. The Penumbra acquisition adds optionality but also integration risk.

Evidence the inflection has started: stock found support at ~$67-68 (the 52-week low) and has bounced slightly. Analyst PTs, while being cut, still imply 45%+ upside. Insider selling has been minimal.

## Bull Case
- FARAPULSE competition fears are overdone; BSX maintains 50%+ PFA market share as the market itself grows 20%+ annually
- Operating margin expansion continues toward 22-24% as scale benefits kick in across all segments
- Penumbra acquisition unlocks neurovascular TAM worth $5B+, creates cross-selling opportunities
- At 20.6x forward PE with 10%+ growth and margin expansion, significant multiple re-rating potential toward 28-30x
- Class action dismissed as meritless (typical post-selloff ambulance chasing)

## Bear Case
- FARAPULSE market share erodes faster than expected as Medtronic and J&J ramp competing PFA platforms
- Penumbra integration stumbles; $11B in new debt constrains capital allocation flexibility
- Class action reveals genuine management misrepresentation, resulting in SEC investigation or settlement
- Medical device tariffs imposed under current trade policy, pressuring margins
- Growth decelerates to mid-single digits as EP normalization spreads to other segments

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: MEDIUM-HIGH

Boston Scientific is a best-in-class medtech compounder trading at a multi-year valuation trough after a growth normalization scare. At 20.6x forward PE with 10-11% organic growth, expanding margins, and $4.2B in guided FCF, the risk/reward is skewed to the upside. The AutoResearch model (0.97 confidence) and 5 of 6 GBM models agree this is meaningfully undervalued. The DCF/RIM models are structurally broken for this type of company and should be ignored.

Even if EP growth normalizes to 15%, BSX still delivers elite-tier organic growth. The 20x forward multiple underprices this quality.
