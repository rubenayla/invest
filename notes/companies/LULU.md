# Lululemon Athletica Inc. (LULU)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Apparel Retail
**Price:** $165.57 | **Market Cap:** $19.6B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

Lululemon just reported Q4 FY25 (Jan 2025) earnings that beat estimates ($5.01 EPS vs consensus, $3.64B revenue), but guided FY26 to only 2-4% revenue growth ($11.35-$11.50B) — a dramatic deceleration from the 10%+ growth investors expected. Americas same-store sales are expected to decline 1-3% for yet another year, while tariffs will cost $220M net in FY26 (up from $213M). The stock sits at $165, down 52% from its 52-week high of $348, near 52-week lows. A proxy fight with founder Chip Wilson scored a partial win (board member Mussafer stepping down), adding governance uncertainty. International growth remains strong (China +20%, rest of world mid-teens), but the market is pricing the Americas stall as structural.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** LULU is a mature brand that has lost its growth premium. Americas comps are structurally stalled, tariffs compress margins, and the proxy fight creates uncertainty. Hold rating with ~$190-210 targets, implying limited upside from here. The market treats it as a value name at 11.5x trailing PE.
- **Our view:** The market is over-extrapolating Americas weakness and undervaluing three things: (1) International is now ~25% of revenue and growing 20%+ — at this rate it becomes the dominant growth engine within 2-3 years; (2) FCF yield of 8.1% with a net cash balance sheet gives massive capital return capacity (buybacks at 11x PE are accretive); (3) The brand's pricing power is intact — gross margins at 56.6% are near all-time highs despite $220M of tariff headwinds. This is a temporarily out-of-favor premium franchise, not a broken business.
- **Trigger:** Americas comp improvement in H2 FY26 (lapping easy comparisons + new product innovation cycle) or resolution of the proxy fight giving strategic clarity. Next earnings report (June 2026) showing Americas stabilization would catalyze a re-rating.

## Inflection Point

**Partial inflection — evidence is mixed.** The Q4 FY25 earnings beat suggests the business is performing better than feared, but FY26 guidance disappointed. The real inflection test comes in H2 FY26 when Americas laps weak comparisons. If Americas comps turn positive for even one quarter, the narrative flips from "mature brand" to "recovery story" and the multiple re-rates. The proxy fight resolution (Mussafer stepping down) could accelerate this if it leads to strategic clarity. However, we don't yet have OBSERVABLE evidence that Americas has turned — this is still a "waiting for inflection" setup.

## Bull Case
- Americas comps stabilize in H2 FY26 (lapping -5% comps), any positive print triggers re-rating from 11x to 16-18x PE
- International grows to 35%+ of revenue within 2 years, offsetting Americas maturation and reaccelerating total growth to 8-12%
- $1.5B+ annual FCF funds aggressive buybacks at depressed multiples — 7-8% of shares annually, compounding EPS growth
- Proxy fight resolution gives management runway to execute; possible strategic moves (DTC optimization, new categories)
- Tariff headwinds moderate or get mitigated further — gross margins expand back toward 58%

## Bear Case
- Americas comps continue declining through FY26-27; brand has structurally peaked in its largest market
- Competition from Alo Yoga, Vuori, On Running erodes premium positioning, especially in men's
- Tariff escalation (new rounds) compresses margins below 54%, destroying the valuation floor
- Proxy fight escalates; founder pushes for disruptive board changes or management turnover
- China growth decelerates sharply due to macro slowdown or local brand competition (Li Ning, Anta)
