# SK Square (KRX: 402340) — Backdoor SK Hynix at a Discount

**Date:** 2026-05-10 | **Status:** First-pass research | **OTC:** SKSQF | **IBKR:** Korean local share tradeable

## Thesis in one line
SK Square is a Korean holdco whose ~20.07% stake in SK Hynix dominates NAV (~96% of asset value). It currently trades at roughly a 42–45% NAV discount, narrowed from ~66% at end-2024 and ~75% historical highs — driven by aggressive buybacks, value-up program participation, and HBM-fueled Hynix re-rating. The question is whether the remaining ~43% gap is closable or structurally sticky.

## Historical Discount Range

- **End-2024:** 65.7% (Hynix beaten down, Korea sentiment poor, 11st write-down overhang)
- **End-2025:** 51.5%
- **April 2026:** 45.1%
- **Today (5/10/26):** ~42–43%
- **Korean holdco benchmark:** Samsung C&T trades 50–60% discount; LG Corp 55–65%; SK Inc. 60%+. SK Square's 43% is already inside the chaebol-holdco band.

What closed the gap so far: (1) Hynix HBM3E ramp and AI memory pricing, (2) 100bn KRW buybacks Sept-2025 and Feb-2026 with cancellation (9.5M shares, 6.7% of float retired), (3) value-up plan disclosure, (4) clean-up of 11st overhang via forced sale.

## Structural Reasons the Discount Persists

1. **Tax leakage on a Hynix sale:** A spin-off or sale of the Hynix stake would crystallize ~25% Korean corporate capital-gains tax on ~200 tn of unrealized gain — math-impossible to fully close NAV via monetization.
2. **Korea discount + chaebol governance:** SK Inc. controls SK Square (~30%); minority shareholders bear governance risk. Cross-holdings reduce optionality.
3. **Reinvestment risk:** Past M&A (11st, ADT Caps — sold 2022, SK Shieldus underperforming) destroyed value. Market prices in management empire-building option.
4. **Hynix concentration cycle risk:** 96% Hynix means SK Square is effectively a leveraged Hynix proxy; HBM cycle peaks would punish the holdco harder than the operating co.

## Capital Return (the real catalyst)

- **Cumulative shareholder return:** 810 bn KRW; 6.7% of shares retired
- **Active buyback:** 40 bn KRW (Mar 26 – Jun 25, 2026)
- **Plan:** SK Square is a flagship value-up disclosure under Korea's CVUP program (launched 2024). Mgmt commentary explicitly targets NAV-discount narrowing.
- **Dividend:** modest; buyback-led return policy.

## Risks

- **Hynix HBM cycle peak** — consensus expects HBM oversupply by H2 2026/2027. If Hynix corrects 30%, SK Square corrects more.
- **Won/USD FX:** US investor return exposed to KRW; recent KRW weakness an additional drag.
- **Korean political risk:** value-up program is policy-dependent; a government rollback would re-widen discounts.
- **Korean dividend withholding:** 22% domestic rate for non-residents; US-Korea treaty rate is 15% (portfolio); IBKR typically withholds at treaty rate with W-8BEN on file. Spanish residents: Spain-Korea treaty caps at 15%, recoverable partially.
- **Dilutive M&A:** mgmt has free cash from buyback cancellations; redeployment into another 11st-style deal is the biggest non-Hynix risk.

## Verdict

**Real arb, partially closed, but limited remaining upside on the discount itself.** The "43% discount" headline is misleading — 25% of that is structural (tax leakage on Hynix, chaebol governance, reinvestment risk) and unlikely to compress. Realistic floor is ~25–30% discount, matching what NH and Daishin model in their target prices (1.0–1.1 M KRW vs current 1.09 M KRW).

That means the discount-closing trade is **mostly done**. From here, SK Square ≈ leveraged Hynix beta minus a fixed 25–30% structural haircut. You're now buying a Hynix call, not a holdco-arb. If you're bullish Hynix HBM through 2027, SK Square gives ~1.0–1.2x beta with continuing buyback tailwind. If you think HBM peaks in 2026, the holdco-discount cushion will not save you — Hynix down 30% means SK Square down 35%+.

**Position sizing implication:** Treat as Hynix exposure with a small residual catalyst kicker, not as a deep-value holdco arb. Size like a semis cyclical, not like a special situation. Pass if you already own Hynix directly.
