# ASM International (ASM.AS)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Semiconductor Equipment & Materials
**Price:** EUR 690.80 | **Market Cap:** EUR 33.9B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-18

## Situation Summary

ASM International just reported record FY2025 results (revenue EUR 3.17B, +8.2% YoY) with gross margins hitting 51.8%, driven by surging ALD demand at the 2nm GAA node. The Q4 earnings beat expectations and bookings rebounded to EUR 803M (+26% QoQ), fueled by a surprise recovery in Chinese demand that management had previously guided to decline double-digits. Q1 2026 guidance of ~EUR 813M (+16% YoY) exceeded consensus, and the company raised its China outlook from decline to growth. ASM acquired Axus Technology (CMP equipment) for EUR 81M, broadening its process portfolio, and authorized a new EUR 150M buyback. The stock has nearly doubled from its mid-2025 lows around EUR 350 but remains ~6% off its 52-week high and trades at a forward PE of ~30x on consensus estimates.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** ASM is a high-quality semi equipment play levered to the GAA transition, but growth is well-understood and mostly priced in at 47x trailing / 30x forward PE. Analysts have a mean target of ~EUR 770, implying modest 12% upside. The market sees ASM as a "fair value" compounder, not a deep value opportunity.
- **Our view:** The market underestimates the duration and magnitude of the ALD supercycle. ASM's ALD tools are becoming the process-of-record for 2nm and sub-2nm GAA at TSMC, Samsung, and Intel, with new applications (Mo ALD, area-selective deposition) expanding the served addressable market per wafer. The China recovery is not a one-quarter blip but reflects structural buildout of mature-node and specialty fabs that will persist through 2027. At 30x forward earnings with 20%+ EPS growth, the PEG ratio is ~1.5x, which is reasonable but not cheap for this quality. The real edge is if 2026-2027 earnings estimates are too low due to underappreciated China demand and new ALD applications.
- **Trigger:** Q1 2026 earnings (April 21) confirming above-consensus revenue trajectory. Sequential Q2 guidance increase would validate the upcycle thesis. 2nm HVM ramp at TSMC in H2 2026 is the structural catalyst.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- early-stage ALD supercycle.** The 2nm GAA transition is the most significant node change since FinFET (2015). ALD steps per wafer are increasing meaningfully at 2nm vs. 3nm, expanding ASM's content per wafer. This is not a one-year story but a multi-year structural shift:

- 2nm HVM ramp at TSMC starting H2 2026
- Samsung and Intel also ramping GAA with ASM tools
- New ALD applications (Mo ALD, ASD) entering production for the first time
- China demand recovery adding a cyclical tailwind on top of the structural story
- Axus acquisition opens CMP market as a new growth vector

The inflection evidence is observable: record bookings (EUR 803M in Q4), above-guidance revenue, China orders recovering, and raised 2026 outlook.

## Bull Case

- 2nm GAA ramp drives ALD content per wafer increase of 30-50%, pushing revenue above EUR 4B in 2027
- China demand recovery sustains through 2027 as mature/specialty fab buildout continues
- New applications (Mo ALD, ASD, CMP via Axus) expand TAM beyond consensus estimates
- Operating leverage drives margins toward high end of 28-32% target range
- Multiple re-rates to 35x forward as growth accelerates, implying EUR 900+

## Bear Case

- Semi capex cycle peaks in 2026, with 2027 correction as AI spending moderates
- China export controls tighten further, cutting off recovering China revenue
- 2nm GAA yields disappoint, delaying HVM ramp and pushing out ALD demand
- Competition from Lam Research / Applied Materials intensifies in ALD
- At 47x trailing PE, any earnings miss triggers sharp derating

## Verdict

**WATCH** -- Conviction: MEDIUM

ASM International is an exceptional business (21/25 quality score) with a genuine structural tailwind from the GAA transition and ALD supercycle. The GBM models are unanimously bullish, and the China recovery adds cyclical upside. However, at EUR 690 the stock is not obviously cheap: 47x trailing PE, 30x forward PE, 1.75% FCF yield, and expected value of only +9% from our scenario analysis. The risk/reward is asymmetric to the downside at current levels -- the bull case offers +42% but the bear case is -35%, and the base case is only +9%.

**Would upgrade to BUY if:** (1) Stock pulls back to EUR 580-620 range (~25x forward PE), offering better risk/reward; (2) Q1 2026 earnings confirm above-consensus trajectory AND bookings continue accelerating, reducing bear case probability; (3) A broader semi equipment selloff creates an entry opportunity. The April 21 earnings report is the key decision point -- strong results with raised guidance would justify paying up.
