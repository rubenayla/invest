# Danaher Corporation (DHR)

**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Diagnostics & Research
**Price:** $191.50 | **Market Cap:** $135B

## Situation Summary

Danaher is the premier life sciences/diagnostics compounder, running a portfolio of mission-critical franchises (Cytiva bioprocessing, Cepheid molecular diagnostics, Beckman Coulter, SCIEX, Leica Biosystems) under the Danaher Business System (DBS). After a painful 2023-2024 bioprocessing destocking cycle that saw revenue drop from $26.6B (2022) to $23.9B (2023-2024), the recovery is underway: bioprocessing grew high-single-digits in Q4 2025, led by monoclonal antibody consumables. Full-year 2025 revenue was $24.6B (+3% YoY), with adjusted EPS of $7.80 (+4.5%). The stock is down ~21% from its 52-week high of $242.80, trading at 38x trailing and 21x forward earnings.

The big news: Danaher announced a $9.9B acquisition of Masimo (pulse oximetry/patient monitoring) in February 2026, at 18x 2027E EBITDA. This is classic Danaher playbook -- buy a good business with margin expansion potential and apply DBS. Expected to close H2 2026 with $125M+ annual cost synergies and $50M+ revenue synergies by year 5.

2026 guidance: 3-6% core revenue growth, adjusted EPS $8.35-$8.50, ~28.5% adjusted operating margin. Bioprocessing expected to grow high-single-digits again.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- confirmed bioprocessing recovery + Masimo transformation catalyst.**

- **Bioprocessing inflection**: After 6+ quarters of destocking, bioprocessing consumables are growing high-single-digits, driven by GLP-1 and monoclonal antibody manufacturing demand. Equipment orders have improved for 3 consecutive quarters (guided flat for 2026, potential upside surprise in H2).
- **Earnings trough**: 2025 EPS of $7.80 is the trough. 2026 guidance of $8.35-$8.50 represents 7-9% growth, with potential upside if bioprocessing equipment inflects.
- **Masimo deal**: Classic DBS transformation opportunity. $125M+ cost synergies + $50M+ revenue synergies = meaningful EPS accretion starting 2027. Market is not fully pricing in DBS upside.
- **Margin expansion**: Adjusted operating margin guided to 28.5% for 2026, up from 2025. DBS flywheel drives continuous improvement.

**Caution**: China remains a headwind (unclear when policy clarity arrives). Life Sciences segment still contracting (-1.5% in 2025). Equipment spending cautious globally.

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: MODERATE

Danaher at $191 / 21x forward earnings is the cheapest this quality compounder has been in years. The bioprocessing recovery is confirmed and accelerating, earnings are inflecting off a trough, and the Masimo acquisition adds another classic DBS transformation story. Business quality score of 20/25 reflects a best-in-class operator with durable competitive advantages.

The expected value of +12.3% is attractive with asymmetric skew: the base case alone delivers mid-teens returns, and the bull case has meaningful upside if equipment spending recovers. The main risk is near-term balance sheet leverage post-Masimo and China weakness, but Danaher has managed larger integration cycles before (GE Biopharma at $21.4B).
