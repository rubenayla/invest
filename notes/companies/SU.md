## Investment Case

### Why Suncor?

1. **Integrated Business Model**
   - Upstream: Oil sands production (300k+ bbl/day)
   - Downstream: Refineries (reducing margin volatility)
   - Retail: Petro-Canada gas stations
   - **Advantage**: Profits when refining margins high OR oil prices high

2. **Canadian Stability**
   - No political risk (vs PBR)
   - Stable regulatory environment
   - CAD exposure (not USD debt like PBR)

3. **Strong Balance Sheet**
   - Only 32% debt-to-equity (vs HAL 84%, PBR 92%)
   - Investment grade credit rating
   - Can weather oil price downturns

4. **Shareholder Returns**
   - Consistent dividend payer
   - Share buybacks when cash flow strong
   - Capital discipline (learned from 2014-2016 crash)

5. **Oil Sands Assets**
   - Long-lived reserves (decades)
   - Low decline rates (vs shale)
   - Improving costs ($30-35/barrel breakeven)

### Business Breakdown

**Upstream (Production):**
- Oil sands mining and in-situ production
- Offshore East Coast Canada
- ~750,000 barrels/day total

**Downstream (Refining):**
- 4 refineries in Canada
- Integrated with upstream (captive supply)
- 1,500+ Petro-Canada retail stations

**Advantage**: When oil prices drop, refining margins often improve (offsetting production losses)

## Risks

### 1. Oil Sands Cost Structure (MEDIUM)
- Higher production costs than conventional oil ($30-35 vs $10-20)
- Energy-intensive (natural gas needed for extraction)
- Vulnerable if oil drops to $50-60

### 2. Environmental/ESG Pressure (MEDIUM)
- Oil sands = high carbon intensity
- Regulatory pressure in Canada
- ESG investors may avoid

### 3. Oil Price Dependency (MEDIUM)
- Despite refining, still 60%+ revenue from production
- Needs $60+ oil to be very profitable
- Revenue down 7% YoY (oil price related)

### 4. Operational Complexity (LOW-MEDIUM)
- Mining operations can have outages
- Refinery turnarounds reduce output
- Integration complexity

### 5. Canadian Dollar Exposure (LOW)
- CAD weakness helps (sell oil in USD, costs in CAD)
- But CAD strength hurts margins
