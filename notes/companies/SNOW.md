# Snowflake Inc. (SNOW)

**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Software - Application
**Price:** $175.40 | **Market Cap:** $60.0B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-20

## Situation Summary

Snowflake just reported a strong Q4 FY2026 (ended Jan 31, 2026) beating estimates with $1.28B revenue and 30% YoY product revenue growth. The company guided FY2027 product revenue to $5.66B (27% growth) with a non-GAAP operating margin of 12.5%. Despite these solid results, the stock trades at $175 -- 37% below its 52-week high of $281 -- pressured by the broader tech selloff and persistent GAAP losses (-$1.29B net income in FY2025). The competitive battle with Databricks (private, growing 65% YoY at $5.4B ARR, valued at $134B) continues to cast a shadow, though Snowflake's Cortex AI platform and $400M record deal size demonstrate enterprise AI demand is accelerating. RPO of $9.77B growing 42% YoY signals strong forward visibility.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** Snowflake is a high-quality data infrastructure company transitioning to AI, but growth is decelerating (from 70%+ to 27%) and GAAP profitability remains years away. Databricks is gaining share. Fairly valued at ~$240 (analyst mean target). "Strong Buy" consensus with 91% buy ratings -- this is a consensus long.
- **Our view:** The market is underweighting two things: (1) the $9.77B RPO growing 42% suggests revenue growth will RE-ACCELERATE, not decelerate -- the gap between RPO growth (42%) and revenue growth (30%) is the widest it's been, meaning booked business hasn't yet flowed through; (2) Snowflake's consumption-based model means the AI workload explosion (Cortex AI adoption, agentic workflows) creates a usage multiplier effect that compounds faster than seat-based competitors. The stock at 10.3x forward P/S is the cheapest it's been since IPO.
- **Trigger:** Q1 FY2027 earnings (late May 2026) -- if product revenue growth re-accelerates above 30% (guided 27%), the narrative shifts from "growth deceleration" to "AI-driven re-acceleration." The Databricks IPO (expected H1 2026) will also force a public comparison that could catalyze a re-rating given Snowflake's ~2x valuation discount.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- Snowflake is at a profitability and AI monetization inflection.**

1. **Profitability crossover:** FY2027 is guided for 12.5% non-GAAP operating margin (up from ~8%), with FCF margins already at 24%. As SBC dilution moderates (typical for maturing software companies), GAAP profitability comes into view within 2-3 years. The market tends to sharply re-rate software companies as they cross into sustained profitability.

2. **AI monetization:** Cortex AI adoption is moving from experimentation to production. The record $400M deal in Q4 and 430+ new AI features suggest enterprise AI workloads are becoming material to revenue. Consumption-based pricing means AI usage directly increases revenue without new customer acquisition.

3. **RPO acceleration:** RPO growth (42%) outpacing revenue growth (30%) by the widest gap yet -- this is a forward indicator of revenue acceleration, not deceleration.

Observable evidence: FCF margins expanding, RPO accelerating, record deal sizes, AI feature adoption. This is not a speculative inflection -- it's underway.

## Bull Case

- AI workloads drive consumption growth above guidance; product revenue re-accelerates to 30%+ in FY2027
- Non-GAAP operating margin expands faster than guided as Cortex AI usage has high incremental margins
- RPO conversion accelerates, driving revenue beat-and-raise cadence for 3+ quarters
- Databricks IPO reveals inferior margins, forcing market to re-rate Snowflake's profitability at a premium
- Data sharing/marketplace network effects create a flywheel that competitors can't replicate

## Bear Case

- Databricks continues winning AI/ML workloads with superior open-source ecosystem; Snowflake remains "the SQL warehouse"
- Cloud hyperscalers (Redshift, BigQuery, Fabric) bundle competing functionality at lower prices, compressing margins
- Consumption-based model vulnerability: macro slowdown causes customers to optimize spend (as happened in 2023)
- SBC remains elevated, diluting shareholders by 5-7% annually; GAAP profitability keeps slipping
- Key customer concentration risk -- large deals like the $400M one can create lumpy, unpredictable revenue
