# SoftBank Group Corp. (SFTBY)

**Sector:** Communication Services / Tech Investment Holding | **Industry:** Telecom + AI Infrastructure
**Price:** $11.50 (ADR) / 4,817 JPY (9984.T) | **Market Cap:** 27.5T JPY (~$176B)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

SoftBank Group has transformed from a diversified VC-style conglomerate into a vertically integrated AI-infrastructure holding company under Masayoshi Son's "Izanagi" strategy. The company has committed ~$35B to OpenAI (11% stake), leads the $500B Stargate AI datacenter joint venture (40% ownership), and retains ~90% of ARM Holdings. Q3 FY2025 (Dec 2025) showed net profit of 248.6B yen with 9-month profit of 3.17T yen. However, the stock has pulled back sharply from its 52-week high of $22.50 to $11.50 amid concerns about the sheer scale of capital commitments, PayPay IPO postponement, and broader tech sector weakness tied to geopolitical risk (US-Iran conflict). NAV stands at 30.93T yen with LTV at a manageable 20.6%.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** SoftBank is an over-leveraged bet on AI that depends almost entirely on ARM and OpenAI valuations. The massive Stargate capital commitments ($500B over 4 years) create execution risk and potential dilution. Wall Street is split: 12 buy / 3 hold / 1 sell among Tokyo-based analysts, but the ADR has a "sell" rating from only 1 US analyst covering it.
- **Our view:** The market is pricing a ~34% discount to NAV on the ADR (NAV/ADR = $17.33 vs $11.50), implying either (a) NAV will decline significantly or (b) the holding company discount should be permanent. We think the discount is too wide because: (1) ARM is being repositioned from a licensing business into a direct AI chip provider (Graphcore + Ampere acquisitions), creating a vertically integrated AI compute stack that justifies premium valuations, (2) OpenAI at $500B valuation (where SoftBank holds 11%) is not yet reflected at fair value -- if OpenAI IPOs at $750B+ SoftBank's stake would be worth $80B+, and (3) the LTV at 20.6% is well-managed and below the 25% danger threshold.
- **Trigger:** OpenAI IPO filing (expected 2H 2026), ARM earnings showing AI chip revenue traction, Stargate first datacenter completion demonstrating execution.

## Inflection Point

**Yes -- SoftBank is at a major strategic inflection.** The company has pivoted from a scattered VC portfolio (Vision Fund 1&2 era, 2017-2023) into a focused AI-infrastructure play. Key evidence the inflection has begun:
- FY2025 marks return to profitability after 3 consecutive loss years
- ARM repositioned from licensor to direct AI chip provider (Graphcore + Ampere acquisitions)
- OpenAI investment generating visible returns ($4.2B gain in Q3 alone)
- Stargate construction underway (UAE datacenter opening 2026)
- PayPay IPO (priced March 2026 at $16/share) provides additional liquidity

The risk is that this is a "narrative inflection" more than a "fundamental inflection" -- the massive capital commitments create a path-dependent outcome where success requires AI capex to continue at current pace.

## Bull Case

- OpenAI IPOs at $750B+ valuation in 2026, crystallizing SoftBank's 11% stake at $80B+ (vs ~$35B cost basis)
- ARM AI chip revenue accelerates as Graphcore/Ampere integration delivers vertically integrated AI compute
- Stargate generates recurring infrastructure revenue, transforming SoftBank from investor to operator
- NAV discount narrows from 34% to 15% as portfolio becomes more transparent/liquid
- PayPay + other IPOs provide capital recycling to fund commitments without dilution

## Bear Case

- AI capex cycle slows or reverses -- Stargate $500B commitment becomes a liability
- OpenAI faces competitive threats (Google, Anthropic, open-source) that compress its valuation
- ARM multiple contracts if AI hype cools (currently trading at ~70x forward earnings)
- Leverage risk: if ARM stock drops significantly, margin loan capacity shrinks, forcing asset sales at distressed prices
- Geopolitical risk: US-China tensions could restrict ARM's market access; Middle East conflict disrupts Stargate UAE

## Verdict

**BUY** -- Conviction: **MEDIUM**

SoftBank trades at a 34% discount to NAV on the ADR, with NAV anchored by ARM (~90% stake, publicly traded) and a growing OpenAI position. The stock is technically oversold, down 48% from its 52-week high, creating an attractive entry for a company sitting at the intersection of the largest AI infrastructure buildout in history. The variant perception is that the market is pricing in AI capex cycle deceleration and execution risk on Stargate, while the actual LTV (20.6%) and portfolio quality (ARM + OpenAI) support a significantly narrower discount. Conviction is MEDIUM rather than HIGH because: (1) FCF is deeply negative, (2) Stargate's $500B commitment is unprecedented in scale and execution risk, and (3) holding company value realization depends on events (OpenAI IPO, ARM re-rating) that are probabilistic.
