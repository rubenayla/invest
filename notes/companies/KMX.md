# CarMax, Inc. (KMX)

**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Auto & Truck Dealerships
**Price:** $42.89 | **Market Cap:** $6.3B | **EV:** $23.8B
**Analysis Date:** 2026-03-23

## Situation Summary

CarMax is in the middle of a management transition and activist campaign. The stock has fallen 43% from its 52-week high of $82.79 as FY2026 results showed declining unit volumes (-8% retail used units in Q3) and earnings compression (EPS $0.43 vs $0.81 YoY). New CEO Keith Barr (ex-IHG) started March 16, 2026, and Starboard Value has built a $350M stake demanding $300M+ in cost cuts, dynamic pricing, and improved reconditioning efficiency. Q4 FY2026 earnings report on April 14 is the next catalyst. Auto tariffs are a mixed tailwind — they hurt reconditioning costs but constrain new car supply, pushing buyers toward used vehicles.

## Variant Perception

- **Consensus view:** CarMax is a broken growth story. Volume declines, negative operating leverage, management turnover, and high leverage make this a "show-me" stock. Analyst consensus is HOLD with a mean target of ~$40, essentially "fairly valued" at current prices. Short interest at 12.2% signals meaningful bearish conviction.
- **Our view:** The market is pricing in continued deterioration but ignoring the convergence of three change agents: (1) Starboard's activist pressure for $300M+ cost cuts creates accountability absent in prior management, (2) Keith Barr's hospitality background in multi-unit scaling/loyalty/digital transformation is directly applicable to CarMax's reconditioning and CX challenges, (3) auto tariffs structurally benefit used car dealers over the next 12-18 months by constraining new supply. The stock at 1.0x book and 14x trailing earnings prices in no recovery at all.
- **Trigger:** Q4 FY2026 earnings (April 14) — first results under Barr's watch. Any evidence of unit volume stabilization or concrete cost-cut targets exceeding $150M would catalyze a re-rating. Starboard proxy fight resolution at June annual meeting is the second catalyst.

## Inflection Point

**Yes — early-stage turnaround with multiple catalysts converging:**

1. **New CEO** (Keith Barr, started March 16): Fresh perspective from hospitality/multi-unit operator. Mandate to fix execution.
2. **Activist pressure** (Starboard, $350M stake): Demanding $300M+ cost cuts, dynamic pricing, reconditioning efficiency. Creates urgency and accountability.
3. **Cost reduction program**: $150M already announced; Starboard pushing for $300M+. Operating leverage is massive at CarMax's scale — small margin improvements drive big earnings swings.
4. **Tariff tailwind**: New car supply constraints push buyers to used. This is structural for 12-18 months.
5. **Price already reflects significant pessimism**: At 1.0x book and 14x depressed earnings, a lot of bad news is priced in.

**Risk:** The inflection is not yet proven. Q4 earnings (April 14) are the first real test. If volumes continue declining despite price cuts, the turnaround thesis weakens.

## Bull Case

- Starboard forces $300M+ in cost reductions; operating margins recover to 3%+ (from 1.8%), doubling operating income
- Tariffs constrain new car supply for 12-18 months, driving structural demand toward used vehicles
- Keith Barr implements dynamic pricing and digital transformation from hospitality playbook
- Unit volumes stabilize in H1 FY2027 as price cuts gain traction and macro improves
- Multiple re-rates from 14x to 18-20x as earnings inflect upward — stock returns to $65-80

## Bear Case

- Volume declines accelerate despite price cuts — used car market enters deeper downturn
- Tariff costs on parts raise reconditioning expenses, squeezing already-thin margins
- New CEO fails to gain traction; Starboard proxy fight creates distraction/dysfunction
- Consumer credit deterioration hits CAF portfolio — rising delinquencies on 11% weighted avg rate loans
- High leverage constrains flexibility if recession hits; stock tests $25-30

## Verdict

**WATCH** — Conviction: MEDIUM

CarMax has a compelling turnaround setup with Starboard activism, a new CEO, and tariff tailwinds converging at near-book-value pricing — but the inflection is unproven. The EV of +16% is positive but modest for the risk profile, and the bear case (-49%) is severe. The right play is to wait for the April 14 Q4 earnings report: if volumes stabilize and management provides a credible $200M+ cost-reduction roadmap, the risk/reward shifts decisively in favor of a BUY.
