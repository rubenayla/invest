# Iran Conflict & Oil Prices: Macro Thesis

*2026-03-22*

## Iran's Role in Oil Markets

- **3.1-3.2 mb/d** production (OPEC's 3rd largest), ~1.7 mb/d exports
- **Strait of Hormuz**: 20 mb/d transit (~20% of global petroleum, ~34% of seaborne crude trade)
- Only ~3-5 mb/d bypass pipeline capacity exists — a blockade is nearly impossible to route around

## What Happened (2025-2026)

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| Jun 2025 | Israel struck Iranian nuclear facilities; Iran retaliated; US joined with bunker busters. Ceasefire Jun 24 |
| Feb 28, 2026 | US-Israel launched ~900 strikes in 12 hours. Khamenei killed |
| Mar 2-4, 2026 | IRGC closed Strait of Hormuz. Mines deployed |
| Mar 11, 2026 | IEA: 400M barrel strategic reserve release from 32 nations |
| Mar 15, 2026 | Iran declared control of the strait |

Oil: $68 → $126/bbl peak (+85%), settling ~$108. **Most severe disruption since the 1970s.**

## Historical Precedents

| Event | Supply Lost | Price Impact | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1979 Iranian Revolution | 3-4 mb/d | 2x ($16→$39.50) | 12+ months |
| Iran-Iraq War (1980) | ~4 mb/d combined | $16→$36 | Years |
| 2019 Aramco drone attack | 5.7 mb/d temp | +18% in one day | ~2 weeks |
| 2024 Israel-Iran escalation | Minimal | 5-10% spike | Days |
| 2026 Hormuz crisis | ~8 mb/d partial | +85% peak | **Ongoing** |

**Pattern**: Actual physical supply loss = sustained spike. Threats without disruption = short-lived premium.

## Oil Price Scenarios

| Scenario | Brent Range | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| Quick resolution (Hormuz reopens in weeks) | $75-85 | ~20% |
| Prolonged partial blockade (current path) | $90-120 | ~50% |
| Full sustained blockade (months) | $130-200+ | ~20% |
| De-escalation + OPEC+ oversupply | $55-70 by late 2026 | ~10% near-term |

Forward curves (Jan 2027 futures ~$70) say the market expects this is temporary. JPMorgan base case: Brent averages ~$68 for 2026, possibly $55-60 by 2027. Their bear case: $30s.

## Economic Knock-on Effects

- **Inflation**: US PCE expected at 3.5% YoY by April (up from 2.8%). Gas: $2.90→$3.72/gal. Every $10/bbl ≈ +0.2-0.3pp CPI
- **Fed**: Rate cuts on indefinite pause. Markets price only 1 cut in 2026 (was 2). Possible April hike if inflation accelerates. **Classic stagflation setup**
- **Recession risk**: Prediction markets 24%→38%. Oxford Economics: $140/bbl sustained for 2 months = eurozone/UK/Japan contraction
- **Markets**: S&P 500 down ~5.5% since war began. Asian markets hit harder (oil import dependence)

## Sector Winners & Losers

**Winners:**
- Oil majors (Exxon hit ATH $161.87, Chevron +30% vs S&P YTD)
- US shale/E&P (higher prices incentivize production)
- Defense (extended military action, Gulf security spending)
- Renewables (energy security narrative accelerates transition)
- Gold / safe havens

**Losers:**
- Airlines (jet fuel is largest variable cost)
- Consumer discretionary (higher gas = less spending)
- Transport/logistics (fuel surcharges, insurance up 4-6x for Hormuz)
- Tech growth (higher rates for longer kills DCF valuations)
- Emerging market oil importers (India, Turkey, SE Asia)

## Long-Term View (3-5 Years)

**The paradox**: Short-term spike enriches oil producers, but medium-term effect accelerates the forces that reduce long-term oil demand. Every oil shock in history has triggered permanent demand destruction and substitution.

- **Energy transition accelerates**: Import-dependent economies (Europe, Asia) now have visceral motivation for domestic renewables/nuclear
- **Oil structural oversupply**: OPEC+ spare capacity, US shale ramp (6-12 month lag), demand growth slowing (EVs, efficiency)
- **Defense spending**: Elevated for years given new Middle East security architecture
