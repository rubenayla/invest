## Executive Summary

**Thesis**: Copper faces a structural supply deficit starting 2026, driven by AI/EV/grid infrastructure demand while mine supply peaks and cannot scale fast enough. This creates a multi-year opportunity.

**Verdict**: **CAUTIOUSLY BULLISH** - The thesis is sound, but current valuations in miners are stretched. Wait for better entry or use DCA approach.

| Factor | Assessment |

| Risk/Reward | Moderate - thesis priced in partially |

## 2. Mining Capacity & Scalability

### Critical Constraint: Miners CANNOT Scale Rapidly

| Constraint | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **New Mine Development** | 20-30 years in developed regions |
| **Chile Projects** | 13 projects worth $14.8B - most won't boost output until 2028-2029 |
| **S&P Global Forecast** | Production from current assets **peaks 2025-2026**, then declines 2.3%/year through 2035 |
| **Ore Grade Decline** | Existing mines producing lower quality ore, requiring more processing |

### Recent Supply Disruptions

- **Grasberg (Indonesia)**: Fatal mudslide Sept 2025, force majeure until Q2 2026 (world's 2nd largest mine)
- **Quebrada Blanca (Chile)**: Production guidance downgraded
- **Chile blackout** (Feb 2026): Major mines temporarily offline

### Mine Supply Growth

J.P. Morgan's 2026 mine supply growth estimate: **+1.4%** (down ~500kt from start-of-year estimates)

**Key Insight**: If copper demand grows 2.8%/year (UBS estimate) and supply grows only 1.4%, the deficit compounds annually.

## 5. Risk Assessment

### Geopolitical Risks

| Country | % of Production | Risk Level | Key Risks |
|---------|-----------------|------------|-----------|
| Chile | ~25% | Medium | 46.5% tax cap, infrastructure fragility |
| Peru | ~10% | Medium-High | Social license challenges, protests |
| DRC | ~11% | High | M23 rebels, ESG crackdowns, 15-20% risk premium |
| Indonesia | ~5% | Medium | Grasberg force majeure |

### Operational Risks

- **Declining ore grades**: More rock processed for same copper
- **Cost inflation**: Labor, energy, equipment
- **ESG requirements**: Increasing compliance costs

### Market Risks

- **China slowdown**: 50%+ of copper demand
- **Recession**: Copper is cyclical
- **Substitution**: Aluminum in some applications

### Valuation Risk

**Primary concern**: Current valuations assume copper prices stay elevated. If copper pulls back to $8,500/mt, miners could drop 30-40%.
